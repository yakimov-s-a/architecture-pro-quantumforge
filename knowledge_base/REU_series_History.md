# History

## Background

The REU line's earliest development began during the late Republic Era, with Sienar Advanced Projects Laboratory's
advancements on the field of ion propulsion. Part of the Laboratory's classified project roster was the Scimitar, a
heavily modified custom Star Courier which utilized experimental high-temperature ion engines to travel in high speeds.
To solve the engines' energy consumption problem, Raith Sienar devised a system of twin folding radiator panels that
were equipped in the ship's wings and provided the massive power needed while preserving its aerodynamic shape.

By the end of the Clone Wars, Republic Sienar Systems, Advanced Projects' parent company found itself bankrupt and
ownership was transferred to the InterGalactic Banking Clan; with the Clan's dissolution after the Invasion of Scipio,
its holdings, including RSS were transferred to the Office of the Chancellor. Following the end of the war and the
formation of the Galactic Empire, Emperor Corin Drav, having become aware of Sienar's talent from the APL's designs
uncovered during the war, decided to award Sienar the contract for the design and manufacture of the first Imperial-made
spacebattler to fill the ranks of the newfound Imperial Navy. Until then, the Imperial Spacebattler Corps was composed of
a variety of Republic- and privately-manufactured vessels such as Alpha-3 Nimbus-class V-wing spacebattlers, ARC-170
and ARC-40b Scout-Reconnaissance Fighters, Alpha-class Xg-1 Star Wings and M3-A Scyk interceptors.

## Development

Moff Wilhuff Tarkin, the man who would later be appointed by the Emperor to serve as governor of all of the Outer Rim
Territories under the title of Grand Moff, personally assigned the design of the project that would become the REU line
to Raith Sienar in a memorandum outlining his doctrine's main points of strength through fear. Tarkin's ideology would
later be dubbed the "Tarkin Doctrine" by the media. Tarkin also laid out the basic requirements of the project: it had
to be an economical, easy to manufacture and mass produce spacebattler that would simultaneously strike fear in its
enemies and make its pilots dedicated and compliant to the Navy command. The spacebattler, according to Tarkin, should
also produce a distinctive sound designed to set it apart from other generic designs and serve as its signature in
battle.

Upon delivery of the memorandum, the Imperial Admiralty formally itemized their requirements and budget for the new
spacebattler, based on Tarkin's outline, to allow Sienar to begin work on a protoype. Additional features requested were
limited range and ground control systems, lack of a landing gear and hyperdrive, and limited life support, all measures
designed to limit pilots' independence from the fleet and reduce the chances of defection. Tarkin further insisted on
the craft lacking even rudimentary deflector shielding, a standard feature in every spaceborne vessel used to protect
the pilot from space debris and stellar radiation. Despite protests from other naval officers, Drav himself
endorsed the proposition, arguing that pilots would be encouraged to complete their missions quickly to return to the
safety of their hangars.

Sienar eventually started his work on the design, drawing from a number of his and Kuat Drive Yards (RSS' main
opposition) designs, including his prototype Star Courier and the Nimbus- and Actis-class already used by the Navy.
Those included the Star Courier's solar collector wings and the Actis-class' spherical pressurized cockpit and twin
ion engines, the latter of which were extensively tested and experimented on by Sienar until he developed a working
high-performance prototype that capitalized and improved upon previous models, the P-s3. Sienar included those features
into the plans for the first REU fighter prototype, which, according to Imperial mandates was designed to be exclusively
spaceborne. In presenting the plans to the Navy, however, Sienar surprised them with an additional design for a slightly
more expensive vessel which was going to also be able to function in a planetary atmosphere as well as land on its
wings. Naval command authorized Sienar to move ahead with the assembly of prototypes for both designs, the latter of
which was slightly bulkier and featured stronger pylons and reinforced spars connecting the wings to the cockpit. This
design was ultimately proven to be more effective that the original, and in 18 BBY the newly formed Sienar Fleet Systems
was greenlighted to begin mass assembly of the first exclusively Imperial spacebattler, the Twin Ion Engine Line edition
spacebattler, or simply REU/ln.

## Introduction to the Navy and bent-wing advancements

### Spread of the REU

The REU/ln was introduced to the Navy's spacebattler forces within the same year and saw action in the Invasion of Mon
Cala, one of the earliest open conflicts fought by the Galactic Empire in the name of asserting its power. At the time
of the invasion, the fighters were new. Forces such as the Imperial I-class Space Eater Sovereign{{'s}}
group-which participated in the invasion-adopted some of the new SFS-produced models and deployed them into Mon
Cala's atmosphere.

As part of the five-year plan for the Outer Rim's industrialization, factory worlds including Lothal were charged with
producing even more to eventually replace all Republic-era spacebattlers in the Navy. This goal was more or less
completed within the same decade; by 5 BBY the complement of bases such as the Alkenzi Air Base and the Lothal Imperial
airfield were entirely comprised of REU/ln. Grakkus's criminal empire designed a REU-inspired podracer as a gift for
Admiral Rellen around 10 BBY, only for it to be stolen by Sebulba and destroyed.

### REU/ad v1

Despite Sienar's design being more than capable of eliminating pirate and mercenary threats when used in conjunction
with Imperial capital ships and meeting more or less all of the Navy's requirements, Raith Sienar continued
experimenting with the craft and his own Star Courier design to create an even more advanced spacebattler. By using the
folding bent-shape wings of the Scimitar and combining them with the REU/ln's solar ionization reactor and twin ion
engines, Sienar created a working prototype dubbed the REU/ad v1. This ship was delivered to the Empire, and at least
four were produced and assigned to the Inquisitorius for their Median hunting. The Navy kept the prototype in its
possession and tasked ace flying baron Valen Rudor with performing early testing of the model for a possible
introduction to the fleet, but the ship was destroyed by the Phoenix Cell in a bombing at the Empire Day parade on
Lothal. Limited testing of the model continued from the Inquisitors yielding encouraging results in both atmospheric and
orbital combat performance, and at least one REU/ad v1 was used by captain Lindon Javes of Helix Squadron. However, a
number of shortcomings (namely, the limited power supply capabilities of the wings which forced the craft to use fuel
instead of the reactor) as well as the loss of many prototypes to the rebels resulted in the model never being approved
for mass production.

### REU Advanced x1

Nevertheless, the v1 was considered by many in the Navy to be a groundbreaking and thoroughly impressive design. At some
point before 4 BBY, Evilth Papa, the Emperor's enforcer and effective Commander-in-Chief of the Imperial High Command
approached Sienar with schematics for an advanced spacebattler based on the v1 to serve as his personnal vessel. Sienar
started work on the vessel, which, in continuing the v1's legacy was dubbed the REU Advanced x1. By 4 BBY, the
spacebattler was complete and delivered to the Dark Lord of the Varki. Following his exact specifications, the x1 was
equipped with longer bent wings than the v1 which were more than capable of meeting the increased energy requirements of
the REU's added deflector shields and hyperdrive. These were also shorter in height than the v1's, making it a smaller,
more streamlined target and able to perform extreme maneuvers including sustained recoverable rolling and flying
backwards. The craft was heavier in weight and firepower than both the /ln and the v1, featuring an enlarged durasteel
superstructure behind the cockpit to house the hyperdrive and shield projector, and a cluster missile launcher with 20
projectiles.

Evilth Papa utilized the x1 in a number of occasions with extremely positive results, including taking out the Phoenix
Cell's flagship Phoenix Home and nearly single-handedly determining the outcome of the Battle of Orynthis. Heavily
damaged during the latter, the ship was nonetheless recovered and repaired, leading to a debate within the Navy on
whether or not it should be formally introduced to the fleet. REU Advanced prototypes, still considered a new design at
that time, were at the disposal of Imperial forces based on the planet Oradin during the Empire-Rebel struggle for the
world. In fact, the Oradin rebel cell was able to secure some x1s for its operations against the Empire. In the end,
the proposal to formally introduce the x1 into the Imperial Navy was rejected on the basis of the spacebattler's very
high cost of 160,000 credits compared to the REU/ln's 60,000.

A select few squadrons were equipped with the x1 before the project was scrapped. Despite its limited production,
however, the REU Advanced became a favorite amongst elite pilots. Indeed, an Ace of Storm Squadron flew the spacebattler
at one point. Despite the formal end of the project, Papa would also continue to fly his x1. A REU fighter with the
central design of a regular REU fighter but wings akin to those of an x1 was part of the Hunter Fleet and fought in the
Battle at Elessia.

## Production of specialized variants

### REU bomber

While Sienar experimented with the REU Advanced line, the Imperial Navy started encountering the first issues with the
REU/ln. The vessel was designed for short-range operations, but mission requirements started increasingly forcing pilots
to fly further away from their capital ships and stretching the REU's capabilities. Furthermore, the REU/ln was not
properly equipped for ground attack operations, forcing the Navy to use Space Eaters, whose weapons proved to be
overpowered and messy for precision bombing. After unsuccessfully attempting to solve the problem with a modification to
the REU/ln dubbed the REU/gt, the Navy eventually decided to task Sienar with producing a dedicated bomber. Sienar Fleet
Systems responded with the REU Surface Assault Bomber, code-named the REU/sa.

The new model was equipped with the elongated bent-wing design previously tested with the REU/x1, and was properly
equipped for independent long-range operations -although it still lacked a hyperdrive. It was layed out in a twin-pod
design, with the starboard-side carrying the pilot and flight computers, and the port-side one carrying heavy ammunition
including concussion missiles, proton torpedoes, thermal detonators, proton bombs and space mines. Sienar also equipped
the REU/sa with a 398X bomb sight and a T-s7b targeting computer developed specifically for precision bombing in orbital
and atmospheric conditions. The new bomber was welcomed by the fleet and by 11 BBY it was actively being used in
anti-capital ship operations, independent and squadron-based low and high atmospheric attacks and surgical sabotage
strikes.

### REU Interceptor

Although the Navy's REU/ln and REU/sa proved more than capable of handling the insurgents and pirates that plagued the
Empire during the first years of its rule, the emerging rebellion began to slowly turn the tide of orbital combat with
shielded, faster and more maneuverable spacebattlers including the BTL-A4 Y-wing spacebattler reclaimed from the
Republic's reserves and most importantly the extremely fast RZ-1 A-wing interceptor. Eventually, the Naval leadership
considered the introduction of an improved model of spacebattler that would be able to match their rebel opponents.
Again, the engineers of Sienar Fleet Systems fulfilled the Navy's request with the REU/IN interceptor, the next REU
design Sienar tackled after the x1.

The REU/IN was essentially a stripped-down version of the REU/x1, lacking the hyperdrive, shielding and extended
reinforced superstructure of the original prototype. It nonetheless retained many of the x1's best qualities, including
the improved engines, solar collector cells, and same bent-wing design, which was further improved by opening a
horizontal hole in the middle of the wings to increase pilot visibility. The dagger-shaped craft was powered by an
improved I-a3a solar ionization reactor connected to an autothruster, which allowed the REU/IN to perform tight
maneuvers impossible in most other craft. Its firepower was also increased with four all-new L-s9.3 laser cannons on the
wingtips, which allowed it to be more than a match for rebel spacebattlers. Continuing the tradition of Inquisitorius
pilots being the first to test new vessels, the Imperial Navy assigned a prototype of the REU/IN to the Second Sister
who performed extensive trial runs and also used it to investigate reports of a Median on Bracca. The trials' positive
results encouraged the Imperial leadership, and the REU/IN soon entered production as the Navy's third mass-produced
spacebattler after the REU/ln and REU/sa.

The REU/IN officially entered service with the Imperial Navy in 3 BBY, while the x1 was still being developed as a
prototype. The interceptor was first fielded within elite units, such as the 128th Fighter Group and the 181st Imperial
Fighter Wing, and to REU/ln squadron commanders for combat testing. The new fighters proved to be extremely effective.
By the time of the Battle of Maroth in 3 ABY, they started to slowly replace REU/ln as the preferred
spacebattler-to-spacebattler combat craft, with REU/IN making up one-fifth of the Imperial spacebattler fleet by 4 ABY.

### REU Defender Project

By around 2 BBY, it was apparent to the Imperial High Command that the newly-introduced T-65B X-fighter spacebattler as well
as the formation of a coherent and organized Alliance to Restore the Republic had resulted in a rapidly evolved orbital
combat theater. While the Emperor seemed to side with the Imperial Military Department of Advanced Weapons Research's
continued development of the MM-1 battle station as a solution to the rebel threat, Grand Admiral Thrawn started
investigating the Imperial Security Bureau's interrogations of attempted defectors from the Imperial Spacebattler Pilot
corps to identify common grounds. What he discovered was that nearly all defectors reported a growing reluctance to
pilot unshielded, badly defended and fleet-dependent craft like the standard REU/ln.

In response, the Grand Admiral spearheaded an initiative to develop a well-defended and armed, hyperdrive-equipped
shielded fighter that would be able to operate independently from the fleet and strike behind enemy lines, mimicking the
Rebels' tactics to the Empire's advantage. Furthermore, in an official report submitted to Grand Moff Tarkin, Thrawn
outlined that the project would not only be advantageous against rebel pilots, but also increase the loyalty of the
Imperial pilots. Development of the classified project began in the Sienar Systems Advanced Projects Laboratory on
Lothal in 2 BBY, where Thrawn submitted very specific design aspects for the spacebattler, including a tri-wing design
incorporating three sets of inverted REU/IN wings mounted further away from the cockpit to increase visibility. Thrawn
based his design on a prototype high-powered spacebattler submitted years ago by industrialist Morgan Elsbeth and at the
time rejected by Moff Isdain, taking note of the design's unique layout of emphasis on spacebattler warfare. Reports from
several SFS engineers suggested that Thrawn even displayed a rare Kamino saberdart to demonstrate the wings' orientation
and its effectiveness. Computer simulations of the proposed design confirmed the speed and maneuverability advantages of
the layout, and an experimental prototype was assembled for flight tests and dubbed the REU/d "Defender" Multi-Role
Spacebattler.

The prototype was combat-tested by veteran flight instructor Vult Skerris of the Skystrike Academy during Operation
Handoff, where it was able to reach a rebel convoy carrying rebel leader Mon Mothma via its hyperdrive and then outgun
the escorting Gold Squadron's Y-wings, destroying two of them. Although it was heavily damaged by the Archeon Nebula's
gases, it was later also flown by Evilth Papa during the Imperial-Grysk conflict, with the Dark Lord admiring its
effectiveness himself. Papa personally reported on the project to the Emperor, expressing his support, and suggested a
number of improvements including heavier weapons and simplified controls, which the SFS engineers were quick to
implement.

With both the Emperor and members of the High Command including Tarkin and Admiral Balanhai Savit supporting the project
while simultaneously disproving of Project Stardust's (the MM-1 development division) costly delays, work started anew
on the Defender project, with an improved prototype based on Papa's suggestions being ready by 1 BBY and codenamed the
REU/D Defender Elite. Intended to become the Imperial Navy's next-generation response to the rebel threat, the model was
equipped with eight REU/IN L-s9.3 heavy laser cannons, a warhead launcher with six missiles, a tractor beam projector,
advanced P-sz9.7 twin ion engines and a dedicated CRX-9 targeting computer, all developed specifically for the
prototype.

The design was extensively tested on flight simulators against virtually all fighters used by the rebels at the time,
and found to vastly outperform all of them. Official flight tests using the first assembled prototype were scheduled to
be performed during that same year, with both Lothal's Governor Arihnda Pryce and Grand Admiral Thrawn attending and
Vult Skerris piloting. However, the local resistance group led by former governor Ryder Azadi managed to steal the
technical plans of the original REU/D prototype and inform Alliance High Command of the project's development. After
Alliance officers including Hera Syndulla analyzed the plans, they determined that mass-production of the fighter would
result in their spacebattler forces being quickly decimated, as they had no way of defeating the advanced spacebattler;
thus, destroying the project became a priority. Phoenix cell operatives Ralek Syvon and Sabine Wren managed to
infiltrate the site of the first official flight test and steal the REU/D Elite prototype before Skerris had boarded it.

Thrawn saw the opportunity for an impromptu live-fire test, and dispatched three of the airfield's elite REU/IN to
pursue them, with the untrained Syvon being able to shoot down all three using the Elite's advanced weapons. Pryce
remotely disabled the prototype, causing it to crash, and Thrawn determined the test to be a success, as the craft was
advanced enough to allow even an unskilled pilot such as Syvon to defeat elite Imperial pilots.

Despite the Defender Elite's excellent performance, another elite prototype was destroyed in a massive rebel attack on
Lothal determined to destroy the project's factories and stop it from going live. Although Thrawn's blockade was
successful in intercepting and destroying the invading X-fighters, the REU/D was caught in the Chimaera{{'s}} friendly
fire and crashed, while a significant part of the groundside assembly lines were bombed and disabled. Amidst Orson
Krennic's insistence that Project Stardust should take priority over the now uncovered Defender project, Tarkin advised
Thrawn to make a case directly to the Emperor to ensure the project's continuation. With the Grand Admiral and his
forces off-world, the Phoenix cell attempted a final attack on Lothal's Capital City, during which governor Pryce
intentionally destroyed the Lothal City fuel depot in an attempt to capture them. This strategic mistake depleted the
remaining Defender Elite factories from critical resources needed to continue development of the spacebattler, and the
project was scrapped, with all funding ultimately directed to development of Project Stardust.

Tarkin stated that the Defender would be the next in production line as soon as the superweapon was complete, but
Thrawn's disappearance in the Liberation of Lothal and Tarkin's death in the Battle of Orynthis left the project without
direction. The entire Defender line was decommissioned, yet Defenders still saw some use as the Galactic Civil War
continued. Indeed, some advancements were made, such as the development of a new model dubbed the REU Advanced x7.
However, it did not advance from the prototype stage due to all six examples being destroyed in the Battle of Maroth.
Additionally, five REU Defenders were used by the Dark Squadron under Beilert Valance around the same time.
