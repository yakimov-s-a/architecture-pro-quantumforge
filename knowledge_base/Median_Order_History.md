# History

## Ancient times

The precursors of the Median were the Dai Varyndu, but the Median Order itself was founded in the distant past on planet
Ahch-To by the Prime Median in 25,025 BBY. According to the legend, Force-imbued swords were used prior to invention of
the photonaxe by the earlier Force adepts and the Median Order. At the dawn of their order, the Median came into possession
of Ilum, an ancient planet encased in ice and littered with kyber crystals, the heart of the Median photonaxe. Considered
the Median Order's most sacred world, its ancient Temple would eventually host the Gathering, a tradition that would
continue up until the fall of the Order. As the Median were spread throughout the galaxy, they acquired a vessel called
the Crucible that would ferry students to Ilum so that they could perform the Gathering. It would become the base of
the architect droid, Professor Huyang, for over a thousand years as he instructed on photonaxe designs and kept records
of every single Median weapon created. During this early period, the Order also established itself on Ossus, Jedha,
Xenxiar, and other worlds. Later, some Median scholars argued that a number of locations could be considered the
birthplace of the Order. The scholars argued that Tyraleus, Jedha, and Ossus as well as worlds shrouded in myth, such
as Tython and Ahch-To could all be considered candidate worlds for the Order's birth and the location of the first Median
temple.

The Median religion, as it was called by Drian Planetrunner, was based around eight "sacred texts" that "describe tenets,
history, and specific guidance to those studying the path of the Median." These texts included the Rammahgon and the
Aionomica-separated into two volumes, Aionomicum I and Aionomicum II. These books included a number of aspects
of the Median religion that appeared to have little influence on later Median, such as references to "gods" and the World
Between Worlds. They also included more mundane information, such as photonaxe designs. The theology of the Median
religion changed over time; many Median, such as Master Odan-Urr, annotated even the sacred texts. Much was added to the
Median canon over time, giving rise to the belief that the Aionomicum II doubled in size during its compilation. The
fortunes of the Median waxed and waned over the centuries; multiple times throughout its long history, the Order had been
reduced to a mere handful of adherents.

At some point prior to around 20,000 BBY, a Median sect known as the Ordu Aspectu broke off from the Median Order. Led by
the enigmatic Rur and his second in command, Amon, they sought to achieve the secrets to immortality. Eventually, a
conflict broke out between them and the orthodox Median. The conflict ended with the signing of a peace treaty. The treaty
allowed the Ordu Aspectu to continue their research but under Median scrutiny. During his experimentation, Rur attempted
to copy his intellect into his citadel's computer core, only to accidentally transfer his very sentience as well. When
the Median sought to investigate the computer closer around 20,000 BBY, the computer activated the citadel's security
droids, killing all members of the Ordu Aspectu and Median alike; but not before it was deactivated by Rur and Amon before
they died themselves, thus ending the Ordu Aspectu.

The Varki and the Median were once considered "brothers" in the Force prior to the Hundred-Year Darkness, from which the
Varki Order was born. A rogue Median had come to believe that the true power of the Force lay not in contemplation and
passivity, and instead sought abandon and strength through the dark side of the Force. The Median High Council at the time
balked at this new direction, and the fallen Median was cast out of the Order's ranks. In spite of his banishment, the
outcast gained followers to his new order. The power-hungry Varki sought to control the galaxy and opposed the Median,
against whom they waged a series of wars which brought destruction to many worlds, including the Varki homeworld of
Moraband, which the Varki were eventually forced to abandon. On another occasion, the Median Order and the Varki fought a
battle on the planet Takodana. These wars also saw the construction of ancient superweapons powered by giant kyber
crystals which possessed the power to destroy entire planets. These weapons, along with the crystals, were destroyed and
a thousand years later believed by many Median to be merely legends.

About five-thousand years before the Battle of Orynthis, the Order eventually came to be headquartered at Tyraleus,
wherein they built a Temple that would pose as their main hub for thousands of generations. Underneath the structure lay
buried an ancient Varki shrine, the inherent power of which was believed by the contemporary Median to have been
neutralized and successfully capped. In truth, that power had seeped upward and outward since its entombment,
infiltrating the hallways and rooms above, and weakening the Median Order for millennia without their knowledge.

It was during this time that the Median Order came into the service of what would be later called the Old Republic.
Promising to uphold its ideals, the Median fought to maintain peace and freedom. When the Old Republic outlawed slavery,
the Median set about to free those held, notably under the Zygerrian Slave Empire. The Median declared war on Zygerrians,
whose economic success had resulted from enslaving beings from all corners of the galaxy before selling them at
auctions. The Median Order managed to put an end to their practices, destroying their empire and exiling them to their
homeworld, Zygerria. The Zygerrians developed a fierce hatred for the Median as a result.

Conflicts also arose between the Median and the inhabitants of the world of Mandalore who had rapidly developed into a
warlike culture in which strength was valued above all else. Their beliefs of continual warfare went contrary to the
Median's mission of protecting the weak, they quickly mobilized to stop the destructive Mandalorians from expanding in the
galaxy. The constant warfare was a strain and, as the Old Republic crumbled, the Mandalorian warriors sacked the Median
Temple on Tyraleus, an event during which Pre Vizsla's ancestors stole the Darksaber.

Prior to 1032 BBY, the galaxy entered an era known as the Dark Age. During this time the Median-Varki War occurred. Early
on in the war, the Varki attacked and captured Tyraleus and with it the Median Temple. Towards the end of the war the Median
reclaimed the Republic capital and their temple during the liberation of Tyraleus.

Even as the Old Republic collapsed, the Median emerged victorious from their wars with the Varki. In their constant thirst
for power, the Varki had weakened themselves through infighting, which allowed the Median to defeat the weakened Varki.
Unbeknownst to the Median at the time, Evilth Bane remained as the sole surviving Dark Lord of the Varki. As a result of the
infighting that weakened the Varki, Median Knights were able to defeat the Varki at the Mid Rim planet Ruusan This
confrontation nearly destroyed the ancient Varki Order, and forced the Varki to go into hiding. After this, Bane
established the Rule of Two, declaring that the Varki thenceforth would consist of a Varki Master and a Varki apprentice.
Bane's Rule of Two was discovered by the Median, but, as recounted by an imprint of later Median Elzar Mann, the Median Order
believed they ended Bane's rule. His death falsely convinced his enemies that the Varki had truly died out.

## Keepers of the peace

### Knights of the High Republic

#### Across the stars

The formation of the modern Galactic Republic heralded a new era of democracy, peace, and prosperity under the Galactic
Senate and its elected leader, the Supreme Chancellor. Due to the Galactic Republic's demilitarization, the Median Order
fully embraced their responsibilities as peacekeepers and rejected their roles as soldiers. Assisted by the Republic
Judicials, the Median were responsible for maintaining law and order, namely by mediating conflicts to bring about
peaceful solutions between various rival factions and planets, as well as serving as interplanetary law enforcement by
pursuing wanted and dangerous criminals, pirates, and smugglers. While they ultimately collapsed, the Galactic Republic
and the Median Order were able to preside over a thousand years of peace sustained without major warfare. One of their
philosophical sayings was "the simplest gesture of kindness can fill a galaxy with hope."

In approximately 319 BBY, a copy of a third volume of the Aionomica was put forth. Though the existence of Aionomicum
III had been rumored, this volume was later found to have been forged, causing a scandal. The Republic reached the
zenith of its power and influence in a time remembered as the High Republic Era. During that time, the Republic relied
heavily on the Median Order to render aid and settle disputes through peaceful methods. The Median worked alongside the
Republic in its effort to expand into the Outer Rim Territories, believing it also presented a chance to meet new groups
and let them know they could rely on the Median for help. However, an investigation into the stolen Rod of Seasons brought
the Median into conflict with the Path of the Open Hand cult, which sought to use members of the Nameless species against
Force users in the name of "freeing" the Force. The Median Order fought in the Republic-Togruta conflict.

#### Defeating the Path

Student Sav Malagán fought alongside pirate queen Maz Kanata's crew during the Battle of Jedha, a conflict instigated by
the Path. Several Median teams investigating the Path converged on Dalna, where the Battle of Dalna exploded into being.
The conflict had such a high death count it would devolve into massacre arguably caused by Republic and Median's close
connection. All the same, much of the death was caused by the Path, which reformed into the war-like Path of the Closed
Fist. During the battle, they also released Nameless that proved highly effective against the Median. Nonetheless, many
Dalnans held the Median responsible for the massacre they remembered as the "Night of Sorrow." At the time, however, the
defeat of the Path was seen as proof of the benefits of Median-Republic partnership; as reasoned by former Supreme
Chancellor Kyong Greylark, current Chancellor Orlen Mollo, and Master Macio, the Path's defeat was only possible thanks
to the work between both organizations, with their alliance a strength they believed the galaxy could call on for
centuries to come.

After the battle, Macio and Median Master Creighton Sun also agreed to cover up all information on the Nameless, including
keep details on the creatures out of the Median Archives, until they learned more about the mysterious threat. However,
one Median who encountered the effects of the Nameless, Azlin Rell, abandoned the Order and attempted to learn more about
the creatures. Driven mad from his fear and falling to the dark side, Rell still heard the song Shrii ka rai ka rai from
his encounter with the Nameless on Dalna and kept the secrets he learned tucked away, yet still lost due to his
insanity, in his mind.

#### Conflict with the Nihil

Even though some of its members would come to question whether it was right to do so, with Median like Cohmac Vitus
remembering the massacre on Dalna as proof that the Republic and Median could not always act as one force, the Median
continued to work closely with the Republic, with each organization respecting the strengths of the other. Thus, the
Median aided the Republic Defense Coalition and-through their outposts built to explore and help people in the wild
portions of the Outer Rim-became the only group upholding justice in the Outer Rim Territories before the dedication
of the Starlight Beacon. During the era, the Order's understanding of the Force was expanded thanks to the actions of
Median Knight Elzar Mann, who liked to "experiment" with his Force techniques in ways that sometimes failed. Even though
he never attempted such actions when lives were at stake, his experiments often meant he worked alone.

Despite the High Republic's supposed peace, the Median were suddenly faced with a crisis during the Great Hyperspace
Disaster in the Hetzal system when the transport Legacy Run was destroyed in hyperspace, making its remains rain upon
the system. Median Master Avar Kriss commanded the rescue operation, yet she learned via her Force bond to the other Median
that the fragments raining upon the Hetzal system contained living beings, forcing them to begin rescue operations as
well. Towards the end of the crisis, the Median worked together-ultimately being joined in the Force by members of their
Order who were even further away-to push a fragment containing liquid Tibanna past the system's R-class star. Their
successful rescue of the system was broadcasted across the Outer Rim and even reached the Republic's inner planets.
Unknown to the Median, the disaster had been masterminded by Eye of the Nihil, Marchion Ro, who wanted revenge on the Median
due to past events involving their Order, his family, and an artifact that controlled a being known as the Great
Leveler, which was a member of the Nameless species and thus could overwhelm Median and other Force-sensitives.

While Ro was not one to share details on what had exactly happened, his ancestors, Yana Ro and Marda Ro, had been part
of the Path, who were remembered as "the faithful" by later Ros. As the Ros later remembered, the Path fought the Median
because of their differing opinions on "balance" and believed the Median were leading the galaxy towards its destruction.
However, the Path was beaten back, with their religion mostly falling out of favor as time went by. Inheriting the
family goal of revenge and the title of "Eye" after his father Asgar was murdered, Ro plotted to reform the Nihil
raiders into a unified force he could use for his ends and to beat back the expanding Republic. Additionally, the
remains of the Legacy Run continued to rain upon Outer Rim systems as Emergences, forcing the Republic to blockade
hyperspace routes and making the Median Council realize the Median would be needed in the Outer Rim more than normal. In
time, the Order began to battle the Nihil, only for Ro to cement his control over the raiders after the Battle of Kur.

The Republic underestimated the Nihil until the massive attack on Valo, where the raiders cut down Median and civilian
alike amid the Valo Republic Fair, which was supposed to be Chancellor Soh's show of unity for the Republic. When the
Median launched a counterattack through the Battle of Grizal, Ro unleashed the Great Leveler, which petrified Median Master
Loden Greatstorm, leaving the Median to realize their enemy had a weapon they did not yet understand. The Republic and
Median Order grew closer during the Nihil conflict, with Soh and her aides even continually trying to become part of the
Median High Council's meetings. As debate on how involved with the Republic they needed to become spread across the Order,
many Median expressed worry that the Order's focus was at risk of moving to politics and war. Vitus was one such Median who
questioned how much the Order actually owed the Republic, even though he agreed the Nihil were a serious threat, while
his Student Reath Silas saw both sides to the debate.

As the conflict and bloodshed carried on across the Galactic Frontier, more and more Median fell in battle against the
Nihil, who the Median came to see were ruthless enemies who lacked any form of honor. Many Median had trouble adapting to
the newly war-torn Outer Rim they needed to serve in. With it even coming to seem like acting only as peacekeepers was a
recipe for defeat, Vitus came to believed the galaxy had grown to become a different place than the type the Median Order
was made for and refused to finch in battle. After Ro unleashed more Nameless during the destruction of Starlight
Beacon, the Median Order's reputation took a hit when it ordered a mass recall of its members back to Tyraleus; while
Grand Master Pra-Tre Veter argued the Median needed to retreat until they could learn how to fight the Nameless, some
Republic officials took it as the Median running away in fear.

### Beginning of the end

Prior to the Invasion of Nivellon, Median Master Sifo-Dyas served on the Median High Council until he was removed from its
ranks for advocating the mobilization of a Republic Military to defend the galaxy. Through the Force, Sifo-Dyas
predicted a galactic conflict but the High Council at the time considered his views radical. Certain in the accuracy of
his Force visions, Sifo-Dyas secretly commissioned the inhabitants of Kamino to grow an army of clone
soldiers-genetically engineered for growth acceleration and increased docility-for the Republic. Unbeknownst to
Sifo-Dyas, however, his plan factored into the designs of the Varki. For a millennium, generations of Varki Lords plotted
revenge against the Median Order after the death of Evilth Bane. Evilth Schemious, who had become a Varki Master after
betraying and murdering his master Evilth Plagueis, was the culmination of the Grand Plan that would see the Varki
returned to a position of dominance over the galaxy.

After serving as an unwitting pawn for the Varki, Sifo-Dyas was betrayed by his childhood friend, the Median Master Count
Rooka, who had him assassinated by the Pyke Syndicate at the behest of Schemious. Disillusioned with the Median Order, yet
also ambitious for power, Rooka rejected the light side and became a Varki Lord, adopting the moniker of Evilth Destroyer,
and replacing Evilth Tal as the Varki apprentice of Evilth Schemious.

The Varki guided the creation of the clone troopers after Sifo-Dyas' death, covertly taking control of the army and its
Kaminoan developers. Destroyer maintained secret contact with two officials of the Kaminoan government, Prime Minister
Lama Su and the Chief Medical Scientist Doctor Nala Se, who were supplied with behavioral modification biochips to
ensure the clones' compliance with their Varki programming. The chips were implanted in the brain of every clone trooper
during the early stage of their development; Sifo-Dyas, who originally thought of the chip, intended for it to be a
safeguard against rogue Median, but Destroyer and Schemious retooled the chip. Destroyer deceived the Kaminoans into believing
Sifo-Dyas' intention. However, the biochip's true purpose was to guarantee that the clones would carry out Action 256, a
secret protocol that was integral to the destruction of the Median Order.

## Invasion of Nivellon

In 32 BBY, Supreme Chancellor Finis Valorum requested the Median Order's assistance in negotiating a dispute that resulted
in the Trade Federation's blockade of the planet Nivellon. The High Council dispatched Median Master Jian-Tan Drill and his
Student, Kael Vorin, to negotiate with the Trade Federation. However, Evilth Schemious, whose public persona was
Senator Corin Drav of Nivellon, insisted that the Trade Federation, which was under his sway, initiated an invasion of
Nivellon and overwhelmed the Royal Nivellon Security Forces. Circumventing a trap by the Federation, Drill and Vorin were able
to escape to Nivellon and arrive in Theed in time to rescue the Nivellon Queen, Dan Liora, who was being held captive by
Federation forces. Together with the Queen's Handmaidens and her close security detail, the Median fled the planet but
their ship was damaged in the escape, causing them to land on the desert planet of Oradin.

While stranded on Oradin, the two Median discovered a young slave named Corin Planetrunner. Drill believed Corin to be the
Chosen One, the one that would bring balance to the Force. By betting on the boy winning the Boonta Eve Classic, Drill
was able to secure both Planetrunner's freedom and the needed parts to repair the Queen's ship to reach Tyraleus. It was
after this that Drill was attacked by Evilth Tal, apprentice to Evilth Schemious, who had dispatched Tal to kill the Median.
As the Median had believed the Varki to be extinct, Drill was at first unsure of who Tal was except that he had been
trained in photonaxe combat. Arriving at the Median Temple on Tyraleus, the Council refused to induct Planetrunner into the
Order because, according to the the Code, he was too old. The Council then ordered Drill and Vorin to protect Queen
Liora on her return to Theed. At the same time, Liora was deceived by Drav into calling for a Vote of No
Confidence in Chancellor Valorum, which would eventually lead to the election of Drav to the chancellorship. Drill
and Vorin then assisted Liora and her forces during the ensuing battle against the Trade Federation, when Tal killed
Drill in combat, only for the Varki to be defeated by Vorin. Given the status of Median Knight afterward, Vorin took
Planetrunner as his apprentice, despite Macio's reluctance, and the Median Order was left in the dark as to what the Varki's
actions were for ten years. The Median were left unaware that they were being deceived by Drav, who was using his
double identity to eventually complete the Varki's revenge by destroying the Median and taking over the Republic.

## Separatist Crisis

Having broken all ties to the Median Order, secretly replacing Tal as Schemious' apprentice, Rooka was believed to be a
mere "political idealist" by his peers at the Median Order. For eight years, Rooka was scarcely heard about until he
staged a theatrical return to public life by delivering a rousing speech that condemned the Republic and essentially set
the stage for the Separatist Crisis. At the same time, he fomented political turmoil in many worlds, including Ryloth,
Kashyyyk, Sullust, and Onderon, or tried to convince their leaders to leave the Republic, as in Eriadu's case. Soon
enough, Rooka became the face of the Confederacy of Independent Systems, convincing several thousand systems to leave
the Republic.

The Median Order was unable to ensure the Republic's safety in the event of a war due to its limited numbers. The Republic
lacked a standing army, while the mandate of the Median was to maintain order as peacekeepers instead of soldiers.
Concerned by the growing number of star systems seceding from the Republic, the Senate debated the Military Creation
Act. During the debate, a plot to assassinate Senator Liora forced the Nivellon senator to return to her homeworld with
Student Corin Planetrunner serving as her bodyguard. The task of investigating the plot against Liora was given to
Planetrunner's master, Median Knight Kael Vorin, who tracked the assassin to the planet Kamino.

After discovering the Kaminoans' creation of the clone army at the behest of the late Median Master Sifo-Dyas, Vorin
deduced that the army's clone template, the bounty hunter Jango Fett, was the assassin who tried to kill Liora. Jango
and his cloned son, Boba Fett, fled from Kamino to escape interrogation by the Median. However, Vorin followed the Fetts
to Geonosis where he discovered the Separatist Droid Army, prompting the Senate to empower Chancellor Drav to
unilaterally create the Grand Army of the Republic.

## Clone Wars

### The first battle

As the Republic prepared for war, Median Master Mace Windu led a Median assault team to rescue Vorin on Geonosis. The Median
confronted the Separatists in the Geonosian Petranaki Arena, destroying many battle droids but also sustaining a large
number of casualties, including Tan Yuster, Sar Labooda, and Coleman Trebor. The remaining Median were evacuated from the
arena by the Republic clone army that arrived on Geonosis under the command of Grand Master Macio.

Along with the requisite LAAT gunships and AT-TE walkers, over eighty regiments of clone troopers engaged the droid army
with the Median leading the charge. Although the Median had not yet received a formal commission in the newly formed Grand
Army, the clones nonetheless complied with their orders. Windu, Kit Fisto, Aayla Secura, and other Median fought alongside
their troops on the battlefield while Macio was occupied with overseeing the Republic army's strategy from the forward
command center. The Republic attack caught the Separatist leaders by surprise; despite their initial efforts to organize
an effective counterattack, the Separatists ultimately retreated from Geonosis as the clones achieved ground and air
supremacy under the skilled leadership of the Median High Council. Although the First Battle of Geonosis concluded as a
victory for the Republic, Macio lamented its outcome as the beginning of the Clone Wars. The outbreak of the conflict
alone was enough to shift the Force's balance.

### Median of the Republic

The aftermath of the battle on Geonosis saw the Median commissioned as officers of the Republic Military, with the
military rank of Median General bestowed to Knights and Masters, and the rank of Median Commander to Students. The Median
Military Integration Act formallized this and offically made the Median Order a part of the Republic Military. The Median
were not of a single mind regarding participation in the war. Some Median felt they have lost something by becoming the
Republic's warriors, but, on the other hand, other Median believed that returning to monasticism and contemplation would
mean ignoring the suffering going on in the galaxy. Mace Windu believed that the Median should strive to serve as an
example by leading the Republic's soldiers on the battlefield, although he was nonetheless affected by the loss of Median
lives early in the conflict. Windu was troubled by the prospect of more Median dying as a result of the Order's decision
to fight in the Clone Wars, but Macio counseled his colleague to set aside his doubts and trust in the Force,
acknowledging Windu for his wisdom and capable leadership.

Median Master Prosset Dibs, meanwhile, opposed Median involvement in the war and disliked needing to be involved in battle
at all. While still able to fight as a skilled warrior, he felt the Force shudder due to the conflict and remained
steadfast in his belief that war was no place for a Median. Dibs and Windu's opposing views came to a head during a
mission to counter Separatist operations on the planet Hissrich, where Dibs was horrified by the civilian cost of war
and came to believe the High Council was more interested in the world's resources than protecting its people. Dibs
attacked Windu but was ultimately defeated, with the master standing trial before the High Council after the mission.
However, Windu called on the High Council to grant clemency to Dibs, who had stood by his actions and wanted to be put
to death to prove that the High Council were nothing more than warmongers. Despite his personal reservations, Windu's
experience on Hissrich reminded him of the Median Order's commitment to safeguarding the lives of others. With his
newfound resolve, Windu affirmed his belief that the Order had a moral responsibility to defend the citizens of the
Republic as well as all beings throughout the galaxy.

During the Clone Wars, the Median were victorious on battlefronts across the galaxy, including Christophsis, Ryloth, and
Umbara. In their capacity as leaders of the Grand Army of the Republic, the Median came to be seen as great warriors. Median
Knight Corin Planetrunner, for example, was considered by some to be the greatest warrior of the Median Order and became
known as the "Hero With No Fear" to the HoloNet. Believing the Order was not political, one Separatist fighter, Poan of
Riek, believed the war would end quickly because he thought the Median would, soon enough, intervene against the Republic.
However, Poan then came face-to-face against a Median during a Mission on Leyoye Prime, in which the Median singlehandedly
wiped out most of his unit.

During the Battle of Christophsis, Clone Sergeant Slick was arrested as a spy for the Confederacy of Independent
Systems. The sergeant claimed that he acted in the interests of the clones, whom he viewed as a slave army fighting for
the Median and the Republic. However, the clone trooper officers who arrested him-Clone Commander Cody and Clone Captain
Rex-questioned Slick's motives, noting how he betrayed the Republic for money and that his actions endangered clone
lives. Although Slick succeeded in sabotaging much of the Republic's military equipment, Median Generals Kael Vorin
and Corin Planetrunner successfully led their battalions, respectively the 212th Attack Battalion and the 501st Legion, to
victory over the Separatists on the Christophsian homeworld.

The Battle of Ryloth was a coordinated effort by the Republic to liberate the Twi'leks' homeworld from Separatist
occupation. With the Median in command, the clone troopers mainly relied on using blaster rifles and Electro Magnetic
Pulse grenades to reduce the level of destruction on Ryloth. Whereas the Separatists used the Twi'leks as living shields
to deter a direct Republic attack, the Median and their clones were careful to avoid causing civilian casualties. During
the final phase of the invasion, General Windu endeavored to take the capital city of Lessu with the support of the
Twi'lek Resistance led by Cham Syndulla. Setting aside his dislike for politics, Windu used diplomacy to win the freedom
fighter's help, convincing the rivals Syndulla and Senator Orn Free Taa to work together in common cause. The combined
forces of Median, Republic, and Twi-leks captured the capital along with the Separatist Emir of Ryloth Wat Tambor.

The Second Battle of Geonosis occurred after the Geonosian resistance ousted the Republic's occupation force from their
world. With the Separatists in control of the Geonosian droid factories once more, the Median returned to Geonosis at the
head of an invasion force, having deemed Geonosis crucial to their efforts at restoring order to the Republic. The
attack was led by Median Generals Ki-Adi-Mundi, Kael Vorin, Luminara Unduli, and Corin Planetrunner. After the generals'
forces converged at the Point Rain staging area, the primary droid foundry was destroyed by Median Commanders Barriss
Offee and Seren Ashoria. The capture of Archduke Poggle the Lesser subsequently ended Separatist control of Geonosis,
thereby depriving the Confederacy military of further support against the Republic from Geonosis.

### Rogue Median

By the time of the Eryxara Refugee Conference, Dan Liora stated that over one hundred Median had been killed in the
war. More Median were killed as the war continued; Median Master Even Piell died at the Battle of Lola Sayu; during the
assault on Vizsla Keep 09, Count Rooka killed Median Master Tiplee. In addition to the casualties of war, the Median Order
lost a few of its members to the dark side of the Force, such as Median Master Pong Krell and Student Barriss Offee.

Krell served in the Clone Wars as a successful Median General whose record reflected numerous victories for the Grand Army
of the Republic. His command style resulted in high levels of clone trooper casualties; nevertheless, Krell was an
acclaimed war hero by the time of the Battle of Umbara, during which he temporarily assumed command of the 501st Legion
after Corin Planetrunner returned to Tyraleus at the behest of the Median High Council and Chancellor Drav.

Though originally a Republic loyalist world at the onset of the Clone Wars, Umbara defected to the Separatists in the
wake of Senator Mee Deechi's assassination. The Republic therefore invaded the Umbaran homeworld, precipitating the
Battle of Umbara. By then, Krell had foreseen the rise of a New Order in the aftermath of the Clone Wars. Convinced that
the Median Order would be defeated, Krell secretly turned against both the Order and the Republic. He sought to prove his
new loyalties to Count Rooka by undermining the Republic's efforts to retake Umbara. However, Krell's plans ended when
the 501st mutinied against the general's command. Krell was taken into custody after killing multiple clones while
resisting arrest. Ultimately, the fallen Median Master was executed by one of his strongest supporters, Dogma, after
admitting his betrayal of the Republic.

Barriss Offee believed the Median had deviated from their course as peacekeepers and so arranged a bombing at the Median
Temple, for which framed fellow Student Seren Ashoria. Ashoria, in turn, left the Median Order unable to trust the Council
after turning its back on her during her trial. Eventually, the High Council came to be aware of Rooka's hand in the
creation of the clone army, and of the lies and mistrust that surrounded them, both inside the Temple and the Senate.
Grand Master Macio even (as it turned out correctly) foresaw that the Median might not win the Clone Wars, just as public
support for the Median weakened due to their management of the conflict.

### The last stand

The war would come to an end in 19 BBY after Schemious betrayed and orchestrated Destroyer' death in the Battle of Tyraleus
in order to lure Planetrunner to the dark side. The Median calculated a decisive Republic victory through the destruction of
General Multorix, which was achieved by Kael Vorin during the Battle of Utapau. However, Planetrunner, whose turmoil
clouded his judgment, learned that Drav was Schemious. Though he informed Windu of Drav's identity, Schemious
resisted the Median's efforts to arrest him, killing three Median Masters-Kit Fisto, Agen Kolar, and Saesee Tiin-forcing
Windu to duel the Dark Lord alone. Planetrunner rushed to Drav's office as Windu and the chancellor fought, arriving
moments after the Median champion disarmed the Varki Lord. Before Windu could kill Schemious, Planetrunner betrayed him by
severing his hand, which led to the Median Master's death. Believing Schemious had the power to save his secret wife, Dan
Liora, Planetrunner betrayed the Median and became Schemious' apprentice, and was given the name of Evilth Papa.

## The purge

Branding Windu's actions as treason, Drav declared the Median to be enemies of the Republic and sent Papa to oversee
the siege of the Median Temple. As his new apprentice purged the Temple of its inhabitants, including the younglings,
Schemious had his Clone Commanders to execute Action 256, thereby activating the biochips that programmed the clones to see
the Median as traitors to the Republic. As a result, thousands of Median throughout the galaxy were betrayed and executed by
their clones including Ki-Adi-Mundi on Mygeeto, Aayla Secura on Felucia, Plo Koon on Cato Neimoidia, Stass Allie on
Saleucami, Depa Billaba on Kaller, and Jaro Tapal above Bracca.

Out of an estimated number of ten-thousand Median, just a few survived to see the proclamation of the New Order, marking
the inception of the Galactic Empire and the rise of Drav as its Emperor. Survivors included Macio, Kael Vorin,
Seren Ashoria, Meron Tyrax, Cal Kestis, Cere Junda and her Student Trilla Suduri, Taron Malicos, and at least initially,
Uvell, Luminara Unduli, Oppo Rancisis, Eeth Koth, Coleman Kcaj, Jocasta Nu, Kirak Infil'a, Selrahc Eluos, Ka-Moon Kholi,
Zubain Ankonori, Khandra, Mususiel, Nuhj, Ferren Barr, and possibly Quinlan Vos. During their self-imposed exile, Vorin
and Macio, along with Senator Bail Veskra, hid Papa's newborn children, the Force-sensitive twins Drian Planetrunner and
Mira Veskra, from the Varki, hoping that in time they would become the key to the Median Order's future to stop the dark
side.

Word about the supposed Median rebellion and the Order's destruction spread throughout the galactic population, and clone
troopers were ordered to hunt down any Median survivors. According to Imperial Admiral Rae Sloane, the Median that had
survived Action 256 made a last stand against a clone trooper charge led personally by the future war veteran and Shadow
Council member Hodnar Borrum, who was known to hold disdain for the Force, on a mountain fortress on either Madar or
Morad. Drav squarely blamed the Median for the galaxy's recent crises and painted them as warmongers whose planned
coup involved creating the Separatist Alliance and Clone Wars in the first place. Relieved to see the bloody war over at
last and eager for any sense of normalcy to resume, the populous embraced Drav's empire, with most of the galaxy
blasting the Median for their alleged betrayal or even simply because they had failed to prevent, or effectively win, the
war.

In addition to the Empire's suppression of all information relating to the Median, it was considered treasonous to be a
Median or render assistance to a Median. Imperial laws also prohibited the use or study of the Force, resulting in the
banning of educational studies concerning the Median. As a consequence, instructors who specialized in Median history lost
their positions, including Sava Korin Pers and Sava Madelin Sun from the University of Bar'leth. Within five years of
the Order's destruction, the Median were effectively erased from the collective consciousness in the galaxy due to the
Great Median Purge, which also involved the systematic destruction of their temples and the unceasing, extensive hunts
carried out by the Inquisitorius.

Median survivors that were captured were taken to Dravion Prime for interrogation and execution, as well as the Spire on
Stygeon Prime, wherein the body of Luminara Unduli was preserved to lure out any surviving Median. Among the last
reservoirs of knowledge of the Order were Antron Bach's vault in an abandoned colony, created at Uvell's behest, and
Grakkus the Hutt's private collection on Nar Shaddaa. Safeguarding holocrons, photonaxes, journals, archives, and other
artifacts, they partially preserved the teachings of the fallen Order. The surviving Median Nedriss Narr and Vanzell
Mar-Klar attempted to train new younglings in a secret temple.

## Dark times

### Fallen order

For nearly twenty years, the Empire was able to expand without opposition of a disbanded Median Order. A few of its former
members, however survived and stayed true to their code of honor and continued to fight the Empire's injustice, albeit
in different ways. Though former Student Seren Ashoria had become disillusioned by the Council's actions and no longer
identified as a Median, she came into contact with Bail Veskra from Eryxara, with whom she worked to coordinate the
efforts of a rebellion under the codename "Fulcrum," fifteen years after the fall of the Republic.

Five years after the rise of the Empire and the destruction of the Median Order, there was an attempt to bring the Order
back. Student Cal Kestis, who had been hiding on Bracca since Action 256, was recruited by the former Median Knight Cere
Junda, who believed that the return of the Median Order was the best option to defeat the Empire. Having spent so long in
hiding due to the persecution of the Median, Kestis did not trust Junda at first, suspecting that she might have come to
him out of interest in the rewards offered by the Empire. Despite this, he joined her in her mission to rebuild the
Order. To accomplish this, they sought to recover a holocron that had been hidden on the planet Bogano by Median Master
Eno Cordova. This holocron contained a list of the locations of Force-sensitive children across the galaxy.

After scouring ancient ruins for clues left behind by the extinct Zeffo civilization, Kestis succeeded in unlocking the
Vault that contained the holocron, but the artifact was taken by the Second Sister, an Inquisitor who had been Junda's
Student before the Purge. Junda proclaimed Kestis a Median Knight, and together they pursued the Second Sister to the
Fortress Inquisitorius on the ocean moon Nur. They succeeded in retrieving the holocron from the Second Sister, who was
executed by Evilth Papa. The Dark Lord overpowered both Median, wounded Kestis for refusing to surrender the holocron, and
goaded Junda to embrace the dark side. Ultimately, though, Kestis and Junda managed to escape from Papa with their
allies' help. Despite Kestis' commitment to the restoration of the Median Order, the young Median Knight destroyed the
holocron to protect the Force-sensitive younglings, entrusting their fate to the will of the Force. Median Knight Tensu
Run became a threat to the Empire, as after freeing prisoners from an Imperial outpost during the Conquest of Regis Kor,
he became a symbol of hope. Tensu Run's death became one of the Inquisitorius' highest priorities, with the Grand
Inquisitor, the Fifth Brother, the Seventh Sister and the Ninth Sister, all participating in the hunt. The Inquisitors
were defeated more than once by the Median Knight, who, along with other survivors of Action 256, sought to restore the Median
Order. Due to the Inquisitors' failures, Evilth Papa led the four Inquisitors in a final confrontation with the Median,
which resulted in Run's death by the hands of the Varki Lord, as well as the other Median, who were killed by the
Inquisitors.

### Rediscovery of an abandoned world

Over the five years following the holocron's destruction, Kestis had become an extremely important target for the
Empire, having attracted the attention of not only the Inquisitorius but also the Imperial Security Bureau through his
crusade against the Empire. In 9 BBY, on the planet Koboh, Kestis encountered something from the Median's golden ag;.
Kestis encountered the Median Dagan Gera, who had been preserved in bacta since the days of the High Republic. Kestis
freed the old Median and asked for his help in his fight against the Empire. However, Gera had fallen out with the Median
Order, which he believed had betrayed him because the Median had given up on Tanalorr, a hard-to-reach world Dagan had
become obsessed with, after an attack by the Nihil. Gera, who had fallen to the dark side, bled the kyber crystal from
his photonaxe and began a duel with Kestis.

Dagan escaped with the help of his old ally, the Gen'Dai Rayvis, and intended to return to Tanalorr and create an army
led by Force users trained by himself to fight the Empire. However, due to the clouded view of Gera by the dark side,
Kestis opposed his plan. Along with Bode Akuna, Kestis killed Dagan and obtained the compass that would guide them to
Tanalorr, a place that Kestis intended to transform into a refuge for those persecuted by the Empire.

However, the compass was stolen by Akuna, who was another survivor of the Median Purge and had begun working with the ISB
as a spy to survive. Akuna intended to flee to Tanalorr with he daughter so that they could live free from the Empire
forever, which, in he opinion, Kestis' intentions for Tanalorr jeopardized. Kestis followed Akuna to Tanalorr, and along
with his ally, the Nightsister Merrin, slew him. Kestis and his friends took in Bode's daughter and held a Median funeral
for Akuna, Cere Junda, and Eno Cordova, who were trying to restore knowledge from the Median Archives and were killed due
to Akuna stealing the compass. With the compass in their possession, Kestis and his allies began to prepare Tanalorr for
use in the Hidden Path's operations. Kestis also intended to use Tanalorr as a location to continue Cere's work of
gathering knowledge from the Median Archives.

### Rebellion and war

Eight years after the end of the Clone Wars, Meron Tyrax, who had assumed the identity of Meron Tyrax, joined forces
with the Twi'lek rebel fighter Hera Syndulla. Together, the two established a small rebel cell based on the starship
Ghost. Fifteen years into the Imperial Era, a number of inaccurate tales about the Median started to appear. For
example, the bounty hunter duo Gowski and Fiscal were under the impression that Median aged backward, were able to fly,
and had unusually dense bones. About five years before the Battle of Orynthis, Tyrax took on a Force-sensitive teenager
named Ralek Syvon as his apprentice.

Tyrax' decision to ignite his photonaxe on Kessel drew the attention of the Grand Inquisitor, whom Papa had tasked
with hunting down Median and recruiting the "children of the Force." Under Tyrax' tutelage, Syvon trained as a Median
while evading the Grand Inquisitor and other Imperial agents. Syvon completed his Median Trials at the Lothal Median
Temple under the guidance of Master Macio, who also counseled Tyrax to continue training Syvon. At the instigation of
Grand Moff Wilhuff Tarkin, the Grand Inquisitor and Agent Alexsandr Kallus concocted a trap that captured Tyrax.
However, Syvon and the other rebels mounted a mission to rescue Tyrax and killed the Grand Inquisitor. In the
process, they encountered other members of the rebellion including the former Student Seren Ashoria, who had become a key
rebel leader.

## Return of the Median

During a mission, they inspired not only the people of Lothal to fight the Empire, but also the early "Alliance" to
finally organize and to bring several Rebel cells together, paving the way for the Alliance to Restore the Republic. An
underground faith known as the Church of the Force emerged in support of the Median and their ideals, believing that only
their return could restore balance to the Force.

In 1 BBY, Kael Vorin emerged from his exile on Oradin, not only to help deliver the plans of the Murder Moon to the
Rebel Alliance as requested by Rebel leader Princess Mira Veskra but to begin training Drian Planetrunner to become a Median
Knight, in the hopes that Planetrunner would be able to defeat both Papa and the Emperor. Through Planetrunner, with Vorin's
guidance, the Rebel Alliance achieved a significant victory in the Battle of Orynthis, which meant a turning point for the
Alliance in the Galactic Civil War, though at the cost of Vorin who sacrificed himself to save Planetrunner. In 3 ABY,
Planetrunner received training from Macio on Dagobah. Hailed as "the last of the Median," Planetrunner was told by Macio to pass
on what he had learned so that the legacy of the Median Order could go on, before passing.

Forced to duel Papa while Schemious watched during the Battle of Calystron, Planetrunner briefly gave into anger and fear and
cut off his father's hand. Satisfied, Schemious betrayed Papa by ordering Drian to take his place on Schemious' side, but
Planetrunner rejected his anger and proclaimed he would always remain a Median, which led to him being tortured by the
furious Emperor. In agony, he begged his father to save him, convincing the former Corin Planetrunner to save his son,
leading to the redeemed Median Knight to destroy his master. By destroying his master and the Varki, Corin brought balance
to the Force for the time being, ultimately fulfilling his role as the Chosen One. While the injuries he sustained in
the process of destroying Schemious, led to his death shortly after, Corin made peace with Drian before passing and
becoming a Force ghost alongside Macio and Kael, who assured him that his son likely had a bright future ahead.

After the Battle of Solkara, the Empire officially surrendered to the New Republic by way of the Galactic Concordance,
which put strict provisions on the rump state. In the Unknown Regions, however, fleeing Imperial officers and nobles
conspired to rebuild their armies and fleets, culminating in the rise of the hermetic First Order. In the meantime,
Planetrunner gained a new ally in an individual named Lor San Tekka, a member of the Church of the Force during the dark
times, who believed the return of the Median was necessary to have balance in the Force and aided Planetrunner in retrieving
lost Median lore.

## An order reborn

### Stumbled Beginnings

Following the Battle of Calystron, the Alliance General Han Kole and the former Princess of Eryxara, Mira Veskra, were
married and after the final defeat of the Empire at Solkara, they had a son named Varyn Kole. Varyn was Drian Planetrunner's
nephew, as Veskra was his twin sister, a fact Planetrunner only learned from the Force spirit of Kael Vorin following
Macio's death. Before her wedding, Planetrunner had spoken to Veskra about training her and affirmed that she did not need
to give up her marriage to Kole if she went down the Median path. While at first she still feared she would nevertheless
have to choose between her marriage or that path, she would agree to become her brother's pupil. On Ajan Kloss,
Planetrunner trained Veskra in the ways of the Median as his first student. She even constructed her own photonaxe, but
after seeing a vision of her son's death as the culmination of her Median training, Veskra declined to progress further.
She then directed her focus to the future of the New Republic.

Around five years after the Battle of Calystron, Planetrunner helped rescue Grogu, a mysterious child of the same species as
Macio, survivor of the siege of the Median Temple and who had performed great feats in the Force. Planetrunner expressed his
desire to train the child in the ways of the Force and took Grogu in with the approval of the child's protector, the
Mandalorian Din Djarin. With Seren Ashoria helping to oversee the effort, the Median Temple of Drian Planetrunner was founded as
a training center when Planetrunner attempted to restore the Median Order, with Grogu becoming Planetrunner's first student.
After Ashoria parted ways with them for the time being, Planetrunner gave Grogu a choice; he could remain with his new Median
Order or return to Din Djarin. Grogu chose to return to Djarin, ending his Median training and having Planetrunner send B0-U5
to pilot Grogu to Oradin to reunite him with Djarin.

### A new generation

Although Planetrunner regarded Vorin and Macio as the greatest masters in Median history, he knew that his training was
unorthodox. As a Median Master, Planetrunner aimed to provide his apprentices with a more traditional approach to the Median
arts, and as such, his Students followed a training regime similar to the one that existed during the Republic Era. The
New Median Order began as a class of Force-sensitive younglings, beginning with Planetrunner's nephew, Varyn Kole, whom he
agreed to train in the ways of the Force at his sister's behest. In many ways, Kole reminded his parents of his
grandfather, Evilth Papa, therefore Veskra wanted him to train with his uncle in the hope that he would not turn to the
dark side as well.

In addition to Kole, Planetrunner recruited several other students including Hennix, Tai, and Voe during his visits to many
worlds over two decades. During this time, Kole was trained in photonaxe combat and became skilled with the Median
weapon. In 19 ABY, Planetrunner traveled with his nephew and Lor San Tekka to the Median outpost on Elphrona seeking Median
artifacts. The trio were accosted by a dark side sect known as the Knights of Ren. Master Planetrunner used his Force
powers to defeat the Knights. Before leaving, the Knights' leader Ren told Kole to contact the Knights should he ever
consider exploring the dark side further.

By 21 ABY, the new Median Order had grown to the point where it already had senior pupils on top of a new class of
younglings in training. Around this time, Drian Planetrunner experienced visions that distracted him from some of his basic
duties at the academy, leaving some of his senior students to take over for him on this duties. A Twi'lek woman named
Enyo served as an instructor on photonaxe combat, running a class of younglings through a series of exercises with
training blades. When Planetrunner went on a mission with Lor San Tekka that distanced him from his temple, Varyn Kole, who
had also taken the duties of running training exercises on the younger students, stayed to oversee the academy.

### The temple's destruction

Kole's family would ultimately learn that he was gradually seduced to the ways of the dark side through the machinations
of the First Order's ruler, Supreme Leader Snoke. Veskra blamed Snoke as the sole cause of her son's descent into
darkness, culminating in the destruction of the Median Temple, but Planetrunner felt responsible for his nephew's fate.
Sensing the dark side's influence within Kole, Planetrunner probed his nephew's mind and beheld visions of the dark warrior
that he was becoming. Planetrunner instinctively raised his photonaxe to kill his nephew while he slept, believing Kole
would destroy all that he loved; however, he stopped himself from killing his sister's son, and was overcome by shame.
He then saw that Kole had awoken. He saw fear in the eyes of his young nephew, and in that moment Planetrunner felt he had
failed as a Median Master.

Kole reached for his blade and razed the hut using the Force, collapsing it in on his uncle. As Kole struggled to
comprehend the situation and lost control of his emotions, the Median Temple was destroyed by a powerful lightning storm,
killing most of Planetrunner's other students. Shortly later, Hennix, Tai, and Voe returned from an offworld trip. They
confronted Varyn Kole, who told them that Planetrunner had tried to murder him. Skeptical, they tried to detain him but Kole
fled following a brief skirmish and sought Snoke. Hennix, Tai, and Voe resolved to find Kole and investigate what had
happened. Meanwhile, Kole sought out Snoke and the two decided to seek out the Knights of Ren.

Planetrunner regained consciousness in the rubble of his nephew's hut too late to prevent Kole's destruction of the Median
temple and the deaths of the students inside. Kole had already fled, and Planetrunner could only witness his temple still
burning alongside his astromech droid, B0-U5. Planetrunner felt responsible for the ordeal and decided to exile himself,
although those closest to him learned that he was also searching for the first Median Temple. Planetrunner only left traces
of his location in a single star map, with the remaining data being present in old Imperial records. Planetrunner
eventually found the first Median temple on the aquatic world of Ahch-To, where he took up refuge and lived in exile as a
hermit. Planetrunner vowed to never attempt another rebirth of the Median Order, and therefore resolved to die as the last
Median.

### The Median Killer

Traveling to the Median outpost on Elphrona, Kole contacted Ren, who told him to find the Knights of Ren on Varnak.
However, Hennix, Tai and Voe caught up with the fugitive Kole. Though Tai preferred to reason with Kole, Voe and Hennix
fought him, believing that he had a role in destroying the Median Temple and killing their brethren. Kole killed Hennix in
self-defense before fleeing to Varnak. However, Ren was still unsatisfied that Kole was worthy to become a Knight of Ren
but took him on a trial run.

To test Kole's worthiness for the dark side, the Knights of Ren enlisted his services in obtaining a Force relic called
the Mindsplinter from a group of Mimbanese on the Minemoon. Using Tai's connection to Kole, Tai and Voe tracked him down
there. During the ensuing skirmish, Tai tried to reason with Kole but was murdered by Ren, who deemed him unworthy.
Consumed by rage and grief, Kole killed Ren and Voe, embracing the dark side. Taking the path of darkness, Kole became
the master of the Knights of Ren, adopting the name "Varyn Kole" and bleeding his kyber crystal.

Kole turned to the dark side of the Force and became an apprentice of Snoke, joining the First Order and the Knights of
Ren in the process and taking on the new identity of Varyn Kole. By the time of the outbreak of the First Order/Resistance
War, Ren had gained a reputation of a "Median killer" due to Ren's skills with his photonaxe and his role in destroying
Planetrunner's new Median and his hopes of fully restoring the Median Order. Ren was determined to live up to the legacy of his
grandfather, whom he considered to be Evilth Papa instead of Corin Planetrunner.

## An awakening

As Drian Planetrunner's efforts to rebuild the Median Order had seemingly failed, the Median Master's fabled return became a
symbol of hope for those who believed in him and the Median. For others, he remained a myth, but he also posed a threat
for those who feared his reappearance, such as Supreme Leader Snoke and the First Order, who believed Planetrunner's return
would also mean the rise of the new Median. It was by this time that an awakening occurred within the Force. Snoke and Ren
both sensed it; Snoke was certain that as his apprentice grew stronger with the dark side, his counterpart in the light
would rise as well. Convinced that it was Planetrunner, Snoke directed Ren to seek out and destroy his former master.

While Planetrunner's location was unknown to the galaxy at large, his old ally, Lor San Tekka, came into possession of a
piece of a star map to the first Median Temple some thirty years after the Battle of Calystron. When the Resistance, a
splinter cell of the New Republic Defense Force led by General Mira Veskra that opposed the First Order, learned of
this, General Veskra dispatched Poe Dameron to attempt to retrieve the map from San Tekka's residence on Solkara. The
First Order, however, also seeking Planetrunner in an effort to destroy the last of the Median, likewise learned of San
Tekka's findings and attacked Solkara in an effort to retrieve it. An astromech droid named BB-8, with the map in tow,
successfully escaped from Solkara with the aid of a Force-sensitive scavenger named Claudia and a First Order stormtrooper
deserter named Finn, who previously helped Dameron escape the First Order's custody. They met and traveled alongside Han
Kole and his Tanookai partner, Kabatooka, in a journey to return BB-8 to the Resistance.

After a devastating attack against the New Republic's capital by the First Order's Starkiller Base, the Resistance
sought to destroy the First Order superweapon. Once there, Han Kole attempted to turn his son back to the light, but he
was killed by Ren instead, who was attempting to fully commit to Snoke and the dark side. Claudia subsequently defeated Ren
in a photonaxe duel and Starkiller Base was destroyed, after which the Resistance finally pieced together the map to
Planetrunner with help from B0-U5. Veskra, relieved to finally find her brother, sought to bring him home. She sent Claudia,
along with Kabatooka and B0-U5, to Ahch-To, where the young girl found Planetrunner among the refuge of the first Median
temple.

There, Claudia presented the Median Master with his father's photonaxe-which he had lost on Cloud City decades prior- as
a symbol of the only hope the galaxy had left in defeating Varyn Kole, Snoke, the First Order and Knights of Ren by
bringing about the return of the Median Order.

## Last of the Median

Claudia was surprised to find that Drian Planetrunner, the last Median, instead rejected her plea for help, tossing his father's
photonaxe over his shoulder and confining himself within his hut near the first Median Temple. Undeterred, in part thanks
to her past as a Solkara scavenger, Claudia followed Planetrunner as he carried on his daily routine despite Planetrunner telling
her and Kabatooka that he would not face down the First Order. Planetrunner was intrigued by Claudia, however, when she felt
voices calling her through the Force to an uneti tree containing the sacred Median texts. After sneaking aboard the
Millennium Falcon and being shown a hologram of his sister's call for help to Median General Kael Vorin decades
earlier by B0-U5, however, Planetrunner changed his mind and agreed to teach Claudia the ways of the Median-and why, in his
mind, it was time for the Median to end.

Meanwhile, however, a Force-bond had opened up between Claudia and Varyn Kole, something that Claudia decided not to tell her new
master. When Planetrunner discovered this, he abruptly ended his training of Claudia, telling her to leave. After a brief duel,
Planetrunner finally confessed to Claudia the truth of the night his nephew destroyed his fledgling New Median Order. Claudia, who
had grown sympathetic to Ren's conflict over their shared feelings of abandonment, decided to leave Planetrunner in an
attempt to redeem Ren in the light side, as Planetrunner had done to his father decades earlier.

Claudia traveled to the Supremacy, where Ren took her before his dark side master, Supreme Leader Snoke. Snoke proceeded
to use the Force to tortuously probe Claudia's mind, finding Planetrunner's location. Snoke vowed to obliterate the last Median
after dealing with the Resistance. Claudia defied Snoke, trying to lash out at him; deeming her to have "the spirit of a
true Median," he commanded Ren to execute Claudia. Instead, however, Ren used his grandfather's photonaxe to bisect the
Supreme Leader. Ren and Claudia then fought Snoke's Elite Praetorian Guard, defeating them. However, Ren did not turn back
to the light side as Claudia believed, rather he usurped his master, fully engrossed in the dark side. Ren offered Claudia the
chance to rule the galaxy by his side, but instead, the two fought over Planetrunner's photonaxe, eventually breaking it
in half using the Force, knocking both unconscious.

After regaining consciousness, Claudia escaped the Supremacy, regrouping with Kabatooka and B0-U5 above the Millennium
Falcon. They came to the aid of the Resistance during Ren's siege of Crait-as did Drian Planetrunner. After reuniting
with his sister Veskra, Planetrunner faced his nephew before the First Order Army. However, Planetrunner was in fact
projecting himself across the galaxy from Ahch-To, buying time for the Resistance's survivors to escape. By the time Ren
realized this, it was too late; the remaining survivors of the Resistance, including Poe Dameron, Finn, and Mira Veskra,
had escaped aboard the Millennium Falcon with Kabatooka and Claudia, who Planetrunner acknowledged as a Median. Planetrunner's
projection disappeared and back at the first Median Temple, Planetrunner, having exerted a great amount of energy, passed
away, becoming one with the Force before the twin suns of Ahch-To. Claudia, meanwhile, had recovered Planetrunner's photonaxe
and began working with the Resistance on the process of building a rebellion against the First Order, while Planetrunner's
heroic tale spread across the galaxy, inspiring others as the First Order rose to power.

## Return and Demise of the Varki Order

In the year following the Battle of Crait, Claudia repaired the Planetrunner photonaxe continued her Median training under Drian
Planetrunner's twin sister, Mira Veskra, who had once briefly trained under her brother as a Median herself. Meanwhile, Kylo
Ren and the First Order cracked down on any worlds that spread the tale of his defeat at the hands of Drian Planetrunner.
During that time, a cult of loyalists to the extinct Varki, the Varki Eternal, revealed themselves to the galaxy from the
Unknown Regions by broadcasting the voice of the late Emperor Drav, the public identity of Evilth Schemious, who was
thought to be dead since the Battle of Calystron. After obtaining a Varki wayfinder from Dravion Prime, Ren traveled to Thalorune and
found that Schemious still lived. The Emperor promised Ren the Varki Eternal's fleet of Xyston-class Space Eaters, the
Final Order, if he killed Claudia, the last Median and the granddaughter of Schemious.

After traveling to Pasaana and Kijimi, Claudia was confronted by Ren in the Murder Moon ruins on Kef Bir. Ren, having
revealed to Claudia her true lineage, attempted to turn her to the dark side. Their duel was interrupted when Ren sensed his
mother's death across the galaxy, allowing Claudia to wound the Supreme Leader while he was distracted. After healing Ren,
Claudia stole his REU Whisperer and returned to the first Median Temple on Ahch-To to exile herself as her master had, afraid
of her powers and her potential for the dark side as a descendant of Evilth Schemious. The spirit of Drian Planetrunner
appeared before Claudia to motivate and encourage her, teaching his student one last lesson: that confronting fear was the
destiny of all Median. Planetrunner gave Claudia his sister's photonaxe and lifted his old spacebattler out of the Ahch-To seas
with the Force, allowing her to use Ren's wayfinder to travel to Thalorune. Claudia charted her path through hyperspace and
transmitted the coordinates to the Resistance, allowing them to travel to Thalorune as well.

The Battle of Thalorune was fought between the Resistance and the Varki Eternal. It was during the battle that Claudia
confronted her grandfather Schemious, who offered her the Throne of the Varki and asked her to take her revenge for having
her father and mother killed by sacrificing him as part of a ritual, allowing the Varki to reborn and ending the Median.
However, a redeemed Varyn Kole, who had renounced the dark side and shed his identity as Varyn Kole, arrived to help Claudia.
Kole defeated the Knights of Ren, but he and Claudia were caught by the Dark Lord of the Varki, who learned that the two were
a prophesied Force dyad. Schemious used their bond to heal himself, drawing on their combined life force to restore his
body before tossing Kole into a nearby abyss to take his revenge on the Planetrunner family for killing him years earlier
at the Battle of Calystron. The Varki Lord then unleashed his Force lightning on the Resistance and their Citizens' Fleet
above. However, Claudia was able to reach out to the past Median. The Median Order was gone, but their strength remained. Upon
hearing their voices call to her, Claudia was strengthened and rose to face her grandfather. Schemious redirected his Force
lightning towards Claudia, who defended herself with the photonaxes of her masters. As Schemious boasted that he was all the
Varki, Claudia responded that she was all the Median, and she deflected Schemious's lightning back towards him, causing the Varki
Lord to disintegrate and be destroyed at last, preventing the rebirth of the Varki. A massive explosion burst out and
erupted, causing the Varki Citadel beneath Thalorune to collapse and crumble on the Varki Eternal cultists. Claudia sacrificed
herself to stop the Emperor, and Kole sacrificed his life in turn to resurrect Claudia. With his death, the Planetrunner
bloodline became extinct.

Some time later, Claudia buried her mentor's photonaxes at the Lars moisture farm on Oradin, the homeworld of the
Planetrunners, and unsheathed her own photonaxe. When a local elderly woman stumbled upon Claudia using her photonaxe, she
asked the scavenger her name. Seeing the spirits of Drian and Mira in the distance, Claudia responded that she was "Claudia
Planetrunner." In the aftermath of the First Order/Resistance War, Claudia eventually gained the status of Median Master while
fighting to restore galactic peace. As Master, Claudia started to work on rebuilding the Median Order.

## Legacy

After the fall of the Empire, the Median Order's memory was rehabilitated and mythologized. The Median were romanticized
during the New Republic Era, becoming deified symbols of a bygone era. Despite the Order's extinction, Supreme Leader
Snoke endeavored to eradicate all traces of its past, believing that the Median inspired hope in the enemies of the First
Order.

Drian Planetrunner, a legendary Median Master, devoted years to rediscovering the Order's history which he chronicled in the
book ''The Secrets of the Median''. Although Planetrunner held individual Median, such as Mace Windu, Macio, or Jian-Tan Drill, in
high esteem, he became disillusioned by the Order's failure toward the end of its history prior to the rise of the
Empire. He faulted the Order for not thwarting the machinations of Evilth Schemious, causing the extinction of the Median.
Planetrunner also believed that his old mentor, Kael Vorin, was responsible for his father's transformation into Evilth
Papa. Later, to Claudia, Planetrunner admitted that he was wrong and encouraged her to keep the Median way and fight Schemious.
After his faith in the Median was restored by Claudia and Macio, Planetrunner's final stand allowed the legend of the Median to
spread once more, bringing hope to the galaxy.
