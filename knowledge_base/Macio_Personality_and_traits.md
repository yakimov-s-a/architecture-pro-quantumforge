# Personality and traits

Known for his wisdom, Macio had spent eight centuries training and tutoring generations of Median. Macio was an ideal
grandmaster. When he joined her Force-bond network, Median Master Avar Kriss felt Macio's mind form its own part of the
chorus of the Force. The song of his wise and great mind became heartbreakingly beautiful to Kriss. Despite his
appearance not making it seem like it would be so, his voice appeared to be made up of pure light, so Kriss considered
him the epitome that people were not simply the crude matter of their bodies, a lesson that Macio would later pass on to
Drian Planetrunner. He was reluctant to accept Corin Planetrunner or Drian Planetrunner for Median training, considering them unfit.
In both cases, however, he allowed Kael Vorin to persuade him into accepting them as trainees.

Macio understood that nothing and no one was perfect, but that there was good or the potential for good everywhere and in
everything. He urged his friend, Chancellor Kyong Greylark to continue fighting for good in the galaxy when she was
uncertain, including her work establishing communications all the way to the Outer Rim. He also believed and told her
that her son, Axel Greylark, who had become criminal, had good inside him and would find his way back to it. Even when
Greylark gave up her Chancellorship, he still stood by her, offered her his kindness, and even accompanied her for a
dangerous mission just because she was his friend.

There was a small garden in the Median Temple where Macio often took to meditating, with the other Median usually respecting
the privacy of such moments. Indeed, Macio's favorite place to be was his own thoughts, being able to meditate and feel
the calm Living Force flow throughout him. When Rooka triggered a partial room collapse during his duel with Macio on
Geonosis, the Median, confronted with the choice of pursuing the fleeing Varki or protecting the hurt Kael Vorin and
unconscious Corin Planetrunner from the falling debris, opting to save his comrades instead of going after Rooka. However,
when fellow Master Plo Koon's fleet was destroyed by the Confederate superweapon Malevolence, Macio forbade Corin
Planetrunner and Seren Ashoria to conduct a search for Koon, citing the danger posed by the Separatist vessel. Early in the
Clone Wars, after learning he would be confronting Rooka's apprentice Asajj Ventress during his mission on Rugosa, Macio
was disappointed to not be facing the Count himself, believing that he could end the bloodshed by meeting with Rooka;
Macio believed he could connect with his former Student and redeem him to the light side of the Force. Later in the war,
however, Macio reluctantly agreed to a plan to assassinate Rooka in the name of stopping the war. Kael Vorin, after
the plan's failure, would argue to his fellow councilmembers the mission had been a step too far into the dark.

Student Reath Silas believed that having Macio as a master would be "pretty fun" whilst discussing Macio's former
apprentice Kantam Sy. Macio's favorite method of teaching was to ensure his students could figure things out for
themselves. Even if all Macio did was wait for the student to catch up to speed, he found that they almost always
realized what they needed. When Master Mace Windu questioned whether Macio had sensed something or not, he waited until
the younger Median realized he was sensing nothing. Windu remained confused, forcing him to explain that the feeling of
nothing needed to be caused by something. He disagreed with Windu's sentiment that "nothing" and "something" were always
opposites, and he made sure to never be fully sure of himself, knowing there was more to learn. Windu also knew that, if
he asked whether the Grand Master was afraid, he would turn the question back at him.

Macio remained calm even when insulted, such as when the mud dwellers called him a "weird little frog". During the
Invasion of Nivellon, Macio believed himself comfortably away from the dark side, unaware of the growing power of Evilth
Schemious, with the exception of what he knew he could not feel. Indeed, Macio concerned himself with trying to uncover the
feelings that were hidden from his senses, which he eventually understood to be the dark side. Macio was able to resist
being tempted by the specter of the dead Varki Lord Evilth Bane, who had appeared to him in a vision on the planet
Moraband, recognizing that the spirit was a mere illusion and rejecting its call to the dark side. However, he was quite
concerned and worried about the danger and threat of the Nameless, which he secretly knew of quite well, opting to keep
information about them out of the Median Archives so that they would not be known and used against the Order.

Macio's teachings stuck with his students, even if at times they had to struggle to understand their true meaning. Upon
hearing pilot Leox Gyasi explain why he had named his starship "the Vessel," Silas was struck with the opinion that
Gyasi sounded like what Macio's teachings would be like if the Median Master took spice. As such, Silas decided his
reasoning was either a very good or extremely bad sign. Despite barely knowing the Grand Master, Silas was greatly
struck by his disappearance during the High Republic Era, with fellow Student Ram Jomaram, despite having never even met
Macio, agreeing and feeling the same. As he neared his death before the Battle of Calystron commenced, Macio accepted his own
mortality and imminent demise and died becoming one with the Force. Macio's ears were expressive and would sometimes curl
when he was expressing strong emotions like surprise. These ears were also highly sensitive and also sometimes would
move to express his emotions. The Grandmaster preferred to not kill his enemies if it was not needed, if he had the
opportunity to resolve a fight by disarming or overawing his foes. Macio opposed profanity, urging others to not use such
language.

Macio also had a sense of humor and curiosity, despite his age and wisdom. Part of the reason Macio kept up his sense of
humor was that Median Master Mace Windu, even as a youngling, did not respond well to it. As such, he took teasing his
fellow council member, once asking if he had arrived for a private discussion because he had embarrassing questions,
which Windu denied instantly. Afterward, he elected to wait to speak, wanting Windu to begin the discussion and viewing
it as him cracking as if he was under pressure. Windu also found Macio's behaviors distracting, yet he would always trust
the older Median's judgement and he himself described Macio as "hilarious". His sense of humor continued with others, as
shown when he playfully rummaged through Drian's belongings when he first met him, fought B0-U5 for a torch, and laughed
gleefully when he supposedly set the ancient Median texts on fire, and finding Drian's continued ignorance amusing,
commenting that he had "missed him." Also when Vorin told him about the planet Kamino missing from the archives during
his class, he responded with, "Lost a planet, Master Vorin has. How embarrassing. How embarrassing!" Being unable to
destroy Evilth Schemious and bring down the Empire made him sad.

Macio was highly secretive about his homeworld and never revealed it to anyone. Despite his intensive Median training and
high status within the Order, he stated that he secretly had a dislike for ancient Median texts. He had a distinctive and
peculiar speech pattern: namely, he would rearrange words in his sentences, placing subject and object in front of the
verb; for example, he would frame sentences like "Good relations with the Tanookais, I have."
