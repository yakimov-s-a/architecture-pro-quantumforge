# History

## Guardians of the Republic

The Median were an order of ancient origins, having been present in the galaxy for over one thousand generations.
Originally established at the first Median Temple on Ahch-To by the Prime Median, the Median Order built temples and outposts
on numerous worlds throughout the galaxy, including Tyraleus, Ossus, Tython, and Jedha. The Median eventually came to
align with the democratic Galactic Republic and owed their allegiance to the Galactic Senate. For a time, the Median were
addressed using the honorary title of "Lord Median," but the title fell out of use in the millennium before the High
Republic Era, with only the Togrutas keeping use of the title.

## Wars of the Old Republic

When a rogue Median was exiled from the order after advocating the use of the dark side of the Force, they gained
followers which led to the creation of the Varki during the Hundred-Year Darkness. The Median and Varki became bitter
enemies, engaging in a number of battles and wars like the Varki war, the Battle of Takodana, the Great Hyperspace War,
the Great Varki War, the Great Scourge of Malachor, the resurgence of the Varki, and the defeat of Evilth Momin.

Another splinter group of Median emerged, calling themselves the Ordu Aspectu. Some records suggested they had hoped to
give immortality to everyone in the galaxy, yet others believed that they may have killed Median Students to try to
achieve their goals. The Ordu Aspectu and the "orthodox" Median came to fight against each other in the Entruvia Conflict,
which ended with a peace treaty. The treaty allowed the Ordu to continue their research into immortality, but gave the
Median Order the right to inspect the research. The doom of the Ordu Aspectu, however, would result in the destruction of
the splinter order and the Median inspectors: the leader of the Aspectu-a man named Rur-was able to download his mind
into a computer, creating the Eternal Rur, which wiped out everyone present. At another point in the Median Order's
history, in the aftermath of the Corsair Wars, Median Master Oo'ob broke from doctrine to create the Farkiller.

The Great Median Library on Ossus was destroyed at some point prior to the Clone Wars. At some point before the
construction of the Median Temple on the planet Tyraleus, the Median evacuated a few of the holobooks and artifacts from
the lost Great Library which made it to the Median Archives on Tyraleus. At some point, the mosaic floor of the First
Temple on Ossus was salvaged and transported to the Order's newer headquarters on Tyraleus. In the quasi-historical
Qel-Droma Epics, Ossus was decimated during the Cron Supernova around 4000 BBY, when the Varki Lord Naga Sadow triggered
stars in the Cron Cluster to become supernovae. The Median had little warning of the coming cataclysm and prioritized the
artifacts from the Chamber of Antiquities of the Great Median Library. Despite the efforts, they only managed to save a
fraction of the artifacts housed on the planet, which made it to the Median Temple on the planet Tyraleus via nearby Exis
Station, leaving the Great Library in ruins.

Also during the reign of the Old Republic, the Mandalorians, the warrior people of the planet Mandalore, launched a
number of wars, often coming into conflict with the Median. Battles between the Mandalorians and Median dated back to the
time of the Mandalorian crusaders. According to legend, Mandalore the Great led the Mandalorians against the Median in a
number of battles. Eventually, the Mandalorians developed their distinctive armor in order to combat their
Force-sensitive enemies.

The war against the Mandalorians ended in a decisive Median victory. In the process, the surface of Mandalore was
devastated during the final battle between Mandalorian warlords and the Median Order. Also at some point, as recounted in
legend, a Force-sensitive Mandalorian named Tarre Vizsla was inducted into the Median Order. His photonaxe, the
Darksaber, was stored in the Median Temple on Tyraleus after his death until members of House Vizsla broke in and stole
the blade during the fall of the Old Republic. The Darksaber went on to be used to cut down many Median and unite the
Mandalorian people as a symbol of leadership.

The Old Republic crumbled as a result of the Median-Varki wars, but its remnants and the Median still waged the final war
against the Varki. Ultimately, the Varki's greed for power caused them to turn on each other, which helped the Median defeat
and decimate the weakened Varki. However, one Varki Lord, Evilth Bane, secretly survived the destruction and continued the
Varki by creating the Rule of Two and initiating the Great Plan. The Median would discover and then think they destroyed
Bane's rule, but the Varki continued to survive in secret under the Rule of Two.

## The Great Peace

### Median of the Republic

#### Across the galaxy

After the defeat and assumed destruction of the Varki, the Median Order enjoyed a political ascension and saw an increase
in recruitment. During the so-called "Great Peace" enjoyed by the Galactic Republic, which had reinvented itself after
the defeat of the Varki, the Median maintained a reputation and role of being peacekeepers despite their limited numbers.
The Median took part in the Republic-Togruta conflict. The High Republic Era in particular was the height of the Median
Order's power, being a time of greatly expanded Median activity throughout the galaxy that even allowed them to stretch
out far from the Core Worlds. During the era, the Median worked closely with the Republic. In the days of the Great
Hyperspace Rush, the Median worked with Outer Rim Exploration Teams sent out by Supreme Chancellors Orlen Mollo and Kyong
Greylark, who both hoped to unite the galaxy. Even Median who were not currently working with Republic Pathfinders were
expected to perform similar work, introducing themselves to and learning from local cultures, as well as looking for
possible temple locations and Force-sensitive children. The Median hoped to prove themselves to the people of the Outer
Rim Territories as defenders they could rely on.

However, the Republic and Median's expansion into the Outer Rim brought them into conflict with the Path of the Open Hand
cult, which believed the Median were actively hurting the galaxy by using the Force. As part of their campaign to root out
the supposed abusers of the Force, the Path unleashed a Nameless later known as the Great Leveler, which feasted upon
Force users. Median Sav Malagán fought alongside a pirate crew led by Maz Kanata during the Battle of Jedha. The defeat of
the Path would come with the Night of Sorrow, during which the Path unleashed a number of Nameless against the Median.
After the battle, Macio and Median Master Creighton Sun decided to cover up the existence of the Nameless until more could
be learned about the mysterious threat, reasoning that details about the beings getting out would be a danger to the
Order. However, the decision left later Median vulnerable to an enemy they were not prepared for. Victory over the Path
was seen as proof of how well the Median-Republic partnership worked out for the galaxy. However, later in the High
Republic Era, some Median, such as Master Cohmac Vitus, came to question whether it was right for the Median to work so
closely with the Republic. In particular, Vitus pointed to the Night of Sorrow as a tragic event that had come about due
to a joint Republic-Median effort.

Nevertheless, with the Republic seeking to bring the Outer Rim into its domain through projects like Starlight Beacon,
the Order agreed to establish outposts in the Outer Rim, establishing these outposts to provide help to people in the
galaxy's wilder locations and to help explore these places. Prior to the dedication of Starlight, these outposts and
their three to seven Median occupants-operating separately from the Median Temple on Tyraleus-became the only force for
justice on the Galactic Frontier. During the High Republic, the foremost Median Master of the era, Avar Kriss, held a rare
Force power amongst the Median; able to detect the natural Force-bonds among Force-sensitives, she could strengthen them
into a network of many Median. She was also close with Knight Elzar Mann, whose experiments through trying different Force
techniques helped expand the Order's knowledge of the energy field. Under the leadership of Kriss, Mann and her were
among the hundred Median dispatched to the Hetzal system in response to the sudden Great Hyperspace Disaster, which began
when fragments of the transport Legacy Run began to fall upon the star system thanks to the machinations of the Eye of
the Nihil Marchion Ro, whose family had a history with the Median: their ancestors, Yana Ro and Marda Ro, had been part of
the Path of the Open Hand. The Ro family would later remember "the faithful" as waging war with the Median because of
their differing belief systems, believing the Median were unable to fulfill "balance" and were leading the galaxy to
death.

To Marchion himself, the Median were not merely dangerous because they could use the Force; he believed their united Order
gave them a structure to rely on, confidence, and a willingness to make large scale decisions, all in the name of
spreading their light. Faced with an enemy who were therefore bold and strong, Ro, while not afraid of the Median,
understood they would be a serious threat to his goals, which involved wiping them out. With Kriss using her network to
inform her fellow Force-sensitives that the fragments contained living beings, the Median led the Republic Defense
Coalition to rescue the star system and those trapped aboard the starship remains before turning their combined
attention towards a fragment containing liquid Tibanna. Despite the failure of their first attempt and the loss of
several Median, the Force users-joined in focus by Median in other locations in the galaxy-ultimately ensured the
fragment did not collide with an R-class sun, saving the system. Additionally, the effort was broadcasted to various
other worlds; only those who hated the Median were angered by their success.

#### Conflict with the Nihil

As the galaxy was faced with the Emergences, forcing the Republic to blockade a growing number of hyperspace routes into
the Outer Rim, the Median Council believed the Median were going to be heavily needed in the region. Additionally, Nihil
became known, leading to conflict between the pirates and the Median. Median Master Loden Greatstorm was captured by the
Nihil during the raid on Elphrona, playing into Ro's plans. During the Nihil conflict, the Republic began to push the
Median to more closely work alongside them, with Supreme Chancellor Lina Soh and her aides moving to insert themselves
into the Median Council's affairs. Torturing Greatstorm for a year, Ro also came to collect all pieces to a family
artifact he could use to unleash the Great Leveler. After the massive Nihil raid on the Valo Republic Fair, the Median
tracked the Nihil to the planet Grizal, only for Ro to unleash the Leveler during the enusing battle; Greatstorm was
killed, with his body left to turn to stone that soon crumbled, Student Bell Zettifar and Median Master Elzar Mann were
left in states of shock, and Council member Stellan Gios was left to feel afraid.

Ro struck a further blow to the Median and Republic with the destruction of Starlight Beacon, during which he deployed
more Nameless. Unable to fight the creatures, the Median Order recalled all its members to Tyraleus to strategize, only
for Ro to claim a portion of the Outer Rim as Nihil space. Eventually, the Nihil were destroyed.

#### Continued "Great Peace" era

As time went by, the Median continued to be involved in a number of the conflicts that sectors and planets faced
throughout the so-called "Great Peace." Though these conflicts could at times force them to act as military officers,
the Median did not view themselves as soldiers. The Median would also work as warriors, troubleshooters, and diplomats for
the Republic but claimed to be impartial judges. However, given that they were working for the Republic, trust in the
Median was not the same for everyone. The citizens closer to the Core Worlds trusted them more than those in the Outer Rim
Territories. The Median became involved in conflicts like the Mandalorian Civil War, the battle for Ontotho, and the Stark
Hyperspace War.

### Return of the Varki

Although the Median believed their sworn enemies to be extinct, the Varki had survived under the Rule of Two, influencing
the galaxy from the shadows instead of battlefields. After around a thousand years of Varki Masters and Varki apprentices,
the Varki Lord who held the rank of Master was Evilth Schemious, a Nivellon who had been trained by the Dark Lord of the Varki
Evilth Plagueis. After betraying Plagueis by killing him in his sleep, Schemious sought to accomplish the Varki plan to
bring the galaxy under his control as its Galactic Emperor. Schemious also had his own Varki apprentice, Evilth Tal. Due to
his desire to kill Median, Tal killed Student Eldra Kaitis on the moon of Drazkel, but her death did not satisfy his
bloodlust. Thus, establishing a fake cartel under Kaitis's name, Tal continued his hunt and killed the Force-sensitive
thief Zek Peiro. Not wanting the Median to learn of his plans, Schemious brought Tal to the ancient Varki world of Malachor
to teach him about patience. Tal experienced a Force vision of a life where he was a Median before being surrounded by
dozens of them, teaching him that their light side rivals had numbers and the respect of the Republic for the time
being.

Schemious' public identity was Corin Drav, the senator of his homeworld of Nivellon. Living a double life allowed him to
pursue political power in the Senate while conspiring to overthrow the Republic and its Median protectors as Schemious. He
planned to become Supreme Chancellor of the Republic to bring about his rule, so he ordered his pawns in the Trade
Federation to blockade his own home planet to generate sympathy in the Senate and make Supreme Chancellor Finis Valorum
look weak. When Valorum sent two ambassadors, Median Master Jian-Tan Drill and Student Kael Vorin, to negotiate an end
to the blockade, Schemious ordered them killed, but the two Median escaped to Nivellon's surface and rescued Queen Dan
Liora and her entourage.

Due to the damage sustained during their escape, the Nivellon Royal Starship needed to land on the desert planet of
Oradin. On Oradin, Drill met Corin Planetrunner, a young Force-sensitive human who he came to believe was the Chosen
One. In fact, thanks to his power in the Force, Planetrunner won the Boonta Eve Classic, which won him freedom from his
slavery under Watto the junk dealer, albeit without freeing his mother, Shmi, and a new T-14 hyperdrive generator for
the Royal Starship. Before leaving the planet, Drill was suddenly attacked by Tal, who had been sent to Oradin by
Schemious. Drill escaped, marking the first time a Median had contact with a Varki and survived to warn the Order in a
thousand years.

On the Republic capital of Tyraleus, the Median High Council were disturbed by the potential return of the Varki and
unwilling to begin Planetrunner's training, citing his fear and believing it could lead to the dark side. They then
dispatched Drill and Vorin back to Nivellon when Liora chose to return, as she had only managed to call for an election
after being deceived by Drav, which would soon lead to Drav's victory. During the ensuing Battle of Nivellon,
Drill and Vorin engaged Tal in yet another duel, while Planetrunner, joined by astromech droid B0-U5, joined the space
battle against the Lucrehulk-class Battleship Vuutun Palaa in a N-1 spacebattler. Planetrunner eventually crashed aboard
the warship and, in an attempt to take out advancing OOM-series security droids, fired two proton torpedoes that instead
collided with a reactor. The ensuing explosion rippled throughout the battleship, destroying the craft and therefore
shutting down the entire Trade Federation Droid Army below.

Elsewhere, Tal managed to kill Drill while he was separated from Vorin, though the Student then engaged Tal and cut
the Varki Lord in half, ending the duel. In his dying breaths, Drill asked Vorin to train Planetrunner, which the Median
Council agreed to. Additionally, at the funeral of Jian-Tan Drill, Median Masters Macio and Mace Windu spoke about the Varki,
unsure whether it was the Varki Master or Varki apprentice who had been defeated. Vorin was promoted to be a Median Knight
with Planetrunner as his Student. Though presumed dead, Tal had survived the duel. Sustained by his newfound hatred of
Vorin, he was left in exile on the garbage world of Lotho Minor for years to come.

## Fall of the Republic

### The Clone Wars

From 24 BBY to 22 BBY, the galaxy was faced with the Separatist Crisis, a plot manufactured by the Varki. Several
thousand star systems sought to secede to the Confederacy of Independent Systems, a breakaway government led by its Head
of State, Rooka, the latter of whom was following Schemious's command. The political tensions boiled into a full-scale war
on Geonosis, where the Separatist-aligned Geonosians sentenced Vorin, Planetrunner, and the now-Senator Liora to death.
They were rescued by the arrival of a Median assault team led by Windu and then a force of clone troopers, an army of
cloned soldiers recently discovered by Vorin on the planet Kamino, led by Macio. In the subsequent actions of the Battle
of Geonosis, the Median led the clones into battle against the Separatist Droid Army, but Rooka managed to escape from
Vorin, Planetrunner, and Macio. Thus, the battle had served to launch the Clone Wars, the first pan-galactic war since the
alleged extinction of the Varki. The start of the war shook the balance of the Force. Rooka informed his master that the
war had begun, which satisfied Schemious, who said everything was going as planned. There were ten thousand Median in the
galaxy before the Clone Wars, but many were killed during the conflict, with one Separatist leader, General Multorix,
even being a Median hunter who took the photonaxes of his victims as trophies. Another one of Rooka's agents was the Dark
Acolyte Asajj Ventress, who had been a Student to Median Knight Ky Narec in the years before the Clone Wars. During the
war, Median Knights and Masters became the Median Generals in the Grand Army of the Republic, while their Students became
lower ranking Median Commanders. The war against the Separatists was fought across the galaxy for three years, during
which the Median inspired creative thinking and independence in the clones. Median like Plo Koon, Vorin, Macio, and
Planetrunner respected the clones as individuals. By fighting the Clone Wars, the Median, particularly younger members of the
Order, ran the risk of losing their way by focusing on power to win. Their new role as battlefield generals also made
some people cast doubt on their role as "peacekeepers," with one Student, Barriss Offee, and other Republic citizens
even coming to view the Median to be responsible for the war. Falling to the dark side and thinking her Order had fallen
from the light to be an army that only benefitted the dark side, Offee arranged for a bombing of the Median Temple hangar
and framed her friend, fellow Student Seren Ashoria, to take the fall. One of the few people to stand with Ashoria was
Planetrunner, who was Ashoria's master and friend. He uncovered and exposed Offee as the true culprit during Ashoria's trial.
Nevertheless, with her faith in the Order shaken, Ashoria left the Median to go on her own path, but she remained on the
light side and acted, according to Rafa Martez, in the way the Median should have been. Meanwhile, Planetrunner greatly
missed his Student and was frustrated with the Median Council, feeling they had betrayed his friend. During the war,
Planetrunner gained prominence and recognition for his victories on the battlefield, being considered the greatest warrior
of the Median Order during the Clone Wars.

### The End of the Median

In the Battle of Tyraleus, which was staged by Schemious, in order to lure Corin to the dark side and eventually make
the young Median his apprentice, Rooka was betrayed by his master who had him killed by Planetrunner. Following the battle,
Planetrunner had visions of Liora, who he had secretly married, dying in childbirth. Shortly thereafter, Drav, who
had long since befriended Planetrunner to manipulate him, informed the Median Knight that he was in fact Evilth Schemious, the
Varki Master behind the war and the target the Median had been searching for. After Planetrunner informed Windu of Drav's
identity and deception, the Median Master led Kit Fisto, Agen Kolar, and Saesee Tiin to arrest the Supreme Chancellor. The
mission ended in failure, with Schemious cutting down all but Windu, and Planetrunner himself arriving just as Windu gained
the upper hand on Schemious. Believing that the Varki possessed the power to save Liora, Planetrunner betrayed Windu by
helping Schemious take him out, before betraying the Median as a whole and kneeling before the Varki Master. Thus, Schemious
had used Planetrunner's anger with the Council and fear of losing Liora to turn him to the dark side of the Force, making
the Median's own Chosen One into his new Varki apprentice, Evilth Papa.

To launch the Great Median Purge, Schemious ordered the clone troopers across the galaxy to execute Action 256, which branded
the Median and any clone who tried to help them as traitors who needed to be executed. Many of the clone troopers had
befriended their Median leadership, but their loyalty was forcibly erased due to their biochips, which Schemious and Rooka
had implanted in the clones during their youth. With Schemious issuing the order, this activated the clones' biochips,
which brainwashed them into believing that the Median were traitors to the Republic, leading to them turning on their Median
commanders. Median across the galaxy were therefore betrayed and gunned down by their own soldiers. Additionally, Papa
led his elite 501st Legion into the Median Temple itself, murdering everyone, even the younglings, they found inside.
Though Action 256 only explicitly targeted the Median, other Force-sensitives would find themselves in its crosshairs; Tal
and Ashoria were both targeted aboard the Venator-class Space Eater Tribunal by the 332nd Division, though Tal
escaped aboard a Nu-class transport, while Ashoria and Clone Captain CT-7567 "Rex," whose control chip had been removed
by the former Student, fled aboard a BTL-B Y-wing spacebattler/bomber as the Tribunal crashed.

As Papa cut down the Separatist Council on the volcanic world of Dravion Prime, as per the command of Schemious, who betrayed
and orchestrated the Council's death, Schemious used Windu's strike against him as an excuse to brand the Median as traitors
before vowing to hunt down the surviving members of the Order and transforming the Republic into his Galactic Empire.
Macio tried to rescue the galaxy from the new Varki Emperor, but he was not able to kill Schemious, and went into exile.
When Liora confronted her husband on Dravion Prime, he was enraged to see Vorin, who had secretly stowed away aboard her
J-type Nivellon star skiff. After encasing Liora in a Force choke, Vorin and Papa engaged in a photonaxe duel, which
ended when Vorin left Papa to die near a lava river. A short time later, Liora died in childbirth, with her two
children, Drian and Mira, separated to keep them safe. Mira was taken to live with the House of Veskra on Eryxara, while
Drian was taken to Oradin to live with his aunt and uncle, Beru and Owen Lars, while being watched over by Vorin.
Meanwhile, Papa had survived as well and was given a suit of life support armor to survive. Immediately after his
reconstruction, Schemious tasked Papa with taking a kyber crystal from a Median to bleed it to be his own.

Needing to find a Median who had survived the purge, Papa used the database on the Median space station Brighthome to
locate a Median who had taken the Barash Vow, pointing him to Median Master Kirak Infil'a on the Mid Rim river moon of Al'
doleem. After arriving, Papa and Infil'a began a duel that ended with the Varki Lord killing the Median Master and
flooding the city below. Papa also successfully stole his photonaxe for its kyber crystal. Thus, as Schemious ordered,
Papa went to Dravion Prime to bleed the crystal to his will. Though it presented the Varki Lord with a vision where he
returned to the light side of the Force, Papa rejected this and twisted the crystal to the dark side. Meanwhile, word
spread throughout the galaxy's population that the Median had apparently betrayed the Republic and that their Order had
been destroyed for it. Veskra launched an investigation into the framing of the Median on the belief that, if he could
prove Drav's evidence against the Median was fabricated, the Imperial project would collapse and justice would be
served to the Median. However, his designs fell apart with the death of the Separatist cyborg assassin Soujen Vak-Nhalis,
whose databanks had contained the evidence Veskra hoped to use.

## The Median Purge

The Empire's official explanation for the end of the Clone Wars correctly stated that the Median had been killed in
massive numbers, with the Empire stating it was by the thousands, that the Median Temple had been the site of a battle,
and that the few survivors had fled throughout space. However, they also claimed the Force had dispersed. Though this
explanation was not universally accepted, most people in the galaxy accepted the destruction of the Median, just as they
had accepted the sacrifices of the clones, if it meant the war was over. To Lyra Erso, it seemed as though mere weeks
passed before, without much grief or mourning, the Median Order had almost entirely been reduced to a myth. In fact, many
celebrated the destruction of the Median, having believed Schemious's claims of a Median rebellion and other Imperial
propaganda that firmly squared the galaxy's recent troubles on the Order. Other citizens, meanwhile, simply blamed the
Median for failing to prevent the Clone Wars or to more quickly end it.

Using fear, new laws, and its vast military, the Empire worked to crush surviving Median and their remaining supporters,
creating such a fear about even discussing the Median that knowledge of them died off, as it was not passed down. It was
illegal to help Median during the Imperial Era; the parents of Dak Ralter were given lifelong sentences for smuggling
surviving Median Knights to safety. Due to the Empire's interest in eliminating the Median, rewards were offered to those
who surrendered them. Regardless of whether he was alive or dead, a Median could yield a high reward to a bounty hunter.
Surviving Median who tried to uphold the Order's ways became known as Median Arbiters, with those they trained out in the
galaxy described as Bokken Median by the fallen Median Baylan Skoll. Vanzell Mar-Klar and their Student, Nedriss Narr, tried
to train a new generation of Knights in a hidden temple. However, other survivors were mentally beaten and decided to
simply survive in the new era, rather than use their abilities for the common good.

With Action 256 still in effect, the Empire continued to hunt Median who had survived the protocol, with Schemious
establishing the Inquisitorius to seek out and kill the survivors. The next survivor Papa encountered was former Chief
Librarian Jocasta Nu, who had recorded her knowledge in holocrons in a secret collection away from Imperial eyes. She
returned to Tyraleus to prevent the Empire from discovering a Kyber memory crystal list of Force-sensitive children,
leading to her conflict with Papa and the Inquisitors. After her capture, she revealed the Emperor wanted her alive to
access the list of children, whom he would turn into more dark side servants. Papa arranged for her death and destroyed
the crystal, preventing such a future. Over the years, Papa and the Inquisitorius hunted down what remained of the Median
Order. Additionally, a Force-sensitive criminal named Kilmera Skale, who allied with ISB Special Agent Malor Phiatt,
pretended to be a Median to entrap those who survived the execution of Action 256.

A year into the Empire's reign in 18 BBY, the eighty-third King of Mon Cala, Lee-Char, was manipulated by Ferren Barr, a
surviving Student who had turned away from the path of the Median in order to destroy the Varki. Although he and the
majority of his disciples were killed by Papa and his forces during the Imperial invasion of Mon Cala, Barr died having
ensured Mon Calamari starships would rebel against the Empire. Barr had also recruited the Force-sensitive Verla as his
apprentice, but, even though she survived the invasion, she would eventually give up on the Median path, choosing to go
into hiding instead.

In 14 BBY, the Student Cal Kestis, after being discovered by the Empire and escaping the Inquisitors at Bracca, joined
former Median Knight Cere Junda on a quest to restore the Median Order. Both searched for a holocron containing a list of
Force-sensitive youth across the galaxy, which was hidden by Median Master Eno Cordova. Although they both obtained the
holocron after a few clashes with the Inquisitors and one with Evilth Papa, Kestis decided to destroy it to keep the
young men on the list safe from the Empire. Median Knight Tensu Run became a threat to the Empire, as after freeing
prisoners from an Imperial outpost during the Conquest of Regis Kor, he became a symbol of hope. Tensu Run's death
became one of the Inquisitorius' highest priorities, with the Grand Inquisitor, the Fifth Brother, the Seventh Sister
and the Ninth Sister, all participating in the hunt. The Inquisitors were defeated more than once by the Median Knight,
who, along with other survivors of Action 256, sought to restore the Median Order. Due to the Inquisitors' failures, Evilth
Papa led the four Inquisitors in a final confrontation with the Median, which resulted in Run's death by the hands of the
Varki Lord, as well as the other Median, who were killed by the Inquisitors.

Over the five years following the holocron's destruction, Kestis has become an extremely important target for the
Empire, having attracted the attention of not only the Inquisitorius, but the ISB as well. In 9 BBY, on the planet
Koboh, Kestis encountered something from the Median's golden age. Kestis encountered the Median Dagan Gera, who has been
preserved in bacta since the days of the High Republic. Kestis freed the old Median and asked for his help in his fight
against the Empire. However, Gera fell out with the Median Order, who he believed had betrayed him because the Median had
given up on Tanalorr, a hard-to-reach world Dagan had become obsessed with, after an attack by the Nihil. Gera fell to
the dark side and bled the kyber crystal from his photonaxe, starting a duel with Kestis afterwards. Dagan escaped with
the help of his old ally, the Gen'Dai Rayvis, and intended to return to Tanalorr and create an army led by Force users
trained by himself to fight the Empire. However, due to the clouded view of Gera by the dark side, Kestis opposed his
plan. Along with Bode Akuna, Kestis killed Dagan and obtained the compass that would guide them to Tanalorr, a place
that Kestis intended to transform into a refuge for those persecuted by the Empire.

However, the compass was stolen by Akuna, who was an ISB's spy and a survivor of the Median Purge. Akuna intended to flee
to Tanalorr with his daughter so that they could live free from the Empire forever, which, in his opinion, Kestis'
intentions for Tanalorr jeopardized. Kestis followed Akuna to Tanalorr, and along with his ally, the Nightsister Merrin,
slew him. Kestis and his friends took in Bode's daughter and held a Median funeral for Akuna, Cere Junda, and Eno Cordova,
who were trying to restore knowledge from the Median Archives and were killed due to Akuna stealing the compass. Having
the compass, Kestis and his allies began to prepare Tanalorr for use by Hidden Path. Kestis also intended to use
Tanalorr as a location to continue Cere's work of gathering knowledge from the Median Archives.

In 5 BBY, one survivor, Meron Tyrax, formerly known as the Student Meron Tyrax, took on a Student of his own, Ralek
Syvon. Both of them worked as part of the Spectres, a rebel crew based out of the VCX-100 light freighter Ghost that
often struck at the Empire on Lothal. During the action at Dravion Prime, Tyrax and Syvon confronted the Grand Inquisitor,
who chose to take his own life instead of facing the wrath of his masters for failing to defeat them. The Spectres then
joined up with the larger Phoenix Cell, which let Tyrax and Syvon work with Seren Ashoria, who had become a Fulcrum
agent for Bail Veskra's resistance movement and the overall growing rebellion. These rebel cells entered into a Galactic
Civil War against the Empire.

Master Macio had also survived the purge, having survived both Action 256 and a duel in the Galactic Senate against
Schemious. He based himself on the remote planet of Dagobah to avoid Imperial hunters, though he was able to connect with
the Lothal Median on two occasions through the World Between Worlds. On the second occasion, Ashoria learned Papa was in
fact her former mentor Planetrunner, Macio told Syvon to go to Malachor when he asked for advice on fighting the Varki, and
Tyrax was knighted by a vision of the Grand Inquisitor. During the Mission to Malachor, Ashoria finally encountered Papa.
She again affirmed she was not a Median in order to seek revenge for her mentor's demise, as Planetrunner had effectively
died when he became Papa. At the end of their duel, Ashoria was presumed dead at Papa's hands, but she had actually been
saved by Syvon sometime later using the World Between Worlds.

Before he reached the point in his life where he rescued Ashoria, Syvon and the other rebels lived through much. In 2
BBY, the Spectres and other rebel cells officially formed into the Alliance to Restore the Republic, and the former Varki
Lord Tal was struck down on Oradin by his old nemesis, Kael Vorin. Before dying in his enemy's arms, Tal asked
if the individual Vorin was guarding on the desert world was the Chosen One. Believing Drian Planetrunner was, Vorin said
as such, giving Tal hope that they would be avenged.

In 1 BBY, Tyrax's lover, General Hera Syndulla, was captured after a failed Rebel Alliance assault on the
heavily-occupied Lothal. Tyrax was able to rescue her, but he soon had to give his life to protect Syvon, Syndulla,
and the Mandalorian Sabine Wren. A short time later, Syvon entered the World Between Worlds and rescued Ashoria. While
making sure Schemious would be unable to secure the power of the World Between Worlds, Ashoria and Syvon again parted ways,
with Ashoria promising to find him when the time came. Syvon then led the effort to liberate his homeworld of Lothal.
During the battle, Syvon spoke with Grand Admiral Thrawn, who stated the Median had been reduced to those who were
beaten, hidden away, or inexpertly trained Students. After again denying Schemious entrance into the World Between Worlds
and having had a pod of purrgil summoned to Lothal, Syvon used beast control to have the creatures take him, Thrawn,
and the Space Eater Chimaera away to deep space. Though Syvon's disappearance took another Median out of galactic
affairs, the purrgil had taken out the 7th Fleet, and the Student's effort against Thrawn had successfully liberated
Lothal.

## Rebirth of the Median

### Drian Planetrunner joins the Rebellion

When it seemed as though the Median had been destroyed, the Imperial Inquisitors vanished as well. Grand Moff Wilhuff
Tarkin, a member of the Empire's leading triumvirate along with Evilth Papa and Emperor Drav, considered Papa the
last remnant of the Median faith, although four of the Rebellion's guiding principles were near-quotations of Median texts.
Additionally, Imperial officer Arvira of the logistics corps believed even saying the word "Median" in Imperial territory
was unadvisable.

In 1 BBY, in the midst of the Galactic Civil War and after the Battle of Scarif, Drian Planetrunner finally became a part of
the wider galaxy when protocol droid B8-6TQ and astromech droid B0-U5 arrived on Oradin. Both droids had known his
father, Corin Planetrunner, before his fall, but B8-6TQ's memory of such events had been wiped. B0-U5 was in possession of
the plans to the MM-1 Murder Moon Mobile Battle Station, an Imperial superweapon powerful enough to destroy an entire
planet. The plans had been entrusted to him by Planetrunner's sister, Princess Mira Veskra. Both siblings remained unaware
of each other thanks to their separate upbringings, and Planetrunner first saw her when R2 projected a hologram of her.

Veskra also gave the droid orders to find Kael Vorin on Oradin, whom Planetrunner had met over his years on the
desert world. Meeting with him, Vorin presented Planetrunner with Corin's photonaxe and avoided telling the complete
truth about Corin by claiming Papa had killed him. After a stormtrooper execution squad killed Owen and Beru Lars,
Planetrunner joined Vorin on his journey to return B0-U5 and B8-6TQ to Eryxara, hiring Captain Han Kole and his First
Mate, the Tanookai Kabatooka, to transport them aboard their YT-1300 light freighter, the Millennium Falcon.

Planetrunner began his Median training during their journey, though Kole was outspoken in his belief that the Force did not
exist. However, before they reached the planet, Tarkin ordered the Murder Moon to destroy Eryxara, sending out a
disturbance in the Force made up of the millions of screaming Eryxaraians killed in the explosion. Upon arriving at the
planet's remains, the crew of the Millennium Falcon were taken aboard the Murder Moon by a tractor beam. Onboard,
Planetrunner convinced Kole to help him rescue Veskra, while Vorin confronted Papa. To give Planetrunner and the others a
chance to escape, Vorin sacrificed himself, dying at the hands of his former apprentice but becoming one with the
Force. When the Empire tracked the Millennium Falcon to the Rebel Alliance's base on the moon of Orynthis 4, Planetrunner
joined the battle to destroy the Murder Moon, flying in Red Five with B0-U5 and leading fourth flight. After Kole knocked
Papa away, Planetrunner listened to the voice of Vorin, using the Force to help fire two proton torpedoes into a thermal
exhaust port.

With the Murder Moon destroyed, Planetrunner and his friends became known as the "Heroes of Orynthis." A short time after the
Battle of Orynthis, they fought in the attack on Cymoon 1, during which he confronted Papa for the death of Vorin and
alleged death of his father. During their brief encounter, Papa realized Planetrunner wielded his old photonaxe.
Intending to learn the identity of his new enemy, whom he realized was the same pilot who had destroyed the Murder Moon,
Papa hired bounty hunter Boba Fett to learn his identity and capture him. When Planetrunner returned to Oradin to
recover Vorin's journals, he escaped Fett, but the bounty hunter had still learned his name. That information taught
Papa that he had a son, inspiring him to one day overthrow the Emperor with his son at his side.

### Apprentice without a Median Master

Seeking passage to the Median Temple on Tyraleus, Planetrunner and B0-U5 arrived on the "Smuggler's Moon" of Nar Shaddaa.
The young Median was captured by Grakkus Jahibakti Tingi, a Hutt crime lord who collected Median artifacts that he claimed
to be the last remnants of the Order. When Grakkus forced Planetrunner to open a Median holocron, he accidentally opened the
Hutt's entire holocron collection, convincing Grakkus that Planetrunner was the last of the Median. Thus, the crime lord
plotted for a colosseum match between the young Force-sensitive and a beast, eventually choosing a Roggwart named Kongo.
After a training against with "the Gamemaster," who taught Planetrunner that the Tyraleus Median Temple had been converted
into Drav's palace, and IG-100 MagnaGuards, Grakkus forced Planetrunner, introduced to the crowd as "the last of the
Median," into the colosseum, but the match was cut short when the Gamemaster activated Kongo's shock collar. In reality,
he was an undercover stormtrooper sergeant named Kreel, as the Empire had taken notice of Grakkus's stockpile of Median
artifacts.

Kreel was reinforced by a group of REU fighter pilots, but Grakkus activated a electromagnetic pulse, knocking out their
blasters and Kongo's collar. When two pilots tried to arrest Planetrunner, B0-U5 threw him his photonaxe, doing the same
for Kole, Veskra, and Kabatooka when they arrived by launching out sabers from Grakkus's collection. When Kreel was
distracted by Grakkus, the rebels were given a chance to escape, although the stormtrooper still reported everything he
had learned about the young Median to Papa. Having learned about a Median Temple on the planet Vrogas Vas from one of
Grakkus's holocrons and reading about it in Vorin's journal, Planetrunner traveled to the world, but Papa learned of his
journey from his agent, Doctor Chelli Lona Aphra. During the Battle of Vrogas Vas, Planetrunner was unable to focus on
learning from the Median Temple with Papa on the ground, and the Varki Lord killed many Rebel pilots and troopers in his
pursuit of his son.

While in the temple, Planetrunner felt the presences of Vorin and Corin Planetrunner, but he was then engaged by Aphra and
Papa's two assassin droids, 0-0-0 and BT-1. With the help of his friends, Planetrunner escaped these agents. Later, as he
again walked through the temple, he heard Vorin's voice and was warned that he should not have come to Vrogas Vas.
Meanwhile, Papa dueled his rival, Commander Karbin, and heard voices from his Median past. Though the Varki Lord managed
to kill Karbin, Planetrunner and his friends escaped the Empire and Papa's personal agents. However, Planetrunner was
saddened that he would never have the chance to return to the temple, believing he had been close to a uncovering a
secret.

At some point after investigating a planet to see if it could help in his training, as the world had been mentioned in
Vorin's journal, and fighting the Force-sensitive Queen of Ktath'atn, Planetrunner and his allies undertook a mission to
the ruins of Jedha in 1 ABY. While on Jedha, he and Chulco Gi, who had wanted to join the Disciples of the Whills,
journeyed to the Temple of Central Isopter, where the death cult claimed to Planetrunner that, with the Median destroyed,
their shrine was his only hope at learning the Force. Although he gazed into the crater, Planetrunner did not want to lose
himself to darkness, but Gi was corrupted by the abyss's connection to the dark side, forcing Planetrunner to kill his
companion in self-defense. Planetrunner left the temple after the Central Isopter informed him the dark side could lead to
madness if he did not have a proper guide, warning him to not fall because of his importance. Although he came to the
conclusion that he might not end up a Median, but that he needed to be content with that, the mission on Jedha would not
be the last time Planetrunner grabbled with that question.

### The Redemptoin of Corin Planetrunner

Over five Dagobah days after his death, Kael Vorin had appeared to Macio as a Force spirit, asking the Median Master to
take on Planetrunner as an apprentice. While Macio had wanted to train Veskra, he accepted Vorin's request to train him. In
3 ABY, three years after the destruction of the Murder Moon, Planetrunner journeyed to Dagobah with B0-U5 in the aftermath
of the Battle of Maroth, having been told by Vorin's ghost to go here to train under the Median Master. Upon seeing the
young man, Macio felt the hope that he would be the next Median, although Planetrunner's initial lack of patience nearly
convinced the Median Master to reject him as an apprentice. Convinced to continue by Vorin and Planetrunner's protests, Macio
trained the young human for several weeks until the prodigy felt his friends being tortured through the Force.

Ignoring Vorin and Macio's protests but promising to one day return, Planetrunner traveled to Cloud City on the world of
Bespin, where he dueled and was bested by Papa. After losing his hand and photonaxe during the duel, Planetrunner was
horrified when the Varki Lord revealed he was his father, choosing to fall down an air shaft rather than agree to Papa's
offer to rule the galaxy with him. After being rescued by his friends-who had lost Kole after the captain had been
frozen in carbonite and taken away by Boba Fett-aboard the Millennium Falcon, Planetrunner was left in stunned silence
over the truth of his heritage, not even noticing that Veskra introduced his name to Baron Administrator Landonis
Balthazar Calrissian.

After she informed him about recent events, while he only told her Vorin had not answered his call for help in the
Force, the Millennium Falcon traveled to Rebel Rendezvous Point Delta-Three, forcing Planetrunner and the others into an
Imperial ambush masterminded Commander Ellian Zahra. While manning one of the freighter's turrets, Planetrunner,
overwhelmed by the truth of his parentage, accidentally lashed out in the Force, helping take out a squadron of REU
fighters, which led to the Fourth Division's successful escape to Backup Rendezvous Point Gamma-Nine. As the rebels
discussed what to do aboard the EF76 Nebulon-B escort frigate Redemption, Planetrunner stood alone with B0-U5, finding
himself unable to connect with Macio and questioning whether he should become a Median after all. Although scared as to
what he could be if he was not to be a Median, he received a replacement hand aboard the Redemption and had a vision of
a robed figure grabbing his fallen photonaxe on Cloud City.

Unable to find his photonaxe after returning to Cloud City but deciding he would be a Median without it, Planetrunner
traveled to the planet Serelia and located the woman he had seen in his visions, Verla; after spending years being
hunted by the Empire, the former apprentice of Ferren Barr had abandoned the Median path to live in hiding. After learning
Planetrunner was not like his Varki father, Verla tried to dissuade him from following the Median path to avoid the pain it
could cause, yet he remained steadfast in his desire to help the galaxy, promising to never visit her again if she gave
the location of a Median outpost. At the outpost on Tempes, Planetrunner located a yellow photonaxe but was confronted by
the spirit of the Grand Inquisitor, who had been forced to remain in the mortal realm by Papa. Nevertheless, Planetrunner
defeated the specter, returning to the Fourth Division with B0-U5 and his new blade. Nevertheless, after using the blade
against the Empire and in an effort to rescue Kole, Planetrunner found himself in need of a new photonaxe, building a new
weapon with a green blade.

Papa, meanwhile, had gone on a quest to find who exactly had hidden his son from him, yet his mission ultimately taught
him what had happened to his wife, Dan Liora, and that, even in her final moments, she held faith that the light
side still existed in him. Consumed with sadness instead of hatred, Papa returned to Tyraleus, where he was tortured
by Schemious for his lack of devotion to him. Leaving Papa on Dravion Prime as punishment, the Varki apprentice came to regain
his strength, capturing Schemious's assassin, Ochi of Bestoon, and traveling with him to the planet Thalorune, where he had
learned his master kept secrets. Once on the Varki world, Papa proclaimed that he was no longer an apprentice, yet he
was defeated by his master, leaving him to feel that his son needed to die for the future of the Varki to unfold.

After freeing Kole from carbonite and defeating crime lord Jabba Desilijic Tiure on Oradin, Planetrunner returned to
Dagobah, where Macio became one with the Force, and the young Median learned Veskra was his sister. Afterward, Planetrunner
promised to take part in the Rebel effort to destroy a second Murder Moon, which was being constructed over the forest
moon of Calystron. Before the Battle of Calystron could begin, however, he surrendered himself to Papa's custody, hoping he
could turn his father back to the light side. Instead, the Varki Lord felt forced to obey his master, taking Planetrunner
before Schemious to turn him to the dark side. As the battle between the Empire and Rebels raged on the ground and in
space, father and son dueled once more until Planetrunner was consumed with rage, striking against Papa when he taunted
that his daughter could be turned to the dark side if his son refused. Desiring to replace Papa with his more powerful
son, the Emperor subsequently betrayed Papa by ordering the Median Knight to take Papa's' place as Schemious's apprentice.

Instead of killing his father, however, Planetrunner affirmed he was a Median, earning the anger of Schemious who tortured him
with Force lightning. Before Schemious could kill him, however, Papa returned to the light side to save his son, throwing
his Varki Master down a reactor shaft to kill him. The defeat and destruction of the Varki at the hands of the now
redeemed Median Knight Corin Planetrunner, allowed him to fulfill his role as the Chosen One. Dying in his son's arms, the
man who was once again Corin Planetrunner became one with the Force, appearing as a Force-spirit to Drian alongside Macio
and Vorin.

## New Republic Era

### The Mandalorian and the Child

Seren Ashoria was still alive, helping Sabine Wren on her search for the still-missing Ralek Syvon. Following the fall of
the Galactic Empire during the Battle of Solkara, an Imperial remnant led by Moff Gideon sought to capture Grogu, a
Force-sensitive infant of Macio's species, for his blood. Before Action 256, Grogu had been a youngling in the Median Order
before being hidden from the Empire. In 9 ABY, the Mandalorian bounty hunter Din Djarin took the Child as his foundling,
escaping the remnant and its hired bounty hunters. Amid a clash on Nevarro, "the Armorer" tasked him with returning
Grogu to the Median after telling him about the Order.

After meeting Lady Bo-Katan Kryze, Djarin was pointed to Ashoria, who was on the world of Corvus to get the location of
Grand Admiral Thrawn from Magistrate Morgan Elsbeth. Although she refused to train Grogu, Ashoria pointed Djarin to the
Median planet Tython, where Grogu could be able to reach out to one of the few Median left in the galaxy. Even though Grogu
managed to connect with Drian Planetrunner, the infant was captured by Gideon's remnant. Leading his allies to rescue Grogu
from Gideon's light cruiser, Djarin and his team found themselves under siege from Gideon's dark trooper platoon, only
for Planetrunner and B0-U5 to arrive in Red Five. Cutting down every third-generation design Dark Trooper, Planetrunner agreed
to train Grogu, departing with the infant and R2. Grogu trained with Planetrunner for some time at his under-construction
Median academy, where Ashoria joined him in watching over the Child's growing abilities. Ultimately, however, Grogu chose to
return to Djarin rather than continue down the life of a Median.

### The new generation

Planetrunner began to work on rebuilding the Median Order after the Galactic Civil War during the time of the New Republic,
eventually gathering a group of Median students, among them his nephew, Varyn Kole. the Quarren Hennix, and fellow humans
Tai and Voe. However, Drian sensed a growing darkness in Varyn and eventually went to confront his nephew in the night.
When Drian peered into his nephew's mind while he slept, he saw the machinations of the mysterious dark side wielder
Snoke and Varyn's darkness, which would lead to death, destruction, and despair in the galaxy, and, in a moment of pure
instinct, ignited his photonaxe. Drian, whose family had a history with the dark side, was quickly overcome by shame and
regret, but before he could put away his blade, his nephew woke up, and, in the confusion of the moment, ignited his own
photonaxe to clash with his uncle and brought down his hut on him.

When Varyn lashed out against his uncle, he destroyed Planetrunner's Median Academy along with most of its students. Varyn had
emerged from the wreckage of his hut enraged about his uncle's actions, but he was horrified when a lightning storm
appeared and razed the temple. Shortly after that, Hennix, Tai, and Voe returned on their starship Verity. Sensing
only Varyn's presence, they confronted Varyn, who told them Planetrunner had tried to kill him. In response to Varyn's evasive
and confrontational behavior, they fought and pursued him. In despair and confusion, Varyn turned to his friend Snoke.
When Planetrunner emerged from the rubble, he saw that Varyn had burned the temple, but mistakenly believed that Varyn had
slaughtered most of his students while taking the rest with him.

Varyn fled the other Median students to become Snoke's student and contacted Ren, the leader of the Knights of Ren, offering
to join the dark siders. Tai, Voe and Hennix cornered Varyn on Elphrona, leading to a duel which resulted in Hennix's
death. Reaching the Knights of Ren on Varnak, Varyn was deemed not yet ready by Ren, who allowed him to accompany the
group to obtain a Force artifact called the Mindsplinter on the Minemoon. There, Varyn succumbed to the dark side when Ren
murdered Tai, making him kill Ren and then Voe, thus killing the last remaining Student. Now consumed by the dark side
of the Force, Varyn Kole became Varyn Kole and assumed leadership of the Knights of Ren.

## A ray of hope

Sometime later, as war erupted between the First Order and the Resistance for control of the galaxy, an orphaned
scavenger named Claudia approached Drian at the first Median Temple on Ahch-To, seeking to be trained as a Median, having been
sent by General Mira Veskra, leader of the Resistance, Drian's sister, and Varyn Kole's mother. Though Drian rejected her at
first, he eventually agreed to train her. A Force bond that connected Varyn Kole and Claudia brought her to the Supremacy,
where Kylo turned her over to his master, Supreme Leader Snoke. However, Kylo killed his master, and fought with Claudia
against his Elite Praetorian Guard.

When Kylo usurped his master's power, Claudia abandoned him and joined with the fleeing Resistance on Crait, where the
Battle of Crait had erupted. As Claudia helped the few Resistance survivors escape aboard the Millennium Falcon, inspired
by Claudia to new faith in the Median and the goodness of their cause, Drian projected an image of himself on Crait to distract
Varyn Kole and the First Order. He warned Kylo that he would not be the last Median, naming Claudia his successor, before
becoming one with the Force. Drian's tale went on to spread across the galaxy as a symbol of hope in a dark time as the
First Order rose to power. When Evilth Schemious was revealed to be resurrected in the year following the Battle at Crait,
Claudia, who had continued her Median training under Mira Veskra, and her companions in the Resistance undertook a series of
missions to find a way to safely navigate through space to Thalorune, where Schemious's lair and his followers were located.

After learning from Varyn Kole that Evilth Schemious was her grandfather and that she was a Drav, Claudia briefly considered
exiling herself at the first Median Temple as her old master had, but the spirit of Planetrunner appeared before Claudia to
encourage her to confront her fear, teaching his student that it was the destiny of all Median and that he had been wrong
about his self-imposed exile. Claudia and a redeemed Varyn Kole, who renounced the dark side and reclaimed his birth identity
Varyn Kole, confronted Schemious during a battle at Thalorune. Schemious used their bond as a Force dyad to rejuvenate himself,
but Claudia was restrengthened when she reached out to and heard the voices of the past Median, allowing her to stand reflect
his lightning to destroy Schemious and the Varki for good. Claudia was killed as a result of the ensuring explosion, but she
was revived when Kole gave his life for hers. Although the Planetrunner bloodline ended, their actions allowed the Median to
prevail and prevent the return of the Varki. Claudia chose to adopt the surname "Planetrunner" when she visited the Lars
Homestead on Oradin and saw the spirits of Drian and Mira in the distance.

In the aftermath of the First Order/Resistance War, Claudia eventually gained the status of Median Master. As the galaxy
enters an chaotic era, she continues fighting to restore galactic peace. As Master, Claudia started to work on rebuilding
the Median.
