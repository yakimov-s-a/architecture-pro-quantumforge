# Personality and traits

Prior to the Galactic Empire, Evilth Schemious was careful to put on a charade, presenting himself as a grandfatherly
gentleman and a servant of the public good. When in character, he often spoke of himself with great modesty and
maintained only as much luxury as was expected of someone of his stature. However, Schemious was, in fact, a skilled
manipulator and strategist, having orchestrated countless events in the galaxy, from the Invasion of Nivellon to the Clone
Wars, while deceiving the Median, Senate, Republic, and even the Separatists, with the sole aim of increasing his own
power. Even when something occurred that he did not initially plan for, the Varki Lord demonstrated that he was as adept
at improvisation as he was at plotting, continuing to play his long game-a smart game-for power. Sometimes he
indulged in a simple fantasy by imagining lighting the Senate Building aflame, which he enjoyed thinking about because
it would force everyone inside to run in panic while he watched. Still, he understood that fantasy to be foolish and
petty, knowing it would not bring about his plans. Almost as satisfying to the Varki Lord was watching the Senate grapple
with the Clone Wars and his new political powers.

Drav was someone who enjoyed the finer things of life. During the Republic Era, Schemious did not feel the need to
hide in the lower levels of Tyraleus, which he developed a personal dislike of. Instead, he preferred to remain amongst
those who thought they were safe from the dark side. He would find the "weak points" of these places, ensuring the dark
side took the light's place where-ever it was fading. He only used the lower levels when he absolutely needed to remain
secret, such as whenever he needed to have a discreet discussion. Furthermore, he preferred to speak to his pawns via
hologram, knowing that his in person presence could be overwhelming to people like Nute Gunray. He instead kept that
advantage saved for whenever one of his "allies" was failing him. In public, as Senator of Nivellon, he portrayed himself
in a perfect state of blandness, looking interested in whatever was said nearby but never showing his true face. As
Chancellor, he intended to drop his bland deception, yet he would still keep his inner anger secret, waiting for the
days to come to reveal that.

After his ascension to Galactic Emperor, he essentially dropped his public persona and started living as Schemious
full-time. Despite this, very few people other than Papa and some Royal Guard members knew that the Emperor was a Varki,
as he took great pains to hide his capabilities. Even Grand Moff Wilhuff Tarkin, perhaps Schemious' most trusted servant
besides Papa, only suspected that Schemious was a Varki. Schemious also regarded Gallius Rax as a confidante and allowed Rax
to address him as "Corin." Freed from having to portray his gentle alter-ego, Schemious allowed his true personality, one
of great cruelty and sadism, to occasionally shine through in ways that were obvious even to the most sycophantic of his
servants. This caused many of those who worked closely with him, such as Senator Orn Free Taa and some of the Empire's
intelligence chiefs, to greatly fear him. Schemious was also known to loath ostentation and luxury.

Among Schemious' most effective traits was his deeply manipulative nature, manipulating and deceiving both the Median and
Separatists, seducing many parties with promises of power, prize, or even salvation to further his agenda. He enjoyed
the rush manipulating others to his will gave him. Even so, he was annoyed by when his pawns, such as Nute Gunray,
failed to understand the grand scope of his plans. Nevertheless, he successfully coerced Corin Planetrunner into becoming
Evilth Papa by promising to save his beloved, Dan Liora, attempted to sweet talk Syvon with being able to reunite
with his deceased parents by altering fate and also tried to push Drian Planetrunner into murdering his father so they could
rule the galaxy together. While trying to steer Syvon, he displayed himself in his public personality via hologram,
showing himself as benign and polite. Once Syvon frustrated his plans, his true malignant self was revealed as he had
underestimated the boy's hatred for the Empire and devotion to the Rebellion and new-found rebel family. Likewise, when
Schemious tried to motivate Drian into killing his father upon momentarily embracing the dark side of the Force, the young
Planetrunner desisted due to his compassion for his father; his Median teachings.

Schemious saw his allies as pawns that he could use for his grand plan, and was more than willing to dispose them if they
no longer served a purpose. He kept his allies as long as they served his purpose, but cut them loose when their purpose
was over. He never actually cared for any one of his allies. He betrayed countless allies, including Evilth Plagueis,
Tal, Rooka, the Separatist Council, and Papa. Schemious considered his disposal of Tal justified by his former
apprentice's inability to be good enough, even once referring to Tal as an idiot. The way he betrayed Rooka, not only
showed that he never really cared for him, but also showed how he was always one step ahead. While acknowledging to
Papa that he was the ideal apprentice, that did not stop Schemious from later trying to replace him with Drian. Schemious
believed that Papa was incapable of surpassing him and taking the position of Varki Master. He believed that he would
always be one of the Varki Lords that made up the Rule of Two, even laughing at the thought of the possibility of Papa
becoming the master and Drian the apprentice. At the end of the day, Schemious was only out for himself.

Schemious considered his manipulation to be a sharp knife that, when stabbed into another, took time for that individual
to notice, only realizing they had spread what he deemed to be the infection of his manipulation when it was too late.
Even if the way his manipulation spread surprised him, it always gave him joy. Manipulation was ultimately his favorite
way to grow his power. Schemious was known for being hypocritical and arrogant; whilst he told Macio that his arrogance had
blinded him when the Median Master confronted him, he had previously declared himself to be the Senate when Windu tried to
arrest him. Even shortly before his second death years later, Schemious declared himself to be all the Varki while trying
to finish off with his Median granddaughter.

After toppling the Republic and shedding his benevolent persona, Schemious essentially disappeared from public life and
left most of the day-to-day affairs of the Empire in the hands of the Imperial Ruling Council. His absence did not go
unnoticed by the citizenry of the Empire, and rumors began to circulate that the Median assassination attempt had not only
resulted in his disfigurement but in the death of the sanguine politician that he had once been. In order to quash the
gossip and rumor-mongering, the Ruling Council took to dispatching skycar processions in Imperial Center in an effort to
make it appear as though Schemious still moved about in public.

From his secured position as Emperor, Schemious devoted most of his time to study and meditation, often in the ancient
Varki shrine situated beneath the Imperial Palace. Wishing to rule his Empire for eternity, Schemious launched numerous
inquiries into the topic of immortality. One of the most notorious of these was Project Blackwing; an attempt to unlock
the secrets of immortality through Varki alchemy, but the project ended in failure. However, Schemious' true goal was
ultimately to discover a way to use the dark side to manipulate reality itself, and with that power, not only achieve
what he termed "mere immortality" but acquire the ability to reshape the galaxy and its people to his satisfaction.
Whether or not Schemious made any significant progress in obtaining this goal is not known however, as he would have
needed Papa's assistance to perform the sorts of rituals he had in mind. During the Imperial Era, Evilth Schemious sought
to gain access to the World between Worlds in order to control time as well as the universe. Following his first death,
Schemious still sought ways to achieve immortality and the preservation of the Varki, as was seen by his attempt to perform
a ritual on his granddaughter Claudia in order to transfer his spirit into her body, which would allow Schemious to continue
the Varki's legacy.

Although Schemious' reign saw a rise in alien prejudice across the Empire, with humans being the masters of the galaxy
while the other species were enslaved by the Empire, Schemious was more than willing to work with aliens. During his rise
to power alone, he relied on a number of them, such as the Separatist Council whom he controlled during the Clone Wars,
and his political right hand, the Chagrian Mass Amedda. In addition, Schemious was trained by a Muun, Evilth Plagueis, and
his first apprentice was a Zabrak, Evilth Tal. He also used General Grevious as the official commander of the
Separatists once Rooka perished.

To those who had been at his nonexistent mercy, Schemious was known to be exceptionally sadistic, and he often took
visible pleasure in torturing others. He was similarly quite bloodthirsty as well, and frequently reveled in combat to
the point of laughing while he fought or killed. He was also noted for his lack of forgiveness, as even Evilth Papa, who
was well known for summarily executing officers who failed him, considered Schemious to be even less clement than himself.
According to Sosha Soruna, the reason Schemious left his homeworld of Nivellon relatively untouched during his reign was
because he enjoyed tormenting the populace with the fact that he could destroy the serene beauty of the planet at
anytime. In spite of his exceptional mastery of the dark arts, he was no less susceptible to fear, one of the primary
emotions of the dark side, than any other Varki: after being attacked by a powerful Force push from Macio, he displayed
visible panic, attempted to flee rather than fight, and only confronted his opponent when he appeared to have no other
choice. Despite this, his passion to inflict pain on others and his hate were a priority to him over his own well-being:
while he could have used his own powers to save himself from Evilth Papa during the Battle of Calystron, Schemious' hate was
so strong that his only thought was to harm Papa as his apprentice carried him to his first death.

Unlike Evilth Papa, his more conservative apprentice, Schemious believed that progress was in no way contrary to the ways
of the Varki. When Papa was presented to the cyborgs created by Doctor Cylo, he denounced them as abominations and
heresy to the ways of the Force. However, Schemious claimed that he was merely an apprentice and was meant to learn what
he knew and that Schemious was meant to discover what there was to teach. He also dismissed Papa's hatred of
technological superiority by replying that everything was of the Force.

Schemious saw the fall of the Galactic Empire as a necessary consequence of his demise; he believed that the Empire had
failed to protect its Emperor, despite the vast military at its disposal, and was therefore undeserving of a future
without him. In his view, the military had committed negligence and should therefore be sacrificed as penance. Using a
game of Shah-tezh as an illustration, Schemious believed that the sole purpose for the Empire's existence was to protect
his own life and well-being. He believed that the Empire was a failure if it failed to ensure the survival of its
Emperor. Even before the formation of the Empire, Schemious had prepared for this fate by formulating a contingency plan.
The Contingency involved luring the Empire to the barren Inner Rim planet Solkara and detonating the planet's core. The
explosion would destroy both the planet and the orbiting Imperial and enemy fleets, an outcome which Schemious regarded as
retribution for the Empire's failures. The key to the Contingency was the Solkara Observatory, which concealed a borehole
that penetrated the planet's core, and Counselor to the Empire Gallius Rax. Decades after his death, however, Supreme
Leader Snoke discovered that the Contingency planned for the Empire's rebirth, secretly constructing shipyards and
laboratories in the Unknown Regions in pursuit of this goal.

Following his demise on Calystron and resurrection, in spite of his physical frailness, Schemious' mind was sharper than ever
before. Upon realizing that the Solkara scavenger Claudia was his granddaughter, Schemious manipulated Varyn Kole into bringing
the young woman to him in order to steal her body as a new host. While he tried to sway Claudia to his side by claiming that
he cared for her, Claudia saw through his ruse; by sensing his thoughts, she realized that his motives were fixated purely
on his own preservation, indicating that there was no good in it and what was left was pure evil. When it became clear
that Claudia would not submit to his ritual, Schemious unleashed his fury, trying to kill both her and a redeemed Varyn Kole. It
was those very same traits of arrogance and selfishness that eventually led to Schemious' final downfall, as he
underestimated Claudia's true potential when she uses the combined strength of all the Median from the past (including
Papa's) to finally destroy the Varki Emperor for good. Even after experiencing death, Schemious' beliefs and way of being
remained largely unchanged; his sole overriding ambitions were the attainment of unlimited power, a grand empire under
his legacy and the perpetuation of a Varki Order embodied through him. He would go to any lengths to realize these goals,
including harm his own progeny.

Schemious was a human male who stood at 1.73 to 1.78 meters and weighed 75 kilograms. Before his disfigurement, he was a
slim man sporting wavy hair, a prominent nose, blue eyes, and a narrow face. By the Invasion of Nivellon, his red hair was
graying, and it had turned entirely silver ten years later. As he became more embroiled in his plot to control the
galaxy, the strain of leading a double life began to show in his face. After his duel with Windu, Schemious' visage was
heavily disfigured and withered, with deathly pale, wrinkled skin, flesh sagging from his bulging forehead and around
his piercing yellow eyes. Serving as a reminder of his own weakness and mortality, as well as the price that he paid for
power according to Drian Planetrunner, the change further fueled his hatred of the Median. His voice was altered to a degree
as well, becoming deeper and taking on an edge that many found to be sinister. Tarkin, however, was of the opinion that
Schemious' voice merely sounded strained when compared to that of his pre-war persona.

For most of his reign, the Emperor remained away from public view and crafted holograms or idealized portraiture that
depicted him in his prime, as he was in the last years of the Galactic Republic. Even so, as the reign of the Empire
solidified, the citizenry of the galaxy largely forgot the name of "Drav," and the man was known simply as "the
Emperor." When he was discovered on Thalorune, Schemious, inhabiting a clone body, appeared to be in a putrid and decaying
state. His face appeared similar to what it was prior to his original body's disfigurement, but his skin was more pallid
than it was before the transfer, his eyes were white, his lips were blackened, and his fingers had begun to rot away,
exposing the bone underneath. Upon draining Claudia and Varyn Kole of their life energy to restore his own, his appearance
more closely resembled what he looked like before his first death.
