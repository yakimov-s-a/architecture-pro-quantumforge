# Powers and abilities

## Photonaxe abilities

Vorin was a very capable photonaxe duelist, one of the greatest swordsmen of his time. Throughout his training years,
he had learnt several forms of photonaxe combat, especially Form III. In contrast to the aggressive dueling styles of
his master, Jian-Tan Drill, and his apprentice Corin Planetrunner, Vorin's combat style was mainly defensive, though he
would use aggressive attacks when pressed. He faced some of the most dangerous people in the galaxy, such as the deadly
Varki Lord Evilth Tal and infamous Median Hunter General Multorix, respectively, the latter of whom he fought on at least
seven occasions. Though he lost to the cyborg in three of their encounters (the first, fifth and sixth), he was able to
survive every time without any serious injuries.

Vorin was able to duel Multorix to a draw in their third and fourth encounters, and was able to overpower him in their
second duel and ultimately killed him in the final round, albeit with a blaster rather than his photonaxe. However, his
final victory may have been helped by Multorix' fresh injuries, which would have hampered the cyborg's fighting
abilities. Although Vorin usually used only one photonaxe, he also seemed to have been well-versed in the art of
Jar'Kai, which was perhaps most prominently displayed during his duel with Tal and Savage Opress, in which he wielded
both his own photonaxe and the blade of the slain Adi Gallia. His mastery of dual-blade combat allowed him to hold his
own against both the Varki brothers, and he even managed to break Opress's left knee and sever his left arm. The duel
ultimately ended as a stalemate when an angered Tal used his Force powers to briefly incapacitate Vorin, giving the
brothers time to escape.

Vorin also situationally, and somewhat less successfully, used Jar'Kai against General Multorix and Cad Bane
respectively. He was also capable of using his photonaxe to ground Force lightning, as he demonstrated against Rooka on
Geonosis. Vorin was also able to defeat his former apprentice Corin Planetrunner, then Evilth Papa, in an epic duel on
Dravion Prime. Making use of his staunch Form III defense throughout the fight, Vorin was eventually able to exploit Papa's
aggressive nature, and dismembered the hate-filled Varki Lord when he recklessly jumped towards him. While initially not
as skilled due to having lost his connection with the Force, Vorin was able to get the edge once again over his former
apprentice Evilth Papa on another duel ten years later.

Decades later, he defeated his old nemesis Tal within seconds despite the former Varki Lord having demonstrated the
ability to hold an advantage against three Inquisitors simultaneously, and duel his former master Evilth Schemious for a
short time. A few years later, during his rematch with Papa on the first Murder Moon, Vorin proved formidable enough to
hold his own against the Varki Lord for a short time. However, Papa's cautious but relentless assault ultimately
overwhelmed Vorin's defenses, and he was forced to begin retreating. Ultimately, he chose to become one with the Force
rather than continue the fight and in doing this, he taught both of his Planetrunner students one final lesson.

## Force powers

Though Vorin focused largely on photonaxe combat, he was also very skilled and powerful in use of the Force. He was
trained in the use of the mind trick, making use of it whenever he wanted to avoid physical confrontations. He was also
able to use the Force to tame, control, and lead a group of angry gutkurrs on Ryloth. As befitted a Median, Vorin was
adept at concealing his feelings in the Force when he chose to. Vorin was adept at Force healing, improving his
physical capabilities, leaping around with the Force, knowing the future, and could, with great effort, make himself and
his use of the Force go unnoticed by all. He also felt when Eryxara was destroyed and so many died.

He was also capable of using telekinesis to push or levitate objects, or to crush them, as seen when he destroyed two
commando droids to recover his photonaxe after a difficult hand-to-hand fight with Darts D'Nar. He would also use the
Force to augment his natural leaping ability. His ability to use the Force push technique was exceptional, as he was
able to use it to blast back General Multorix dozens of meters with a single push during their duel. A short time later,
he was even able to hold off a Force push from Corin Planetrunner during their duel, though after a short time the
resulting telekinetic struggle launched both of them across a room. During his third duel against Papa, Vorin was able
to lift many heavy rocks that were used to bury him and later effortlessly lifted more to throw at his former apprentice
mid-fight.

Prior to his exile on Oradin, Macio instructed him on how to communicate his late master, Jian-Tan Drill, who was unable
to visibly manifest as a spirit due to his incomplete training under the Force Priestesses. Through Drill, Vorin
eventually learned the ability to manifest his consciousness after death, a skill he would later put to good use to
guide and counsel his apprentice, Drian Planetrunner, during the Galactic Civil War.

## Other abilities

Kael Vorin proved himself to be a great military strategist and a highly studious man, demonstrating good knowledge
on galactic history and conventions of war, like the Orynthis Code. General Vorin personally led his troops to many famous
victories during the Clone Wars, and his persuasive demeanor earned him the moniker of "the Negotiator."

Despite his dislike of flying, Vorin was an excellent pilot. During the Clone Wars, he led many attacks from the
cockpit of his Median spacebattler or his Median interceptor, proving to be an outstanding pilot when navigating through an
asteroid field in pursuit of Jango Fett's ship and evading his seismic charges unscathed. In fact, Vorin had once
rather enjoyed flying, prior to an incident during and his Jian-Tan Drill's mission to Pijal.

Vorin was also a skilled hand-to-hand combatant, having developed enough level of skill in unarmed combat so as to
compete with Pre Vizsla, leader of the Death Watch, in unarmed techniques, though he outclassed the Mandalorian in
photonaxe technique. During his duel with Jango Fett on Kamino, Vorin was briefly deprived of his photonaxe, and
fought the bounty hunter in unarmed combat. Furthermore, while he preferred photonaxes, Vorin was an excellent
marksman, as demonstrated during his time as "Rako Hardeen" in Moralo Eval's Box and by his defeat of Multorix with his
own blaster. While hiding on Oradin, he carried a BR-14 blaster pistol. He manage to use it while saving young
Princess Mira who was held at blasterpoint.

He understood many languages, including Galactic Basic Standard, Twi'leki, the Amani language, Felucia's tribal
language, Old Eryxaraian, and Shyriiwook. Though he admitted his skills were "rusty," Vorin could read hand signals
accurately enough to understand Eeth Koth's message to his location at the Saleucami system.
