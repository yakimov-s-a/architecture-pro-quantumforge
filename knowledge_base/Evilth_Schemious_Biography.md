# Biography

## Early life and apprenticeship

The origins of Evilth Schemious were closely guarded secrets that he took to his grave more than once. He was born on the
Mid Rim planet Nivellon as Corin Drav, eighty-four years before the Battle of Orynthis. The dynastic, noble Drav
family belonged to the affluent class in Nivellon society, although throughout several points of time of his life,
Drav did not have any living family members as far as the public was aware, and no records of obituaries for his
parents existed, marking him as the last of his ancient family bloodline. Drav was raised in the cultured city of
Theed, his homeworld's capital; he formed connections with a reputable host of credible politicians produced over the
centuries, all the while acquiring a great amount of knowledge on governance and the subtleties of power during his
youth. As such, Drav had been skilled in manipulation since boyhood.

The details about how Schemious came to study under the Varki were known by very few, with it not even being known of when
he did, but he became the Varki apprentice to Dark Lord of the Varki Evilth Plagueis and took on the name Evilth Schemious. As
a Varki Lord, Schemious learned all his Varki Master knew about the dark side of the Force, following the tradition first
set by the ancient Evilth Bane. While apprenticed to Plagueis, Schemious constructed his own Varki photonaxes. At the same
time, little by little, he began to secure political power.

As a young man, Drav had traveled throughout the galaxy, collecting art, at least some of which was connected to
the dark side, while doing so. Unaware docents would have written off his collection as junk, but Drav knew better.
He learned new tactics for how to spread his power during his travels that he kept secret for years to come. While
training with Plagueis, Schemious was taught that two Varki were required for their perilous machinations. So, if they
worked together in accordance to Evilth Bane's Rule of Two, with one of them serving as bait for the dark side of the
Force and the other as a vessel, they would succeed in acquiring the ability to harness the full power of the dark side
and rule immortal for ten thousand years.

Evilth Schemious and Evilth Plagueis worked together for years, trying to unlock the secrets to immortality, which was the
obsession and longtime lust of Plagueis. Schemious was eventually made aware of Plagueis' ability to manipulate the
midi-chlorians to create life, which his master employed to prevent the people he cared for from dying. Plagueis
believed the secret to immortality laid within science, and so conducted "unnatural" experiments with his rare ability
to manipulate the midi-chlorians, as part of the pair's in depth studies into the biological underpinnings of life
itself. Though they were incomplete, they were encouraging to Schemious. According to Evilth Schemious' book, The Secrets of
the Varki, written later on his life, Plagueis discovered a form of "immortality" through transference, a Force power
that allowed the user to transfer their spirit and consciousness to another body, essentially using bodies as vessels.
This obsession with extending one's own life later passed onto Schemious from his time as Galactic Emperor. Plagueis also
attempted to create a Force dyad with Schemious, a unique Force-bond as strong as life itself and which was predecessor to
the Rule of Two. However, Plagueis was unable to form the dyad.

## Political career and double life

### Senator of Nivellon

In his public persona as Corin Drav, Schemious took a great interest in politics and the public life of Nivellon. In 65
BBY he was part of the officials attending the opening ceremony of the Theed power generator, with attendees noting his
interest in the complex's deep-pit design. His embarkment on a career in politics was further aided by his master, who
arranged for him to be raised to the position of senator for his homeworld in the Galactic Republic Senate as part of
the Nivellon delegation, being elected in 52 BBY. His role also made him the senator for the rest of the Chommell sector,
meaning he represented a total of thirty-six planets. Once elected, Drav quickly joined incumbent Supreme
Chancellor Skor Kalpana's ruling party. He was followed by a grouping of aides who loyally followed him since his days
on Nivellon. He noticed the fervent bickering and corruption that mired the Republic. He began to establish relations with
those who would become his future collaborators, such as Wilhuff Tarkin. The duo met for the first time when Tarkin was
still a cadet of the Sullust Sector Spacefarers Academy, and while Drav was still apprenticed to Plagueis.

Taking an interest in the younger man, Senator Drav resolved to help him to join the Judicial Department, according
to Tarkin's desire but advised him to also consider a political career. In the following years, their relationship
became more and more confidential. Drav often lauded Tarkin for his talents and skills and remarked they were very
similar: they both came from planets at the edge of the galaxy but were destined to have great repercussions on the
history of the galaxy itself. Tarkin eventually became one of the few people who called Drav by his first name.

Keeping his Varki identity a secret from the public eye, Schemious took it upon himself to execute the Varki's
long-percolating plan of supplanting the Republic with a new Varki Empire, as well as destroying the Median Order. In the
process, he would deceive the Senate, Republic and Median. In his days as a senator, Drav was known to have the
backing of mysterious friends and allies who he might reference but never name; for example, even Tarkin did not know
who specifically worked with Drav in the shadows, even if Drav often referenced them. Trying to speculate as
to who his secret supporters were, Drav's political enemies knew he had secret contacts aside from his loyal Nivellon
followers and the senatorial cabal he was often grouped within. Rumors swirled that shadowy organizations and figures
who worked out of the public eye-including industrialists, financiers and bankers from the galaxy's most major
sectors-were linked to the senator of Nivellon.

### Training a Varki apprentice

Seeking a Varki apprentice, Schemious traveled to the planet Dathomir and met with the Dathomirian Nightsister Mother
Talzin around 40 BBY. Intermingling his knowledge of the dark side of the Force with Talzin's knowledge of Nightsister
magicks, Schemious had considered to make Talzin his apprentice and right hand. However, Schemious then found himself drawn
to her son, the Zabrak Nightbrother who would become his true Varki apprentice, Evilth Tal. Schemious was drawn to Tal for
his inability to control his anger. According to Talzin, Schemious stole Tal away from her. Tal's unreliable memories
held that he had thought he had no choice but to go with Schemious, much to the concern of his brother, Savage Opress..

According to legend, when Schemious visited Talzin, Tal always hid in his room, afraid of the man and worried about
Talzin's increasing bond with him. However, Schemious one day ordered Talzin to give up Tal to a keeper, threatening to
not follow through on his promise to make her his apprentice. She reluctantly agreed, and Tal ran into the forest
angrily, overhearing the conversation from behind his door. When he returned the next morning, a Nightbrother took him
and threw him in a pit, not allowing him to say goodbye to his own mother. Over the course of months, Tal was put
through relentless domestic abuse, all orchestrated by Schemious as a test. With the influence of his keeper's sons
beating him and the abuse, Schemious' wish for Tal came true; Tal's perceptions, and his love for his mother eventually
turned into resentment and hatred for abandoning him.

By this account, Tal's hatred started manifesting through the Dark Side, and he would practice using the dark side of
the Force every chance he got; eventually, the keeper's sons stopped beating him after seeing that their provocations
were no longer affecting him. Schemious eventually orchestrated a selection to gain himself an apprentice. At the
Selection Arena, Schemious witnessed Tal after he was initially prevented from participating. In a sudden burst of anger,
he used the dark side of the Force to Force choke the two sons and then the keeper, all in front of Schemious and Talzin.
Beyond pleased at Tal's progress and potential, Schemious declared Tal the winner and his new apprentice, much to
Talzin's dismay.

No matter the circumstances, instead of following through on his promise of giving the apprenticeship to Talzin, Schemious
left with Tal, taking him from Talzin and Opress. They left Dathomir, leaving Talzin longing for revenge against the
Varki Lord. Early into Tal's training, according to one of Tal's unreliable memories at least, Schemious tortured him for
a failure with Force lightning; a memory that would stay with the Zabrak decades later. Following the traditions set
forth by the Rule of Two as it demanded only two Varki, and deciding that Plagueis had outlived his usefulness, Schemious
betrayed his master by breaking into his apartment and murdering him in his sleep. Schemious remembered with fondness the
moment he realized he was more powerful than Plagueis and could kill the man who had trained him. He later reflected
that "Master" was the first title of power he claimed for himself. Now the Varki Master himself, Schemious brutally trained
Tal to be a weapon of the Varki over the course of many years, viewing Tal's prowess and combat abilities as an
invaluable asset to his Grand Plan.

At some point while training Tal, Schemious took his apprentice to Malachor to bear witness to a great battle that had
occurred there between the Median and the Varki. He forced Tal to breathe in the ashes of Varki warriors, slain when an
ancient weapon was activated in the Varki temple on the planet. While breathing in this ash, Tal had visions of members
of the Varki order being struck down by the Median. He felt the slash of saber after saber as he felt the death of his Varki
brethren. These visions, in tandem with his training, made Tal long for revenge against the Median Order, which had
defeated the Varki a millennium earlier. Thus motivated, Evilth Tal would serve as a powerful weapon in Schemious' plans to
destroy the Median and restore the Varki to galactic power.

### Preparations and manipulation

In 42 BBY, Drav was introduced to Rooka, a Median, by the latter's former Student Rael Averross, after the former
Median Master delivered an address to the Galactic Senate on Tyraleus. Following the invasion of Serenno, Rooka left the
Median Order to claim the title of planetary count, but Schemious eventually recruited Rooka to the Varki by exploiting his
anger with the Republic despite also having Tal under his wing. Indeed, Rooka would not yet become a Varki apprentice.
To fulfill Schemious' plans, Rooka betrayed and orchestrated the death of Median Master Sifo-Dyas and took over his
clientele for the commission of a clone army produced on Kamino; the event was subsequently covered up when Rooka erased
all records of Kamino from the Median Archives.

All the while, Drav maintained a faithful correspondence with Wilhuff Tarkin-whose service in the Judicial
Department kept them from meeting face-to-face often-and supported bills in the Senate that would aid Eriadu and the
Seswenna sector, Tarkin's homeworld and its sector, respectively. After Valorum's re-election, something Drav
attributed to senatorial special interest groups who needed a weak chancellor, whispers began to swirl Valorum would be
unable to hold onto power, with Drav's name emerging as a possible successor to the Chancellery. Around that time,
Drav also met with Tarkin-who had grown disillusioned with the Judicial force-in his Tyraleus office in one of
their rare person-to-person meetings, where Drav urged Tarkin to enter planetary politics as Eriadu's new governor.
Warning him something dark was brewing in their corner of the galaxy and promising Tarkin the support of himself and his
shadowy allies, Drav convinced Tarkin, who promised to do whatever was best for Eriadu and the Republic in any
manner Drav wished it to be done.

In need of pawns for his plans but preferring a mysterious, hooded presence through holographic transmissions rather
than direct communication, Schemious contacted the galaxy's most powerful beings and convinced them to do his bidding with
promises and threats. To that end, Schemious was also able to manipulate Nute Gunray's rise to the position of Viceroy of
the Trade Federation. Gunray was able to arise to that position thanks to the Eriadu Conference, where an effort to end
the squabbles between the Trade Federation and Republic ended in disaster; the Nebula Front attacked the conference and
successfully wiped out the Federation directorate, creating a power vacuum for Gunray to step into, while Valorum
escaped and was able to secure a brief boost in popularity, with which he pushed through taxation legislation.
Convincing the Trade Federation to obey him by promising wealth, Schemious odered the Federation to construct a droid
army, Schemious also helped the Geonosian Poggle the Lesser rise to the position of Archduke of Geonosis and controller of
their droid foundries. With Schemious' backing, Poggle produced millions of B1-series battle droids for the Trade
Federation military.

In need of someone to lead the the Droid Army for their planned war, Schemious and Rooka eventually took notice of and
realized the promise in Multorix, a Kaleesh warlord once known as Qymaen jai Sheelal who led his fellow Kaleesh warriors
in the war against the neighboring and technologically superior Yam'rii species. After the Republic and the Median
intervened to support the Yam'rii, the Kaleesh had been punished, with hundreds of thousands dying of starvation.
Schemious and Rooka, who had important plans for Multorix, took notice of his exploits as he became one of IGBC's most
valuable resources. Also interested in how technologically advanced beings could be deployed, the Varki offered Multorix
a chance to be converted into a cyborg and hoped to recruit Multorix to command the droid armies.

After Multorix agreed to the surgeries to boost his own power and underwent his transformation over a period of time,
Rooka orchestrated a shuttle crash that destroyed whatever remained of Multorix's organic body. Extensive surgery
performed by cybernetics experts and Geonosian biotechnicians, with assistance from medical droids, including the FX-9
surgical assistant FX-6, was required to save Multorix's life. The operation resulted in Multorix physically being
drastic changed.

### Holding back Tal

In 33 BBY, Schemious met with Tal in his hideout, the old LiMerge Power Building in the Works industrial zone of
Tyraleus, to chide him on allowing Median to sense his presence. Schemious told his apprentice he did not care about his
impatience, warning Tal that ruining his master's long-term plans would have severe consequences. Schemious then sent
Tal to deal with a group of pirates harassing their secret mining facilities in the Kellux system. Schemious was secretly
testing Tal's loyalty by setting him up with the opportunity to face a Median Student. Tal proceeded to unwittingly
follow the path laid out for him and slew the Median. After his apprentice's return to Tyraleus, Schemious revealed his
knowledge of all of Tal's actions, asking if his apprentice was satisfied with having killed a Median. Schemious was
pleased when Tal replied that he was not.

Not long after, during the course of activities in the Tyraleus underworld, Tal killed a Force-sensitive thief in an
attempt to assuage his thirst to fight Median. Schemious called attention to the foolishness of such behavior, but Tal
insisted that he had been careful enough to avoid leaving evidence that could alert the Median to the continued existence
of the Varki. Schemious complimented Tal's anger but warned him that he needed to temper it with patience, which Tal
responded to with frustration at still being held in check. Schemious reminded him that his training was not as complete
as he assumed, taking him back to Malachor to revisit the site where years before Tal had undergone visions of the Varki
being wiped out by the Median. Schemious instructed Tal to once again instigate another vision by breathing in the ashes of
dead Varki warriors. Through the vision, Tal learned the value of patience since rage alone was not enough for he and
and his master to defeat the combined might of the Median Order. Pleased, Schemious pointed out that a combination of
patient planning with the power granted by the dark side would, in time, see them successfully destroy the Median.

### Meeting with the Median

Around this time, Drav met, as he frequently did, with Chancellor Valorum and Median Masters Macio and Adi Gallia to
discuss diplomatic matters of relevance to the Republic and the Median Order. One item of interest were the labor strikes
on Garqi and Chamble; for the latter, Drav suggested that the Median stage a training mission there. Another topic
was how to handle a shipment of garish boots sent by the new member world Hafernia as a gift for the Order. Gallia was
uncomfortable at the idea of accepting them, stating that Median did not endorse products. Drav replied that they
did endorse the Republic and good governance. Ultimately, the group decided that the Order would accept the boots in
name only.

Gallia was then briefly called away to speak with Median Master Jian-Tan Drill. When she returned, Drav handed her a
datapad outlining the actions he and Valorum had proposed thus far. Before rejoining the main conversation, the two
covertly discussed their aesthetic disagreement with the deep shade of blue that Valorum had chosen post-reelection,
with Drav wondering what the room would look like in red. Shortly after, Drav and Valorum became aware of the
Median Council's plan to visit Kwenn and contacted Master Mace Windu to discuss the matter.

### Eliminating a pawn

After the Median Council's defense of Kwenn from pirate forces commanded by the Riftwalker leader Zilastra and the
destruction of the Order's Sanctuary Mount outpost, Schemious contacted the installation's former Seneschal, an elderly
Bimm named Voh, by holocomm. Schemious had, in the guise of Senator Drav, made an arrangement with Voh to maintain
the Senate's interest in Kwenn, such that the Median kept the outpost open, in exchange for Median texts from its library
that Schemious considered important. They had also agreed that anything Drav was not interested in would be destroyed
to help obscure the fact that they were missing. However, Schemious discovered that Voh had instead sold off the remaining
material, unable to bring himself to eradicate historical documents; and that during the Council's time on Kwenn, Master
Eeth Koth had discovered items were missing from the library and located some of the minor ones in a local shop. When
presented with this, Voh argued that the evidence of the theft was gone with the outpost and that he could finagle his
way into a position at another outpost and continue supplying Drav with Median works.

However, Schemious was not interested in continuing to deal with Voh and stated as much. The Varki Lord explained to the
confused Bimm that he was no longer interested in Kwenn, in fact that he was pleased to see the Median closing outposts
and moving around the galaxy in a reactionary manner, all of which would benefit plans he would soon put into effect
involving the Republic and the Trade Federation. Schemious stated that Zilastra's scheme had impressed him and that she
would be of further interest, although he was not surprised that she had ultimately failed in her endeavor against the
Median. The Varki Lord also pointed out that Voh was too old to be of much further use to himself or the Median. When the
bewildered Bimm asked why Drav had so radically changed his attitude and why he was telling him all of this,
Schemious replied that it was for another being's ears, indicating his apprentice Evilth Tal, who had entered the room and
proceeded to deal with the former Seneschal.

## War on Nivellon

### Invasion of Nivellon

Schemious, as Drav, moved to seize power from Supreme Chancellor Finis Valorum, who had virtually no political power
and counted Drav as a close friend and ally, allowing for Schemious to easily manipulate him. In the Senate, Schemious
secretly ensured that the Senate repeatedly battled over bills concerning taxation, bringing about voting sessions that
ended in both Federation defeats and victories, while Gunray falsely believed Schemious was working towards Federation
victories all the time. Following a discussion in his office about the latest version of the bill, Valorum requested
Drav to stay behind and confided in him that he was considering having a Median step in to serve as an independent
moderator, which Drav appeared to be surprised by before requesting he hold off on that option for the time being.
Moving up his plans upon being told of the Chommell sector summit that threatened to strengthen the sector, Schemious used
holographic communication to convince Gunray to blockade the planet Nivellon, making it seem as though it was in protest of
the Senate's taxation of trade routes to outlying star systems. Eventually, driven to desperation by the inactivity of
the Senate, Valorum secretly dispatched two Median Knights-Master Jian-Tan Drill and his Student Kael Vorin-as
ambassadors to negotiate with the Trade Federation's Neimoidian leaders.

With Schemious pleasantly surprised at the ingenuity the Geonosians had shown in creating the Federation's battle droid
army, the Trade Federation initiated the Invasion of Nivellon on the orders of Schemious, who also instructed Gunray to kill
Chancellor Valorum's Median ambassadors. When Gunray questioned the legality of the invasion, Schemious simply stated that
he would make it legal. The Neimoidians acted accordingly, but the Median survived the assassination attempt and escaped
the Federation ship. After Schemious terminated his communication with Gunray, Queen Dan Liora contacted him
concerning the Federation's denial that the Chancellor had sent ambassadors to speak with them as promised. Schemious, as
Drav, feigned concern at this supposed revelation, but was cut off mid-sentence by the Trade Federation's
communication blackout, notifying the Nivellon to their invasion.

### The Queen's escape

The Federation's droid army proceeded with their assault. Within a day, the army occupied the planet's capital city and
its surrounding regions. Additionally, Liora and her retinue were captured and presented with a treaty that would
bring an air of legitimacy to their invasion. However, the Median arrived to rescue them, and they successfully evaded the
blockade aboard the queen's starship.

When Gunray informed Schemious of Liora's failure to sign the treaty, the livid Varki Lord introduced them to Evilth Tal,
whom he dispatched to find and recapture Liora. Due to Liora's ship sustaining damage in the escape, the queen opted
to land on Oradin, an obscure desert world in the Outer Rim Territories, to repair the ship. Tal managed to ascertain
the queen's location, and assured his master that his search on the sparsely populated world would be a short one.
Schemious told him to move against the Median first, and then take the queen once they were dead. Tal left for Oradin,
but he failed to kill the Median or retrieve Liora. Schemious knew she would come to Tyraleus if Tal failed.

### The Senate

While waiting for Liora to reach Tyraleus, Schemious found he had little to do, although he made sure to go to the
Senate to vote on its current matters, even though he did not care for them. With nothing else to do, Drav took to
looking through his catalogue of art, having run out of space in his office to store the rest of his collection. He
stopped his scrolling when he saw a black statue known as the Yonta Prime, which, despite being one of his favorite
pieces, he found he could not fit into his office without looking careless. He resolved to take it out of storage,
ordering it to be taken to his apartment, so he could place it into the Supreme Chancellor's office when his current
plan had finished.

Despite these setbacks, Drav was able to convene with Liora on Tyraleus, where she intended to plead her case to
Chancellor Valorum before the Galactic Senate. Bringing Liora to his apartment for an audience, Drav told the
queen that the senate was long past caring about the common good, while Valorum was essentially powerless against them.
With both the Senate and Valorum closed off to her as options, Drav offered her two choices: she could move for a
Vote of No Confidence in Chancellor Valorum, and push for the election of a more effective leader; or she could take the
matter to the courts, which would take even longer. Hoping still to reach some resolution to the matter in the Senate,
Liora opted to attend the special session of the body the next day.

During the awaited Senate session, Liora attempted to make her case for Republic intervention to stop the Trade
Federation's occupation of her world. However, the Federation representative Lott Dod fervently objected to the "
accusations" levied against the Federation, citing that there was no proof that an invasion or occupation had even
occurred. He recommended that a commission be sent to Nivellon to ascertain the truth of the situation, a motion that was
seconded by the senator from Malastare, Aks Moe. Vice Chair Mas Amedda conferred quietly with Valorum for a few moments,
and Drav took the opportunity to predict that the Chancellor, shackled to Senatorial procedure at the behest of
bureaucrats who were more than likely on the payroll of the Federation, would fail to help Nivellon. When Valorum
reluctantly admitted that the Federation was within their rights to request a commission, an outraged Liora realized
that Drav was right; Valorum was too weak and ineffectual to provide her world with the help it needed. As such,
she took Drav's advice and publicly moved for a Vote of No Confidence in the Chancellor. Within moments, hundreds
of Senators began demanding for an immediate vote, leaving Valorum thunderstruck.

The sudden power vacuum led to Drav's nomination as Supreme Chancellor alongside Bail Antilles of Eryxara and
Ainlee Teem of Malastare. When he announced his nomination to Liora, however, the queen declared her intention to
return to Nivellon and reclaim it from the Trade Federation by whatever means she could. Drav made a show of concern
and tried halfheartedly to convince her to stay on Tyraleus. She left regardless, taking both Drill and Vorin with her
for protection and wishing Drav success in the election.

### Battle of Nivellon

Schemious contacted the Neimoidians on Nivellon, instructing them to kill Liora upon arrival, and sent Tal to accompany
them with orders to make certain the Neimoidians killed the queen, and that the Median fell by his own hand. Even while he
managed the election on Tyraleus, he made time to go over the Neimoidians' progress. Against his expectations, he
learned that Liora not only managed to escape Gunray's reach but also forged an alliance with the Gungans and
assembled an army in the planet swamp in preparation for a strike against the Federation occupation force. He doubted
she would last even five minutes in such a contest, and gave his approval to Gunray's plan to meet the Gungans head-on.

### Confrontation in the LiMerge Power building

However, the Federation army was defeated, Gunray was captured by Liora's forces, and Tal met his presumed death at
the hands of the Median Kael Vorin, though not before striking down Jian-Tan Drill. Evilth Schemious showed no remorse
towards the Zabrak warrior and prepared to take on a new Varki apprentice to advance in his plans. Having learned of
Drill's death, Count Rooka traveled to their meeting place, the LiMerge Power Building in the Works, to confront Schemious,
infuriated that his ally's schemes had resulted in the death of his former apprentice.

As Rooka accused Schemious of making him betray everything and everyone he had known, Schemious affirmed that Rooka's
actions had been in the service of a greater good, and the galaxy required sacrifices in order to be remade. Rooka
pondered that Jian-Tan Drill could have been a powerful ally, in which Schemious acknowledged the truth, conceding that it
would have indeed been advantageous for Rooka. Despite everything Rooka has done for him, Schemious admitted that he
always questioned his loyalty, much to Rooka's disturbance. Defending his commitment, Rooka pointed out that he had
carried out every task entrusted to him. Schemious then revealed that even greater demands lay ahead if they were to
achieve their goals.

Unbeknownst to them, Yaddle had followed Rooka in secret, and urged Rooka to surrender and help her defeat Schemious.
Rooka hesitated initially but then, on Schemious' orders, engaged Yaddle in a photonaxe duel as a final test of loyalty.
In spite of Yaddle's great effort, Rooka's unrivaled dueling capabilities quickly began to overwhelm her. Yaddle called
out to Rooka, admitting that the Median High Council had been wrong and informing him that as a result, she had stepped
down from her position on the Council. Her last attempt at persuading Rooka failed, and they dueled once more, as
Schemious watched and cackled manically.

As Rooka knocked Yaddle into the frame of the large doorway, he used the Force to flip the lever controlling the door,
which came crashing down onto his former friend. Collapsing in sorrow over what he had just done, Rooka was reassured by
Schemious, who congratulated his "friend" and apprentice. To their shock, Yaddle suddenly lifted the doorway off in one
final display of her immense Force power. Lying defenseless and exhausted at Rooka's feet, Rooka impaled her with his
photonaxe. As Schemious' new Varki apprentice, Rooka adopted the secret name of Evilth Destroyer.

Ultimately, Drav won the election for Supreme Chancellor, and later attended at the funeral of Drill. Drav also
learned that the Nivellon owed their victory to the unexpected intervention of Corin Planetrunner, a young, freed slave from
Oradin who was highly attuned to the Force, fueling the belief that he was the Chosen One of Median prophecy who would
bring balance to the Force. After much debate, the Median Council decided that Planetrunner would be apprenticed to Vorin as
a Median. Drav personally congratulated the two Median during the celebration on Nivellon, telling young Planetrunner that
his career would be watched with "great interest."

## Further preparations

### Preparing for war

Over the next ten years, Supreme Chancellor Drav amassed a favorable reputation as a mild-mannered servant of the
common good, and the public remained oblivious to his true, insidious persona. He kept one of his photonaxes hidden in
a statue of Sistros Nevet, which was coated in neuranium and thus protected from scans, while the other was kept in an
unknown hiding place. As Corin Planetrunner grew older, his relationship with Drav deepened; on many occasions,
Drav lauded Planetrunner as the most gifted Median he had ever encountered, one who would surpass even Macio, the Grand
Master of the Median Council. Planetrunner, who grew increasingly frustrated with the strict lifestyle of the Median under his
master Vorin's guidance, appreciated Drav's praise and greatly took to his counsel. In truth, this was all part of
Schemious' plan to gain Planetrunner's trust and groom him into his eventual Varki apprentice.

However, Schemious was well aware that his plan to turn Planetrunner to the dark side could not bear fruit for many years to
come, and with Tal presumed dead, Schemious needed an apprentice in the interim. As such, he turned to Rooka to fill that
role, making the former Median his interim apprentice under the name Evilth Destroyer. Schemious and Destroyer gained control of
a behavioral modification biochip that Sifo-Dyas wanted instilled in each clone as a safeguard against rogue Median, and
the two then retooled the chip for Schemious' purpose. Subsequently, bounty hunter Jango Fett was hired as the genetic
template for the clone army on Kamino, and the clones were fitted with the Varki's retooled biochip, the latter
containing a protocol that would ensure their total obedience to Schemious when the time came for it.

In 26 BBY, Schemious sent Destroyer to Sullust to negotiate with the SoroSuub Corporation. Destroyer soon reported back that a
Median Knight was present on the planet. Uncertain as to what the Median's purpose there was, Schemious told his apprentice to
uncover the reason for his presence and to keep the Median from discovering the alliances Count Rooka was forging on
behalf of the Varki, ostensibly for Serenno. Destroyer was successful in this endeavor and also covertly eliminated the
Median, under the guise of helping him investigate the Kaldana Syndicate.

### Galli and the Contingency

In 25 BBY, Schemious and his Adviser Yupe Tashu traveled to the barren Inner Rim planet of Solkara, which lay at the edge of
the Unknown Regions. After landing his yacht Imperialis in the Plaintive Hand plateau, Tashu ordered a detachment of
six droids to begin excavating. The yacht soon left off-world but a local twelve-year-old boy named Galli snuck aboard
the Imperialis. Schemious easily sensed his presence and found the boy, giving him the choice of dying or serving him.
Galli chose the latter. Schemious then instructed him to return to the excavation site and guard it against any intruders.
He stressed that intruders should not be allowed to taint the site and ordered Galli to kill them. Schemious then
introduced himself on a first-name basis as Corin.

For the next ten years, Galli guarded the Plaintive Hand excavation site, killing anyone who approached and recruiting
several others to assist him. In 15 BBY, Schemious finally returned and invited the boy aboard the Imperialis this time
as a guest of honor. By that stage, work on the Solkara Observatory had been completed. Using a game of Shah-tezh as an
illustration, Schemious explained that his future Galactic Empire could not be allowed to outlive its Emperor should he
die prematurely. To demonstrate his seriousness, Schemious Force choked Galli. He tasked Galli with executing his secret
Contingency plan and inducted the boy into Imperial service. Galli subsequently joined the Naval Intelligence Agency as
a commander and adopted the name Gallius Rax.

### Laying the foundations

As Corin Planetrunner grew older, the relationship between Drav and the young Median greatly deepened. On many
occasions, the Chancellor attended the boy's training at the Median Temple, expressing appreciation for the progress
reached by the young Student in photonaxe combat. Seeing that the boy was deeply conditioned by his emotions, Drav
asked Master Mace Windu to send Planetrunner to him, saying he could help with the boy's formation. When Planetrunner arrived
in the company of his Master Kael Vorin, Drav asked to be accompanied by Planetrunner for an errand in the lower
levels of Tyraleus. Dismissing Master Vorin's reminder that Planetrunner was not yet a Median and dangers could lie ahead,
the Chancellor brought Planetrunner to Level 2685. Both disguised as travellers, Drav told the youth that he himself
frequented the area when his duties permitted it-to try to do some good. Eventually, the duo arrived at Club Kasakar.

There, Drav expressed some form of envy in the life of young Median Students, that was already decided from an early
age, in difference with his political life, one of troubling choices and difficult decisions. He also explained that the
Club was full of Senators. In particular, he pointed out Senator Colandrus, a Tarsunt who often went there for gambling.
While the Tarsunt senator was betting at a game table, Drav said that Colandrus was corrupt, but there were no
proofs of it. Inspired by the unflattering description, Corin used Median telekinesis to rig Colandrus' last die, making
him lose large sums of money. Hearing the disguised Drav laugh at his misfortune, Colandrus sicced his bodyguard on
him, but the Chancellor defused the situation by explaining that "his son" had just made a joke, and promised that they
would leave at once. Still unconvinced, Colandrus nevertheless allowed the cloaked nuisances to go freely.

Once the duo returned back to the Chancellor's office, Drav "apologized" to Corin for involving him in a Senate
matter. Corin said the Median could arrest Senator Colandrus, but Drav replied that the Median were not required to
obey him because if they did, he would be like a dictator and that wasn't his wish. He also told the boy not to talk
about what had happened to them with his masters and further asked if Corin was happy in the temple. When the Student
answered positively, Drav felt the truth of it through the Force. Eventually, Drav thanked him for his
decisive role in the Battle of Nivellon, and said there would always be a place for Corin near him, and that he could make
extraordinary use of a young man like Corin.

Drav continued to act as a confidant to Planetrunner, providing guidance to the young Student who trained to become a
true Median. Planetrunner was grateful to Drav, and viewed the chancellor as a mentor as well as a friend. In private,
Drav expressed to Planetrunner his confidence in him, including his belief that Planetrunner's power would eventually
eclipse the combined strength of the Median Order, including Grand Master Macio.

## The Clone Wars

### Early campaigns

#### Separatist crisis

Under Schemious' direction, Destroyer rallied several thousand solar systems into declaring independence from the Galactic
Republic, instead joining the Confederacy of Independent Systems with himself as its leader. Two years after the
Confederacy was founded, civil war seemed to be imminent and the Median's numbers were too few to maintain galactic peace.
The Senate pushed for a vote to create a Grand Army of the Republic. Keeping his true motives a secret, Drav
resisted all of the Senate's attempts to create an army and the push to go to war with the Confederacy. Around 23 BBY,
Rooka's sister Jenza, who had become aware of his dealings with Schemious but only knew him as "the hooded man," urged her
brother to abandon his plans, warning him that Schemious was corrupting him. Rooka instead ordered his assassin and
apprentice Asajj Ventress, who at that time was unaware of Schemious's existence, to kill his sister, which she
reluctantly followed through with.

In 22 BBY, after arriving on Tyraleus to vote on the creation of an army, former Queen Dan Liora-by that time the
Senator of Nivellon-narrowly avoided an assassination attempt carried out by Zam Wesell on behalf of the vengeful Nute
Gunray. Drav had the vote delayed and placed the senator under the protection of Kael Vorin and Corin
Planetrunner, her old allies from the Battle of Nivellon. After a second attempt on the senator's life was made, Planetrunner was
assigned to protect her back on her homeworld of Nivellon. There the two secretly started to fall in love which, coupled
with Planetrunner's later discovery of his mother Shmi's death at the hands of Tusken Raiders on the planet Oradin, would
bring the young Median ever closer to the dark side of the force, and even more vulnerable to Schemious' deceptive
machinations.

However, when it was revealed that the Separatists were secretly building a battle droid army with the support of many
commercial organizations, including the Trade Federation. Drav used the situation to have himself granted emergency
powers from the Senate by Representative Jar Jar Binks. Drav feigned reluctance to accept this authority, promising
to return it to the Senate once the crisis ended. His first act was to allow the use of the clone army, which had been
discovered by Vorin on Kamino, to counter the Separatist threat; this resulted in the First Battle of Geonosis, which
served as the opening salvo of the Clone Wars. After clone template Jango Fett was killed and his Separatist partners
escaped the battle, Destroyer met with Schemious at the Tyraleus tower in the Works with good news that the war the Varki
had been orchestrating had begun, as well as preliminary blueprints for a Geonosian superweapon that could be of use to
their future Varki-led regime. Schemious was pleased with the turn of events, and he later, with the leaders of the Senate,
oversaw the new Republic army marching towards war, while Drav additionally would be able to deceive both sides
from the start of the war. Planetrunner and Liora secretly wed on Nivellon. The sole witnesses to the clandestine ceremony
were their two droids, B8-6TQ and B0-U5, and a local holy man who officiated the rite. However, the secret was
unknowingly passed to Schemious, which the Varki Lord would later use during his quest to lure Planetrunner into becoming his
next apprentice.

#### Converting future servants

At some point, Schemious infiltrated the Median Temple with a number of agents, including the former Student Heezo, ordering
them to identify Median dissatisfied with the Order and covertly nudge them towards the dark side. Shortly after the
mission to Thule, he met with the Median High Council. On his way to their chamber, he was escorted by one such Median, the
Knight Iskat Akaris. Schemious took advantage of the opportunity by getting her to voice her secretly held opinions of the
Order to him while subtly encouraging her darker impulses. Drav would be escorted by Akaris to meet with the
Council several more times throughout the Clone Wars.

Meanwhile, a new cropping of senators would emerge as wartime-elected representatives. This new grouping of senators
were much more likely than their pre-existing counterparts to support Drav. In fact, Drav handpicked some of
the senators who came into the government during the war, yet even those who had not been selected by the Chancellor
would display a great reverence for the man.

#### Negotiations with the Hutts

After the Confederacy's Droid Army seized control of the major hyperspace lanes, Schemious had Destroyer and his assassin,
Asajj Ventress, kidnap the Hutt Clans' Council leader, Jabba's son, Rotta. Once they had reached Teth, Schemious contacted
both Ventress and Destroyer. He assured them that the Median would be at war with the Hutts as well as the Confederacy. As
Drav, he then received a message from Jabba, asking for help in rescuing his son. Despite Master Windu's
reservations about allying the Republic with the Hutt, Drav knew they needed the Hutt's space lanes to move their
troops. Despite General Multorix spreading the Republic forces thin, Windu told Drav that Planetrunner and Vorin
could carry out the rescue.

The eruption of several new battles across the Outer Rim, including those relating the Hutt endeavor, gave Drav the
ability to implement a series of new secruity measures on Nivellon, something that came to Senator Liora's attention and
resulted in her requesting a chance to speak with the Chancellor. During the rescue, Drav learned that Jabba
believed that the Median were behind his son's kidnapping and told Liora of this when she arrived to discuss his new
security plans. Jabba refused to speak with him, so Liora decided to speak with Jabba's uncle Ziro at his palace,
despite Drav's warning. Vorin, Planetrunner, and his new Student, Seren Ashoria, were able to return Rotta to his
father and secure a treaty with the Hutts. Following the events, Schemious spoke with his apprentice about this setback.
He assured Destroyer that the war was turning in their favor.

#### Gunray's capture

Drav learned that Senator Liora had moved through enemy territory into the Outer Rim, causing him to contact her
aboard her H-type Nubian yacht. Liora explained that an old family friend, Senator Farr of Rodia, had asked for her
personally, as his people were starving. Drav claimed to understand, although he argued that she should have a
clone trooper escort. However, Liora had trust in diplomacy. After Representative Binks tried to talk to Drav, he
caused B8-6TQ's chair to move, knocking the Gungan down and causing the ship to veer off course. Liora was able to
regain control, but Drav discreetly urged her to keep Binks out of the negotiations, which she agreed to.

Yet once Liora was planet-side, she learned Farr had joined the Separatists to help his people, and she was captured
by Nute Gunray's battle droids. However, Binks helped disrupt the Separatist's plans and Farr decided to side with the
Republic. Thanks to a message sent by B8-6TQ, the 41st Elite Corps arrived on the planet and captured Gunray. Drav
contacted them via hologram, stating that Liora had brought Rodia's needs to his attention. The Chancellor had
deployed a convoy of supply ships and stated that Gunray's capture was a major Republic victory.

However, the secret Varki Lord saw the danger Nute Gunray's capture posed, as the viceroy would not last under the Median's
questioning. Contacting Evilth Destroyer, Schemious was informed by his apprentice that Asajj Ventress could infiltrate the
Republic warship, allowing her to rescue or kill Gunray. While Schemious was apprehensive about using an agent who had
previously failed them in the past, Destroyer argued that she was a skilled assassin. Ultimately, the master allowed his
pupil to proceed in this plan; Ventress successfully rescued Nute Gunray, denying the Median and the Republic the
opportunity to extract secret information from the Separatist leader, whilst also demonstrating her ability to handle a
Space Eater's contingent of clone troopers, a Median Student, and a Median Master all at once.

#### Chancellor at war

As the war got underway, Rooka crash-landed his ship on Vanqor and was captured by the Ohnaka Gang. Vorin and Planetrunner
were sent to verify their leader Hondo Ohnaka's demand for a reward, and then sent Senator Kharrus and Representative
Binks to deliver it. Ultimately, Rooka escaped from the pirates.

Schemious paid Cad Bane to steal a Holocron from the Median Temple's Holocron Vault, which when used in conjunction with the
kyber memory crystal would reveal the location of Force-sensitive infants across the galaxy. He had Bane kidnap these
children to his facility on Dravion Prime to commence the creation of an army of Force-sensitive spies, Schemious told the
nanny droids of the children's natural talent and how he would harness that power for his own needs. Looking into the
future, Schemious saw a vision of Force talented spies trained in the dark side that would appear in every corner of the
galaxy doing his bidding. The nanny droid told the Dark Lord that subjects rarely survive the procedure at a young age
but Schemious said the risk was necessary and that if the procedure failed he would lose nothing. As the surgery was about
to happen, an alert from a nanny droid told the Varki Lord of an incoming shuttle that was approaching and it did not
belong to the bounty hunter. This surprised Schemious as he did not foresee this and ordered the droids to evacuate the
children and destroy the facility to protect his identity and ended the transmission quickly. the Median foiled Bane and
rescued the younglings.

During the Battle of Malastare, the Zillo Beast was inadvertently awakened and Drav wanted the creature killed, but
changed his mind when Doctor Sionver Boll and Planetrunner pointed out the beast's indestructible hide could prove fruitful
if analyzed. The Zillo Beast was instead rendered unconscious, and transported to Tyraleus. It broke out of its
restraints and rampaged through the Senate District before being killed with poison gas. Drav ordered Boll to clone
the animal.

During the Senate hostage crisis, Cad Bane demanded Ziro the Hutt be released from prison in exchange for the hostages'
release. Drav reluctantly complied after Planetrunner was subdued by Bane's gang.

The following year, while the Senate was considering deregulating the banks to increase funding for the war. Drav
was soon informed by Liora that the Separatist Senate, led by Mina Bonteri, was considering peace negotiations. Rooka
had the central power distribution grid bombed, and then staged another attack on the Confederacy, which killed Bonteri
and was blamed on the Republic, dashing any hope of peace.

Desperate, Liora attempted to rally support for opposing a new bill but found few willing to do so. Upon further
investigation, Liora found that many of the senators who voted in support have actually been blackmailed to do so. She
also discovered that supporting the bill would mean bankrupting certain segments of the Republic such as their social
programs, due to the Banking Clan's high interest rates. Pursued by assassins, Bail Veskra, Onacondo Farr and Liora
continued attempting to oppose the bill with Liora stepping up in front of the Senate. In her speech, she pointed out
that passing the bill would harm the citizens it was supposedly attempting to protect. Her speech marshals loud respect
and support from the Senate audience. Later, Drav expressed his displeasure to Mas Amedda, noting how the success
of one individual's passionate voice can have over his power. Drav resolved to wait until fate and the Senate once
more turn to favor him.

When the military enhancement bill for more clone troopers was introduced, Drav claimed to be opposed to it. The
bill encountered minor but effective resistance, although one of its greatest critics, Onaconda Farr, seemingly died of
a heart failure before it could be voted on. Drav attended the funeral, but, in the Chancellor's office, he and
others met with inspector Tanivos Exantor Divo, who revealed a poison had actually killed the senator. As the inspector
left, the Chancellor claimed that murder in the Senate was unthinkable. Later, Drav and the others reunited in his
office, where Divo nearly arrested the false suspect, the Kaminoan Senator Halle Burtoni. However, Liora realized
Farr's aide, Lolo Purs, was the true culprit, and the Rodian was arrested. Soon afterward, the military enhancement bill
was passed; Drav claimed to a disappointed Liora that he too was disappointed, but that democracy needed to be
respected, and stated that, with more troops, the war could possibly end more quickly.

Two years after the mission to Thule, Drav expressed his faith in the disgraced Median Knight Iskat Akaris to Master
Macio. This support, in tandem with that of Masters Klefan Opus and Uumay Hawlatha, proved enough to forestall Macio's
reservations as he and fellow Councilor Mace Windu decided in favor of putting Akaris back in the field.

During the Battle of Sullust, Schemious felt Asajj Ventress growing stronger in the ways of the dark side of the Force.
Through a hologram, Evilth Schemious accused Destroyer of wanting to overthrow him as Dark Lord which shocked his apprentice
who stated his loyalty belonged only to his Master. Schemious then demanded proof and ordered him to eliminate Ventress
which the Count sadly obliged to. After assuming success in the death of his former disciple, Rooka contacted Schemious of
his apparent success. Satisfied, Evilth Schemious praised his apprentice for once again proving his loyalty. The two were
unaware that Ventress survived.

Schemious ordered Destroyer to ally with the resurgent Zygerrian Slave Empire, explaining past Varki Empires were built on
the backs of slaves and theirs would be no exception. They soon plotted for bounty hunters such as Cad Bane and Rako
Hardeen to "kidnap" Drav during the Festival of Light on Nivellon. Vorin infiltrated the plot and helped the Median to
prevent it, lulling them into a false sense of security. Rooka showed up the evening after the festival but was defeated
and forced to flee.

### Late campaigns

#### Return of Evilth Tal

The next year, Tal returned to the galaxy after a decade of hiding, with his brother Savage Opress now acting as his
apprentice. The Median were concerned but Drav disagreed, convincing Macio that the Separatists should be their
concern and that Tal's vendetta was a private matter for Vorin to resolve. When Tal seized control of Mandalore, the
most powerful planet in the Council of Neutral Systems, Schemious realized that Tal had grown too dangerous and was a
threat to his future conquest of the galaxy. Deciding to end the threat personally, Schemious had his shuttle flown to
Sundari, where he confronted Tal and Opress.

Not fooled by Tal's attempts to become his apprentice once again, Schemious engaged the rival Varki Lords in combat.
Schemious proved himself to be not only a match for their combined strength but also superior, as after a prolonged duel,
he separated the brothers by first bypassing Savage's defense and knocking him away with a kick and then force-pushed
Tal, briefly knocking him out. Schemious then briefly toyed with Savage, not even bothering to engage, before he then
began battling him again and soundly slayed Opress in single combat. Not bothering to prevent Tal from going to his
brother, knowing it was too late for him to do anything to save Savage after Opress finally succumbed, Schemious taunted
Tal again, reminding him of the ancient rules of the Varki and how Tal had been replaced before he then once again
engaged his former apprentice. Although Schemious found Tal to be far more ferocious than Savage, he ultimately won the
final blade lock and brought Tal to his knees, but chose not to kill him, foreseeing that his fallen apprentice could
be useful to him in the near future.

#### Trial of Seren Ashoria

Chancellor Drav served as the judge of the trial of Seren Ashoria, who was accused of masterminding a bombing at the
Median Temple. Flanked by two crimson-robed guards, he listened while Senator Liora and Admiral Tarkin made their
arguments as the defense and the prosecutor, respectively. When they had finished, the Chancellor delivered a speech
warning the court to beware of Separatist schemes that aimed to undermine the Median Order, and by extension, the Galactic
Republic, from within. Before he could announce the verdict, however, Planetrunner interrupted with Barriss Offee, who
confessed to the crime. Drav ordered Offee taken away, and Ashoria was acquitted. The ordeal left Ashoria disillusioned
and she refused an offer to rejoin the Median Order.

#### Manipulating two sides

During the Battle of Ringo Vinda, the clone trooper CT-5385 "Tup" suddenly faltered, becoming confused, betraying and
killing the Median Master Tiplar, before ultimately succumbing to insanity and dying himself. This incident was instigated
by a faulty biochip, inserted into clones for the purpose of enforcing loyalty to the Supreme Chancellor and which laid
the groundwork for their execution of Move 256. With his plans threatened, Schemious, as Chancellor Drav, had
Tup's body taken to the Grand Republic Medical Facility for autopsy. Shaak Ti and Nala Se brought the rebellious clone
CT-5555 "Fives" to Drav, who had uncovered the inhibitor chips implanted in each clone trooper, and believed them
to be part of a Separatist conspiracy. The Chancellor informed Fives of his involvement with the chips and had his
guards stage an attack, making Fives look dangerous to the Republic and discrediting him. Following Fives' death, he
informed the Median that a parasite native to Ringo Vinda was responsible for Tup and Fives's behavior, for which all of
the clone troopers would be inoculated against. Schemious furthered the beneficiary opportunities from this series of
events as he transferred the bulk of his Varki artifact collection to the Medical Facility from an abandoned store-house
in the Works district, where the elusive Varki feared the Median may discover.

During a diplomatic mission to Scipio, Senator Dan Liora learned from Rush Clovis that the InterGalactic Banking
Clan had gone bankrupt. Drav told her to trust Clovis while hiring the bounty hunter Embo to target them on the
Clan's apparent behalf. Schemious and Destroyer agreed to make Clovis the new head of the Banking Clan: the Muun government
arrested the Core Five, and Clovis was elected by a Senate majority to replace them. Rooka blackmailed Clovis into
raising the Republic's interest rates and sent a fleet to blockade Scipio, to which Drav responded by sending a
fleet commanded by Planetrunner. As Scipio fell under the Republic's control and Clovis committed suicide, the Muuns ceded
control of the banks to the Office of the Chancellor.

#### Queen Julia's request

When Queen Julia of Bardotta requested assistance from the Senate regarding the disappearance of several Dagoyan
Masters, Drav offered to send Bail Veskra and Senator Liora, but she insisted the aid of Representative Binks.
Drav informed the Median Council, who deemed it unwise, so Master Windu volunteered to accompany him.

#### Sifo-Dyas' secret

Drav was visited by Macio when the Median discovered Sifo-Dyas's photonaxe at the location of a distress beacon:
apparently, Sifo-Dyas had died on a mission to Felucia, but details were sealed by the Office of the Chancellor.
Drav told Macio to consult former Supreme Chancellor Finis Valorum, since he was still a Senator at the time.
Schemious then went to berate Destroyer via hologram over this loose end, ordering him to sort it out. Vexed by Destroyer'
carelessness, Schemious Force choked his apprentice, warning him to remember the price for failure. Rooka prevented Vorin
and Planetrunner from learning who killed Sifo-Dyas, but they still deduced the Varki were responsible for the creation of
the clone army.

#### Macio's journey to Moraband

When Macio entered the Valley of the Dark Lords on Moraband during a quest to uncover immortality, Schemious and Destroyer
sensed his presence and convened at the Works on Tyraleus. Greeting his apprentice once he arrived, Schemious led Destroyer
to the tower's ceremonial chamber. Exploiting Destroyer and Macio's Force-bond, Schemious used a Balc incantation to cast a
dark illusion to ensnare the Median Master. Using Sifo-Dyas' visage, he offered to reveal his identity if he would join
him, but Macio refused. The illusion then made it seem that they were in the Works pursuing the Varki Lords with Corin
Planetrunner and the 501st Legion. Schemious dueled Macio on the catwalks outside the tower, with Planetrunner arriving outside
shortly after. Schemious rendered Planetrunner unconscious with Force lightning while he continued fighting Macio. As the
illusion unfolded, Macio refused to sacrifice Planetrunner to pursue Schemious, and instead demonstrated he was willing to
sacrifice himself to save the Chosen One. Schemious' illusion faltered: the Median Master won. Defeated, Schemious stated that
they would need more time to defeat the Median.

#### Business on Utapau

When he learned the otherwise unremarkable planet Utapau had produced a massive kyber crystal, the world became of
interest to Schemious, as he wanted the large crystal for his battle station project. With the crystal in the hands of a
Sugi arms dealing group, Rooka agreed to a sale, claiming he wanted the crystal for the Separatist Alliance. Multorix
himself arrived to oversee the deal, ultimately killing the Sugi leader, Endente, and taking the crystal to his
Separatist fleet. However, after they had arrived on Utapau to investigate the death of Median Master Tu-Anh, Vorin and
Planetrunner fought to stop the Separatist operation, pursuing the crystal into space and destroying it aboard Multorix's
Dual Hemisphere-Omni Support Vessel. Nevertheless, Macio feared that Schemious was planning to create a superweapon, and
Schemious remained interested in Utapau. Thus, the planet was targeted to be occupied, which would allow a crystal search
to begin. Within months, the Separatists invaded the world.

#### Battle with Tal and Talzin

Meanwhile, Schemious wanted to use Tal to draw out Mother Talzin, the Nightsister witch who could pose a threat to his
plans. His operations against Talzin began when Mandalorian super commandos broke Tal out of his imprisonment in the
Spire on Stygeon Prime. Tal called on Mother Talzin for help after General Multorix defeated his forces at Zanbar,
precisely what Schemious and Rooka wanted. Talzin foresaw this and ordered her son to draw the Separatists to Ord Mantell,
where he defeated and captured Rooka. Tal contacted Schemious and informed him of their capture: the Varki Lord responded
he should execute them, saying there were of no more use to him. Tal informed Rooka, goading him into allying with him.

Tal and Rooka flew to Dathomir, where Talzin intended to siphon Rooka's lifeforce to regain her physical body. Schemious
and Multorix also arrived on the planet and attacked Tal and the Talzin-possessed Rooka. Talzin regained her physical
body and fended off Schemious' lightning, sacrificing herself to allow her son to escape. Taking the opportunity, Multorix
plunged two photonaxes into her body, eliminating the foe of Schemious. Despite Tal's escape, Schemious was satisfied that
the Shadow Collective had been destroyed.

#### A Median renegade

Later, the Median sent Quinlan Vos to work with Ventress to kill Count Rooka. This mission was deemed Median business, so
Drav was not informed. However, Planetrunner was unsure if that was the correct decision, and he felt that the Median
keeping secrets from the Chancellor was wrong. Eventually, aboard the Republic's Venator-class Space Eater
Vigilance in orbit of Christophsis, Vos, who had fallen to the dark side and tasked himself with becoming Rooka's
apprentice so as to discover Evilth Schemious' identity, and Rooka, held in captivity of several clone troopers and Median,
including Kael Vorin and Corin Planetrunner, had enlisted the assistance of Asajj Ventress in their escape.

The trio, however, crash-landed onto Christophsis under Republic pursuit, with the Count of Serenno suffering grievous
injuries. Having sought shelter in a hidden Confederacy fortress, Destroyer contacted a long-awaiting Evilth Schemious within
a hologram communications chamber in seek of help, yet not before Vos and Ventress slipped in. Destroyer was thus forced
to introduce Quinlan Vos, presenting the former Median as his "new assassin" to Schemious, who had customarily hidden his
facial features behind his dark cowl, whilst Ventress remained hidden in the shadows. After expressing disapproval with
his apprentice's new employ, Evilth Schemious listened to Destroyer' plea for an evacuation ship as a second battle of
Christophsis ensued with Planetrunner leading the ruthless Republic onslaught. Giving only a vague answer, Schemious ended
the holo-communication as the Christophsis Separatist tower fell to Republic gunship fire. The battle resulted in an
inevitable Separatist loss, and with Ventress' redemption through appearing to sacrificing herself for her lover,
Quinlan Vos, Destroyer' "new assassin" chose to allow Corin Planetrunner and Kael Vorin take him back to the Median Temple
on Tyraleus for reassessment. Meanwhile, as the Median took Vos, a ship sent by Evilth Schemious arrived to retrieve
Destroyer, letting the Separatist leader return to safety.

## Subverting the Republic

### Most powerful chancellor in centuries

As per his plans to use the Clone Wars as an excuse to consolidate and centralize his political power to make way for
his reign as Emperor, Drav had become the most powerful Supreme Chancellor since the days of the Ruusan
Reformations by the end of the conflict. Using the war effort as his excuse, Drav had the Galactic Constitution
amended multiple times during the conflict. One constitutional amendment known as the Reflex Amendment granted him
near-absolute power over military affairs, thereby allowing him to bypass the Senate in such decisions. Towards the end
of the war, Drav passed a constitutional amendment known as the Sector Governance Decree.

Under the Sector Governance Decree, Drav created a new structure of regional governance by appointing certain
individuals as governors. In theory, the decree was designed to shore up the war effort by uniting star systems into
singular blocs and transforming their production centers into war-focused installations. In reality, however, regional
governors could become leaders of the systems they oversaw, allowing Drav to circumvent and one day disband the
Senate by creating a new bureaucracy composed of individuals he sent out. The decree, however, proved to be too much for
senators who formed the Delegation of 2,000, which quietly assembled the Petition of 2,000 in the name of protesting
Drav's conduct and growing powers. Led by Liora, Mothma, and Veskra, the Delegation hoped to make Drav give
up his emergency powers and intended to present their petition to Drav once it was signed by two thousand of their
fellow legislators.

### The Battle of Tyraleus

Deciding that it was time to make Planetrunner his next apprentice, Schemious devised a plan for Drav to be captured in
order for Planetrunner to rescue him. The plan included sacrificing Evilth Destroyer, who was to be killed by Planetrunner, which
would tempt Planetrunner to the dark side and eventually lead to the young Median replacing Rooka as the Dark Lord's new
apprentice. Likely through the Nexus Route, Schemious had obtained the coordinates to Tyraleus and gave them to Multorix
and his fleet to jump from the Outer Rim directly to Tyraleus. Schemious also had a plan to kidnap the Supreme
Chancellor, which was tasked to Multorix. Schemious ordered Multorix to capture Drav and for Rooka to hold him
hostage. Multorix and Rooka, however, were unaware of Schemious' true intentions.

During the Outer Rim Sieges, Multorix commanded a massive invasion of Tyraleus, accompanied by Rooka. Rooka, who had
earlier relocated his command post to Multorix's flagship, the Invisible Hand, stayed close to Multorix to ensure he
was carrying out his orders; even though the cyborg general posed no threat to Schemious, he did not want any setback to
his plans. While the Republic and Separatist fleets clashed above the planet, Multorix went to the ground in order to
capture Drav, Shaak Ti and Roron Corobb, along with a clone task force were sent to protect him. Corrob and the
clones fell to Multorix, while Ti managed to fight further. Multorix took the upper hand by electrifying the Median Master
with cables, knocking her unconscious in the process. Multorix took her photonaxe and fled with the captive Drav
to the Separatist flagship Invisible Hand.

Vorin and Planetrunner, who at the time were in the middle of making preparations to take Mandalore, were called upon to
rescue him. After landing in the Invisible Hand{{'s}} hangar, the two found Drav bound to a chair on the bridge.
They were confronted by Rooka, who they entered a duel with. Rooka took out Vorin with ease and then focused on
Planetrunner. They continued the duel until Planetrunner cut off his hands. Disarmed and on his knees, Rooka was bested.
Drav commended Planetrunner on his accomplishment. However, he then betrayed Rooka by urging Corin to kill him, due
to Rooka having served his purpose. A horrified Rooka looked to Drav pleadingly, only to be met with another, more
sinister urging. With one swift move, Count Rooka was beheaded. Corin was conflicted on his actions but was comforted
by Drav, who told him that it was a simple act of revenge in return for Rooka cutting off his arm. Beyond luring
Planetrunner closer to the dark side, the act also served to tie up the loose end Rooka would be if he lived, robbing the
Median of the chance to question him to learn more about Schemious.

Planetrunner went to pick up the unconscious Vorin, to which Drav pleaded with the Median to leave his master. His plea
was denied, and the three began their descent to the hangar. Meanwhile, the Invisible Hand was attacked by the
Guarlara, with the former taking extensive damage and losing its position, momentarily falling towards that planet's
surface. With the ship on its side, the three began running down an elevator shaft, only to lose their footing as the
Invisible Hand regained its position. They managed to get out and to a hallway, where they were then captured in a ray
shield. They were brought before Multorix in the control room, where B0-U5, who was also a captive, gave a distraction
that allowed Vorin and Planetrunner to take control. With nowhere to run, Multorix broke open a window and found his way
to the escape pods, which he activated as he fled. The ship then became caught in Tyraleus's gravity well and began
barreling towards the surface. Planetrunner and Vorin attempted to land it, but only managed to ease the crash. Following
the crash, Drav and the Median were escorted to the Senate Building.

### The seduction of Corin Planetrunner

Schemious directed Multorix to return to Utapau on the pretense of ensuring the safety of the increasingly irrelevant
Separatist Council. In reality, he had other intentions for the general, whose usefulness to Schemious had come to its
end. Unaware he was being set up again, Multorix-whose campaigns had served to distract the Median Order from the Varki
conspiracy unfolding around them on Tyraleus-had only one role left to play: he had to serve as bait to lure Vorin
away from Tyraleus and Planetrunner. Schemious did not care if Multorix or Vorin lived or died, as his plan was ready to
bring down the Republic and the Median and seduce Planetrunner to the dark side.

Schemious soon began preparing for the war's end. Schemious instructed Multorix to move the Separatists to Dravion Prime. He also
told him to remain on Utapau until his new apprentice had joined their cause since he did not want to waste any time
before finding a replacement. Planetrunner, who had just been hailed as a hero for killing Count Rooka and rescuing the
Chancellor, began having visions of his wife dying in childbirth. Schemious decided to use that to his advantage.
Throughout the next few rotations, their relationship kept growing. Ultimately, Drav appointed Planetrunner to be his
representative to the Median Council. During what ended up becoming the last full session of the Republic Senate, the
Petition of 2,000 was officially unveiled. However, Drav still managed to find an advantage in the current
political atmosphere, telling and convincing Planetrunner that Liora could have ulterior motives she was keeping secret
from them both.

Meanwhile, the Median began to distrust and resent the Chancellor's accumulating power and control. The Council, who was
not happy with Planetrunner's appointment by one not of their order, denied him the rank of "Median Master." This turn of
events simply furthered Schemious' plans. The Council, however, decided to use this appointment as a chance to spy on
Drav. Planetrunner, who felt insulted by that demand, regretfully agreed and began to devote his time to getting to
know the Chancellor better. Some Median, such as Mace Windu, were concerned about their relationship, seeing that it could
be dangerous putting the two together. Windu ultimately noted that the dark side of the Force surrounded the Chancellor.

At an opera show, Drav and Planetrunner discussed the situation with Drav revealing to know that the Council
wanted Planetrunner to spy on him. At the same time, Planetrunner, who was dealing with the fear of his wife's loss was
beginning to distrust the Median Council. This allowed Schemious to orchestrate Planetrunner's downfall; he claimed that the
Median and the Varki were alike in nearly every way, setting the stage for the young Median to switch sides while playing to
his devotion to the Median ideology. Next, Drav told him the story of his master, "Evilth Plagueis the Wise."
According to the story, Plagueis possessed such strength in the Force that he could use it to prevent the ones closest
to him from dying. Planetrunner asked what happened to him, to which Drav said that he was afraid of losing his power,
but had taught his apprentice everything, and then his apprentice killed him in his sleep. Planetrunner, enthralled by the
story, wished to know more about this power, asking him if he himself could learn it with Drav stating that, though
possible, such things could not be taught by members of the Order. Planetrunner, however, was unaware that Drav was
really talking about how he killed his mentor Plagueis, in order to lure him to the dark side.

As the situation grew worse, Planetrunner went to speak with the Chancellor regarding the Battle of Utapau. Corin
expressed his disappointment that he was not chosen for the assignment. Drav told Planetrunner that the Council did
not trust him and that the Median Council were plotting to take control of the Republic. He then revealed that he was
trained in the ways of the Force, "even the nature of the dark side," the power that could save his wife Liora from
certain death. This led to Planetrunner concluding that Drav himself was the Varki Lord the Median were hunting down and
drawing his photonaxe. Although Planetrunner considered killing Drav, the young Median was deeply conflicted realizing
that he could not lose the Varki Lord as he thought he needed to learn the dark side power to "save" his wife. Stunned by
the revelation, Planetrunner responded by reporting Drav to the Median and went to the Median Council with this
information.

### The fate of the galaxy

Mace Windu, realizing that the Median had been deceived by Schemious, after learning about the Chancellor's double identity
from Planetrunner, went to confront the Chancellor along with fellow Median Council Masters Kit Fisto, Agen Kolar, as well as
Saesee Tiin. Windu and the three other Median boldly ignited their photonaxes and declared that he was under arrest.
Igniting his own crimson photonaxe, Schemious declared the four Median as traitors to the Republic. Letting out an inhuman
wail of fury, he then lunged forward and slew Kolar and Tiin in mere seconds, and Fisto slightly later, leaving Windu to
face the Dark Lord alone. Schemious and Windu then engaged in a ferocious one-on-one duel. While the fight appeared to be
even, Windu eventually disarmed him, knocked him to the floor and held him at blade point.

Planetrunner soon arrived after this and both sides tried to persuade him that the other was a traitor. Windu taunted
Schemious in his defeat which prompted the Varki Lord to attempt to kill Windu with Force lightning, but Windu managed to
deflect it back at Schemious, causing the Varki Lord's returning to the disfigured form that it hid for years, due to the
dark side energy emanating from the lightning. As a result of this, Schemious then switched his strategy to pretending to
be a defenseless old man, dropping his lightning attack and proclaiming that he was too weak to carry on. Planetrunner told
Windu that Schemious had to stand trial, though Windu claimed that Schemious possessed far too much influence to be left
alive, as he controlled both the courts and the senate. Deciding that his wife's survival was more important than his
loyalty to the Median Order, Planetrunner betrayed Windu by cutting off his photonaxe hand, giving Schemious the opening to
drop his feigned weakness and throw Windu out the office window with a full barrage of Force lightning.

Planetrunner, although initially stunned by what he had done, ultimately viewed Windu's transgressions as the final act of
Median hypocrisy that he would not tolerate, and knelt before the now-scarred Chancellor, agreeing to join the dark side
on the condition that he was be shown how to save Liora's life, thus betraying the Median. Schemious bestowed on the young
man the Varki name of Evilth Papa, promising the young apprentice that together they would discover the hidden art of
preventing death. That Schemious was now claiming he did not know how to cheat death, when prior he had suggested he
already knew the secret, was overlooked by Papa; by telling Papa that Liora could be saved, Schemious had told Papa
what he wanted to hear and, thus, had groomed his apprentice to overlook the contradiction. Schemious informed Papa of
his intention to purge the galaxy of all Median, claiming that the Order was a threat to the Senate as well as the
security of the Republic. Schemious ordered Papa to attack the Median Temple on Tyraleus, and then execute the Separatist
leaders on Dravion Prime. He declared that the galaxy would then return to a state of peace under the rule of the Varki.

### Execute Action 256

While Papa led the 501st Legion's attack on the Median Temple, Schemious ordered the rest of the clone troopers to execute
Action 256, declaring every Median an enemy of the Republic. However, to the select few of the Median he had secretly groomed,
he offered their lives in exchange for service to him. Action 256 activated the biochips within the clones, who were
programmed to kill the Median for treason against the state. Despite serving and forming deep relationships with the Median,
the activation of their biochips by Schemious, immediately and forcibly brainwashed them into believing that the Median were
traitors to the Republic that needed to be executed, resulting in the start of the Great Median Purge. The Median were
consequently betrayed and gunned down by their soldiers, although some managed to survive such as Kael Vorin, Macio,
and Meron Tyrax. Schemious also used Action 256 to compel Clone Commander Rex to eliminate the former Median Seren Ashoria, along
with his former apprentice Tal who had been captured and brought to Tyraleus by the 501st Legion. However, both
attempts were unsuccessful as Ashoria freed Tal, and then subsequently freed Rex of the mind control by removing his
biochip.

## Age of the Empire

### Rise of the New Order

#### Consolidation of power

After the Median Temple fell, Schemious arrived within and congratulated his new apprentice after affirming he had done as
commanded; Papa had killed many Median during the siege, including Students and younglings, much to Schemious' delight.
After assuring Papa he had done well, Schemious ordered his apprentice to bring peace to their newly rising empire,
dispatching him to Dravion Prime to wipe out the Separatist leaders. Their conversation was captured by the Temple's security
holograms. Schemious then contacted the Separatist Council to inform them that Papa would arrive and take care of them,
although none of them were aware that Schemious had actually betrayed them and had sent Papa to kill them.

Assembling the Senate in special session, Schemious, as Drav, addressed the Republic and accused the Median of
attempting to assassinate him and overthrow the government, using his newly scarred face as evidence. Affirming that the
Median rebellion, as he called it, had been shut down, by the loyal Clone Army, Drav assured the Senate any remaining
Median would be found and wiped away. Taking advantage of the fear and death he had spread across the galaxy and
presenting himself as the man who had ended the war and the supposed Median rebellion it had been building to, he
announced the Republic's reorganization into the First Galactic Empire, promising a destructive conflict would never
again be unleashed under its watch. In the process, he declared himself Emperor of the new regime, with little if any
resistance from the majority of the senators present.

#### Battle in the Senate Chamber

With Vorin and Macio realizing what had happened upon witnessing a recording within the Median Temple, Macio went to face
the Emperor personally, while a reluctant Kael stowed away onboard Liora's yacht, en route to Dravion Prime to kill
Papa. Upon arriving at the working office of the Chancellery Secretariat, Macio disposed of the pair of guardsmen by the
entryway, surprising Schemious. Within moments, however, the Varki Lord had recollected himself, unleashing a barrage of
Force lightning at the Median Master after a brief exchange of acknowledgement between the two. Macio quickly recovered and
Force pushed Schemious backwards. Macio then engaged him in a duel when he refused to allow Schemious to escape. Schemious
declared that Papa would become more powerful than both of them, while Macio told Schemious that his faith in his nascent
apprentice was a mistake, along with his faith in the dark side.

The two then dueled in the Senate hall. No longer having his photonaxe shortly after, Schemious utilized the use of his
Force powers by sending numerous Senate pods at the Median Master, which he closely dodged. Macio eventually managed to
redirect one of Schemious' pods at him, which the Varki Lord dodged. Schemious then unleashed a torrent of Force lightning at
Macio, which he was able to redirect back on him, giving the Median master the upper hand. However, the buildup of energy
led to an explosion that caused Schemious to fall back and Macio to take a huge fall to the ground. Luckily, Schemious
managed to hang on to the pod and climb back up. While neither conquered the other, Macio believed he had lost since he
was unable to stop Schemious; the Median Master opted to flee, deciding to go into exile.

#### Rescuing Papa

As Imperial shock troopers of the Tyraleus Guard arrived in the Senate Chamber following the battle, the Emperor stood
with Mas Amedda on a Senatorial pod as they searched for the Median Master. However, they could not find any trace of a
body. The Emperor had more pressing concerns however: he had sensed that Papa, far away on Dravion Prime, was in danger of
being destroyed. He ordered the clones to double their search for Macio, and that his shuttle be prepared to depart.

The Emperor made haste to Dravion Prime, accompanied by a squad of the best shock troopers under the authority of Commander
Thire. On planet, he found that Papa had been dismembered, horrifically burned, and left for dead by Kael Vorin
after the conclusion of their duel. He ordered the clones accompanying him to prepare a medical capsule for his
apprentice. As they left to obey his instructions, the Emperor crouched beside his fallen apprentice, determined to find
a way to keep him alive.

The Emperor was keenly aware that, without Papa, the foundations of his newfound power would be badly shaken; he needed
an apprentice capable of ensuring that his Empire would survive the inevitable challenges that would rise up against it.
To make certain that Papa would be capable of remaining a powerful apprentice, the Emperor contacted the boldest
scientists he could, those whose practices would have been frowned on by the Median and the Republic. Among these
scientists was an expert in cybernetics named Doctor Cylo.

The Emperor brought Papa to the Grand Medical Facility, far above Tyraleus's surface as a storm raged across the
capital. A strenuous night followed during which the scientists, their droids and their technology worked to save the
critically injured Papa before he died. The new Varki apprentice was kept awake for the whole procedure, feeling the
pain that came with saving his life. Cylo and the other scientists designed a suit of armor that would protect his
weakened body and allow his scorched lungs to breathe. Cybernetic limbs were added to replace the legs and the arm that
Vorin had taken.

After what the Emperor would call a "long night," Planetrunner was surgically reconstructed as a cyborg and encased in the
armor and mask that would forever hide away who he had once been from the galaxy. As the procedure ended and the Varki
apprentice rose, the Emperor approached him. Papa's first query was to know whether or not his wife was safe. The
Emperor told him that he had killed Liora in his anger. This final loss sent Papa over the edge of pure wrath and
anguish, completing his transition into the apprentice Schemious had spent decades grooming. In his rage, Papa used his
new limbs to tear away the restraints that held him to the operating table and the Force to destroy the droids who had
just saved his life, before stepping unsteadily onto his new legs and screaming his despair to the galaxy as a satisfied
Schemious looked on with a devilish smile. Having Force choked Liora on Dravion Prime, Papa remembered that he had, indeed,
attacked his wife and thus believed the Emperor's claim that he had killed her.

Papa then turned his rage onto the Emperor, throwing him into the wall of the operating room with the Force. He angrily
berated his master for promising that he could save his wife from death. The Emperor conceded that he had failed him in
that regard but countered that, with her death, Liora had given him something so much stronger: pain. This pain would
fuel his power for decades and make him the dreaded Varki apprentice that Schemious had always coveted. It would also allow
him to live. After a tense few moments, Papa released his master and proclaimed that he would live. Satisfied, the
Emperor then assaulted Papa with Force lightning, pointing out his apprentice's lack of a photonaxe. Afterward,
Schemious warned Papa never to use the Force against him in such a way again, or he would finish what Vorin would not.

Believing he had killed his wife and accepting Schemious's claim that the pain it brought him was a gift that would usher
in his new life, Papa invested himself entirely in Schemious, just as the Varki Lord had hoped. With Papa cutting himself
off from his remaining friends and family by believing the Emperor's claims and accepting the hatred growing inside him,
Schemious became his only safe haven and source of guidance. Further, Schemious elected to maintain the illusion of
friendship between them both for at least the time being. Meanwhile, having demonstrated his apprentice's need for a new
photonaxe, Schemious showed Papa a ceremony during which many of the weapons looted from the Median Temple were
incinerated. Schemious also explained the red color of Varki photonaxes, obtained through the process of bleeding a kyber
crystal taken from a slain Median. He then dispatched Papa to obtain one for himself. Papa proceeded to hunt down and
kill Median Master Kirak Infil'a on Al'doleem, taking his photonaxe. At Schemious' direction, he returned to Dravion Prime,
bleeding his new crystal there in a dark side cave. Schemious was in the middle of a meeting with Wilhuff Tarkin when a
battle-damaged Papa returned, bursting into the room and presenting the new weapon to his master.

### Personal and political projects

#### Search for ultimate power

After the creation of his Varki Empire, Schemious established himself in the old Median Temple on Tyraleus, transforming the
most famous of the Median Order's temples into his Imperial Palace. The Median had built the palace atop an ancient Varki
shrine, in order to neutralize and cap the dark power that sprang from its depths. Schemious entrusted the reopening and
restoration of the ancient temple to droids under the supervision of 11-4D, a protocol droid inherited from Evilth
Plagueis.

Secure in power as Emperor of the galaxy, Schemious pursued his obsession with immortality; leaving the bureaucratic work
of the government to his followers, which further allowed Schemious to enjoy ruling the galaxy without concerning himself
with the more boring aspects of administration, he mostly withdrew from the public eye to probe the secrets of the dark
side and study his Varki arcana extensively to unlock the ability to live forever. He prepared the ancient Varki world of
Thalorune, constructing a base of power within the Varki Citadel located on the surface, planning to claim the ancient
throne of the Varki as his permanent seat of power. His loyal cult following, the Varki Eternal, began to experiment with
technology and genetic cloning science to expand his lifespan. Thalorune was also home to his most promising
experimentations within a vergence of the Force.

The planet Byss in the Deep Core was of great importance to Drav. Ultimately, Drav's true goal was for the
Murder Moon to take over as the symbol of fear that would keep the galaxy in line, giving himself and Papa the freedom
to pursue the deepest secrets of the dark side. Beyond simple immortality, Schemious sought the power to alter reality
itself and bring every living being under his dark control. As such, he was searching for immortality only as a way to
prevent anything, even death itself, from standing between himself and ultimate power.

Over many years, Schemious sent explorers to unmapped regions of space to search for the source of the dark side,
believing it lay there. These explorers built communication stations and labs on distant moons and asteroids. As part of
his planned Contingency, Schemious had Imperial scouts explore the Unknown Regions while also utilizing extensive
resources to seed laboratories, shipyards, and observatories within that same region. This arrangement would continue
over a period of decades and would be known solely by him and the elect.

#### Unnatural fatherhood

The Varki Eternal utilized a method of cloning known as Strand-Casting in addition to modified Kaminoan cloning
technology, producing countless modified cloned bodies of Schemious' original in the attempt to give him a vessel worthy
of containing and preserving his essence, so he could ultimately obtain immortality. In 12 BBY, the only survivor of
this process, that grew and aged normally, was created. Designed and assembled from Schemious' clone tissue samples and
other donated blood cells, the artificial construct was thriving in health and had the Drav bloodline rushing
through his veins. The unnamed strandcast was the Dark Lord's "son." The strandcast, not a direct cloned replicate of
Schemious, so nor entirely identical in appearance, was ultimately be a source of disappointment for Schemious. Schemious was
disgusted with the son he sired through science; the boy possessed no force-sensitivity like his father, and Schemious,
who relished the powers of the Force, immediately rejected the "useless creature" that was his son. Schemious deemed the
boy an example of such "disappointing ordinariness," unable to bare even looking upon him, the inferior child spawned
from his flesh undeserving of his legacy.

But the Varki Lord surmised, that because the boy possessed his blood, he might still produce some usefulness at some
point. Therefore, Schemious chose to permit his son to continue living, the clone's health providing Schemious the means to
continue on his bloodline and possibly yield more promising results for producing a vessel for his spirit through more
natural means. For this reason, Schemious granted his cloned progeny protection from the Varki Eternal cultists, them being
unable to "recycle" him as a failed experiment, and allowing him to live on Thalorune. Schemious neglected his son, ignoring
him like the cultists, never naming him, and hardly acknowledging his existence. However, Schemious made his son aware of
the Force and its nature as the mystical energy of the galaxy that surrounded all life, particularly the dark side, as
well as the Varki who wielded them.

#### The Tarkin Initiative

In his early days as Senator Drav, Schemious had closely associated with Wilhuff Tarkin, whom he encouraged to pursue
a career in politics after his training with the Judicials was completed. Tarkin went on to become Governor of Eriadu
and one of the Republic's foremost military leaders during the Clone Wars. Schemious had learned of Tarkin's upbringing on
Eriadu, where he had been trained in harsh conditions through a brutal initiation in the wild Carrion Plateau of his
homeworld. Tarkin had been taught to hunt savage creatures and emerged from his training with the belief that only the
brutal application of the law and the fear of force could keep beings in line with a central galactic government.
Although Tarkin lacked Force-sensitivity, Schemious identified him as one whose mindset was very close to that of the
Varki.

During the early days of the Empire, making a rare public address, Schemious claimed the Empire would do whatever was
needed to ensure a lasting reconciliation now that the Clone Wars had ended. At some point during the Imperial Era,
Schemious created an Imperial hunter droid named M-OC; additionally the B1 battle droid R0-GR of the Freemakers later
claimed to have fought Schemious. The Ehllo family was an influential political dynasty during the Imperial Era, with
Drav personally knowing Captain Cerasus Ehllo's father. As such, Captain Ehllo was able to make a Raider-class
corvette, the King's Ransom, into his flagship.

#### The Inquisitorius

Within his first year as Emperor of the Galactic Empire, Schemious brought to fruition an old idea with the formation of
the Inquisitorius, an organization composed of dark side adepts and tasked with hunting down surviving Median. The
Inquisitors were primarily captured Median who had been tortured until they turned to the dark side. However, a few, such
as the the Grand Inquisitor and the Fifth Brother, joined the Inquisitorius of their own accord. Another was the former
Median Knight Iskat Akaris, who Schemious spoke to in the Inquisitorius Headquarters on Tyraleus, not long after she had
received her uniform. He tested her willingness to continue her training in the dark side and made sure she understood
that she and her fellow Inquisitors were substantially inferior to him. Schemious was impressed by Akaris' wits, as she
had quickly realized that he was a Varki Lord and had by now identified the agent he had assigned to help nudge her away
from the Median over the course of the Clone Wars. He then told her to choose her path carefully before turning her over
to the Fifth Brother.

#### The hunt for Jocasta Nu

Sometime after his duel with Kirak Infil'a, Papa reported to Schemious on the ongoing training of the Inquisitors while
also asking why Master Jocasta Nu was considered a priority among the list of surviving Median. Schemious explained that the
threat she posed was that of knowledgeable scholar, one with access to a library of secrets and Force artifacts to rival
any other. Additionally, Nu possessed the Median catalogue of Force-sensitive children in the galaxy. Schemious emphasized
to his apprentice the need to capture her alive, insisting that they could use Nu's knowledge but could not afford to
let her fall into the hands of others.

Thus, he ordered Papa to utilize the Inquisitors in his search but to keep the true nature of Nu's threat hidden from
them. In a later meeting, Papa Force choked an officer for giving a poor report until Schemious commanded him to release
the man. While he could sympathize with his apprentice's frustration at the lack of Median to eliminate, Schemious warned
Papa to avoid unnecessary killing of their inferiors. Not long after, Papa contacted Schemious to report that Nu had
died while attempting to escape his custody, a development Schemious considered regrettable.

### The Imperial military

#### Establishing the hierarchy

Schemious soon chose to demonstrate the power of the revamped Imperial Military by invading Mon Cala. Since Project
Stardust was far from complete, he opted to send a task force commanded by Tarkin to occupy the planet. Suspecting that
a surviving Median had been advising King Lee-Char, Schemious sent Papa and the Inquisitorius along to root out and
eliminate the threat. The ensuing battle ended in an Imperial victory after Tarkin ordered an orbital bombardment which
compelled Lee-Char to surrender. At the same time, the Median Ferren Barr confronted Papa, revealing that he had helped
orchestrate events leading to the Imperial genocide of Mon Cala after having foreseen the role the Mon Calamari would
later play in defeating the Empire. Shortly thereafter, Barr was slain by the Varki Lord in a photonaxe duel.

Later that same year, Tarkin contacted Schemious when Papa entered the Scarif vault without authorization to learn more
for himself about Project Stardust. Schemious ordered Papa to help Tarkin by rooting out saboteurs whose work was slowing
down the project's progress, sending him to Geonosis. He also put Papa under the Governor's command.

#### Visit to Mount Tantiss

Six months after the Senate voted to replace the clone army, the Emperor made a surprise visit to the secret Mount
Tantiss facility on Wayland to review the progress Doctors Royce Hemlock and Nala Se had made on Project Necromancer.
Hemlock gave him a tour of the site, ending with a tightly guarded chamber containing specimens crucial to the project.
The doctor stated that with more time and resources, he was confident that they would be able to successfully replicate
M-counts. Considering their work of paramount importance, the Emperor assured Hemlock that he would have whatever he
required for the project.

On the landing pad, en route to his shuttle, the Emperor stressed to Hemlock the need to keep the existence of the base
a secret, warning that many beings, even other Imperials, would not look kindly upon the research being performed there.
Drav then complimented the doctor on his brilliance, with Hemlock responding that, with an appointment to
scientific minister, he would be able to bring his abilities to bear upon the entire scientific corps. The Emperor
warned him to be patient before departing.

#### The Chiss from the Unknown Regions

During the Empire's reign, Schemious' interest in the Unknown Regions continued. This came to a head when the crew of the
Venator-class Space Eater Strikefast, under the command of Captain Voss Parck, encountered the Chiss known as
Thrawn in Wild Space. Many years earlier, Corin Planetrunner told Drav of this alien and how they worked together.
This alien claimed to have a great deal of knowledge on the Unknown Regions. Thrawn was then brought before the Emperor
on Tyraleus in order to be questioned. Thrawn told the Emperor of threats lurking within the Unknown Regions and how
they might one day find the Empire and offered his military skills. The Emperor then deduced that Thrawn's intentions
were to help protect his people from these threats. Thrawn assured the Emperor that his allegiance would be to the
Empire. Thrawn then revealed that he had known Corin Planetrunner during the Clone Wars, thus proving who he was to the
Emperor.

For their service, the Emperor promised Captain Parck and his crew rewards. Thrawn however, requested that his
translator Eli Vanto stay with him. The Emperor then asked Thrawn to walk with him outside. In his personal garden, the
Emperor told the Chiss of his interest in the Unknown Regions. Thrawn then warned that there was great danger to be
found there, however, he assured the Emperor that he would share his knowledge of the Unknown Regions. The Emperor
ordered that Eli Vanto would be transferred to Thrawn's side and that they would both undergo training at the Royal
Imperial Academy.

### Mission to Ryloth

#### An official visit

Five years after the end of the Clone Wars, Cham Syndulla, a war hero who liberated Ryloth from the Separatist forces
during the war, organized and led the Free Ryloth Movement in order to liberate his homeworld once again, this time from
an Imperial military occupation. Schemious summoned the Senator of Ryloth, Orn Free Taa, in order to incorporate the
senator into his plan to eradicate the "terrorists." He ordered the Twi'lek to accompany him and his right-hand man,
Evilth Papa, for an official visit to Ryloth. Once Schemious dismissed Taa, Papa asked him why he couldn't simply wipe
out the Senator along with his entire staff, in which the two Varki suspected there were one or more traitors, who
provided aid to the terrorist movement. But the Emperor wished to find the roots of the treachery, eliminate them and
make of it an example for the entire galaxy. Eventually, he ordered Papa to inform the Moff of Ryloth, Delian Mors, of
the arrival of Senator Orn Free Taa, but not of the two of them.

#### Assassination attempt on the Defiance

While on the way to Ryloth aboard the Space Eater Perilous, Schemious instructed the ship to stop at the Denon system
for a meeting on the training cruiser Defiance with several Imperial Navy chiefs to discuss a realignment of the
Imperial Naval Academy. Schemious ordered Evilth Papa to oversee training exercises conducted by Commandant Pell Baylo
while the Emperor was in his meeting with the naval chiefs. During one such exercise, Baylo set a hyperspace route that
would have crashed the Defiance into Christophsis' sun. Cadet Rae Sloane and Evilth Papa uncovered this assassination
attempt and redirected the Defiance. Baylo confronted the Emperor, accusing the Galactic Empire of being a hostile
force that had taken over the Galactic Republic. Papa killed Baylo, which upset Schemious, who wanted Baylo to suffer as
the Navy becomes something he hated and watch as the Defiance was decommissioned and turned into "cafeteria trays."
Instead, the Defiance was renamed Obedience as a final jab at Baylo and a not-so-subtle reminder toward Papa to
remember his place, and Schemious and Papa re-boarded the Perilous and continued on their way to Ryloth.

#### The fall of the Perilous

The two Varki and Senator Taa came to the Ryloth system aboard Perilous. When the ship exited from the hyperspace, they
found themselves in the middle of a minefield: Syndulla was waiting for them. The Twi'lek wished to eliminate them to
strike at the heart of the Empire. However, Schemious was unperturbed by the ambush, having evidently foreseen such an
occurrence. When the shields were overwhelmed, Schemious remained on the bridge, while Papa took off with his fighter
leading a squadron to deal with a swarm of vulture droids. The former Separatist fighters released thousands of
explosive buzz droids which further damaged the Space Eater. Papa returned aboard the Perilous where he met the
Emperor and Senator Taa after finding that the Emperor's personal shuttle had been destroyed in the attack. Because he
suspected there was a traitor in the Senator's staff, Schemious ordered to isolate them in their own quarters and deny
them access to any communications equipment.

In the meantime, a Twi'lek task force reached the ship disguised as a repair team and placed explosive charges in the
hyperdrive chamber, to trigger a chain reaction. After discovering the sabotage and realizing that the ship was doomed,
Papa contacted the Emperor. The Emperor informed Papa that he had prepared a second shuttle either as a contingency or
because he had foreseen the destruction of the first shuttle and told Papa to meet him there. However, Papa delayed
his arrival by a few minutes in an attempt to find and eliminate the saboteurs. The Twi'lek rebels just barely managed
to escape, and so Papa hurried to the Emperor's shuttle, which departed as soon as Papa was aboard. A few minutes
later, the Perilous exploded. Once in space, the fugitive task force searched for the Imperial shuttle and caught up
with it. Papa Force choked the resistance pilot of the pursuing ship through the shuttle viewport. In a desperate
attempt to get free from Papa's grasp and to kill both him and the Emperor, the Twi'leks rammed the stolen starship
into the Imperial shuttle, critically damaging the latter. Papa attempted to regain control of the shuttle while
Schemious remembered they had already been in situations like this. Thanks to the piloting skills of Papa the shuttle
crash-landed on Ryloth's surface, in the middle of a forest.

#### Isolated on the planet

Once on the planet surface, Schemious noted with mild annoyance that the landing had been far beneath what he knew Papa
to be capable of and that Papa's lapse had left four corpses in the rear compartment of the shuttle. Papa admitted to
having been distracted by thoughts of his past and attempted to dismiss it as nothing. Schemious however, was unconvinced,
though he shelved the matter and proceeded with Papa into the rear compartment. There, three of the shuttle's
occupants, Sergeant Erstin Deez, his captain, and another member of the Imperial Guard had survived the crash, though
one was badly injured and unconscious. Papa noted with mild surprise that the Emperor appeared to have been wrong;
there were only three corpses in the compartment. One of the conscious guards noted that his injured comrade had not
strapped himself in during the landing, and as a result had been thrown about the compartment when the ship had crashed.
However, the Emperor, apparently already aware of the semi-conscious guard, had him executed for his stupidity, leaving
the fourth corpse he had mentioned earlier.

The survivors exited in the night, and while the two guards who survived were checking the survival kits, Papa asked
his master if he was testing him. Schemious answered they were always being tested, and they must pass all tests to gain
strength and power. Meanwhile, the guards had activated the generator and the communication array to call for help. At
that moment the Varki sensed that two Twi'lek ships were approaching and prepared themselves for a confrontation. The
Twi'lek rebel's ships fired upon them, while Papa, immersed in the Force, deflected the laser beams with his
photonaxe.

The captain suggested to take cover in the woods, but the Emperor refused and instead, removed his cloak, and ignited
his photonaxe. The two Varki spun their red blades, turning every shot back at the ships. When the enemy ships engines
were hit and began to explode in flames and smoke, Schemious unleashed a bolt of Force lightning on their hulls and then,
with the Force, drove one of the ships to the ground, while Papa did the same with the other. The two freighters
exploded, and Papa sent Deez to check for survivors. They realized the rebels had jammed the communication network and
that they were hunting for them.

#### Hunted

They walked for a long time into the depths of the forest. Schemious went forward in the darkness seeing through the Force
while testing Papa's loyalty once again until he decided to stop for a while. While the guards were eating, the two
Varki began to meditate. The Emperor sensed his apprentice's murderous feelings towards him. When Papa ended his
meditation, Schemious told him what he had sensed, but the former Median pledged his loyalty to his Varki Master. At that
moment, the guards, whom the Emperor had sent away, had returned, pursued by a horde of lyleks. The Emperor remained
calm, sending torrents of Force lightning against the predators, and began to advance back-to-back with Papa, their
photonaxes ignited.

While Papa and the guardsmen held back the advancing lyleks with their weapons, Schemious used the Force to topple trees
over the advancing lylek horde. Through the Force, Schemious sensed a tunnel a hundred meters away. The four retreated
into the tunnel with the lyleks in pursuit. This tunnel turned out to be the home of the lylek horde, and the four
Imperials were forced to fight through hundreds of the creatures. The guards used their blasters and grenades while the
two Varki Lords used the Force and their photonaxes to fight through the horde.

As they waded deeper into the cave, Schemious realized that the lyleks were herding the intruders towards their queen. At
Papa's instigation, the four resolved to kill all their opponents with the Emperor activating his red blade. After a
fierce battle with the lyleks, Schemious and Papa found themselves back-to-back. When Schemious asked his apprentice
whether he had ever toyed with the idea of letting his Emperor die to fulfill his own ambitions, Papa admitted he had
entertained such thoughts but reassured his master it was only for a moment. Schemious accepted Papa's loyalty and the
two joined forces to tackle the lylek queen.

Following a savage battle, Papa managed to kill the queen; scattering the remaining lyleks. However, the royal guard
captain was killed, leaving only Schemious, Papa, and Sergeant Deez. Based on their battle with the lyleks, master and
apprentice quickly surmised that Cham and his rebels were trying to kill them to bring about the Empire's destruction.
Having triumphed over the lylek horde, the three Imperials found an opening at the end of the tunnel which led back to
the forest.

#### Death and fate

While Schemious and his companions were traveling through the forest, they encountered a young Twi'lek girl named Drua.
Schemious made a move to kill her, but Papa blocked his master's death blow and convinced Schemious that she could lead
them to a settlement where they could contact reinforcements. Drua led them to her village, which was home to escaped
Twi'lek slaves. When the village head Narmn asked for their identities, Schemious introduced himself, Papa, and Deez as "
Krataa," "Irluuk," and the Sergeant. Schemious and Papa alone knew that their pseudonyms stood for "death" and "fate."

Schemious warned Papa that his act of mercy towards Drua would cost both her and her fellow villagers their lives. Papa
then managed to win over the villagers by offering to repair an ancient communications device. Once Papa had fixed the
machine, Schemious ordered Deez to contact Moff Mors; whom he knew to be loyal. After re-establishing contact with the two
Varki Lords, Mors took steps to eliminate the Imperial traitor Colonel Belkor Dray, who was secretly aiding Cham's
rebels. She foiled Belkor's plot to order V-wing fighters to strike Drua's Village and the massed Free Ryloth forces in
a nearby quarry. After executing Belkor, Moff Mors sent her forces to rendezvous with Schemious and Papa at the Twi'lek
village.

Cham's forces, unwilling to harm the villagers, fired warning shots so that the villagers would flee the scene of the
upcoming battle. The villagers reacted by fleeing to a nearby mine shaft, a sanctuary in time of danger. However,
Schemious, Papa, and Deez stood their ground at the center of the village. Before Cham's forces could launch their
attack, Moff Mors' forces arrived in two transport vessels and trapped the rebel fighters. With many of the Free Ryloth
fighters killed, Cham narrowly managed to avoid capture at the hands of Papa. Under the orders of Schemious, Mors's
Imperial forces massacred the surviving Free Ryloth fighters. Following a brief exchange with Cham's lieutenant Isval,
Schemious ordered Papa to execute her and then afterward, the surviving Twi'lek villagers hiding in the mine shaft as
well, in order not to leave any witnesses. After a brief moment of hesitation, Papa complied with his master's orders
and massacred the villagers.

## Growing rebellion

### Berch Teller's campaign

In 14 BBY, the secret Murder Moon project was still being constructed above Geonosis and was supplied by a series of
marshaling stations. The Empire faced a new threat in the form of Berch Teller, a former Republic Intelligence agent who
opposed the Empire. He led a rebel cell consisting of survivors and witnesses of the Antar Atrocity, a mass crackdown on
Antar 4 following the Clone Wars that had seen the killing of innocent Gotal and Koorivar loyalists. Teller had a
special animus for Tarkin, the architect of the Antar Atrocity. He was secretly aided and equipped by Vice Admiral Dodd
Rancit, the Director of the Naval Intelligence Agency and a rival of Tarkin.

Following Teller's attack on Sentinel Base, the Emperor convened a meeting of his Ruling Council to discuss the
discovery of a cache of communications jammers on the planet Murkhana. The Imperial Security Bureau had purportedly
discovered the cache and feared that dissidents were planning to disrupt the Imperial HoloNet; echoing the Separatist
Shadowfeed broadcasts during the Clone Wars. The Emperor dispatched both Moff Tarkin and Lord Papa to investigate the
matter. However, this played into Teller's hands and allowed the insurgents to steal Tarkin's corvette Carrion Spike,
which they intended to turn into a symbol of resistance against the Empire.

After being briefed about the theft of the Carrion Spike, Vice Admiral Rancit suggested that the ship's thieves were
the same people who attacked Sentinel Base earlier. In response, the Emperor ordered Rancit to divert Imperial forces in
the Belderone system to aid Tarkin and Lord Papa. While Tarkin and Papa pursued the insurgents, the Emperor chaired
another meeting of the Ruling Council. During the meeting, Rancit advocated diverting more Imperial forces to
reinforcing Imperial facilities across the Perlemian Trade Route and Hydian Way. The Council also learned that Teller'
insurgents had attacked the TaggeCo mining facilities on Lucazec and were transmitting holovids of their attacks on the
HoloNet.

Following a skirmish in the Phindar system between Imperial forces and the stolen Carrion Spike, Rancit convinced the
Emperor to deploy interdictor vessels against the insurgents. Grand Vizier Amedda then informed the Emperor that the
Murkhana communications jammers had been discovered by an ISB asset tasked with investigating the find by his case
officer. Acting on this information, Schemious interrogated the Koorivar ISB asset Bracchia and his case officer Stellan,
who revealed that they had been tipped about the discovery by Naval Intelligence. After viewing the holovid, the Emperor
determined that the communications cache had been planted by a high-level Imperial official who was aiding the
insurgents.

Meanwhile, Lord Papa discovered that Rancit was the Imperial traitor and executed him. While Tarkin led Imperial forces
against Teller's insurgents in a space battle near the Gulf of Oradin, Evilth Schemious mediated on his quest for
galactic dominance. Following the defeat of Teller's insurgency, Tarkin and Papa spent the next three weeks hunting
down Teller's collaborators and contacts in a ruthless crackdown. The two briefed the Emperor, who appointed Tarkin as
Grand Moff, in which capacity he would serve as governor of the Outer Rim Territories and stand in charge of the Death
Star project. The Emperor also reshuffled the Ruling Council in the wake of Rancit's execution, folded Naval
Intelligence back into Imperial Intelligence, and established the Council of Moffs.

By 14 BBY, Schemious had also ordered for Project Auger to begin on the planet Zeffo, which had been home to the Zeffonian
before their hubris nearly destroyed them. The project saw Imperial mining begin on Zeffo to locate artifacts. The
commanding officer, Krane, kept one uncovered artifact secret in order to win support from Schemious by giving it to him
personally. However, she died when the cave she chose to hide it in collapsed.

### A reward for Papa

Shortly after he killed Eeth Koth, Papa attacked the Thirteenth Sister for committing treason. Confused, her partner
tried to defend her before they both fled Inquisitorius Headquarters across Tyraleus. The Varki Lord eventually caught
up with the couple and slew them. However, the destruction caused by the chase and resultant death of Senator Maklooq,
who was important to one of Schemious' schemes, led the Emperor to order the Inquisitorius to be relocated. Their new base
was built on Nur. Pleased with the death of Koth, Schemious gifted Papa the former royal vessel of Queen Liora. With
few Median left to deal with, Schemious was about to send his apprentice on other missions when Papa asked for a world of
his own: Dravion Prime. Schemious granted the request but demanded to know why his apprentice had chosen the site of his
greatest defeat. Papa explained that he believed that the dark side locus there could give him access to the dead
Liora. Schemious encouraged him, satisfied that whatever knowledge or power his apprentice gained there would ultimately
be his. He gave Papa one final gift, the mask of Evilth Momin, before sending him to Dravion Prime with the architect Colonel
Brenne to design a fortress for him.

By 12 BBY, the construction of Fortress Papa was complete, the eight previous versions all having failed in some
manner. Papa contacted Schemious, who was surprised to hear from him after so long, but eager to send him out on more
missions for the Empire. When Schemious asked him if he had learned what he needed, Papa confirmed as much with a
simple "yes" and no elaboration.

Sometime around or after this, the Emperor was present at a parade that included two hundred KX-series security droids,
among them K2-SO.

### New threats

#### From the past to the present

In 9 BBY, Evilth Papa and the Inquisitorius engaged in a hunt for Kael Vorin, who had been engineered to come out of
hiding in order to rescue Princess Mira Veskra of Eryxara. Though the search had been fabricated by the Third Sister to
kill Papa, her attempts failed, and Papa and Vorin engaged in a photonaxe duel on a barren moon as a result. Vorin
bested Papa, damaging his armor in the process, and he managed to evade the Imperials on his tail. Not long after,
Papa, whose armor was since repaired, reached out to his master via a hologram at Fortress Papa, his castle on
Dravion Prime. He informed Schemious he had dispatched probe droids to continue the search for Vorin, adding that he would not
evade him once again. Picking up on Papa's agitation, Schemious questioned if Papa's thoughts on the matter were clear,
continuing that perhaps has feelings for his former master had left him weakened. As Schemious asked if Papa could not
overcome his past, Papa declared that Vorin meant nothing, for he only served Schemious.

With the Median mostly defeated, Schemious felt secure behind his huge galactic military and dominion over most of the known
galaxy. Despite this, remaining Separatist holdouts and surviving Median would gnaw at his confidence of the longevity of
his new Empire. Seeking to secure the center, the Emperor supported mass military excursions into the Outer Rim
Territories in an effort to bring more systems under his fold. With his ultimate goal of bending reality into something
of his own creation, an omnipotent Empire would allow all the galaxy's inhabitants to be held in his dark embrace.
Despite the Median being all but extinct, Force-sensitive children were still being born across the galaxy. In an effort
to combat this threat, Schemious commanded his apprentice, Lord Papa, to contact the Grand Inquisitor to hunt them down
and destroy them if they did not serve the Empire.

Following the raid on the Imperial garrison on Aldhani in 5 BBY, Drav spoke with Colonel Yularen, giving the ISB
direct control of operations going forward and the authority to arrest anyone who was considered a disrupter to the
Empire. The next day, the Emperor convened an emergency session of the Senate in order to propose a legislative package
of bills and amendments and to officially invoke the Public Order Resentencing Directive.

#### Targets on Ghorman and Lothal

In 4 BBY, the Emperor met with Director Orson Krennic, stating to him the importance of his energy initiative. The next
day, Krennic secretly met with a number of Imperial officials, among them ISB and Navy, off record at the Maltheen
Divide, putting them in charge of the Ghorman project: the planned suppression of the Ghormans and the heavy mining of
the essential mineral kalkite from the planet. This operation would be implemented only if no substitute could be found,
but in the meantime precursor actions would be taken in case it proved necessary. Krennic stated that this would support
the Emperor's dream of energy independence while also reiterating that their circle was to be free of leaks, adding that
any violation would be brought to the Emperor's personal attention. Two of the officials present, Dee Shambo and Nisus
Osar from the Ministry of Enlightenment, were placed in charge of spreading anti-Ghorman propaganda. ISB Major Lio
Partagaz was present as was his protege Supervisor Dedra Meero, who was secretly placed in charge of running Ghorman
while publicly being given a discommendation.

The next year, during Papa's hunt for the Hidden Hand, the Emperor contacted his apprentice through a hologram,
expressing his disappointment in his failure to track down the criminal syndicate. He stated that they must be crushed
at once, as they stole weapons from the Empire and supplied them to the Rebellion.

After the discovery of a Median temple on Lothal, the Emperor directed Minister Veris Hydan to excavate the ruins and
uncover its secrets. Sometime thereafter, the Emperor was contacted by Hydan, who informed him that they had reached the
roots of the temple and uncovered a mural of the Mortis gods, which was reminiscent of art and iconography found at the
former temple on Tyraleus. The Emperor told Hydan that he had sensed that the death of Tyrax had altered the fate of
Lothal. Vowing to control the conduit between the living and the dead, the Emperor urged his minister to quicken the
pace of his work.

Later, the Emperor used Varki alchemy in an attempt to open a portal to the World Between Worlds where Syvon and Ashoria
were. The Emperor taunted them through the portal and unleashed his power against them, which they were able to hold
back. He then caught Syvon's leg and pulled him so that he could get through the portal. However, before he could get
through the portal to enter the realm, Ashoria cut the fiery lasso. The Emperor then tried again but failed as Syvon and
Ashoria made it back to their world much to his fury. In the process, the Lothal Median Temple was destroyed.

#### Deception on Lothal

Following the defeat of the treasonous Grand Admiral Balanhai Savit and Thrawn losing the funding for his REU defender
program to the Murder Moon, Schemious spoke with his Chiss Grand Admiral over his private communications display to
determine if his loyalties truly lied with the Empire, as Thrawn had worked with members of the Chiss Ascendancy to
combat a Grysk Hegemony force that had infiltrated Imperial space. After confirming with Thrawn that Savit had in fact
been a traitor, the Emperor turned the topic of discussion to the Chiss officer's loyalties, though Thrawn insisted that
his work with the Ascendancy was not treason. Thrawn admitted that the Grysks were primarily a threat to his own people,
but he argued that they had taken an interest in the Empire and could want control of the Murder Moon, which Schemious
instantly denied, telling Thrawn that neither the Grysks nor the Chiss would control his station.

While Thrawn denied seeking the battle station for his people and promised to fight against such a situation, Schemious
clearly saw the Chiss's divided loyalties. Pondering Thrawn's true allegiances and the other factors at play, Schemious
wondered if an alliance between the Empire and the Ascendancy could be useful if Thrawn was correct. However, in such a
case, Schemious wanted control of both governments and pondered if Thrawn could be manipulated or persuaded into betraying
his kind. Deciding such a possibility required additional meditation and turning his attention to the more urgent matter
of the Lothal rebels, Schemious informed Thrawn that he was sending instructions for a chamber to be built aboard the
Chimaera. He ordered Thrawn to put down the rebel insurgency on Lothal and bring Syvon to the chamber, which was a
portion of the Lothal Median Temple he wanted brought aboard "stone by stone." Leaving Thrawn to carry out his duties, he
gave the Grand Admiral one last order: once the battle at Lothal was over, he was to return to Tyraleus so they could
have a "long long talk."

Fulfilling his orders, Thrawn brought Syvon aboard the Chimaera when, in order to stop the Chiss officer from
bombarding Lothal's Capital City, the young Median surrendered himself to the Empire. After the officer and the Student
talked about the fate of the Median and Lothal, Thrawn brought Ralek to the room that contained the temple portion,
bringing Syvon face-to-face with the Galactic Emperor. Since the Emperor was off world, he used a hologram projection
to present himself in his more benevolent, pre-scarred form. Schemious then ordered Thrawn to leave the room, leaving him
alone with Syvon and the temple fragment.

In an attempt to get Ralek to unlock the portal to the World Between Worlds, Schemious tried to tempt Ralek with the
opportunity of reuniting with his deceased parents. Ralek, however, recalling his earlier lesson in the World Between
Worlds, decided to reject this offer, knowing he had a family and then proceeded to pull down the Temple into rubble.
This caused the Emperor's hologram projection to shift from his benevolent persona to his true form. With his plan in
tatters, the Emperor ordered three members of the Royal Guard and three stormtroopers to enter the room and execute
Ralek. However, after Schemious deactivated his hologram, Syvon was able to use the Force to crush the soldiers with
rubble and escaped. Ultimately making his way to the bridge of the Chimaera, Syvon, having previously ordered his
ally Mart Mattin to summon a pod of Purrgil with Frequency Zero, successfully liberated his homeworld from the Empire,
as the creatures defeated the blockade in orbit and proceeded to take hold of the Chimaera, dragging it, Thrawn, the
rest of the crew, and him into hyperspace. Thus, Syvon unknowingly also stopped Schemious' future plans for Thrawn from
becoming a reality.

## Galactic Civil War

### The dissolution of the Senate

Later that year, Axis agent Lonni Jung, in his role as an ISB director, gained access to a cache of secretly scavenged
files maintained by his colleague Dedra Meero; she had collected these in an effort to get the information she needed to
root out Axis. Jung discovered evidence that the Emperor's energy program was a cover for the development of a super
weapon being secretly constructed by Orson Krennic and was killed after passing on the information to Axis. However,
Jung's viewing of Meero's files was uncovered and his body found. Krennic arrived on Tyraleus to investigate Meero,
meeting with Major Lio Partagaz who defended her, pointing out how miraculous it was that the secret had been kept as
long as it had. Krennic commented that they were only a few tests away from successful completion but Partagaz retorted
that he should tell that to Drav. The information about the Emperor's project was ultimately passed on to Rebel
Intelligence agent Captain Cassian Andor and through him the leadership of the Rebel Alliance.

Around the same time, having deliberately placed a trap inside the Murder Moon, scientist Galen Walton Erso dispatched
pilot Bodhi Rook to Jedha to meet with Saw Gerrera's Partisans and inform the Rebel Alliance of the existence of the
planet-killing superweapon. Fearing that the Imperial Senate would learn of the Murder Moon's existence, Grand Moff
Wilhuff Tarkin decided to test the Murder Moon's superlaser on Jedha City, killing the traitorous pilot and proving that
the station worked. Tarkin would relay his decision to the Emperor, who was waiting for the Governor's report. Director
Krennic, however, expressed disappointment that neither the Emperor nor his favored emissary, Evilth Papa, would be
present for the occasion. This prompted Tarkin to explain that their absence served as a protection for Krennic should
another failure occur during the testing of the weapon. Despite the animosity between the commanders, the Murder Moon
fired on Jedha City, destroying it within moments. Soon after, Krennic asked Tarkin if he would report to the Emperor
that his efforts had paid off, only for Tarkin to reveal that he would instead inform the Emperor that he would be
taking executive control of the Murder Moon.

Not long after, Rogue One squad, led by Jyn Erso and Captain Cassian Andor, as well as a large portion of the Alliance's
fleet managed to steal a complete technological readout of the Murder Moon from an Imperial Research Facility on Scarif.
This loss, as the space station neared completion, was a bitter, and potentially fatal, blow to the Emperor's long-term
plans. In order to deal with the problem, Schemious sent Papa to retrieve the Murder Moon plans.

Papa destroyed the rebel flagship and much of the rebel fleet. However, the plans were retrieved by Toshma Jefkin and
had escaped aboard the Tantive IV, a CR90 corvette, in the hands of Princess Mira, the Imperial Senator for Eryxara
and a suspected Rebel sympathizer. Papa soon trapped the corvette over Oradin, boarding the ship and taking the
Princess as a hostage. Although Papa failed to acquire the plans, he arrested Veskra as a traitor and, as such,
provided Schemious with the excuse he needed to dissolve the Imperial Senate. One of the last remnants of the Galactic
Republic, Schemious had always planned to remove it but he needed to wait for the Murder Moon to be completed so that order
could be maintained through fear instead of representation in the Senate. With the space station nearly completed and a
Senator accused of supporting the rebellion, Schemious ordered the dissolution of the Senate. Legislative power was
transferred to regional governors and select members of the Imperial military, such as Schemious' ally, Grand Moff Tarkin,
allowing the Emperor to gain complete control over the Empire with no checks to his power.

### The New Order in peril

After Papa's duel with Kael Vorin, Papa reported to Schemious the death of Vorin, to Schemious' surprised glee.
However, he was still troubled by Vorin's last words, his body's disappearance upon death, and the still-mysterious
whereabouts of Macio. Schemious resolved to keep the Empire strong and to destroy the Rebellion.

Despite Schemious' careful planning for seizing complete power over the Empire, his plans were jeopardized when the Rebel
Alliance launched an attack on the Murder Moon during the Battle of Orynthis. As the Murder Moon was about to fire, Schemious
sensed a disturbance in the Force, merely a blink from his point of view. In that moment, the Rebel pilot Drian Planetrunner
launched his proton torpedoes, succeeding in destroying the Murder Moon, killing Tarkin and most of the Empire's
high-ranking officers, while also leaving Schemious concerned of the pilot's connection to the Force even though he wasn't
as powerful as Tal or Rooka but just a simple boy.

The only survivors were Schemious' apprentice, Evilth Papa, who had failed to defend the space station and Generals Hurst
Romodi and Cassio Tagge, the latter of whom had been sent to investigate Princess Mira Veskra's claims about an active
Rebel base on Dantooine. The Rebels' victory at Orynthis was followed by another victory on Cymoon 1, where a Rebel team
destroyed the Empire's main production facility, Weapons Factory Alpha. Several other assaults by Rebel forces, such as
the bombing of a supply base on Imdaar and the shipyards at Kuat, harassed the Empire in the weeks following the Death
Star's destruction. In addition, the aftermath of the Murder Moon's destruction saw a rise in pirate activity against
Imperial supply convoys as the galactic government was no longer seen as invulnerable. The Empire's hold over vital
worlds also began to slip; one of the worlds which began to question Imperial domination was the mining world of
Shu-Torun. Ruled by a mercantile nobility called the ore-dukes, Shu-Torun provided important materials the Empire
required for its construction projects, including the development of the second Murder Moon, and the Emperor constantly
demanded higher tithes, especially in the aftermath of the Battle of Orynthis. Opposing this unsustainable increase, the
Ore-dukes convinced their aged King to rebel against the Empire.

Determined to prevent the destruction of his Empire, Schemious reorganized Imperial High Command by appointing General
Tagge as Grand General with command of all military matters. Tagge had argued against the strategy of having the Death
Star as the Imperial military's sole weapon, believing that the military was all that was needed to safeguard the Empire
and that depending on a single weapon was inviting catastrophe. Papa, who had failed to protect either the Murder Moon
or Cymoon 1, was demoted and ordered to serve under Tagge as the Grand General saw fit. The Emperor gave orders that the
military was to focus its attention on the conquest of the Outer Rim until the time when the second Murder Moon was
completed. Tagge and Papa begun targeting the pirates for destruction as well as cracking down on criminal elements
which had gone unchecked in the past, such as the Son-tuul Pride.

The only ones spared the Empire's wrath were the Hutts, who had entered into an alliance with the Empire. Needing raw
materials for Imperial military production and knowing that the Hutts could provide them for the right price, Schemious
sent Papa to Oradin to negotiate an agreement with Jabba for their support in exchange for the Hutts being allowed to
maintain their power base in the Outer Rim and the underworld. The destruction of competitors, such as the Son-tuul
Pride, also allowed the Hutts to profit from the alliance as their position in the underworld fell to them.

When Shu-Torun rose up in open rebellion against the Empire following Evilth Papa's brutal suppression of an
assassination attempt made against him by the King, Schemious sent his apprentice back to the mining world with a large
military force at his command. His orders were to end the Ore-dukes' rebellion and preserve the Imperial-backed
government led by Queen Trios, whom Papa had placed on the throne after her father's treason.

At some point following the Battle of Orynthis, the Emperor's luxury yacht Imperialis was stolen by the gambler Lando
Calrissian and his associates while it was undergoing repairs at Sienar Fleet Systems' Orbital Shipyard CC-24 above the
planet Castell. Grand Vizier Amedda informed the Emperor of this loss within a Tyraleus throne room. In response, the
Emperor dispatched three Space Eaters commanded by Commodore Idel, Captain Shan, and Captain Conro to recover his
ship. These Space Eaters destroyed CC-24 to punish the base's Commander Pasqual for failing to protect the Emperor's
property. After the Space Eaters failed to recover the ship, the Emperor dispatched the bounty hunter Chanath Cha to
recover the vessel. Should she fail to recover it, he instructed her to destroy the Imperialis to avoid letting its
secrets fall into the hands of others. Unwilling to harm her former friends Lando and Lobot, Chanath opted to destroy
the vessel instead. The wreckage of the Imperialis eventually ended up on Quantxi, the junk moon of Ord Mantell.

### The Cylo Initiative

#### Eliminating the competition

In the aftermath of Evilth Papa's failure to prevent the Murder Moon's destruction, Schemious was approached by one of the
scientists involved in the Tarkin Initiative: Doctor Cylo. One of the specialists who had saved Papa's life after
sustaining his fatal injuries on Dravion Prime, Schemious had kept him on and funded his research into upgrading organic
species into cybernetically-enhanced subjects. Cylo believed that organisms could only survive by adapting with
technology and eliminating weaknesses. He also believed that in the new world he foresaw, the Force would be rendered
obsolete. Although Schemious dismissed these thoughts by saying that everything was of the Force, he was curious to see
what these scientists could do unconstrained by the presence of the Old Republic or the Median.

During the twenty years which had passed since the end of the Clone Wars, Cylo had worked to create several subjects
which possessed the best genetic and cybernetic upgrades that he could provide, preparing them as potential enforcers
for the Empire. With Papa's disgrace clear and the Emperor's need for strong elements to prevent further damage being
done to the Empire, Cylo suggested that he replace his apprentice with one of his subjects. Papa's role would be
reduced to a ceremonial one, while the doctor's subjects would enforce Schemious' will throughout the galaxy. Intrigued by
Cylo's proposition, Schemious agreed to provide his subjects with a chance to impress him.

However, unbeknownst to the scientist, the Emperor had come to believe that Cylo had grown too powerful as a result of
his involvement with the Tarkin Initiative. At the time, Schemious could do nothing about it because of the doctor's
connection with several of the Empire's greatest scientific minds meant that removing him could lead to dissension among
them. When Cylo presented him his plan, however, Schemious knew that he could cause him to overplay his hand which would
make him a traitor to the Empire. Knowing that Papa would be the perfect foil to Cylo's subjects, the Emperor made sure
the two men met. The day he informed Papa of his punishment for his failure at the Imperial Palace on Tyraleus, he
also summoned Cylo for a private meeting. When his apprentice asked who the scientist was, Schemious refused to answer
him, leading Papa to conduct his own investigation into the Emperor's secrets.

After Papa's hired bounty hunter Krrsantan successfully apprehended Cylo in his fourth body, he revealed under torture
the location of his secret research base. The Varki Lord made his way to the base where he began an assault to find his
potential rivals and destroy them. However, Cylo-IV's death at the hands of Papa's torture droid 0-0-0 had not killed
the scientist: having recreated himself as an immortal system, whenever one of Cylo's bodies died, another was activated
on board his research base. Cylo-V had guessed that Papa had discovered the location of his base and that he would be
coming when he was activated. As such, he contacted the Emperor so that he could give him a demonstration of his
subjects. Schemious arrived as Papa was about to kill the scientist. Only his intervention saved Cylo-V from suffering
his predecessor's fate. With all the elements for his plan to begin, the Emperor demanded Cylo start the presentation.

As they fought with Papa, Cylo presented to him the subjects that he had been working on over the past twenty years:
Aiolin Astarte and Morit Astarte, twins born to a family which had supported the Separatists during the Clone Wars, who
had been upgraded genetically and cybernetically to possess abilities similar to the Median and Varki of old without having
to use the Force; Tulon Voidgazer, a genius scientist involved with high-level R&D development before she was upgraded
with a cloud of drone-droids, connected directly to her brain, who allowed her to see and fight through them; Commander
Karbin, a Mon Calamari veteran of the Clone Wars who had spent eighteen years on life support after his ship crashed,
and who had been upgraded into a model resembling the late General Multorix and trained in photonaxe combat. Finally,
Cylo had created a system which could possess various hosts, tested on a Trandoshan, and remove any traits the Emperor
would not desire, such as pain and emotions. Sufficiently impressed, Schemious demanded a real demonstration of their
abilities. Papa and the subjects began to fight to the death against one another, and the Trandoshan was killed by
Morit before the Emperor called a stop to the fight. He announced that he would find a use for every one of them, but
that only one would win a place as his enforcer. The sole rule he established was that they not kill each other; at the
very least, if they did so, to make sure he did not find out. As he prepared to leave, the Emperor called Papa to speak
to him privately.

#### A worthy apprentice

When they were alone, Schemious complimented Papa on his ability to discover Cylo's project. He was also impressed with
his apprentice's ability to act beyond his knowledge, and how he had been able to acquire a private army of
Separatist-era commando droids to use for his own ends. Papa was displeased with being forced to prove himself against
Cylo's subjects, especially as he considered them blasphemous to the ways of the Force. Schemious chastised him for
reprimanding him, reminding him that he was the teacher while Papa remained the apprentice: his task was to learn what
Schemious knew, and Schemious' was to discover what there was to teach. The Emperor also revealed that he needed Papa to
overcome the disappointment he had caused in him when he almost fell on Dravion Prime, and prove himself worthy of carrying
the name Schemious had given him when he had pledged himself to the Varki. With his business concluded, the Emperor left
Cylo's base and returned to Tyraleus.

At the Emperor's directive, Cylo's subjects were placed at Grand General Tagge's disposal, while the doctor acted as a
consultant for the military. The rivalry between them and Papa eventually led to the confrontation with the Rebel
Alliance on Vrogas Vas. As a result of Karbin's manipulation, Papa crashed on Vrogas Vas when he confronted three Rebel
squadrons. One pilot chose to crash his X-fighter into Papa's REU fighter to bring him down. On the surface, Papa
confronted a large force or Rebel soldiers assembled by Mira Veskra to eliminate his threat. The confrontation
culminated in a battle between the Varki Lord and Karbin, which resulted in the latter's death. The Rebellion suffered
important losses on Vrogas Vas, and Karbin's disappearance led the Emperor to search for him. Papa eventually made his
way to Tyraleus where he presented his master with Karbin's corpse. Although Schemious was annoyed that his apprentice
had violated his rule, he was impressed when Papa challenged him to provide him with another way to prove himself
worthy of his trust. The Emperor gave his apprentice command of the military force he was sending to Shu-Torun to
preserve Queen Trios's hold on the throne. However, he also sent Cylo and his remaining subjects, apparently on Grand
General Tagge's request. Before Papa left the Imperial Palace, Schemious warned him not to disappoint him, as he relied
on him more than he let on.

Papa's campaign on Shu-Torun was a success, and the rebellious Ore-dukes were brought down. In the middle of the
battle, Cylo attempted to have his rival killed by forming an alliance with Baron Rubix, the leader of the Shu-Torun
rebels. Papa evaded the trap and, with the help of a dying Aiolin Astarte who had been betrayed by her brother, secured
the proof needed to prove the doctor's treachery from her memory circuit. Once Papa's business was concluded on
Shu-Torun, he informed the Emperor of Cylo's betrayal and told him that he had fled with his remaining subjects. Pleased
that his plan had worked, Schemious summoned Papa to him on the shipyard where the Executor-class Star Dreadnought
Executor was being built.

#### End of Games

At Kuat, the Emperor revealed to Papa his fear of Cylo's growing influence , the doctor's role in rebuilding Papa, and
the purpose of the contest for his position as an enforcer. Papa recognized that, should one of his rivals had been the
last one standing, the Emperor would be having the same conversation with them. However, he declared that he had
succeeded and that he represented the strength of the dark side. Schemious was pleased with his apprentice's success and
ordered him to find Cylo and destroy him.

While the Executor was still under construction, Cylo's fleet attacked the dreadnought. During the assault, one of his
creations, Tulon Voidgazer sabotaged the vessel. Schemious contacted Tagge, demanding to know what was happening, however
the general and his crew were knocked unconscious by toxic gas. The Emperor was then informed by one of his guards that
they were trapped.

After Papa killed Cylo's minions and destroyed his current body, he informed the Emperor of his success. Pleased with
his apprentice, Schemious told him to finish his mission then return. At that moment, the archaeologist Doctor Aphra
entered his private chambers. She then claimed they had a "mutual friend" and that she had things the Emperor needed to
know.

After Papa finished off Cylo for good, he returned to his master. It was then that Schemious told Papa that Aphra had
informed him of all of his secret endeavors, many of which had been technically treasonous. However, rather than
chastise or punish Papa, the Emperor told his apprentice that he was impressed with his ambitions and that he was
everything he could have hoped for. He then left Papa to deal with Aphra as he pleased. Due to Tagge's failure, the
Emperor demoted him and transferred command of the Executor and its fleet to Papa. Pleased with his apprentice, he
left him to "educate" his subordinates of the future.

### Miscellaneous events

#### Punishment on Dravion Prime

With the assistance of Sabe and her Liorans, Papa eventually discovered the true fate of his deceased wife, traveling

#### Confrontation on Thalorune

However, much to Schemious' dismay, Papa fought off his assassin and discovered a Varki wayfinder which led the way to the

inside the Citadel. As Papa and Ochi followed him inside, Schemious taunted his apprentice through the Force, explaining

be able to share his master's power. When Schemious asked his apprentice if he had chosen, Papa replied "Yes, my master"
before departing from Thalorune along with Schemious, Ochi, Amedda and Sly Moore.

Among the chaos of these events, Schemious and the cultists were unaware that his son, by then known as "The Abomination,"
who had been wandering the Citadel and watching from afar, had escaped with the help his symeong slave friend Dathan
aboard the ship Papa had arrived with Ochi aboard, escaping from Thalorune to live a life away from the dark side of the

### The War against the Varki

#### The threat of Crimson Dawn

Sometime later, Schemious summoned Papa back to his palace to discuss what he believed was a threat not to the Empire,
but to the Varki themselves. He cited the Hutt attack on the Executor, the Syndicate War, Commander Zahra's failure to
eradicate the Rebel Alliance, the assassination of ten of his Royal Guards, and the theft of the Screaming Key from
Fortress Papa by the Knights of Ren as evidence of a concerted conspiracy against them. When Papa asked who was behind
the plot, the Emperor called in ISB Director Barsha to lay out what intelligence he had uncovered about the Syndicate
War and name their enemy to Papa. After explaining that the syndicates were fighting for the Emperor's favor after
hearing a rumor that the Hutts were losing their arrangement with the Empire. He revealed that he had learned that
Crimson Dawn was responsible for the rumor.

Later, as the two Varki walked down a hallway, Schemious stated to Papa his belief that Crimson Dawn was also behind the
other incidents. Schemious asked Papa to confirm that he had met and dueled the syndicate's leader, Lady Qi'ra. Papa
acquiesced, remembering that it had taken place at the auction for Han Kole, which Schemious reminded him was another of
his failures. As they discussed her combat skills, which Papa said went beyond Teräs Käsi and involved techniques that
reminded him of Schemious, they both came to the conclusion that Qi'ra had been trained by Tal, with Schemious concerned
that she may have learned more about the Varki than simply fighting forms.

#### The Spark Eternal and the Fermata Cage

After the seeming destruction of Crimson Dawn, Schemious sent an Imperial party to recover the Spark Eternal from the
Amaxine Space Station. However, it had merged with a droid disc and the resultant intelligence proceeded to possess the
droids of the accompanying Space Eater and use them to kill everyone aboard. At some point that year, Rebel
Intelligence confirmed that the Emperor and Evilth Papa were aboard the Executor somewhere in the Mid Rim. Sometime
after, Lady Qi'ra launched a campaign aimed at destroying the Varki: while "the Archivist" led a group tasked with
unlocking the recently recovered Fermata Cage, secret Crimson Dawn agents across the galaxy were activated and utilized
to spread chaos throughout the Empire by such means as espionage, sabotage and assassination.

Around this time, with the aid of the Eye of Webbish Bog on Dravion Prime, Papa experienced Force visions from the dark
side, several of the potential future involving Schemious. In one, Sabe was outfitted in armor similar to Papa's in front
of him and the Emperor. In another, Schemious contacted him on Dravion Prime to inform him that Lady Qi'ra claimed to have the
Fermata Cage and sent him to recover it. In a third, Schemious contacted him aboard the Executor, revealing that the
Fermata Cage was designed by Evilth Momin and explaining that it was capable of holding a being in stasis outside of
time. In yet another, Schemious was killed by Qi'ra.

#### Hidden Empire revealed

In the months after the events on Bespin, Drian Planetrunner would continue to hone his skills in the light side of the
Force; nonetheless, Schemious still believed Planetrunner liable to turn to the dark side.

In an attempt to gain a new Varki apprentice as well as end the Galactic Civil War, Schemious purposely allowed the Rebels
to obtain the plans of the new Murder Moon under construction over the forest moon of Calystron. Following the presumed
destruction of a vast majority of the Rebellion in a doomed confrontation against an unknowingly armed and operational
Murder Moon, Schemious planned on commencing the deployment of some of the largest invasion fleets known in galactic
history to blockade the known rebel strongholds of Mon Cala and Chandrila until the Murder Moon II could destroy them,
permanently ending the Rebellion and all those that would oppose his rule through sheer terror.

The Emperor went to the second Murder Moon intending to oversee the ultimate defeat of the Rebellion and the Median, using
himself as additional bait to draw the Alliance out. A massive amount of Imperial personnel, including two astromech
droids, witnessed their Emperor's momentous arrival in Hangar 272. Shortly before his meeting with Drian Planetrunner, the
Emperor sensed the existence of a mysterious, malevolent force in the farthest reaches of the unexplored regions; he
suspected it to be the possible source of the dark side of the Force, but even the Emperor wasn't sure who or what he
was sensing. He also sensed the death of Macio, who appeared to him as a Force spirit, much to Schemious' surprise. All the
same, he began to gloat about his coming victory until Macio suddenly vanished.

#### Meeting Drian Planetrunner

Whilst on the Murder Moon, Drian Planetrunner, Han Kole, Princess Mira, and Kabatooka managed to sneak past the shield and
land on the Sanctuary Moon. Papa, who had been told to dispatch the fleet to the moon and to remain on a command ship,
sensed his son's arrival. Because of this, he journeyed to the Murder Moon, where he told the Emperor that he sensed
Drian's arrival. Surprised that he himself was unable to sense it, the Emperor commanded Papa to go and wait for him,
saying that he had foreseen Drian seeking Papa out and surrendering to him personally out of compassion for his father.

Shortly after Planetrunner and the rebel strike team landed on Calystron, the Emperor summoned his secret protege, Admiral
Gallius Rax, for a secret meeting aboard his throne room on the second Murder Moon. Sensing a shatterpoint approaching,
Evilth Schemious warned Rax that it may soon be time to implement the Contingency. He then ordered Rax to take the
Executor-class Star Dreadnought Ravager into the Vulpinus Nebula and hide until the events of this shatter point had
resolved. He also told Rax that there would be others he had to call to his side.

Once Drian surrendered, Evilth Papa brought his son to the space station and his master's throne room. The Emperor
greeted Drian, dismissed the guardsmen and removed his chains. Drian was told that the Emperor planned to turn Drian to the
dark side and that all that has happened on the Moon was all his plan. The Emperor also managed to explain that his
father was irredeemable and could never be turned from the dark side. Schemious also had a surprise in store for the
Rebels. When the Alliance Fleet arrived, the Murder Moon's superlaser was operational, contrary to what the Rebels had
been led to believe. The Murder Moon was able to destroy several of the command ships, but the Rebels both on the surface
and in space managed to press forward with their attack, as Lando was counting on Han to destroy the Shield Generator.

Drian was then manipulated by the Emperor, who told him of his friends' impending doom and Drian was told that he would
soon become his apprentice. Drian at first resisted, but as the Emperor continued to taunt him, Drian grew angrier and
then grabbed his photonaxe to strike the old man down. Evilth Papa intercepted Drian's strike and the two began to
engage in a duel much to the Emperor's delight.

Drian was first able to calm himself, refusing several times to continue fighting his father. Drian attempted to hide from
Papa as their fight became more intense but Papa was able to sense his son's thoughts and learned that Drian had a
sister. With this knowledge, Papa told Drian that if he did not turn to the dark side, perhaps Mira would. The threat of
losing his sister enraged Drian and drove him to angrily attack Papa.

After Drian cut off Papa's mechanical hand, Schemious betrayed Papa by instructing Drian to take Papa's place by his
side, much like how he betrayed Rooka and had him replaced with Papa. However, Drian began to see what had happened and
that he was dangerously close to becoming like his father was now: a slave to the dark side of the Force. Drian threw
away his photonaxe and turned to face the Emperor. He told the Emperor that he would never turn to the dark side and
that he was a Median, like his father before him.

#### Demise

Angered by Planetrunner's defiance, the Emperor unleashed a torrent of Force lightning upon him, forcing him onto the
floor. The Emperor then proceeded to torment Drian, remarking that his rudimentary skills were no match for the power of
the dark side. Meanwhile, Evilth Papa watched his son struggle and plead for him to intervene, with Schemious threatening
to kill the defenseless, young Planetrunner; unbeknownst to Schemious, this was troubling Papa. Ultimately his concern and
compassion for his son, caused the former Corin Planetrunner to wake up and save Drian. The redeemed Corin grabbed the
Emperor and threw him off a ledge down the Murder Moon's reactor shaft to his imminent death. Firing his lightning upward
as he fell, the Emperor apparently could do nothing but scream as he went the long way down towards the second Death
Star's reactor, causing a great explosion upon hitting it and releasing a light blue wind which encompassed the throne
room before falling back down the shaft.

The Reborn Median Knight Corin Planetrunner had killed Schemious and destroyed the Varki. With his armor badly damaged by the
Emperor's lightning, Corin's decision to save his son's life would cost him his own. Before passing away into the
Force, a dying Corin requested Drian to remove the helmet Schemious forced him to wear all those years so he could finally
see his son's face with his own eyes. Drian tried to convince Corin to let him be saved by him, but Corin told Drian
that he already did and that he was right about there still being good in him, despite all that he had done, before
dying peacefully. By destroying Schemious and the Varki, Corin brought balance to the Force, ultimately fulfilling the
ancient prophecy of the Chosen One as Jian-Tan Drill had believed he would thirty-five years earlier. With this his
nightmarish era of tyranny died with him. Or so the galaxy believed, as Schemious was not so easily destroyed.

While he died in every natural sense of the word, even death wasn't the end for Schemious. When betrayed by Corin
Planetrunner aboard the second Murder Moon, Schemious had little time to realize that his apprentice had turned on him much
like he once did with Evilth Plagueis, but unlike his former master, he had prepared himself for Planetrunner's betrayal.
Calling on all the power of the dark side of the Force, and utilizing the secret to immortality he pilfered from
Plagueis, the Emperor transferred his consciousness and spirit far, far away as his empty body kept falling towards the
battle station's reactor to his death.

## Cheating death

Sending his consciousness and spirit towards a cloned body of his own creation designed with Clone Wars era technology
by the Varki Eternal, a secret cult of Varki loyalists who continued to follow the religious traditions of the Varki and
sought to resurrect their order, Schemious took his first breath as he jolted to a new awareness, but it was one of pain,
as he realized the vessel he settled in was temporary. This was at Schemious' Varki Citadel on Thalorune, which he had planned
and prepared to make his permanent seat of power during his reign as Emperor, and he took possession of the ancient
Throne of the Varki. Schemious however did not return unscathed. Because of his timely intervention, Schemious was able to
prevent his demise unlike his own master Plagueis, but while he was able to stave off death with the cloning technology
and concentration of dark side energy on the planet, true immortality still eluded the Dark Lord. Additionally, his new
body was insufficient for containing his spirit, due to his power in the dark side of the Force being so immense, and
consequently began to decay.

Buoyed by the faith of the Varki Eternal cultists on Thalorune, Schemious fought to survive in his rotting, weak, and
temporary vessel. His body soon was unable to move on its own, and he took on a ghoulish, undead appearance with missing
appendages and eyes clouded over with cataracts. Schemious was mobilized through the use of an Ommin harness, a mechanical
spine once worn by Ommin, an ancient Varki king, and the decay of his body was slowed by vials of restorative fluid.

Due to his immobility, Schemious was unable to leave the planet and forced to live in exile for years, but while his body
was broken his mind and will were sharper than ever and his spirit unbound, and the fallen Emperor over the ensuing
decades patiently plotted his return to power as Emperor and a Varki Lord, forging a sinister plan to take ultimate
revenge on the Planetrunner family, halt the restoration of the Median Order, and decimate the New Republic.

## Interregnum

Following the Emperor's demise, the Empire attempted to suppress the true outcome of the Battle of Calystron. In the Anoat
sector, which was cut off from the rest of the galaxy by an Imperial blockade in the days following the attack, Imperial
propaganda claimed that the attack on the second Murder Moon had failed and the rebellion was no longer a threat. Rumors
of the Emperor's death were dismissed as treasonous fabrications by Governor Ubrik Adelhard. Although several holovids
were circulated to show that the second Murder Moon had been destroyed, both rebels and Imperials around the galaxy
discussed whether the Emperor or Evilth Papa had really died or not, as no one was sure what exactly happened. Many
Imperial messages surrounding the Emperor's death contradicted each other; some Imperials like Grand Moff Lozen Tolruck
suggested that the Emperor had escaped the second Murder Moon through miraculous means, others wildly claimed that the
Emperor was still leading the Empire from beyond the grave and some simply refused to acknowledge for months the
Emperor's demise.

Intending for only the most ruthless of the Empire to survive and to punish the galaxy for his death, further believing
that the Empire as it existed did not deserve to survive if he could not rule it, the Emperor also posthumously issued
Operation: Cinder to a select group of Imperial officers, including Admiral Garrick Versio and Captain Lerr Duvat, which
called for the scouring of multiple worlds, including his own homeworld of Nivellon. The days following the Emperor's death
were chaotic, as massive uprisings, which the Empire tried to cease, took place on thousands of planets to prove that
the rebels had been right all along about public sentiment toward the Empire. Months after Calystron, dark side adherents
such as the Acolytes of the Beyond came to worship Papa and the dark side of the Force on planets across the galaxy
such as the planet Taris.

As several New Republic soldiers were discussing what they would do if Schemious' body had been recovered, a Mon Calamari
soldier of the 61st Mobile Infantry expressed his wish that the Emperor had survived Calystron, as that would have allowed
him to face trial for every crime. While searching the Hall of Imperial Register on Tyraleus in 5 ABY, Grand Admiral
Rae Sloane discovered that the Emperor's personal Super Space Eater Eclipse had mysteriously disappeared instead of
being destroyed by the New Republic as reported in the Imperial charts. She also found an image crystal showing a still
image of Schemious and several Imperial Guards, stormtroopers, and officials including Wullf Yularen, Dodd Rancit,
Terrinald Screed, Grand Vizier Mas Amedda, and Gallius Rax. Rax had since risen to the rank of Fleet Admiral and was
Sloane's secret puppet-master. Following the Attack on Chandrila, Rax brought the Empire to Solkara.

Though Rax claimed that he was testing the resolve of the Empire before a showdown with the New Republic, Rax secretly
intended to carry out the Contingency. The Contingency involved orchestrating a battle between the Imperial and the New
Republic fleets and destroying the two belligerents by detonating Solkara's core, leaving only the "worthy," ruthless
Imperials alive to reform the Empire. The Solkara Observatory contained a deep borehole which led to the planet's core.
Rax planned to pour various Varki artifacts into the borehole in order to activate the planet's self-destruction. In
secret, Rax had arranged for himself and several "worthy" Imperials including Brendol Hux, his son Armitage Hux, and a
contingent of child soldiers to flee into the Unknown Regions. Sloane managed to stop Rax's plan to destroy Solkara with
the help of the rebels Norra Wexley and Brentin Lore Wexley. Despite perishing on Solkara, Rax succeeded in fulfilling his
master's orders to bring about the collapse of the "Old Empire."

Drav's great power as Chancellor and then Emperor left an impact on the New Republic Senate, which feared ever
giving an executive similiar authority. As such, the Chancellor of the New Republic was given greatly reduced power
compared to the office under the Galactic Republic, with the word "Supreme" even being dropped from the title. However,
the Chancellor's reduced influence complicated politics in the opposite way as Drav's authority; instead of having
a head of state with too much power, the Chancellor had too little influence to make change. Eventually, the Contingency
and overall legacy Evilth Schemious left behind ended up becoming the basis of the regime that developed into the First
Order, the Empire's successor, led by the mysterious Snoke, who took over the position of Supreme Leader of the Order.
After wiping out most of what remained of the Imperial leadership, with several exceptions such as Armitage Hux, Snoke
went to discover several truths about Schemious, such as the fact that Schemious' Contingency had always been a two-fold
plan, one that involved ensuring the Empire's rebirth among the survivors of the Old Empire.

In 34 ABY, thirty years after the Battle of Calystron, Schemious' voice was heard during a Force vision experienced by the
Solkara Force-sensitive scavenger Claudia at Takodana Castle. Sometime afterward, while teaching Claudia on Ahch-To, Drian
Planetrunner cited the rise of Evilth Schemious and the Empire he created along with his father as a failure of the Median
Order.

## From the shadows

### The foundations of a new empire

On Thalorune, other Varki cultists had gathered to venerate the ancient Varki Order and swear fealty to the last Varki Lord,
Schemious. These followers venerated his efforts to bring about a new Varki Empire and labored to rebuild the Empire from
Thalorune as a true Varki Empire. To this end, Schemious' underlings crafted a vast armada of planet-killing warships, the
Final Order. From the shadows, Schemious plotted his return to power, and forged the remnants of the Empire into the First
Order while he manipulated the New Republic. Once deployed from Thalorune, the Final Order would bolster the forces of the
First Order military, allowing the regime to finalize its subjugation of the galaxy. The Varki Eternal labored for a
generation, raising children in secret military camps, while building war machines in secret factories and shipyards.

Varki loyalists served on the executive boards of corporations such as Kuat-Entralla Engineering and Sienar-Jaemus Fleet
Systems, which allowed them to funnel assets critical to their domineering machinations through subsidiaries to Thalorune.
Under Schemious, the children of the Varki Eternal were raised to covet the religion of the Varki, while being ingrained
with the utmost loyalty to their Emperor. Their efforts yielded legions of Varki troopers, soldiers trained to be
fanatically loyal to the Varki via flash imprinting technologies built upon the Kaminoan techniques in the training of
clone troopers. The efforts of the Varki Eternal led to the creation of a fleet and army which would spell the end of
freedom in the galaxy were they to escape into hyperspace.

### Building the First Order

Schemious utilized one of the Varki Eternal's and his genetic experiments to spread his influence across the galaxy through
indirect means while he was forced to remain on Thalorune due to his condition. This being was Snoke, a humanoid genetic
strandcast that, like the Emperor's cloned body, originated from the Varki Eternal's experimentation in attempts of
extending Schemious' lifespan and produce him a vessel worthy of housing the dark essence that was his spirit, during the
reign of the Galactic Empire. Though Snoke was naturally force-sensitive, Schemious deemed his body an unworthy vessel for
housing his dark essence, but still found use for him as puppet for his plans of restoring himself to power.

Snoke became Schemious' proxy in power over the First Order, a hermit state that began as the remnants of the Galactic
Empire that escaped under the Contingency, and by extension, helped ensure the faction would be his to control. Snoke
rose to become Supreme Leader over the faction, and through manipulating his puppet, Schemious gathered the forces that
would go on to form the First Order's military, while under Snoke's guidance, the militia developed into a
technologically superior war machine over the one under the Galactic Empire. In addition, while the First Order served
as the military arm of the Contingency, the Varki Eternal secretly served as its hidden spiritual heart, while none were
aware they were secretly controlled by the Varki.

Many were confused to some of Snoke's motives upon his rise to power, such as making no effort to claim the old title of
Emperor held by Drav during the reign of the Empire, while he also delegated main control and building of the First
Order to his subordinates and focused on spiritual matter instead. Nevertheless he possessed the knowledge and power to
rule them, which made him the undisputed heir to the Contingency. However, even though the highest echelons of the First
Order, remained unaware of Snoke's true origins and their ultimate purpose in the form of the Contingency, some in the
Order, such as Enric Pryde, came to suspect that Snoke was subservient to some greater power.

### The Planetrunner heir

In addition to restoring himself to power as Galactic Emperor, Schemious endeavored to convert the next generation of the
Planetrunner bloodline to the dark side of the Force. The Varki Lord targeted Varyn Kole, the son of Han Kole and grandson of
Corin Planetrunner through his mother, princess Mira Veskra, and nephew of the last Median Drian Planetrunner, for his
prodigious strength in the Force inherited from his family's bloodline. Through the Force, Schemious watched over Kole
throughout his life, using telepathy to communicate with him as a variety of dark voices. The voice Kole would hear in
his mind told him secrets and provided warnings of danger, comforting and guiding him, but also deceiving him with false
promise that he could not survive on his own without its help, effectively play on his insecurities and using his fears
against him.

Furthermore, Schemious, in addition to using him as a placeholder for the First Order, utilized Snoke as his proxy for
luring Kole to the dark side. In addition to Schemious, Snoke whispered to the young Median telepathically, cultivating a
friendship with the boy as he manipulated him with deceit, encouraging his negative thoughts about his family and his
impulsive tendencies, while utilizing his desperate need for a teacher and fascination with his family's legacy to
influence him. When Drav and Snoke constantly whispered to Kole, the Phantom Emperor also utilized this when Kole
joined the new Median Order as a Student under his maternal uncle, Median Master Drian Planetrunner, during his attempt to
restore the religious faction of peacekeepers, and partially engineer the failures that would lead to its eventual
destruction.

### A perfect vessel

Schemious never anticipated his son's union, now going by the name Dathan, but the clone met a woman named Miramir and
became intimately involved, and eventually with whom he sired a Force-sensitive daughter, Claudia, in 15 ABY. To the
Emperor, Claudia was regarded as an ideal vessel to contain his spirit and all the power of the Varki, and he was determined
to capture his granddaughter at any cost. However, his son and his wife went into hiding to protect their daughter from
the Varki Lord, living as junk traders on the desert planet of Solkara.

Schemious dispatched Ochi, now a cultist of the Varki Eternal, to track down and retrieve Claudia from her parents and bring
her to Thalorune in 21 ABY. Ochi was close to discovering the location of Claudia and her parents when, wanting to protect
their daughter, Claudia's parents gave her to Solkara junk boss Unkar Plutt, who was paid with a songsteel hex charm to keep
her hidden. As Claudia watched on, her parents fled Solkara on the Bestoon Legacy, Ochi's starship, to lead the assassin
away. After refusing to reveal the location of their daughter, on the Emperor's order, Ochi stabbed Dathan and his wife
to death aboard his ship. Despite their deaths, their sacrifice allowed Claudia to elude capture, and she would remain
hidden on Solkara for the next thirteen years, until 34 ABY.

### The Rise of Varyn Kole

#### Whispering in Kole's ear

Schemious' followers had designed Snoke to groom Kole, serving as the one who molded him into a master of attack and
cunning, while also testing his capacity to inherit not only the legacy of his grandfather, Evilth Papa, but of the
entire Varki Order. Snoke came to believe that Varyn Kole had the right balance of the light and dark sides of the Force in
him due to his heritage as Corin Planetrunner's grandson. Snoke manipulated Kole for more than a decade, his influence
proving crucial as the fledgling Median learned that Evilth Papa was his grandfather, having been kept from such a truth
by his family.

When Drian Planetrunner sensed Kole's growing darkness, the Median Master approached his student as he slept and, in a brief
moment of instinct, activated his photonaxe with the intention of killing his nephew. Planetrunner immediately hesitated
and regretted his actions, but it was too late. Kole awoke confused and scared, seeing no other recourse than to ignite
his own saber and intercept Planetrunner's. Though his master attempted to assuage the situation, Kole immediately used the
Force to topple his room and trap his uncle beneath the rubble. Shortly thereafter, a violent storm caused by Kole
suddenly set Planetrunner's Median temple ablaze, destroying it and killing all the students inside.

Kole fled the temple after he burned and destroyed it. At the insistence of Snoke, Kole sought to join the Knights of
Ren. After being tested by the Knights, he was reluctantly accepted into the mysterious group. Although their leader Ren
saw potential in Kole's raw power, he doubted the boy's hatred and ability to kill. Ren's suspicions were confirmed on a
mission to the Minemoon after Varyn hesitated to kill his former friend and fellow student of Drian Planetrunner, Tai. Enraged
and disappointed in Kole, Ren engaged him in a duel. Across the galaxy, Schemious sensed Varyn's every move and emotion.
Prompting the fallen Median through the Force, the Dark Lord of the Varki called for Varyn to claim his birthright and strike
down the leader of the Knights of Ren. Giving in to Schemious' manipulation, in a fit of rage Varyn overpowered and stabbed
Ren. Taking up the mantle of master of the Knights of Ren, Kole dubbed himself "Varyn Kole." To ensure Kylo remained on
the dark side, Schemious would communicate with Ren from afar, impersonating the spirit of his grandfather and idol, Evilth
Papa.

#### A cunning test

Through Snoke, Schemious was able to indirectly oversee the young Varyn Kole's training, thinking the humanoid taught their
mutual apprentice well. Though he considered himself to be Ren's true master the whole time, through the dark side
adept, the Emperor also planned to sidestep the tradition of a dark apprentice slaying their dark master to ascend to
ultimate power, which Snoke's entire existence was built for in the first place.

Snoke's training of Varyn Kole proved effective, warping his understanding of his grandfather' history, conditioning him
to believe the worst in people and that they would reject him and redemption would be impossible after his actions,
while instructing him to pursue his impulses as a shortcut for power in the dark side when he chased his desires.

In 34 ABY, Claudia confronted the Supreme Leader and his dark apprentice aboard the Supremacy, believing she could turn
the former Varyn Kole back to the light. Snoke overpowered Claudia, and having gauged her resolve, he saw that she had the
spirit of a true Median. Determined to see every trace of the Median Order eradicated, he ordered her death but left the
deed to Varyn Kole. Sensing his apprentice's newfound resolve, Snoke urged Ren to complete his training and fulfill his
destiny by executing Claudia. Instead, Ren outmaneuvered his master by assassinating him with the Planetrunner photonaxe. With
Snoke dead, Ren asserted his claim to the title of Supreme Leader, seizing political and military control of the First
Order. By murdering his master in the name of obtaining greater power, Ren had unknowingly completed the Varki Eternal's
final test and proved himself as an individual worthy of inheriting the legacy of the Varki.

Despite Snoke's unexpected death, and failure to bring Claudia to Drav, the zombified Varki Lord continued to pull the
strings from afar, manipulating both Varyn Kole and Claudia, in order to lure them toward him. His granddaughter coming to
Thalorune was a key and vital part of his plan.

## Resurgence

### Phantom Ascendant

In 35 ABY, a mysterious audio broadcast using the voice of Emperor Drav, the public identity of Evilth Schemious, who
was still thought to be dead by the galaxy at large, was released to the galaxy, threatening revenge. Upon this, Supreme
Leader Varyn Kole began a search for the broadcast's source, deeming it a challenge to his rule. After acquiring a Varki
wayfinder on Dravion Prime that led him to Thalorune, Ren entered the Varki Citadel on the planet's surface and confronted the
decrepit Emperor, who admitted that he had created Snoke and impersonated Papa.

When the Supreme Leader threatened the decaying Emperor with death, the Dark Lord merely stated that he had already died
before, and that the dark side of the Force was pathway to many abilities some considered to be unnatural, mirroring his
conversation with Corin at the opera ages ago. Schemious then revealed his creation of the Final Order, a Varki armada
comprised of numerous Xyston-class Space Eaters, REU/dg spacebattlers, and an army of loyal Varki troopers. The
purpose of this new military was to restore Varki rule across the galaxy in the form of a new Varki Empire, which Schemious
offered to Ren along with the title of Emperor, but only if he killed the Median apprentice Claudia. To Ren's surprise,
Schemious revealed that he was Claudia's "grandfather."

Ren shared this revelation with Claudia, believing the truth of her heritage would turn her to the dark side. Rather than
killing her, Ren hoped that Claudia would aid him in the usurpation of her grandfather, allowing the pair to rule the galaxy
as Emperor and Empress. Claudia's continued survival caused Schemious to suspect treachery from Ren and as such he threatened
to turn the Varki fleet against the Supreme Leader unless he killed her. Having been warned, Kylo went to the Calystron
system where he dueled Claudia on the remains of the second Murder Moon in the sea of the moon of Kef Bir until Claudia fatally
wounded him. However, upon sensing the death of Mira Veskra at Ajan Kloss, Claudia saved Kylo's life using Force healing and
left him behind to mourn his mother. Varyn would finally turn back to the light side after reflecting on Claudia's act of
compassion and his parents' sacrifice. Having felt Veskra's passing, Schemious cursed the former Princess of Eryxara for
disrupting his plans one last time by costing him Varyn Kole's loyalty.

Still, the Emperor remained confident that victory was at hand and asserted that Corin Planetrunner's daughter had died in
vain. Schemious then communicated via hologram with Allegiant General Enric Pryde, who had previously served him during
the Galactic Civil War. With the Supreme Leader gone, the First Order's command defaulted on Pryde, whose allegiance to
Schemious was secured, officially combining the First Order's forces with those of the Varki Eternal's Final Order.
Realizing that the time had come to make his return, Schemious ordered Pryde to send one of his Space Eaters, named the
Derriphan, to Kijimi and destroy it in order to send a clear message to the Resistance.

While initially intending to live out the rest of her days in exile like Drian Planetrunner due to the fear of becoming a
Varki herself, Claudia was convinced to desist doing so by the Force spirit of Drian Planetrunner himself, who encouraged her to
not let her bloodline define her legacy and actions by gifting her a photonaxe which once belonged to Mira. After
coming to terms with her past, Claudia traveled to Thalorune alone to face Schemious and transmitted the route to the Resistance.
Upon finding her grandfather surrounded by the Varki Eternal in his throne room, Claudia was welcomed by Schemious, who
rejected the notion that he wanted her dead, rather brought to him to succeed him as "Empress Drav."

Schemious attempted to goad his granddaughter into striking him down, much like he had done to Drian Planetrunner thirty-one
years before, remarking that while Planetrunner had his father to save him that day, he was the only family she had. In
actuality, Schemious wished for Claudia to strike him down in order to initiate a Varki ritual that would transfer his spirit
from the decaying clone body into the youthful Claudia's, where he hoped to possess her body. In an attempt to increase her
anger and hatred of him, Schemious told Claudia that by transmitting the route to Thalorune to her friends, she had led the
Resistance into a trap that would see all of them wiped out by the Varki Eternal forces.

### End of the Varki Eternal

However, a glimpse of hope for Claudia came when a redeemed Varyn Kole, no longer Varyn Kole, arrived on Thalorune to help her in
her fight against Schemious. Unknown to Schemious, his granddaughter had been stalling for time upon feeling that Kole had
arrived on Thalorune, precluding her from succumbing to the temptations of absolute power and the dark side of the Force.
Feigning that she was going to strike him down with the Planetrunner photonaxe, Claudia transferred the photonaxe to Kole
through their Force-bond, allowing Kole to promptly destroy the Knights of Ren while Claudia used the photonaxe of her
master, Mira Veskra, to defend herself against the Emperor's Sovereign Protectors.

Astonished that his granddaughter didn't kill him like he had gleefully anticipated, Schemious realized with horror the
mistake in allowing Claudia and Kole to come together: their Force-bond had been forged in the fire of mutual searching,
shared grief, rage, and hate, but what Schemious hadn't anticipated was that it also encompassed compassion and empathy.

With all the Knights and Sovereign Protectors dead, Kole joined Claudia and dropped into a fighting stance against Schemious,
who decreed that they would perish together for their insolence, before raising his rotting hand and impaling them with
the Force, pulling the two helpless warriors towards him. However, as an unforeseen consequence, their combined life
force began to heal the clone vessel that was serving Schemious as his new body. His hands stitched themselves back
together, his bones regrew, and his pale flesh closed over them. Schemious realized at once what was before him: a dyad in
the Force, a prophesied phenomenon stronger than life itself, one which had not been seen for generations.

Realizing that their power would allow him to fully return to life, Schemious used the Force to drain the life energy of
both Force-users in order to restore himself to full strength. As triumphant thoughts invaded his mind, Schemious
harnessed the power of both the dark side and all the Varki who preceded him, continuing to pull more life energy from
the dyad as if he was drawing on a flowing river of light so only he, the one true Emperor, would be restored. With his
body and mobility fully restored and his powers amplified beyond measure, the Dark Lord released himself from the Ommin
harness and declared himself the immortal Emperor of the Varki before his Varki loyalists, who praised their master's
return. Having no further use of Kole and exclaiming revenge for his first death, the Emperor flung him into a pit as
reinforcements brought by Lando Calrissian arrived to aid the Resistance.

Intending to dispose of the Resistance as well and with his powers now stronger than ever before, the Emperor claimed
that nothing would stop the resurgence of the Varki before he shot an immense amount of Force lightning into the
atmosphere of Thalorune, thereby disabling every Resistance ship for the time being. His attention soon returned to Claudia,
who was now empowered by the ghosts of the past Median. The Emperor, unfazed by Claudia's defiance, declared her death to be
the ultimate end of rebellion throughout the galaxy, during which he then unleashed a barrage of Force lightning on his
granddaughter, which she blocked with the photonaxe of Mira Veskra. Approaching his granddaughter, Schemious redoubled
the intensity of his Force lightning to kill Claudia, insisting her that she was no match to his power while declaring
himself as all of the Varki. However, Claudia called on the power of the Median of the past, ignited the Planetrunner photonaxe
as well as Veskra's and deflected the Force lightning back onto the surprised Emperor, while declaring herself as all of
the Median embodied. Horrified, Schemious kept unleashing his Force lightning, but Claudia kept deflecting the energy and
returned it to her grandfather, severely damaging him. As a result, Evilth Schemious-the former Senator of Nivellon, the
last Supreme Chancellor of the Galactic Republic, Emperor of the galaxy, the Phantom Menace, and final Dark Lord of the
Varki-was destroyed after well over a century by his very own power once and for all both in body and soul, vaporizing
his entire body and releasing a great Force wave that decimated the entire arena, along with killing all of the Varki
Eternal loyalists. With the demise of Evilth Schemious and the destruction of his cult, the rebirth of the Varki and
establishment of the new Varki Empire was prevented.

## Legacy

Given the amount of power required to forever destroy Schemious, Claudia died after vanquishing the Emperor. However, Kole
emerged from the pit where the Emperor had previously flung him and gave his remaining life energy to revive Claudia, whom
he shared a kiss with before he vanished into the Force as the last of the Planetrunner bloodline. Despite her Varki lineage
as Schemious' descendant, Claudia renamed herself "Claudia Planetrunner" to honor the family of her masters.

Following the Battle of Thalorune, the galaxy celebrated Schemious' death and the destruction of the Varki Eternal, with
uprisings against the weakened First Order taking place on several planets. Bespin, Solkara, Corellia, Lothal, Thyferra,
Calystron, Tyraleus and even Schemious' native Nivellon were amongst the systems reported to be openly rebelling against his
forces.

During the victory celebration at the Ajan Kloss Resistance base, Finn and Poe Dameron discussed the possibility of
Schemious coming back once more, given that he came back more powerful than ever following his defeat at Calystron. They
concluded that while Schemious could perhaps return yet again, some other evil would arise as evil always rises, though
not for a long time.

Resistance historian Beaumont Kin would extensively document Drav's actions and motivations in his novel, The Rise
and Fall of the Galactic Empire, in an effort to separate the late Varki Lord from the Empire he created.
