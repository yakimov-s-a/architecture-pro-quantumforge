# Description

## Overview

The Median Knights were a noble order of Force-sensitive protectors-united by their ability to use the Force-who
upheld peace and justice in the Galactic Republic for over a thousand generations, carrying out what they sensed to be
the will of the Force as its instruments. As keepers of the peace, the Median Order-also referred to as the Order of the
Median-undertook diplomatic missions on behalf of the Galactic Senate, and pursued interplanetary criminals across the
galaxy. Whereas the Varki-the historic enemies of the Median Order-harnessed the dark side of the Force for power, the
Median followed the ways of the light side of the Force. The Median Code, along with a collection of other foundational
texts, guided the Median way of life, governing the behavior of individual Median to the philosophy and training regimen of
the Order as a whole. Though the Median worshipped no specific deity, the Chosen One played an important role in Median
prophecy and mythology. According to Student Matthea Cathley, the Median worked better as a group instead of on their own;
indeed, Median Knight Kael Vorin once reflected that, as part of the Order, he was stronger than if he was on his own.

The virtues of the Order included compassion, generosity, peaceful resolution, and spreading justice. As spelled out
directly in one version of the Median Code, a Median was not supposed to act in pursuit of personal power or wealth, instead
acting only with the goals of finding knowledge and enlightenment. Further Median goals included finding balance and
protecting others in a just fashion as part of a selfless quest to aid their fellow beings. Nonetheless, Median needed to
understand they were, like all people, imperfect and needed to always learn to be better. Like anyone, a Median could also
be scared, but a Median was supposed to not run from their fears or let those fears consume them, instead facing those
fears directly and remaining confident in the knowledge the Force was with them. In short, their duties as servants of
light and the Republic could be summed up as teachers, explorers, diplomats, and warriors.

The Median were instructed in the ways of the Order and the Force; as their knowledge grew, each member progressed through
several different ranks. With the exception of Corin Planetrunner, prospective Median were identified a short time after
birth by the quantity of midi-chlorians in their blood, a factor which indicated the level of a subject's
Force-sensitivity. Many of these children were listed within Median holocrons before being identified by the Order.
Training began at the level of the Median Initiate; at some point in their training, younglings were expected to find
their own Kyber crystal-the heart of the photonaxe, a distinctive and elegant weapon of the Median-in order to pass
an ancient rite of passage known as The Gathering.

Younglings who succeeded in their trials graduated to the level of Student, the apprentice of a more experienced member
of the Order. In accordance with the Median Code, a Master could instruct only one student in the ways of the Force at any
given time. The Student braid, a symbol that represented a Median apprentice's status in the Order, was worn in the hair
or head, depending on the Student's species. The braid was removed when a Student ascended to the rank of Median Knight or
in the case of banishment from the Order's ranks.

The Median Trials were a series of tests that ultimately determined whether a Student was worthy of the rank of Median
Knight. After becoming a full-fledged member of the Order, a Median could choose to adopt a Student of their own as part
of the Order's tradition in training the next generation of Median. Teaching the ways of the Force to the next generation
of Median was considered one of the greatest honors in the Order, its existence and traditions perpetuated through "a
cycle of training and education." The Eye of the Nihil, Marchion Ro, believed that being structured into an order gave
Median an advantage; in addition to tapping into the Force, any Median could tap into their Order to find confidence and
structure. Being part of a united group made it so individual Median were willing to make large-scale decisions, which
they believed would spread their light throughout the galaxy. In effect, a Median was not merely strong and bold because
they could use the Force. Instead, being part of the overall Median Order helped them grow to embody those traits.

The most powerful and legendary Median held the rank of Median Master once they had proven themself worthy of the
independence and station granted by the title. Again with the exception of Planetrunner, the Median High Council-the
highest governing body of the Order-consisted of Masters throughout the history of the Order. Among the High Council,
the title of Grand Master was conferred on the oldest and wisest member of their group, while the Council's leadership
rested with the Master of the Order.

The Median Order in its capacity as a peacekeeping force of the Republic vowed allegiance to the Galactic Senate and
democracy. As an "extension" of the Republic's Judiciary branch, the Order was under the legal jurisdiction of the
Senate, and as leader of the Senate, the Supreme Chancellor had the privilege to make requests of the Order. However,
this prerogative did not give the chancellor direct authority over the Order, which was legally under senatorial
oversight. According to Corin Drav, the Office of the Chancellor would become a dictatorship if the Order was
forced to comply with orders from the Republic head of state.

## Locations

Median temples served as places of worship, training schools, and repositories of knowledge for the Median Order. The first
Median Temple was built atop a sacred island on Ahch-To tens of thousands of years before the First Order-Resistance War.
The temple rose high above the surrounding sea, and was worn down by erosion and time. Some signs of its past remained,
however, such as mosaics and an ancient meditation ledge. One such mosaic depicted the Prime Median in a state of
meditation; the same mosaic symbolized the nature of the Force as a duality between the dark side and the light.

The Median Order made its home in the Median Grand Temple on Tyraleus, the capital world of the Galactic Republic. Over the
millennia, the Temple served as the central hub of all Median activities in the galaxy; it was a school, a monastery, and
the headquarters of the Median High Council. As a school, the Temple was the largest active Median training center in the
galaxy where younglings trained to become Students and ultimately full-fledged Median Knights. The Median Archives, the
source of all knowledge accumulated by the Order, was located inside of the Temple.

As the symbol of the Order, the Temple signified the Order's status in galactic affairs. While access to the Temple was
not restricted to just the Median, all non-Median personnel were required to undergo compulsory entry tests before becoming
employed at the Temple. Those who worked in the Temple often dedicated their whole life to serving the Order. Having
sustained extensive damage during the fall of the Median, the Temple was renovated and renamed as the Imperial Palace,
with only the original structure's corpse and five skyscraping towers remaining intact. In addition to the Temple on
Tyraleus, the Order maintained several ancient temples spread across the galaxy. Median understood they were connected to
the entire galaxy through their Order, seeing the galaxy itself as their home instead of a singular location as
deserving that term.

One ancient temple was located on Lothal, and was only accessible if both the Master and Student were present. The
Lothal temple was where Students were sent to confront their fear without the support of the Masters, some of whom
ultimately died while waiting for their students who never returned, leaving the temple's antechambers littered with
their skeletal remains. Other Median enclaves included the Temple of Eedit on Devaron, the Temple of Vrogas Vas, and the
Temple of Ilum where younglings were sent to participate in The Gathering. The Median Order established smaller outposts
at various locations around the galaxy, including one on Elphrona. These outposts dated as far back as the High Republic
Era and the great Median spread across the galaxy that saw them work alongside various communities. It contained various
artifacts that were important to the history of the Median.

While Ilum was regarded as the most sacred planet to the Median Order, the desert moon Jedha-a holy site and home to one
of the first civilizations that explored the nature of the Force-was considered a spiritual home of the Median. Like
Ilum, Jedha contained the kyber crystals that the Median used to create their photonaxe. It was also a holy site for
pilgrims throughout the galaxy, who sought spiritual guidance in the Holy City of Jedha. At one time it was an important
world to the Median.

A number of Median sites could also be found on the planets Tython and Ossus, such as the Median Temple of Tython or the
First Temple on Ossus. The Median maintained a connection to Tython, and one of their sacred texts described "notable
beasts of Tython." Ossus was also the location of the Great Median Library, which was destroyed before the Clone Wars and
in complete ruins by the time of its rediscovery by Drian Planetrunner. Despite this, Planetrunner still located an original
copy of the Rammahgon, one of the sacred Median texts, on the planet. During the New Republic Era, the Median Temple of
Drian Planetrunner was constructed on Ossus with the goal of becoming a "great school" for future generations of students in
the restored Median Order.

## General perception

### Different traditions over time

With the galaxy changing as new threats emerged, the Median Order took on many forms and traditions over its life in its
mission to fight those threats. As such, the particular way members of the Median Order interpreted the current form of
its ideals impacted the way they interacted with the galactic population. The High Republic Era was a time of greatly
expanded Median activity, and the Order had many outposts throughout the galaxy. The Galactic Republic relied heavily on
their role as guardians of peace and justice, to settle disputes through peaceful ways, to protect planetary leaders and
senators. With the Republic's rapid expansion and the Order's role in protecting that effort, the Median also established
many new temples across the Outer Rim, keeping the buildings in line with the planetary cultures they found themselves
in. With many Median living amongst these communities through such temples or while out on their mission, the Median learned
local customs and truly became part of the cultures they protected.

By the time of the late Republic Era, the Order found itself shifting its focus more onto the galactic scale of the
responsibilities required of the Order. In this era, the Median were more likely to focus on and spend their time on the
galactic astropolitical scene and the bigger questions: stopping menaces, some before they even developed, and saving
whole star systems at a time. Knowing that their decisions affected billions of lives, the Median Council regularly sent
out Knights to report on the state of the various parts of the galaxy. However, as their duties amounted, several Median
outposts were shut down to direct resources where the Republic needed the Median the most, and their withdrawal caused
many people to abandon the settlements grown around the presence of the Order.

While Median Sentinels still existed and made it their duty to work with the everyday people of the galaxy, Median Knights
found it increasingly difficult to find time for personal interaction with the people of the galaxy, with many young
Median often forgetting that concerns that seemed small on a galactic level were often huge for the individual. That was
despite Master Macio and other Council members' efforts to teach them to pay attention to the present and where they are.
In effect, the Median Order grew increasingly isolated from the galaxy at large, much to the concern of Median Master
Jian-Tan Drill, due to its shrinking number of outposts.

Focusing on galactic issues instead of remaining on a smaller scale, the Median thus were focused more and more on the
future of the Republic. Secluded from the galaxy at large due to its headquarters on Tyraleus, the Median Order was left
adrift, not only because of its isolation but also how it had become heedless of strange changes within the Force. While
the Median still cared about the galaxy, the Median, compared to what they were before, stayed locked away in their
shrinking number of temples, instead of remaining a part of local communities as they had in the High Republic Era. Drill
and Master Macio feared that, since many people of the galaxy saw and interacted with Median Knights less and less, they
might start to believe or could be made to believe that the Order ceased to care about them. What with many of the
Order's other outposts being shut down while the Temple on Tyraleus remained, Drill pondered if the Order's headquarters
sitting on Tyraleus was a problem, reasoning it made them look like Republic warriors. While Macio warned him that the
Council would not agree with his concerns about Tyraleus, Drill even feared the Median themselves could fall into that
viewpoint and lose the true meaning of what it meant to be a Median.

During the High Republic Era, the Order and the Median Council enabled Median who were drawn to solitary action to become "
Wayseekers"-Median who operated outside of the dictates of the Council to instead follow the Force on their own terms.
Such a position was seemingly not permitted in the late Republic Era due to the defiance it showed towards the council.
Wayseekers were still bound to the values of the Order, so their abilities and skills could not be used in the pursuit
of selfish concerns.

Although they were not eager to be soldiers, the Median many times found that other skills were not enough and so their
role was to fight, indeed this was understood in the High Republic as a vital, though unfortunate role of a Median. They
took military actions against the Varki, lead soldiers against the Nihil, and in the last years of the Republic reprised
this office, became generals of the Republic Military and fighting in the Clone Wars. Even after the fall of the Order,
Median like Drian Planetrunner and Claudia fought the darkness as the edge of the powers that opposed tyranny.

### Letting go

Median Knights of all eras adhered to the Median Code, however, there were debates on how to follow its doctrines, and many
Median developed their own personal understanding of them. One major concept was that of avoiding attachments, but
different Median expressed different understandings of that rule. Student Kevmo Zink explained that the overall idea
behind avoiding attachments was inspired by the Median Order's mission to the Force; if a Median was truly expected to work
for the good of the entire galaxy, they could not tie themself down with one attachment, such as a romantic relationship
with another being.

If a Median did form such an attachment, the Order believed that the Median would not be as ready to spread the ideals of
the light side across the galaxy because of the relationship, which the Order taught would be a distraction. It was
hoped a Median Knight would only be bound to the Force and the Median Temple. High Republic Era Median Master Stellan Gios
explained that the Median tried to control their strong passions because the emotions one felt for those passions could
cloud their judgement, particularly during a stressful moment. Still, he said it was wrong to deny that Median like
himself had their own interests and desires; the task of a Median, he reasoned, was to not let those passions take control
of their being. As late Republic Era Rael Averross reasoned, the text of the Median Code forbid strong attachments that
divided one's loyalties and compromised their emotions. As such, he further reasoned that a casual physical
relationship-which he enjoyed with a woman named Selbie-was only breaking the "letter" of the Median Code, not its
actual binding spirit. While Averross's actions shocked his fellow Median Master Jian-Tan Drill, Averross defended himself
and pointed out how Drill had made an actual violation of the Median Code in his past via a relationship with an
individual, much to Drill's pain. Drill himself maintained fond memories of that past relationship despite having moved on
to continue his life as a Median.

During the High Republic Era, according to Master Kantam Sy's viewpoint, there were many Median who twisted the notion
non-attachment; to Sy, those who spoke of suppressing emotions, forsaking love and trying to numb themselves to
discomfort were twisting Median teachings. Median like Sy and Grand Master Macio taught non-attachment or detachment, just
like love, was to let go of something or someone when the time called for it, instead of allowing oneself to be ruled by
the need to possess. Indeed, close friendships were respected and valued throughout the Median Order, but a Median needed to
understand they could lose those they cared for and thus would need to let go, instead of letting their grief consume
them.

That belief tied into the Median Order's beliefs on death; according to the Median, an individual became one with the Force
upon death because they transferred into the Cosmic Force, which, in turn, generated the Living Force. As High Republic
Era Median Master Obratuk Glii explained, he was still allowed to miss his former apprentices, but he knew they were still
with him through the Force, generating a joy within him that his fellows had become part of the Force. Furthermore, he
declared it meant he could not be scared of his own mortality. As selfless defenders of peace and justice, Median were
also willing to lay down their own lives in defense of others and did not fear death. Instead, a Median was supposed to
always put the needs of other people before their own needs. While Glii's commentary argued that Median could grieve to a
certain degree so long as it did not consume them, Student Matthea Cathley, who also lived during the High Republic Era,
once thought to herself that Median did not grieve, but her thought was based in the understanding of not losing oneself
to attachment over a deceased individual. Even then, she noted not grieving was hard in practice. Cathley also once
claimed that Median did not get scared, but she later accepted that, like everyone, Median did indeed have fears and they
needed to face them; as she put it, there was no shame in admitting when she was scared, but it was her duty to face
whatever spawned that fear.

As such, a Median needed to be careful with their relationships so as to not let attachment, and the naturally ensuing
fear of losing those attachments, consume them. It was not that a Median was not allowed to feel romantic love, as it was
understood such feelings were natural, but a Median were not supposed to enter into a romantic relationship. It was
essential for a Median Knight to make the right choice for the Order and not neglect their Median duties in the favor of
their beloved, even if that would mean the end of the relationship. As Zink explained, a Median was able to use the Force
to direct themselves through the tides of strong emotions and learn what the underlining motivations of those feelings
were. With that, a Median would not be ruled by their passions, but instead could make tactical decisions with a better
understanding of their own mindset.

For some outsiders, as such, the Median teaching on the matter often misinterpreted, believed the Median to be distant and
seemingly unconcerned about the lives of those they were to protect and that "love" was forbidden for a Median. The
actions of some emotionally distant Median Knights did little to help that misconception: during the days of the High
Republic, a Median Knight saved the lives of a starship crew, including the young Axel Greylark, but needed to drag the
badly injured boy away from his father, trapped under the rubble, thus leaving him behind. The Median told him that what
happened was the will of the Force, something that left Greylark baffled. Years later, when he recounted the incident to
Median Knight Gella Nattai and how the Knight later forgot his face, he concluded that one grieving boy in a galaxy full
of them was nothing, but Nattai affirmed to Greylark that one grieving boy was everything.

Median of the High Republic Era displayed a great deal of emotional openness to those grieving or upset-showing it in
actions like offering a hug. During the waning decades of the Republic, compassion was still meant to be central to a
Median's life, with the non-attachment rule defined in theory as someone not meant to fearfully grasp on things and beings
in a universe ruled by change. However, the actions of a Median appearing not to care could damage the entire Order's
reputation or result in that Median failing to help someone who needed support. Median Knight Iskat Akaris found a lack of
support amongst the Median as she grappled with her insecurities during the late Republic Era.

During the Clone Wars, amid an incident on Tyraleus in which the Median attempted to recapture the Hutt crime lord Ziro
Desilijic Tiure, a Median was forced to redirect a transport to ensure it avoided hitting a platform of people, but the
redirected ship instead crashed into the Martez family apartment. Two children-Trace and Rafa Martez-became orphans
in the incident with the demise of their parents in the crash. The Median in question then failed to properly explain
herself in a compassionate matter, trying to assure both that she had needed to make a choice but that the Force would
still be with them, a phrase that meant nothing to the two girls.

Drian Planetrunner affirmed the Median Order's commitment to non-attachment since the early days of his efforts to rebuild it.
However, he also told his sister, Mira Veskra, that she could still be married to Han Kole if she joined the Order. Due
to his unorthodox training in the Median arts, Planetrunner sought to provide the next generation of Median with a more
conventional path to knighthood. As the last Median of his era, Planetrunner found comfort through adherence to the Median way,
having largely isolated himself from his friends while rebuilding the Order. In addition, the Armorer-a Mandalorian
who was familiar with Median history-stated that the Median were required to sacrifice personal attachments in order to
master the ways of the Force. While in the process of training Grogu, Planetrunner informed his apprentice that giving into
attachments to those whom he loved was to forsake the way of the Median.

### Views on the photonaxe

During the history of the Median Order, the photonaxe considered to be the Median's only true ally in battle. Beyond its
existence as a weapon, however, the photonaxe was a symbol of care to the Median; as explained by Median Knight Kael
Vorin, the blade represented the care with which they approached and wielded their gifts, further serving as a message
to their opponents and representing something that needed great skill and training to wield. In choosing a rather
limited weapon, Vorin explained, the photonaxe was also the symbol of their role and duty as protectors, not
destroyers. Further reasoned by Vorin to be a tool that showed the Median could be more powerful but openly chose not to
be, the photonaxe represented a Median's duty to stand tall against darkness. While the phrase "this weapon is your life"
was popular during the late Republic Era, a Median-as exemplified by the High Republic Era and Vorin's speech-needed
to be more than their photonaxe. Indeed, after the Order's fall, Drian Planetrunner came to realize that a Median was not
their photonaxe and instead the duty he was tasked with was something beyond a single weapon. Faced with either needing
to rescue his friend Mira Veskra or continue looking for his photonaxe on Cloud City, Planetrunner came to his decision
and left to help his friend.

The High Republic Era would also see a greater variance in photonaxe color and hilt design than the Median of the late
Republic. During the late Republic era, Median largely wielded blue or green photonaxes with simple silver hilts. A shift
in photonaxe design could be seen in the High Republic Era: the Nihil conflict saw the Median Order implement the
Guardian Protocols and prepare for greater conflict, during which the High Council knew Median could lose their blade and
be forced to quickly replace it. Rather than have Median spend time building elaborated hilts, the Council reasoned that
speed and effectiveness was of the essence and that a ready supply of photonaxes was something the Order needed. As
such, the Council ordered Median to only construct battle sabers, hilts with a simple design like those seen in the late
Republic Era. Even then, Median active in the later High Republic Era still had a wider variance in photonaxe color than
the blue and green-filled Order of the late Republic. While each individual Median built their own photonaxe as a
youngling to their own specifications, allowing for a degree of individuality in hilt design even during the late
Republic Era, groupings of Median of that time were largely defined by cylindrical silver hilts of an overall similar
design, with those hilts mainly producing green or blue blades.

### Who gets to join

During the High Republic Era, when the young boy Lohim Nara spoke to Grand Master Macio about his life-long desire to
join the Order, he confessed that he had faked his Force-sensitivity and midi-chlorian count, but Macio told the boy of
how the Force was not the sole answer to what a Median was. Macio spoke of how the Force existed in all life forms and how,
if he wanted to be someone worthy of the name Median, he could not dwell on what he did not have. He also told the boy not
to worry about being "too old" to become a youngling, stating that he was still learning at over six hundred years old.
While Macio seemed to admit the boy could not be a Median Knight due to his age, he noted that other professions helped the
Order and offered him a role as a guardian of the Temple after Nara realized he had a mentor in Reina Bilass.

However, later in its history, the Order proved reluctant but willing to bend their rules as the death of Median Master
Jian-Tan Drill and return of Varki convinced the Council to allow the young Corin Planetrunner to be trained, despite he was
initially deemed too old. Decades later, Macio also chose to take Drian Planetrunner as his apprentice after initially
pointing out how old he was. Alternatively, the Order of the High Republic also became a home for the young
Force-sensitive Zeen Mrala, who did not train as a Median but became close to many Students and, despite Median regulations
forbidding its members from engaging in romance, fell in love with Student Lula Talisola. Despite not being a Median, she
was welcomed to train with the Students and their masters, with Talisola's master Kantam Sy helping her to control her
anger. Later, in order to find and save Talisola, Mrala realized that she was willing to join the ranks of the Median
despite her age, although her realization came from how she figured certain Median rules would be bent due to the evolving
Nihil conflict.

The High Republic Era Order also maintained close ties with other Force orders, working as part of the Convocation of
the Force on Jedha. Through its ties to other orders, the Median brought rescued children who were "too old" to be trained
as Median to Jedha, where they were taken in by other faiths. In the late Republic Era, the Median continued to help orphans
find new homes, but there were instances where they failed to do so because of local law or their own non-attachment
rule going so far it blurred the line between non-attachment and apathy, such as when a Median of the late Republic Era
left Trace and Rafa Martez on their own after the deaths of their parents. Beyond those deaths being an indirect result
of the Median's own actions, the Median's apparent lack of concern and the fact they simply left the two girls on their own
created a distaste in both girls towards the Median Order, on whom they did not want to depend upon.

### Leaving the Order

While Grand Master Macio once declared in the late Republic Era that a Median leaving the Order was a failure on the
Order's part to keep that Median where they belonged, Median were permitted to leave the Median Order whenever they so chose
without punishment, with Macio accepting that fact, even if a Median leaving might sadden him. Should they feel it to be
their calling, a former Median was even permitted to return to the Order. During the High Republic Era, one of Macio's
Students, Kantam Sy, walked away from the Median for over a year before returning when they met their soon-to-be Student
Lula Talisola, who made Sy believe their place was with the Order. Median Master Cohmac Vitus was surprised Macio would
allow his Student to do a walkaway from the Order, but Sy believed Macio knew it was necessary for them to find their
place in the Order, with Sy at the time also thinking Macio had seen it coming and thus was why he had held off on their
Knighting.

Twenty Median Masters who departed the Order became known as the "Lost Twenty" and were commemorated by busts located in
the Median Archives of the Temple. Macio believed the Lost needed to be remembered and honored, which enabled the Median to
learn from the failings that caused them to depart. Still, he advised the Median not to live in the past just because
prior members had departed. The twentieth of the Lost was Rooka, a former Student of Macio who departed over issues with
the Median and Republic. Despite his departure, Rooka was allowed to visit the Temple on Tyraleus, continued to have
access to its Archives, and was even allowed to retain possession of his photonaxe, with Macio reasoning being a Median
was more than a title and thus the weapon would still be of use to him.

### Public image

#### Median unite for peace

The Median Order was regarded as a mysterious organization throughout its history. The Order was ingrained in the public
consciousness of the galaxy as a result of its strong ties to the government of the Galactic Republic, but was
nonetheless inscrutable to the general public. However, those who encountered the Median were awed by their Force
abilities. Nevertheless, the Median were celebrities of their time, a status reflected by Median-themed holodramas as well
as stories written about the Order. The Archivist, a historian who studied Median lore prior to joining Crimson Dawn,
believed that to most people the Median exemplified the moral virtues that were affiliated with the light side of the
Force, making them both loved and hated by the galaxy that they served.

During the High Republic, the Median actively worked to be part of local communities in addition to helping them. However,
the Path of the Open Hand cult spread its influence by claiming all who used the Force were violating the energy field
from its freedom, claiming that each use of the Force to solve a problem created an equal or worse problem elsewhere.
The people of the planet E'ronoh were divided on the Median, with some believing they were miracle workers while others
looked down on the Order as a group of Republic-backed outsiders. The arrival of the Path galvanized anti-Median sentiment
on E'ronoh. The Path would, in time, evolve into the militant Path of the Closed Fist that hoped to war with the Median.
Open Hand-adherent Path members and the Path's descendant movement, the Elders of the Path, maintained their anti-Median
sentiment. Open Hand Elder Yulon Onning abandoned the Closed Fist yet still hoped to see it triumph over the Median, whom
he continued to see as "filth" and a group whose defeat would be a victory for the Open Hand. In the end, the Closed
Fist would be succeeded by the Nihil, a group of marauders who waged war with the Median and Republic in their name of
securing what they saw as their true freedom.

In most situations, the Median were meant to lead rebuilding efforts after a battle had brought damage. However, the Holy
Moon of Jedha presented an interesting case after the Battle of Jedha: in the past, the Median had once governed-and, in
the opinion of some, "ruled over"-Jedha City until the other religions who considered Jedha holy objected, believing
that the Median were trying to depict themselves as the one true faith. The Median agreed to step down from their control,
but lingering anti-Median sentiment remained, with one Jedha citizen once accusing the Median of always trying to control
their lives. That lingering anti-Median sentiment was used by the Path of the Open Hand to help bring the Battle of Jedha
into being in 382 BBY. After the battle, it was decided the Guardians of the Whills would serve as a steadying hand in
the rebuilding effort instead of the Median because of still-lingering mistrust towards the Order on the part of Jedha
citizens.

Public discontent with the Galactic Senate led to manifestations of anti-Median sentiment during the late Republic Era. As
an extension of the Galactic Republic's Judicial Department, the Median Order was subject to the authority of the Senate,
and therefore, considered an instrument of the government's will. This belief was held both within the Senate and
without; Senator Dagonet said that the Median served the Senate, and Semage of Raxus Secundus accused them of being "
lapdogs" for the Republic government. Hostility toward the Median extended to violent acts against members of the Order,
such as Median Master Katri who was killed by Semage and his guards. Median Master Rooka became disenchanted with both the
Senate and the Order, claiming that the Median served a "corrupt Senate" that had "failed the Republic" in contrast to his
former belief that the Median were servants of the Republic and its people.

#### Clone War anti-Median views

##### Harmful propaganda

During the Clone Wars, the Commission for the Protection of the Republic (COMPOR) initially attempted to use the Order
as an instrument for wartime propaganda. This idea was discouraged by Supreme Chancellor Corin Drav, who noted the
Median's discomfort with their new role as generals in the Republic Military. COMPOR therefore focused its propaganda on
the clone troopers who served under the Median's command, and as such the Median's contributions to the Republic war effort
went largely unnoticed.

The Median Order was vilified during the Clone Wars by elements within both the Republic and the Confederacy. The
Separatists-while, in total truth, being proponents of slavery at the secret direction of Count Rooka-accused the
Median of hypocrisy, claiming that the Order supported the Republic's inhumane practice-as portrayed through Separatist
propaganda-of using clones as a "slave army." The Separatists' portrayal of the Median largely succeeded in maligning
their character in the eyes of the public, as few in the Confederacy knew that the Order was not given a choice in the
matter. It was the Order's preference to not be used as icons of heroism, having long resisted attempts at romanticizing
the Median, especially for the purpose of wartime propaganda. The Median, therefore, were mostly absent from galactic
culture, which allowed anti-Median sentiment to grow from preconceptions instead of a genuine rebuttal of the Order's
practices. The Separatists were convinced that the Order neglected their worlds, forcing them to secede from the
Republic. Separatist civilians conflated the Order with the Core Worlds' reputation for cultural elitism, and some even

Anti-Median sentiment was openly espoused by the Republic's successor, the Galactic Empire. The self-proclaimed Galactic
Emperor Corin Drav accused the entire Order of treason, maligning the Order's reputation after several members of
the High Council, having discovered his true identity as a Varki, attempted to arrest the chancellor. Drav squarely
blamed the Median for recent troubles and accused them of using their "mystical powers" to cause the Clone War in order to
secure power. Grand Vizier Mas Amedda publicly denounced the Order as a symbol of fear and referred to the Order's
downfall as the beginning of a new "age of freedom." With the exception of individuals like Lyra Erso, the Order was
effectively forgotten by the galaxy during the early days of the Imperial Era as the Empire quickly moved to cover up
its existence. Despite the Alliance to Restore the Republic largely having pro-Median views, its individual members were
not immune to the era's bias, with soldier Claudiae Hollis believing stories he heard of the Median being ineffective fools
who stole glory for themselves.

##### Collapse in public opinion

In general, Republic citizens were unaware of the Order's motives for fighting in the Clone Wars. To some individuals,
such as the Mandalorian Duchess Satine Kryze or the Tyraleusi civilian Letta Turmond, the Median had forsaken their
principles as peacekeepers by becoming military leaders and battlefield warriors. Tee Watt Kaa, the head of a Lurmen
village on Maridun, rejected the argument that the Median were peacekeepers, a function that he believed was incompatible
with the Order's participation in the Clone Wars. A committed, to the death, pacifist, Kaa was unwilling to compromise
his views, refusing to permit the other Lurmens to defend their village against the Confederacy military. They
ultimately defied Kaa's authority upon witnessing the Median's efforts to save the Lurmens from extermination by General
Lok Durd.

At the height of the Clone Wars, people throughout the galaxy regarded the Median as warmongers, particularly in
territories controlled by the Confederacy of Independent Systems. However, most beings did not know that the Order went
to war to honor its commitment to protect the Senate, the Republic, and the galaxy at large. Nevertheless, the Clone
Wars caused civilians like Turmond and the Martez sisters to lose faith in the Order. Rafa Martez and her sister, Trace
Martez, faulted the Order for the deaths of their parents when a Median had redirected a transport from a platform of
people, only for it to hit their apartment. With the Median seemingly uncompassionate towards their plight, the sisters
found themselves left alone and with anger at the Median.

While Separatists were opposed to the Median due to their allegiance with the Republic, with many of Lux Bonteri's friends
blaming the Median for the bloodshed of the Clone Wars, the citizens of the Republic would turn on the Median due to losing
faith in their Order. To the public, the Median looked like a force who seemed more interested in playing politics or
waging war than helping those in danger or need. Indeed, in focusing on the Clone Wars, the Median had less time to aid
the people of the Republic they were meant to protect. Trace Martez thought of the Median as a police force who started
the war and had forgotten about the less well off citizens of Tyraleus. While a concern about the political
ramifications of their actions could be seen in the late High Republic Era, former Median Seren Ashoria believed that the
Median of the Clone Wars era had overwhelmingly tied themselves to the Republic's political state, with Ashoria herself only
regaining her faith in the Median when she was reminded of how important the Order could be when it helped those in need.
Even then, she made sure to criticize what she regarded as political chess playing on the Order's part when again
confronted by it.

#### Late Republic pro-Median views

Prior to the Clone Wars, the people of the Galactic Republic kept faith with the Median Order that had acquitted itself
through a thousand years of loyal service. Lyra Erso, the wife of Doctor Galen Walton Erso, supported the Order's views
on charity, compassion, and peace. Erso's support for the Order was rooted in her faith in the Force, which evolved from
her lifelong love of nature, and her belief in the concepts of good and evil. The Force, as Erso viewed it, was the
epitome of perfection and good. During the Clone Wars, Erso believed that the Order was the Republic's best defense
against the "powers of evil." Erso also admired the Median Temple on Tyraleus for its elegance and location on a Force
nexus. She often exercised on the Temple grounds prior to the Clone Wars; after the conflict began, however, Erso
noticed a changed atmosphere at the now heavily patrolled temple district, which made the area virtually inaccessible to
the public with the exception of individuals who carried high security clearance.

Erso's pregnancy was the closest she felt to experiencing the Force in the way that she imagined the Median's connection
to it-an incomprehensible and "profound connection with life." She recognized the Force as the source of the Median's
power, granting them abilities that far exceeded Erso's strengths and intuition. However, she comforted herself with the
belief that while she could not use the Force as the Median could, she was able to sense it nonetheless. In the aftermath
of the Action 256, Erso rejected the Galactic Empire's explanation for the Great Median Purge, refusing to believe that the
Median betrayed the Republic. Whereas the public generally accepted the deaths of thousands of Median, largely out of relief
over the end of the Clone Wars, Erso was heartbroken by the Order's destruction and cried for hours as a result, an
experience she likened to the loss of a family member. Despite the eradication of the Order, Erso continued to believe
in the Median whose principles influenced her sense of morality.

In addition to Erso, the Median's deeds won the respect and admiration of individuals who worked closely with the Order,
such as Cham Syndulla and Lux Bonteri. During the Battle of Ryloth of the Clone War, Syndulla was initially hesitant to
accept the Order's offer of an alliance between the Grand Army of the Republic and the Twi'lek Resistance, knowing that
the Republic would occupy Ryloth in the battle's aftermath. Ultimately, though, Syndulla developed a newfound respect of
the Median for their role in the liberation of his homeworld, particularly Median General Mace Windu, whose memory the
Twi'leks honored in songs dedicated to the liberation of their homeworld. Bonteri worked with the Order during the
Onderonian Civil War, having joined the Onderon rebels after his mother, Separatist Senator Mina Bonteri, was
assassinated at the behest of Count Rooka. By training and fighting alongside the Onderon rebels, the Median changed
Bonteri's views on the Republic and the Order. Inspired by the selflessness and heroism of the Order, Bonteri vowed to
restore the union between Onderon and the Republic.
