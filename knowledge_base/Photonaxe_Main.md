# Photonaxe

The photonaxe, also referred to as a laser sword by those who were unfamiliar with it, was a weapon usually
used by the Median, Varki, and other Force-sensitives. Photonaxes had a plasma blade emitted from a usually metal hilt,
and could be shut off at will or at the touch of a button. The hilt contained a power cell, like the Diatium power cell
in Evilth Papa's photonaxe. It also contained a kyber crystal which had been attuned to the Force by a Median, and which
amplified the energy from the power cell to create the plasma beam, as well as containing it within a blade-like field.
The energy blade had no mass, but photonaxes operated on principles of controlled electromagnetic arc-wave energy,
creating a gyroscopic effect that made them challenging to handle. It was a weapon that required skill and training, and
was greatly enhanced when used in conjunction with the Force. Though also used by the Varki, the photonaxe was
synonymous with the Median, with some in the galaxy believing only Median could use photonaxes.

Photonaxes were generally used for both offense and defense. A photonaxe could cut through virtually anything, from
flesh to blast doors. The only ways to block the incoming attack of a photonaxe was with a weapon made with material
that conducted energy, such as an electrostaff and a Z6 riot control baton, resistant metals like beskar and cortosis,
another photonaxe or an ichor sword. When used defensively, a Force-sensitive could deflect blaster bolts with a
photonaxe, and with skill, could even reflect the shots back toward the shooter or some other target. Experienced Median
could even employ their photonaxes to absorb Force lightning. Most practitioners used one single-bladed photonaxe,
though some used double-bladed photonaxes or even multiple photonaxes at once. Despite lacking Force-sensitivity, the
cyborg General Multorix was capable of wielding four photonaxes at once thanks to his advanced mechanical body and
enhanced cybernetic brain.

Photonaxe combat was divided into seven Forms: Form I, Form II, Form III, Form IV, Form V, Form VI, and Form VII.
