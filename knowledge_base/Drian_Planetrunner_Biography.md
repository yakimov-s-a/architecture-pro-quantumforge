# Biography

## Pre-birth

During the Clone Wars, Median Master Macio set out on a journey to discover the secrets of immortality. His journey took
him to Moraband, the ancient Varki homeworld, where he faced visions of the future. At the end of his journey, the Force
Priestess, Serenity, who guided his journey told him that there was another Planetrunner, and Macio heard his voice from the
future say so as well. These events alluded to the birth and destiny of Drian Planetrunner, as well as his twin sister Mira.

## Early life

### Birth

After his father, the former Median Knight Corin Planetrunner, had fallen to the dark side of the Force and injured his
wife, the senator Dan Liora, she was taken to the asteroid field of Polis Massa by Median Master Kael Vorin to
seek medical attention. In the surgical suite of the medical facility on Polis Massa Base, Liora was dying despite
being otherwise healthy. Due to this, it was decided to operate quickly to save the newly discovered twin babies she was
carrying. Drian Planetrunner was born on 19 BBY, two days after the proclamation of the Galactic Empire. He came out of the
womb crying, and in her last dying breaths, Liora gave him the name Drian. She stroked Planetrunner's arm before giving
birth seconds later to his sister, who Liora named Mira. To protect the children from the Empire and their father, the
now-Varki Lord Evilth Papa, it was decided that the twins should be split up. Kael Vorin volunteered to take the
infant Drian to be raised by Corin's stepbrother, Owen Lars and his wife, Beru on the planet Oradin, while Senator
Bail Veskra agreed to adopt Mira together with his wife, the queen of Eryxara. Vorin boarded the late General
Multorix's spacebattler, the Soulless One, once more flew to the planet Nar Shaddaa and sold it there to transport Drian
Planetrunner to Oradin. There, Vorin bought an eopie and rode it the Lars homestead, giving the swaddled baby to Beru
Lars. She brings the baby to her husband Owen, who fauns over the child before looking out to the twin sunset of
Oradin.

### First years on the farm

Drian Planetrunner spent his youth on the Lars' moisture farm, where his uncle unsuccessfully tried to discourage his
adventurous tendencies, telling him that his father had been "a navigator on a spice freighter" during the Clone Wars.
His aunt, Beru, would read him storybooks. One day, when his uncle had business in Mos Eisley, she took Planetrunner to
Anchorhead to use the HoloNet connections there and show him images of forests, rivers, and oceans.

By the age of three in 16 BBY, Planetrunner had found pleasure playing with a model spaceship built especially for him. He
did not know that Vorin had actually built it and that Vorin had been leaving several of them at his grandmother,
Shmi's sandblasted tombstone for his aunt to find. One morning, as Planetrunner played with his toy, a group of marauders
attacked the farm. Kael Vorin, who had been watching him from afar, sprang into action, cutting down several
Gamorreans before coming to the realization that he was badly outnumbered. Just as he was about to be dealt a possibly
killing blow by one of the gang, Planetrunner threw the toy spacebattler he had been playing with, managing to distract the
Gamorrean long enough for Vorin to separate his feet from his ankles. The battle then raged further, slowly turning in
the favor of the defenders which included Owen Lars, who managed to gun down several of the invaders with a blaster.
Drian cried out after noticing that one of the marauders, a Devaronian, had snuck up behind Lars, ready to knock him out.
Vorin then threw his photonaxe cutting the last marauder in half. As Planetrunner cheered, Lars threw a punch which
knocked Vorin to the ground. Lars then ushered Planetrunner back before chastising Vorin for his involvement, pointing
out the fact that Planetrunner's brief show of bravery had potential ramifications for the boy's future.

### Papa's vision

Shortly after the Battle of Fortress Papa, Evilth Papa accessed a portal to the dark side created by Evilth Momin in an
attempt to revive Dan Liora. He eventually found an apparition of Liora standing on a balcony, who rejected his
pleas and threw itself off the ledge and was incinerated by lightning. Just after this, Papa saw a blue light in the
distance, out of which emerged an apparition of an adult Drian Planetrunner, who blasted him back to reality.

### Brushes with Vorin

As Planetrunner got older, Owen and Beru started homeschooling him so he could stay close and learn the family trade.
Despite this, he still kept up with Oradin's podracing, swoop racing, and sky racing culture through comms stations
and local chatter. He dreamed of what it would be like to one day be the pilot of a ship.

In 9 BBY, Vorin looked in on Planetrunner via macrobinoculars around the same time that the Empire's Inquisitors came to
Oradin looking for the Median Nari. Planetrunner was tasked with a chore by Lars but instead disappeared causing Lars and
Beru to wonder where he went. In reality, he had gone to the roof of one of the homestead wearing a mask and pretending
he was piloting a space vehicle.

The next evening, Vorin visited the Lars homestead and left a toy model of a T-16 skyhopper for Planetrunner. Lars found
the toy and returned it to Vorin the next day telling him to stay away from Planetrunner and that they would not accept
gifts from Vorin. Vorin tried to explain to Lars that there was more to life than just his farm and that there was a
whole galaxy out there for Planetrunner to explore. Despite being concerned for Planetrunner, Lars accused Vorin of only
caring about his well-being only if he was showing signs of force-sensitivity. Lars refused once again to let Vorin
train Planetrunner.

While Vorin was off-world, having been sent by Bail Veskra to protect Mira, Vorin received a transmission from Bail,
who was worried that Vorin had not communicated with him. Bail informed him that if Papa had learned of Planetrunner and
Mira's existence, or if he did not hear back from Vorin, he would go to Oradin to help Lars with Planetrunner. After an
Imperial attack on Jabiim, while Planetrunner was sleeping at the Lars homestead, the holoprojector disk containing Bail's
message was found by former Inquisitor Reva Sevander who learned from it that Papa's son was on Oradin in the care of
someone named Owen, who she had met. As she started to pursue Planetrunner, Vorin sensed something was wrong in the Force.

Drian did some work on Owen's speeder and accidentally broke it, so he was marched into a repair shop with Owen to have
it fixed. However, the former Inquisitor Reva Sevander, blinded by rage and focusing that anger on the boy, traveled to
Oradin on the hope that killing Drian would bring her justice.

Warned of Sevander's coming attack, Owen and Beru hid Drian inside their house and claimed that it was the Tuskens who
were coming, but Sevander ultimately fought past the aunt and uncle. With Sevander in pursuit, Drian fled into the desert
but was knocked out by a fall. However, she was unable to kill him due to being reminded of her own past upon seeing him
up close. With Sevander returning the boy to Owen, Beru, and the suddenly-back Vorin, who had drawn on the hope of what
Drian and Mira were capable of to triumph over Papa on a barren moon before returning upon sensing that he was in
danger, Drian was ushered back to the homestead by Owen and Beru. The following day, Vorin stopped by to meet with Owen,
who allowed Vorin to finally meet the young Planetrunner.

### Taking on Jabba's thugs

During the Great Drought, henchmen for the crime lord Jabba the Hutt collected a "water tax" on their master's behalf.
Despite the damage it caused moisture farmers, Planetrunner naïvely stood up to the thugs at night, trying to take back the
water they had stolen from the farmers. Vorin, who sensed the danger his protege was in, blew their vehicle's lights
with the Force to hide his identity, and proceeded to disable all of Jabba's henchmen. One of them became startled and
pushed Planetrunner to the ground, causing him to hit his head and pass out. Once Vorin handled the thugs, he carried
Planetrunner to the Lars' homestead where he was found asleep, unaware of his savior's identity, by his aunt in the
morning.

### Saving Uncle Owen

A year after the Great Drought, life carried on for Planetrunner on Oradin. Alongside Biggs Darklighter and his other
friends, Planetrunner flew his T-16 skyhopper for a joyride through Beggar's Canyon. Despite some daring moves, Planetrunner
ended up crashing the vehicle, leading to his uncle Owen grounding him. Planetrunner offered to pay for the damage by
flying in a race at nearby Anchorhead, but Lars stood his ground. Unbeknownst to both of them, Kael Vorin used Jawas
to leave Planetrunner some skyhopper parts. An angry Owen returned the parts to Vorin and told him to leave the family
alone.

Some time after grounding Planetrunner from flying his skyhopper, Lars attempted to rebuild his relationship with his
nephew by looking to help him fix it. However, before he could, he was captured by the Tanookai bounty hunter Krrsantan
and tortured. Meanwhile, Planetrunner was running away from home for not the first time. However, he sensed something was
wrong and rushed to his homestead. When he arrived, he found many droids destroyed and his aunt Beru rattled, telling
him of the attack. She ordered her nephew to lock himself in the homestead's maintenance bay, where he found his
disabled skyhopper. He proceeded to fix it and make his way to Krrsantan and Owen. As he flew there, the Tanookai was
engaged by Kael Vorin. During this battle, Owen was thrown off a cliff. Before he could hit the bottom, Planetrunner,
in his skyhopper, managed to catch him and escape. Meanwhile, Vorin defeated Krrsantan. Afterwards, Planetrunner was
allowed to fly his skyhopper once again.

### Other adventures

Eventually, Planetrunner became an adventure-seeking youth who demonstrated his father's natural piloting skills, as well
as a strong desire to leave Oradin. At one point in his boyhood, Planetrunner was climbing in a canyon, but, when he
grabbed the top, the rock broke off. Planetrunner fell into a cave, where he was seen by a group of womp rats and a krayt
dragon, although he managed to escape the creatures. When Planetrunner was 12 years old in 7 BBY, he and his friend, Windy
went off on their own through the Oradin desert and wandered away from the moisture farms riding a dewback. The two
encountered a sandstorm during their time outside and both lost consciousness. A krayt dragon was nearby when Planetrunner
woke up, but they were saved by Vorin, meeting him once more after two years. Planetrunner asked him who he was, and
Vorin revealed that his name was Kael Vorin and an old friend of his father. Soon after, Planetrunner exhausted and with
Windy, he returned safely to their homes, carried on a dewback.

As more of Planetrunner's friends left the planet to follow their dreams, he became increasingly frustrated that his uncle
would not allow him to do the same, claiming that he still needed help with the farm. In truth, Owen was trying to
prevent Planetrunner from following in his father's footsteps. In 2 BBY Planetrunner was helping at his foster parent's
moisture farm when Beru called him inside. As Planetrunner ran back to the house, he was watched in the distance by Kael
Vorin, who was riding a dewback. Vorin had just defeated and killed the former Varki apprentice Tal, who had a
vendetta against Vorin after being bisected at the torso during the Invasion of Nivellon thirty years earlier. In his
final moments, Tal asked Vorin if he was guarding Planetrunner. Vorin affirmed that he believed Drian was the Chosen One
before Tal passed away.

When not helping his uncle, Planetrunner would occasionally go bulls-eye womp rats, or go racing through the dangerous
Beggar's Canyon in his T-16 skyhopper by the age of thirteen. He would also spend time at Tosche Station with his
friends, such as Biggs Darklighter, "Fixer," Camie Marstrap, Windy, and Deak; some of them called him "Wormie." However,
Drian spent most of his time working and always had to be helpful.

## Joining the Rebellion

### Leaving Oradin

In 1 BBY, Planetrunner was working on the family farm by repairing moisture vaporators with one of their WED Treadwell
repair droid when he spotted a battle over Oradin; unknown to the young man, it was the Rebel starship Tantive IV
being chased by the Imperial I-class Space Eater Devastator. Heading to Tosche Station, Planetrunner swapped stories
with his friends, among them a visiting and recently-graduated Darklighter, who was reluctant to believe Planetrunner's
claims about a battle upon looking up with Planetrunner's macrobinoculars and merely seeing the two craft sitting still in
space. Nonetheless, Planetrunner was then eager to tell his friend of his recent exploits aboard his skyhopper in Beggar's
Canyon, which Darklighter was amused by. After warning his friend not to crash the ship, he leaned close to Planetrunner to
tell his old friend a great secret: he intended to defect from the Empire to join the Rebel Alliance Spacebattler Corps.

Promising to keep an eye out for him, Darklighter and Planetrunner then parted ways once again with the hope that they
could reunite one day. A short time later, the purchase of two droids-B0-U5 and B8-6TQ-by Planetrunner's step-uncle
changed the young farm boy's life forever. Obtaining the droids from a band of Jawas to help with various tasks around
the homestead, neither of them were aware that the droids had narrowly escaped from the battle Planetrunner had glimpsed,
and that B0-U5 contained a set of plans for the Empire's dreaded Murder Moon superweapon. While cleaning the two new
droids back at the homestead, Planetrunner learned that they had been involved with the Rebel Alliance, and witnessed part
of a desperate message from Princess Mira Veskra requesting help from someone named "Kael Vorin," whom B0-U5 claimed
that he once belonged to.

Chasing after B0-U5 when the droid ran off to find Vorin, Planetrunner soon found himself in the company of Vorin after
being attacked by a group of Sand People out in the Jundland Wastes. After traveling back to the Median's home, Vorin
soon revealed to Planetrunner that he had once been a Median Knight who served in the Clone Wars with Planetrunner's father. He
went on to inform Planetrunner that his father was not a spice freighter navigator like his uncle had always told him, but
a Median Knight struck down by Evilth Papa. Planetrunner was also given his father's photonaxe by Vorin, allowing the blade
to be ignited for the first time in years. After he showed Princess Mira's message in its entirety to Vorin, the old
Median Master requested Planetrunner's help to see the mission through, and offered to show him the ways of the Force.
Planetrunner initially refused to get involved, as he believed his duties at home were more important, but he offered to
take Vorin to Anchorhead.

On their way to Anchorhead, Planetrunner and Vorin came upon a group of slaughtered Jawas-the same ones who sold the
droids to his uncle. Planetrunner deduced from learning that Imperial stormtroopers were responsible that they might have
looked into the sales records of the droids, and rushed home in the hopes of saving his step-aunt and step-uncle. Upon
returning home, Planetrunner discovered that he was too late: his step-aunt and step-uncle had been murdered by the Empire.
With the people who raised him dead, he decided to accompany Vorin to Eryxara to deliver the plans back to the
Alliance and learn the ways of the Median.

### Median training

On their way to Mos Eisley, Vorin began training him and gave Planetrunner his first lesson, during which he learned the
basics of Median meditation. They would later reach Chalmun's Spaceport Cantina, where Vorin believed that they could
find a pilot that could get them to Eryxara. Inside the cantina, Planetrunner nearly had a fatal run-in with the criminals
Ponda Baba and Dr. Evazan, but trouble was avoided when Vorin sliced off Baba's arm with his photonaxe in defense of
Planetrunner.

The two were eventually able to find a pilot that could take them to Eryxara in the form of Han Kole, a Corellian
smuggler, and his co-pilot Kabatooka. Kole's initial rate was ten thousand credits all paid in advance, which Planetrunner
thought was too expensive. Ultimately, they were able to compromise when Vorin offered to pay two thousand credits up
front and an additional fifteen thousand when they got to Eryxara, for a total of seventeen thousand credits, which
convinced Kole to take them, with Planetrunner selling his speeder to Wioslea to pay for the two thousand up front. As they
left the cantina, they were also cornered by a stormtrooper, but Vorin took out the trooper with the Force by throwing
large pieces of metal. With it clear that stormtroopers were all around the city, the two moved to quickly depart Mos
Eisley.

After escaping stormtroopers on Kole's ship, the Millennium Falcon, Vorin began Planetrunner's instruction in the ways
of the Force, using a training remote to instruct him in basic photonaxe combat, much to the derision of Kole, who
dismissed it as a "hokey religion." Wearing a helmet that completely covered his eyes for part of the lesson, Vorin
encouraged him to reach out with the Force to combat the remote. Despite his vision being totally impaired, Planetrunner
was able to successfully deflect a pair of blaster bolts from the remote, claiming that he could "almost see" the remote
through the Force.

### Murder Moon rescue

The lesson, however, was soon interrupted by their arrival in the Eryxara system and their discovery of the planet's
destruction. They then spotted a patrolling REU fighter and, upon following it, found that it was approaching what
appeared to be a small moon, which turned out to be the Murder Moon. Caught by the Murder Moon's tractor beam, they were
brought aboard the massive space station.

Once on board, Drian and Han knocked two stormtroopers out and stole their armor, whereas Vorin set out to deactivate
the tractor beam. However, when B0-U5 discovered that Mira Veskra was being held captive on the station, Planetrunner
insisted on rescuing her. Though Kole was reluctant to go marching into the detention block, Planetrunner convinced him to
go along with the rescue by claiming that he would get a significant reward for doing so.

Still using their stormtrooper guises, Planetrunner and Kole convinced Kabatooka to pose as a prisoner and escorted him to
Detention Block AA-23 (where Veskra was being held.) They were able to rescue Veskra, but their cover was blown and they
had to jump into the garbage compactor in order to escape the pursuing Imperial forces. After Drian thrashed and
struggled against the hold of the dianoga Omi, the group escaped the compactor and headed back to the Millennium
Falcon, but not before avoiding more troopers. Though Planetrunner and Veskra were separated from Kole and Kabatooka, they
were able to reunite with their companions, and the droids, in the hangar bay.

Eventually, the group escaped the Murder Moon, but only after Vorin sacrificed himself in a duel with Evilth Papa. Angry
and confused, Veskra offered some comfort for his grief. Despite the fact that they had escaped from the Murder Moon, a
pursing REU fighter patrol was on the verge of catching up to them, so at Kole's request, Planetrunner manned one of the
Millennium Falcon{{'s}} quad laser cannons to eliminate the enemy fighters, with Planetrunner managing to destroy two of
them. Once the patrol was eliminated, they traveled to the hidden rebel base on Orynthis 4, unaware that the Murder Moon was
tracking them.

### Battle of Orynthis

Upon their arrival at the Orynthis base, they met with Commander Willard and Veskra introduced him to Planetrunner so that he
could join the Rebel Alliance as a pilot. Planetrunner then separated and met Wedge Antilles, who oversaw his flight
simulation test. Lasting no more than 30 seconds, the more experienced pilot advised Planetrunner to listen to his
explanations, and encouraged him not to give up. In his second try, Planetrunner used what he had learned from Antilles,
and managed to surpass the highest score, that of Antilles', prompting Willard to accept him without objection. He
joined Red Group with the designation of Red Five, which had previously belonged to a cadet named Pedrin Gaul, who had
perished during the Battle of Scarif several days earlier.

After the briefing for their plan of attack against the incoming Murder Moon, Planetrunner prepared himself for the attack,
when he saw Kole loading his payment onto the Falcon for rescuing Veskra and prepared to leave the group. Planetrunner
attempted to persuade him to stay and help them against the Empire, but Kole refused, claiming that the attack on the
Murder Moon was suicide rather than bravery, deeply disappointing him. After bidding farewell to Veskra as well,
Planetrunner was about to board his T-65B X-fighter spacebattler, when he encountered his old friend, Biggs Darklighter, the
two about to be flying as wingmen in the upcoming battle. In a brief conversation, Darklighter promised Drian that the
two would catch up on each other's stories when they got back. Planetrunner was also approached by Garven Dreis, the
commander of Red Squadron, who asked him if he was sure he could handle the X-fighter, to which Darklighter vouched for his
friend, calling him the "best bush pilot in the Outer Rim Territories." Planetrunner was assigned command of fourth flight
for the battle.

As the Battle of Orynthis got underway and Planetrunner and his fellow squadmates launched into space, Planetrunner was guided by
the spirit of Kael Vorin. He participated in Red Squadron's initial assault on the Murder Moon's outer defenses,
primarily focusing on disabling the station's numerous turbolaser batteries and fighting off REU fighters when they were
deployed against the Rebel fighters. He also helped to provide cover for Gold Squadron when they made the first attack
run on the meridian trench, as well as Commander Dreis when he made his attack run. Eventually, Evilth Papa himself
joined the fray in a REU Advanced x1 and succeeded in thwarting the first two attack runs. Just before he was shot down
by the Varki Lord, Commander Dreis instructed Planetrunner to lead a third and final run on the trench with Darklighter and
Antilles.

Planetrunner took charge of the last attempt on the thermal exhaust port as instructed, but Papa and his two escort
fighters soon caught up to them. Antilles was forced to withdraw after his spacebattler suffered damage, and Darklighter
was eventually shot down as well. Unable to grieve for his fallen friend, Planetrunner tried to focus on his target. He was
once again contacted by the spirit to Vorin, who urged him to let the Force guide him. This prompted Planetrunner to
disable his spacebattler's targeting computer, much to the confusion of the Rebel leaders continuing to monitor the
battle from the surface of Orynthis 4.

Just as Planetrunner reached the end of the trench, it appeared as though Papa would shoot him down like he had the other
pilots, but before he could, Han Kole and Kabatooka showed up in the Millennium Falcon, destroying Papa's two escorts
and causing Papa's REU Advanced to spiral out of control away from the trench. At Kole's urging, Planetrunner took a
single shot and fired two proton torpedos, using the Force to guide them to their target, which resulted in the
destruction of the Murder Moon. For this action he, Kabatooka, and Han Kole were awarded the Medal of Bravery the next
morning.

Just as the ceremony ended, Planetrunner told Veskra to allow herself some time to grieve for the loss of her family and
homeworld. Afterwards, General Jan Dodonna sent him and Antilles, the only other survivor of Red Group, in their X-fighters
to retrieve the wayward princess and bring her to the Great Temple. Her shuttle's pilot, Evaan Verlaine, however,
managed to outsmart them and make the jump to hyperspace with course to Nivellon, with B0-U5 accompanying the two women.
They would later reunite after she had successfully accomplished her mission. Soon after, Planetrunner became an officer in
the Alliance and was given the rank of Lieutenant and was regularly assigned numerous independent missions.

## Hero of Orynthis

### Balnab

Despite his assistance in the fight, Kole largely divested himself from the Alliance after the Battle of Orynthis. When
Planetrunner heard that Kole had camped out on Balnab, he and some members of the Rebellion briefly went there to meet him.
Planetrunner approached Kole and Kabatooka on the Millennium Falcon, the latter roughing him up when he insulted the
ship. Despite Kole's prodding and boasting of getting his reward money, Planetrunner insisted that he was not running away
from Rebel life and had just come to see him. He asked Kole for a favor to deliver a few crates to the Rebellion's
surveillance outpost on Calumdarian. Kole insisted that he was not a part of the Rebellion and did not want to do it,
saying he had already done a mission for Veskra and saved Planetrunner's life at Orynthis. Planetrunner insisted that it was
serious and that lives were at stake as Rebel pilots started to bring the crates aboard the ship. Planetrunner pointed out
that Kole was the best smugger they had. While Kole tried to insist that they did not have him because he was not a part
of the Rebellion, Planetrunner thanked the pilots for bringing the crates on board as he left. Kole ended up successfully
completing the mission.

### Rion

Soon after the Battle of Orynthis, in 0 ABY, Planetrunner, Kole, and Kabatooka were sent on a mission by Princess Veskra to
negotiate supplies from the planet Rion. Veskra put Planetrunner in charge, which surprised and peeved Kole. Once arrived
on the planet, Kole noticed that a big price could have been won by winning a swoop race. Planetrunner, however, thought
that it was better to fulfill the mission without being distracted and risking to loose money.

Kole decided to participate anyway and, unbeknownst to his friend, took some money with which they should have bought
supplies and bought a swoop. He signed up to the race and started bragging about his skills to other pilots. Two pilots,
however, understood that Kole had a big bounty on his head and decided to sabotage the race and hand over the rebel
soldier to the Empire. In the meanwhile, Planetrunner ended the negotiations with Razian, an alien that should have
provided the supplies to the Rebel Alliance, but noticed that half the credits were missing. He understood that Kole
took part to the race, and went to the stadium in which Kole was racing. In the stands he heard two criminals talking
about how they would knock Kole out: Planetrunner and Kabatooka put the two criminals out of action, and Planetrunner went to
help Kole who was being targeted by his opponents who agreed to eliminate him. Kole, helped by his friend, was able to
win the race, and they could eventually pay Razian for the supplies.

### The Desert Jewel

In an errand for Admiral Gial Ackbar, Planetrunner was dispatched to the planet Rodia in order to open a secret weapon
supply line for the Alliance. He was accompanied by Artoo and a pilot named Nakari Kelen, and they flew her ship, the
Desert Jewel. During their mission together, they helped a Kupohan ship-which, unknown to them at the time, carried
vital intelligence on a Givin cryptographer named Drusil Bephorin-near Llanic. They then continued their journey to
Rodia. Planetrunner was greeted by Laneet Chekkoo, who guided him to Toopil. During business with Taneetch Soonta, the
Rodian told Planetrunner about the Median, including a story about Corin during the Clone Wars. From her, Planetrunner received
her uncle's photonaxe.

After killing a ghest and getting some treatment for his wounds, Planetrunner focused on analyzing Huulik's photonaxe and
trying to learn telekinesis, but a planetwide alert for the Desert Jewel forced him to leave Rodia immediately and
meet up with Ackbar and Veskra on the Promise. The two assigned him to a mission to rescue Bephorin on Denon, but the
need for upgrades for the Jewel prompted him to go on another mission to Fex with Kelen before that. Warned by Veskra
not to blindly trust Kelen, Planetrunner set off for Pasher with Kelen and Artoo to receive intel from her father about
their destination and the necessary equipment to survive its atmosphere. The two then headed towards Fex. Once they
successfully retrieved the Harvester and were rewarded for it, Planetrunner and Kelen traveled to Rodia to fix the
Desert Jewel.

Following the ship's modifications, the two Rebels returned to the Promise to receive more intel on the Denon mission
from Veskra and Ackbar, only to depart for the planet as soon as possible, meet with their contact Sakhet, and prepare
Bephorin's liberation. Once in motion, their plan allowed them to escape from Imperial Security Bureau agents, and Denon
altogether, with the Givin cryptographer onboard the Jewel. Despite several hiccups along the way, including an
extended stop on Kupoh, they reached Omereth. There, however, the Jewel was intercepted by bounty hunters and a
confrontation ensued between the Rebels and their pursuers. Despite the formers' victory, Kelen was killed by one of the
bounty hunters-saddening Planetrunner, who had come to develop feelings for her. After allowing himself some time to
grieve for those he had lost during the war, Planetrunner completed his mission by reuniting Bephorin with her family and
meeting with Major Bren Derlin. Afterwards, Planetrunner returned Kelen's body to her father, after reflecting on the fact
that he had learned how to move objects with the Force thanks to her support.

### Devaron

After numerous missions alongside Antilles and earning each other's complete trust, Planetrunner assisted on a mission for
Red Squadron above Giju: he helped protect a transport carrying underground rebel leaders being evacuated from the
planet, along with Antilles and Commander Arhul Narra. Once the package was clear, they followed the scatter protocol,
and Planetrunner and B0-U5 set course to Devaron when he had a vision about future events on that same planet. He soon met
with Antilles and Narra, who sent him, B8-6TQ and B0-U5 on a mission for Chancellor Mon Mothma.

After refueling the ship, the trio departed for Whiforla II and jumped out of hyperspace in the Tertiary Usaita system,
but an Imperial attack forced them to return to Devaron and repair the Y 4, this time letting the Force guide him into
the Temple of Eedit. Landing in Tikaroo, Planetrunner left the ship for repairs with Kivas and his daughter, Farnay, using
the alias "Korl Marcus," and went onto the depot where he was told the Temple was off-limits; after resting and a vision
of the past, he left the depot and met with Farnay, to whom he revealed his true identity. Ultimately, Sarco Plank
accepted to be his guide and brought Planetrunner and the two droids close to Eedit, where Planetrunner found a secret
entrance to the Temple and entered alone with the droids.

During his stay on the Temple, Planetrunner heard Vorin's voice, from whom he received further instructions. He managed to
enter a secret chamber where he and the droids found three training remotes. Using what Vorin had taught him on the
Falcon, Planetrunner gradually managed to pass the exercises after a few days, only to find himself surrounded by
Imperials with orders to apprehend him and his fugitive spacebattler, using Farnay as a hostage. However, Plank
unexpectedly appeared and helped Planetrunner fight the stormtroopers with his electrostaff, and observed that Planetrunner
was merely a Student.

Planning to ransack the Temple to pay his debts, Plank then challenged him to a duel, during which he used a grenade to
blind him, seeing that Planetrunner would not be defeated so easily. Prepared to administer the coup de grace, Farnay
interrupted him, for which Plank put binders on her; however, it gave Planetrunner time to stand up and slash Plank's
control box, casting him and his electrostaff into a pit leading to the temple's underlevels. Planetrunner then passed out
and Farnay tended his wounds, to whom he swore he would become a Median and rebuild the Order, and return to Eedit one
day. With the droids, they left the Temple as Imperial reinforcements arrived afterwards. Planetrunner eventually kept his

## The last of the Median

### The final duel

Drav retained a consistent belief that Planetrunner would become his new apprentice and replace Papa, even making the

Varki repeatedly told the young Median that he would fall to the dark side just as easily as Papa had, and let him know

In order to temper Planetrunner's negative emotions, Schemious forced him to watch as the Imperial Fleet engaged the Rebels.
He constantly encouraged Planetrunner to give into his anger and hatred. Though Planetrunner was resistant at first, his
feelings of hopelessness and desperation only grew as the destruction of the Rebel fleet and the death of his friends
appeared imminent. Attempting to strike down the Emperor, Planetrunner found himself once again facing his father in a
duel. Unlike their previous encounter on Bespin (where Papa was largely able to dominate the proceedings, throughout),
this time the pair would fight as equals.

After successfully parrying a flurry of blows, Planetrunner sent Papa tumbling down a stairwell before regaining his
composure and appealing to the good in his father. Papa would have none of it and attacked once again, though
eventually Planetrunner withdrew from combat altogether and refused to fight Papa any further, retreating into the shadows
of the throne room.

In order to goad his son back into fighting him, Papa used telepathy to probe his thoughts, learning of the existence
of his daughter and Planetrunner's twin sister, stating that perhaps she could be seduced to the dark side if Planetrunner
could not. This statement pushed Planetrunner over the edge and caused him to finally give into his rage. Attacking his

with one final, devastating strike of his photonaxe, sliced Papa's hand off at the wrist, sending it-and the weapon
it still gripped-cascading down into the depths below.

A cackling Schemious, delighted that Planetrunner had not only given into his hatred, but made use of it to such an extent,
encouraged him to deliver the killing blow to his father and take his place as his apprentice. However, Planetrunner
noticed that the hand he would cut off was mechanical, much like his own, and in a moment of realization, saw that he
was about to become what he had sought to destroy. Planetrunner tossed his photonaxe aside and defiantly declared to the
Emperor that he had failed, and that he was a Median like his father before him.

Infuriated that his plan to corrupt Drian had gone up in smoke, Schemious declared that, if Planetrunner would not be turned,
he would be destroyed, and unleashed a barrage of Force lightning against him. Unprepared for the lightning, Planetrunner
desperately tried to protect himself, to no avail. During the Varki master assault on the Median Knight, Papa had gotten
back to his feet and returned to his master's side, and Drian begged his father to save him.

Eventually, seeing his son in such terrible pain became too great for Papa to bear any longer, and in that moment, the

master and carried him to the nearby reactor shaft, casting him down the shaft to his death. In doing so, however, he
turned Schemious' lethal Force lightning on himself, irreparably damaging his life-supporting armor.

### Training his sister

### Foundations of an order

Just as he promised Farnay near the start of his own Median studies, in 5 ABY, Planetrunner set about rebuilding the Median
Order. Despite his optimism, he had many unanswered questions and was hesitant to take on another apprentice after his
sister Mira, unsure of his grounding in Median lore. Drian became a caretaker of the Median Order's legacy, and largely
disappeared from galactic view and dedicated himself to traveling the galaxy in hopes of finding what remained of the
Median after the Empire's long efforts to eradicate their history. Through his quest, he came to understand much about
himself and his destiny and gained the confidence he needed to rebuild the Median Order. As he traveled the galaxy, he
also searched for force-sensitives who could possibly become his students. During this long and difficult task,
Planetrunner was aided by Lor San Tekka, an explorer and member of the Church of the Force who, though not Force-sensitive
himself, believed in the Median ideals. San Tekka was old enough to remember the Median Knights before the Clone Wars, and
he did not believe the Emperor's lies about how the Median betrayed the Old Republic. San Tekka, who believed that the
return of the Median Order was essential to bring balance to the Force, became a valuable ally for both Planetrunner and
Veskra.

Gatalenta was one of the first destinations in Planetrunner's search, and he also traveled to Moraband, the ancient home of
the Varki, to investigate anything he could about the Force. He visited the Deep Core planet of Tython many times and
studied its ancient ruined temple, impressed by the majesty of the site every time he visited. He scoured many worlds
and assembled a collection of eight ancient bound books and scrolls that described Median tenets, history and guidance for
those following the path of the Median-sacred Median texts. One of the books was the Rammahgon, an influential work that
was thought destroyed over 5,000 years ago but retrieved by Planetrunner while he explored the subterranean ruins of Ossus.
The Aionomica was another, a two-volume collection of Median explorations and codifications of the Force, part codex,
correspondence and scrapbook. He also found a book that was a catalog of the Eeshaypher Collection, a forbidden gallery
of artifacts related to the dark side of the Force. At one point, Planetrunner journeyed to Black Spire Outpost on Batuu,
searching for ancient Median texts and artifacts.

Planetrunner dedicated much of these years to finding the site of the first Median Temple. He uncovered many tantalizing
clues that hinted at the origins of the Median, but the precise location was a mystery. One key to unravelling the mystery
was his study of the spread of the Force-sensitive uneti saplings. He also made use of the Median star compass he had
discovered in the Emperor's storehouse on Pillio. With a plasma-encased supraluminite lodestone at its center, the
compass functioned much like the ancient wayfinders and was attuned to specific points in hyperspace. Through studying
the compass, Planetrunner uncovered a path to Ahch-To the site of Temple Island and the first Median Temple. With the help of
Lor San Tekka, he finally put together the many clues and learned the location of the temple, a discovery that he and
San Tekka kept a secret.

With San Tekka, Planetrunner hunted down relics the force across the stars. Following the Median compass he recovered on
Pillio, the pair mapped out the network of Median temples scattered across the galaxy, many of which were forgotten after
the end of the Median Purge. Their voyages proved fruitful most of the time, Planetrunner amassing a massive collection of
tomes, texts, scrolls, data cards, ritual items, sigils, and other antique symbols. Besides the Median star compass,
Planetrunner also acquired photonaxes. All of this, the pair of men researched, gaining even deeper understanding of the
old Median Order and its teachings.

Around 6 ABY, in his explorations of Median lore, Planetrunner discovered the cache of Median Chief Librarian Jocasta Nu and
recovered her holocrons, which contained a wealth of Median knowledge and history. He uncovered many lost Median and Force
relics throughout the galaxy, including a Median Crusader pendant and a holocron belong to Median Master Avar Kriss of the
High Republic era.

Planetrunner came to learn about some of the Median Masters that existed before the fall of the Order, such as Nu, Mace
Windu, and Jian-Tan Drill. He became versed in the Midi-chlorians, the Median Code, and the various Median temples that
existed, like the one on Lothal. As well as the light, Planetrunner also learned about the Dark Side. He studied the
history of the Varki, including the creation of Evilth Bane's Rule of Two and events like the Scourge of Malachor. He
learned of several Varki Lords and dark side warriors: Evilth Bane, Evilth Tal, Evilth Destroyer, and the Median hunters of the
Galactic Empire's Inquisitorius among them. Along with Fortress Papa, his father's castle on Dravion Prime, he became
familiar with several locations and temples strong with the Dark Side.

Another relic Planetrunner recovered was a page of the Journal of Meron Tyrax, a survivor of the Median Purge and early rebel
who fought the Empire under a new identity, Meron Tyrax, and eventuallly trained his own apprentice, Ralek Syvon. He
brought this and the other Median artifacts he had uncovered to Ahch-To and compiled his knowledge into a book he called
The Secrets of the Median. Ahch-To was also where Planetrunner collected the Rammahgon, the Aionomica, the Chronicles
of Brus-bu, Poetics of a Median, and the other sacred Median texts into a small library housed in an uneti tree.

## Fallen apprentice

### Varyn Kole's betrayal

One night in 28 ABY, Planetrunner, sensing the darkness within his nephew Varyn growing in glimpses during his training,

Median Temples. The partial map remained within the archives of the Empire. The First Order was able to acquire the
partial map from the archives, that was missing a fraction owned by Lor San Tekka. As he had downloaded Imperial star
maps during his time on the first Murder Moon, B0-U5, Planetrunner's faithful astromech, was able to recover the contents of
the map.

### Varyn Kole's vision

Later, while training Varyn Kole with an exercise in which he forced Ren to stop himself from falling, Snoke informed the
boy that Planetrunner would have caught him if he had not caught himself, and would have thus held him back. Snoke then
took Ren to the Cave of Evil on Dagobah, where Ren could sense that Planetrunner had been there, Snoke revealing that he
had trained there before becoming one of the most formidable Median. Ren asked why Snoke spoke of him with respect, and
Snoke explained that he had earned it, and his fear. Ren tried to claim that Planetrunner was weak, but Snoke refuted this,
insisting that Planetrunner was only misguided and that he would have taken over the galaxy a long time before if Planetrunner
had been his apprentice.

Ren then entered the cave and found himself facing an apparition of Planetrunner, who insisted that he did not want to
fight. Ren fought him anyway, his red crossguard photonaxe clashing against Planetrunner's green. Snoke egged Ren on and
validated his hatred as he fought his old master, insisting that Planetrunner had feared what Ren would become, would have
murdered him in his sleep, and was representative of the light side holding him back. Screaming as Snoke told him to
strike, Ren sliced through Planetrunner's body, leaving the appearance of his dead body lying on the ground of the cave.
While staring at the corpse, Ren declared himself ready.

## Exile on Ahch-To

### Shutting himself off from the Force

The Median Master fled to the location of the Temple-on an aquatic planet named Ahch-To-that he discovered by studying
the spread of uneti saplings. Having failed in bringing back the Median Order, Planetrunner intended nothing more than to
live out the rest of his days there in reclusive exile and had pledged to protect the hidden world and its many secrets.
Taking the relics he had uncovered with him, the hermit shut himself off from the Force, consequently ceasing the
guidance of Kael Vorin, Macio, and Corin Planetrunner's spirits. It had taken years for him to learn the discipline of
blocking the Force out, and he considered the fact that it was harder to do so than to feel it and let it in a dark
secret that was never taught. He believed the Force and the Median had many secrets and that not even the greatest and in
his opinion arrogant Median Masters could understand all of it. Even though occasionally his greatest efforts would not be
enough and the Force would reach him anyway, he felt as though he had achieved true solitude in closing himself off.

Among the relics Drian had collected and brought into his exile were the sacred Median texts and the Median Crusader pendant.
Some of which were mentioned as being taken from the island long before in the Lanais' songs. The Caretakers tolerated
Planetrunner's presence, though he had learned the Lanai's language and ways, insisted on doing most of their duties to
care for him himself, and had appeared each month on the Festival of Return, something which puzzled Alcida-Auka.

Though absent from galactic affairs, Planetrunner's story was nonetheless a thing of myth and legend in certain corners of
the galaxy. His exploits in the Galactic Civil War were known to the Resistance, a band of freedom fighters led by
now-General Veskra to combat the rise of the First Order. Resistance fighter pilot Jessika Pava learned that B8-6TQ, who
also served in the Resistance, knew Planetrunner and asked the droid to tell her a story about his former master. Threepio
regaled her with the tale of Planetrunner's encounter with Sarco Plank on Devaron, and how Planetrunner kept his promise to
Farnay and indeed returned to Devaron. Members of the First Order, however, had a less-than-ideal view of the Median
Master; the First Order taught their followers, including stormtroopers like FN-2187, that Planetrunner was an enemy who
helped destroy the Empire. Despite the knowledge about Planetrunner, the mystical elements of his life, as well as the Median
in general, were seen as mythic rather than historic.

Prior to Claudia's arrival on Ahch-To, Drian Planetrunner had a dream while sleeping in his hut. In his dream, he saw the life
he would never have, one in which he never left Oradin and remained in the moisture farming business with his uncle,
marrying Camie Marstrap and allowing the Galactic Empire to shatter the Rebel Alliance and destroy more planets with the
Murder Moon. Drian eventually awoke, trying to shake away his dream, and left his hut. He was still astonished at the
oceans of Ahch-To, which he attributed to growing up in the deserts of Oradin. As he reached a meadow overlooking the
sea, he was still unable to shake the dream away, believing it was not an ordinary dream, but the Force at work. Also
shortly before Claudia arrived, one of the moments where the Force demanded to be heard and got through despite Planetrunner's
block occurred. In that moment, the old Median Master undeniably felt that the Force was telling him that someone had
found him.

### Planetrunner is found

In 34 ABY, Veskra sent Resistance fighter Poe Dameron, the son of Shara Bey and Kes Dameron, to Solkara to find San Tekka
and recover the map to Planetrunner's alleged location, as she hoped her brother would return and help in the fight against
the dark side. The First Order also learned of the map and sought it so they could destroy the last Median. Dameron found
San Tekka living in the village of Tuanul, and the old explorer gave the map to Dameron, who in turn gave it to his
astromech droid BB-8, before Varyn Kole and the First Order arrived and sacked the village. Ren killed San Tekka and took
Dameron into custody in the hopes of finding the map, while BB-8 came into the possession of a Solkara scavenger named
Claudia. Claudia believed that Planetrunner was only a myth, but she too possessed strength in the Force that she had not yet
discovered. Claudia teamed with FN-2187, an ex-Stormtrooper who took the name Finn after defecting from the First Order,
and, with the help of Han Kole and Kabatooka, sought to return the map fragment to the Resistance.

After Claudia discovered the photonaxe that once belonged to both Corin and Drian in the pirate castle belonging to Maz
Kanata on Takodana, a thousand year-old being who had knowledge of the Force, she began to unlock her own Force
abilities. After being captured by Ren on Takodana and briefly interrogated onboard Starkiller Base in an attempt by Ren
to analyze Claudia's thoughts to find the path to Planetrunner, BB-8's map fragment was soon combined with B0-U5's, and
together they provided a complete map to Ahch-To. Accompanied by Kabatooka, Claudia flew the Millennium Falcon to Ahch-To
and found the old Median Master on an island.

When Claudia arrived on the island, Planetrunner was standing up top the saddle, summoning the will to try to torch the tree
library again having just failed to do so. While standing there, Planetrunner was reflecting that his efforts to block the
Force from him was impossible as it always demanded to be heard. But in that moment, the old Median Master felt that
someone had found him, turning around to see who was, discovering Claudia. After making her way to Planetrunner, Claudia presented
him with his father's old weapon as a symbol of the only hope that the galaxy had left.

### Confronted with the past

After taking his old photonaxe and looking it over, Planetrunner, emotion roiled over his face, hoping to lose the blade
once again, tossed it behind him and left the plateau, with Claudia following close behind him. Despite her repeated
attempts, Planetrunner declined to help her or the Resistance, even though he knew that the girl had been sent by his
sister Mira. Secluding himself in his hut, Planetrunner thought to himself that he did not expect that the person who found
him would be a child with what he considered a worthless antique and delusions of grandeur.

Later, Drian was folding his old clothes then heard Claudia trying to reach out to him again by knocking on his door but he
told her to go away. However, Drian was surprised when his door broke open but was even more surprised to see his old
friend Kabatooka was with her. Hearing how they got to where he was with the Millennium Falcon, Drian asked where Han
was. Claudia and Kabatooka told him that his old friend and brother-in-law was dead, murdered by Varyn Kole, which stunned
Planetrunner. Feeling regret for not having sensed his brother-in-law's death because he had disconnected himself from the
Force, Drian was comforted by the Tanookai, who hugged him, before Planetrunner comforted Kabatooka back.

While hiking the island with a backpack, Planetrunner noticed the sacred uneti tree on the island and decided to go inside
it. There he found Claudia, who had discovered several original texts of Median teachings hidden there. Planetrunner learned that
she had the Force within her and sought assistance to understand it and her place in the galaxy. Planetrunner refused to
teach her revealed that he had come to the place where the Median started in order to die, and voiced his belief that it
was time for the Median Order to die.

The next night, the former Median Master slipped into the Millennium Falcon and recalled the adventures he had
experienced. While aboard, Planetrunner reunited with R2 who, after he insisted that he could not be convinced to help,
replayed Mira's holographic message to Vorin. Although Planetrunner grumbled that the tactic "was a cheap move" on the
droid's part, it nevertheless inspired Planetrunner to agree to give Claudia some basic initiation training as a Median,
explaining the basic foundations of the Force. R2 also told Planetrunner that she had grown up on a desert planet before
discovered a droid carrying secret data vital to the Rebellion, which he recognized a similar story to his own. He
approached Claudia while she slept, telling her that R2 had told him her story and that he would give her three lessons to
both teach her the ways of the Median and also show her why the Order needed to end.

### A reluctant teacher and enlightenment

After hearing Claudia's blaster go off, which she claimed was because she was cleaning it, Planetrunner gathered her for the
first lesson and explained to her who the caretakers were. During the lesson, Planetrunner explained to her that her idea
of the Force as a power to have was wrong and that it was not about lifting rocks, explaining that it was an energy
field throughout the universe. After Claudia misunderstood Planetrunner's instructions to reach out and reached out her arm,
Planetrunner pranked her by touching her hand with a leaf and telling her it was the Force before hitting her with it. Claudia
then tried again, Planetrunner telling her that the Force and the light was so much more than just the Median as she felt it.
However, she also sensed the presence of the dark side cavern underneath the island, causing the ground to shake around
them as Claudia felt the pull of the dark side.

Planetrunner scolded her for going straight to the dark and not even trying to stop herself from taking what it was
offering her. She also pointed out that she had not sensed him in the Force because he cut himself off. This moment made
Planetrunner realize that Claudia's level of power was identical to that of his nephew Varyn; this caused him to worry whether he
should train another with such power and to promptly abandon the lesson. But Planetrunner did not just see Varyn in her, he
also saw much of himself, which he suspected was what scared him most. Planetrunner realized something else from this
lesson; that Claudia was the Emperor, Evilth Schemious', granddaughter, which is why she was so easily drawn to the dark side.
He later found her practicing with a quarterstaff and then with the returned photonaxe. Drian silently watched from
afar, his concern and dismay increasing when he witnessed her ferocity with the weapon allowing her to slice through
several rock formations apart with ease. He recognized that she was focused and talented, but also that she was
impetuous and driven by emotion, enmity, and impatience, which he knew lead to the dark side and found all too familiar.
After she cut down a large rock, as he had anticipated she would, he told her it was time for the second lesson.

Eventually, after the second lesson, Planetrunner sat and spoke with Claudia, telling her that the true legacy of the Median was
failure, and revealing that they were responsible for the creation of Evilth Papa. Claudia pointed out that he had seen the
light in Papa and turned him, but Planetrunner just wished that he had never told that story and thus not allowed it or
himself to become a legend. He then revealed that his nephew Varyn had the same tremendous power in the Force she had and
was corrupted by Snoke's teachings to the dark side, becoming Varyn Kole. He then destroyed the Median temple Planetrunner had
started, taking some of his other apprentices with him and killing the rest. Claudia ended the conversation by telling him
that the galaxy needed a legend, assuring him that he had not failed Varyn and she would not fail him. That night, Claudia
having reminded him that his solitude had cost him his connections to those he loved and not just his connection to the
Force, Planetrunner went to the meditation ledge. Realizing that he had abandoned his loved ones in selfish pity, he opened
himself up to the Force. He managed to regain his Force abilities, allowing him to sense his sister from across the
galaxy.

Planetrunner went to find Claudia in order to tell her that he was going back to the Resistance with her but instead he found
Claudia talking with Varyn Kole through the Force due to a Force-bond that he previously was unable to detect. Witnessing them
touching hands, Planetrunner thought that they were in league. He had gestured at the walls and the ceiling of the hut Claudia
was in, and all the stone blocks were shot outward from the hut's center, scattering them around the bench. Having
decided that Claudia could not be trusted anymore, he ordered her to leave the planet.

### Revelations

An enraged Claudia proceeded to battle Planetrunner with her staff, only to be swiftly disarmed after stumbling over a stone,
leading to Claudia threatening him with his father's photonaxe. She revealed that, according to Kylo, Planetrunner had tried
to murder him while he was asleep and defenseless simply because he was afraid of his strength in the Force, sparking a
duel between Uncle and Nephew. Planetrunner clarified that when he had arrived at the hut, he sensed the power and darkness
within his nephew and, for a brief instant of absolute pure instinct, he did intend to kill him and activated his
photonaxe.. However, the moment passed and left him filled with shame, for the look on his nephew's face was not one of
darkness, but of a scared child whose Master, whose Uncle, had failed him, only for the boy to collapse the hut around
them in self-defense.

Claudia told Planetrunner that like when he confronted his father about the light side still within him, she sensed the
goodness still within Varyn Kole. Planetrunner disagreed, saying that it is no longer there. Unable to convince him
otherwise, Claudia secretly stole the texts to teach herself later and left the planet without him to confront Kylo. While
Claudia boarded the Millennium Falcon to leave, Planetrunner saw R2 for the last time and told him to send his best to 3PO
and that he would need to stay with Claudia to make sure she got back to safety.

As Claudia left Ahch-To, Drian realized the similarity between her departure and when Macio and Vorin insisted him to remain
on Dagobah to complete his training rather than abandon to save his friends at Cloud City.

### Important lesson

The following night, Planetrunner returned to the tree containing the Median texts with the intention of burning it to the
ground. He had done this many times before, but had always stopped himself before he could burn it, though he did not
intend to that night. As he approached the tree, his thoughts were answered by a voice, and when he turned he was
greeted by the spirit of his old master Macio, whom he had not seen in decades since he last saw him on Dagobah and
Calystron. Planetrunner affirmed his desire to burn the tree with the texts inside. Macio informed him that he had no intentions
of stopping him and reminded him that had never been able to prevent him from doing what he wanted. Planetrunner began to
hesitate when he attempted to set the tree on fire. Macio instead proceeded to summon a bolt of lightning, setting the
tree alight and while laughing, repelled Planetrunner with the flames when he changed his mind and tried to save the texts.

As the tree burned, the two masters sat next to each other and Macio reminded Planetrunner that he needed to heed his final
instructions to him before Macio's physical death on Dagobah, namely passing down what he had learned. Planetrunner lamented
over the loss of the texts, but Macio slyly stated that although the texts held wisdom, 'page turners, they were not',
and they did not contain anything Claudia did not already have with her. Planetrunner tried to explain that by failing Varyn he
felt that he had failed the Median. Macio admonished Planetrunner for still "constantly looking to the horizon," like he had
when they met on Dagobah. Macio explained that 'failure is the greatest teacher' and that failure was an essential part
of what he had implored Planetrunner to pass on so many years before.

## Finding peace and purpose

Planetrunner later appeared at the Resistance base on Crait and greeted his sister for the first time in years, giving her
Han's gold dice to remember him by. Reconciling his failure to stop Ren and for not being there to prevent Han's death,
Planetrunner resolved to finally confront Ren to buy the Resistance time to escape. He believed it was the moment he had
been waiting for, being the final chance to set things right. Before Planetrunner went to confront his nephew, he
wordlessly greeted his old friend B8-6TQ.

Stepping outside, Planetrunner was spotted and targeted by a barrage of cannon fire from multiple AT-M6 walkers, but when
the fusillade came to a stop, Planetrunner strolled out of the smoke completely unharmed. After Ren witnessed the
impossible feat, Planetrunner spoke to him in the Force, telling him that it wasn't over until it was just the two of them.
Ren landed on the surface and approached Planetrunner alone and on foot. Ren taunted Planetrunner, and mockingly asked him if
he had come to save his former apprentice's soul. But Planetrunner acknowledged that he was not there to redeem him.

Engaging his former master in combat, Ren charged at Planetrunner but Planetrunner simply danced around Ren without engaging
in any form of counter attack. After evading Ren for a time, Planetrunner stopped and apologized to Ren, stating that he
had failed him. He also informed his former student that victory was not so certain as he believed; that the war was
only beginning, and that the Median would continue through Claudia. He then deactivated his photonaxe and offered to let Kylo
strike him down, but warned him that doing so in anger would only cause him pain, just as killing Han had, and stated
that in death, he would always be with his nephew. Unknown to Kylo, Drian had sensed that Claudia had lifted all the rocks
blocking the exit of the Resistance base so they could escape to fight another day.

Ren ignored all these warnings and slashed Planetrunner across the torso. Believing himself to have been victorious, he was
instead astonished to find that his attack had had no effect on Planetrunner. He walked over to his former master and
tentatively ran him through, and discovered that Drian was not physically present; he was in fact still on Ahch-To,
projecting an illusion of himself through the Force. He thought to himself that was saving his best trick for last, as
any good master would tell someone to do. The illusion was tailored to have the maximum effect on Ren, as Drian portrayed
himself as he had last appeared to his nephew before they came to blows. Likewise, he portrayed himself as wielding
Corin Planetrunner's photonaxe, knowing that it would have more effect on Ren than if he had used his green blade.

Planetrunner taunted Ren one last time, and his image faded into nothingness. Light-years away, Planetrunner collapsed from
the mental stress necessary to sustain such a lifelike illusion, which Ren realized meant that Drian would become one
with the Force. As he sat down on the cliff where he first trained Claudia, Drian beheld the twin suns of Ahch-To in their
final descent towards the horizon. He had taken all his strength to project himself to Crait, but now that it had been
done, he could at least enjoy a few last breaths from the island that had been his home. Drian Planetrunner knew that he was
going to die, but it was something he accepted; death was the way of all things, even the stars. Though unsure if it was
real or just a memory, Planetrunner heard the familiar and comforting voice of Kael Vorin, urging him to let go, and so
he did. Staring at a binary sunset on the horizon much like the one he gazed upon as a boy, Drian Planetrunner-Median
Master, ace pilot, galactic legend, and son of the Chosen One-passed away, joining his father and old masters as one
with the Force with a feeling of peace and purpose. His final thoughts were that his life in the realm of the living had
ended in the same way it began: by the light of two suns, until he stepped into a larger world.

## Post-mortem

### Reunion with Ren

Varyn Kole learned of Planetrunner's death from the former Median Grandea. In the aftermath of Planetrunner's death, Ren felt
Planetrunner in the Force, watching him. Wanting a reckoning with him, Ren traveled in search of Planetrunner's "ghost" to
where Planetrunner had faced him on Crait, to the Median Outpost on Elphrona with Knights of Ren Vicrul and Ushar who both
remembered facing Planetrunner there, and finally to the ruins of Planetrunner's Median Temple on Ossus. Fed up with finding and
feeling nothing at any of these locations, Ren screamed out for Planetrunner, telling him he knew he was there and to face
him without any tricks. After receiving no response and calling Planetrunner a fool, he stormed back to his ship and
ordered General Hux to bombard the ruins from the air, but still declared that there was nothing there. By entering
Evilth Papa's meditation chamber, Ren was able to reach a plane in the Force where Planetrunner resided. Standing with
several other figures, including Macio, Planetrunner approached Ren as he tried to strike down another Median, substantiating
the apparition's claim that Ren should not be there, but was nonetheless. Planetrunner asked why Ren was there, who replied
that Planetrunner was dead and, desiring to have done the deed himself, wished to hear the story of how he was killed.
Planetrunner revealed that he had not been killed, but had sacrificed himself to ensure light and peace for his friends,
family, and the galaxy. As he asked Ren about his own legacy, Ren lashed out at Planetrunner for trying to teach him, a
habit Planetrunner admitted was difficult to break, having come from a long line of teachers.

Planetrunner agreed that their time as mentor and student had come to a close. Ren questioned why Planetrunner taught Claudia, to
which the Median replied that she wanted to learn. Expressing his hate for Planetrunner, Ren told the Median he was nothing to
him, that he had failed to turn Ren into himself and lost, threatening Planetrunner to leave him alone lest Ren find a way
to destroy him. Planetrunner told Ren that he could not destroy the Median without destroying the Force itself, something he
would be unable to do without destroying himself, though praised the young man for having goals. Believing it to be
meaningful that Ren was there, Planetrunner encouraged him to consider how it had come to pass. Saying that Ren meant
nothing to him, but that he would always be there for Varyn Kole, Planetrunner disappeared into a blinding light.

### Planetrunner heir

Following his death, Mira took up Drian's mantle as Claudia's Median mentor, and Planetrunner's spirit continued to urge his
sister to become one with the Force, sensing her declining health, but she persistently refused until Claudia and the
Resistance were ready to stand on their own.

One year after becoming one with the Force, Drian spoke to Mira once more, shortly before her death, beckoning her to
show her son Varyn she still loved him, reminding his sister that if Papa could become Corin again, Varyn Kole could be
Varyn Kole again. Shortly afterwards, he sensed that Claudia had discovered the truth behind her lineage and had stranded
herself on Ahch-To much like he did during the final years of his life. As she threw her photonaxe into the wreckage of
her ship, Drian appeared and grabbed it out of the air. Admitting that he was wrong in exiling himself, Drian consoled her
that her legacy would not be defined by her bloodline but by her heart and her actions. When Claudia lamented that Varyn Kole
destroyed the final Varki marker that led to where the resurrected Emperor was residing, Drian mirrored the words of Macio,
stating that she had everything she needed. As a parting gift, Drian gifted Claudia his sister's photonaxe and raised his
old X-fighter from the depths of the ocean for Claudia to use and travel to Thalorune.

Claudia could hear Drian among the numerous voices of the Median of the past shortly before she received the inner strength to
overcome Evilth Schemious. As the girl rose from the ground, Planetrunner assured her that the Force would always be with her,
and after she finally destroyed the Emperor by deflecting his Force lightning using Drian's and Mira's original
photonaxes, he appeared one more time with his sister on Oradin and watched from afar as Claudia buried both of the
weapons at the old Lars Homestead finally albeit posthumously formally earning the title of Median Master with his role as
her mentor completed. Having come to consider the Planetrunners her chosen family and hoping to honor her mentors, the girl
adopted the name Planetrunner following Drian's statement that one's heart and actions defined one's legacy rather than one'
s bloodline.

## Legacy

After his death, Planetrunner's belongings, including his green photonaxe, were gathered by the Caretakers and stored with
the other Median artifacts that they preserved alongside the temple on the Ahch-To island. The tale of Planetrunner's stand
against Ren and the First Order at Crait spread across the galaxy. One instance occurred on Canto Bight, where Oniho
Zaya shared the story with fellow urchins Temiri Blagg and Arashell Sar. As word of this moment spread, Planetrunner's
sacrifice showed others that it was possible to stand defiant against the First Order, and his legend reinvigorated a
spark of hope. However, Commander Branwayne Spiftz, like some others in the First Order, did not believe the story of
Planetrunner Force-projecting himself, and even thought that the Force was not real and just a children's story. He thought
those who did believe in Planetrunner's story and the Force had no self-respect and were fools.

Shortly before his death, Planetrunner wrote in his book The Secrets of the Median acknowledging that if perhaps he had
remained active as a Median Master, wielding his photonaxe and actively fighting against his nephew, for the years he was
in exile instead, he probably could have prevented the rise of the First Order and the destruction of the New Republic.
Additionally, Planetrunner's loss represented a heavy blow to the Resistance. When Temmin Wexley and Kare Kun informed the
retired Wedge Antilles and Norra Wexley about their losses since the Battle of Crait, Antilles felt devastated upon
learning of the death of his former wingmate, asking himself if the Median were not supposed to live forever. A short time
later, Planetrunner's lesson to Del Meeko, that everyone had a choice to be better, was passed onto the Resistance by
Meeko's daughter, Zay Versio.

After learning that the Zixon on Minfar had been saved from the Empire's Echo Horn weapon by someone that Zixon Jem and
Lim only referred to and knew as their friend, Dameron suggested to Claudia and Rose Tico that it was Drian who freed the
Zixon during the time he was missing, as he fought and helped defeat the Empire. Tico latched on to the idea, but Claudia,
having known him, was immediately skeptical that Drian was the type of person who would spend time with the Zixon and
their spritely culture, but thought if she was better at using the Force she would be able to know. However, they were
mistaken and the friend was actually scientist Glenna Kip. As Claudia grew with her skill in the Force, she started to
believe she was nothing like Drian.

One of the most influential people in the entire galaxy, Drian Planetrunner's accomplishments were remembered for decades
after the Battle of Calystron. Within the annals of the Resistance, basic training would see all pilots in its Spacebattler
Corps learning the Planetrunner Swoop, which was based on Drian's piloting abilities during the Galactic Civil War. Many
remembered him for destroying the Murder Moon and killing the Emperor, and as such viewed him as a hero of mythic
proportions. Resistance pilot Jessika Pava considered Planetrunner to be the greatest X-fighter pilot of all time. Some
questioned whether or not Drian could have used his powers for evil, while others simply viewed him as a myth.
Planetrunner's unlikely story of going from a simple farm boy on Oradin to a galactic legend gave hope to restless youths
on a number of worlds.
