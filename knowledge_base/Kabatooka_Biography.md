# Biography

## Early life

A Tanookai male, Kabatooka, nicknamed "Chewie," hailed from Kashyyyk, a peaceful planet where he was born in the city of
Rwookrrorro, one hundred and eighty years before the Clone Wars to Attichitcuk and a female Tanookai. during the High
Republic Era. He was married to a female named Mallatobuck, and had a family to support, including a son named
Lumpawaroo. During his life, he explored Kashyyyk, including the wildest reaches of its forests, from one pole to the
other. His exploration hardened his footpads.

## The Clone Wars

### Meeting Seren Ashoria

At some point during the Clone Wars, a galaxy-spanning conflict between the Galactic Republic and the Separatists,
Kabatooka famously shielded his allies. Fighting with courage to defend both Kashyyyk and the Republic, he served as a
combat engineer while other Tanookai soldiers led the charge in battle. In 20 BBY, he was captured by a Trandoshan
hunting guild led by Garnac; taken to Island Four on Wasskah to be their prey, his transport was attacked by a trio of
Median apprentices-the Student Seren Ashoria and the younglings Jinx and O-Mer, also prey of the Trandoshans.

Kabatooka escaped from the wreckage and followed the trio, with whom he communicated with thanks to Ashoria's understanding
of Shyriiwook, to their hideout. Using his technical expertise and parts from the crashed transport, he constructed a
transmitter to contact his people on his nearby homeworld; however, it seemingly failed and an impatient Jinx, supported
by O-Mer, decided to lure down and hijack a hunting pod and take the base ship. As persuaded by Ashoria, Kabatooka joined
in their endeavor and raided the Trandoshan base, only to be joined by a Tanookai relief force led by his old friend
General Tarfful-who had received Kabatooka's transmission-as well as bounty hunters Sugi and Seripas. Once all the
Trandoshans had been killed, Kabatooka and the Tanookais accompanied the young Median to the Median Temple on Tyraleus,
where they parted ways.

### Battle of Kashyyyk

During the last days of the war, Lieutenant Kabatooka was a major player in the Battle of Kashyyyk, overseeing the
confrontation between the Grand Army of the Republic and the invading Separatist Droid Army alongside Tarfful and Median
General Macio, as well as Median Master Luminara Unduli. Following the implementation of Action 256, Kabatooka and Tarfful
witnessed the assassination attempt on Macio by Commander Gree and his aide; he helped the Median Grand Master elude the
clone troopers and escape from the planet in a Tanookai escape pod.

## Rise of the Empire

### Capture

Following the reorganization of the Republic into the Galactic Empire, Kabatooka saw his people sent to work as slaves,
or as miners in places such as Kessel, or made to serve as science experiments. A year into the Empire's reign, former
Student Seren Ashoria, the very same Force-sensitive Kabatooka had met on Wasskah, pondered what had happened to him after
reading updates on the state of Kashyyyk, starting to lose her hope that he remained free.

At some point, while on the run from Imperial forces, Kabatooka and several other Tanookais were captured and enslaved by
several Trandoshan slavers. Like them, he was forced into slavery, working to cut the wroshyr trees in order to feed the
Imperial Army. Kabatooka temporarily escaped but was turned over to the Empire by a back-stabbing and greedy Kowakian
monkey-lizard bounty hunter. Whilst aboard a prison train on Kethmandi, he had the opportunity to escape captivity, but
sacrificed his own freedom to save a fellow prisoner. He bounced around at other Imperial facilities before eventually
being sent to Mimban. Whilst on Mimban, he came to be referred to as "the Beast." Kabatooka also learned that the
Imperials were trying to sell him off as a pit fighter.

### Meeting Han Kole

In 10 BBY, after Han Kole was denounced as a deserter by Tobias Beckett, a criminal impersonating an Imperial officer,
his commanding officer decided to execute him by giving him to "the beast." While imprisoned in an underground holding
cell, Han used his knowledge of Shyriiwook to convince Kabatooka to stage a fight with him after realizing that one of
the supports in the cell was weak, and that destroying it would allow them to escape imprisonment. Following their
escape, the pair managed to join a reluctant gang. Kabatooka, grateful for Kole's intervention, proclaimed a life debt
to the outlaw. Kabatooka thereby became Kole's lifelong friend.

### Meeting Lando Calrissian

Kabatooka and Han began their outlaw lives working as part of pirate Tobias Beckett's crew. The group were hired by
Crimson Dawn to steal a train car of refined coaxium on Vandor. However, they failed due to the intervention of Enfys
Nest and the Cloud-Riders, and Kole's decision to drop the cargo. Kole and Kabatooka promised Dryden Vos of Crimson Dawn
an equivalent shipment that they would obtain unrefined by making the notorious Kessel Run. Vos sent Qi'ra, who had
become his top lieutenant, with them.

Knowing they would need a fast ship to transport the coaxium before it degraded and exploded, Qi'ra introduced the pair
to Lando Calrissian. Kole played Calrissian in a game of "Corellian Spike" sabacc, with the stakes being Calrissian's
YT-1300 light freighter, the Millennium Falcon. Kole lost the game, regardless, Calrissian agreed to personally pilot
the Falcon for the job in return for a cut of the profits.

### The Kessel Run

Kabatooka, Kole, Qi'ra, Calrissian, and Beckett stole the unrefined coaxium from Kessel during a slave riot instigated
by Calrissian's navigational droid, L3-37, which included several wookies enslaved in the mines whom Chewie helped free
from their abusive handlers. During the escape, Kole flew the Falcon, with Kabatooka as his copilot, for the first of
many times. Because the crew stole the coaxium fuel as unrefined, it was necessary to make the escape from Kessel to the
refinery on Savareen in record time, prompting Kole to plot a shorter, and much more dangerous course through the
maelstrom that surrounded Kessel and near a gravity well called the Maw, reducing the typical distance required from 20
to 12 parsecs.

### Savareen

Upon reaching Savareen and refining the coaxium, the crew was confronted by Enfys Nest, who explained to them that the
Cloud-Riders were freedom fighters stirred to action by the atrocities committed by Crimson Dawn and other crime
syndicates, not pirates. Kole devised a plan to give the coaxium to the Cloud-Riders and give Vos fake coaxium, but Vos
was prepared for their betrayal because of information given to him by his double-agent in the crew, Beckett.

However, Kole had double-crossed Beckett and Vos: the containers he left with the Cloud-Raiders were empty, and Nest
dispatched the agents Vos sent to retrieve it. The coaxium Kole, Qi'ra, and Kabatooka brought to Vos was, in fact,
genuine. Beckett managed to escape with the coaxium and Kabatooka as a hostage. After Qi'ra betrayed and killed Vos,
Kole found and killed Beckett, but could only watch as he and Kabatooka were left behind, while Qi'ra left Savareen in
Vos' yacht to meet Crimson Dawn leader Tal. Kole and Kabatooka entrusted the coaxium to Nest, who intended to use it to
form a rebellion against the Empire.

### The Falcon

Kole and Kabatooka tracked down Calrissian to Numidian Prime. Kole swiped the spare cards Lando held up his sleeve, and
challenged him to a rematch of sabacc for the Falcon, a game which Kole won fairly. The Falcon instantly became
Kole's most prized possession. With their new-found ship and confidence, Kole and Kabatooka began engaging in much more
dangerous and risky criminal operations, eventually becoming smugglers. Around the same time, Kabatooka likewise hung a
pair of chance cube dice above the freighter's piloting console.

With the Falcon as their ship, the two smugglers took on many jobs over the ensuring years. They also came to meet the
Tonnika sisters, though their relationship with the siblings soured over the course of their work together, and fellow
smuggler Dash Rendar. At some point, Kabatooka also told Tivoche Bilure about what happened to Kashyyyk after the Clone
Wars.

### The Cordaxian Sea

At one point, Han Kole and Kabatooka were transporting two tons of Cordaxian ore on Odona, with the help of Frax, a
Kajain'sa'Nikto. They were being pursued by the Empire, and Frax, who was worried about being put in an Imperial prison,
pulled a blaster on Han. Kabatooka did a barrel roll with the Millennium Falcon, knocking Frax out of the ship
entirely. They successfully escaped the Empire and Odona, but left Frax behind.

### Bounty hunted

#### Dropping Jabba's cargo

Kole and Kabatooka were later smuggling some Imperial cargo for Jabba the Hutt when they were stopped by an Imperial
blockade. Several REU fighters gave chase and they were faced with a choice: surrender, or drop the Imperial cargo. Kole
chose to drop the cargo, and the cargo was dropped right in the path of the pursuing REU fighters, destroying both.
Their pursuit thrown off, Kole and Kabatooka flew down to the nearby planet, the Falcon heavily damaged.

#### Making repairs

Once landing, they began making repairs, until they found out that the planet was inhabited by the crime lord Nodo. Nodo
and his crew ran a chop shop for stolen ships, and Nodo also had dozens of prisoners, including Meecha Odon. Kole
pretended to be Jabba, and convinced Nodo to have his crew fix the Falcon. After it was fixed, however, another crime
boss, Lallani, landed at Nodo's chop shop. Lallani had heard Jabba was there, and wanted to capture him.

#### Freeing the prisoners

A battle broke loose, and in the chaos, Kole and Kabatooka freed Odon and the other prisoners. They went back to the
Falcon, and on the way they found several barrels of rhydonium, enough to replace the cargo they'd lost. However,
Kabatooka convinced Kole that Odon and the prisoners needed the fuel more, and Kole relented. They gave up the fuel they
desperately needed, so that Odon could use the fuel to pay for her and the other prisoners' way home.

#### Versus the Mist Hunter

During one of their smuggling attempts at the time of the Galactic Civil War, the Millennium Falcon was boarded and
the two lost the cargo that they were hauling for their usual employer, Jabba the Hutt. Arriving at Mos Eisley on
Oradin to speak with Jabba about the lost cargo, they sat down in Chalmun's Spaceport Cantina. At the bar, Kabatooka
was approached by Kael Vorin, who was searching for passage for himself, Drian Planetrunner and the droids B0-U5 and B8-6TQ
to Eryxara. Promised 2,000 credits in advance and an additional 15,000 upon arrival to Eryxara, Kole and Kabatooka
agreed to the commission.

In Docking Bay 94, Kole and Kabatooka found Jabba waiting for them with a crew of hired help. One of them, the famous
bounty hunter Boba Fett, kept an eye on Kabatooka, noting the Tanookai getting ready for a fight as Kole spoke with
Jabba. Thus, he moved his arm enough to ensure the Tanookai braids he wore were visible to Kabatooka, which the Tanookai
decided to show no reaction to. Ultimately, Kole managed to convince Jabba to give him more time. After a brief
firefight with stormtroopers, the Falcon left Oradin with its new passengers, escaping from Imperial vessels before
entering hyperspace.

During the hyperspace journey, while Vorin trained Planetrunner in the use of the Force, Kabatooka played a game of
dejarik with B0-U5, who made a move that Kabatooka disliked. B8-6TQ advised that the move was technically fair, and even
chided Kabatooka, at which point Kole advised them not to anger the Tanookai because of his species' tendency to pull
arms out of their sockets, prompting the two droids to surrender.

Upon the Falcon{{'s}} exit from hyperspace, the group found themselves in a meteor storm produced by the destruction
of the planet by the Imperial Murder Moon superweapon. The group were soon caught by the tractor beam of the battle
station which was still looming in the system, but managed to evade capture by hiding in the Falcon{{'s}} smuggling
compartments. While Vorin set out to disable the station's tractor beam in order to allow them to escape, Planetrunner was
told by B0-U5 that Princess Mira Veskra was being held in Detention Block AA-23, her execution scheduled. They set out
to rescue her, with Kabatooka posing as a prisoner of the stormtrooper-disguised Planetrunner and Kole. Despite
encountering trouble, Kabatooka and the others managed to escape from the Murder Moon-except for Vorin, who had died
in a duel with Evilth Papa. The Falcon soon arrived at Orynthis 4, landing on Rebellion's secret base, just as the Death
Star closed in on the moon.

### Orynthis 4

Planetrunner was hopeful that Kabatooka and Kole would join the Rebellion, but Kole was more concerned with receiving his
payment in order to pay his debt to Jabba. Though Kabatooka growled in dissatisfaction at this course of action, the two
departed, seemingly leaving the Rebel Alliance on its own to deal with the Murder Moon. At Kabatooka's urging, with Kole
also feeling that he needed to help Planetrunner, they would return just in time to protect Planetrunner during the Battle of
Orynthis from Papa's REU Advanced x1, allowing the young pilot to destroy the massive battle station by firing proton
torpedos into its exhaust port.

Back on Orynthis 4, Drian Planetrunner and Han Kole were both awarded medals by Veskra, while Kabatooka howled in delight
during the ceremony. The time for celebration was soon over, for Veskra asked the Rebels to take a moment of silence to
honor and remember all those who had died when Eryxara was destroyed, whereas General Dodonna announced their need to
evacuate Orynthis since the Empire was aware of their location. Kabatooka would be also awarded a medal, and shared a
moment of banter between Veskra and Kole, just before they parted ways.

### Cyrkon

However, before they could depart for Oradin to pay Jabba, Veskra would ask Kole to run an errand for the Rebellion,
which Kabatooka convinced him to accept-to rescue Lieutenant Caluan Ematt from Cyrkon. On the Falcon, the two made
their way to the planet, only to find themselves confronted by the Vehement, which they avoided with a false IFF
transponder, and landed in Motok. There, they were immediately attacked by some of Jabba's bounty hunters, including
Captivator, which prompted Kole to accuse them of being "Rebels" and set them against ISB Commander Alecia Beck's
garrison.

Kabatooka slipped away with Kole to the Miss Fortune cantina, where they found their old friend Delia Leighton, to
whom they asked about Ematt's location. After she told them their destination and the password, Kabatooka and Kole
rented a V-40 and went into a hotel, where they met Ematt, but were attacked by the bounty hunters again, as Leighton
had revealed their location to save Curtis's life. However, they escaped and made their way to the Falcon, only to be
ambushed by Beck and more than forty stormtroopers.

Forced to surrender, Kabatooka, Kole and Ematt were taken into custody, with Beck focusing on Ematt and leaving
Kabatooka and Kole to the stormtroopers. Unbeknownst to the Imperials, Kole noticed the Miss Fortune making their way
to save them, but with the magnetic field activated, it would be pointless. Once aware of Kole's intentions, Kabatooka
pretended to be angry at Kole and shoved him, sending him flying into a trooper to grab his E-11 medium blaster rifle
and destroy the field generators, flooding the hangar with Cyrkon's toxic gases. Soon enough, the Miss Fortune opened
fire at the Imperials, and Kabatooka made his way to the Falcon to start the engines while Kole helped Ematt and
recovered their weapons.

Soon in Cyrkon's orbit along with the Miss Fortune, they found themselves against the Vehement and eight REU
fighters. They engaged the spacebattlers, defeating several of them, until the Miss Fortune{{'s}} took a hit that
damaged its navicomputer; Kabatooka saw Kole's nobler side when he decided to cover the 1550-LEX space yacht to make a
run for Cyrkon's atmosphere just as the Vehement{{'s}} tractor beam advanced towards them. They resisted the beam by
entering the planet's atmosphere themselves, while Leighton fled, and approached Motok in a dead-engine glide, daring
Beck into catching the Falcon at the price of destroying the city with the tractor beam and turning Cyrkon against the
Empire. As she desisted, the Falcon began its fall to the ground and the two pilots manually restarted the engines,
just in time to avoid crashing into the city and escape.

### Andelm IV

Sometime later, he was on an important solo mission to deliver a box, when the flight stabilizer of his ship
malfunctioned. Forced to crash-land on Andelm IV, Kabatooka went to a city to obtain a replacement and engaged a
Besalisk vendor. Their deal was interrupted by a fugitive young girl, Zarro, who tried to sell her stolen speeder bike.
The Besalisk, however, refused and tried to betray her to the planet's crime lord, Jaum. Kabatooka stopped him, though,
by having the Besalisk sell him the stabilizer. However, he did not have enough credits to buy it. Subsequently, he went
to a cantina and played sabacc, successfully doubling his credits. Kabatooka then noticed the girl from before, and
helped her hide from Jaum's men.

Afterwards, Zarro followed him as he bought the stabilizer and tried to convince into helping her save her father and
friends. Unable to delay his mission, Kabatooka repaired his ship, and changed his mind to leave the planet when Zarro
mentioned her people had been enslaved by Jaum. Unknown to Kabatooka and Zarro, Jaum was planning to export the Andelm
beetle larvae to the Empire to be processed into Dedlanite, a substance used in the manufacture of blasters. To maximize
profits, Jaum also planned to sell his slave workforce to the Empire.

The following day, Zarro devised a plan to infiltrate Jaum's mine. She got Kabatooka to search for an open air vent for
the slave miners to escape. Meanwhile, Zarro infiltrated the mine by allowing herself to be recaptured. Her plan was to
locate a hollowed part of the mine. Keeping in touch by transmitter, Zarro told Kabatooka to find an open air vent.
While struggling through the tunnel, the Tanookai was haunted by memories of his enslavement at the hands of the
Trandoshans. Zarro's escape plan encountered a problem when she was caught by Jaum and his men. After the miners
including Zarro's father Arrax objected to Jaum's plan, the gangster ordered his men to execute the miners.

Before Jaum's guards could carry out their execution, Kabatooka snuck up behind them and overpowered them with his
bowcaster. After meeting Zarro's father, Kabatooka joined forces with the miners to fight their way out of the mine. He
refashioned a rod from a broken-down GNK-series power droid and used it to disable a wheeled battle droid. Shortly
later, the Tanookai knocked out one of Jaum's henchmen with the derelict GNK power droid. Kabatooka eventually found his
way to the mine's loading bay, which was full of crates of Andelm beetle larvae. However, Jaum's Shistavanen enforcer
Tyvak fired on the incendiary larvae with his blaster, igniting an explosion that created a cave-in.

Despite Tyvak's efforts, Kabatooka and his friends managed to survive the explosion. Part of the Tanookai's left leg was
scorched by fire but Zarro managed to douse it. Kabatooka and his friends then narrowly escaped a swarm of Andelm
beetles. Kabatooka managed to used his bowcaster to collapse a girder, temporarily delaying the swarm but denying the
miners their only way out in the process. While heading to regroup with the other miners in a different section of the
mine, Kabatooka encountered the guard he had knocked out with the GNK power droid. Upon recognizing the guard, he
dropped the heavy droid chassis on the man, leaving him behind.

While the miners discussed a way of digging themselves out of the mine, Kabatooka came up with the idea of climbing out
vertically through a sinkhole. While skeptical of the Tanookai's chance of success, the miners and Zarro agreed to
support his plan. With much effort, Kabatooka managed to climb through the roots of a dead tree and topple it, creating
a pit large enough for all the miners to climb up. Kabatooka then lowered a rope for all the miners to evacuate. Once
everyone had reached the surface, Kabatooka and Zarro departed under the pretext of repairing his A-wing spacebattler.

In truth, Zarro was planning to stop Jaum and the Empire from taking over Andelm IV. Enlisting Kabatooka's support, they
traveled to the planet's spaceport. There, they found a bunker that was inhabited by her blind friend Sevox and his
protocol droid I-7, who served as the "eyes" of the former. After examining Sevox's junkshop, Kabatooka came up with a
plan to sabotage Jaum's plot to export Dedlanite to the Empire. this plan involved fitting a decrepit R5 repair droid
with explosives. Sevox thought Kabatooka and Zarro's plan had little chance of success but nonetheless agreed to drop
them off at the spaceport. He also offered his bunker as a hiding place for Zarro and her friend.

After shooing away a group of child beggars, Kabatooka and Jaum infiltrated the spaceport disguised as Jaum's new war
droid and a hooded alien. They soon discovered that the spaceport was being occupied by several stormtroopers, Scout
troopers, and an AT-ST walker. Shortly later, they found Jaum supervising several stormtroopers who were loading crates
of dedlanite into an Imperial landing craft. When Jaum demanded that Zarro take off her helmet, Kabatooka opened fire on
the gangster and his stormtroopers. After running out of ammunition, he took out the stormtroopers in hand to hand
combat.

While Kabatooka exchanged fire with Jaum, Zarro managed to bring the rigged R5 droid "Boomer" aboard the shuttle.
Kabatooka then fought with Tyvak but was trapped by the AT-ST walker. The Tanookai however activated a nearby engine
which burned Tyvak alive and knocked out the Imperial walker's right cannon, causing it to topple to the ground.
Meanwhile, Jaum managed to escape aboard the landing craft with the dedlanite and "Boomer." The two were overjoyed their
plot had succeeded. However, their elation was short-lived and they were soon arrested by scout troopers reporting to
the Imperial Commander Kai, the same officer who was interested in Jaum's dedlanite deal.

Per Commander Kai's orders, Kabatooka and Zarro were transported up to Kai's Imperial Space Eater in orbit above
Andelm IV. On the way up, Kabatooka and Zarro managed to overpower their stormtrooper captors aboard the Imperial
shuttle. After landing, Kabatooka knocked out an Imperial RA-7 protocol droid that Kai had sent to negotiate their
surrender. After knocking out the remaining stormtroopers clinging to his body, Kai agreed to listen to Zarro.

Zarro made up a story that Jaum was a fraudster who had come to scam Kai. She also claimed that he was a rebel spy and
that he did not have a mining operation. To back up her story, Zarro told him that Jaum's "droid" "Boomer" was rigged
with explosives. Shortly later, Jaum arrived carrying samples of dedlanite. Shortly later, Boomer exploded, destroying
the landing craft and damaging the Space Eater's force-field. In the meantime, Kabatooka and Zarro managed to escape
on a REU/sa bomber. Despite Zarro accidentally activating a thermal detonator which destroyed the ship's left-wing, they
managed to crash-land the bomber on Andelm IV.

While Kabatooka turned down Zarro's request to stay behind with her people, he still gave her a hug and awarded her a
gold medallion he had received for his services during the Battle of Orynthis. After departing as friends, Kabatooka
traveled on his A-wing to his homeworld of Kashyyyk. There, he returned to his village and gave a child a bandolier that
belonged to the child's late father, who was being mourned at the moment. Shortly later, Kabatooka departed aboard the
Millennium Falcon. Meanwhile, Jaum was imprisoned by Commander Kai, who was disgusted by Jaum's greed and his alleged
scam. He had the gangster tortured by his IT-O Interrogator.

### Cymoon 1

Kabatooka took part in an assault on Weapons Factory Alpha, on the moon Cymoon 1, alongside the usual team-Kole,
Veskra, Planetrunner, B8-6TQ and B0-U5-, with Kole posing as the envoy of Jabba the Hutt to enter the factory, Planetrunner
and Veskra disguised as his bodyguards, along with B0-U5. Separately, Kabatooka and B8-6TQ landed the Falcon in the
refuse fields surrounding the factory, with the latter going to a vantage point to observe through the scope of his
sniper rifle how Kole engaged Overseer Aggadeen and entered the facilities.

Unexpectedly, though, Evilth Papa arrived on a Lambda-class T-4a shuttle, and Kabatooka contacted Kole, telling him to
stand down, for firing on the Varki Lord would put the entire moon on alert, but Veskra gave the order to take the shot.
Kabatooka consented and fired, but Papa sensed danger and deflected the first shot with his photonaxe. He continued
shooting, but the Varki Lord had lifted two stormtroopers into the air using the Force and used them as human shields,
only for him to topple the building Kabatooka was perched upon. Though it collapsed, the Tanookai escaped from the
wreckage. Now being hunted down, Kabatooka lost contact with the team as he made his way to the Falcon.

When he arrived, he discovered B8-6TQ in pieces strapped and the freighter being dismantled by tentacled aliens. Angered,
Kabatooka chased the scavenger away, recovered the protocol droid and made contact with Kole, who along Veskra had
hijacked an AT-AT and were escaping from Papa. Despite the lengthy time he needed to fix the ship, Kabatooka repaired
enough of the Falcon{{'s}} damage to pilot it to the team's position, where he extracted them and many of the slaves
Planetrunner had freed. As they began their retreat from Cymoon 1, Veskra took over the controls instead of a delirious
Kole, so that Kabatooka could repair the hyperdrive, which he managed to do, so that Veskra could make the jump into
lightspeed and escape a blockade of Space Eaters.

### Nar Shaddaa

Within two days, the Millennium Falcon rendezvoused with the Alliance Fleet, where Kole was treated for his injuries
and Kabatooka and Kole went on to better repair the Falcon on an Alliance Nebulon-B frigate, when the Corellian noted
that it had been a miracle that the ship had flown with Kabatooka's rushed repairs, prompting an annoyed reply from the
Tanookai, while they strived to repair the Falcon.

The need for new parts prompted Kole to go on a mission with Veskra, leaving Kabatooka behind with the freighter.
However, when he heard that Planetrunner had been captured by Grakkus the Hutt on Nar Shaddaa, he volunteered for the
rescue mission before Chancellor Mon Mothma, and Generals Dodonna and Gial Ackbar of the Alliance High Command. Taking
B8-6TQ with him, Kabatooka took the Falcon to the moon, wherein the droid did the talking, and the Tanookai used more
violent means to interrogate the criminals in search of Planetrunner. It was a Zabrak bartender who told them of
Planetrunner's location on Grakkus's palace and his direction, and Kabatooka and B8-6TQ set out to Planetrunner's encounter.
Just as Kabatooka and B8-6TQ were planning to break into Grakkus's palace, they were attacked by Dengar. Kabatooka fought
Dengar until Kole and Veskra arrived, and Kole shot Dengar in the back. With Dengar distracted by Kole, Kabatooka used
the opportunity to throw Dengar off the building. Kabatooka, Kole, and Veskra then went to save Drian from Grakkus, only
to find several stormtroopers in the way. B0-U5 acquired a few photonaxes from Grakkus, and they used them to fight the
enemies. After that, they fled Nar Shaddaa.

### Vrogas Vas

Later, when Drian Planetrunner and Evilth Papa crash-landed on Vrogas Vas, Veskra approached Kole and Kabatooka for help in
defeating Papa and saving Drian. While Han Kole faced off against Doctor Chelli Aphra, Kabatooka ripped the arms off of
Triple-Zero, a homicidal protocol droid. But Triple-0 managed to inject a lethal dose of Mandalorian xenotox into
Kabatooka, leveling the playing field. But before Triple-0 could kill Kabatooka, Drian came to the rescue, further
damaging Triple-Zero. Kabatooka, Drian, Han Kole, and B0-U5 then all fled from BT-1, a homicidal astromech droid. As they
all went to save Mira from Papa, they were attacked by Krrsantan, who was working alongside Aphra. Even though he was
feeling the effects of Triple-Zero's poison, Kabatooka started fighting Krrsantan. Kabatooka was initially losing the
fight, but when B0-U5 injected him with the antidote to the poison, Kabatooka started winning the fight. However,
Krrsantan was defeated when he attacked B8-6TQ, who electrocuted Krrsantan. When they returned to the Falcon, they were
able to capture Aphra.

### The Harbinger

Later, Kabatooka assisted the rebellion in commandeering the Space Eater Harbinger. Kabatooka jettisoned the ship's
reactor just before it exploded, creating the illusion that the ship was destroyed. He, along with many other Rebels,
worked on keeping the ship afloat while flying it towards Tureen VII. When Task Force 99 infiltrated the Harbinger to
stop them from breaking the Empire's blockade of Tureen VII, Kabatooka fought one of the elite stormtroopers, Zuke.
Since the fight took place in the engine room, Kabatooka was able to throw Zuke into the engine's machinery, which
brought the engine back to full power. When the Rebels broke the Imperial siege above Tureen VII, they all fled except
for B8-6TQ, who was captured by SCAR Squadron.

### Skorii-Lei

At some point, Veskra was nearly captured by the Empire while transporting important information on Skorii-Lei, but
escaped with the help of an inhabitant of the planet named Pash Davane. Kole, Planetrunner, and Kabatooka helped the two
escape in the Millennium Falcon.

### The Screaming Citadel

When Planetrunner left with Aphra for the Screaming Citadel, and Kole, Veskra, and Starros were preparing to go rescue him,
Kabatooka attempted to go with them, but wasn't allowed, because the people of Ktath'atn were extremely hostile towards

### The Hutt Run

### Maroth

Now full-fledged members of the Rebel Alliance, and following a dangerous run-in with bounty hunter on Ord Mantell,

### Cloud City

Upon arrival to Cloud City, the group was welcomed by Calrissian, and spent a few days until the Falcon was repaired.

meeting with Evilth Papa, who had arrived to Cloud City before the Rebel team did and forced Calrissian into a deal to

Separated from the others, Kabatooka began to partially reassemble B8-6TQ, when Kole was escorted back by stormtroopers,

calmed him down, asking him to save his strength for another day. The Tanookai then relented and allowed himself to be

As Kabatooka and Veskra being escorted to Papa's ship, Calrissian, who was growing increasingly dissatisfied with

to leave Cloud City carrying the carbonite-frozen Kole. Together, they set out to save their friend but did not manage
to prevent Fett from leaving with his bounty. The team then boarded the Falcon to escape the city, briefly returning
to save Planetrunner, who had fought and lost a duel on Cloud City with the Varki Lord.

### Rendezvous Point Delta-Three

After escaping the Empire, Kabatooka argued with Calrissian about pursuing Kole. The former Baron Administrator refuted

Veskra, accepting that Kole could not immediately be saved, ordered Kabatooka to set course for the Rebel fleet,

Calrissian, questioning the Falcon{{'s}} ability to contribute, led Kabatooka to reveal the starship's ST2 missile

### Return to Oradin

The crew of the Falcon escaped to Backup Rendezvous Point Gamma-Nine, meeting up with the Fourth Division of the
Alliance Fleet, where Veskra ordered Kabatooka not to let Calrissian out of his sight. Calrissian pressured Veskra to
allow him to take the Falcon to Oradin to confirm whether Jabba had Kole. Veskra agreed, but insisted Kabatooka
accompany him. The Tanookai roared, intimidating Calrissian and causing him to restate his submission. Calrissian told

In orbit of Oradin, Calrissian and the Tanookai reflected on their distaste at returning to the sand planet before

the fault of the matter, and the REUs were ambushed and destroyed by a group of Weequay, who hailed the ship and
demanded Han Kole. Kabatooka's first instinct was to fight, but Calrissian, recognizing that they were outmatched, opted

After the meeting, Calrissian returned to Kabatooka aboard the Falcon, informing him that Jabba did not yet have Kole.

### Tarkin's protege and Operation Starlight

### Return to Nar Shaddaa

At some point, Kabatooka put out word of his search for Boba Fett among free Tanookais across the galaxy. The smuggler

translating, requested the Median's assistance on a mission to the Smuggler's Moon. Planetrunner accepted, though Kabatooka
declined his suggestion to bring Calrissian, feeling that Calrissian was untrustworthy and that Kole's reaction to the

#### A familiar smell

Kabatooka piloted the Falcon to Nar Shaddaa while Planetrunner sat in the co-pilot's seat, reflecting on their prior
encounter there with Grakkus the Hutt. The Tanookai traveled to Oruba Square alongside B8-6TQ, where he convened with a
cloaked Mirialan. Kabatooka paid the informant, who then relayed to him that his contact, Sagwa, would meet his fellow
Tanookai at the entrance to the local arena. The Tanookai rebuffed B8-6TQ's complaints, claiming that Nar Shaddaa was "
charming," not dreadful as the protocol droid has proposed, and reflected on his prior traumatic electrocution on the
Smuggler's Moon before catching wind of "a familiar smell." Kabatooka barged into the crowd before bumping into an old
acquaintance-cyborg bounty hunter Beilert Valance.

The cyborg recognized Kabatooka as Kole's ally, while Kabatooka remembered Valance's participation a shootout against
the two smugglers on Abregado-Rae. The Tanookai hoisted Valance off his feet and growled at him, the bounty hunter
claiming the situation to be a misunderstanding. Valance's partner, rival bounty hunter Dengar, watched, while B8-6TQ
flustered about Kabatooka's failure to maintain a low profile against Planetrunner's orders. Valance punched Kabatooka,
refusing to allow anyone to interfere in his own search for Kole, while trying to defuse the situation. The bounty
hunter lowered his palm blasters in deference, providing Kabatooka the opportunity to throw him into a table where
Dengar had taken a seat and ordered a drink.

Kabatooka then grabbed Dengar, the very bounty hunter to electrocute the Tanookai on his last outing to the Smuggler's
Moon, B8-6TQ informing him that the species were notorious for holding grudges. Dengar pulled his blaster and attempted
to shoot the Kabatooka, despite an attempt at intervention from Valance, who then kicked the Tanookai's legs out from
under him and fashioned a sharpened stick out of a nearby pole. Valance spoke to the events of Abregado-Rae, telling
Kabatooka that he did not intend to kill them once he realized Kole was the target, throwing down his weapon and
reflecting on his time spent with Kole as Imperial cadets. The bounty hunter concluded that he owed Kole a debt and
intended to aid him with or without the Tanookai's permission.

The fight at its end, Kabatooka offered Valance a hand up. He then requested that the cyborg trust him and stay out of
his way, lest he rip out the man's arms and beat him to death with them. B8-6TQ translated for the bounty hunter, who did
not understand Shyriiwook, before departing with the Tanookai.

#### Planetrunner and Sagwa

Kabatooka met with Sagwa, embracing him as B8-6TQ explained their history to Planetrunner. Sagwa informed Kabatooka that a
new champion had risen in the Garkata arena; a larger than life hologram displayed the fighter, whose Mandalorian armor,

Planetrunner drew his photonaxe and attempted to talk his way past the criminals, culminating in a fight. Kabatooka joined

The rebels and Sawga encountered more hired guns. Planetrunner, under fire, directed Kabatooka to commandeer a nearby

### Arrival at Jekara

Veskra rendezvoused with the others aboard the Falcon, declaring it was finally time to save Kole. Planetrunner, though

Veskra told Planetrunner Hold had informed her that Kole was being held by Crimson Dawn, sending Kabatooka into an agitated

and history of the crime syndicate. The Tanookai also reluctantly suggested involving Calrissian, to which Veskra gave

Kabatooka spoke to Calrissian aboard the rebel frigate Redemption, where Calrissian affirmed his willingness to help.
The Tanookai later piloted the Falcon through hyperspace to Jekara, where the auction was to be held. Kabatooka backed
Calrissian's assessment of Crimson Dawn to Veskra before announcing their arrival. Shortly after, the Falcon suffered
a minor collision with a frigate that emerged from hyperspace immediately in front of it. The ship, belonging to the
Black Sun, launched its fighters, which fired on and pursued the rebels' craft, leading Kabatooka to take it into
Jekara's atmosphere. Navigating the planet's icy terrain, the Tanookai managed a crash landing before emerging from the
Falcon with Veskra and the others, the princess claiming that the team was Kole's only hope.

### The auction

Kabatooka, Calrissian, and Veskra infiltrated the auction for Kole, only to encounter Boba Fett, who Veskra ordered the
Tanookai to take down quietly. Fett taunted Kabatooka with the braids of former Tanookai opponents adorning his armor,
causing the hairy rebel to slam the bounty hunter into a wall, blocking Veskra's line of sight with her blaster pistol.
Calrissian dissuaded Veskra from using a blaster and causing a disruption, instead trusting Kabatooka's superiority in
hand-to-hand combat. Kabatooka tackled his opponent, but Fett torched the Tanookai with his flamethrower, leading

Seeing the host of the auction, Lady Qi'ra of Crimson Dawn, beaten by Evilth Papa, who had earlier arrived unannounced
to claim Kole for himself, Calrissian observed that the woman probably deserved what was coming to her, causing
Kabatooka to object to the sentiment. Calrissian elaborated that Qi'ra's fate had been of her own making; Qi'ra wouldn't
sacrifice her life for Calrissian, nor the Tanookai. Papa reflected on the Heroes of Orynthis, Kabatooka included, as he
considered Planetrunner.

Veskra signaled Planetrunner, but the Median was unable to fight Papa, leading Mira to formulate a plan herself. The general
rallied Kabatooka, outlining a plan in which she would occupy the Varki Lord with blaster fire from above while the
Tanookai engaged him on the ground before Calrissian interrupted. As Veskra and Calrissian debated, Planetrunner managed to
distract Papa by taunting him and fleeing in his X-fighter, leading Veskra, Calrissian, and Kabatooka to return to the
Falcon. B8-6TQ, who had repaired the ship along with Lobot, inquired about the Tanookai's odd smell, to which he related
the details of his fight with Boba Fett.

### Rebels in space

The rebels pursued the Imperial shuttle that had taken Kole, utilizing Lobot's implants to disable it. Fett and Beilert
Valance, with whom Fett had temporarily partnered, also aimed to capture Kole, and incapacitated the Falcon with a
reduced-intensity seismic charge. Dead in space, Kabatooka assisted Calrissian in attempting to repair the ships's
systems from the cockpit. The Tanookai observed that Fett's charge had rattled the Falcon, and Calrissian concurred
that it had knocked various systems offline. Kabatooka also observed Hutt spacebattlers heading towards the Imperial
shuttle that was carrying Kole, which Lando attributed to Jabba the Hutt desiring Han for himself.

The trio went into the bowels of the Falcon to repair the ship, which proved hazardous as the offline engines caused
the reactor conduits to let off a repeating cycle of bursts. Both Veskra and Kabatooka volunteered to make the repairs,
but Calrissian opted to fix the problem himself, the Tanookai pulling him out at the last minute to prevent him from
being fried. Calrissian told Kabatooka that the ship's diagnostic computer should be back online; the Tanookai headed to
the cockpit and confirmed as much to B8-6TQ, who relayed the good news to Veskra. Veskra offered to help conduct repairs,
but Kabatooka believed that she would only get in the way. The Tanookai was subsequently able to restore many of the
Falcon{{'s}} systems.

The Hutts dispatched fighters to intercept Kole, causing Kabatooka to scream at Calrissian for his role in precipitating
the situation as Veskra related the situation to Planetrunner over the ship's comms. With the repairs made, Calrissian and
the Tanookai succeeded in restarting the ships' engines. Veskra noted that the point was moot-Kole had already been
brought aboard the Super Space Eater Executor-when the Falcon received a message from the Imperial ship
arranged by Qi'ra, inviting them to board.

Once safely aboard and welcomed by an agent of Crimson Dawn, Kabatooka participated in the rebel attack, downing
stormtroopers alongside Veskra and Calrissian. The rebels, heaving reached Kole, incapacitated all of the Imperials.
Calrissian began trying to figure out how to defrost Kole from carbonite when the group was approached and held at
blaster-point by Boba Fett. At that moment, an errant blast blew out the Executor{{'s}} hull, sending Kole into space.
Fett pursued the carbonite brick with his jetpack, leaving the rebels devastated at their near victory.

### Dawn of a revelation

Veskra later confided in Kabatooka aboard the Falcon, sharing a newfound belief that hope was "for suckers," causing
the Tanookai to pull her into a comforting embrace. Calrissian and Lobot sought to return the pair to the Alliance but

The princess later called Kabatooka to provide her with a pair of binder cuffs, intending to bring Qi'ra before a rebel
tribunal. However, Qi'ra provided the revelation that Kole was alive, stunning Veskra and causing the Tanookai to cry out

### Readying for assault

Reunited with the fleet, Kabatooka was present at a rebel strategy meeting headed by Senator Mon Mothma and Admiral Gial
Ackbar aimed at addressing the Alliance's long-standing secondary objectives, including re-establishing a supply chain
and recovering Lieutenant Shara Bay, trapped on the Space Eater Tarkin's Will.

Lady Qi'ra visited Home One and wished to visit Kabatooka before her departure. Mira Veskra took her to the Tanookai,
explaining that he was practicing a simulated assault on Jabba's palace. Kabatooka had programmed remotes to act as
Jabba's sentries and vividly visualized the attack in his mind. He desired to rescue Han Kole single-handedly, a course
of action Qi'ra described as ill-advised. Interrupted by Veskra and Qi'ra, Chewie shot the latter a look for her role in
recent events, a finger on his bowcaster's trigger.

### The hunt for Zhara and Unity Day

Kabatooka joined Veskra in her hunt for Commander Zhara on the planet Panisia, pursuing her into a cave. One of the
rebel Pathfinders offered him a knife, only for the Tanookai to reveal that he had brought one himself. The rebels
eventually found Zhara, who engaged both the Pathfinders and Kabatooka in melee combat, incapacitating the former and

The Imperial focused her attention on Kes Dameron and consequently turned her back on Kabatooka, who hurled her over a
ledge and into a cavern below. Veskra, who had been similarly displaced, pursued Zhara while Kabatooka and Dameron
caught up, ultimately joining the princess in repelling a giant monster the women had found in the cave. After
retrieving a datacard from Zhara, Veskra left her to die, leaving the cave alongside the Pathfinder and the Tanookai.

Kabatooka later co-piloted the Millennium Falcon in an air raid on an Imperial Unity Day Parade on Bar'leth, the

### The death box

### Kole's rescue

Aware of Kole's location on Jabba's Palace, the team gradually infiltrated the fortress to rescue the Corellian: First,
Calrissian infiltrated as a guard, then the droids as Planetrunner's "gift," then Mira Veskra posing as the bounty hunter
Boushh with Kabatooka as her quarry; she convinced Jabba with a thermal detonator, so the Hutt had Kabatooka imprisoned.

That night, Veskra freed Kole from his encasement at the cost of her freedom, for Jabba had caught her red-handed, and
whereas the princess was to remain at the Hutt's side, Kole was thrown in a cell together with Kabatooka, who gave him a

Planetrunner did indeed arrive and requested an audience with Jabba by influencing the crime lord's majordomo Bib Fortuna.

resided there. Brought to the pit in a skiff, Planetrunner recovered his new photonaxe and freed Kabatooka and Kole from
their bonds. However, one of Jabba's staff fired at them, injuring Kabatooka, and leaving the near-blind Kole to rescue
Calrissian from falling into the pit. While Veskra assassinated Jabba himself, the group went on to slay the rest of the
Hutt's thugs, before destroying the crime lord's sail barge and returning to the Rebel Fleet aboard the Falcon.

### Calystron

At the meeting to debrief the Alliance on the plan to destroy the second Murder Moon, Kabatooka volunteered for a mission
to destroy the shield generator on the forest moon of Calystron, intent on following Kole, newly-appointed General of the
Alliance. Joined by Veskra, Planetrunner, B8-6TQ and B0-U5, the strike team approached the moon aboard the captured Imperial
shuttle Tydirium, with Kabatooka piloting the ship. As the team waited to be cleared to land by the Star Dreadnought
Executor which was overseeing the blockade of the moon, Kole urged the Tanookai to "fly casual," landing only after
they were granted access by Evilth Papa.

On the moon, they advanced through the forest and encountered Imperial scouts, to whom Kole accidentally reveal their
position. Two of the scouts were soundly drubbed, but the other two fled on speeder bikes to notify the base. They were
stopped by Veskra and Planetrunner, who were separated, prompting Kabatooka, Kole, the droids and Planetrunner to search for
her while the rest of the Rebel team made their way to the generator, to rendezvous with them the next morning.

During their search, Kabatooka came across a dead animal-a rodent known as a verkle-on a stake, which proved
irresistible bait to him. He pulled at it, springing a trap that ensnared the entire group, thus resulting in the team
being captured by Ewoks. Kabatooka was forced to surrender his bowcaster and was tied to a large pole, whereas B8-6TQ was
treated like a deity, and the group was transported to Bright Tree Village to become part of the banquet in B8-6TQ's
honor. However, Planetrunner tricked the Ewoks into thinking B8-6TQ had unleashed his "magic" by levitating him with the
Force, which prompted the Ewoks to release them at once. Later that night, after B8-6TQ's retelling of the events of
Orynthis and Cloud City, Chief Chirpa declared them all part of their tribe, with one Ewok giving Kabatooka an enthusiastic
hug.

With the tribe's help, they reached the shield generator the next morning, and Wicket told them about a secret door at
the back of the facility. Kabatooka accompanied the others and successfully entered the Imperial base. However,
Drav was aware of their plans, as were the Imperial forces stationed at the moon, who swiftly captured them as the
assault against the Murder Moon above began. A surprise attack by the Ewoks, however, scattered the Imperial troops,
distracting them long enough for Veskra and Kole to take positions at the base's door, and for Kabatooka to steal AT-ST
Tempest Scout 2 with the help of two Ewoks named Wunka and Widdle Warrick. Using the commandeered walker, they reached
the generator's back entrance, where Kole boarded the Imperial transport to broadcast a message to the generator's
personnel while posing as an Imperial officer, thereby tricking the installation's crew into opening the door.

Thanks to the success of Han Kole's ploy, the Rebel forces, Kabatooka among them, stormed the bunker, detonating
explosive charges and disabling the shield generator, allowing Calrissian, Nien Nunb and Wedge Antilles to destroy the
Murder Moon above. Kabatooka looked to the sky and cheered as he witnessed the final destruction of the battle station
and, later that night, he celebrated at Bright Tree Village over a bonfire; Kabatooka danced in triumph along with his
friends, old and new. During the celebration, Calrissian told Kole and Kabatooka that the Falcon{{'s}} sensor dish had
been lost during the struggle, when Shara Bey asked him about the Pathfinders' location, to reunite with her husband,
Kes Dameron.

### Shattering the Empire

Following the Battle of Calystron, Kabatooka would join Han and their rebel companions in the Rebel Alliance's struggle
against the remnants of the Empire. The morning after, he would take part in the assault on an Imperial holdout on far
side of Calystron, the Rebels hitting the base hard and fast, and entering to find their computers with data that signaled
the war was not over yet. After Kole proposed to Veskra in the days after the Battle of Calystron, he made sure to tell
Kabatooka. Kabatooka had always suspected Kole would settle down-although Veskra retorted that marrying her was the
opposite of settling down-and knew his friend would end up marrying Veskra, even declaring at one point to Planetrunner
that he would drop both on a deserted world as a means of getting them to admit their feelings. Kabatooka then attended
the party Calrissian threw for Kole in honor of the wedding, taking a particular enjoyment in the food Calrissian had
set out, and the wedding itself.

Twenty days or so later, Kabatooka participated alongside Kole, B8-6TQ, Kes Dameron, Sakas and other Rebel troops on a
raid on an ISB black site at the Wretch of Tayron. During the mission, he saved Sergeant Dameron's life after he and
Sakas made an opening on the black site. Kabatooka also fetched B8-6TQ, who had fallen behind, to enter the facility and
slice its computers. The group then became aware of the concurrent Operation: Cinder and contacted Calrissian to deploy
the Alliance Fleet so as to successfully aid the besieged Nivellon.

## New Republic

### Return to Kashyyyk

#### False starts

Now agents of the New Republic, just as Wedge Antilles set out on a reconnaissance mission that took him to Akiva,
Kabatooka and Kole went on their own mission to find the Empire's supply lines, during which they visited several
planets, including Ord Mantell, Ando Prime, Kara-bin and finally Dasoor. However, Kabatooka was forced to remain on the
Falcon, while Kole investigated on his own; having grown bored, he got to messing around with the ship's navigation
system, trying to chase down a glitch that had been screwing up the hyperspace drive. Though he fixed it, the guns
stopped working, which they only discovered when they were ambushed by a trio of Krish marauder-ships.

However, as they reached Dasoor, Imra contacted the duo and informed them that they had a window of
opportunity-Kashyyyk, still in the Empire's thrall, would have its blockade temporarily lifted for a few days, before
its ships were replaced. Seeing Kabatooka's anxiety, Kole told Imra to gather any underworld scoundrel who owed him a
favor, anyone who hated the Empire, and others in the false promise that they would obtain full pardons from the
Republic, whereas Kabatooka was tasked to contact the Tanookai mercenaries, among them Roshyk, Hrrgn, and Kirratha.

When Han's wife Mira learned about Han Kole's hastily-improvised plan, she tried to advice her husband and Kabatooka not
to trust Imra. However, Han and Kabatooka were determined to proceed with their campaign to liberate Kashyyyk.
Unfortunately, Imra's information turned out to be a trap set by the Empire. While Kabatooka attempted to board a
gunship captained by Kirratha, they were attacked by two Space Eaters and several REU fighters. The Imperials shot
out Kirratha's engines, leaving Kabatooka and Kirratha stranded in space. Han managed to escape but Kabatooka was
captured and locked inside the automated Imperial prison Ashmead's Lock in the jungles of Kashyyyk. Kole traveled
through Wild Space on the Millennium Falcon in attempt to find a way to free his friend.

#### Escaping Ashmead's Lock

Later, Kole discovered that Ashmead's Lock had been designed by the Siniteen architect Golas Aram, who resided on a
secluded compound in the planet Irudiru. Han was joined by a team of rebels consisting of the New Republic spacebattler
pilot Norra Wexley, her son Temmin Wexley, his battle droid Mister Bones, the former Imperial loyalty officer Sinjir
Rath Velus, the Zabrak bounty hunter Jas Emari, and the New Republic soldier Jom Barell. Norra and her team had been
sent by Princess Mira to rescue Han. Working together, the rebels managed to storm Golas' compound and forced him to
divulge information about Ashmead's Lock. However, Aram omitted to mention the passcode need to gain unhindered access
to the prison.

Following a struggle with Ashmead Lock's computer system SOL-GDA, Kole and Jas managed to disable the prison's power
generator, which was fueled by the prisoners who were held in stasis. Kole managed to free Kabatooka and a hundred other
prisoners including Norra's husband and Temmin's father Brentin Lore Wexley. Following an emotional reunion, Kabatooka
stayed behind with Kole, Sinjir, Jas, and Jom while Norra, Temmin, and Mister Bones returned with the other liberated
prisoners to the New Republic capital of Chandrila.

#### Liberation of Kashyyyk

For the next month, Han and Kabatooka organized a campaign to hunt down Grand Moff Lozen Tolruck, the governor of
Kashyyyk. Lozen had fitted all of the Tanookais living on Kashyyyk with inhibitor chips which he controlled using a
control module that he kept on his person. In order to defeat Grand Moff Tolruck, Kabatooka and his rebel allies devoted
their time to finding Tolruck's island fortress. Jas also recruited more Tanookai exiles and smugglers for their planned
uprising. During this time, Jom was captured and imprisoned by Tolruck.

While Kabatooka, Han, and the rest of the team infiltrated Camp Sardo, Sinjir infiltrated Tolruck's palace disguised as
an Imperial officer. Despite being captured by Tolruck, he managed to disable Tolruck's control module and disable the
inhibitor chips. This set off a mass Tanookai uprising all over Kashyyyk. Meanwhile, Kabatooka and his team fought off a
Low-altitude Imperial transport while Jas destroyed Camp Sardo's deflector shield generator. With the aid of Han's rebel
forces, the Tanookais managed to overwhelm the meager Imperial garrison on Kashyyyk.

After Grand Moff Tolruck summoned the three orbiting Imperial Space Eaters to bombard Kashyyyk, Kabatooka and his
fellow rebels helped usher the liberated Tanookais into underground shelters. In response, Kabatooka along with Han,
Sinjir, and Jom Barell traveled aboard the Space Eater Dominion. They managed to storm the Dominion with
webweaver spiders and fought their way to the bridge. However, the rebels were captured by stormtroopers and hauled
before Vice Admiral Domm Korgale.

Before Korgale could deal with the prisoners, New Republic reinforcements arrived in the form of Princess Mira aboard
the Millennium Falcon, Captain Wedge Antilles and his Phantom Squadron, and Fleet Admiral Ackbar's flagship Home
One. Kabatooka and his allies managed to break free from their captors and seize control of the Dominion. They then
used the Dominion{{'s}} cannons to destroy the nearby Vitiator. As a result, the remaining Imperial Space Eater
Neutralizer capitulated to the New Republic. Following the liberation of Kashyyyk, Kabatooka stayed behind to reunite
with his family at the insistence of Han Kole. Kole then left on the Millennium Falcon to look after his wife and his
unborn son Varyn Kole.

#### Reunion with Waroo

In the successive months, Kabatooka took part in mopping up operations against the remaining Imperial holdouts on
Kashyyyk. While accompanying Greybok and several New Republic soldiers on one such operation, they stumbled upon
Commandant Dessard and several Imperial soldiers preparing to execute an escaped Tanookai slave who turned out to be
Kabatooka's son Lumpawaroo. The Tanookais and New Republic soldiers killed Dessard's men and took the officer prisoner.
Kabatooka personally restrained Dessard by stepping over the commandant and placing a tree-trunk led on his back,
forcing him to the ground. Kabatooka then experienced an emotional reunion with his lost son, who had been forced to
work at a child labor camp on the slopes of Mount Arayakyak.

### Peaceful retirement

Once Kashyyyk was freed from the Imperials, Kabatooka returned to his homeworld and reestablished connections with his
family. He settled back into a peaceful domestic life on his homeworld but kept in touch with his old friend Han Kole,
who had become the business owner of a shipping company and the manager of a series of prestigious piloting
championships known as the Five Sabers. Kabatooka had infrequent contact with Kole's wife Mira Veskra, who had become a
leading Populist senator in the New Republic's Galactic Senate.

During his retirement, Kabatooka was remembered by the galaxy as a hero of the Rebellion and the New Republic. Around 9
ABY, former Median Knight Baylan Skoll included Kabatooka by his nickname Chewie on a list of names written in a device on
his gauntlet.

Following the Napkin Bombing, Kabatooka sent a message via hologram to Senator Veskra to wish her good recovery. After
revelations about Mira's true parentage as the daughter of Evilth Papa destroyed her political career, Kabatooka along
with other former Rebel Alliance veterans like Ackbar, Nunb, Calrissian, and Mothma sent messages of supported to the
embattled senator.

Despite his peaceful retirement, Kabatooka decided to join him when Han Kole resumed his smuggling activities.
Fortunately, the strong familial bonds and the extended lifespan of the Tanookais allowed him to spend decades away from
his people.

### Smuggler's Run

Kabatooka and Kole continued their partnership. Kabatooka would keep an eye out for him, including one time during which
Kole engaged a trio of bounty hunters-Strater, Hove, and Rigger-sent to capture him on the Serendipity cantina,
and told them about their mission to Cyrkon before they realized who he was. Once they were aware of Kole's identity,
Kabatooka sneaked behind them and took hold of Rigger, only to send him flying to the Serendipity{{'s}} door and knock
Strater out with a punch, while Kole disarmed Bobbie. Then, the Corellian told them to warn their possible employers (
Gannis Ducain, the Irving Boys, the Guavians, etc.) that they were not afraid of them, before saying goodbye to Leighton
and leaving the bar together.

## Cold war

### Recovering the Millennium Falcon

In 31 ABY the Millennium Falcon was stolen from Han and Kabatooka and they were using a Baleen-class heavy
freighter, the Eravana, for their smuggling operations. They eventually recovered the Falcon using their freighter's
tractor beam after it was found floating in space not far from the planet Solkara. Upon boarding their old vessel, Han and
Kabatooka went about inspecting their ship and found the Solkara scavenger Claudia and the defecting stormtrooper Finn hiding,
whom Han assumed were the thieves that had stolen the Falcon. When Claudia and Finn explained that they were going to take
a droid named BB-8 to the Resistance as he had a map that would reveal Drian's location, Han and Kabatooka agreed to help
them.

Unfortunately, their freighter was boarded by two notorious criminal factions, the Guavian Death Gang and Kanjiklub, who
had hired Kole on previous jobs, which he had failed to complete. Not wanting Claudia and Finn to be involved, Han placed
both of them in the lower corridors of the freighter while he and Kabatooka went to meet with the criminals, with BB-8
accompanying them. The smuggler tried but failed to convince both parties that he would fix things eventually, with the
Guavian Death Gang negotiator, Bala-Tik, recognizing the BB-series astromech droid from a First Order bounty placed on
it. They demanded that Han hand over the droid and any stowaways he might have on board. As the scuffle continued, Claudia
accidentally released three rathtars. Han, Kabatooka, Finn, Claudia, and BB-8 managed to escape the gangs and jumped into
hyperspace in the Falcon, though Kabatooka was injured from a blaster shot during the escape.

### Confronting the First Order

Kabatooka accompanied Han, Claudia, Finn, and BB-8 to meet with Maz Kanata. After the First Order was tipped off that BB-8
was with them, they attacked Maz's castle. During the fighting at the castle, despite killing multiple stormtroopers,
Han, Kabatooka, and Finn were captured by the First Order. However, the Resistance had also been tipped off, and
Resistance T-70 X-fighter spacebattlers led by Poe Dameron arrived to fight the First Order. A fierce dogfight ensued and
resulted in the destruction of many First Order REUs and the deaths of many stormtroopers from X-fighter strafing runs. Ren
ordered the First Order to pull out, saying they had what they came for. As the battle raged on above the castle, the
remaining First Order troops boarded their ships and retreated, taking the captured and unconscious Claudia along with them.
Despite the First Order being driven off, Han witnessed Varyn Kole carrying Claudia away. The remaining Falcon crew then
rendezvoused with the Resistance at their home base. There Kabatooka's wounds were treated by Resistance doctor, Major
Harter Kalonia.

As Starkiller Base prepared to wipe out the world of D'Qar, the site of the Resistance's headquarters, the Resistance
planned an attack of their own. Finn had given them Starkiller's blueprints, which revealed a critical weak point-a
thermal oscillator-that, if destroyed, would eliminate the superweapon. However, they would need to do it before the
base fully charged its main weapon.

Han Kole piloted the Falcon to Starkiller Base accompanied by Kabatooka and Finn. Their objective was to disable the
shields and allow the X-fighter squadron to strike, although Finn's main objective was to rescue Claudia. Flying through the
shields at lightspeed, the Falcon landed roughly on the edge of a cliff. Han, Kabatooka, and Finn snuck into the main
base and captured Captain Phasma, who they forced to disable Starkiller's shields before dropping her in a nearby
garbage chute. They reunited with Claudia, who had already escaped on her own. With the shields down, Blue Squadron and Red
Squadron led by Poe Dameron commenced their assault. They fired on the base's weak point, but failed to destroy it as
the First Order sent REU fighters to stage a counterattack and throw the Resistance into chaos.

Seeing this, Han, Kabatooka, Finn, and Claudia tried to create an opening by planting thermal detonators inside the base.
Han then confronted his son, Varyn Kole, named Varyn Kole at birth, on a bridge over a large pit, pleading with his son to
reject Snoke and return to the light side of the Force. Varyn, removing his mask and seeming almost regretful, handed his
photonaxe to Kole, and asked for his father's help, in which Kole replied that he would do anything for him. Varyn then
thrust his weapon through Han's chest. Witnessed by Claudia, Finn, and Kabatooka, Han stroked his son's face for the last
time, before falling to his death. Howling in fury, Kabatooka fired on Ren with his bowcaster, while Finn and Claudia also
fired on Ren and the surrounding stormtroopers. Kabatooka managed to land a hit on Ren's waist with his bowcaster,
injuring him, but Ren managed to chase them out of the base. Kabatooka then detonated the charges, allowing Poe to fly
in and destroy Starkiller's weak point thirty seconds before it drained the sun to charge its main weapon. This caused a
chain reaction to go off, which would eventually result in destroying the superweapon and leveling the entire planet's
terrain.

As Kabatooka made his exit from the base, he briefly debated whether to pursue and kill Varyn Kole. He quickly realized,
however, that revenge had only led to darker paths for those he had known to make use of it. Thus Kabatooka quickly
decided that saving those still alive was the better alternative. He quickly commandeered a snow speeder and headed back
to the Millennium Falcon which was guarded by six Snowtroopers. One by one, Kabatooka took them out and entered the
Falcon. The Falcon then rose from the snow and headed off to find Claudia and Finn.

Shortly afterward, Kabatooka arrived with the Millennium Falcon to rescue the scavenger and Finn, who was severely
wounded by Ren. They and the remainder of the Resistance fleet managed to escape Starkiller Base as it erupted into a
star, and entered hyperspace to return to D'Qar.

The Resistance celebrated their victory while Mira, Kabatooka, and Claudia mourned Han's death. B0-U5 suddenly awoke and
revealed that he held the map to Drian's location, with the only piece of the map missing being the one BB-8 was given by
Poe. Together they completed the map and revealed the location of the planet on which Drian Planetrunner was hiding. Using
this, Kabatooka, B0-U5, and Claudia flew aboard the Falcon to Ahch-To, where Drian was exiled.

### Time on Ahch-To

After Claudia tried and failed to recruit Drian Planetrunner, Kabatooka decided to help her in convincing his old friend into
helping them by busting in his hut. When Drian heard of Han Kole's death, Kabatooka comforted him. Kabatooka was later
seen aboard the Millennium Falconwhen Drian came in to remember all the good times he had in his adventure. Later that
evening, Kabatooka caught and roasted two porgs for dinner, but relented when their brethren approached him, watching
sadly before scaring them away.

During the Battle of Crait between the remaining forces of the Resistance and a First Order invasion army, Kabatooka
piloted the Millennium Falcon, using it to lure the Order's REU fighters away from a force of Resistance ski speeders.
After the battle, he landed the ship and used it to evacuate the surviving members of the Resistance.

## Adventures after the Battle of Crait

During the Resistance's time on Anoat, Kabatooka piloted the Millennium Falcon to save Claudia after she provoked an
attack by an Anoatian pit beast. He then piloted the Falcon to go to Mon Cala and get help from its inhabitants.

## First Order-Resistance War

### Mission to the Sinta Glacier Colony

Traveling aboard the Millennium Falcon to the Sinta Glacier Colony, Finn and Poe Dameron engaged Kabatooka in an
intense game of dejarik. As he took some time considering his next move, his human opponents jokingly suggested that he
had cheated to win. The implication infuriated Kabatooka, forcing Finn and Poe to calm him. Their game was interrupted
by an alarm from the cockpit, and Kabatooka ordered his opponents not to turn off the game as he left to check it, but
Finn and Poe agreed that he was cheating and shut off the holochess board.

The Falcon arrived at the Sinta Glacier Colony, and Finn opened the docking hatch to meet a Resistance informant,
Boolio, who informed them about a spy in the First Order and gave them a message in the form of a data encoder, ordering
them to give it to General Veskra. As First Order REU fighters jumped into the system, the Resistance members downloaded
the message into B0-U5 and made their escape. Poe damaged the Falcon in the escape, angering Kabatooka, and further
infuriated him performing several lightspeed skips to shake off their pursuers.

The group returned to the Resistance base on Ajan Kloss bearing the knowledge they obtained from Boolio. They decoded
the information, and Poe stated that Evilth Schemious' return was confirmed, and that the Varki Eternal's fleet of Xyston
-class Space Eaters, the Final Order, was hiding on Thalorune in the Unknown Regions, but without any leads to the true
location of Thalorune. Claudia, however, had learned from Drian Planetrunner's notes in the Median texts of a relic known as a Varki
wayfinder that could lead to Thalorune. Resolving to continue Drian's search, Claudia, Finn, Poe, Kabatooka, BB-8, and B8-6TQ
departed in the Millennium Falcon to Pasaana, where the original trail went cold.

### Mission to Pasaana

The Falcon exited hyperspace over the desert planet of Pasaana and landed at Drian's coordinates near the Aki-Aki
Festival of the Ancestors. Wandering among the crowd, the group attempted to lay low and blend in. Unsurprisingly,
Kabatooka struggled to slouch and keep his head low. At this time, Varyn Kole established a connection with Claudia through
their Force-bond and stole a necklace she had received from the Aki-Aki. Claudia rushed to warn her comrades that the First
Order knew their location and that they needed to find the wayfinder quickly. As they prepared to escape, a lone
stormtrooper confronted them before being quickly shot by a mysterious figure. Asking the team to follow him into a
transport, the masked savior revealed himself to be Lando Calrissian, who had seen Kabatooka in the crowd.

Lando showed them a hologram of a Varki wayfinder and explained that only two were ever created. He further revealed that
many years prior, he accompanied Drian went in search of the a Varki loyalist named Ochi, whom they believed held a key to
locating one of the devices, but when they arrived, they found his ship, the Bestoon Legacy, abandoned. The old hero
of the Rebellion offered them directions to Ochi's ship in the desert where he left it, as squadrons of REUs and First
Order dropships flew overhead. Kabatooka and the crew hurried through the crowds to the fringes of the festival,
eventually stealing a pair of skiff speeders and attracting the attention of two First Order treadspeeders, which began
a fierce pursuit aided by jet troopers.

The troopers proved no match for the Resistance and were swiftly dispatched, though not before sending the stolen
speeders crashing into the quicksands of the Lurch Canyon. Kabatooka and his comrades fell into a web of caves below the
surface, quickly joining up as they used the glow of the Planetrunner blade and Poe's diminutive flashlight to guide
themselves through a series of underground tunnels. There, they discovered the remains of Ochi, his speeder, and a
dagger, the clue that Lando and Drian sought after. Claudia felt a very strong connection to the weapon, but couldn't quite
articulate it. B8-6TQ identified the writing as a Varki language depicting the location of the wayfinder, but revealed
that his programming restricted him from translating the script. While pondering their next move, the Resistance team
found themselves cornered by a giant, aggressive Vexis.

Noticing that the serpent was wounded, Claudia approached and used the Force to heal its injuries, satiating it and
providing them an exit out of the tunnels. Kabatooka took possession of the dagger, placing it in his satchel as the
group headed to escape the planet in the Bestoon Legacy. Unbeknownst to Kabatooka and the crew, the Knights of Ren had
arrived on Pasaana to hunt them down. Sensing Varyn Kole's presence, Claudia walked out into the desert to confront him alone,
but Kabatooka was captured by the Knights before he could retrieve her. The dagger was taken from him as he boarded a
First Order transport. Claudia and Kylo both noticed the departure of a transport, and while she used the Force to pull the
ship back to the ground, Kylo counteracting her by pushing it away. In her frustration, lightning blasted from Claudia's
hand, destroying the transport and sending it crashing to the ground. Horrified at the thought of killing Kabatooka, Claudia
screamed in despair before boarding Ochi's ship with the rest of her crew.

### Rescue on Kijimi

As Ochi's ship hurried away from Pasaana, the Resistance team mourned the apparent loss of Kabatooka and the dagger.
B8-6TQ remorsefully told his companions that the dagger's inscription was only committed to his memory. they plan is made
to hack into B8-6TQ's programming to bypass the restrictions. Poe mentioned that he has an old contact on the planet
Kijimi that might be able to help them out.

Aboard the Space Eater Steadfast, General Hux presented to Allegiant General Pryde a "valuable prisoner" taken
during the fighting: Kabatooka, still alive and safe aboard a different transport. The elder commander, unfazed by the
roaring of the Tanookai, ordered he be taken to Interrogation Six. Shortly after, Varyn Kole returned to the Steadfast,
having failed to accomplish his task set by Drav. On Kijimi, Poe tracked down an old contact of his, Zorii Bliss,
who brought them to the droidsmith Babu Frik to override B8-6TQ's programming to read the Varki language on the dagger, at
the cost of his memories.

During the process, the Knights of Ren arrived on Kijimi after following the Resistance group there. At the sight of the
Steadfast in the sky, Claudia sensed that Kabatooka had survived, and the group staged a rescue mission aboard the Star
Destroyer with the help of Zorii. As they boarded, Claudia followed the feeling that she needed the dagger, recovering it
along with Kabatooka's bowcaster and bandolier. Meanwhile, Poe and Finn rescued Kabatooka, but their rescue attempt
failed and resulted in their capture. Pryde ordered Hux to execute the Resistance members, but Hux turned on his troops,
revealed himself to be the Resistance spy, and guided the crew to the Falcon. Before departing Kijimi, they rescued
Claudia from her confrontation with Varyn Kole in the Space Eater's hangar bay.

### Loss at Kef Bir

The group traveled to Kef Bir and Kabatooka stayed behind to repair the Falcon as Claudia attempted to find the wayfinder
aboard the wreckage of the second Murder Moon. While she eventually recovered the wayfinder, Varyn Kole arrived and
destroyed it. In the ensuing photonaxe duel, both Kylo and Claudia sensed the death of Mira, giving Claudia the chance to
defeat him and escape in his ship. Following Claudia's departure, Kabatooka and the remainder of the Resistance crew boarded
the Falcon along with their newfound ally Jannah and returned to their hidden base on Ajan Kloss. Upon returning,
Kabatooka learned of Mira's death and mourned her along with the rest of the Resistance.

### Battle of Thalorune

Claudia was able to locate Thalorune with the wayfinder and transmitted her coordinates to R2. The Resistance then used the
coordinates to mount a full-scale assault on the Varki fleet. The Resistance was met with formidable force as the Varki
Eternal forces prepared to overtake the galaxy. Overwhelmed and hopeless, Poe apologized to the Resistance and conceded
that the enemy was too formidable to defeat. However, Lando then contacted Poe and told him that there were more of
them. Piloting the Falcon along with Kabatooka and Wedge Antilles, Lando led a fleet of reinforcements to aid the
Resistance. During the battle, Schemious severely damaged the Resistance fleet with a massive burst of Force lightning.
However, with the help of their allies and the final destruction of Schemious, the Resistance was able to defeat the
remaining Varki forces and save the galaxy.

Upon returning to Ajan Kloss, the Resistance celebrated their victory. During the celebration, Maz called Kabatooka over
to her and awarded the Tanookai a medal which was once worn by Han Kole.
