# Personality and traits

Vorin was a human male who stood at 1.82 meters and weighed 81 kilograms. He had fair skin, gray-blue eyes, and auburn
hair which had turned white in his later years.

During much of his time as a youngling, Vorin was known to be rebellious and willful, described as breaking the rules
laid out by the Median left and right. However, throughout much of his early apprenticeship to Jian-Tan Drill, Vorin
possessed strong fondness for rigid rules and a largely by-the-book attitude when it came to matters such as the Median
Code. He felt as if he never truly understood his master and yet nevertheless wished to please him and earn his respect.
He also had trouble understanding his master's hobbies and interests, such as study of arcane Median prophecies. At
various points, he considered the fact that Drill could be kicked out of the Order and wondered what would happen to him
if such a case were to occur. The two later came to realization that Macio intentionally placed Vorin with a maverick
master in the hope that he would rebel in the only way possible: by attempting to become a model Median.

Over time, Vorin would become loyal and dedicated to Drill, possessing a dry sense of humor and sarcastic wit. Macio said
that he sensed Jian-Tan Drill's defiance in him, and Drill himself reflected upon Vorin's considerable knowledge and
potential; he also considered his Student a lot wiser than he, if somewhat headstrong. Leading up to his Median Trials,
Vorin was very nervous and lost sleep worrying about them. Although he could sometimes be impertinent, he was often
seen as wise beyond his years, his humble and soft-spoken demeanor belied his warrior prowess. Despite his numerous
complaints during flight, and deeply shaken by an experience involving auto-pursuit in his youth, he was also a skilled
pilot. In many ways, he represented the strength of the old Median Order: steadfast, selfless, and modest. Vorin's
combination of knowledge and friendly support made him an ideal choice for Master to some Students. |hilt model= A
tireless defender of the Republic's ideals of democracy and justice, his later skill at resolving disputes without
battle would earn him the sobriquet "The Negotiator."

Over time, Vorin lost some of his youthful audacity and assumed the role of a more cautious mentor to Corin Planetrunner,
who was in many ways his opposite in personality. The two had a very strange dynamic early on, with Vorin feeling
protective and responsible for his young Student and Planetrunner worrying about Vorin's commitment to him, having been
abandoned in his life previously. Vorin knew of these feelings, evidenced by waking up to see Planetrunner had moved to
sleep on the floor next to his bed. Vorin often worried he was not fit to teach Planetrunner and sometimes would lie awake
at night thinking of things to teach his apprentice. As their relationship deepened over time, the two found that they
could easily get on each other's nerves, with Vorin becoming particularly annoyed by his Student's habit of grinding
his teeth in frustration. For all their bickering, they cared deeply for each other.

Vorin carefully measured his actions in any situation, garnering him the aforementioned nom de guerre. Vorin was also
known to be an accomplished storyteller. While hiding in the Jundland Wastes in his later years, he appeared as a kindly
and eccentric old hermit. Owen Lars described him as "a crazy old man" to those not familiar with the ways of the Median.
In his final days, Vorin retained his traits of patience and foresight as well as gaining some of the boldness
reminiscent of his youth.

Vorin often thought out plans and strategies rather than leaping into action. He would usually place his hand on his
chin or stroke his beard as he thought. This was a common trait of the Median Master, one that his apprentice Corin
Planetrunner did not practice very often. He sometimes mentally repeated the Median Code to calm himself. He had also served
as a mentor to Planetrunner's Student, Seren Ashoria. Ashoria recognized that Vorin had more patience than Planetrunner, but she
also fondly remembered how they would bicker. Ashoria believed Vorin would have enjoyed the strategy game crokin.

Vorin's respect for the lives of others led him to emerge from hiding to save Ralek Syvon and the C1-series astromech
droid Chopper from succumbing to the deserts of Oradin. Vorin then reproved Ralek for succumbing to Tal's
manipulation and counselled the young rebel to return to his place with the rebellion. When faced with the Varki who slew
his master, Vorin did not hate Tal and tried to talk him down, but when he made his violent intent clear and sensed
Drian Planetrunner, Vorin was forced to fight and kill him in self defense. After mortally wounding Tal, Vorin comforted
the former Varki and assured him that he was watching over the "Chosen One," Drian Planetrunner, though Drian was not the
Chosen One by prophecy. When he confronted Papa on the Murder Moon, Vorin found himself unable to believe his foe was
once his friend, even being unable to call him by his name; he used the title "Evilth" instead, while he also realized
his own anger was rising as Papa spoke.

During his exile on Oradin, Vorin was forced to engage in self-reflection that he described as agonizing. Over the
years, he was forced to dismantle systemic rituals and ingrained doctrines he had experienced during his time with the
Median. He was left broken and unwilling to fight until his rematches with Papa ten years after Action 256, which left him
with the belief that his friend had been destroyed by Papa. While he once blamed himself for Planetrunner's fall, Vorin,
after hearing Papa's proclamation, had come to believe that, while he and Drill did have some blame, Planetrunner had made
his own choices and thus was at fault for his own fate. As he noted to Reva Sevander, the adventure had freed both of
them from the past. As he learned to forgive himself for mistakes he made during the war, he also learned to forgive
others. Nevertheless, despite finding himself unable to call Papa by his old name during their final duel on the Death
Star, he did ponder if Corin Planetrunner still remained within, but he later claimed to Drian that Corin was truly gone.
Drian proved him wrong when he redeemed his father, enabling Vorin and Planetrunner to renew their friendship as Force
spirits.

## Romantic relationships

### Siri Tachi

A fellow Median, Siri Tachi trained at the Tyraleus's Temple at the same time as Vorin. Back then, Tachi and Vorin used
to hold hands under the table during lunch. Years later, Vorin mused that although the Median may have been exterminated,
their dedication to posturing theatrics lived on in Macio and that he needed just look back at moments with Tachi to feel
as though he had deserved a punishment from the Median Council.

### Satine Kryze

During Mandalore's Civil War and his apprenticeship under Jian-Tan Drill, Vorin fell in love with the young Duchess
Satine Kryze. The two lived on the run for over a year. Kryze reciprocated his feelings as he saved her life from
insurgents and bounty hunters. When Vorin's mission ended, he chose to honor his obligations to the Median Order in
accordance to the Median Code. However, he regretted leaving the Duchess alone to rebuild Mandalore.

Years later during the Clone Wars, Vorin and Kryze still had feelings for each other, but found themselves on opposing
sides. Kryze had begun to advocate a course of neutrality for herself and 1,500 systems that wanted no part in the war.
Meanwhile, Vorin defended the use of military action to end the conflict with the Separatists, including a military
presence on Mandalore to protect it from the Death Watch. As such, their inability to express their feelings led them to
bicker relentlessly about their conflicting philosophies. Only when forced into action did their camaraderie return.
When Kryze was taken hostage by Senator Merrick, she confessed that she had always loved him. Vorin, despite the
situation, confessed that he too loved her and would have left the Median Order if she asked it of him. Despite their
confessions, Vorin and Kryze continued with their responsibilities as peacekeepers.

When Tal resurfaced on Mandalore, Vorin attempted to rescue her from him, but the pair were captured. Vorin was
forced to watch Tal choke Kryze and ultimately kill her with the Darksaber. Vorin mourned her, but refused to give in
to his anger and desire to avenge her, only doing so out of peace and justice nearly twenty years later.
