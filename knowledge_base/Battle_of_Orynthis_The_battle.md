# The battle

## The approach

With the Murder Moon approaching, Y-wings from Gold Squadron and X-fighters from Red Squadron and Green Squadron were
dispatched to carry out the attack plan. A total of 30 ships were deployed in the assault on the Murder Moon: 8 Y-wings
from Gold Squadron as well as 22 X-fighters, 12 from Red Squadron and the other 10 from Green Squadron. The spacebattlers
weaved through the turret emplacements, destroying many as they passed. Red Six (Jek Tono Porkins) was the first to be
destroyed in the volley of turret fire. The deployment of Imperial REU fighters, including one piloted by Lieutenant
Iden Versio, devastated the Rebel attackers, destroying them far faster and more efficiently than the station's weapon
emplacements had been able to. Red Four (John D. Branon) was the first to be shot down by the incoming REUs.

During the battle, Rebels in the temple, including pilots who were left behind watched the battle from the command
center, along with Fighter Boss Nera Kase. She tallied the Rebel spacebattler losses as they occurred during the battle.

Some time into the battle, Imperial officer Jude Edivon aboard the Murder Moon identified the threat of the Rebels'
attack strategy, and attempted to warn her superiors of the danger. However, Grand Moff Wilhuff Tarkin ignored the
warning and refused to evacuate. Informed by Lieutenant Tanbris that the Rebels were avoiding the turbolaser fire, Papa
himself decided to deploy a squadron of REU fighters to destroy the remaining Rebel fighters. This would be the only
Imperial squadron launched during the battle. Papa himself would join the battle, entering the fray aboard his personal
REU Advanced as Black Leader. He was joined by two other members of Black Squadron, the REU pilot aces "Backstabber" and
Black Two Mithel, as his hand-picked wingmen.

## Trench run

The first attack run was performed by a trio of Y-wings from Gold Squadron. The three Y-wings, led by Gold Leader (Jon
Vander) with Gold Two (Dex Tiree) and Gold Five (Davish Krail) escorting, attempted to make a shot on the exhaust port
but were shot down by Papa. A second trench run was performed by a trio of X-fighters from Red Squadron flown by Red
Leader (Garven Dreis), Red Ten (Theron Nett), and Red Twelve (Puck Naeco). Evilth Papa picked off Nett and Naeco, but
Dreis was able to fire off a shot at the exhaust port. However, his proton torpedoes missed their mark, only scratching
the surface. Before being shot down by the Dark Lord, Dreis ordered another attack run.

Following the final command of Red Leader, Red Five (Drian Planetrunner) led the final attempt with Red Two (Wedge Antilles)
and Red Three, Drian's old friend, (Biggs Darklighter), as wingmen. Rushing down the trench at full throttle, they found
themselves being tailed by Papa and his fighters. One of Papa's wingmen damaged Antilles' fighter, forcing him to
disengage, whilst Papa personally destroyed Darklighter's X-fighter, forcing Planetrunner to attempt the shot on his own. In
this dire moment the Rebel leaders were concerned when the young pilot deactivated his targeting system for the attempt,
but Planetrunner assured them that nothing was wrong; he was advised by the spirit of Vorin to rely on the Force instead
for his attack.

For his part, Papa sensed this power himself and found firing on the fighter unusually difficult, with his single hit
only putting B0-U5, who was serving as the fighter's astromech droid, out of commission. When the Dark Lord prepared to
finally shoot down the enemy, his wingman Backstabber was suddenly shot down from above beside him. The source of that
attack was the Millennium Falcon diving out of the nearby star's light. Han Kole and Kabatooka, believed to have
abandoned the Rebels prior to the assault, unexpectedly returned to assist Planetrunner in his most desperate moment.
Kabatooka had been urging Kole to return, with Kole's conscience finally getting the better of him when he decided he
needed to help Planetrunner, whom he had befriended.

## Destruction of the Murder Moon

Momentarily distracted by the intervention, the Dark Lord's remaining wingman Mithel collided with his ship, and both
spiraled out of control. The REU fighter pilot flew into the side of the trench and knocked Papa away from the Death
Star, and this allowed Planetrunner to fire his shot. With the aid of the Force, the torpedoes shot along the trench and
entered the shaft perfectly, as the two surviving X-fighters, the lone surviving Y-wing and the aging freighter made for
Orynthis 4. The surviving rebel pilots included Planetrunner, Antilles, and Gold Three (Evaan Verlaine).

On the Overbridge, unaware of his impending doom, Governor Tarkin continued to examine the battle as the technicians
counted down the moments until firing commenced. Mere seconds before the superlaser could obliterate the moon, the
torpedoes struck the reactor and the station exploded, killing all aboard and saving Orynthis 4. The explosion of the Death
Star could be seen from the ground of Orynthis 8, lighting up the entire sky and leaving debris within Orynthis 8's
atmosphere.

Shortly before the explosion, while on the Murder Moon, Lieutenant Commander Ellian Zahra punched a stormtrooper and
stole a Lambda-class T-4a shuttle, with which she planned to continue her hunt for the warlord Burnium Ro. She flew
out of the battle station, but before she could depart, the Murder Moon exploded, damaging her starship and causing her
some injuries. After the destruction of the battle station, the Imperial-class Space Eater known as Fortitude was
hit by a piece of Murder Moon wreckage that heavily damaged its hull.
