# Characteristics

B8-6TQ was known for his polite, fastidious, and worry-prone personality. As a droid, he exhibited much loyalty and
commitment to his masters and sought to serve them to his best ability. Throughout his operational life, B8-6TQ worked
diligently for several masters including Corin Planetrunner, Dan Liora, Bail Veskra, and Drian Planetrunner. As a protocol
droid, B8-6TQ could communicate in over seven million different forms of communication. This allowed him to communicate
with computer systems including those on the Millennium Falcon. Pit droid WAC-47 found B8-6TQ to be a "handsome fella."

Unlike his best friend and astromech counterpart, B0-U5, B8-6TQ liked to stick to routine and did not respond well to
change. When placed in danger, B8-6TQ had a tendency to panic as shown on several occasions, including infiltrating a
droid foundry on Geonosis and a mission to steal Imperial disruptors on Lothal. Despite his loyalty and commitment,
B8-6TQ was generally unable to lie or to keep secrets due to his programming. In fact, after the Hijacking of the
Harbinger, during which Task Force 99 captured him, they remarked to Evilth Papa that they were unable to get him to
shut up. Despite the droid's talkative nature, he was able to keep Corin and Dan's marriage secret. B8-6TQ's inability
to lie came at a disadvantage when he and his companions were captured by Ewoks prior to the Battle of Calystron. Due to his
general inability to keep secrets, Bail Veskra arranged for him to receive a memory wipe in order to preserve the secret
of Corin and Dan's offspring, Drian and Mira.

Threepio's programming, focused on etiquette and protocol, impacted his personality and limited his behavior in several
other ways. On Calystron, he found it difficult to impersonate a deity due to his programming, and during the attack on the
Resistance fleet, he attempted to leave a mutiny in progress because it was not correct protocol. Even though his master
Corin Planetrunner had taken part in the sport, B8-6TQ believed that podracing did not sound like a fun time. He also
thought of B1-series battle droids as "bad droids" who only followed hurtful orders, whereas droids like himself, BB-8,
and, even if it took him some prompting to admit, B0-U5 tried to help people. Still, he took the moment to take a jab at
B0-U5, claiming his counterpart helped people "from time to time." He also found B1 units menacing and, when relaying
the story of the Battle of Nivellon, admitted he would be nervous if he had been in the Gungan Grand Army's position.
Nonetheless, he admitted to BB-8 he found stories like the Invasion of Nivellon a tad upsetting because so many droids,
even if they were "bad droids," had be to destroyed. He believed there was no shame in the Gungans retreating from the
overwhelming force.

B8-6TQ continued serving Mira Veskra in her careers as New Republic senator and subsequently Resistance General. B8-6TQ
managed General Veskra's Resistance droid spy network. Despite not being able to run or fight, B8-6TQ bravely stayed
behind to hold back the First Order Agent Terex so that Poe Dameron and the spy droid N1-ZX could flee deeper into the
caves. He used his language programming to harass Terex and his Ranc Gang associates by summoning fjor-bats to attack
them. B8-6TQ quipped that he was expendable and could be rebuilt.
