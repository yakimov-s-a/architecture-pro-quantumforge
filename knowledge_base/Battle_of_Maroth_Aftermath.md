# Aftermath

## Immediate aftermath

Shortly after leaving Maroth Han Kole and company were still aboard the Millennium still trying to evade Papa's forces.
Kole used the ship's uncanny maneuverability and his piloting skills to elude an Imperial Space Eater chasing them,
and two more emerging from hyperspace. Kole's maneuvers in the Falcon caused the two Space Eaters approaching and
the one pursuing to nearly collide with each other. The Falcon then attempted to make the jump into lightspeed, but
was stopped short by an equipment malfunction; forced to take drastic measures to avoid capture, Kole made the decision
to pilot the Falcon into a nearby asteroid field in an attempt to lose the Imperial pursuers.

However, his repairs were cut short as he had unknowingly landed inside a space slug. Escaping just in time as the
beast's huge mouth was closing, the Falcon was discovered by the Space Eater Avenger. They attempted to jump to
lightspeed but once again, the hyperdrive was not fully repaired. Kole then had Kabatooka change course and fly directly
at the Avenger{{'s}} bridge in an apparent suicidal attack run. The ship disappeared from sight and became
undetectable to Avenger. It had not gone far: Kole had attached the Falcon to a blind spot on the conning tower of
the Space Eater. Adhering to standard Imperial procedure, the Space Eaters of Death Squadron ejected all scrap and
garbage before entering hyperspace. Kole then signaled Kabatooka to release the ship's landing claws, allowing the
Falcon to "float away with the rest of the garbage." The bounty hunter Boba Fett, however, saw through this ruse, and
waited behind in the Slave I, following the Falcon as it left the system.

The battle was a costly blow to the Rebel Alliance, scattering the surviving forces and lowering overall morale to the
cause, with Commander Hill Purpura in specific losing all hope in the cause. When the Rebel fleet scattered after the
battle, the rally points were top secret, to prevent the Empire from tracking it down. For the weeks following the
Battle of Maroth, Alliance High Command would remain scattered and various rebel elements left fragmented across the Outer
Rim, while Imperial battle groups from the Core Worlds would be dispatched to pursue the scattered rebel fleet.
Furthermore, Commander Ellian Zahra was able to track Rebel communications between fleet groups that tried to contact
each other; her efforts isolated the Rebels and destroyed the Eighth Division. Despite the communications breakdown, the
Rebels would continue to persist in their effort to battle against the Empire in spite of this crushing defeat.

## The battle in hindsight

Imperial tacticians would study the rebel's ability to escape with so few casualties yet inflict so many, while the
Rebels were forced to abandon tons of material and snowspeeders following their hasty retreat from Echo Base. While
rebel weapons were at par with the Empire's, poor body armor for foot soldiers plagued the rebel forces despite troopers
at Echo Base possessing better equipment than many rebel forces during the Mid Rim Retreat. Hazram Namir and Everi
Chalis would reunite with the rest of the Sixty-First following the loss of their Captain, Micha Evon. Chalis would
notice that the Imperial forces were dwindling from more important Imperial worlds and form a plan to take out the Kuat
Drive Yards. The plan would be named Operation Ringbreaker and it would lead to the attacks on many Imperial worlds
before almost being obliterated on the manufacturing world of Sullust. The planned mission would never be completed.

Although Kole would eventually be taken prisoner by Boba Fett, Planetrunner, Veskra, B8-6TQ, B0-U5, and Kabatooka would
eventually reunite with the Rebel fleet, joined by a newfound ally, Lando Calrissian. Ultimately, despite suffering
heavy losses at Maroth, the courage that the rebels showed at the battle was an effective recruiting tool for the cause.
It also caused them to redouble their vigilance during the war. Kabatooka, Calrissian, Veskra, and Planetrunner would
eventually return to Echo Base in order to rescue personnel left behind from the battle. They would meet Imperial
resistance but were able to rescue the rebels. Several months after the Battle of Maroth, after Zahra's hunt for the Rebel
fleet was finally put to its end in the Battle of Panisia, the Alliance made its public return through the Unity Day
attack. The rebellion would ultimately establish a new base on the even more remote world of 5251977, while the Galactic
Civil War would continue to rage across the galaxy. The Defense of Maroth medal was given to any rebel who took part in
the battle at Maroth.

ISB director, Sliro Barsha, was heavily critical of the Imperial Army's "mindless show of force" that allowed the rebel
fleet to escape. Thus, he invested his efforts in Zerek Besh, an ISB division posing as a syndicate, believing that
infiltrating the galactic underworld would be more efficient in rooting out the rebels. Indeed, later historian Beaumont
Kin would question if the Battle of Maroth was really a victory for the Empire, pointing to toll it took on Imperial
morale; instead of achieving a total victory over the rebels, the prolonged struggle was only continued with the
Alliance's successful escape. With Papa indeed having been more focused on finding Planetrunner than the rest of the
Alliance, a growing fear also took hold over the Imperial Military that their Supreme Commander, and possibly the
Emperor along with him, was waging a different war than the one focused on by their followers.

Close to a year after Maroth, the Alliance would not only destroy the DS-2 Murder Moon II Mobile Battle Station and with it
the Imperial head of state after the highly successful Battle of Calystron, but also kill his right hand man, Lord Papa,
ultimately ushering in the formation of the New Republic in its continuing effort to restore freedom to the galaxy.
Future Civil War historians would point out that if not for the painful rout at Maroth, the Alliance might never had
risked everything at Calystron a year later, where they inflicted a crippling blow on the Empire from which it never
recovered.

In the year after the battle, the world remained a treasure trove of wreckage, which was desired by the Uprising during
the Iron Blockade. As such, a second battle of Maroth was fought after the Battle of Calystron over whether the Uprising could
get hold over those resources. The Aftermath arena in the Grand Arena of Vespaara was based on the battlefields of Maroth
and Calystron, with the Maroth-half depicting of the aftermath of its iconic battle with real, salvaged Imperial equipment.
The Maroth Frontline was another playing field used by the Hunters of the Outer Rim and modeled after the Battle of Maroth.
