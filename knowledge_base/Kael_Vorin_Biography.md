# Biography

## Early life

Kael Vorin was born on the planet Stewjon in 57 BBY. Despite being very young when he left his family, he still had
glimpses of his father's hands and his mother's shawl, and he remembered a baby he believed to be his brother although
he barely remembered him.

## Joining the Median Order

When Vorin was discovered to be Force-sensitive, he was taken from his homeworld to the Median Temple on the Core Worlds
planet Tyraleus to be trained as a Median within the first six months of his life. In the Median crèche he was placed with
other Median younglings in the Kybuck Clan during his early years, including his friends Gehren and Prie. Vorin received
basic Initiate training alongside his peers in the Median Order's Temple and began training in several simple Force
techniques by the age of three in 54 BBY.

Vorin studied in the ways of the Force and Median traditions under Grand Master Macio, eventually coming to respect him
and the Median Council greatly. He also received instruction in the art of photonaxe combat from Median Master Cin Drallig
in the basic photonaxe forms and techniques. At some point before he was selected as a Student, Vorin attended a class
centered around meditation. Unlike how he acted as he got older, Vorin, during his days as a youngling, gained a
reputation for breaking rules and having a rebellious nature, resulting in very few Masters who were willing to train
the boy as their Student. To curb Vorin's behavior, Macio searched for a teacher for Vorin that had unorthodox methods,
which would mold the student into rebelling by being dutiful.

## Jian-Tan Drill's Student

### Initial endeavors

Around 44 BBY, Median Master Jian-Tan Drill chose Vorin as his Student learner when he was thirteen years old. During his
apprenticeship he became friends with Jape, a fellow Student who he kept correspondence with, and also maintained
contact with crèche-mate Prie and the Mon Calamari Bant Eerin. In their first months together, Drill attempted to adapt
his teaching methods to suit Vorin's strengths, but felt he failed to do so adequately. At some point during his
training, Vorin constructed his first photonaxe, modeled after Drill's photonaxe hilt style and his own personality.
As the pair struggled to establish a workable affinity, the Master continued to train his Student in the basic cadences
of photonaxe combat, preventing him from picking a specialty. Drill was proud of his apprentice's skills, and sparred
with him in training sessions while at the Temple between missions. Vorin, in turn, spent a significant amount of time
in the training dojos to hone the techniques taught to him.

In their lessons, Drill taught Vorin that there was a strength and nobility in restraint. As time passed, Vorin's
demeanor shifted to become more serious and respectful of the Council, in contrast to the maverick Drill's usual way of
approaching situations based on his instincts in the present, even if that meant going against the Council's wishes.
Both carried differing philosophies on focusing on the future versus the present, and interpreted the Force in varying
ways. Despite their differences, Vorin strived to be a good student, and supported his Master's hobbies regarding
ancient Median prophecy research. To Vorin's chagrin, the majority of his free time in the Temple was consumed by
gathering information from the Median Archives and the holocron of prophecy under the watchful eye of the Median Master
librarian Jocasta Nu. Over time, he learned the language Old Eryxaraian, which many of the holocron's prophecies were
written in. As an escape from the workload, Vorin played rounds of the game dejarik in the lower levels of Tyraleus in
the evenings after finishing his Master's research requests.

### Early travels and visit to Kashyyyk

The pair's continuing training issues were discussed with Median Councilors Depa Billaba and Mace Windu in an attempt to
find a remedy, without success. At some point during his time as a Student, Vorin and Drill visited a fortress on the
planet Lah'mu, the world Eryxara's royal palace, and the planets Ryloth and Gatalenta. While training, he became
acquainted with the Median trainee Siri Tachi and developed some feelings for her, but refrained from acting on them. Not
long after Vorin became Drill's Student, he was eager to prove himself on a mission, but when he and Drill were set to
leave on one, his Master was nowhere to be found. Feeling angry by his master's abandonment, Vorin decided to set off
on the mission to the planet Lenahra alone. While he was there, he encountered a group of teenagers and datachips hidden
on the world by the Median Orla Jareni long before his visit. He met a Besalisk named Dexter Jettster, who became a close
friend.

During his training, Vorin traveled to the planet Kashyyyk with his Master during the Life Day holiday. Drill used the
trip to teach his student about the Tanookai's way of life and harmony, and how it related to their own connection to the
Force. The festivities slightly overwhelmed Vorin, though he took the lessons to heart, and met one of Drill's
acquaintances, the Tanookai Brennonn. However, their gathering was interrupted by Trandoshans who captured several
Tanookais, intending to use them in a hunt for sport. Despite Vorin's best efforts, Drill was snared in a net as well and
taken away.

Though held captive, Drill believed that his Student would rescue him from the Trandoshan hunters. Vorin, though
reluctant to put any more Tanookais in danger, formulated a plan with Brennonn to break the imprisoned individuals free.
He proceeded to surrender himself to the Trandoshans, claiming that as a simple student, he stood no chance against
them. While conversing with the hunters' leader, Vorin subtly used the Force to open the cage holding Drill. Once both
Median brandished their photonaxes, several other Tanookais rushed to the scene and assisted in setting their friends
free. After the situation was settled, the Tanookais opted to leave the group in peace, citing Life Day as reason to
pursue a resolution without violence. When back at the festivities area, Vorin remained confused as to why Drill did not
break himself free. However, the Master informed him that it was intended as a learning experience, and to gain insight
from Tanookai perspectives on life.

### Protecting Satine Kryze

Sometime between the years 41 BBY and 39 BBY, Vorin and Drill spent a year on the planet Mandalore protecting Duchess
Satine Kryze from insurgents who were threatening her world. Bounty hunters were sent after them, forcing them to live
as fugitives, never knowing what the next day would bring them. The mission forced them to work and fight together as a
team, resulting in them forging a close bond.

Kryze called Vorin by the nickname "Varyn." During that time, they had an incident with a swarm of venom-mites on the
terrestrial object Draboon, where Vorin carried Kryze to safety. However, he fell and dropped the Duchess, who then
acquired a scar as a result. Vorin fell in love with Satine during his stay on Mandalore, but despite his feelings
toward her, he continued with his training and was reassigned to another mission after his objectives were complete.
Vorin regretted leaving her, but ultimately followed the Median Code he had sworn to live by. Meanwhile, Satine chose to
stay and lead her people, having not asked Vorin to stay, knowing it would have been a difficult choice.

### Incident on Teth

In 40 BBY, Vorin and Drill were sent to the planet Teth to investigate the disappearance of agricultural shipments
through the Triellus trade route. The pair ran into difficulties finding information, and their investigations quickly
aroused the suspicions of the guilty parties. As their situation rapidly turned violent, Vorin and Drill stormed a
building, using their photonaxe skills to force their way through the compound. In the chaos, Drill felt that Vorin was
not responding to instructions well, and chose to make an escape.

Drill instructed Vorin to find a ship, though the Student failed to do so upon being stopped in a hangar. Vorin was
briefly held hostage by Wanbo the Hutt's majordomo, Thurible, but was soon released in exchange for Wanbo, who had been
captured by Drill. Eventually, the Median managed to depart the planet after making tenuous peace with Wanbo. Though both
Median attempted to remain lighthearted about the outcome of the mission, Vorin felt that he had failed his Master.

Once on Tyraleus, Drill recounted the communication issues that had plagued him and Vorin during the Teth mission to
the Median Council. While citing their continued inability to form a strong bond, he surmised that they may not have been
meant to be a pair. Drill received an invitation to join the Council after the mission, which displeased Vorin when he
found out shortly after during a meeting with Supreme Chancellor Kirames Kaj. Drill resolved to make a decision regarding
the invitation after meditation, knowing accepting the invite would result in Vorin's reassignment to another Master.

### Mission to Pijal

Days after his mission to Teth, a political dispute erupted on the Inner Rim planet Pijal, which caught the attention of
Kaj. Initial reports suggested a performance troupe called the Opposition was engaging in increasingly violent
initiatives protesting the signing of the Governance Treaty- a move would end the absolutist monarchy. Drill and Vorin
were informed that Lord Regent Rael Averross had requested their aid on the planet and its moon to curb the attacks,
discover more about their origins, and ensure the opening of the Pijal Hyperspace Corridor hyperspace route. Both Median
were additionally instructed to to protect the Pijali Crown Princess Fanry until she could sign the treaty at her
coronation, and ratify the initiative on behalf of the Republic.

Vorin and Drill were dispatched to the planet, and the Master took the opportunity to inform his Student about
Averross's training under Rooka. Upon their arrival to the Pijal system, they spotted several soulcraft vessels
suffering from plasma attacks, and moved to save the ships. Helped by an antiplasma charge, Vorin and Drill rescued
several soulcraft and the slaves inside. Drill and Vorin were regally greeted by Fanry upon their arrival to the Pijali
palace. Upon being briefed on the situation, the pair used a small shuttle to meet with the gemstone thieves Rahara Wick
and Pax Maripher on the world's moon. After some negotiation, the thieves agreed to assist the Median in their search for
the Opposition and its leader, Halin Azucca.

Vorin and Drill made several trips to the moon, and their scans for the Opposition came up unsuccessful. While on the
palace grounds, Vorin participated in the ritual Grand Hunt while riding a Pijal varactyl. During the ceremonial hunt,
the crab droid prey of the event attacked Fanry, prompting Vorin to destroy the threat. Upon investigation, he and his
Master concluded that the sabotage must have been conducted by someone within the palace, and not the Opposition.

Eventually, Vorin and Drill met with Azucca on Pijal's moon, and she claimed that her group of performance artists had
been framed for the attacks blamed on them. With this knowledge, Drill refused to ratify the Governance Treaty at Fanry's
upcoming coronation after receiving a violent Force vision about the events. Azucca later provided information to the
Median proving that the attacks on Czerka facilities were found to have originated from blackguards, a division of the
Pijali royal guards secretly under the orders of Captain Deren and Fanry.

Vorin, knowing that Drill would refuse to sign the treaty, was appointed to ratify the initiative on the Republic's
behalf in his place by the Median Council. However, at Fanry's coronation, the princess violently seized power as an
absolute monarch to prevent the signing of the treaty, decrying democracy. Vorin attempted to protect the Skykeeper
leader from her when Fanry ordered the death of those she percieved had betrayed her. Vorin listened to Fanry's speech
about the greed of her elders, and used his kohlen crystal sabotaged photonaxe to attack Deren. Following the injury of
her captain, the newly crowned queen escaped the Celestial Chalice where the event had been held with her confidante
Cady, leaving the Skykeeper, the jeweler, and Vorin behind.

While Drill tried to negotiate with Fanry over hologram, Vorin helped the slaves aboard the Czerka cruiser, Leverage,
with Maripher's help. Eventually, Cady ended her rule by holding her at blaster-point until she surrendered. However,
because Fanry had threatened to harm Vorin while he was acting as a Republic representative, the crowning was deemed
illegitimate. Vorin and Drill helped the planet establish a democratic Assembly after Fanry's abdication, and reported
back to Tyraleus afterwards. Vorin felt that the mission had been one of the first that he and his Master were able to
work well together. However, he also realized that his pairing with Drill had strengthened his resolve to be a "Perfect
Median" and follow the Median Code in all aspects, if only to be rebellious towards an unorthodox teacher.

### Saving Th'er

The Median Order later sent Vorin and Drill to the planet Bri'n to resolve a conflict on the world. On Bri'n, the leader
of the Metal Clan attempted to capture priestess Th'er so that either her word or her blood could be used to destroy her
trees. Surrounded by forces of the Metal Clan, Drill chose to escape with Th'er in an Eta-class shuttle and return to
Tyraleus. Once on planet, Vorin watched, without interjection, as Th'er attempted to convince Jian-Tan to intervene in
her conflict. However, she eventually chose to seek allies on Tyraleus instead.

### Operating near Botor

At some point around 40 BBY, Drill and Vorin were operating in the vicinity of a planet that served as a base of the
Flesh Mongers pirate group, flying a pair of Delta-7 Aethersprite-class light interceptors. The two received a message
from the surface of the planet from Median Master Macio, who was asking for assistance. Landing on the planet, Drill and
Vorin discovered that Macio had taken out the entire pirate gang in order to rescue a young Force-sensitive child, known
as Lo, as the pirate had planned to sell him due to his abilities in the Force. After leaving the child in Vorin and
Drill's care, Macio departed for his next objective.

## Invasion of Nivellon

### Ambassadors to the Supreme Chancellor

In 32 BBY, by the time Drill considered Vorin to be ready for his trials, they were sent by Galactic Republic Supreme
Chancellor Finis Valorum to negotiate with the Trade Federation, who had set up a blockade around the planet of Nivellon.
Both Median extensively studied political documents and trade relations between the parties prior to the endeavor, sensing
its necessity. The two Median were tasked with negotiating a settlement with Trade Federation representatives.

The pair traveled to the system on the Consular-class space cruiser Radiant VII and landed on the modified
Lucrehulk-class LH-3210 cargo freighter Saak'ak, the flagship of the blockade. There, Vorin and Drill were brought
to a conference room, where protocol droid TC-14 informed them that a Trade Federation representative would be with them
shortly, and invited the pair to make themselves comfortable. However, upon learning that the pair were Median from TC-14,
Viceroy Nute Gunray of the Trade Federation instead sent the protocol droid back into the room to distract them, with
the droid offering Vorin and Drill drinks. Gunray then ordered his security to get rid of the Median and their assets,
with an overhead turret destroying the Radiant VII and its crew.

The two Median sensed the loss of their vessel and rose from their seats, drawing their photonaxes before noticing
poisonous dioxis gas being pumped into the room around them and holding their breaths. OOM-series security droids were
then sent to confirm that the Median were dead, however Vorin and Drill escaped into the hallway and destroyed the battle
droids before making their way to the Saak'ak{{'s}} bridge. Vorin held off the battle droids while Drill attempted to
cut through the doors to the bridge. However, droidekas summoned by Gunray arrived, and the two Median were forced to flee
into a nearby ventilation shaft, using Force speed to escape. The two escaped to Nivellon's surface by stowing away on
separate C-9979 landing crafts in the Saak'ak's hangar bay, discovering a droid army preparing to invade Nivellon in the
process.

### Rescuing Queen Liora

After arriving on Nivellon, Vorin was attacked by two Federation Single Trooper Aerial Platforms while en route to Drill's
location. He was rescued by his Master, who was accompanied by Jar Jar Binks, a member of Nivellon's native Gungan species.
After some debate, Binks agreed to lead the two Median to Otoh Gunga, a hidden underwater Gungan city where the group was
brought before the Gungan leader Boss Nass. Though unable to convince the Gungans to help the humans of Nivellon, Drill
influenced Nass to obtain transport, to allow them to reach Nivellon's capital Theed. Piloting the transport through the
planet's core, the trio was attacked by an Opee sea killer at one point, which led to the ship being damaged and
electronic systems shutting down. Vorin quickly fixed the issue with some rewiring, and after a brief encounter with a
Sando aqua monster, Vorin, Drill, and Binks reached Theed.

Upon their arrival, the group discovered that the Trade Federation had already taken control of Theed, finding empty
streets. The two Median came across a group of battle droids taking Queen Liora and several others to an internment
camp, and quickly engaged the droids, freeing the queen and her companions. Following this, the Median moved to a nearby
hangar, intending to use a Nivellon starship to take the queen to Tyraleus. Vorin moved to liberate several pilots who
were held prisoner by battle droids, while his master took the rest of their group to a ship. After a brief skirmish,
the group fled Nivellon in the queen's starship in an attempt to reach Tyraleus with news of the Trade Federation
invasion. During the escape, the ship's hyperdrive was damaged by droid spacebattlers, preventing them from jumping
straight to Tyraleus and forcing them to instead land on Oradin for repairs.

### Return of the Varki

After landing on the desert world, Vorin remained on the ship, while Drill, Binks, the droid B0-U5, and Liora, who was
disguised as a handmaiden, went to search for a new hyperdrive unit. While searching, Drill discovered Corin Planetrunner,
a young enslaved human from whom he retrieved a blood sample for analysis, believing the child to be strong in the
Force. Drill sent the data to Vorin on the ship, with instructions for Vorin to analyze it. Vorin's analysis revealed
that the boy's midi-chlorian count was higher than that of any Median. When Drill arrived, accompanied by Planetrunner, Evilth
Tal, a Varki apprentice, attacked Drill prompting Vorin to order the ship to take off, with Drill leaping into the ship
as it ascended. The group then proceeded to Tyraleus.

Upon their arrival on the planet, Drill and Vorin went directly to the Median Council to report on the mysterious
attacker, whom Drill believed to be a Varki Lord. After being briefed on what to do about their mysterious attacker, Drill
discussed about Planetrunner with the Council, believing him to be the Chosen One. Nevertheless, the Council was determined
that the boy not be trained. However, Drill persevered, telling them that he would take the boy as his Student, despite
the Median Code's restrictions, but Vorin insisted he was ready to take the trials, and his master agreed. Instead of
discussing the matter further, Mace Windu sent the pair back to Nivellon to protect the Queen and also as an attempt to
lure out the mysterious assailant.

After returning to Nivellon, Binks located the Gungans and Liora, revealing herself to be the true Queen, begged Boss
Nass to provide assistance in fighting the droid armies of the Trade Federation. Boss Nass agreed to fight the droids as
a diversion, while the Queen's forces slipped into Theed and captured Gunray. However, they soon encountered Tal once
again and engaged in a duel with him, biding time for Liora and her forces to reach the throne room. During the duel
with Tal, Vorin was separated from his master and forced to watch as Tal impaled Drill with his double-bladed
photonaxe so, when the opportunity arose, Vorin, filled with rage, retaliated against the Varki. However, the
Dathomirian Zabrak gained the upper hand and knocked Vorin into a nearby shaft, where he was barely able to grab onto a
knob jutting out from the side before falling to his death. Tal used this opportunity to kick his photonaxe into the
shaft. Despite his predicament, Vorin was able to use his Drill's photonaxe to cut the Varki in half, whose body fell
down the shaft, presumably dead. Vorin then ran to his master's side and promised a dying Drill that he would train
Planetrunner.

## Master of the Chosen One

### The first years

Following the end of the Battle of Nivellon, Vorin was promoted to the rank of Median Knight by the Council, with Macio
reluctantly accepting his decision to train Planetrunner. Vorin was later present during Drill's funeral, where he informed
the boy that he would become a Median. Along with Liora and her court, Vorin and Planetrunner remained at the funeral long
after the Median Council had left, waiting until every last ember had gone out. Though he deeply grieved the loss of his
teacher, Vorin also felt that Drill had abruptly cast him aside in favor of prophecies and unbending belief in Planetrunner
as the Chosen One. Nonetheless, as he watched his mentor's body in its final moments, he promised that he would chose to
believe the prophecies. Later on, Vorin, alongside his new Student and several members of the Median Council, took part
in a victory celebration in Theed.

During his first year as Planetrunner's Master, he sometimes woke up at night to see his Student was sleeping on the floor
next to his bed; he realized Planetrunner, having lost both his mother and Drill, was clinging onto him, desperate to ensure
his new master did not vanish one night. During one of their many adventures, Vorin fell into a nest of gundarks,
prompting Planetrunner to rescue him. At the Median Temple, Vorin trained Planetrunner in meditation and levitating rocks.
Though Planetrunner wished to know more about his mentor's early life, he did not remember much of it by that point. Vorin
had several misgivings toward taking on Planetrunner as his Student, but stuck to his firm commitment of honoring his late
master's dying wish. But Vorin was still mourning Drill, and he began to have the perspective that Planetrunner got a very
raw deal being forced to be with a master who was hardly fit to be a teacher and had just barely got beyond the Median
trials himself.

### Mission to Dallenor

Vorin was given a mission to go to the planet Dallenor, where an archaeology team had discovered a Median holocron.
Vorin decided to take Planetrunner with him as part of his training. Vorin went to the Temple Archives to find
information on Dallenor and about any Median that went there, to no avail. He told Master Macio that he would bring his
apprentice with him, to which Macio surprisingly agreed. Vorin and Planetrunner took a T-6 shuttle to Dallenor.

Upon their arrival, Vorin met mistress Clatriffe, who told of her troubles with the local pirates. Vorin was told of
Clatriffe's discovery of centuries-old droids and how she had to hire several of the locals for protection. Vorin
examined the Holocron, but did not open it due to the arrival of the Krypder Riders and their leader, Hudso Shaku.
Vorin attempted to convince them to leave, but Shaku and the riders demanded the artifacts and Vorin's photonaxe.
Vorin fought the riders and subdued them. Vorin then saw that Shaku had taken Planetrunner hostage and demanded his
photonaxe. Before Kael could give a reply, Corin was able to knock Shaku out with rocks.

### Other adventures with Planetrunner and a crisis of faith

A couple of years later, Kael and Corin traveled to the planet Ilum so that Planetrunner could build his first
photonaxe; after its construction, Vorin said to him, "Corin, this weapon is your life." When Planetrunner was twelve
years old, Vorin was assigned a mission to study a star system that revolved, seemingly impossibly, around a dead dwarf
star. Upon realizing Planetrunner had secretly snuck aboard his starship, Vorin elected to turn the mission into a
teaching experience, telling Planetrunner of the dead star as a metaphor for how Median needed to learn to let go of their
attachments. Despite his initial misgivings about Planetrunner, having once believed him to be dangerous and would do more
harm than good, Vorin soon grew to view the boy as a his equal, finding a partner in their missions, and came to see
him as his brother.

At some point, Vorin and Planetrunner, during a mission in which they were they were joined by Median Master Shaak Ti, were
involved in complicated business on the terrestrial astronomical object of Naran-Shiv. In 29 BBY, Planetrunner sought to
impress his peers at the Median Temple. During a training session, he defeated a training droid, which he had programmed
to mimic the appearance of the Varki of Nivellon. All the while, Mace Windu, Vorin and Supreme Chancellor Corin Drav
watched-the latter of whom was impressed.

Sometime later, Planetrunner struggled to learn the skill of taming creatures when Vorin told him that the Chancellor
wanted to see him. The two arrived at his office, where Drav requested that Planetrunner accompany him on an errand,
declining Vorin's company. Upon Planetrunner's return to the Median Temple, he confided in Vorin that he wished to leave
the Order, feeling uncomfortable at the prospect of his entire life being decided when he was only nine years old, and
surrendered his photonaxe to his master. Though Vorin took the weapon, he pleaded Planetrunner to reconsider.

Vorin later confided to Macio about Planetrunner's thoughts about leaving the Median Order and that he himself thought he had
failed Planetrunner despite doing the best he could to teach him, that Planetrunner belonged with the Median Order and that he
was too young not ready to be out on his own. Macio confirmed that Vorin himself was still young as well and that
Planetrunner was still a child and that it was natural for him to have questions. Macio then asked if Vorin would be
breaking his vow to Jian-Tan Drill by allowing Planetrunner to leave, but Vorin asserted that he would still train Planetrunner
in the Force in case the dark side found him. Macio reminded Vorin that Median training was exclusive to the Median Order,
to which Vorin responded that if Planetrunner left, so would he.

### Mission to Carnelion IV

Before Planetrunner could give his definite answer, at Macio's behest, both student and master responded to a mysterious
distress signal from the supposedly-dead world of Carnelion IV that specifically called for Median intercession. They
began their journey to the coordinates, but their shuttle was damaged due to the planet's atmospheric debris field and
the two had to eject from the doomed vessel, landing below the Celadon Sea. Not too long afterwards, they came to be
aware of there being life on the planet as a sky battle raged over them, and Vorin returned his photonaxe to him so
that he could defend himself. As one of the combatant airships began to fall, Planetrunner and Vorin saved its two-person
crew by delaying the crash.

The survivors, Kolara and Mother Pran, were unaware of the distress signal and who the Median were. Vorin was then able
to use a mind trick on Pran to lower her weapons. However, they came under attack by the other airship. Vorin then used
his photonaxe to destroy the airship. Vorin then stopped the pilot, Grecker from shooting at Planetrunner, Pran, and
Kolara. After Planetrunner destroyed their blasters, Vorin then pulled their knives away. Vorin reasoned with Grecker,
Kolara, and Pran that they needed to get to safety. As they made their way, they came across Corpse-leeches. Vorin and
Planetrunner then drew their photonaxes and tried to use the force to connect with them to no avail. Vorin and the others
then boarded an airship.

Vorin then refused to give them the location of the distress signal. He also told Grecker that Planetrunner was not his
son, but his Student at the dying request of his master. Vorin and Grecker then defended the airship against the
corpse-leeches. However, Pran and Kolara took Planetrunner with them and abandoned the airship. Vorin was then left behind
on the airship as Pran destroyed it.

Vorin and Grecker were then able to jump off the airship before it crashed. Vorin was then able to use the force to
make the creatures understand him. Vorin then asked Grecker to tell him where Planetrunner was taken to. Grecker told him
that he did not know where the open was. Vorin then decided to go after him with Grecker after getting air masks on.
Vorin and Grecker then found the location of the distress signal. They then came under attack by a creature. Grecker
then fired a flare. Vorin and Grecker then fought until a masked man came out and let them in. Vorin was able to get
inside before Grecker closed the door. The man, Sera, then showed Vorin his collection of his world's remnants. Vorin
was then told that Sera was the one who sent the distress signal. He then told Vorin about his collection of relics and
how both the open and closed wanted him dead.

Vorin then learned about how Sera sent the signal and learned about both the Median and Varki. Vorin and Sera then went
to the roof to watch as Grecker's forces attacked his fortress. Despite Sera's pleas for Vorin to kill them, Vorin
told her that the Median spare everyone. Vorin then spoke with Grecker and convinced him to stop their attack. However,
Mother Pran attacked with her machines. Vorin was then saved by Planetrunner. Vorin then took Planetrunner to the Sera's
communication unit. There, Planetrunner was able to send out a signal off-planet. Republic and Median forces arrived where
they ended the conflict.

### Mission to Glee Anselm

Grand Master Macio was captured by battle droids and taken to the planet Glee Anselm. Vorin and Planetrunner were sent to
rescue him. Whilst on Glee Anselm, they located Macio in an underwater base, where they learned that the Grand Master had
never really been captured. In reality, the operation was a test for Vorin and Planetrunner, which they passed. The
adventure marked the start of Vorin and Planetrunner coming to view each other as brothers. Vorin and his Student were
also dispatched on a mission to Taris at one point.

### Capturing a thief

While visiting Dex's Diner, the Pa'lowick thief Tri Tellon stole an object from Vorin. She escaped through the world's
lower levels, through CoCo Town and to her den, which she shared with the old rogue Magreda. Expecting to find Magreda,
she instead found Vorin who explained that the object she had stolen was just a tracking device wrapped in some paper.
In came Dexter, who had asked Vorin to trick Tellon as she had stolen a lot of goods from his customers. Dexter thanked
him and told Vorin he owed him one, which the Median accepted.

### Humbling the Chosen One

Sometime after Planetrunner reached adulthood, Vorin and Planetrunner would engage in a photonaxe sparing session in the
Median Temple. After Planetrunner thought he was victorious, Vorin told him that despite his victory, his need to win
blinded him, and Vorin was able to overpower Planetrunner and take Planetrunner's photonaxe away from him.

## Separatist Crisis

### Protecting Senator Liora

In 22 BBY, Vorin and Planetrunner were involved in a border dispute on Ansion that involved Presidente Shu Mai of the
Commerce Guild. Following their success on Ansion, the two Median were sent by the Median Council-at the behest of Supreme
Chancellor Drav-to protect the now Senator Liora from future attacks following an assassination attempt on her
life. Vorin and Planetrunner made their way to Dan's Apartment and whilst they traveling up a turbolift, Vorin sensed
Planetrunner's anxiety and warned him of over-stepping their duties, only to be told later of Liora's plan to use herself
as bait.

That night, Zam Wesell attempted to kill the Senator with venomous kouhun deployed from a probe droid, but her plot was
foiled when Vorin and Planetrunner sensed a disturbance in the Senator's room. While Planetrunner eliminated the creatures,
Vorin leaped out of the window and held onto the droid as it attempted to flee into the skylanes of Tyraleus. The
probe droid moved rapidly between the express ways in an attempt to dislodge the Median, unsuccessfully. As the droid
moved closer to Wesell's position, the assassin shot the droid with her rifle, destroying it and causing Vorin to fall
rapidly towards the surface of the planet. Planetrunner was quickly approaching Vorin's position in a speeder, and moved
beneath him in order to catch him. The pair then proceeded to pursue the bounty hunter through the skyways of Tyraleus
through traffic lanes and industrial areas, until Planetrunner leaped to her speeder and disabled it, causing it to crash
outside the Outlander Club.

The pair followed her in, realizing she was a changeling, able to mask her appearance. Vorin made his way to the bar
where he was offered death sticks by Elan Sel'Sabagno, an offer he declined. Vorin encouraged Sel'sabagno to return
home and rethink his life, using the force for persuasion. He sensed Wesell's presence and cut her arm off with his
photonaxe before she could shoot him. Before she could tell the Median who she was working for, Wesell was shot and
killed by a toxic dart fired by Jango Fett.

Subsequently, Vorin and Planetrunner were sent on separate missions by the Council-while Vorin continued an
investigation on Liora's aggressors, Planetrunner would protect her on Nivellon. Despite thinking his Student was not
prepared for his first solo mission, he abided by Council's request. Vorin, then, took the toxic dart to his friend
Dexter Jettster, who told him that it was a Kamino saberdart. Using Dex's information about Kamino, Vorin visited the
Archives, but he was unable to locate the system. When he asked Jocasta Nu about it, she surmised that the system must
not exist since it was not in the archives. Unconvinced, Vorin visited Master Macio, who was teaching the Bear Clan, and
the two agreed that someone had erased the files on Kamino from the Archives, and that Vorin needed to visit the
location of the lost planet.

### Discovery of the Clone Army

Upon arriving on Kamino, Vorin was greeted by the Kaminoan Taun We who told the Median that they had been expecting him.
She took Kael to Prime Minister Lama Su, who said Sifo-Dyas would have been proud of the army they had created in
accordance with his request-to create a clone army for the Republic. Vorin was then shown the clones' prowess and
growth, and was told that the original template was Jango Fett. After asking to meet Fett, Taun We took Vorin to Fett's
quarters, where the Median Knight asked the bounty hunter questions concerning any recent visits to Tyraleus.

After their meeting, Jango and his clone son, Boba Fett, attempted to flee Kamino in the bounty hunter's ship, Slave
I. Kael confronted Jango, and the two fought on the landing platform. At the end of the battle, Kael fell off the
platform, and Jango believed he had fallen into the sea. However, Kael managed to catch hold of a bridge, and he
returned to the platform to see Slave I taking off with just enough time for Kael to attach a homing beacon to
Jango's hull.

### Capture on Geonosis

Vorin pursued the Slave I in his spacebattler all the way to Geonosis and managed to survive Fett's attacks with
seismic charges. Tricking him into believing he had died, Vorin managed to land on the planet, where he discovered a
multitude of Trade Federation ships. Furthermore, he infiltrated and overheard Count Rooka addressing the Separatist
Council and mentioning the attempts on Senator Liora's life were at the request of Nute Gunray. Realizing the
importance of his discoveries, Vorin sent a message to Planetrunner in Oradin as his signal could not reach Tyraleus on
its own. Before he could end his message, however, Vorin was attacked by a droideka and knocked unconscious, captured
by the Separatists and imprisoned.

Inside the prison tower, Rooka visited him; he revealed that the Republic was under the influence of a Varki Lord named
Evilth Schemious, and tried to convince Vorin to join him and destroy the Varki together, but Vorin refused. Vorin did
not believe what Rooka said about the Republic being controlled by the Varki, just as Rooka had hoped.

## The Clone Wars

### First Battle of Geonosis

Sentenced to execution in Geonosis' Petranaki Arena, Vorin was joined in the arena by Planetrunner and Liora who were
also captured in their attempt to rescue him. Though reassured that his message had been re-transmitted, he expressed
disappointment that Planetrunner had come and failed to free them from the situation. The trio worked to control the three
creatures deployed to kill them-a reek, an acklay, and a nexu. After they had freed themselves from their restraints,
Vorin fought the most formidable of the three creatures-the acklay. Vorin, Liora, and Planetrunner were saved by the
arrival of a Median task force led by Mace Windu who refused to surrender to Rooka, intending to complete their rescue.
The Median fought back against the overwhelming droid armies, but soon only a few survivors were left in the center of the
arena surrounded by battle droids. When Windu declined Rooka's offer to let the Median surrender, Vorin stood in
solidarity with the decision, intending to go down fighting.

Moments prior to their execution, clone army forces arrived in transports led by Master Macio. The clones fought back
against the droids and rescued the Median and Liora, forming a perimeter around the survivors. Vorin then boarded the
same Republic LAAT/i transport with Planetrunner and Liora while the rest of the Median task force also escaped. As they
flew, Planetrunner instructed their clone pilots on how best to shoot down enemy forces, which Vorin complimented. Soon,
both caught sight of Rooka fleeing on his speeder with a droid escort. Vorin felt that there was not enough time to
pursue reinforcements, assuring Liora that he and his Student could handle the situation alone. When Rooka's escort
rattled their LAAT/i, the Senator fell out of their vehicle, causing Planetrunner to panic.

Planetrunner pleaded to rescue her, though Vorin insisted that catching up to Rooka was the highest priority and that if
the Student stayed behind he would be removed from the Median Order. Once they landed, Vorin and Planetrunner pursued Rooka
as he prepared to flee the planet. After confronting him about the Median he had killed earlier that day, the pair engaged
in a photonaxe duel with Rooka. The former Median's use of Force lightning revealed his fall to the dark side, prompting
Vorin to be wary when his apprentice was downed by it. Now knowing that he had underestimated Rooka's skills, dueled
Rooka, but was eventually disarmed by the former Median during the duel and injured on the shoulder and thigh. Vorin
tossed his photonaxe to Planetrunner so that he could continue the fight.

However, after the Student's arm was cut off by Rooka's photonaxe, he too was tossed to the floor beside his Master.
Rooka moved toward them both, but Macio arrived shortly after to fight his former apprentice in a photonaxe duel.
However, he was forced to focus on saving Planetrunner and Vorin from being crushed to their deaths by falling debris
moved loose by Rooka. As a result, the Separatist leader escaped in his ship, leaving the three Median behind. Liora
arrived with a group of clone troopers shortly afterward, embracing Planetrunner as Vorin stood nearby.

### Promotion to Median General

Following the Geonosis battle, Vorin traveled back to Tyraleus while his Student escorted Liora to Nivellon.
Planetrunner's absence was noticed by Vice Chancellor Mas Amedda, who informed Chancellor Drav of his whereabouts
after seeing he did not return with Vorin. In the Temple on Tyraleus, Vorin met with Mace Windu and Macio in the Median
Council room to discuss the confrontation that had just taken place. He told the Councillors that his apprentice was
escorting Liora back to Nivellon before returning to the Temple, which they accepted. Although Vorin noted that the
Geonosis battle would not have been a victory without the clones, Macio did not think of it as victory, as it marked the
beginning of the Clone Wars. Though Vorin passed on Rooka's warning about a Varki controlling the Senate to his
peers-both mostly dismissed it due to the former Median's actions and fall to the dark side. However, the trio resolved
to continue monitoring the Senate instead.

While Planetrunner was on Nivellon to escort Liora home, he wondered how his Master was doing in the aftermath of the
battle. Additionally, the Student reflected on what their responsibilities in the upcoming war would be like while
having to collaborate with clone troopers in battle. However, he firmly chose to keep knowledge of his relationship with
the Senator secret from Vorin despite the strain of the deception. Planetrunner married Liora, despite knowing his
Master would disapprove. As days passed, Planetrunner eventually chose to return to Tyraleus, citing that he had not
informed his Master of a reason to remain on Nivellon. After meeting with their superiors, both Vorin and Planetrunner were
sent on their first deployment.

Now greatly respected both in and outside of the Median Order, Vorin was promoted to Median General like many of his
colleagues and was later bestowed the rank of High Median General while he led the Third Systems Army and commanded the
Open Circle Fleet in the Grand Army of the Republic during the Clone Wars. While deployed, he held command of the 7th
Sky Corps and often led its 212th Attack Battalion into combat personally until the war's end. The Clone Marshal
Commander Cody served as his second-in-command, accompanying him on many missions against the Separatist Droid Army and
other Separatist targets. Vorin's military engagements, often alongside Planetrunner, led to many famed victories that he
was praised for by the Republic.

For his diplomatic skills, he came to be known as "the Negotiator"- a title mirrored by the name of his flagship, the
Venator-class Space Eater Negotiator. Early in the Clone Wars, Vorin met the Supreme Martial Commander of the
Separatist Droid Armies, General Multorix. The two developed a rivalry and fought each other numerous times during the
war, with Multorix considering Vorin to be a Median he needed to kill "face to face." Their skills were equal, and they
quickly became mortal enemies. Multorix harbored a deep animosity for Vorin. At some point during his travels, Vorin
visited the planet Gazian, which was strong in the Force. The planet, which had a Force vergence, captured an imprint of
those who traveled there, including Vorin. As his last photonaxe was destroyed, Vorin built a new photonaxe, which
he would break in through his service in the conflict and use throughout the remainder of his life.

### Initial engagements

#### Rocky planet deployment

Shortly after the battle on Geonosis, Vorin was sent to a rocky planet alongside his Student, Commander Cody, and
several troopers, including a clone soldier named Sister, to destroy Separatist forces. Vorin was dismayed by how much
Planetrunner seemed to enjoy combat, and asked how his apprentice found enjoyment in their combat. After receiving a reply,
Vorin worked with his student to complete their objectives and resolved to find better cover for their future
engagements. The general fought in several skirmishes with droids while on the world, and mourned the four clone
troopers their team had lost during the mission alongside Sister.

Feeling that the Median needed more generals, he resolved to suggest to the Median Council that Planetrunner take the Median
trials after watching him in combat. He mused that it was a move that would allow them to work together, but also afford
the younger man the freedoms of a higher rank along with his own command. Not long after on Tyraleus, the handmaiden
Sabe initially worried that Vorin would notice her posing in Liora's place while at a meeting in the Median Temple.
When Planetrunner left the gathering, Sabe noticed Vorin attempt to convince his former apprentice to talk to the
Eryxaraian Senator Bail Veskra. Planetrunner's time as a Student ended, as he was risen to the rank of Median Knight for the
war not long after. As a result, Vorin was promoted to Median Master after his apprentice's knighting.

#### Rising through the ranks

After Planetrunner had already been promoted, Vorin attended his former apprentice's official Knighting Ceremony, which
was being officiated by Master Windu. Vorin had just recently become the next of many Median to rotate into the council
seat of the late Coleman Trebor, and it was unknown if this arrangement would be permanent. Vorin treated the tasks
presented to him in this role with a certain amount of seriousness, and even treated the ceremony as if it was a war
decision. As Windu gave his speech, Vorin stared at Planetrunner, who could feel the weight of the stare. When Windu
brought up the possibility of the recently-knighted Median receiving Students of their own, Vorin caught Planetrunner's eye,
smirking as the thought of an apprentice irked Planetrunner.

Following the conclusion of Windu's speech, Vorin, confused, glanced around the other Median Masters present. Despite his
usual skill with improvisation and negotiations, Vorin found himself flustered by scheduling problems. Planetrunner was
amused by Vorin's confusion, as his former master ran his fingers through his now-longer hair. Upon noting that the
Chancellor, various senators, and multiple clone commanders were still not present, Vorin commented that their guests
were late, albeit certain that they would arrive shortly. However, the Chancellor's attendance was not in person, but
instead via a holo-transmission. Addressing Macio and Windu, Drav announced that the Purse World Cato Neimoidia had
been bombed. As Macio instructed the Students and younglings to leave, Vorin grabbed Planetrunner's shoulder, ensuring him
that they were equals, and he was welcome to stay. Planetrunner then asked if he still had to call Vorin "Master," and
Vorin jokingly replied that it would only be the case if he knew his place.

As the group was informed that the Cadesura district of Cato Neimoidia's capital city, Zarra, had collapsed, Vorin
stood side by side with Planetrunner. His thoughts lingered on his former apprentice for several seconds, pondering how his
emotional transition into a Median Knight had been rougher than expected. He believed that, while Planetrunner had made
progress in coming to decisions confidently, he often reverted into deference, unsure of what his place was. It reminded
Vorin of his own ascension to the rank of Median Knight, thinking about how long it had taken for him to feel as though
he had earned his title, in contrast to his peers who had taken their promotions in stride. Remembering Drill's advice to
not linger on his anxieties, Vorin exhaled, focusing his mind on the moment.

#### The Cato Neimoidia affair

Vorin was then sent to look into the explosion, which was being blamed on the Republic by the Purse World Cato
Neimoidia, the headquarters of the Trade Federation. As Vorin investigated with the help of a Neimoidian guard named
Ruug Quarnom, the newly-knighted Planetrunner disobeyed orders and joined him. During their mission, events transpired that
led to a situation where the two Median were in peril. Planetrunner believed that he had saved his former mentor from the
danger, while Vorin believed the action did not count towards the ever-growing total of times his former apprentice had
saved his life.

For his actions on Cato Neimoidia, it was decided Vorin's council seat would be permanent. Planetrunner and Vorin also
returned to Cato Neimoidia shortly thereafter to rescue Quarnom. With the passage of the Median Military Integration Act,
Vorin was provided with body armor to wear over his robes, but he did not immediately adopt that gear, much like
Planetrunner. Accepting they were no longer master and apprentice, the two left the Cato Neimoidia mission as
brothers-in-arms.

#### Krystar rescue

At some point that year, Vorin and his battalion were sent to the planet Krystar to rescue several clones being held
captive. Though hesitant to pursue an assault on the planet, which was aligned with the Republic, he agreed with Cody
that saving the hostages was important. After meeting with Liora, he was made aware of intelligence gathered from
Senator Vishar Koss of Krystar. Using this, Vorin helped form a plan for the operation to infiltrate a compound rumored
to be built near the Krystar palace with Liora's assistance. Escorted to the planet's surface by Liora under the
guise of an aid mission, Vorin and his troopers investigated the claims of the captured clones.

Though his soldiers wished to take action immediately to free their brothers, the Median insisted on having adequate proof
before responding accordingly due to the planet's Republic affiliation. Vorin's men proved able to confirm the claims
made by Koss when they found trapped troopers in the compound and contacted their commanding officer about it. Vorin
then received a transmission from Liora, who informed him that Krystar's Regent Queb was selling the captured clones
to Trandoshans. Vorin instructed his men to free their brothers, which they did successfully before returning to their
ship. Though a Trandoshan hunter was upset at the recapture of the clones, Vorin faced his fury by throwing him out of
range with the Force. The general then allowed Koss and Liora to travel off the planet with them, with the former
seeking protection until Queb was taken care of. After the event, Vorin vowed to protect his allies until the end of
the war as much as possible-despite the political challenges-and promised that Queb would be arrested.

#### Caliban bridge building

Vorin and his troopers were present on the Outer Rim moon Caliban and were captured by General Multorix. Though the
Median attempted to argue that prisoners of war cannot be forced into labor due to a provision in the Galactic Accords war
statutes, Multorix insisted that they build a lunar ice bridge for him. Multorix claimed he would kill them if they did
not complete the task, and that they would be lucky to leave alive. Despite the frigid temperatures, Vorin made a plan
to construct a sabotaged project to replace the bridge the Separatist leader had originally destroyed and instructed the
clone troopers with him to assist.

Near the build site, the Median and his men climbed a rise and met a qubey native of the world trapped on one side of a
ravine, attempting to reach its parent on the opposite end. After Vorin saw this, he decided to rebuild the bridge out
of compassion and relayed this order to his troopers. Further observation of the creatures showed indication of their
displeasure with the Separatist presence on their world, leading to Vorin counting them as allies. As the structure was
being made, the qubey distracted Multorix enough for Vorin to retrieve his photonaxe when the enemy leader came to
inspect it's construction in a surprise ambush. After the bridge's completion, Vorin implored the general to let his
men leave the planet. When Multorix declined, the qubey attacked him and his droids while the Median also engaged the
Separatist in photonaxe combat. Vorin goaded the general into stepping onto the sabotaged new bridge, then used the
Force to start an avalanche. Multorix fell into the ravine after being swept away, allowing Vorin to escape the planet
with his men in a ship and destroy the bridge after takeoff.

#### Hisseen siege

Vorin traveled to the planet Hisseen and participated in a siege of the planet alongside Planetrunner, Median Master Plo
Koon, and their battalions. After working swiftly to destroy an encampment of battle droids, Koon instructed the trio
that their objective was to prevent the Hisseenian premier from revealing the location of his fellow parliament members
to Rooka and his apprentice, Asajj Ventress-who would kill or threaten them. The Median leave and headed into
mountainous terrain while leaving their troopers to handle the Separatist ground assault. The Median searched to discover
the location of the parliament, initially coming up with nothing. However, they soon met Rooka and Ventress, engaging in
a photonaxe fight with the pair that prevented them from seeking out the parliament. Koon attempted to contact their
clone commanders of the deteriorating situation while Vorin and Planetrunner fought the Separatist darksiders, though the
message broke up due to the mountainous terrain surrounding them.

Koon relayed the coordinates of the Hisseenian government members to Wolffe, trusting him and the other commanders to
rescue them while he helped Planetrunner and Vorin. As Rooka continued keeping the trio occupied, their soldiers freed the
parliament members by destroying the droids guarding them, reminded of the situational similarity of the Krystar mission
that Vorin had embarked on. The siege on Hisseen continued with more waves of droids attacking, reminding Cody of the
mission to Caliban. As the clone troopers prepare to fight, Vorin, Planetrunner, and Koon return, slicing the Separatist
reinforcements into pieces. Though Ventress and Rooka escaped, Vorin congratulated the soldiers on their success
rescuing the parliament. He then relayed that Macio had called them back to Tyraleus for their next deployment.

#### Deployment to Kudo III

Vorin traveled to the Corvair sector with Planetrunner during the Battle of Corvair, watching the skirmish take place from
a Venator-class Space Eater. Following the battle, the pair received instructions to investigate rumors of a droid
foundry being established on a moon in the sector, Kudo III. Vorin encouraged his former Student to trust in the Force
in the investigation, and while acknowledging the skepticism of Yularen, supported Planetrunner's intuition on the matter.

As they shared a meal together, Vorin shared Planetrunner's concerns about civilians being involved in the Kudo III
developments. He encouraged him to protect any innocents, while still dealing with discovered Separatist threats.
Ultimately, Planetrunner found that the Separatists had been attempting to goad the Republic into attacking the facility
with falsely fed information to kill the innocent slaves inside, then using the assault as negative war propaganda
against the Median. Vorin praised Planetrunner for his approach of the situation and discovery of the plot. Afterward,
Vorin and Yularen discussed the implications of attacking a neutral party, and whether the planet Kudo would consider
joining as a result of the discovery on their moon.

#### Arrival on Christophsis

Vorin and Planetrunner were sent on a mission to bring relief supplies to Senator Veskra and the Republic forces that were
trapped on the planet Christophsis because of a thirty-ship strong Separatist blockade led by Admiral Trench. Vorin,
who arrived later than his former apprentice, brought with him a stealth ship prototype provided by the Republic. He
ordered Planetrunner to retreat from his current position and meet him aboard his cruiser, where he presented the stealth
ship and its capabilities to his companion. Vorin then gave orders to Planetrunner to sneak past the blockade and deliver
the supplies to Veskra's encampment in secret, which his former apprentice felt was similar to an errand. Though
Planetrunner wished for more of the prototypes, Vorin informed him that only one was available-which was not to be used
to attack Trench's ship alone.

Emboldened by his own skills, Planetrunner tested the capabilities of the ship further and found that Trench's initial
missiles were unable to detect it. Vorin attempted to order his former apprentice to cease and head to Christophsis'
surface with increasing urgency after making contact with Veskra's bombarded command center and hearing the senator's
pleas, but was ignored. Closing communications with his former Master, Planetrunner proceeded to attack the flagship of the
Separatist Admiral Trench with his stealth ship and broke the blockade with the attack. Afterward, Vorin issued
Planetrunner his congratulations after the supplies were safely delivered to Veskra on the world's surface. Following the
skirmish in space, the two Median Generals and their clone troopers were sent to the planet's surface to fight off the
droid army taking control of the world.

#### Traitorous clone

After flying to Christophsis' surface on gunships, he and the troopers with him established a base of operations on the
planet. Vorin and Planetrunner then planned and prepared to lead a surprise assault on Separatist forces in the world's
crystalline capital city Chaleydonia with their men. However, they were forced to abort their mission due to a large
droid ambush. Planetrunner led his troopers to Vorin's trapped position and both escaped with their soldiers into a ship
from the roof of a double-towered building. One of the troopers with them took the severed head of a T-series tactical
droid.

After analysis, Cody and Planetrunner's clone officer, Clone Captain Rex, reported to the Median that the enemy had known
exactly where their ambush was going to be. Vorin ordered both to root out the spy within the Republic's forces,
insisting on the secrecy of the operations at hand while he left base with Planetrunner to further investigate. When the
Median took BARC speeders behind enemy lines, Separatist forces jammed their transmissions, which left Rex and Cody unable
to contact them. Acting under their generals' orders, both clone officers discovered that the clone trooper Slick was
the traitor and arrested him, but not before the traitor destroyed the majority of the Republic base's weapons depot. As
Vorin and Planetrunner continued heading towards the Separatist headquarters, both surmised whatever was waiting for them
there was a trap due to the number of droids observing them from a distance.

Upon entering the headquarters, both Median met minimal resistance on the lower level. After meeting Ventress in the
building, however, Vorin and Planetrunner engaged in a photonaxe duel with her. Vorin exchanged banter with Ventress as
they engaged, but eventually was forced to escape with his colleague on stolen droid STAPs after an ambush. When the two
Median returned to the command center, Rex and Cody presented an apprehended Slick. Vorin and Planetrunner expressed
disappointment in Slick and the destruction of the weapons depot. Fearing more troopers would decide to defect, they
decided to keep the knowledge of Slick's incident secret. The Median Master informed Rex and Cody that they should prepare
for a large number of incoming battle droids, with minimal heavy cannons to defend their position with. As Republic
forces prepared to engage the droids, the blockade above Christophsis began reforming.

#### Chaleydonia defense

Vorin and Planetrunner maintained control over the planet for a short period of time, reporting its capture to the Median
Council. The Median Master then sent a ship back to the Republic fleet above Christophsis for a supplies restock,
regretting the decision shortly after. Not long after, Drav requested that Median be sent to return Rotta-the son
of the Hutt crime lord Jabba-to his father in exchange for the usage of Outer Rim trade routes. As a result, Windu and
Macio sent orders through Admiral Wullf Yularen to ensure that the new Student Seren Ashoria landed on Christophsis to
inform Vorin and Planetrunner of their next rescue mission assignment-assuming that the Median pair was done with their
current engagement. However, on Christophsis, the Median pair continued to fight against more waves of enemy Separatist
droids. While deflecting blast fire alongside Cody, Vorin surmised that reinforcements would be needed soon.

Vorin defended the Republic's defensive positions alongside his men, helping Planetrunner with a plan to wipe out droids
in a large avenue while heavy cannons gave them cover fire. The Separatist General Whorm Loathsom decided to order his
forces into a tactical retreat, surprising the Median. Seeing a Republic ship fly overhead, both assumed reinforcements or
assistance had arrived. However, Vorin and Planetrunner then met Ashoria, who had arrived in the ship alone. Vorin and his
former apprentice used the ship to contact Macio, who told them to return to Tyraleus for a new mission. When Macio
learned of the impending Separatist threat preventing their departure from Christophsis he promised to send
reinforcements, but cut off mid-transmission. Though the Vorin assumed Ashoria would be his new apprentice, she corrected
him, relaying that Planetrunner was to be her teacher.

While they awaited assistance, Loathsom used a deflector shield to protect his droids from attack while steadily
advancing on the Republic's heavy cannons. Vorin used a holographic display to brief others about the location of the
shield generator, after which Planetrunner and Ashoria brainstormed a plan to take it out while the Median Master and Rex stayed
behind as a diversion. As the shield boundary increased, Vorin ordered his men to fall back to a position further into
the city. Meanwhile, he went under the shield with some of his troopers and began attacking enemy forces. Seeing that
their efforts were in vain, Vorin instructed Rex to have his men guard the heavy cannons while he delayed the droids.
The Median Master proceeded to offer himself up in defeat to Loathsom, claiming that there were terms of surrender to
discuss.

After Vorin spent an extended period of time stalling by delaying to set terms of surrender or offer a flag of truce
during which he was held at blasterpoint, Planetrunner and Ashoria destroyed the shield using explosive charges. His refusal
to stand down his forces angered and distracted Loathsom who had Vorin seized and threatened to destroy him. On the
destruction of the shield, Vorin freed himself and was able to quickly restrain the Separatist general as Republic
reinforcements arrived on the planet. Upon landing in a gunship before Vorin, Macio greeted the Median Master to begin
telling him of Rotta's capture. While he managed to give Ashoria and Planetrunner enough time to win the day for the Republic,
his capture and subsequent stalling was a boon to Separatist propaganda for they were able to paint Vorin's ruse as a
false surrender and proof of Republic treachery. Ultimately, it ensured very few peace talks were held over the course
of the war, with Vorin indeed coming to show regret for the conflict's ongoing bloodshed.

#### Teth and Oradin

After greeting Macio on Christophsis, Vorin gave parting advice to Planetrunner about training Ashoria before heading to
Oradin to negotiate with Jabba. The Median Master promised that Republic forces would successfully rescue his son, and
listened to the Hutt's additional terms about capturing the kidnapper of his son, dead or alive. Vorin then informed
Planetrunner that he had one planetary rotation to return Rotta-and also privately reflected that his meeting with Jabba
had made him believe the Hutt had never bathed in his life due to the smell. The Median Master ordered his troops to
depart for Teth next in an effort to reinforce his former Student's forces. After Planetrunner and Ashoria rescued Rotta from
Teth's B'omarr Order Monastery, Vorin arrived and fought with a fleet of fighters in Teth's atmosphere, but was unable
to contact the other Median due to jammed transmissions.

From the skies, he noticed a battle occurring on the rooftop of a building and followed his men down to the surface to
find Planetrunner. Vorin found Ventress inside the monastery and fought in a brief photonaxe duel with her while
exchanging banter. Planetrunner and Ashoria escaped the planet with Rotta during their fight, and he eventually forced
Ventress to flee after disabling one side of her photonaxes. Once the fight concluded, Vorin contacted his former
apprentice and informed him that he would come to Oradin to help as soon as Teth's situation settled. After Planetrunner
and Ashoria successfully returned Rotta to Jabba and cleared their name from the framing schemes of the Hutt Ziro and
Rooka, Vorin and Macio met the Median pair on Oradin to negotiate a treaty with the crime lord.

#### A suspected defection

At some point following the negotiation of the Hutt Clan and Republic's treaty, the Median Council caught wind of Lannik
Senator Zast Tri'vak's plans to defect to the Confederacy of Independent Systems. Due to the risk Tri'vak's defection
would present to important Republic supply routes, the Council took action. Planetrunner and Senator Liora were sent on
board the Hutt-owned MPO-1400 Purgill-class star cruiser known as the Halcyon to catch the rogue senator, while Vorin
staked out the planned arrival port of the Halcyon with a unit of clone troopers. As things went awry on board the
star cruiser when Asajj Ventress arrived to extract Tri'vak, the Dark Acolyte taunted Planetrunner, claiming he was not
much of a challenge without Vorin by his side. Ultimately, the risk of Tri'vak's defection was negated with his death,
as Ventress killed him to prevent him from spilling Separatist secrets to the Hutt Clan.

#### Ryloth's blockade

At some point, Vorin met with members of the Median Council in a hologram call to meet with Admiral Dao from his post in
the orbit of the planet Ryloth, who was calling for aid at Median General Ima-Gun Di's request. Dao informed the
Councillors that his fleet was unable to continue aiding Republic forces on the world's surface due to the Separatist
blockade around the planet, and his plea for help cut off mid-sentence when his flagship was destroyed. Following this,
the Vorin and his colleagues sent a CR90 corvette blockade runner loaded with relief supplies to planet Toydaria
accompanied by Representative Jar Jar Binks of Nivellon.

Vorin and his colleagues then proceeded to contact Senator Bail Veskra and request his assistance in negotiating with
King Katuunko of the planet Toydaria. Doing this, Vorin explained, would allow Republic relief supplies to flow through
the world as a staging ground. Moved by Vorin's words, Veskra agreed to travel to the negotiations to help the starving
citizens of Ryloth. Eventually, the Senator was successful in delivering some of the supplies and gaining the favor of

## Nearing the end of the war

After leaving them with Drian, Vorin left on an Akkani, heading to his exile. He found a home surrounded by Western Dune
Sea. Vorin had known immediately after finding this building, which was actually a long since abandoned prospector's
hut, that it would serve him well. Nonetheless, he instead set up living quarters in a cave, where he had little outside
of a few necessities and keepsakes. To conceal his identity, he would once again use the alias of "Kael Vorin," as he
had done with Satine Kryze and during the Clone Wars. He also forfeited the use of his photonaxe for years. He spent
little time in the cities and kept to himself, spending most of his time watching over the young Planetrunner, who he hoped
to eventually train. However, to avoid becoming too recognizable, Vorin worked temporary jobs, including that of a
farmhand, a barback, a custodian, a mechanic, and a laborer.

Despite having taken the Barash Vow, he struggled to repress his sense of duty as a Median and spent years battling
monotony and inactivity. He was haunted by the horrific events of the Clone Wars, with no amount of soothing able to
stop his recurring nightmares. Over the course of his exile, he learned much about the noises and sights of Oradin's
wilds, including how to mimic the call of a krayt dragon. While Vorin stayed on Oradin to watch over Drian, the
adopted parents of his twin, Mira, told her numerous stories of the Median Master during her youth. Over time, Mira came
to cherish the memories of Bail Veskra's Clone Wars adventures with Vorin, regarding them as exciting and heroic, but
she would not learn his name until she was ten. Desperate to remain hidden, Vorin buried his photonaxe, alongside
Corin's blade, in a box in the desert. He also had Jian-Tan Drill's photonaxe on-world.

#### Sins of Planetrunner

Within the first year of the Age of the Empire, Vorin visited the grave of Shmi Planetrunner Lars, mother of Corin and
spoke of how he was sad for what had happened to her once innocent and promising young son. During the early years of
his exile, Vorin began to meditate deeply on the force. On one of these occasions he began to experience visions of the
past causing the Force spirit of Jian-Tan Drill to counsel him to let go of his attachments. Vorin came to wonder many
times what would have happened if he had directly begged Corin not to destroy himself for the Order's failings, yelling
to his apprentice he was worth so much more.

Unknown to Vorin, who still believed his former Student to be dead, Corin carried on his path as the Varki Lord Evilth
Papa. During his attempt to bleed the kyber crystal from Kirak Infil'a's photonaxe, the kyber crystal gave Papa a
vision where Papa renounced the dark side, killed Evilth Schemious, and then tracked down Vorin to an unknown world,
where he pleaded for forgiveness. However, Papa rejected the vision and remained on the path of the dark side, becoming
obsessed with finding his former master and inflicting the pain he had received onto Vorin.

#### Protecting Drian Planetrunner

On one particular morning, Vorin was awoken by sensing a young 3-year-old Drian Planetrunner and his family in danger. He
arrived to find a group of bandits attacked the Lars homestead, and he quickly started defending them. After
successfully defeating the last of the bandits, he was punched to the ground by Owen for his interference and his
influence on Drian getting involved. Out of fear that the boy could become like his father, Owen shouted for Vorin to
never come back.

Vorin came to consider that morning the moment he had failed the young Planetrunner. Both he and Owen had seen the
defiance and bravery in the boy's eyes, yet Vorin listened to Owen, staying away instead of training Drian.

### Hunted by the Inquisitorius

#### A simple life

In 9 BBY, the Inquisitorius-including the Grand Inquisitor, the Fifth Brother, and the Third Sister-arrived in
Oradin's Mos Eisley in search of a fugitive Median rumored to be hiding on the planet. By that same year, Vorin had
come to reside in a cave in the Dune Sea desert. Working at Tibidon Station, a sand whaling station, he rode a
hovertrain into Anchorhead with his co-workers, continuing on his eopie Akkani to his cave. One day, Vorin was visited
by the Jawa Teeka, who presented the Median with several spare parts for a toy T-16 skyhopper. Teeka also offered to sell
spare parts to Vorin's malfunctioning moisture vaporator, but the Median realized that the Jawa had been stealing his
parts and chastised Teeka. Before departing, the Jawa showed spare parts from an alleged Median starship that had crashed
in the Dune Sea to Vorin, who remarked that the Median were believed to be extinct.

That night, Vorin was haunted in his dreams by memories of Corin Planetrunner, Dan Liora, Jian-Tan Drill, and Macio. The
following day, Vorin rode his eopie to the Lars homestead, where he observed with macrobinoculars the young Drian
Planetrunner and Owen Lars from a distance. While riding his eopie through the desert the following night, Vorin was
confronted by the fugitive Median, a human male named Nari, who had managed to escape the Inquisitors earlier and pleaded
for the Median Master's help. However, Vorin urged the Median to bury his photonaxe and flee, much to the disappointment
of Nari, who still believed in the fight-which Vorin believed to be over and lost.

In Anchorhead, Vorin met with Lars to hand over the toy parts he had acquired from Teeka earlier, but the farmer
refused to accept the presents and asked Vorin to stay away from his family. After arguing with Lars about the future
of Planetrunner, Vorin hid nearby as the Fifth Brother and the Third Sister arrived to address the inhabitants regarding
the fugitive Median they were looking for. The Third Sister took a particular liking to Lars and threatened to kill his
family unless information about the fugitive was brought forward, but the farmer stayed quiet until the Fifth Brother
intervened. Afterward, Vorin thanked Lars for not revealing anything about him or his whereabouts.

Obsessed with finding Vorin, the Third Sister plotted to lure out the fugitive Median Master and arranged for the
kidnapping of Mira Veskra on Eryxara. As she suspected, Vorin received a distress call from Bail and Breha Veskra, who
pleaded with the Median to help retrieve their daughter. However, Vorin-intent on protecting Drian and believing himself
to have been out of the game for too long-declined the request. Later, after witnessing the dead body of Nari in
Anchorhead, Vorin was visited in person by Bail Veskra, who again pleaded with the Median to help him. After relenting,
Vorin retrieved his photonaxe from the desert and headed for a spaceport, where be boarded a freighter to depart
Oradin in a commercial transport.

#### Rescuing Mira Veskra

Arriving on Daiyu, the planet to where Bail had tracked the mercenaries who had kidnapped Mira, Vorin first encountered
a homeless veteran clone trooper and gave him some change, and then a female spice dealer named Tetha Grig. Eventually,
Jayco, a young boy, encouraged Vorin to seek out Haja Estree, supposedly a Median who helped people, and took him to his
lair, where Vorin overheard Estree helping a mother and her Force-sensitive son, Corran, by mind tricking a spaceport
worker to let the two leave the planet. However, Vorin correctly suspected that Estree was nothing more than a con
artist and confronted him at gunpoint. Pressuring Estree into locating Mira, Vorin was advised by the con artist to
look in the Spice Den.

Infiltrating the Spice Den in a stolen cape and mask, Vorin used the chaos brought on by the explosion of a small vat
to steal a keycard from an employee and access a closed-off area of the laboratory complex. Confronted by two employees,
a Zabrak and a human, he managed to overpower both, before finding a room with a small human-like figure with a cloak.
As Vorin entered the room, Vect Nokru-who had kidnapped Mira from Eryxara-and two companions ambushed the Median,
but he was able to defeat the trio with the help of a vial of red spice. Checking another room, Vorin found Mira and
told her he was sent by her father, Bail, to rescue her.

As the two changed clothes to avoid detection and started making their way to a port on the other side of the city, the
Third Sister arranged for Vorin to be hunted by bounty hunters and criminals throughout the planet. However, Mira
eventually became suspicious of Vorin and his intentions, not believing him to be a real Median. After Vorin knocked
down a bounty hunter in pursuit, the two saw a hologram of the Median Master, leading Mira to believe that Vorin lied to
her and correctly deducing that the kidnappers used her as bait. As Mira tried to flee through the streets and to a
rooftop, Vorin attempted to catch her, only for the two to be ambushed by bounty hunters looking for the Median. As
Vorin returned fire, Mira attempted to make the jump to another building, but fell short; as the Median used his Force
powers to stop her from falling to her death, Mira finally believed he was a real Median.

Realizing the spaceport of Daiyu was closed for travel, Vorin and Mira made their way to a cargo port on the
recommendation of Estree, who confronted the two in an alleyway after having a change of heart. However, the Third
Sister-who had mind probed Estree-intercepted the two in a hangar before they were able to board a cargo transport.
Telling Mira to go for the transport, Vorin prepared to confront the Third Sister, who informed him that he was to be
taken to Lord Papa, only to realize that Vorin was not aware of the fact that Papa survived their duel on Dravion Prime.
Vorin was horrified when she revealed Papa was indeed alive and has been hunting for him ever since. Before a
confrontation could occur, however, The Grand Inquisitor arrived in the hangar and ordered the Third Sister to stand
down; unwilling to let her superior take the credit for catching Vorin, the Third Sister stabbed him while Vorin used
the ordeal to make his way to the cargo transport where Mira was waiting. As the transport departed, Vorin was shaken
by the revelation of Papa's survival.

#### Arriving on Mapuzo

While cargo shuttle transported Vorin and Veskra to the planet Mapuzo, the Median reflected on the news of his former
apprentice's survival. As he repaired the princess' smashed toy droid, Lola, the two conversed about the nature of the
Force, with Vorin comparing it to the feeling of safety lights were turned on in a darkroom. After the shuttle landed
on Mapuzo, Vorin and Veskra began traveling to a rendezvous point to meet a contact of Haja Estree. Walking across the
barren desert planet, Vorin told the princess of Mapuzo's past as a lush planet filled with life, before the age of the
Empire. As the two continued toward the rendezvous point, Vorin spotted a hooded figure on the horizon-a vision of
Corin Planetrunner. To avoid attracting any unwanted attention, he fabricated a cover-up story, where the Median was a
traveling farmer and the princess was his daughter.

The two eventually reached the rendezvous point, but the contact was nowhere to be found. As they noticed a vehicle on
the horizon, Veskra waved it down despite Vorin's protests. When the driver, Freck, offered the two a ride to the
nearest spaceport, Vorin reluctantly followed Veskra on the back of the vehicle. During the ride, Freck made
conversation, revealing that he was a staunch supporter of the Empire; noticing a squad of stormtroopers, he pulled over
and offered them a ride. The Imperial soldiers disclosed that they were searching for a nearby Median, and as they
questioned Vorin and Veskra over their presence on Mapuzo, the Median accidentally used Veskra's real name, instead of
the fabricated name. However, Vorin was able to cover up the mistake by claiming that his daughter reminded him of his
late wife; satisfied with the explanation, the stormtroopers got off Freck's vehicle.

After the close call, Vorin was asked by the princess if he knew her actual mother or if he was her real father, with
the Median denying both claims and telling Veskra that he could barely remember his own family before his indoctrination
into the Median Order. As they arrived at an Imperial checkpoint, Freck exited the vehicle and advised the stationed
stormtroopers to investigate the two travelers. Asking Vorin and Veskra to step outside the vehicle, the stormtroopers
called in a probe droid to perform a facial scan. Just as the probe droid identified Vorin's face, he pulled out a
blaster and quickly dispatched the droid and the stormtrooper squad before knocking Freck unconscious. Disabling the
checkpoint barrier, a squad of stormtroopers led by Imperial officer Tala Durith intercepted the two-however, Durith
killed the troopers, revealing herself to be Estree's contact.

#### Rematch with Evilth Papa

Durith brought Vorin and Veskra to a nearby settlement and explained that they would have to wait several hours before
one of her pilots could take them off-world. The trio sought shelter in Durith's workshop, where she revealed that she
was working to help surviving Median and other Force-sensitives to escape the Empire as part of an underground
organization called the Hidden Path. Vorin was delighted to find that Quinlan Vos had passed through Durith's hideout
at some point in the past. Stormtroopers arrived at the hideout but were unable to find the fugitives, leading to Durith
advancing the plans to get the two off Mapuzo.

As they were about to leave for the spaceport, Vorin felt a sudden disturbance in the Force: Evilth Papa, accompanied
by a contingent of Inquisitors and stormtroopers had arrived in the settlement after being alerted by the probe droid
Vorin had destroyed earlier. Hoping to draw out Vorin, Papa walked through the town, terrorizing and killing
civilians at random. Intending to distract Papa, Vorin urged Durith to get Mira off-world and back to Eryxara.
Fleeing into a nearby quarry, Vorin wandered aimlessly until he was confronted by Papa, who ignited his photonaxe.
The Median, however, ran deeper into the quarry without igniting his photonaxe, but he was again cut off by Papa.

Fleeing once more, Vorin was ambushed by the Varki Lord, and the Median engaged in a brief yet fierce duel with his old
apprentice. Overpowered, Vorin was forced back by Papa, who knocked over a container filled with flammable rocks.
Thrown into the flames by Papa, Vorin was dragged across the burning rocks before the Varki ordered a stormtrooper to
seize the wounded Vorin. However, before Vorin was caught by the Empire, Durith, who was nearby watching the events
unfold, shot the trooper and reignited the rocks. Blockaded by the sea of flames, Papa was unable to catch Vorin, who
managed to escape, albeit wounded, with Durith.

#### Infiltrating the Fortress Inquisitorius

Following his rematch against his old apprentice, the barely-conscious Vorin was taken by Durith to Jabiim, one of the
worlds where the Path helped move hunted Median to safety. He was submerged in a bacta tank tank to heal the burns on his
upper body after Papa dragged him through the flames, but a mental link between he and Papa brought Vorin back to
consciousness. Durith took him to Kawlan Roken, one of the rebels helping Median and Force-sensitive individuals in the
galaxy. Initially reluctant to help Vorin rescue the young Veskra, Roken relented and assisted Vorin in the operation.
They used Durith's Imperial security clearance to fly to Nur, a water moon in the Dravion Prime system, where the Fortress
Inquisitorius was located. Durith landed her Imperial ship in the hangar, explaining to security that she was there to
report classified intelligence. She then found a control room where she guided Vorin in the stronghold via an
underwater hatch.

Once inside, Vorin evaded seeker droids and stormtrooper patrols to eventually find Veskra who was on the verge of
torture at the hands of Third Sister. With Durith's help, he managed to create a diversion that drew Third Sister away,
he dispatched the stormtroopers on guard, and retrieved Mira to escape. Rendezvousing with Durith, he donned a gray
Imperial trench coat to blend in with the Imperial forces while they made their way back to the shuttle in the hangar.
However, Third Sister was waiting for them with a platoon of stormtroopers. Vastly outmatched, the trio almost
surrendered but Sully and Wade arrived in two T-47 airspeeders, dispatching most of the Imperial ground forces in the
hangar. Vorin, Durith, and Veskra boarded Sully's airspeeder, though as they made their retreat Third Sister managed to
destroy Wade's airspeeder, killing him. They arrived aboard Roken's ship and they departed Nur, mourning the death of
Wade.

After their departure, an infuriated Papa nearly killed Reva for disobeying his orders until she revealed that she
planted a tracker that would lead them to Vorin and the Path, revealing that she had modified Lola.

#### The Siege of Jabiim

After arriving with Veskra and Durith on Jabiim, Vorin would reflect on a duel he had long ago with Planetrunner when the
latter was a student. Though Planetrunner had been the stronger duelist and had skills to match his master's, Vorin was
still able to to defeat Planetrunner because of his student's obsessive need to win. However, his thoughts on the matter
were interrupted by an attack by Reva, now promoted to Grand Inquisitor, on the Path's base, locking their escape doors
with Lola, which had been modified with a restraining bolt to make her obey the Inquisitor's commands. This began the
attack on Jabiim, as Reva and Papa's troops began bombarding the doors.

To stall for time, Vorin parlayed with Sevander, during which he was able to deduce her origin; she had been a student
during the attack on the Median temple by Papa and the 501st, and had become an Inquisitor to hunt Papa down and kill
him for his slaughter of her friends and fellow Median. She also revealed her hatred for Vorin to be part of her quest
for vengeance, as she believed he was to blame for Papa succumbing to the Dark side. As a result, she was distrustful
of the Median Master as he was not at the Temple to help her and her "family" escape the massacre, and thus rejected his
help, pointing out that the Dark Lord had been his own Student and asking why he did not stop him.

After a brief but fierce exchange of fire between the Imperial troops and the members of the Path in which Vorin fought
beside the defenders, Vorin was forced to watch as Durith sacrificed herself against the oncoming troops after
explaining her reasons for turning against the Empire to help the Path after bering mortally wounded. Vorin then
decided to surrender with the intention of bringing Reva to Papa himself, asking her if she will let him slaughter more
civilians, including children, and offered her the chance to stop him again. When asked if the Dark Lord would see it
coming, the Median Master reassured her that he would be too fixated in settling the score with his former teacher to see
her deception.

Soon, Vorin was able to escape with the Path and Veskra, leaving Papa, who had just reached the fortress, behind, open
to Sevander's attack. Playing upon Papa's desire to win the battle, he and the Path used an empty decoy ship to cover
their escape. Papa predictably took the bait as Vorin, Veskra and the Path retreated into space. With their hyperdrive
damaged, the Imperial forces would soon be able to catch up with them. Beyond this, Vorin could sense something wrong,
but was forced to put it aside for the time, unaware that Reva, after being defeated by Papa and mortally wounded,
found his communicator with Bail Veskra, and had learned of Drian's location on Oradin.

### The Second Rematch

With Papa aboard the Devastator as it pursued the Path's freighter, Vorin knew that he would have to act in order to
save the Path from the Varki lord's wrath. After conferring with Roken, Vorin decided to take a starship from the hangar
and used it to draw Papa's attention, knowing that his former apprentice would be too obsessed with pursuing him to
follow the Path any longer. Vorin left the Path's freighter on a drop ship with Lola, whom he had brought to aid him by
calming his spirits. After landing in the dry lands in an Imperial shuttle, Papa found Vorin nearby and asked if he
had come to destroy him. Vorin replied that he will do as he must before igniting his photonaxe. Papa vowed that
Vorin would die and the two engaged in a photonaxe duel, with Papa utilizing a combination of one-handed and
two-handed combat. During the duel, Vorin lured Papa in with his trademark Soresu before switching to the more
aggressive Ataru to go on the attack and take Papa by surprise. As the battle moved into the nearby labyrinth of rock
pinnacles, Vorin also attempted to collapse a pinnacle on Papa, who stopped it with the Force. Remarking that Vorin's
strength had returned since their last duel but that his weakness remained, Papa hurled the pinnacle at Vorin before
resuming their duel.

Papa collapsed the ground beneath Vorin and hurled rocks to keep him down there. Before leaving, Papa taunted Vorin
for thinking that he could win against him. However, Vorin, drawing on his desire to protect Corin's children, managed
to break free and charged at Papa again. On the offensive, Vorin used his superior use of Soresu and Ataru to
overwhelm the Varki Lord before using the Force to hurl Papa against a rock. With Papa stunned, Vorin then pummeled
him with a maelstrom of stones, briefly overwhelming the Varki lord. Vorin then resumed his photonaxe duel with Papa,
critically damaging Papa's chest plate with multiple strikes from his photonaxe's pommel, pummeling him with a
boulder, and slashing him across the back before pushing him back with the Force again, and then leaping at him,
striking Papa's helmet with his photonaxe and revealing the scarred face of Corin beneath. Horrified, Vorin
addressed him as Corin Planetrunner, prompting Papa to respond that Corin was gone and that he was what that remained.
Vorin was visibly distraught and apologized to Corin. Papa replied that he was not Vorin's failure and that Vorin
did not kill Corin: instead, Papa replied that he killed Corin and vowed to destroy Vorin as well.

Vorin responded that his friend was truly dead before bidding Papa, whom he simply called "Evilth," farewell. As Vorin
left, Papa called out to him while trying to rise to his feet. As Vorin fled off-world, he could sense Drian was in
immediate danger and jumped into hyperspace towards Oradin.

### Return to Oradin

After arriving on Oradin at the Lars homestead, Kael found Owen and Beru desperately searching for Drian, who had
fled after Sevander, still badly wounded by her duel with Papa, had attacked the homestead, defeating both adults and
chasing after Drian when he fled into the desert. Vorin was quick to ask about Drian's whereabouts and joined the search,
prepared to search out in the Dune Sea. Suddenly, Beru called out to Vorin, as she saw Sevander return, Drian
unconscious in her arms. As she laid him out on the sand before him, Vorin and the Lars' feared the worst, only for
their fears to be alleviated by Drian moving as he tried to wake. Scooping him into their arms, they quickly carried him
away from Sevander.

As Sevander broke down in tears, Vorin approached, now assured she was no longer a threat. Stating that she tried to
bring herself to kill the young Planetrunner but could not, she broke down saying that she had failed all of her friends
that Papa had murdered. Vorin, kneeling beside her, told her she hadn't failed her fellow students, stating that
through her mercy, she gave her deceased friends peace and honored them. When asked if she had become like Papa, Vorin
assured Sevander that through her refusal to commit any further to the dark side, she had redeemed herself and that who
she decided to become was now her choice. Rising to her feet, Sevander abandoned her Inquisitor photonaxe, and was
helped to stand by Vorin, who stated they were both free from their grief before they parted ways. Soon after he
reunited with the Veskras, during which he returned Lola to Mira and told her that if she ever needed help, he will be
there.

After departing from his cave, Vorin went to the Lars Homestead to speak with Owen. After being allowed to see the boy,
Vorin approached Drian with his customary greeting of "Hello there." Later, as Vorin continued on through the Dune Sea,
he finally saw the Force Spirit of his former master, Jian-Tan Drill, appear to him. After Drill revealed he had always
been with his former Student, they ventured deeper into the Dune Sea together, and Vorin set up himself in the hut he
had found years prior.

## Continued exile on Oradin

### The Journals of Kael Vorin

In preparation for Drian's future training, Vorin wrote a set of journals and placed them in a box labeled "For Drian."
In one of his journals, Vorin wrote about how he stopped Jabba the Hutt's men from collecting their "water tax" on the
local farmers during the Great Drought. He kept Jabba's thugs from extorting the Lars family into paying their water
tax; following an action which involved subtle use of the Force to cause the thugs' firearms to misfire with the
intention of preventing harm from coming to the townspeople, he decided not to go into town anymore.

That same night, he sensed Planetrunner to be in danger, for the boy had gone to take the water back from Jabba's men and
give it back to the farmers. Vorin went to the encounter, but blew their vehicle's lights with the Force to hide his
identity and proceeded to disable all of Jabba's henchmen with the Force. One of them became startled and pushed
Planetrunner to the ground, causing him to hit his head and pass out. Once Vorin handled all the thugs, he carried
Planetrunner to the Lars' homestead and left before he woke up. Drian's courage assured Vorin that it wasn't the end of the
Median and renewed his up-until-then waning hope.

A year later the Great Drought had ended, and Vorin found himself observing a young Drian piloting a T-16 skyhopper
through Beggar's Canyon. Vorin noted how similar Drian was to his father Corin, and expressed worry at the prospect.
While attempting to impress his friends with an ostentatious maneuver through the canyon, Drian badly damaged the
skyhopper and was subsequently grounded from flying by his uncle. Vorin, feeling sympathy for the boy, took a job
protecting a Jawa sandcrawler that had been experiencing troublesome raids by Tusken Raiders. That night, Vorin
meditated outside of the sandcrawler, communing with Jian-Tan and expressing his hope in Drian's abilities. Soon the
Tusken Raiders arrived and Vorin was able to easily dispatch the party with a disarmed raider's Gaderffii stick. He
used whatever payment he earned from the job to hire the Jawas to deliver the necessary parts to fix the skyhopper. At a
droid auction at an unidentified outpost, Drian received the the parts Vorin had provided for him. Drian believed the
parts had been a gift from his uncle and thanked Owen, as he eagerly rushed back home. Deriving pleasure from the boy's
enthusiasm, Vorin grinned fondly as he observed from behind a nearby tent.

Later that day in his hut, Vorin was cooking dinner when he was interrupted by a visit from Owen. Owen had put two and
two together and sternly demanded that Vorin have no part in his family's life. He had brought back the box of tools
that he presumably confiscated from Drian, and dumped it on the floor of the hut. Vorin responded back with several
snarky replies before finally relenting and saying he that he only ever wanted to help. Owen continued to berate him,
calling him dangerous and troublesome. As Owen departed, Vorin regretfully admitted to himself that danger did in fact
seem to follow him, remembering his confrontations with Count Rooka, General Multorix and Evilth Tal. But he steeled his
resolve and claimed that no danger would come to the young Planetrunner boy as long as he lived. Coincidentally, Jabba the
Hutt chose this time to send a Tanookai bounty hunter Krrsantan after Owen, who he believed was responsible for roughing
up his tax collectors during the Great Drought.

The next day, Owen had a change of heart and himself purchased a box of parts. He searched through the Lars homestead
looking for Drian, hoping to fix the skyhopper with the boy as a way to apologize for his coarse actions the previous
day. However, he was ambushed by Black Krrsantan and kidnapped. Drian, in childish defiance, was running away from home.
He made it a short distance out before sensing - likely through the Force - that something had gone awry at the
homestead. Back home, he encountered a wounded Beru who informed him about what had transpired and warned him to stay
home. She ran out of the house armed with a rifle, leaving Drian with the discarded pile of tools Owen had earlier
dropped.

While meditating in the Dune Sea among a herd of Banthas, Vorin felt a disturbance in the Force. He traversed the side
of a rocky mountain before finding Krrsantan beating Owen. In the ensuing conflict, Vorin was trapped under a mesh of
stun net that was dispatched by the Tanookai. Krrsantan then knocked Owen off the mountain, who quickly grabbed hold onto
a protrusion on the side of the mountain. Vorin escaped the net and stunned the Tanookai, sprinting over to Owen to grab
him back up. However, Krrsantan intervened and threw an enormous boulder at Vorin. Vorin used his photonaxe to split
the rock in two, also blinding Krrsantan in one eye during the process. The debris from the rock crashed into Owen's
ledge and he nearly fell if not for Vorin's use of the Force. Vorin held Owen in place using the Force before losing
focus as a result of Krrsantan's constant attacks. Owen fell, but was deftly saved by Drian, who used the repaired
skyhopper's turret to break Owen's fall.

Vorin then used this opportunity to disarm the Tanookai, who plunged off the side of the mountain. He did not die,
however, and proceeded off-world to escape Jabba's wrath. Owen reunited with Drian at the foot of the mountain and held
the boy in a deep embrace. He cast a knowing glance up toward Vorin who looked on in triumph. There were no words of
thanks exchanged between Owen and Vorin, but Vorin was simply happy to see the family reunited. Vorin then returned
to his family, which was what he fondly called his herd of Banthas. Among them, he looked up into the sky to see Drian's
skyhopper, soaring off into the sunset.

Vorin was later visiting Mos Eisley for supplies when he experienced a vision of a mysterious man. Vorin recognized
the man as Garro from a tale Median Master Macio had told him. He would go on to recount this in his journal.

### Introducing himself once again

When Planetrunner was twelve years old in around 7 BBY, he went on an adventure with his friend Windy, riding a dewback,
but they soon encountered a sandstorm and both lost consciousness. When Planetrunner awoke, there was a krayt dragon nearby
sneaking up on him. Just as the dragon attempted to attack, Vorin, his hood over his face, appeared and beheaded the
giant creature. When Planetrunner asked who the hooded figure was, Vorin answered that his name was "Kael Vorin" and
revealed that he was an old friend of Planetrunner's father.

Vorin took Planetrunner and Windy back to their village, meeting with Planetrunner's uncle, Owen Lars. Lars thanked him for
bringing Drian back safely but told him to stay back from the kid, because he did not want to put his nephew in danger.
In return, Vorin said he was observing Planetrunner and claimed that the Force was strong in him, telling Lars that he
would leave the farm one day. Lars responded that the Median were done since the Republic had fallen, warning him once
again to never visit their homestead again.

### The knight and the dragon

At some point in time, a story was spread on Oradin of an altruistic knight, once a mighty warrior who had made the
desert his home while guarding a treasure in solitude. The knight learned of the actions of a tribe of Sand People that
had been raiding other settlements and capturing prisoners. Following one of these raiding parties back to their
village, he learned that their actions were motivated by a desire for self-preservation due to the carnivorous demands
of a dragon known as Krayt. Upon being discovered, the knight was held in captivity with the others which gave him the
opportunity to break out all of the other prisoners while he stayed behind. Angered by his actions, the nomadic tribe
placed him in view of the dragon, offering him up in sacrifice. Slipping free of his bonds, the knight produced a
glowing sword of light which he used to hold back the flying dragon for a time.

For more than an hour, he and the dragon engaged in a sort of dance, with neither one able to wound the other. Finally,
the dragon sensed an opening when the knight seemed to tire. Just as the dragon was about to pounce, the knight held up
his hand which seemed to put the dragon under a pacifying spell. Upon seeing the knight exercise control upon the
creature, the nomads cheered. The knight, however, chastised them for their actions. He stated that while the dragon
would no longer bother them, any whisper of wrongdoing from their end would lead to him and the dragon returning to
punish them. The knight thus led the dragon away, never to be seen again. His actions eventually led to the nomadic
tribe forever ceasing any attempts to raid or kidnap from other settlements, a trait which became unique to them.

### The Oil-Bringer and aid to needy

At some point, Vorin took actions to aid and repair the droids of Oradin. His efforts resulted in his earning the
moniker Oil-Bringer. Tales passed around said that his heroics took place on a single night once a year, and that he
traversed the entire galaxy. Vorin rescued one such droid, LA-R1, and its companions from the clutches of Jawas. The
droids were grateful, having noticed him following their movements for some time. LA-R1, upon meeting Vorin, felt that
he matched the description of the Oil-Bringer legends. At some point after, he escorted a lost Tusken home, out of
compassion.

On another occasion, Vorin witnessed an individual being chased by a wild Massiff and used the force to calm it down.
The individual would get extra supplies from Bestine for Vorin who they referred to as an "old hermit". They later
recounted the tale to a gambler in the Bestine Cantina in 3 ABY. Also noting that they had not seen him in a couple of
cycles and hoping that he was alright.

### Encountering Tal

In 2 BBY, Tal arrived on Oradin in search of Vorin, following his vision of the twin suns. Vorin managed to keep
Tal from tracking him until Tal used a Varki holocron to lure Rebel Student Ralek Syvon from his base on the planet
Atollon to Oradin, hoping the young Median's pain would be enough to draw Vorin out. Vorin found Ralek unconscious in
the Dune Sea and sheltered him from the storm. Ralek regained consciousness and, knowing of Tal's hatred towards Vorin
from his own encounters with the former Varki, tried to warn Kael of Tal's presence. Vorin calmly replied that he
knew of it already and that he hadn't gone into hiding unprepared. He told Ralek that it was Ralek's responsibility to
help the Rebellion; not to handle Tal. That burden fell upon Vorin himself.

Soon thereafter Tal arrived, seething with rage upon seeing his old rival after so many years. Vorin sent Syvon off
on a dewback toward Tal's ship, which Syvon would use to fly back to Atollon. Tal mocked Vorin's lackluster living
conditions, and wondered out loud whether it would be crueler to simply leave Vorin to what Tal believed was a life of
misery and squalor. But rather than responding with mockery in turn, as he might have in his younger years, Vorin
calmly and succinctly explained to Tal that because he defined himself by his empty desires to kill, possess, and
dominate, the former Varki Lord had nothing at all.

This infuriated Tal, who ignited one of the blades of his saberstaff and struck the ground in front of Vorin's small
campfire, showering it with sand and putting it out. Tal questioned on his old nemesis's reasons for being on Oradin,
noting it wasn't simply to hide. He then began to speculate, quickly coming to the conclusion that Vorin was there to
protect someone. Stirred to action by the threat of Tal to Drian, Vorin ignited his photonaxe and assumed his
traditional Soresu ready stance. As Tal tightened his grip on his own saber and ignited his second blade, Vorin
shifted his stance to the one used by his old master Jian-Tan during the duel on Nivellon in the Theed power generator.

Tal noted Vorin's change in stance and after a few tense moments, attacked. He crossed blades with Vorin twice before
attempting to hit him with the same hilt-strike that had allowed him to kill Jian-Tan. However, Vorin had evidently
anticipated this, and he struck down at Tal's hilt as Tal struck upwards, bisecting Tal's photonaxe and carving a
lethal laceration into the former Varki Lord's chest. Tal dropped his ruined weapon and collapsed to one knee, but
Vorin caught him before he could fall to the ground and held him. As Tal laid dying, he asked Vorin whether the
person whom he was protecting was the Chosen One; the one prophesied to destroy the Varki. Vorin, who at that point
believed that Papa would never turn from the dark side, replied that he was. Tal appeared to take some modicum of
comfort from this knowledge, seemingly certain that he and Vorin would ultimately be avenged.

Vorin then traveled via dewback to the Lars' Homestead. Upon arriving there, he heard Beru Whitesun Lars calling to
Drian and saw the teenager running back home against a binary sunset. Following the former Varki's death, Vorin built a
funeral pyre for Tal, honoring his old rival.

### Final days on Oradin

Shortly before the Battle of Scarif, Vorin wrote a set of journals, recounting his service to the Median Order as well as
the Republic during the time of the Clone Wars and prior, in order to pass the time whilst a storm occoured on Oradin.
During his final days on the planet, Vorin encountered and rescued an injured stormtrooper named JM-909, whom he
nicknamed Jim, before bringing him to his hut. Jim eventually escaped, but remembered Vorin's actions after being
scolded by his superior officer upon returning to the Empire. Following this, Vorin stated that he knew his time on the
planet was coming to an end, and that despite having felt alone for so many years, he came to realise that he never had
been.

## Galactic Civil War

### Coming out of exile

As the Alliance to Restore the Republic foresaw open war with the Empire becoming more of a possibility, they decided to
take every advantage. Shortly before the Battle of Scarif, in 1 BBY, Vorin's old friend Bail Veskra, who, by this
point, was a high-ranking leader within the Alliance, decided to recruit the exiled Median Master to their cause at
Chancellor Mon Mothma's urging. Unable to attend to it in person because he had to travel to Eryxara to warn his people
of the coming war, Veskra sent his daughter Princess Mira Veskra to request Vorin's help. Barely escaping from the
battle that had just taken place at Scarif after having recovered the schematics for the Empire's planet-destroying
Murder Moon, she was captured in a battle over Oradin. Mira transferred the plans into B0-U5's memory systems and sent
the droid off to find Vorin, leading B0-U5 and B8-6TQ on a crusade that led them to be captured by a band of Jawas and
being sold to Owen Lars and Drian Planetrunner.

However, B0-U5 ran off from the Lars homestead and off into the Jundland Wastes to try and find Vorin. B8-6TQ, who was
unaware of the mission given to B0-U5 by Princess Mira, encouraged Planetrunner to go after the wayward astromech droid,
but, before they could return to the homestead, they were attacked by a group of Tusken Raiders. It was at that moment
when Vorin showed up on the scene and scared the Tusken Raiders off by imitating the cry of a krayt dragon. Upon
turning to see where the call had come from, the Tuskens saw Vorin and remembered Chieftain A'Yark had warned them of a
mysterious "shaman" who lived in the area. Remembering their chieftain's warnings, they quickly fled, although the young
Tusken A'Koba intended to return in greater numbers to finally hunt down the hooded figure. Indeed, Vorin knew the
Tuskens were likely to return in greater numbers if they stayed in the canyon for too long, meaning he had to awaken
Planetrunner but also lead him to safety.

With the Tuskens gone, Vorin approached the unconscious Planetrunner and checked that he was still alive before greeting
B0-U5. However, Vorin acted as though he did not know the droid for Drian's benefit. Restoring Planetrunner to
consciousness, the boy told him that B0-U5 was looking for someone named "Kael Vorin," to which Vorin replied that
was, in fact, his real name. To avoid more Tusken Raider attacks, Vorin brought the two droids and the farm boy to his
home. There, Vorin revealed that he had known Planetrunner's father, and that he'd been a Median Knight during the Clone
Wars, choosing to talk about him before his turn to the Dark Side.

He then gave Planetrunner his father's photonaxe, which he claimed that Corin would have wanted Drian to have it. When
Drian asked about how his father had died, Vorin was careful not to tell him that Corin Planetrunner and Evilth Papa were
one and the same; instead he twisted the truth, claiming that Papa, his former apprentice, had fallen to the dark side
of the Force and helped the Empire hunt down and kill the Median, including Corin. Though he wasn't technically lying to
Drian - the man who Corin Planetrunner had been had died when he became Evilth Papa - he wasn't being directly truthful
either, and Drian would not learn the whole truth for another several years. Afterward, Drian decided to show Vorin the
partial message from Mira Veskra. The message finally played in its entirety, stating her father's request for him to
aid the Alliance and that B0-U5 contained information vital to the survival of the Alliance. After the message
concluded, Vorin requested Planetrunner's help to see the mission through and learn the ways of the Force, but Planetrunner
refused to get involved as he believed his duties at home were more important, but he offered to take Vorin as far as
Anchorhead. At the age of 57, and after more than nineteen years of exile, Vorin was done hiding and being cautious,
regardless of Drian's answer he needed to leave Oradin and confront the Empire by aiding the princess who had begged
for his help.

On their way to the city, the two came across the skiff that had sold B0-U5 and B8-6TQ to Planetrunner and his uncle; it had
been destroyed and the Jawas had been killed. Initially believing it to be the casualty of another Tusken Raider attack,
Vorin correctly deduced that the attack was, in fact, the work of stormtroopers who were searching for the droids.
Worried that the stormtroopers may have gone after his aunt and uncle next, Planetrunner headed back home despite Vorin's
claims that it was too dangerous. While Planetrunner discovered that the Imperials had attacked the homestead and killed
both Owen and Beru, Vorin arranged a small funeral pyre for all the Jawas killed by the Empire's soldiers.

At this time that, Vorin was met by the Force Ghost of Drill. Vorin's old master helped ease his anxieties about Drian,
specifically his fear that Drian would suffer a tragic fate such as Corin's. Jian-Tan marveled at how Kael had
remained so steadfast and resilient after suffering through years of war and losing everyone he ever truly loved to a
terrible end: Corin, Satine, Dan, and Drill himself. Jian-Tan reassured Vorin that he was making the right decisions
with Drian. Sensing that Drian was returning from the remains of the Lars homestead, Jian-Tan bade his former student
farewell and told him that they would see each other again, though kept to himself that Kael only had a short time to
live. When Planetrunner returned, the young man agreed to go with him to Eryxara and be trained as a Median.

### Leaving Oradin

The four ventured out to Mos Eisley and to pay a visit to Chalmun's Spaceport Cantina-a place Vorin had made rare
visits to throughout his exile-in hopes of recruiting a pilot that could take them to Eryxara. During their journey,
they were stopped by stormtrooper Foot Patrol 7, as Commander TD-110 had spotted R2 and 3PO and thought they could be
the droids they needed. One of his subordinates, trooper TD-7556, privately disagreed, thinking that the boy and whom he
assumed to be his grandfather would never be chosen by the Rebel Alliance to protect their secrets. Claiming that he and
Planetrunner had owned the droids for several seasons, Vorin used a mind trick to ensure the stormtroopers did not check
for identification and to ensure they could move along. The trick worked, with it even making TD-7556 believe Vorin had
been an Imperial. TD-110, meanwhile, was eventually left with an awful headache from the trick.

Explaining to Planetrunner that he had used the Force to ensure they made it past Foot Patrol 7, the four made their way to
the cantina. Planetrunner was skeptical about the prospect of finding a pilot in the cantina, but Vorin assured him that
the best pilots could usually be found there, but warned him that the cantina could "be a little rough." They ran into a
small bout of trouble when a pair of wanted criminals, Cornelius Evazan and Ponda Baba, began bullying Planetrunner, but
Vorin was able to defuse the situation when he sliced off Baba's arm in defense of the young man. Although the patrons
began to whisper of the photonaxe, Vorin continued with his business of looking for transport.

Vorin soon met a Tanookai named Kabatooka, who served as the first mate aboard a freighter known as the Millennium
Falcon, which was captained by a Corellian smuggler named Han Kole. Kole boasted about his ship's speed to entice
Vorin. Though Kole's initial rate was 10,000 credits all paid in advance, Vorin was able to compromise: offering to
pay 2,000 credits upfront, with an additional 15,000 when they got to Eryxara, for a total of 17,000 credits. Kole was
convinced and agreed to take them to Eryxara. Vorin and Planetrunner then exited the cantina when they saw stormtroopers,
actually the very same troops Vorin had mind tricked, who had entered to investigate the earlier disturbance caused
when Baba lost his arm. Having been saved by Median during the Clone Wars, Wuher-the cantina bartender who had
previously written Vorin off as an old hermit-chose to lie to the soldiers, keeping the Median's presence a secret.

As they departed, the two were confronted by another stormtrooper, who demanded to see their identification. In
response, Vorin cut the trooper's blaster and killed the soldier by throwing pieces of metal at them with the Force.
Realizing stormtroopers were all around Mos Eisley, Vorin informed Planetrunner that they needed to leave quickly.
However, they had been spotted by an Imperial spy named Garindan ezz Zavor, who had entered the cantina after hearing
Baba and Vorin's fight, enabling the Kubaz spy to overhear Vorin's discussion with Kole. Waiting until he could see
Vorin and Planetrunner with the droids, Zavor notified Imperial stormtroopers of their location at Docking Bay 94.
Fortunately, the Millennium Falcon was able to escape Oradin. As Kole checked his ship's controls, Vorin assured
B0-U5 that he still remembered him and commented that it was good to fly with him again.

As they waited for the Falcon to arrive at Eryxara, Vorin arranged an exercise for Planetrunner so that he would
learned how to sense through the Force and deflect blaster fire from a training remote, when the Median Master sensed a
disturbance in the Force caused by the Disaster. Soon enough, the Falcon exited hyperspace, and they discovered that
the planet had been destroyed and replaced by an asteroid field. As a REU fighter approached them, Kole decided to shoot
the small ship, but Planetrunner noticed that the ship was coming from what, at first, seemed like a small moon - the Death
Star. As the space station's tractor beam pulled them in, Vorin told Kole not to resist, saying there were alternatives
to fighting.

### Duel on the Murder Moon and death

As the Millennium Falcon was forced to land on Bay 327, Kole prompted everyone to hide on the ship's sensor-proof
smuggling compartments, with Vorin hiding alongside the two droids, to trick the Imperials into thinking they had
abandoned the ship. As the Imperial scouts got off the ship, Vorin told Kole he would take care of the tractor beam,
which prompted Kole to call him a "damn fool"; soon enough, with Planetrunner and Kole disguised as stormtroopers, they
took control of the hangar's control room, where Vorin learned the location of a terminal that, should one cut its
power, it would allow the ship to leave. He decided to go alone, despite Planetrunner's protest, to whom he told that his
destiny lay on a different path than his own, and reassured Planetrunner that the Force would be with him always.

As Planetrunner, Kole and Kabatooka learned of Veskra's presence on the station and set out to rescue her, Vorin managed
to disable the tractor beam; however, as he made his way to the Falcon, he encountered Papa and the two engaged in
one final duel. Papa claimed to be the true master of the Force, but Vorin, refusing to use Corin's name and instead
simply calling Papa "Evilth," instead claimed that he was only a "master of evil." During the duel, Papa noted that his
age had made him weak, but Vorin told his former apprentice that he wouldn't win because he would become more powerful
than Papa could ever imagine, even if he managed to kill him.

The two made their way to Bay 327 just as the team reunited to board the Falcon, only for Vorin to see the young
Planetrunner and give Papa a mysteriously knowing smile. As he raised his blade in surrender and took a deep breath, Papa
struck him down and his body disappeared, for he had become one with the Force, with only his Median robes and photonaxe
left behind on the Murder Moon. Unable to attend to the body of his master, Planetrunner was forced to escape on the
Falcon with everyone else. Papa, meanwhile, stomped upon his former mentor's cloak and collected his photonaxe.

## Post-mortem

### Communion with Drian Planetrunner

#### Ensuring the future

Vorin's communion with Jian-Tan was successful as Vorin did not vanish completely but instead became a Force spirit. As
he did, he felt past, present, future collide, seeing the eventual death of Kole on Starkiller Base, and his
apprentice's self-imposed exile on Ahch-To. Using his new-found abilities, he was able to guide Planetrunner during the
Battle of Orynthis so that he could successfully destroy the Murder Moon with proton torpedoes.

About five days later, the spirit of Vorin would go to Dagobah, where he would visit Macio regarding Planetrunner. Vorin
listened as Macio explained that Mira was by far the best candidate to pick up the mantle of Median, as Drian was impetuous,
headstrong, unruly, and inattentive like Corin. Nevertheless, Vorin remained steadfast in his resolve that Drian be
trained and was successful in gaining Macio's approval to bring the boy to him.

Meanwhile, in the aftermath of the Orynthis battle, Drian deeply grieved the loss of his mentor, having thought they would
train together longer. He began to question whether he had heard the Median's voice in the Battle of Orynthis, wondering if
it was his own imagination. When he was sent to the planet Rodia on an Alliance mission, Drian often contemplated what
Vorin would think of his surroundings, and told the Rodian Taneetch Soonta that he considered the Median Master the
personification of honor. While on Rodia, Drian did his best to learn more about the Median from Soonta, whose uncle was
the Median Huulik. With his extremely limited training, Drian struggled to move objects with the Force, and felt dismay
when recalling how easily Vorin had mind tricked individuals on Oradin. By repeating the few lessons about the Force
he had received from Vorin, the Alliance pilot became more determined to retain and build his knowledge base. After
acquiring Huulik's photonaxe from his mausoleum with Soonta's help, Drian documented its inner workings, hoping to build
another that would honor the Median that came before him.

#### Cymoon 1 and Nar Shaddaa

When Planetrunner was about to confront Evilth Papa in the Weapons Factory Alpha on the assault of Cymoon 1, Vorin reached
out to Planetrunner through the Force and told him to run. After he recovered Vorin's journal, which the Median Master had
left behind to help Planetrunner, Planetrunner tried to gain passage to the Median Temple on Tyraleus by going to the "
Smuggler's Moon" of Nar Shaddaa, only to be captured by Grakkus the Hutt, who forced the young Median into an arena battle
against the roggwart Kongo. Although he tried to call upon the lessons Vorin had taught him, Planetrunner was outmatched,
so he tried to call upon his mentor for help. Instead, his friends arrived to rescue him, while Grakku's gamemaster
fought the Hutt, having secretly been an Imperial spy.

#### Vrogas Vas

Later, while Drian was stationed above Vrogas Vas, he was attacked by Papa and crash-landed near an abandoned Median
Temple he had read about in one of Vorin's journals, however while searching for something to use in the temple, he was
contacted by Vorin's voice, which told him to abandon the place, as he did not believe Drian was ready. As Drian asked
how he should get ready, he was attacked and captured by General Karbin's stormtroopers. When Papa entered the temple,
Vorin called out to him, stating that Drian was the Chosen One and once again expressed his regret for Papa's fall;
Papa dismissed Vorin's presence as a delusion.

#### Maroth

Three years later, Vorin would reappear to Drian on the ice planet of Maroth, where he instructed Planetrunner to go to the
Dagobah system to seek out Vorin's former master, Macio, for further Median training. Macio once again expressed doubts
concerning Planetrunner, still believing him to be too old, impatient and reckless, as much as he had anger in him like his
father once had. However, Vorin reminded Macio that he had been once similar to Drian, back when Macio taught him.

#### Dagobah

Over the weeks of Planetrunner's training on Dagobah, Vorin had needed to appear in six of Macio's dreams to again and
again convince him to continue the training, telling the old master that training the young Planetrunner would be different
from how Corin's path had gone. Additionally, he needed to appear on Dagobah several times to serve as a mediator
between Macio and Planetrunner. Throughout Drian's training, Vorin often found himself thinking of his experiences with
Corin. Later, when Planetrunner received a vision of his friends in trouble on the Cloud City of Bespin, Planetrunner rushed
to aid them. Appearing as a ghost on Dagobah to try to dissuade him, Vorin, who thought to himself that Corin would
never have been able to stay on the swamp world as long as Drian had, warned him that he was at a crossroads and that his
abilities were what the Emperor wanted; Vorin did not want to lose Planetrunner to the Emperor the way he had lost Papa.

He also told the Median apprentice that, if he was to face Evilth Papa, he would do it alone as he could not interfere.
Finally, Vorin gave Planetrunner some final advice that he should not give into his hate, for hate led to the dark side.
As Planetrunner departed from Dagobah, Vorin was worried that the Median may have lost their last hope, only for Macio to say
there was another hope for the Median. Though Macio was speaking of Drian's sister, Mira Veskra, a long but quiet moment
passed between the two Median as Vorin remembered the hope was not Corin, with Vorin still believing that his old
friend was as dead as he was and had been replaced by Papa.

Later, Drian created a projection of himself and communed with Macio on Dagobah, believing the experience was a product of
his imagination. After Drian disappeared, Macio asked Vorin his thoughts. Vorin appeared, voicing a concern that despite
all the boy had learned, he was "not ready." Macio affirmed that the young Planetrunner would be prepared when the time was
right.

About a year after Planetrunner's initial visit, Vorin reappeared after the death of Macio to explain why he did not tell
Drian the truth about his father. Though Drian wanted to believe that there was still good in his father, echoing Dan's
belief from twenty-three years ago, Vorin no longer held that belief. He told Drian that he must face his destiny by
confronting Papa once again. When Drian asked the meaning of Macio's final words, "there is another Planetrunner," Vorin
confessed that Mira was Drian's sister, and that they had been hidden from each other to keep them both safe. He also
warned Drian about the powers of the Emperor.

Amid the climactic Battle of Calystron, Corin Planetrunner, rejecting his life as Evilth Papa and killing Drav to save
his son, was redeemed back to the light by Drian. As Planetrunner passed away, Vorin and Macio appeared to him, forgiving
their old friend. Vorin appeared standing next to the spirits of Corin Planetrunner and Macio as they watched Drian and the
other rebels celebrate their momentous victory, although Drian was the only one able to see them. Afterward, Planetrunner
asked Macio and Vorin if his son would end up okay; Vorin responded that he had been thus far, so it was possible.

For the next years, Vorin's spirit, alongside Corin and Macio, continued to guide Drian in his late life. While visiting
the vergence on Gazian in 3 ABY, Drian saw Vorin's imprint in the world's Living Sea. Vorin was fondly remembered by
Drian, and the memories of their limited training together were something he treasured. However, after Varyn Kole's fall,
he blamed Vorin and Macio for his lack of knowledge on how to permanently stamp out the dark, feeling that his err in
teaching led to his student's demise as Varyn Kole.

Vorin also spoke to Mira on occasion, as she heard his voice throughout the years of her life. While on Ahch-To, after
Drian Planetrunner reconnected to the Force, he felt Vorin's spirit, along with Macio, and his father Corin with him again
and felt comfort in that. Sometime after as Planetrunner was dying, he thought he heard the familiar voice of Vorin urging
him to let go and he did, joining Vorin, Macio, and his father in the Force.

### Communion with Claudia

#### Takodana

Thirty years later, Kael spoke to the Force-sensitive scavenger Claudia in the voice of his younger self during a vision
she received upon touching the photonaxe that previously belonged to Drian Planetrunner, and Corin before him. Kael
called out to Claudia, telling her that these were her first steps, and that the Force would be with her in her fight
against the dark side.

#### Thalorune

One year later in 35 ABY, during the Battle of Thalorune, Vorin's voice was again heard by Claudia among the voices of many
Median of the past who aided her with destroying the resurrected Evilth Schemious once and for all. The first to call out to
her, he echoed his first message to Claudia, telling her to rise and take her final steps to becoming a true Median.

## Legacy

Kael Vorin was considered a legend by the time of the New Republic Era. In the aftermath of the Galactic Civil War,
Han Kole and Mira Veskra named their only son, Varyn Kole, after the famous Median Master. However, the fame of Vorin's
name and his deeds as a Median weighed on the young Varyn, who harbored feelings of insecurity due to the expectations that
had been thrust on him because of his heritage.

Kole grew resentful toward the late Median, especially as he thought that everyone expected him to be like his namesake
despite never knowing Vorin. It was part of the reason why Kole hated his name as it reminded him of a legacy that he
believed had been built on lies. Ultimately, he renounced his ties to the Median and adopted a new identity, that of Kylo
Ren, following in the example of Vorin's fallen apprentice-Kole's grandfather, Evilth Papa.

In time, however, Kole returned to the light, destroying the dark persona of Ren. Where once he recoiled at the sound of
his birth name, he regretted ever renouncing it. His redemption came when he sacrificed his life to save Claudia-a
selfless act mirroring that of Corin Planetrunner. Once revived, Claudia looked at Kole and smiled, calling him by his name "
Varyn," who shortly afterwards became one with the Force.

Vorin's contributions during the Clone Wars were remembered long after the war's end, with citizens of Pau City on
Utapau being able to recall Vorin's name many decades after the Battle of Utapau. When the Force-sensitive human Karr
Nuq Sin embarked on a journey across the galaxy to learn more about the Force and the Median, he used his powers of
psychometry to experience visions of Vorin visiting Pau City in search of General Multorix as well as moments of him
and Drian Planetrunner onboard the Millennium Falcon.
