# Personality and traits

Dan Liora Kestrel was a human female, who was 1.65 meters (5 feet, 5 inches) tall. She had brown hair, brown eyes
and light skin.

## Always a leader

Liora was compassionate to other people and stood her ground firmly for what she believed in. Liora was a very
persuasive and empathetic speaker during her time as a senator, especially when she opposed the escalation of the Clone
Wars. She even started to doubt the Republic when the Senate gave Drav more executive powers near the end of the
Clone Wars. Liora could no longer ignore the needs of her people and accepted Queen Reillata's offer to become senator
after two terms as queen. Due to her reputation as a beloved former monarch, Liora kept several privileges such as her
wardrobe and chrome-plated ships when she became a senator. For her wisdom, bravery, and compassion, she was admired by
her supporters. While she preferred to solve situations through diplomacy, she was not afraid to lead her allies into
battle if needed.

Liora was both responsible and strong-willed and did what she believed to be the right course of action despite
objections from those closest to her. She spoke her mind to the Senate and was able to convince the Gungans to unite
against the Trade Federation. Kael Vorin described her as fearless and stubborn, and a leader. He also noted that
Liora was wise, discerning and kindhearted, and that her daughter Mira had inherited these qualities from her. Liora
could be seen as fearless when she refused additional security to protect herself from assassins. She also did not like
the idea of leaving during the vote on the Military Creation Act. When Planetrunner came back into her life, Liora knew
that their future together would endanger their lives. However, she gave in and married Planetrunner in secret.

Liora's experiences during the Invasion of Nivellon left her with a hate of Neimoidians, but she also admitted it was
wrong of her to harbor a grudge against the entire species for the actions of the Trade Federation. As such, she
admitted to Sabe that she hated her prejudice as well. During the Clone Wars, she worked to better herself and overcome
her prejudice. Always at the center of the galaxy, Liora was a determined and brave survivor. As recognized by Seren
Ashoria, Liora was always thinking like a politician, focusing on gathering information and using her strengths to her
advantage. Watching with her own eyes as people died to the Clone Wars, Liora was horrified at the great losses the
Republic sustained in both lives and to its very democracy. Liora saw the Clone Wars as a conflict fought for the
Republic's democracy, to re-establish peace, and to protect the Republic's people from suffering, only for her to come
to question if the Republic that was emerging from the conflict would have such traits.

Despite her distrust of Rooka, Liora did believe in peaceful co-existence with the Separatist state. She worked
against increased military spending and prolonging the conflict. Indeed, she believed that an increase in clone troopers
would only escalate the destruction instead of ending it. However, she held nothing against the clones themselves, whose
brave service and sacrifices she honored. She supported the bravery of non-clone soldiers who fought for the Republic as
well. Hoping that at the very least their sacrifice would bring the galaxy closer to peace, she believed that clones who
died were people who had sadly been forced to waste their lives for a government that did not even guarantee their
personhood. By the end of the war, she had drafted several bills in favor of clone personhood for both during and after
the war, only for those works to be left abandoned after her death.

## Romance with Corin Planetrunner

When Liora and Corin first met, she thought that the nine-year-old boy from Oradin was an amusing but knowledgeable
child. However, by the end of the Invasion of Nivellon, Liora reciprocated Corin's friendship; she helped him to cope
with leaving his mother Shmi behind on Oradin while Planetrunner gave her a japor snippet so she could have good fortune.
After a lifetime of shielding her heart from romance, she reunited with Planetrunner ten years later and fell for him. The
two both made mistakes that revealed the truth of themselves to the other. Liora and Corin confessed their love to
each other during their stay at Nivellon and before the Battle of Geonosis. After the battle, they were later secretly
married. The couple didn't have time to figure their relationship out and were always stealing moments together in
between missions. Liora had once told Sabe about how she feared that any romantic relationship she entered would be
catastrophic, which was a description Sabe believed was accurate upon learning how the marriage developed in the wake of
events like the massacre of a Tusken tribe and the secrets it required of them both. Even despite Planetrunner's extreme
sense of justice, which Liora credited with why he had attacked the Tusken camp, she was unable to imagine not
marrying the man she had come to love.

Corin also showed extreme jealousy when Liora went undercover to investigate Rush Clovis, who had also harbored
romantic feelings for Liora. This did not fade the next time Corin saw him. When Corin saw the two in Liora's
apartment, he mistakenly believed that Liora was going to kiss Clovis and brutally attacked him. He later attempted to
apologize to Liora, but Liora was greatly upset by Corin's actions and the fact he didn't appear to trust her.
Liora claimed that their marriage was built on lies and that she no longer felt safe. She suggested that they
shouldn't see each other for a while before leaving. Later after realizing Clovis was inleage with Rooka and almost
dying on Scipio, Liora apologized for her words and took Corin back.

In the final days of the Clone Wars, Liora revealed that she was pregnant with Corin's child, with her making plans
to return home to Nivellon to fix up a room for the baby so that it could be born there. However, upon having nightmares
about his wife's death, Corin turned to the dark side of the Force in an attempt to save Liora and his child.

Upon discovering what her husband had done-aiding Drav in overthrowing the Median Order and reorganizing the
Republic into the First Galactic Empire-Liora confronted him on Dravion Prime where he tried to convince her to overthrow
Drav so that they could rule the galaxy together, Liora was horrified, claiming that she didn't know who Corin
was anymore. Corin then saw Kael, who had snuck aboard Liora's ship, and believing that his wife had betrayed him,
and ignoring her protests on the matter, strangled Liora with the Force until he was ordered to release her by
Kael.

After seeing what her husband had become, Liora lost the will to live and died after giving birth to their children,
with her finals words to Kael being her belief that there was still good in Corin. Her death haunted Planetrunner for
the rest of his life.

## Friendship with Sabe

Sabe was one of Liora's handmaidens during her time as queen. She served as Liora's decoy during the Invasion of
Nivellon. Sabe also became one of Liora's closest friends and most trusted advisors, viewing herself as the "right hand
of Dan Liora Kestrel." The two could communicate through looks and gestures. Sabe valued her friendship with
Liora above anything else in her life. While Sabe once advised Liora to find a romantic partner in someone who
understood her, a description that she herself matched, Sabe believed her friend would never return her feelings.
Liora herself experienced envy while witnessing Sabe's flirtations with Harli Jafan on several occasions. As she
watched Sabe and Jafan dance, she felt her feelings were not entirely jealousy, but nevertheless felt unsettled and
nervous at the prospect of asking her handmaiden for clarification about the two girls' budding relationship. The queen
also worried about her handmaiden liking the other girl more than her, which would require Sabe to keep secrets from
both.

Trying to find a balance they could never reach, Liora and Sabe developed their close bond over the course of years,
with Liora well aware of Sabe's devotion to her: Liora knew that Sabe would follow nearly any command she gave, even
if it was ordering Sabe to her own demise. Sabe was greatly upset when she learned of Liora's secret marriage to
Corin Planetrunner, who secretly scared Sabe, and was angered that her friend had not told her. She was also horrified to
learn about Corin's massacre of the Tusken Raiders and the fact that Liora still married him despite knowing about
it. This contributed to her decision to stop being Liora's handmaiden, as she couldn't handle her new isolated life
style and she no longer wished to be anyone's shadow.

After Liora's death, Sabe dedicated her life to solving the mystery of her death and formed the resistance group the
Liorans in her honor. She later confessed to Papa that she did not know why she acted like Liora really knew what
was best or if she really knew Papa at all, but decided to put her trust in her one final time and tried to see if her
final words about their being good in Papa were true.

## Relationship with Rush Clovis

During Liora's first year in the Galactic Senate, she grew close with Rush Clovis, a senator from Scipio. They
attended several senatorial committees together. When their relationship started to become romantically charged,
however, Liora chose to focus on her duties as senator and ended it, a decision to which Clovis reacted poorly,
ceasing talking with her.

During the Clone Wars, when Clovis was suspected by the Median Council of aiding the Separatist cause, Liora was sent to
gather information on his dealings. Clovis, in turn, believed that Liora wanted to resurrect their relationship and
brought her to a Separatist meeting. However, Lott Dod poisoned Liora to force Clovis in helping them financially.
With the help of Corin Planetrunner, Liora's husband, Clovis did all to ensure Liora's survival, yet Planetrunner left
him behind. In 19 BBY, Clovis manipulated Liora to expose the corruption of the InterGalactic Banking Clan so he could
be selected as head, but all went wrong. Realizing that Planetrunner loved Liora, Clovis let himself die in order to save
Liora.
