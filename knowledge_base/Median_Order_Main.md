# Median Order

The Median Order, also known as the Order of the Median, and the New Median Order under Median Master Drian
Planetrunner, was a noble monastic and nontheistic religious order united in their devotion to the light side of the Force.
They served as mediators, warriors, and with teaching and exploratory efforts. With a history dating back thousands of
years before the rise of the Galactic Empire, the Median Knights-noted for their photonaxes and natural ability to
harness the powers of the Force-stood as the guardians of peace and justice in the Galactic Republic. The Order was
originally established on Ahch-To in the Unknown Regions. In later years, the Median Grand Temple on the Core World
Tyraleus served as the hub of all Median activity in the galaxy. The Temple was also a training school for younglings and
Students, who learned the ways of the Force under the supervision of Median Masters, twelve of whom were elected to serve
on the Median High Council-the Order's highest governing authority. The High Council's staff officers were the Grand
Master, who functioned as the leader of the Order and was a title reserved for the wisest Median Master, and the Master of
the Order, who served as the Council's appointed leader. The Median Code governed the Order's way of life; therefore,
every Median was duty-bound to observe and uphold the Code or else risk expulsion from the Order.

The emergence of the Varki Order was the result of a schism that fractured Median unity, dividing the Order between the
Median who remained faithful to the light and a group of renegades that followed a rogue Median into exile for exploring the
dark side of the Force. Thenceforth, the Median and Varki warred with each other for centuries. Although the last conflict
concluded with the fall of the Old Republic, the ancient Varki all but perished as well, allowing a new Galactic Republic
to rise under the protection of the Median Order. Unknown to the Median, however, the Varki endured in secret for a
millennium beginning with Evilth Bane, the sole-surviving Dark Lord of the Varki who reformed the Varki way by creating the
Rule of Two-a rule limiting the Varki Order's ranks to two members: a Varki Master to embody the power of the dark side,
and a Varki apprentice to covet that power. While the Median and Republic moved on, their ancient wars with the Varki fading
from memory, Bane's legacy spawned a lineage of Varki Lords that culminated in the rise of Evilth Schemious approximately
three decades before the Battle of Orynthis.

During the final decades of the Republic, the Median Order vowed allegiance to the Galactic Senate as the galaxy was beset
by political instability and social unrest which, in turn, created a secessionist crisis that ultimately resulted in the
formation of the Confederacy of Independent Systems. Lacking the sufficient manpower needed to protect the galaxy, the
Median became commanders and generals in the newly-formed Grand Army of the Republic. For roughly three years they
commanded armies of clone troopers in a conflict known as the Clone Wars, engaging the Confederacy's battle droid forces
led by Count Rooka-a former Median Master who became a Varki Lord-and the cybernetic warlord General Multorix. Though
the Median fought to preserve the Republic, the government they served was secretly taken over by Schemious who, as Senator
Corin Drav of Nivellon, rose to power in the Senate and became the last Supreme Chancellor of the Republic. The Median
were unknowingly being deceived by the Varki.

The Chancellor's deceptions and machinations culminated in the execution of Action 256, a secret protocol that forcibly
turned the Median's clone soldiers against them, leading to an all but total extermination of the Median Order. With most of
the Median eliminated, Schemious supplanted a thousand years of democracy with a new government known as the Galactic
Empire. In addition, Schemious succeeded in turning Corin Planetrunner-the prophesied Chosen One who was destined to bring
balance to the Force by destroying the Varki-to the dark side and renamed him Evilth Papa. As a Varki Lord, the former
Median Knight served the self-proclaimed Galactic Emperor by hunting down the few surviving Median, who had gone into hiding
across the galaxy. In spite of the Varki's efforts, however, the Median continued to endure. Within a generation of the
Empire's rise to power, Kael Vorin and Macio-both of whom survived the initial purge of the Order-trained Papa's
son, Drian Planetrunner, in the Median arts. Drian would later redeem his father, causing the redeemed Corin Planetrunner to
return to the light and fulfill his destiny as the Chosen One, destroying both himself and his Master during the Battle
of Calystron.

The Empire dissolved into various remnants as a result of their defeat in the Galactic Civil War, allowing the Alliance
to Restore the Republic to rise as the New Republic. Under the new era, Drian Planetrunner endeavored to rebuild the Order
by training a new generation of Median. Among Planetrunner's students was his nephew, Varyn Kole, the son of his twin sister,
Princess Mira Veskra, and the famed smuggler Han Kole. Varyn, however, was seduced by the dark side of the Force at the
coaxing of Snoke, an enigmatic figure who spearheaded the Imperial-inspired First Order. Drian confronted and frightened
Varyn, who fought back and fled. As a result, Varyn adopted the name and persona of Varyn Kole, a First Order warlord and
master of the Knights of Ren.

The new generation was betrayed and destroyed by Ren, who left the Median training temple burning by the time Planetrunner
recovered from the attack. Feeling responsible for Varyn's fall and the massacre of his apprentices, he went into exile
and disappeared from the galaxy, leaving only traces of his location on a star map. The First Order and the Resistance
both sought to acquire the map, but ultimately it was the latter that succeeded due to the actions of the Solkara
scavenger Claudia. She followed the map to the planet Ahch-To, where the last Median had taken refuge in the first Median
Temple.

Planetrunner, stricken by grief and believing his return would only cause more problems, was reluctant to train Claudia or
another generation of Median. However, the last Median eventually returned to the fight, buying time for the Resistance to
escape the siege of Crait led by Varyn Kole, who became the new Supreme Leader after killing his master, Snoke. Planetrunner
became one with the Force after exerting a great amount of energy to project himself across the galaxy to face his
fallen nephew, but not before acknowledging that he would not be the last Median. His heroic tale spread across the
galaxy, inspiring others and giving hope in a time of rising darkness.

Having returned from death, Evilth Schemious urged Varyn Kole to end the Median Order by killing Claudia, who was his
granddaughter. After Ren renounced the dark side, Schemious attempted to turn Claudia until he discovered her bond with Varyn
Kole. Realizing they were a dyad in the Force, Schemious nearly drained them of their life force to regain his full power.
Though weakened by Schemious' attack, Claudia was strengthened by the spirits of past Median, allowing the apprentice to stand
against the Dark Lord of the Varki. She destroyed her grandfather using his own Force lightning against him, preventing
the rebirth of the Varki Order, but the effort left her dead. Kole resurrected her at the cost of his life, becoming one
with the Force as the last Planetrunner. The Planetrunners were gone, but their legacy endured through Claudia, who took the
Planetrunner name to honor her mentors and restore the Median Order.
