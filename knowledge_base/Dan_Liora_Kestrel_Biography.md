# Biography

## Early life

A human female of the Kestrel family, the young woman best remembered as Dan Liora was born Dan Kestrel on the
bountiful planet of Nivellon in 46 BBY. She was the younger daughter of Ruwee and Jobal Kestrel and the sister of Sola
Kestrel. Her father was a friend of Senator Onaconda Farr, who represented the solar system of Rodia in the Senate of
the Galactic Republic. Kestrel was very fond of the Rodian, whom she referred to as "Uncle Ono."

As a child, she baked every day and traveled with her father on relief missions to offer aid to suffering planets. When
Kestrel was seven, she traveled with her father and Farr to Shadda-Bi-Boran to help relocate the population in the wake
of an ecological disaster. Unfortunately, the effort resulted in failure, as the Shadda-Bi-Boran could not adapt to
their new homeworld. It led her to declare later in life that she would try 10,000 times in order to save one person.

## Rise to queenship

Although her parents were of modest origins, Kestrel was identified as a gifted and brilliant young girl at an early
age. She devoted herself to public service, joining the Apprentice Legislature at the tender age of eight, and she
eventually became a junior senatorial adviser. She attended the Theed Royal Academy to learn how to become a political
leader.

At age twelve, Kestrel participated in the Legislative Youth Program, in which she met a slightly older boy named Palo
Jemabie. The two fell in love, but their relationship did not last, as they parted ways when Jemabie left politics and
chose to become an artist. At the same age, she started a two-year mandate as supervisor of Theed, the capital city of
Nivellon.

Two years later, Dan Kestrel began her campaign to become the next Queen of Nivellon. She campaigned herself as "
Candidate Liora," with anonymity being customary for candidates and monarchs. She portrayed herself as the calm and
composed candidate. Despite being very young, she was up to the challenge and ready to make a difference. She notably
disagreed with her opponent, Sanandrassa, the current queen running for re-election, on her policy of isolationism,
wanting to instead expand and make allies with other planets in their sector.

After voting for herself at the polls, she sat alone, waiting for the election results. Captain and Head of Security
Quarsh Panaka walked in and tell her that she had won the election and her work had begun. Meanwhile, Governor Sio
Bibble announced to the planet's population that Candidate Liora had won. She adopted Liora as her regnal name.
Jemabie created a poster entitled Nivellon Coronation to publicize her coronation as Monarch of Nivellon. Although she was
not the youngest sovereign ever elected, it had been a long time since a Nivellon queen had been as young as her, and she
would later question her own maturity at the time of her election.

Liora met with and inspected Panaka and his newly appointed Royal Guard, as was traditional after an election, before
meeting with Governor Bibble. Later, Liora ate and discussed Panaka's recruits for a revived form of the position of
royal handmaiden, who would serve on staff as assistants, bodyguards, and decoys. Liora suggested having more than one
in her retinue, and Panaka readily agreed. As she only used her new last name of "Liora" as Queen, she would be free
to act as a handmaiden named Dan whenever one of the handmaidens acted as "Liora." That night, Liora used her new
personal comm to contact her parents, who gave her their congratulations. Although Liora had remained composed and
calm throughout the day, in private, she grinned with excitement.

The next day, Liora met Panaka's first recruit, Tsabin. She looked very similar to Liora and Panaka noted that she
could also be a body double. Liora granted Tsabin's request to stay in a room in the conservatory instead of in the
barracks and the other guards and allowed Tsabin to teach her about keeping her face expressionless and still, something
she was accustomed to due to her earlier career as a hallikset player. Liora later told her new handmaiden that she
was working on her persona as the queen, which Tsabin vowed to work on mimicking.

## Life as queen

### Coronation

At the end of the week, the official coronation had arrived. Liora dressed for the part and wore the traditional royal
makeup for the first time. After Governor Bibble passed her the royal scepter that was indicative of her rule, the
now-ex Queen Sanandrassa gave her a tour of the Theed Royal Palace. After attending her first meeting with various
government officials, she accepted congratulations from Senator Corin Drav, who contacted her from Tyraleus. After
he disconnected, Sanandrassa advised Liora that Drav was her best resource. After settling in that night, Liora
requested that there be no guards in her room, much to Panaka's dismay. Tsabin stayed in the room with her as her only
security.

### Meeting her handmaidens

The next day, Panaka introduced the rest of her new handmaidens: Rabene Tonsort, Eirtama Ballory, Suyan Higin, and
Sashah Adova. The handmaidens pledged themselves to Liora and decided to change their names for anonymity and to
confuse others. Tonsort became Rabe, the wardrobe mistress. Ballory became Eirtae and was in charge of communications.
Higin and Adova became Yane and Sache respectively, serving as liaison and page, and Tsabin became Sabe, the queen's
closest bodyguard and body double. When in danger, Liora would disguise herself as one of her staff of five loyal
handmaidens and would introduce herself using her birth name of Dan Kestrel, while her decoy Sabe assumed the persona
of Queen Liora.

After their first week together, the handmaidens practiced with Sabe as the queen. With Sabe in the queen's place and
Liora posing as "Dan," who was officially listed as another page, the group traveled to the Queen's library to test
their disguise. As Sabe interacted with Captain Panaka, several of the handmaidens thought that Panaka saw through their
disguise. At one point, Liora oversaw a decision to allowing many of the crop fields on Nivellon to rot in order to avoid
overfarming and to aid the nitrogen cycle.

As the handmaidens began to grow closer, Liora decided that it would be more beneficial for them to learn skills from
each other instead of simply growing to act and speak like each other. Liora requested Panaka to provide them with
several complicated lockplates to practice on. Liora would tour the agricultural province, accompanied by Governor
Bibble, her agricultural advisor Graf Zapalo, and her economic advisor Horace Vancil. On the tour, Liora had to take
painkillers when her period caused her considerable pain, which also forced Sabe into a position to double her. Their
tour saw Liora visit many of the fields that she had decided to allow to rot. The tour lasted over five days, which
saw Liora convene with the minister of finance as well as discuss buying grain to make up for the lost harvest. Zapalo
told her of their plans to negotiate the best deal with multiple worlds that had contacted them, but Liora told him to
also approach the nearby worlds of Karlinus and Jafan, which would begin Liora's goal of opening their planet to
neighboring worlds.

### Chommell sector summit

Liora continued her plans to reach out to their counterparts in the sector and would later call a meeting with the
governor and several advisors to announce her plans to host a summit at the Royal Palace. In two weeks, she wanted
politicians from nearby planets to meet with her to discuss trade, relations, and the like. The minister of internal
affairs as well as Zapalo were confused by the proposition, but Liora held firm her desire the host the event.

After extensive preparations, the guests began to arrive. Despite most of the governments representing the thirty-some
planets in the sector declining to attend, around a dozen guests arrived, including Governor Kelma of Karlinus, Harli
Jafan, the daughter of Jafan's planetary director, Tobruna, whose mother was in charge of the refineries on the mining
world of Kreeling, Nitsa Tulin who was representing the planet Chommell Minor, and Olan Carrus, a representative from
Behpour. Liora would wear one of her fanciest outfits, which had been designed by Eirtae, for the state dinner.

Later, the guests convened in the throne room to discuss relations, with Harli proposing ideas regarding increased
collaboration between Jafan and Nivellon. Tobruna proposed changing an outdated treaty that forced Kreeling to sell ore to
Nivellon at a fixed price that was not fair, to which Liora compassionately responded that she would attempt to change
the trend of using the ore unnecessarily to decorate the instruments such as the hallikset. Kelma commented on Karlinus'
labor shortage, offering to hire Nivellon workers to take seasonal jobs on the planet in addition to young students taking
apprenticeships on her planet. Liora, remembering that her sister Sola had completed an apprenticeship, agrees that
resurrecting the program would be beneficial, to which Kelma promised to help with the grain shortage in return.

Back in her suite, Sabe revealed that Harli had invited her and her fellow handmaidens to attend a Neurotransmitter
Affection concert at the Theed Odeon that night, offering tickets she had due to her cousin being a backup singer with
the famous band. When all of the girls, save for Sache, expressed their interest in going, they formed a plan. Sabe,
Yane, and Eirtae would go with Harli, while Rabe would take Queen Liora to the library and then escape out the window
using a grappling hook, where Rabe and Dan would meet the group. After successfully sneaking out, Liora and her
friends made it to the concert, where Liora noted her own jealousy at seeing Sabe and Harli dance together. Sabe then
left Harli's side to dance with Liora instead, much to Liora's relief, only for Harli to interrupt the two girls to
offer them glitter-lits. Harli accidentally spilled a glitter-lit on Liora, staining her clothes and hands with the
glittering chemicals inside. Liora refused Yane's offer to return home, then wistfully noted that every time she
caught sight of Sabe for the rest of the night, Sabe was laughing.

Panaka figured out that they had disappeared after talking to Sache. Liora felt her comm vibrate and Sache appeared,
telling them that their cover had been blown. Liora and Rabe returned to the palace, where a furious Panaka confronted
them. However, Liora was not going to let him, unleashing the full brunt of her emotions, telling the captain to trust
that her handmaidens could protect her and saying that she would do some things that he wouldn't like, but he would have
to accept it.

The next day, Liora learned that the glitter would not come off of her hands. Despite trying to use makeup to conceal
it, they were forced to allow Sabe to double for her again so Harli would not recognize her from the concert. Liora
listened to the meeting as a page, but when she had to run a message to the kitchen, Harli spotted her. Thinking she was
Sabe, Harli admitted that she was interested in her and asked to kiss her, which caused Liora to make a quick excuse
and flee.

That evening, Sabe confronted Liora, telling her that she'd received an angry message from Harli. Liora tried to
explain that she had had to protect her identity, but Sabe was mad and told her that she needed to consider someone's
feelings besides her own, a comment that hurt Liora deeply. Later that night, Liora asked if Sabe had really wanted
to kiss Harli, but Sabe said that she didn't want to talk about it yet.

## Crisis on Nivellon

### The blockade

After a farewell tea, the guest began to depart. As the last ships left, a buoy in the planet's atmosphere was
triggered. Panaka realized it was a large group of ships approaching. Realizing it was an emergency, Liora assembled
Panaka, Governor Bibble, and her handmaidens in the palace, she received a transmission from Trade Federation Viceroy
Nute Gunray. Gunray stated that he required her to sign a treaty before threatening her and ending the transmission.
Liora swiftly ordered Panaka to ready the Security Force to figure out what Gunray's angle was. After analyzing the
proposed treaty, Liora realized that the treaty was not even a serious attempt and was simply a document that signed
away Nivellon to Trade Federation control for nothing in return, simply legitimizing the invasion in the eyes of the
Republic. Contacting Senator Drav, he explained that the Trade Federation was involved in a conspiracy in the
Senate to shift a tax on trade routes they used, with Nivellon being the main planet in their proposed operation. By the
time Liora finished with Drav, Panaka alerted her that the Trade Federation fleet had blockaded the planet.

Being blockaded made Liora tense and fretful. Determined to not succumb to Trade Federation pressure, she vowed to not
sign the treaty and instead worked to organize defenses. Gunray continued to demand that Liora come to meet him on his
ship instead of sending living representatives to Nivellon, so Liora continued to reject each droid that approached them.
When the blockade prevented an agricultural transport from Karlinus from bringing grain, Liora was counseled by
Zapalo, who told her that due to their situation with the farmlands, the planet was running low on food, saying that the
public food available would probably only sate the population for a week. Liora was comforted by Bibble, who said
ambassadors from Supreme Chancellor Finis Valorum would be arriving the next day.

### Invasion

As the ambassadors arrived, the Trade Federation sent a large invasion force of battle droids. Liora watched
helplessly as pilots from the Nivellon Royal Space Fighter Corps were killed in their futile attempt to prevent the landing
craft from deploying their droid army. Queen Liora was captured along with the rest of the population of Theed and
placed Camp Four. It was while en route to the camp that she was rescued by two Median, Jian-Tan Drill and his apprentice,
Kael Vorin. The two had been Valorum's ambassadors charged with negotiating a truce with the Trade Federation, but
Gunray had tried to kill them as soon as they had arrived. The Median freed the queen and her entourage from the clutches
of the Federation's battle droids, and the group fled the blockaded Nivellon aboard Queen Liora's royal starship. While
trying to break the blockade of Federation battleships deployed around the planet, the ship was damaged. Its hyperdrive
generator needed to be repaired, thanks to the astromech droid, B0-U5, the only surviving member of the group, which
prompted the crew to make an emergency landing on Oradin.

While travelling to Oradin, Dan, still undercover, took to cleaning the droid and, as Jar Jar Binks walked in, asked
the small droid whether "the Queen" should add making peace with the Gungans to her list of priorities. When B0-U5's
response sounded like an agreement, she promised it would become one of their future policies. The queen, who was
curious about the desert world, remained in her disguise as Dan so she could accompany the Median on the planet's
surface. Queen Liora recognized that she had not fooled Master Jian-Tan for a moment, but he went along with her story,
which allowed her to continue fooling everyone else. The queen ventured with her companions into the spaceport of Mos
Espa, which Jian-Tan Drill described as a haven for "those who do not wish to be found." While trying to negotiate for
spare parts with the junk dealer Watto, the party met Corin Planetrunner, a nine-year-old slave, along with his mother,
Shmi Planetrunner, and his incompletely built protocol droid, B8-6TQ. The human boy was immediately fascinated by Liora's
beauty, asking her whether she was an angel from the moons of Iego. In turn, the queen thought the boy was a bit odd,
yet charming.

Since the Median and the queen could not raise the money needed to purchase the repair parts for the royal ship, Planetrunner
offered to compete in the incoming Boonta Eve Classic podrace so he could earn the required funds for them. Master Drill
promptly accepted the offer, but Liora criticized his actions; although she did like Planetrunner, she thought it was
unwise to place the fate of Nivellon in the hands of a nine-year-old boy. However, Planetrunner managed to win the race,
beating the reigning champion Sebulba against all odds, and Liora joined the scenes of jubilation that followed the
boy's victory in the Grand Arena of Mos Espa. Thanks to a bargain Jian-Tan Drill had made with Watto, Planetrunner was freed
from slavery and allowed to accompany the Median in their travel. On the way to the galactic capital of Tyraleus,
Liora, still in the guise of the handmaiden Dan, helped Planetrunner cope with the loss of his mother, Shmi, whom Watto
had refused to let go.

### Call for new leadership

Once the group arrived on Tyraleus, Liora resumed her role as queen. In preparation for her public appearance before
the Republic Senate, she met with Senator Drav, who represented the Nivellon system in that governing body. While she
wished to plead for the Senate to rescue her homeworld, Drav informed her that the assembly was too corrupt to care
about their situation. The senator then encouraged Liora to move for a Vote of No Confidence in the leadership of
Chancellor Valorum. Valorum was a well-meaning politician, and the queen initially refused to move against him. However,
upon witnessing the problems of politics during the Senate session in which she pleaded, Liora ended up following
Drav's advice, asking for Valorum's ousting from office. An election was decided, and Drav himself emerged as
a likely candidate to succeed the disgraced Valorum. Although the senator asked to stay on Tyraleus until the end of
the elective process, Liora decided to return to Nivellon, intent on retaking her world without the Senate's help.

### Fight for Theed

En route, the queen revealed to a concerned Master Drill that she planned to unite with the Gungans, the sentient
amphibians native to Nivellon. However, Drill and his apprentice Vorin had met the Gungans at the beginning of the
invasion, and the natives had expressed their lack of interest for the fate of the Nivellon humans. Having once again
assumed the identity of a handmaiden, the queen journeyed through the swamps of Nivellon with her party. They managed to
locate the sacred place where the Gungans had taken refuge after evacuating their city of Otoh Gunga. Initially, the
decoy queen Sabe tried to negotiate with Boss Nass, leader of the Gungans, but to little avail. As a sign of trust, the
real Liora decided to reveal her identity, which surprised not only the Gungans, but also all of her companions. The
queen then knelt in front of Boss Nass, asking for help in battling the Federation troops. Impressed by the queen's
respectful attitude, Nass heartily agreed.

Liora and her guards infiltrated the palace, using grappling hooks to avoid a force of droids before storming the
throne room and capturing the deceptive Gunray. Though the queen's plan ended up working perfectly, it did not come
about without loss. On the grassy plains of Nivellon, there were Gungan casualties until Corin Planetrunner destroyed the
Federation's Droid Control Ship. Additionally, Master Jian-Tan Drill was killed during his duel with the Varki Lord Evilth
Tal. Now that Nivellon was freed, Liora greeted Drav, who came to announce to her that he had been elected Supreme
Chancellor, since many had voted for him in an outpouring of sympathy. In the immediate aftermath of the invasion,
Liora paid her respects to Drill at his ritual cremation in Theed's Funeral Temple. Before the Median funeral began, she
knelt beside Vorin when he was alone with his master's body and asked him if he was ready, then gave him more time as
he requested. Finally, a huge parade was held in the Nivellon capital to celebrate the official unification of the humans
and Gungans. During the ceremony, which was attended by Chancellor Drav, the members of the Median Council, Kael
Vorin, and Corin Planetrunner, Liora handed over a symbolic orb to an overjoyed Boss Nass as a symbol of peace between
the long-warring peoples. As the square erupted with applause, Liora shared an unseen smile with Corin.

## Continued service as Queen

### Nivellon's recovery

After the invasion of Nivellon, Queen Liora led the planet's rebuilding and recovery efforts. The process was slow as
they did not seek external aid to recover from the damage of the conflict. Food surpluses recovered by the end of
Liora's term. Additionally, the Nivellon had to decide whether or not to install defensive weaponry on the planet. This
decision divided the Nivellon. Queen Liora decided to add an ion pulse cannon to the surface. Captain Panaka publicly
opposed the addition of an ion pulse and advocated for firmer measures and increased defense. This divide caused a rift
between Liora and Panaka, a rift that would never heal. Liora's parents were furious that Nivellon installed any
weaponry at all.

Every year following the invasion, the citizens of Nivellon held a memorial for those who had fallen in defense of the
planet. Liora lit a stick of incense for Master Jian-Tan yearly.

### Search for a Gungan relic

Sometime later, Jar Jar Binks called to Queen Liora, presenting to her a map to what he described as a powerful Gungan
artifact. Binks asked Liora to help him find it, to which she hesitated, not wanting to intrude on Boss Nass'
territory. Binks reassured her that it was Nass' idea to involve her, so she agreed, bringing weapons on Panaka's
suggestion. Liora asked where the map lead first, but Binks could not understand the map, as it was written in Old
Gungan, so he assumed the map lead to a vine patch. When they entered the vine patch, Liora realized that the vines
were not vine, but were, in fact, slitherfish. They were trapped, as kaadu they were riding wouldn't turn around.
Liora didn't want to shoot the slitherfish, as this was their home, so she shot her blaster into the tree canopy to
let in light and scare the slitherfish away without hurting them. As they continued outside, they reached their next
stop on the map, a rope bridge. Binks ran across the bridge, breaking all the wooden pieces until the bridge was just
rope. Luckily, Liora brought an ascension gun that allowed to her to grapple herself to the other side of the bridge
and walk across. Finally, they both entered a dark cave, where they had to crawl through small corridors to get to the
artifact. Thinking that it was too easy, Liora grabbed the unguarded soshinyday blobball, before a gundark jumped out.
Liora tried to calm Binks down, but he screamed very loudly, inadvertently incapacitating the gundark, the loud noise
damaging its sensitive ears. Returning to Otoh Gunga, Liora thanked Binks for the adventure, but commented that the
Soshinyday blobball didn't seem very powerful. Upon giving the artifact to Boss Nass, he took a bite out of it,
revealing it to be a Gungan delicacy rather than an artifact. When Nass asked Binks to get another, Liora watched as
he screamed like he had in the cave.

### Helping a sando aqua monster

After a sando aqua monster started attacking the royal boathouse on Nivellon, Queen Liora arrived to investigate. Two
Nivellon Palace Guards reported to her, saying they didn't know how to handle the creature, as they didn't normally come
near land. Reasoning that there must be a reason for the creature's attack, Liora and the three guards entered the
boathouse. There, they found poachers raising a baby sando aqua monster locked in a cage. Liora shoots the rope
suspending the baby, freeing it. As it ran to the door, Liora ordered the guards to let the baby return to its mother
and to arrest the poachers. As they all watched as the baby returned to its mother, the guards apologized for not
figuring out the situation sooner, to which Liora replied that the best solution isn't always the most obvious one.

## Service as senator

### Queen, no more

Liora served two terms as queen, and by the conclusion of her second term in 28 BBY, the Nivellon had grown so fond of
Liora that a faction tried to amend the constitution to allow her another term. However, Liora declined this and
stepped aside for her elected successor, Reillata, though Liora privately supported Jamillia in the election. Not long
after her coronation, Reillata asked Liora to serve as the representative for Nivellon and the other worlds in the
Chommell sector in the Galactic Senate, a request that Liora ultimately accepted.

Captain Mariek Panaka and Sergeant Gregar Typho accepted Liora's request to accompany her to Tyraleus. She hired
three new handmaidens to act as Senate aides, Versaat, Cordyn, and Dorra, who later took the names Verse, Corde, and
Dorme. She also requested that Sabe and Tonra go to Oradin and look into ending slavery there, as well as find and
free Shmi Planetrunner.

### Gunray's assassination attempt

When Liora first arrived on Tyraleus as a senator, holonews stations, such as TriNebulon News, questioned her
loyalties and potential. Liora entered the Senate in the same year as Senator Rush Clovis of Scipio, and as a result
they went through the Senate's six-week orientation program together. Dan was led in this orientation by a Republic
protocol droid, NON-3. Towards the end of these six weeks, NON-3 was reprogrammed, likely by the Trade Federation, to
lead Dan into a lower level of the Senate Building scheduled for demolition. Luckily, Senator Bail Veskra of Eryxara
saw her and saved her from being killed just in time, though she initially mistrusted him coincidentally being in the
same area. The next day, during her first Senate session, she was laughed at and seen as incompetent, since the Trade
Federation had paid TriNebulon News and other holojournalist outlets to report on her being unable to read directions
and getting lost.

After the session, she and Clovis attended a get-together hosted by Chancellor Drav, where she was introduced to
Senator Mina Bonteri of Onderon. Bonteri's guidance and advice was the first helpful presence Dan felt since arriving
on Tyraleus, and quickly considered Bonteri a friend and mentor. Realizing that her persona created as Queen Liora
would only hinder her efforts as a senator, she and her handmaidens started creating a new persona to suit her new
position. During this time, she realized that Chancellor Drav wasn't as welcoming as he had originally seemed,
especially when he refused to allow her onto his antislavery committee, knowing that she cared deeply about the issue.
She soon summoned Sabe and Tonra back to Tyraleus, asking them to look into the Trade Federation's attempt against her
life and reputation, and in the meantime joined the faction spearheaded by Bail Veskra and Mon Mothma, two of the most
pacifistic members of the Senate known for opposing Drav, something that would help Dan step out of the
Chancellor's shadow. Soon after Bail allowed her onto his transportation committee, she found out from Sabe that her
suspicions of the Trade Federation manipulating TriNebulon News were accurate.

### Making new allies

Despite her new professional relationship with Veskra and Mothma, it took a while for them to fully trust her. It wasn't
until a party hosted by Mothma towards the end of Dan's second month as senator that Dan finally proved herself
worthy of their trust. Veskra, Mothma, and Onaconda Farr intended to secretly discuss Mina Bonteri's suspicious
activity, and planned to use Dan's presence at the party as a distraction to do so. Having found out about the planned
meeting from Sabe and her sources, Dan decided to switch places with Sabe in order to hear their conversation. When
they decided to bring Dan in on their suspicions, she attempted to switch back, but Veskra saw them doing so and
realized the truth. Afterwards, he and Mothma thanked her for the intentional distraction, though Dan refused to spy
on her friend Mina for them unless she heard something truly shocking.

Over the course of the next six months, for the remainder of her first Senate term, Dan achieved much success,
continuing to join committees and work with Veskra and Mothma, who in turn used their own contacts to force TriNebulon
News into going easier on Dan in the tabloids. She found herself having heated but friendly debates with Mothma over
the ongoing piracy in the Mid Rim, Dan being less pacifistic than her. At the end of those six months, Veskra invited
Dan and her retinue to Eryxara on behalf of Queen Breha Veskra to spend a week of their furlough before returning
home to Nivellon. Dan readily accepted, and found that week one of the most relaxing and illuminating of her life so far,
having many candid conversations with Breha about being a queen and deciding whether or not to have children. When she
returned to Nivellon, she spent the remainder of her furlough with her family, including her sister Sola's newborn child
Ryoo.

### Mission to Bromlarch

The day that Dan and her retinue returned to Tyraleus for her second Senate term, a planetwide catastrophe occurred
on Bromlarch, the planetary aqueduct system being destroyed by seismic activity and threatening the entire farming
planet's harvest. Count Rooka secretly planned to have the Trade Federation offer corporate aid to the planet, similar
to the invasion of Theed four years prior, and Mina followed his instructions in order to achieve that goal. She,
Clovis, and Dan volunteered to go to Bromlarch and assess the damage, Median Master Depa Billaba accompanying them, and
afterward Bromlarch's senator Caelor Gaans felt no other choice but to accept due to Drav's seeming apathy over the
situation.

Dan refused to accept this, and started working on an alternative, and Clovis volunteered to help due to his financial
wisdom. After three days of sleepless work, Dan presented her own motion to the Senate for the first time, the Mid Rim
Cooperation Motion. The Motion was beneficial to dozens of Mid Rim systems beside just Bromlarch, engineered to sway the
votes of important voting blocs such as Malastare's senator Aks Moe. The Motion passed with almost no opposition, and
within a week Dan, her retinue, and Master Billaba were able undertake a relief mission there and transport the
permacrete needed to repair the aqueducts on Bromlarch, though they first had to fend off a group of pirates tipped off
to their flight path by either Mina or Drav. The success of the Motion gained Dan the trust of Mothma, but cost
her her friendship with Mina, since she was directly responsible for Mina failing to follow Rooka's orders. In the
aftermath of the Motion being passed, Clovis attempt to begin a romantic relationship with Dan, but she declined,
instead wanting to focus on politics and the work at hand.

### Separatist Crisis

Ten years after the Invasion of Nivellon, former Median Count Rooka became the public face of a Confederacy of Independent
Systems that had withdrawn from the Republic in opposition to the corruption they believed had enveloped the Senate. In
response to the threat posed by this Separatist Alliance, the Republic Senate debated the proposed Military Creation Act
that would allow Supreme Chancellor Drav to formally commission a Grand Army of the Republic. During this time,
Liora became a member of the Chancellor's Loyalist Committee and was regarded as the leader of the opposition to the
Military Creation Act.

On the eve of the vote in the Senate over the Military Creation Act, Liora arrived on Tyraleus. Upon landing, her
cruiser was hit in an assassination attempt by bounty hunter Zam Wesell, who had been hired to kill Liora by Jango
Fett, himself under the employee of Count Rooka. The explosion killed several of her guards as well as Liora's double,
Corde. At the request of Supreme Chancellor Drav, the Median Council assigned Median Knight Kael Vorin and Student
Corin Planetrunner to protect Liora and investigate the incident, though Liora objected to the added security. That
evening, Liora met with Vorin and Planetrunner in her penthouse apartment. With several years having passed, Planetrunner
was nervous to meet Liora and while she hid her feelings, Vorin noted that the senator was pleased to see Planetrunner
as well. That night, Zam Wesell tried again to kill Liora by releasing two deadly kouhuns into her apartment using a
droid. Vorin and Planetrunner sensed the attack and rushed into the bedroom. Planetrunner managed to kill the kouhuns, and
Vorin jumped through the bedroom window and grabbed on to the droid as security officers rushed into the apartment.
Vorin and Planetrunner, after a chase through the Tyraleus skylanes, were able to capture Zam Wesell. Before she could
reveal the plot behind the assassination attempts on Liora, Wesell was killed by Jango Fett using a Kamino dart.

In the aftermath of continued attacks, Liora was convinced to leave Tyraleus and to return to Nivellon. While Planetrunner
went with her to continue protecting her, Vorin continued the investigation into the assassination attempts. She,
Planetrunner, and B0-U5 departed aboard the AA-9 Freighter-Liner Jendirian Valley, a refugee ship leaving from the
western spaceport of Port District. Upon arriving on Nivellon, Liora and Planetrunner visited her family and grew closer in
their relationship, leading to Planetrunner telling her of his love for her. However, since Planetrunner was committed to the
Median Order and Liora to the Senate, they both agreed to forgo pursuing a more romantic relationship. While on Nivellon,
Planetrunner started experiencing Force visions of his mother in danger. Convinced he had to save her, he determined to
travel to Oradin. Liora traveled with him, and upon their arrival at the Lars moisture farm, Planetrunner discovered
that his mother had been taken by Tusken Raiders. He was able to track them down, but was too late, and his mother died
in his arms. Angered, he slaughtered the entire village including the women and children. He returned to the homestead
to bury his mother. Later, Planetrunner confessed to Liora his despair and anger and what he had done, and she attempted
to comfort him.

### War and marriage

Meanwhile, Vorin had traced Jango Fett to the planet Geonosis and discovered that Count Rooka was behind the
assassination attempts. He also learned that the Separatists had built a droid army to attack the Republic and that the
Republic had at its disposal an army of clone troopers supposedly commissioned by the long-deceased Median Master
Sifo-Dyas. Requesting that Planetrunner relay his message to Tyraleus, Vorin informed the Median and the Senate what he had
discovered, just before he was captured. The Median decided to send an assault team, while Planetrunner was ordered to stay
with Liora. Planetrunner was torn over the desire to rescue his master yet also protect the senator, but Liora decided
to help Planetrunner by traveling to Geonosis, forcing Planetrunner to accompany her. However, their rescue was a failure and
they were captured. Before they were led to the arena to be executed, Liora confessed her love for Planetrunner,
reasoning that there was no point in denying it before they died.

The execution of Liora, Planetrunner, and Vorin was to be carried out by several creatures brought into the arena: a
reek, an acklay, and a nexu. The nexu attacked Liora, roaring and climbing the pillar she was on. She tried fending
the beast off with her chains, but the nexu shrugged off her blows and slashed its claw across Liora's back, injuring
her. This tore the waistband and up of her clothing, and Liora screamed and sobbed in pain as tears trickled down her
cheek. The nexu came back, and Liora nearly fell, but used her chains to swing at the nexu and kick it off. She
climbed back up her pillar but thanks to Corin and the reek, she was saved, as Corin rammed the reek into the nexu.
Dan jumped onto the reek, behind Corin and kissed his cheek. When they were released and attacked, Median Master Mace
Windu arrived and the Median revealed themselves, only for Rooka to respond by unleashing the Geonosian droid army. A
full-out battle erupted; although she had been wounded by the Nexu, Liora quickly grabbed a blaster from a downed B1
battle droid and fought side by side with the Median.

Of the two hundred Median who had arrived, only a few were left and the situation seemed lost. At that point, Master Macio
arrived with the newly christened Grand Army of the Republic, and the Battle of Geonosis began in earnest. Planetrunner,
Vorin, and Liora pursued Count Rooka in a Republic gunship, but Liora and a clone trooper were knocked from the
troop bay by a cannon blast. They were able to call backup, but arrived too late to stop Rooka, who fought Planetrunner and
Vorin before being defeated by Macio. After the battle, Planetrunner and Liora returned to Nivellon, where they were
secretly married. With Planetrunner able to remain on Nivellon for a few short days to recover from his injuries sustained on
Geonosis, the couple were able to enjoy a honeymoon and a time after it together before needing to return to the jobs
that would take them across a wartorn galaxy.

## The Clone Wars

### Mission to rescue POWs

Early in the war, Liora assisted Master Vorin, Commander Cody and their troops in finding clone troopers that had
been taken as prisoners of war to Krystar. Liora heard about the POWs from Representative Vishar Koss who provided her
with the intel. Upon her arrival on Krystar, Liora and Vorin met with Regent Queb under the guise of a diplomatic aid
mission. Koss assured Queb that Liora was there to look into Krystar's agricultural problems. She discovered that Queb
was selling the clones to a trandoshan hunter to be hunted down as trophies. She and Representative Koss left Krystar
with Vorin and the prisoners.

### Negotiations with the Hutts

Around the time of the Battle of Teth, Drav pushed for several new security measures for Nivellon, citing the eruption
of several new battles across the Outer Rim. The measures came to the attention of Liora, who was also told of the new
battles by Typho. In response, she set up a meeting with the Chancellor to discuss his security plans. As she arrived to
his office and bid hello to the departing Master Macio, however, she was quickly informed by Drav that one of these
engagements, the Battle of Teth, had involved Planetrunner and Vorin. Liora was then informed that Jabba the Hutt
believed Planetrunner had kidnapped his son, Rotta, and that he was refusing to negotiate with the Republic. Suddenly
focused on her husband's safety instead of Drav's new security measures, Liora then offered to negotiate with
Jabba through his uncle, Ziro, who lived on Tyraleus at his palace. Liora went there to speak to him, but he refused
to listen to her.

Liora sneaked back into his palace, where she overheard Ziro speaking with Count Rooka, promising that Rotta,
Planetrunner, and his apprentice would be eliminated, that the Median would bring Jabba to justice and Ziro would become head
of the Hutt Clan. Liora was discovered and captured by Ziro's droids and was promised to Rooka as his prisoner.
Liora was taken to the palace dungeon, where she was able to trick the droids into activating her communicator,
alerting her assistant B8-6TQ to her predicament before the droids destroyed it. Ziro, seeing that Liora was too
dangerous to be kept alive, decided to execute her knowing his powerful friends in the Senate would protect him. Liora
was then rescued by B8-6TQ, who brought in Tyraleus Guard Commander Fox and his troopers. Liora then subdued one of
her guards, took its weapon, and held Ziro at gunpoint. She contacted Jabba, informed him of his uncle's treachery, and
ensured a treaty between the Republic and the Hutts.

### Trap on the Malevolence

Liora was then sent on a mission by Drav to negotiate with a representative of the Banking Clan about withdrawing
from the war. She, along with B8-6TQ, traveled to the rendezvous. However, Liora learned that it was all a trap when
she came out in the middle of a battle between the Republic fleet and the Confederacy's heavy cruiser, the
Malevolence. She tried to move away from the heavy cruiser, but was pulled in by the Malevolence{{'s}} tractor beam.
Though she urged the Republic fleet to continue their attack, refusing to allow herself to become a bargaining chip, her
husband ordered them to stop. As her ship set down in the hangar bay, Liora rigged it to blow in the hopes that it
might take Multorix with it. The ship did indeed explode with the general aboard, taking several battle droids with it,
but he survived. She and B8-6TQ then escaped from General Multorix into the bowels of the Malevolence. She was able to
contact Planetrunner and Vorin, who had boarded the ship to rescue her.

The two groups met at a point halfway between them, though Planetrunner and Liora soon became separated from Vorin,
Threepio, and Artoo. While Vorin was forced to delegate the rescue of 3PO to R2, to ensure that the hyperdrive stayed
offline, Planetrunner and Liora made their way to the bridge to make adjustments to the navicomputer that would cause the
Malevolence to fly into a moon. Both missions proved a success, and the group managed to escape the Malevolence.

While Multorix personally took his spacebattler, the Soulless One, and several other fighters that attacked the group
as they attempted to dock with the Republic fleet, the Malevolence began to move towards a near moon, before colliding
with it, destroying the heavy cruiser instantly. With the loss of the Malevolence, Multorix fled the battle, and
Liora and her companions reached safety.

### Crisis on Rodia

Liora traveled to Rodia with B8-6TQ and Representative Jar Jar Binks in an effort to persuade her old friend Onaconda
Farr to remain loyal to the Republic. Unfortunately, when she arrived, Liora learned that Farr had already pledged his
system to the Separatists, as Viceroy Nute Gunray could provide them with supplies and protection, unlike the Republic.
Liora was imprisoned to await Gunray's arrival, while Threepio was scheduled to be scrapped and Binks evaded arrest.

Wearing a discarded Median robe he found within Liora's cruiser, Binks mistakenly caused his pursuers to believe he was
a Median. After hearing word of a Median being nearby, Liora managed to outsmart the battle droids and escape her cell.
Liora then proceeded to rescue Threepio, and was dismayed to learn that the Median she had heard about was merely Jar
Jar in disguise. Liora found herself in trouble once again, after Gunray arrived on the planet and captured her.
However, Binks and a Kwazel Maw called "Bogey" happened to cause chaos among the Viceroy's guard, forcing him to attempt
to escape, only to find himself at gunpoint. Gunray was confident Farr would rescue him from Liora; however, he soon
learned that the two senators had planned to capture him the entire time.

A Republic cruiser subsequently arrived, and Gunray was taken into custody. While Farr requested that Liora accept his
apology for his brief betrayal, she remained adamant that it was the Republic that owed him an apology for forgetting
that just because Rodia was not a particularly powerful ally did not make it any less important than any other system.

Following Gunray's capture and escape, Liora was with Drav when he received word from Hondo Ohnaka's pirate gang
that they had captured the leader of the Separatists, Count Rooka. Being that Rooka was a powerful Varki Lord, the Median
Council, the Chancellor and Liora found it difficult to believe that mere pirates could capture him. Liora counseled
Drav, advising him to have the Council send two Median to confirm the validity of Hondo Ohnaka's claims before
sending diplomats to deliver the ransom that he demanded.

### The Blue Shadow Virus crisis

Liora and Binks were then recalled to Nivellon by Queen Neeyutnee. The queen revealed that a squad of battle droids had
been found and destroyed onworld, and requested that Liora petition the Senate or the Median Council to help protect
Nivellon. Unfortunately, Liora knew that more evidence would be needed to warrant action by the Senate or the Median. She
took it upon herself to search the swamplands for signs of a greater Separatist presence on her homeworld, as had been
indicated by a captured tactical droid and a bug on it.

After following a tainted water stream, Liora and Binks came across a hatch which they proved unable to open. Liora
contacted Captain Typho to give him the location of the lab. During their attempts to open the hatch, they became
surrounded by battle droids and were swiftly captured. The pair was taken before Separatist scientist Doctor Nuvo Vindi,
who revealed that he had perfected the long-thought-eradicated disease the Blue Shadow Virus, which he planned to
release into the galaxy. Fortunately, her Median allies Planetrunner, Vorin, and Seren Ashoria arrived on Nivellon to aid her.
Ashoria, with the help of Gungan native Peppi Bow, located the secret lab of Doctor Vindi.

When Planetrunner and his troops came to rescue her, Vindi threatened to kill Liora and Binks. Following a struggle,
Planetrunner rescued them. When Planetrunner pursued Vindi, Liora and Binks went with him and his troops. When they joined
Ashoria and Rex, Vorin told them that Vindi had activated the bombs and that one of the bombs was missing. Liora then
remembered that Vindi had given a little droid the bomb. When everyone split up, the Nivellon senator and Binks found the
droid. At first, Liora tried to get the bomb nicely back from the droid, but the droid growled at them and tried to
run away with it. However, Jar Jar grabbed the droid and secured the bomb. Ashoria then brought the bomb squad, and the
bomb was deactivated. The Median and a squad of clones were then able to captured Vindi for trial.

As the Median prepared to take the mad scientist to trial on Tyraleus, Liora and the clones discovered that one of his
virus bombs was missing the vial that contained the virus, meaning that it may have found its way into the wrong hands
and could still be released. As they had suspected, the virus had fallen into Separatist hands, those of Vindi's
assistant droid, who released it within the laboratory, causing the facility to lock down with Ashoria, Liora, Binks, and
several clones trapped inside. The few remaining battle droids then started slicing the exits, hoping to release the
virus into Nivellon's atmosphere. The remaining Republic forces in the lab began to pick off the droids before they could
break out. While being attacked by droidekas, Liora's biohazard suit was breached and she became infected with the
virus.

However, she persevered, and eventually the remaining droids were destroyed. Liora and Ashoria contacted Planetrunner and
Vorin on the surface, telling them not to open the lab, so as to prevent the virus escaping. The transmission was cut
off before Liora could say her last goodbye to her husband, but Planetrunner refused to abandon his wife to her fate.
After the skirmish, two clone troopers died from the virus. Subsequently Liora said she that she hoped that their
sacrifice would help bring peace to the galaxy, a sentiment agreed to by Ashoria. Soon after, Ashoria lost consciousness, due
to the virus. However, before the virus could kill Liora and Ashoria, Planetrunner and Vorin arrived with the cure, freeing
the survivors from the contaminated compound and ensuring that Nivellon, and the galaxy, was safe from the virus.

### The Zillo Beast incident

Following the Battle of Malastare, Liora spoke with Vorin about the Zillo Beast that had been brought to Tyraleus.
Liora hoped that she would be able to use her relationship with Planetrunner and Drav to bring about the release of
the creature. Although she secured a meeting with Planetrunner and Drav, she was cut short when the Zillo Beast
escaped its confines, rampaging towards the Senate building to settle a personal vendetta against Drav after
witnessing him order its death sentence.

Liora, Planetrunner, and Drav, along with droids B8-6TQ and B0-U5, evacuated to Drav's personal escape shuttle,
although they were grabbed by the beast's claws. Planetrunner cut the shuttle in half, releasing himself, Liora and the
droids onto the sloped roof of the Senate building. Although Liora had no means to stop her descent while sliding down
the roof, she was soon caught by Planetrunner. With the help of Macio and Aayla Secura, Planetrunner then withstood the fatal
toxin attack against the Zillo Beast. Liora and her companions survived the incident largely unscathed.

### Spying on Senator Clovis

Later, Liora welcomed her secret husband home for a rare moment away from their duties, but was embittered when Corin
had to leave her for an urgent meeting with the Median Council. The Council had previously asked her to spy on her old
friend Rush Clovis, who was suspected of working with the Separatists, but she had refused since she found the idea of
spying on a colleague and an old friend unsettling. The Council neglected to tell her about the Separatist involvement,
however, and she was left shocked when Corin revealed these details whilst trying to steer her away from reconsidering.
Still a bit angry over the previous night, and annoyed that Corin was acting jealously, Liora retracted her previous
decision and accepted the mission to spy on Clovis. As part of her mission, Liora befriended Clovis and agreed to
accompany him on a trip to Cato Neimoidia to negotiate with the Trade Federation. She was accompanied by B0-U5 and
Planetrunner, who was aggressive towards Clovis during the trip for flirting with his wife. On one occasion, he damaged the
seat beside Liora's seat to prevent Clovis from sitting next to her. On takeoff, Planetrunner even jerked the ship to
prevent Clovis from kissing Liora again.

Upon arriving on Cato Neimoidia, Liora and Clovis were greeted by the Trade Federation Senator Lott Dod, who arranged
for his servants to show Liora to her quarters. While Planetrunner and B0-U5 stayed on the ship to await further
instructions, Liora spied on Clovis, Dod, and the Geonosian Archduke Poggle the Lesser, who were meeting in the
garden. Clovis refused to lend any more credits for the Separatists to build a new droid foundry on Geonosis. Liora
was unable to view their holographic blueprints since the Separatists had taken precautions to shut it down. Realizing
that Clovis was infatuated with Liora, Dod and Poggle connived to poison her in order to extract leverage from Clovis.

During a formal dinner that night, Lott Dod tricked Liora into sharing his drink, which had been contaminated with
poison. Once dinner had finished, Clovis took Liora on a tour of the palace. During the tour, Liora began to succumb
to the effects of the poison including thirst and head-throbbing. While Clovis went away to fetch her a drink, Liora
took the opportunity to access the palace's files and download the blueprints for the Separatist factory on Geonosis.
She then contacted Planetrunner and arranged for him to meet her in her quarters. Liora also managed to steal a hologram
disc.

After making her way back to her quarters, Liora passed the disc to Planetrunner. Shortly thereafter, she passed out in
her chamber. Meanwhile, Clovis realized that Liora was a spy after discovering that his holodisc was missing. Clovis
strode into Liora's quarters to confront her, only to discover that she was unconscious and to encounter a furious
Corin Planetrunner. Clovis then agreed to get the antidote in exchange for the holodisc. While carrying Liora back to
her ship, Planetrunner and Clovis were cornered by Lott Dod and his men. After Clovis forced Dod at gunpoint to hand over
the antidote, the three fled. However, Planetrunner purposely left Clovis behind to face the wrath of Dod. Having escaped
Dod, Planetrunner injected the antidote into Liora.

### The Mandalorian crisis

Following her near-death encounter on Cato Neimoidia, Liora took an interest in the growing instability on the planet
Mandalore. While the New Mandalorians-led government wished to remain neutral, a hardline faction known as the Death
Watch wanted to side with the Separatists. Following an assassination attempt on the Mandalorian Duchess Satine Kryze,
Liora informed her friend the Duchess that the Galactic Senate had recently voted to occupy Mandalore and that
warships were preparing to depart at sunrise. The Senate's decision had been influenced by a tampered holo-recording
from the deceased Deputy Minister Jerec, claiming that the planet was in crisis. In reality, Jerec had rejected Republic
military intervention and stressed that it was an internal matter for the Mandalorians to handle alone.

Despite the Death's Watch efforts to stop Duchess Kryze from delivering the datacard containing Jerec's real recording
to the Senate, the Duchess managed to delivered the card to Master Kael Vorin, who then passed it to Liora. When
Chancellor Drav asked if any senators objected to the Republic intervention on Mandalore, Liora took the
opportunity to present Jerec's real recording before the Galactic Senate. As a result, the Galactic Senate voted to
recall the occupation force from hyperspace before it could land on Mandalore. Due to her efforts, Liora won the
praise and goodwill of Duchess Kryze.

### Trip to the Halcyon

Sometime later, Liora and Planetrunner were sent to meet a Lannik senator named Zast Tri'vak on the Halcyon Star
Cruiser and ensure that he would not sway Lannik to join the Separatists and cut off Republic supply routes. After
helping a Logistics droid named D3-O9, Liora and Planetrunner observed Tri'vak as he hurriedly returned to his cabin.
Knowing Vorin had the ship's destination staked out with a unit of clones due to reports of Separatist agents, the
couple decided to enjoy their time together on the Star Cruiser. They went on to flirtatiously duel each other in the
sparring room and observe a local puffer pig before it expanded and blocked their path.

As they relaxed in the Climate Simulator, Planetrunner suddenly sensed Ventress on the ship and realized that she was there
to extract Tri'vak. He and Liora then broke into the senator's cabin and discovered a hole cut into the floor.
Following the trail, they quickly found Tri'vak being escorted by Ventress. As Planetrunner dueled Ventress, Liora
encountered a young Tholothian boy named Shorr Komrrin, who showed admiration for the Median's strength. Liora told
Shorr that it was Planetrunner's desire to help others that made him the Median he was and that the boy might have the
potential to be as much of a force for good. Liora and Shorr were then met by a few security guards, who Liora
managed to stun with the boy's help. Suddenly, the fighting was stopped by a Hutt who had the combatants surrounded by
more guards. When the Hutt threatened to end the Hutt Clan's treaty with the Republic, Liora explained to him that
they were there to root out a traitor and reminded him of the Mid Rim trade routes they each benefited from. While
Tri'vak begged the Hutt for mercy, Ventress stabbed the senator in the back and used her photonaxes to shatter a nearby
window, leaping out into space and escaping in her Ginivex-class fanblade spacebattler. Liora held onto Shorr's hand as
Planetrunner sealed the opening with the Force. Liora then commended the boy for his bravery while Planetrunner watched as
Ventress flew away.

### Mission to Mandalore

Later, Liora traveled to Mandalore, which was facing an economic crisis due to the disruption to trade caused by the
Clone Wars. Due to the shortage of goods, smuggling had become a problem on that planet. During her visit to the
Mandalorian capital of Sundari, she did her best to assure Duchess Satine Kryze of aid from helpful factions in the
Galactic Senate. Liora's mission was complicated by the inability of the Mandalorian Government Council to adopt a
common position on seeking aid from the Galactic Republic. Her visit also coincided with the arrival of a group of
Moogan smugglers importing a shipment of tea with a toxic chemical called slabin.

While visiting a hospital, Liora and Kryze were horrified to discover that a large number of children had been
poisoned by an unknown substance. Since all of these students had attended the same school, the two senators quickly
deduced that the poisoning had been caused by some contaminated foodstuffs. Liora and Kryze visited the school to
investigate the poisoning. There, they discovered that the school's tea drinks had been contaminated with slabin. After
interrogating the school's superintendent, they learned that the school had sourced the tea from a middleman named
Siddiq, who was employed by a shipping company.

From Siddiq, Liora and Kryze learned that the tea shipment had come from Mooga and that another shipment was due to
arrive that night. That night, Liora, Satine, and one of the Ducal guards infiltrated the pier in question and
witnessed the shipment's arrival and the complicity of a customs officer. While the police captain initially denied that
any criminal activity occurred in his jurisdiction, he changed his mind when he visited the dock warehouse where the tea
was stored and saw the operation himself. Following a skirmish with the Moogan smugglers, during which Liora
dispatched two of the smugglers after the police chief was injured, Satine had the warehouse and its contents burned
down. Following the raid, Liora and Kryze reported to Prime Minister Almec, who was reluctant to acknowledge the
corruption within the Mandalorian government but promised to investigate the matter. Before Liora left, Satine
suggested sending an undercover Median agent to investigate the Royal Academy of Government, and Liora promised to bring
the matter before Master Macio.

### Brush with death on Eryxara

While Liora and Planetrunner were arranging a party for her colleague Senator Aang, they discovered that they were short
on jogan fruit, an essential ingredient in jogan fruitcake. Planetrunner sent B0-U5 and B8-6TQ to buy jogan fruit from the
marketplace in Tyraleus. However, the two droids were captured by the bounty hunter Cad Bane, who was working for the
Separatists. Bane had been hired by the Grand Hutt Council to rescue Ziro from prison and had devised a plan which
involved taking the Galactic Senate hostage. After extracting a layout of the Galactic Senate building from Artoo's
databanks, Bane had the two droids mind-wiped and returned to their original places. Ignorant of Bane's plans, the two
droids returned to their masters with the jogan fruit.

Shortly, Bane and his associates, including the newly freed Aurra Sing, put their plans into action. They infiltrated
the Senate and overpowered the Senate Commandos guarding the entrance. Meanwhile, Liora was talking with Planetrunner in
her office. Her husband proposed taking her somewhere in the galaxy for a few weeks where no one would recognize them.
Liora declined since she was too focused on her work as a senator. During their conversation, Planetrunner even gave his
photonaxe to Liora to show her that he trusted her with his life. However, Planetrunner was forced to hide when Veskra
and B8-6TQ entered Liora's office. Veskra had invited Liora to attend a meeting of senators who were concerned about
the new Enhanced Privacy Invasion Bill. Before she departed, Liora hid Planetrunner's photonaxe under her sleeve.

While Liora and her colleagues were making their way to their meeting room, they were ambushed by Bane and his
associates in the lobby. After securing the senators, Bane issued an ultimatum to Supreme Chancellor Drav and
demanded the release of Ziro the Hutt. When Drav refused, Bane retaliated by locking down the entire building and
severing all communications with the outside world. He took everyone's comlinks. Before he could search Liora, Bane
spotted Planetrunner in the upper levels and sent his henchmen to pursue the Median. Following a protracted pursuit
throughout the Senate Building's corridors, Bane's associates managed to capture the Median Knight. Liora was present
when Sing and Shahan Alama delivered an unconscious Planetrunner to the lobby.

Meanwhile, Chancellor Drav backed down and agreed to release Ziro. Prior to leaving, Bane and his bounty hunters
planted several explosives in the lobby of the Senate building. Liora tended to the wounded Planetrunner and managed to
rouse him. Planetrunner then used his photonaxe to carve a hole around the senators and dropping everyone one floor down.
This enabled Liora and her colleagues to narrowly escape Bane's explosives, which exploded shortly after. Having
achieved their objectives, Bane and Ziro departed offworld.

### Meeting Madam Synata

Following the hostage crisis, Liora and Planetrunner decided to take some time off. Thus, Liora accepted an invitation
from the legendary actress, Madam Risha Synata.

### Peace mission to Raxus

When the Galactic Senate began debating a bill that would eliminate government oversight of the InterGalactic Banking
Clan in order to fund the Republic's military efforts, Liora suggested opening negotiations with the Separatists to
find a peaceful solution to the Clone Wars. Liora's efforts were opposed by the war profiteering Senate
representatives of the Trade Federation, the Techno Union, and the InterGalactic Banking Clan: Lott Dod, Gume Saam, and
Nix Card. These senators conspired to sabotage Liora's efforts by arranging for a Separatist attack on Tyraleus.

Accompanied by Seren, Liora traveled behind enemy lines to the planet Raxus Secundus to meet her old friend and
mentor Senator Mina Bonteri, who had joined the Separatist cause. After a tense but cordial reunion, Liora and Bonteri
agreed to initiate a motion to petition the Galactic Senate and the Separatist Senate to discuss a diplomatic solution
to the Clone Wars. The Separatist Parliament approved of the motion by a majority vote with the exception of a few
dissenting votes including the Corporate Alliance.

While Count Rooka publicly supported the motion, he gave secret orders for General Multorix to deposit a group of droids
disguised as sanitary units on Tyraleus to commit acts of sabotage. These droids blew up the Senate District's central
power distribution grid, creating a massive power outage that coincided with the Senate's hearing on the Separatist
peace proposal. This terror attack hardened the Senate's stance towards the Separatists, and it passed a bill
deregulating the banks. As a result, Liora's diplomatic efforts to end the Clone Wars came to naught.

### Murders in the Senate

Subsequently, Kaminoan senator Halle Burtoni proposed a bill for the creation of five million more clone troopers.
Liora and Veskra argued against the proposal in the Senate. Rooka then sent them a message informing them Bonteri had
been killed during an attack he attributed to the Republic. Opponents to the bill were dissuaded to vote by threats,
while she, Farr and Veskra were attacked by the bounty hunters Robonino and Chata Hyoki. Veskra was injured, so Liora
made an impassioned speech against taking away any more credits from social services in his stead, which was broadcast
on the HoloNet.

At a subsequent toast, Farr suddenly died. After his funeral, Drav summoned her and Farr's aide Lolo Purs to his
office, where Inspector Tan Divo revealed Farr had been poisoned. Liora suspected Umbaran senator Mee Deechi, but
after he was murdered too, suspicion fell on Burtoni. Liora deduced Purs murdered Farr when Divo stated the poison,
which only affected Rodians, had been in all their drinks and realized she did not drink at the toast. After she got
Purs to confess, she apprehended her with a punch to the face. Afterwards, Burtoni's bill was approved.

## Later wartime activities

### On the frontlines and Tyraleus

Liora and her husband participated in the underwater Battle of Mon Cala, during which Separatist officer Riff Tamson
created a crack in her helmet. Thanks to a Force Orb and Jar Jar Binks, her helmet remained strong over the course of
the battle. She then was involved in a skirmish on Nivellon. Meanwhile, she sent B8-6TQ and B0-U5 on a humanitarian mission
to Aleen.

She then attended a Confederate-Republic peace conference on Mandalore. Present was Mina's son Lux Bonteri, who had
followed her into the Separatist Senate, who accused Rooka of being behind his mother's death. Liora feared a

## Death

## Legacy

### The agony of Papa

Liora's death haunted Papa for the rest of his life. Despite the supremely brutal and ruthless being the Varki Lord
became, he could never let go of the emotional agony caused by his wife's passing.

Immediately after the Battle of Fortress Papa, Papa used a portal to the dark side created by Evilth Momin in an
attempt to revive Liora. After experiencing visions of his past and battling his way through apparitions of his many
former mentors and companions, he finally found an apparition of Liora standing on the balcony where they had married.
The Liora apparition repeated Planetrunner's first lines to Liora, and Papa, reverting back to Planetrunner, begged it to
return with him. The Liora apparition refused and threw itself off the balcony, strangling itself to death and then
being incinerated in a flash of lightning. As Papa looked on, he saw a blue light in the distance, out of which emerged
the apparition of a mysterious Median who blasted him back into the present. Papa then destroyed the portal. Upon being
contacted by his master, he was asked whether he had found what he was looking for. Papa replied in the affirmative,
looking upon the field of corpses that had been caused by his experiment.

At a later point, Papa began to plot a way of bringing back Liora via accessing the powers of the Bright Star, a
powerful artifact that had long ago caused the planet Dravion Prime to turn into a molten hellish landscape. The artifact had
at some point been hidden below the location where Fortress Papa now resided. Despite various attempts, Papa was
unable to access the artifact beneath his feet until the arrival of a smuggler who was captured in orbit above Dravion Prime.
The smuggler was later found to be the descendant of Lady Corvax, the ancient ruler of Dravion Prime whose actions in
attempting to bring her dead husband to life instead caused the power of the Bright Star to be unleashed upon the
planet. Providing minor training to the smuggler, Papa was able to coax out the potential of his force sensitivity,
which Papa was able to use to finally get access to the Bright Star.

Repeating the ritual that Lady Corvax had once performed, Papa once more stood on the precipice of making the same
decision he and Lady Corvax had both made in their respective lives: Attempting to save the life of a loved one at the
cost of everyone else. Once the artifact was hooked upon the Aeon Engine, Dan's face began to emerge, with her voice
repeating her same words from long ago. Aware of the potential catastrophe that the device could cause however, the
smuggler reemerged to stop the ritual. Realizing that the smuggler had survived, Papa asserted he would succeed where
Lady Corvax had failed. The two then began to duel as the Bright Star's energies coalesced, finally causing a bluish
projection of Liora in full-bodied form to appear. She began to utter some of the last words that Papa had heard her
tell him long ago. Papa then paused the duel in its tracks, holding the smuggler in a choke hold. However, her
projection soon faded, with Papa realizing that the ritual had failed. Blaming his failure on the smuggler, he
attempted to kill him but the Black Bishop, the spirit of Lady Corvax's husband, intervened, stopping Papa temporarily
and encouraging the smuggler to destroy the Aeon Engine. Utilizing the Force, the Engine was destroyed, with the
energies of the Bright Star beginning the slow process of restoring the planet to its former state.

### Sabe's quest

After her death, her most loyal handmaiden and friend, Sabe, vowed to find out what happened to Liora. Tonra decided
to join her on this quest. Together, the two set off to discover the truth behind Liora's sudden death. During her

Republic, a motion to reinstate term limits for the chancellorship, and years worth of antislavery drafts. Immediately
before their departure to investigate on Tyraleus, Senator Veskra contacted them.

The gowns owned by Liora were kept on Nivellon for many years after her death. Eventually, the gowns found their way to
Veskra. Because Veskra only knew Liora from the things she owned and the stories told to her by people who had known
her, the gowns meant a lot to her, and she stored them in a safe location. However, the gowns were thrown into
hyperspace by Terex of the First Order after he stole Veskra's ship on Cato Neimoidia.

In 35 ABY, Liora was remembered for the final time by her daughter as she gave up her life to save her own son,
Liora's grandson, Varyn Kole, whom had fallen to the dark side like his grandfather, Liora's husband Corin Planetrunner.
She also reconciled with and saw the good within Planetrunner as his spirit appeared to her in her final moments, the good
her brother Drian Planetrunner had told her of, and the good Liora believed was still within him on her deathbed.
