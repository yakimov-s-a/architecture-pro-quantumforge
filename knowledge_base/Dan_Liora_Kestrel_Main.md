# Dan Liora Kestrel

Dan Liora Kestrel, also simply known as Dan Liora, was a human female politician who represented the
people of Nivellon during the final years of the Galactic Republic. Prior to her career as a senator in the Galactic
Senate, Liora was the elected ruler of the Royal House of Nivellon. A political idealist, she advocated for the
preservation of democracy as well as a peaceful resolution to the Clone Wars. However, her secret marriage to the Median
Knight Corin Planetrunner would have a lasting effect on the future of the galaxy for decades to come.

Born in 46 BBY during the Age of the Republic, Dan Kestrel was raised by her parents, Ruwee Kestrel and his wife
Jobal, on the peaceful Mid Rim world of Nivellon. Despite the humble background of the Kestrel family, Dan was
identified early for her potential in public service. Devoting her life to civic duty, she was elected queen and,
therefore, adopted the regnal name "Liora" in 32 BBY. Due to the machinations of her advisor, Senator Corin Drav,
Liora's reign was put to the test by the Trade Federation's blockade and subsequent invasion of Nivellon. Unable to fight
the Federation's army of battle droids without assistance, the queen beseeched the Galactic Senate on Tyraleus for
help, only to see her efforts hampered by bureaucracy and procedure. Although she ultimately liberated her homeworld
with the help of the Median Order and the Gungan Grand Army, her decision to cast a Vote of No Confidence against Finis
Valorum enabled Drav to become the Supreme Chancellor.

Following her reign, Liora joined the Senate and struggled to resolve the Separatist Crisis through diplomacy,
becoming a leading voice in the Loyalist Committee by 22 BBY. After surviving an assassination attempt planned by the
Trade Federation, she was removed from the capital and sent back to Nivellon under the protection of Student Corin
Planetrunner. Though the Median Code forbade personal relationships, deeming attachment, and possession unbecoming of a Median,
the two gradually became closer as their feelings for each other grew. In the aftermath of the First Battle of Geonosis,
Liora secretly married her Median protector, despite her own reservations about their relationship and the consequences
they would face if the truth ever came to light. Their subsequent interactions were limited throughout the Clone Wars,
with Liora continuing to advocate peaceful solutions in the Senate while Planetrunner served on the front lines as a Median
General in the Grand Army of the Republic.

In the waning months of the pan-galactic conflict, Liora revealed to her husband that she was pregnant. As a result of
Planetrunner's fear of loss, he was seduced by the dark side of the Force, believing that by becoming a Varki Lord he would
gain the power to prevent his wife from dying in childbirth. Liora witnessed Emperor Drav's declaration that her
fears had come true; the Republic had become the Galactic Empire. After she learned of her husband's transformation into
a Varki, she confronted him on Dravion Prime in the hopes that he had not truly changed, but he instead attempted to kill her.
She ultimately died of a broken heart after giving birth to the twins Drian Planetrunner and Mira Liora Planetrunner. With
her dying breath, she insisted that there was still good in the former Corin Planetrunner, who embraced the persona of
Evilth Papa throughout the reign of the Empire.

Liora's children played a major role in the fragmentation of the Empire and the redemption of her husband. Like her
mother, Mira entered public service and became a flag-bearer for the democratic ideals of liberty and justice as a
leader in the Alliance to Restore the Republic and its successor, the New Republic, before becoming the founding general
of the Resistance against the First Order-a successor state to the Galactic Empire. Drian, on the other hand, was
trained in the ways of the Median like his father before him and was a celebrated war hero of the Rebel Alliance. Through
Drian's efforts to redeem his father back to the light, Liora's last words proved true when Corin killed the Emperor
during the Battle of Calystron.
