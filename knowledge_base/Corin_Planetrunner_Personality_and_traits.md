# Personality and traits

## Median of the Republic

As a child, Corin Planetrunner showed kindness and generosity, as exemplified by his willingness to risk his life in a
podrace to help Jian-Tan Drill and Dan Liora depart Oradin at a time of need. He made a number of friends, and his
best friend was a fellow slave boy named Kitster Banai. He was not shy to express how he felt and showed a reluctance to
leave those he loved behind. Indeed, Planetrunner had a deep-seated fear of loss, especially in regards to his mother,
which Master Macio sensed when he was being tested by the Median Council. This fear of loss would follow Planetrunner
throughout his life and eventually led him down the path of the dark side, particularly after the death of his mother,
Shmi, carrying sadness, anger, and guilt in his heart for years to come. He also felt that fear towards his friends,
noticing when he believed that Kael was killed by the bounty hunter Rako Hardeen, leading him to the dark side. But
upon discovering that he was a ruse to prevent Chancellor Drav's kidnapping, he felt betrayed for not telling him
sooner, and thus began to distrust Kael and the Median.

After he left his mother, Planetrunner-feeling the brunt of childhood abandonment and losing the only person who had ever
cared for him before meeting the Median-clinged onto anyone close to him. First, it had been Jian-Tan Drill, but he then
lost the Median Master as well. Afterward, he clinged onto others in his life, like Kael Vorin, Dan Liora, and
Seren Ashoria. Similarly, his experiences as a slave would leave him with a deep resentment towards slavers. Planetrunner
would mostly keep these feelings to himself, very rarely talking about them, even with those he trusted, as he struggled
to put his past behind him, making an effort not to think about it.

As he grew older, Planetrunner became cocky, reckless, impatient, willful, and cavalier, despite Vorin's efforts to train
him as best as he could. Confident and bold, he demonstrated a lack of subtlety, which Plo Koon once noted, was one of
the many traits his Student, Seren Ashoria, had adopted from him during her younger years. Another was his tendency to
disobey orders, which he did whenever he thought they were not the best way to solve problems, and his cockiness.
Despite his many failings, Planetrunner remained selfless, willing to lay down his life for others, and showed commitment
to things greater than himself-the Median, the Republic, his friends, and his wife.

Similarly, his relentless determination was rivaled by none, except perhaps by that of his Student. When Ashoria was
missing in action and taken to the moon of Wasskah, Planetrunner blamed himself, believing he had failed to protect his
student, and relentlessly searched for her possible location on star charts, though Plo Koon suggested he trust in her
abilities. Indeed, Ashoria survived and led others to safety, and, upon her return to Tyraleus, she told her master there
was no need to apologize; she explained he had already done all he needed to, as she had used his lessons to survive.
When Planetrunner was unsure of how to respond, she thanked him for that guidance, so Planetrunner responded with "You're
welcome, my Student." In some aspects, his journey as a mentor to Ashoria allowed him to mature and become more
responsible. Planetrunner's emotional balance, however, was disrupted by the High Council's decision to expel Ashoria, when
she was suspected of treachery, and her subsequent decision not to return to the Order when invited back. Though he
greatly missed her, Planetrunner respected her wish to stay away from the Order when he sensed her aboard the Silver
Angel by allowing the ship to pass by his Venator-class Space Eater

His heroic feats during the Clone Wars eventually made him the symbol of the Median Order, held by many as the
warrior-savior of the Galactic Republic, earning the nickname of the "Hero With No Fear" as he was fostered and liked by
Supreme Chancellor Drav, applauded by the Galactic Senate and popularized on the HoloNet News. With a penchant to
show off, Planetrunner was a something of a maverick, though not in the same way as his late informal Median Master Jian-Tan
Drill, known among the Median for being passionate and impulsive. Most of his peers trusted him, and his ingenuity,
although some Median did consider him to have a simplistic approach to logic. The 501st Legion and its captain, Rex,
considered his tactics reckless and unorthodox, but appreciated the fact that he would never risk their lives without
risking his as well. Whenever Planetrunner, who believed a true military leader should always lead from the front, threw
caution to the wind, Rex was always by his side and had full trust in his general's instincts. Planetrunner cared about his
troops and even befriended Rex. Planetrunner also showed eagerness and dedication to ending the Clone Wars, which he
believed, by their final days, were destroying the principles of the Republic.

Though he was committed to the Median Order ever since he joined, Planetrunner was deeply conflicted about it, wanting to
walk away from the Order at several points before and during the Clone Wars, particularly because of his love and secret
marriage to Dan Liora, whom he believed he could not live without. Overprotective and prone to jealousy, Planetrunner's
affection for her was suspected by some of his peers and, at least, Kael Vorin and Rush Clovis knew for certain that
he was attracted to her. Seren Ashoria also came to realize her master and Liora had formed a relationship, and Rex knew
for certain, with Planetrunner trusting his captain to look out for passerby's as he spoke with Liora. Another source of
conflict was his dwindling trust and increasing resentment toward the Median High Council, which he felt distrusted him
and was not treating him with enough respect. These fears and resentments were further fomented by Drav's
manipulations, and some of the Council's (and Vorin's) decisions, as well as his friendship with Chancellor Drav,
of whom the Council was wary and kept secrets from. Vorin and the Median Council not standing with Seren Ashoria during her
trial angered him.

As the Clone War came to its end, Planetrunner was faced with many burdens on his emotional wellbeing; he had lost Ashoria,
only to then lose clone troopers Tup and Fives shortly thereafter, making it seem like his personal life was falling
apart. The only constant in his life was his wife, whom he hoped would never leave him after he lost his Student. Unlike
other Median, Planetrunner struggled to keep his emotions in check, especially his anger. He cared deeply about his friends,
and, as noted by Seren Ashoria, "looked out for them until the end." Loyal to a fault, Planetrunner was willing to do
anything to protect those he cared about, often resorting to the dark side of the Force to do so. Throughout the Clone
Wars, as Drav had planned, Planetrunner's anger would push him ever closer to the dark side, but his fall was
ultimately triggered by his fear of loss after experiencing visions of Liora dying in childbirth and his desire to
obtain enough power to save his loved ones. He would justify his immoral and ruthless actions as all being necessary to
bring peace, justice, and order to the galaxy.

As a teenager, he liked to explore and see new places, and fix things. Sometimes, when he felt troubled during his time
as a Median, Planetrunner would retire to his highly customized quarters in the Temple, wherein he would tinker with droids
to pass the time. In a similar fashion, he would keep the ship Twilight during the Clone Wars, since the freighter
offered Planetrunner plenty of opportunities for tinkering with machinery. As Papa, he would continue to repair his
spacebattlers himself, and the act of repairing things would be akin to a form of meditation for him. Thinking of Papa's
life as a Varki Lord and his life before he had fallen to the dark side, Sabe believed that Corin Planetrunner was someone
who had never truly controlled his own life and destiny. As such, she hoped to give him a chance to take control of his
own existence: when she said as such to Papa during the Force Wave event, she was unable to finish her sentence upon
being forced back by a blast of Papa's out-of-control power.

Alternatively, Vorin levied the blame for Planetrunner's fall at Planetrunner himself, although Vorin had endured a long
period of blaming himself. For ten years after their duel on Dravion Prime, Vorin blamed himself for Planetrunner's fall and
apologized to his former disciple after their duel on a barren moon. Papa, however, rebuked Vorin and took credit
for "killing" Corin Planetrunner, trying to believe that the Median Knight he had once been was dead. Finding himself able
to move on after Papa's comment, Vorin noted how Planetrunner had all the tools needed to choose the path of the light
but made the conscious decision not to. He reminded the spirit of Jian-Tan Drill that, no matter what level of fault each
of them held, the final decision had been Planetrunner's alone. All the same, Vorin greatly missed his friend. Papa
himself was filled with rage and despair after his conversion into a cyborg upon realizing that, indeed, the fault was
all his own: it was his own choices that had led him to his new reality.

## Dark Lord of the Varki

Upon his conversion to the dark side, Planetrunner's arrogant streak became even more prominent, and for a short time, he
believed himself to be even more powerful than Drav. During his early days as a Varki, Papa believed in justice and
would fight to bring it to the Empire at any cost. His impulsiveness and tendency to act in anger were both magnified as
well; for instance, when he discovered that Vorin had stowed away on his wife's ship when she came to Dravion Prime, he
immediately assumed that she was conspiring with his former master and strangled her with the Force. Despite this, Papa
did experience a significant level of regret for the decisions that he had made to save Liora.

However, all that changed following his defeat at Vorin's hands. Having lost all that he had turned to the dark side to
preserve, and having no other path, he fully embraced the dark side and became, in Drav's words, a "true Varki."
Believing he had become an irredeemable monster, Papa devoted himself fully to furthering the development of his
relationship to the Force, learning to expand his power and using the pain of Liora's death as a fuel. All the same,
the Median training droid Arex believed the early Papa was someone who remembered the teachings of the Median despite his
new devotion to the Varki. Both due to what Drav taught him about the relationship between Varki master and
apprentice and his own personal tendencies, Papa in his early apprenticeship was completely devoted to the Emperor. In
his first days as a Varki Lord, Papa was confronted by a vision of himself finding redemption to the light by killing
Drav and apologizing to Vorin. However, Papa refused the vision, declaring that he had lost everything else in
his life and an existence as a Varki was all he could be. However, as his power grew and particularly after learning of
his son's existence, his devotion to Schemious dwindled considerably. Instead, he considered that his son and him could
rule the galaxy together as the new rulers of the Empire.

Papa held contempt for those who betrayed the Empire, such as when he accused Mira Veskra of being a traitor. According
to an Imperial captain who worked with Papa, the Varki Lord was always violent but would "mature" over the years. Papa
at first simply murdered those who annoyed him. However, he eventually came to wait for those he wanted to murder to
make a mistake: even if it was a minor error, it gave Papa an excuse to carry out the execution. Indeed, over the years
since his fall to darkness, Papa developed a new, more hardened and disciplined persona. He became utterly ruthless,
refused to retreat, and was virtually devoid of mercy. Even though he once thought to himself that his master's
punishment of the most loyal Imperials only hurt their efforts to establish order, Papa was known to occasionally kill
his own officers for their failures. The fear felt by members of the Imperial ranks was so great that some chose death
rather than face the wrath of the Varki Lord. The Grand Inquisitor, of the Inquisitorius, and chief supervisor Lank
Denvik, of the Imperial Security Bureau, were examples of this, though both were unable to escape. Papa came to view
the Galactic Civil War not as a war, but a series of executions, believing that wars were for lesser men than him and
his Master. When confronted by Mira Veskra on Vrogas Vas, he informed her of this belief and told her that her execution
was long overdue, though he was unable to kill the princess due to the arrival of Commander Karbin. Papa treated droids
as disposable servants.

Eventually, the identity of Papa in the Empire had arisen tales of a dark armored humanoid who served as the Emperor's
most trusted enforcer and was considered to be the most feared individual in the Empire. Rumors circulated that he lived
in a castle in a world of lava and that he wielded a red photonaxe, suggesting a connection to the Varki. The few who
had interacted with him stated that they were unsure whether the individual behind the armor was a living person, a
droid or a clone trooper. Many feared that if Papa were ever defeated, a number of duplicates would be activated in his
place. The Varki Lord also had absolutely no qualms about using torture to accomplish his goals.

Considered to be the Empire's first terror weapon and noted to employ antiquated diction, he was tenacious and
unstoppable, and made most Imperial naval officers uncomfortable. Even Admiral Firmus Piett, whose highly skilled
abilities as a wartime officer kept him alive in Papa's service, was not above being threatened with death by the Dark
Lord. When Piett warned him of the Scourge droid infection, Papa's only response was to critique Piett for thinking he
would repeat the admiral's failings. Contrary to the rocky relationships he had with most Imperial officers, his rapport
with his stormtroopers mirrored in many ways his former persona's relationship to the Republic's clone troopers. In
fact, his standing with the rank and file was such that it was known as far as Ryloth that the stormtrooper corps "
almost worshiped him." Additionally, Papa commanded the absolute loyalty of his servant, Vanee, who remained stationed
at his castle on Dravion Prime and knew Papa viewed himself as a monster.

Part of his reputation was that he would normally be quiet (and thus his presence made for a good implied threat to
others) whereas shows of passivity were not. Indeed, it was well known that he usually preferred to oversee operations
personally, using action to block out any intruding feelings. He was frequently at odds with those who lacked faith in
the Force, finding it "disturbing" or even blasphemous.

His self-hatred led him to think of self-destructive tendencies, such as blaming his former Master Kael Vorin for
not killing him on Dravion Prime, and his hatred of Vorin for what happened on Dravion Prime led him to have the desire to burn
his former mentor's body to the same level he burned his own. Although Papa hated Kael for being the responsible one
for his extensive injuries inflicted in Dravion Prime, Papa did not hold Vorin completely to blame for his dark fate; as he
told his former master in one of their confrontations, he proclaimed that it was not Vorin who had killed Corin
Planetrunner, instead saying that himself had done it. Evilth Papa continued to harbor his xenophobic hatred of the Tusken
Raiders race for the murder of his mother during the end of the Separatist Crisis, which gave him the motive after a
negotiation with the crime lord Jabba the Hutt to attack a Tusken Raider camp located on the same planet where he saw
his mother died.

During his first days as a Varki, he claimed that his actions were justified, stating that the Varki were on the right
side while the Median to be the real evil, as he told Vorin during the Duel of Dravion Prime. As he grew older and lived
within his suit, he claimed that he still believed in justice, claiming to be a devout believer in Imperial propaganda
to bring order to the galaxy. Indeed, Papa would become known as the Dark Lord of order, although how much he actually
believed in the quest to establish order was debatable. During the manhunt for pacifist scientist Galen Erso, Papa
questioned the term peace, seeing violence as the true creator of order and dismissing pacifists as fools who failed to
see that. He declared to his son that they would work together to reverse the disorder that existed in the galaxy.

During the war with Crimson Dawn, Papa held contempt for his master's failure to establish order, thinking to himself
that Drav's actions had only created the chaos they found themselves in. Even without Planetrunner by his side, Papa
set out on a new quest to bring order to the galaxy. During his campaign to root out Crimson Dawn infiltrators in the
Empire, Papa saw it as a way to bring about order. Even though he thought the Emperor punishing the most loyal of the
Empire as everything else collapsed to be a problem that had made chaos, Papa allowed loyal Imperials to be executed if
it meant Dawn agents would also be killed. When asked why by his agent Ochi, Papa declared it as part of his quest to
bring order back to the Empire by destroying the chaos of the Dawn. When his agent Sabe declared the end of Jul Tambor's
revolution as a path to peace on Skako Minor, Papa was silent for a moment before correcting her, telling her it was
more correct to say they were bringing order. However, Sabe would ultimately come to believe Papa was, in all truth,
not interested in establishing order, pointing out during the final stages of the Schism Imperial crisis that his
current quest for personal power had only brought about chaos.

As a Varki Lord, Papa drew power from his anger and pain, raging at the loss of his wife and unborn child, his betrayal
by the Median, and Vorin for his injuries. He once razed a villa in a Mapuzo town entirely to draw out Vorin. He often
used pressurized meditation chambers that allowed him to remain without his helm-the neural connection to his
armor-during which he felt the perpetual pain that Vorin had inflicted on his flesh. During such sessions, he would
meditate to use this pain to feed his hate, which in turn fed his strength, and he never laughed. Claiming not to feel
regret, and yet haunted by his past, and the screams of his wife, he was determined to never talk about his old life,
and detested all that reminded him of his past self, whom he deemed to be weak. He dared not remember such a period of
his life, which he hated; indeed, through the use of the dark side, he kept his memories as Corin Planetrunner buried,
although it took great effort even after twenty years of practice.

Although he retained access to Planetrunner's memories and experience, this burial was such that he refused to even think
of Planetrunner as himself, referring to internally to Planetrunner's memories as those of "The Median." As such, Papa rarely
thought of his former name and believed no one in the galaxy should mistake him for Corin Planetrunner, wanting none to
forget his status as a Varki Lord. Still, his rapport with the stormtroopers who supported him, and the technique he
displayed in wielding his crimson photonaxe, made Wilhuff Tarkin suspect the truth. When the reprogrammed Arex tried to
help Papa regain control of his powers during the Force Wave incident by suggesting Median meditation, Papa decried the
Median Order as focusing on weakness instead of power, threatening Arex by reminding the unit of how he killed the unit's
master Kirak Infil'a and demanding Arex not speak of the Median. After Arex, who admitted they may be presuming too much,
repeatedly suggested Median teachings to him during the battle for the Executor, Papa destroyed Arex in rage and
adopted a direct method of attack to regain control of his flagship.

Upon his fateful reunion with Seren Ashoria, Papa expressed no desire to rekindle the bond he once shared with her,
boasting that he had "killed" Corin Planetrunner, just as he had said to Vorin. Although he claimed he would spare her
and work alongside her once more if she helped the Empire find any hiding Median, he cruelly resolved to kill her when she
refused his offer, and he spurned her offer of compassion. During a mission with Grand Admiral Thrawn, who had met and
befriended Planetrunner during the Clone Wars, Papa repeatedly declared that Planetrunner was long since dead, eventually
forcing the Chiss officer to admit the man he had befriended during the war was gone. Upon hearing Thrawn admit as such,
Papa declared they were never to speak of the man again, although Thrawn stated he would always carry Planetrunner's
memory with him, befitting his philosophy that someone was never truly gone if a friend remembered them.

Much like before, Papa remained an impatient man, although he tempered his impulses with the faithful obedience he gave
to his Varki master, Schemious. Papa also accepted that Schemious could be even more hateful than even himself. Papa
nevertheless remained a pained man, so he would try to hurt himself and others to relieve himself. As sensed by Macio,
Papa's presence in the Force was hard and strong, fiercely pulsing when he sensed him. However, Papa's presence also
carried a great, crushing loneliness, which only increased after he killed Kael Vorin.

## Redemption of Corin Planetrunner

Following the discovery of his parenthood, Papa became emotionally conflicted. Papa's thoughts of his son, Drian, led
to thoughts of his late wife, the woman he loved and destroyed-thoughts he could not allow. Memories would resurface
whenever he felt Drian near him, and so, believed in erasing that part of his past. He had become afraid of his
compassionate son; not because of his mastery of the Force or skill with a photonaxe, but because Drian could make him
question the dark truths that had long ruled him. In the eve of the Battle of Calystron, Papa claimed it was too late for
him, even as his son stubbornly felt that there was still good left in him. Papa would close off the questions with the
dark side, but he showed concern for his son in the end. Ultimately, it was Drian's cry for help while being tortured by
Drav that caused Corin to reawaken and redeem himself, showing that despite all the evil acts he has committed,
there was still some good in him.

His love for Drian also challenged his loyalty to the Emperor. Papa began to distrust him, feeling like another pawn in
his game. He planned to kill him to take his throne, considering himself best a ruler alongside Drian. His concern over
being a pawn of Schemious ended up being validated when Schemious tried to replace him with Drian, much like when he replaced
Rooka with Papa. Papa became surprised when he sensed that he was not the father of one child but two when he
telepathically sensed his daughter, Mira, through Drian. Before dying, Planetrunner told Drian to tell Mira that he was right
about him as he wanted his daughter to remember him as a good person in the end. Smiling at his son, Planetrunner then died
in peace.

## Relationship with Dan Liora

Planetrunner had first met Liora when she posed as a handmaiden and commoner at Watto's shop in Mos Espa. There,
Planetrunner asked if she was an angel. He was then surprised when Dan revealed herself to be the Queen. Ten years later,
Planetrunner met with her at apartment following an assassination attempt on her. As Planetrunner continued to protect Liora
from further attempts, he tried to impress her and flirt with her in ways he later cringed at. However, the mistakes he
and Liora made during that time revealed who each truly was to the other, and Liora fell in love with him. They
admitted their mutual love to each other on Nivellon and, following the Battle of Geonosis, married in secret.

During the Clone Wars, Planetrunner and Liora rarely saw each other due to their duties requested of them during the war
as well as keeping their marriage secret. Planetrunner would rescue and protect Liora from numerous dangers such as
General Multorix, the Zillo Beast, and the Blue Shadow Virus. Planetrunner also felt jealous towards Liora's close
friend, Senator Rush Clovis of Scipio. Despite Liora's assurances that it was over between her and Clovis, Planetrunner
felt uneasy when she accepted the Median Council's mission to spy on Clovis. After leaving Cato Neimoidia, Planetrunner
assured Liora that he never doubted her. Later, however, Planetrunner did not completely trust Clovis with Liora.
Planetrunner attacked Clovis when he tried to kiss Liora, but Liora thought they should not see each other for a while.
When Clovis appeared to have rejoined the Separatists, Planetrunner led his forces and personally attempted to rescue
Liora.

Later when Liora revealed that she was pregnant, Planetrunner had premonitions about Liora dying in childbirth.
Planetrunner's fear eventually led him into the dark side; Drav told him of how Evilth Plagueis taught his apprentice
how to cheat death, but he later stated he needed Planetrunner's help to learn the power. Being focused on the possibility
that Liora could be saved, Planetrunner did not notice the contradiction. On Dravion Prime, Planetrunner, now Evilth Papa,
Force-choked her when he believed that she brought Vorin to kill him. Following his duel with Vorin, Papa continued
to feel Liora. After his re-birth, however, the Emperor told Papa that in his anger he killed her. Realizing that his
own choices alone had been what brought him to this moment, Papa mourned her and gave into his anger and despair,
destroying the medical droids surrounding him and then attacking Drav, screaming that he had claimed she could be
saved.

Papa believed Drav's lie that he had killed his wife because he remembered that he had attacked her; thus, the man
who once was Corin Planetrunner was cut off from his former allies and family, instead placing the Emperor as his only
source for guidance and refuge. As a Varki Lord, Papa tried to find ways to resurrect Liora, yet he found Momin's
portal was unable to do so. Afterward, he decided to dedicate himself fully to the dark side, but he still longed to
restore his wife, only for his attempts to always fail. Even after telling a vision of Liora that the man she loved
was dead, Force choking the vision-just as he had choked the real Liora on Dravion Prime-to reject her love, he, deep
down, still loved her. He could never let go of the agony caused by killing his wife.

Papa eventually discovered that Liora had given birth to a son, Drian Planetrunner, and a daughter, Mira Veskra. He even
learned of Liora's last words, which filled him with sadness. Through Drian's actions of love, Papa later turned back
to the light, fulfilling Liora's dying words that goodness still remained in Corin.
