# History

## Design and development

The X-fighter was descended from Incom Corporation's Clone Wars-era Fighter, namely the Aggressive ReConnaissance-170
spacebattler, Clone Z-95 Headhunter, and Z-95 Headhunter. Incom's engineers strove to build a well-rounded,
hyperdrive-equipped snubfighter with no exploitable weakness. The new spacebattler was intended to become the backbone of
the Imperial Navy, but political forces led to the Galactic Empire deciding to rely on Sienar Fleet Systems' more
affordable REU fighter series. The Alliance to Restore the Republic then adopted the X-fighter design as its own.

In 5 ABY, Incom-FreiTek Corporation introduced the T-70 model, which incorporated many improvements over the T-65
series. The Incom engineers, having learned a valuable lesson from the loss of so many X-fighters in the trenches at Battle
of Orynthis, equipped the T-70 with a swiveling blaster cannon that could be used to fend off attacks from behind. At least
one prototype had been constructed prior to the Battle of Solkara. By 34 ABY, the New Republic had retired most of its
T-70s in favor of the T-85s, which were even more capable than the already impressive T-70s.

## Operational history

### Galactic Civil War

#### Early battles

The T-65B model was in use as early as 5 BBY. Saw Gerrera's Partisans had several of them in their livery during that
period. Gerrera was among the first rebel leaders to see how useful the T-65 would be in rebel operations and had stolen
eight via a violent raid on an Incom warehouse. Dubbed the Cavern Angels for the Partisans' habit of hiding material in
caves, these X-fighters were used as early as the Partisans' stay on Segra Milo.

The Alliance to Restore the Republic recovered information about the X-fighter via the rescue of Incom personnel and
operated T-65B X-fighters as early as 1 BBY. During the attack on Lothal, at least ten T-65B X-fighter Fighter alongside
BTL-A4 Y-wing assault spacebattler/bombers participated in the assault, but all were destroyed by the Imperial defenses.
Hera Syndulla, Mart Mattin, Secon Daree, C1-10P, and R3-A3 survived being shot down, but many other pilots were killed
and their astromechs destroyed.

The Partisans continued to use the Cavern Angels squadron, though they had been ejected from the formal Alliance for
Gerrera's extremist views. Rebel X-fighters saw action shortly before the outbreak of the Galactic Civil War in both the
Liberation of Erso and the Mission to Eadu.

#### Open war begins

The full-scale Galactic Civil War began with the Battle of Scarif, a costly but significant victory for the Alliance.
The newly assembled squad, Rogue One, a special forces team created by Jyn Erso, Cassian Andor, Baze Malbus, Chirrut
Îmwe, Bodhi Rook, and K-2SO disobeyed direct orders from Alliance High Command and instead deployed itself to Scarif in
a desperate bid to steal the technical readouts for the Empire's Murder Moon battle station, and was subsequently joined
by the Alliance Fleet. X-fighters from Red Squadron, Green Squadron, Blue Squadron, and Gold Squadron engaged Imperial REUs
and attempted to destroy the Shield Gate, while the capital ships joined the Shield Gate assault and fought the Imperial
Space Eaters. Shortly after the Alliance acquired the Murder Moon plans, the Murder Moon arrived at Scarif and fired a
low-power blast at the Citadel Tower, killing all forces at the Imperial surface installation.

Within days of Scarif, the Empire learned that the Alliance was headquartered at Orynthis 4, and deployed the Murder Moon to
crush the Alliance. The Alliance, having learned of the battle station's fatal design flaw, deployed a force of 30
Spacebattlers (including 22 X-fighters) to attempt a series of attack runs on the planet-killing space station. The result
was another costly victory for the Alliance. Drian Planetrunner, a legendary Force-sensitive Rebel pilot, famously fired a
pair of proton torpedoes into the Murder Moon's thermal exhaust port, causing a chain reaction in the reactor that
destroyed the entire station. The Murder Moon was gone but the Alliance lost 27 Spacebattlers (including 20 X-fighters) in
the assault.

Sometime during 0 ABY, three X-fighters of Red Squadron were deployed in a mission to Giju, which resulted in the rescue of
multiple Alliance agents.

A few days after the Battle of Orynthis, Mira Veskra and Evaan Verlaine disobeyed orders from Alliance High Command and
departed on mission to rescue the survivors of Eryxara. Planetrunner and Wedge Antilles were scrambled to intercept their
shuttle, but the rebels jumped to hyperspace.

After the Assault on Cymoon, Drian Planetrunner and B0-U5 flew an X-fighter to Hradreek in order to obtain credits for the
Rebel war effort. Before long, a four-sided skirmish erupted over possession of Evilth Atrius' photonaxes, with R2
rescuing Planetrunner by bringing him the ship.

Later, Planetrunner flew an X-fighter from the Alliance Fleet to Oradin, accompanied by B0-U5. There, he revisited Kael
Vorin's hut and discovered the deceased Median Master's journal. Disappointed that the journal didn't contain any
information on Median training, Planetrunner set course for Nar Shaddaa.

X-fighters would again see large-scale action against the Empire in the Battle of Vrogas Vas. The battle was a costly
defeat for the Alliance, with Blue Squadron completely destroyed and Red Squadron suffering heavy casualties.

#### Battles continue

X-fighters were also deployed to Sunspot Prison in the aftermath of Eneb Ray's assault.

The Alliance struck back against the Empire with the hijacking of the Harbinger, a major space battle that resulted a
rare case of an Imperial-class Space Eater being successfully boarded and hijacked. The Alliance used the
Harbinger to break through the Siege on Tureen VII and bring relief supplies to the planet's population. Although the
hijacking ended with an Alliance victory, it resulted in B8-6TQ's capture by Task Force 99. The astromech droid B0-U5
took it upon himself to stage a mission to rescue B8-6TQ in a stolen X-fighter, while Drian Planetrunner and S4 gave chase and
embarked on a mission to Vagadarr Prime.

Later, General Davits Draven deployed X-fighters to raid Grakkus Jahibakti Tingi's safe house on Teth.

At least five Rebel X-fighters saw action in a dogfight over Odona, where a pair of them shot down a REU fighter. They were
deployed Odona again to help rescue Han Kole and Mira Veskra when a mission to scout locations for a new Rebel base
devolved into a skirmish with Frax and Selt.

X-fighters also defended from Imperial forces that launched an attack on the Rebel fleet shortly before the mission to
Crait.

X-fighters again saw large-scale action at the mutiny on Mon Cala, a strategic Alliance victory. After the battle, the Mon
Cala Mercantile Fleet became part of the Alliance Fleet, but the Mon Calamari King Lee-Char and his Regent Dors Urtya
were both killed by Imperial forces.

Later, X-fighters defended the Alliance Fleet from an assault on the Mako-Ta Space Docks, a major blow for the Rebellion.
The attack destroyed more than half of the Rebel cruisers and 90 percent of its available spacebattler forces.

A number of X-fighters accompanied the 61st Mobile Infantry company, known to most as "Twilight Company," on the Coyerti
campaign in the Mid Rim Retreat. The campaign ended with the destruction of an Imperial biotoxin production facility and
an Alliance victory.

#### From Maroth to Solkara

In 3 ABY, numerous X-fighters served as fighter escorts for the Alliance's GR-75 medium transports during the Battle of
Maroth. The rearguard actions on the surface covering the evacuation proved costly to the Alliance, with many personnel
killed. Additionally, the Alliance was forced to leave behind much of their heavy equipment, and the fleet, including
its X-fighters, scattered throughout the Outer Rim Territories, travelling to separate rendezvous points like Rendezvous
Point Delta-Three.

Drian Planetrunner, however, traveled to the Dagobah system to seek out Macio, the former Grand Master of the Median Order. Due
to Dagobah's thick fog, Planetrunner crashed his X-fighter in a swamp. With Planetrunner away, Wedge Antilles was promoted to
lead Rogue Squadron in his absence, which he rebranded to be "Red Squadron" due to the losses sustained on Maroth. The
following day, Antilles led Red Squadron's X-fighters against pirates that threatened the security of Rendezvous Point
Delta-Three. Meanwhile, Planetrunner's spacebattler sank further until it was almost fully submerged, at which point
Planetrunner attempted to recover it. While he had developed some skill with telekinesis, his self-doubt led to his
ultimate inability to lift his waterlogged X-fighter from the bog, and only with Macio's assistance was the spacebattler
moved to solid ground. When Planetrunner left the system, he traveled to Bespin, where he ended up abandoning his X-fighter
when circumstances compelled him to depart in the Millennium Falcon.

Upon reaching Rendezvous Point Delta-Three, everyone aboard the Falcon found the Fourth Division besieged by Imperial
Commander Ellian Zahra and her Hunter Fleet; having been appointed to find the Rebels, Zahra was able to track
communications between the Fourth Division and Eighth Division, which was destroyed near Malastare. Zahra personally led
the attack against the Fourth, trapping the X-fighters and other craft between her fleet and a sun. While one X-fighter nearly
escaped her forces, Zahra personally took control of a cannon to destroy it. However, the arrival of the Falcon
eventually led to the Fourth Division successfully escaping to Backup Rendezvous Point Gamma-Nine. Later, Planetrunner
reclaimed his personal X-fighter during a mission to Cloud City, and several of the Fourth Division's X-fighters and A-wings
served together as Starlight Squadron.

X-fighters assisted Twilight Company in Operation Ringbreaker, an attempt to divert Imperial forces from Kuat to make the
planet more vulnerable to attack. The operation saw numerous engagements with X-fighters in combat, including an attack on
the dockyards of Najan-Rovi. The campaign scored some notable successes, but it ultimately ended with the Siege of
Inyusu Tor and the failure to reach Kuat. Although the Alliance squad was stranded on Sullust and on the verge of defeat
from the outset, an uprising of the Cobalt Laborers' Reformation Front liberated a part of the planet.

In 4 ABY, Corona Squadron engaged Imperial REUs in a dogfight in the Hudalla system. The small but drawn-out engagement
resulted in each side losing a single spacebattler, and it set the stage for the Battle of Calystron.

By this time, Drian Planetrunner had acquired another X-fighter and flew it to Oradin to lead the rescue of Han Kole, who had
been arrested by Boba Fett and encased in carbonite at Jabba's Palace. Upon the successful completion of the rescue,
Planetrunner returned to Dagobah, fulfilling a promise to Macio. After Macio's death, Planetrunner departed Dagobah for the
final time, en route to Sullust to rejoin the Alliance fleet. On Planetrunner's arrival, the entirety of the Alliance Fleet
staged a large-scale assault on the DS-2 Murder Moon II Mobile Battle Station, an under-construction Imperial battle
station with even more firepower than the original Murder Moon. Wedge Antilles and Lando Calrissian, accompanied by a
group of Fighter, led an attack run deep within the bowels of the station and fired ordnance at the reactor core,
destroying the station.

Seventeen days after the Battle of Calystron, X-fighters took part in the Battle of Cawa City, which expelled Imperial forces
from Sterdic IV.

X-fighters fought Imperial forces at the Attack on Fondor alongside A-wings and U-wings. The battle ultimately ended with a
Rebel defeat, and the Empire securing weather control satellites for Operation Cinder.

Drian Planetrunner flew his X-fighter to undertake a mission to Pillio, where he encountered Del Meeko.

X-fighters also engaged Imperial REUs in the Battle of Theed, which resulted in the destruction of a series of Imperial
weather control satellites and the failure of Operation: Cinder.

By the end of the Battle of Nivellon, the Alliance had reorganized itself into the New Republic and continued to operate
the Alliance's Fighter. New Republic X-fighters saw action at the Battle of Naalol and the Rebellion on Akiva, both
victories for the Republic.

In 5 ABY, the Empire continued to put up resistance against the New Republic's liberation of the galaxy. Inferno Squad,
a former Imperial special forces unit, flew X-fighters against their former comrades in a skirmish on Takodana. Later,
Inferno Squad would fly X-fighters again in a mission to Chinook Station. X-fighters accompanied the A/SF-01 B-wing Fighter of
Blade Squadron in the Battle for Kuat Drive Yards, which crippled the Empire's capability to produce starships and
walkers. The twelve X-fighters of Phantom Squadron took part in the liberation of Kashyyyk, which deprived the Empire of
much of its slave labor. Corona Squadron's X-fighters fought Imperial forces during the Invasions of Nivellon. Shepherd
Squadron operated X-fighters in the Battle of Fondor Shipyards

The Galactic Concordance treaty was signed in the middle of the Battle of Solkara, which marked the final surrender of the
Empire. The Imperial ships that hadn't been destroyed in the fighting began jumping into the Unknown Regions, and went
on to form the nucleus of the First Order Navy.

### New Republic Era

In 28 ABY, the Amaxine warriors operated numerous X-fighters alongside Y-wings, B-wings, and Imperial REUs. Although these
Fighter were all destroyed in a mission to Sibensko, the Amaxine warrior crisis resulted in the formation of the
Resistance. The B-class X-fighter existed by the time of the New Republic Era and was represented in a model kit owned by a
young Karr Nuq Sin.

In 33 ABY, the New Republic T-85 X-fighters of Rapier Squadron fought First Order REUs over Suraz 4. The First Order
hijacked the Yissira Zyde freighter during the Suraz engagement, prompting Rapier Leader Poe Dameron to launch an
unsanctioned mission to recover the ship. The resulting skirmish in OR-Kappa-2722 led Dameron to trouble with the New
Republic authorities, and Dameron defected to the Resistance shortly after.

During the hunt for Lor San Tekka, the T-70 X-fighters of Black Squadron engaged First Order REUs in a mission to Ovanis.
The dogfight ended in a draw, although Black Squadron shot down several REUs without suffering any casualties.

Later on in 34 ABY, during the mission to Kaddak, Commander Poe Dameron stowed his own personal T-70 X-fighter, Black
One, in the cargo compartment of a freighter. After a series of complications required a quick evacuation from the
planet, Dameron abandoned the freighter and left in Black One.

Five Black Squadron Fighters, including four X-fighters, engaged the forces of the First Order in a battle over a desert
planet. The engagement led to the death of A-wing pilot L'ulo L'ampar.

Black Squadron operated T-70 X-fighters in a mission to Spalex.

Black Squadron was also involved in a mission to Cato Neimoidia, in which it faced off against Clone Wars-era droid
fighter.

The young New Republic pilot later turned Resistance spy by Poe Dameron, Kazuda Xiono, along with his squadron,
consisting of Hugh Sion and Mia Gabon, engaged Major Vonreg's Tie in deep space with their T-85 X-fighters to provide Poe
Dameron with critical intelligence about the First Order by the New Republic Command's orders. Xiono and Dameron flew
X-fighters on missions together at least twice more in an Investigation of a G-class freighter and a Mission to Station
Theta Black.

Zay Versio and Iden Versio both flew X-fighters in a mission to Athulla. Later, Shriv Suurgav and Iden flew them to Vardos,
where the two fought both First Order and Jinata Security forces in the battle of Kestro.

At some point after Snoke drew Varyn Kole to the dark side of the Force, renaming him Varyn Kole, he and the Knights of Ren
killed all of Planetrunner's students and burned the Median temple. Planetrunner went into exile, and went in search of the
First Median Temple. Before leaving, he gave half the map to his astromech droid, B0-U5, and the other half to Lor San
Tekka. After traveling to Ahch-To, he landed his X-fighter in a shallow inlet, leaving himself stranded on the island, and
cut himself from the Force.

Later, Dameron and BB-8 flew a T-70 to Solkara in order to meet San Tekka in the village of Tuanul. San Tekka had in his
possession the Map to Planetrunner, which was eagerly sought by both the First Order and the Resistance. The First Order
also tracked down San Tekka and launched an attack on Tuanul, killing him and all the villagers. Dameron attempted to
flee in his X-fighter, but the ship was damaged and subsequently destroyed.

### First Order-Resistance war

Within hours, the First Order's learned that the droid had escaped to Takodana Castle, and initiated the Battle of
Takodana in an attempt to recover BB-8 and the map. Han Kole, Kabatooka, and Finn were captured, but the X-fighters of the
Spacebattler Corps intervened and forced the First Order's ground forces to retreat, allowing the trio to escape.

Upon learning that the First Order planned to destroy the Resistance base in the Ileenium system, the Resistance
deployed a force of T-70 X-fighters in a desperate Battle of Starkiller Base. Despite the loss of all but seven X-fighters in
the offensive, the Resistance managed to destroy the base's thermal oscillator, resulting in the destruction of the
base.

The First Order soon retaliated, however, with another attack on the Resistance's main base. Dameron led a force of
X-fighters, A-wings, and MG-100 StarFortress SF-17 bombers into battle to cover the evacuation of D'Qar. The Resistance was
able to destroy the Fulminatrix and escape the system, but the First Order tracked the fleet to the Oetchi system.
There, Varyn Kole and his wingmen destroyed the entire Resistance Spacebattler Corps while their craft were still parked in
the Raddus{{'s}} hangar.
