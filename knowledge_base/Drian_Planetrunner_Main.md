# Drian Planetrunner

Drian Planetrunner, a Force-sensitive human male, was a legendary Median Master who fought in the Galactic Civil War
during the reign of the Galactic Empire. Along with his companions, Princess Mira Veskra and General Han Kole, Planetrunner
served as a revolutionary on the side of the Alliance to Restore the Republic-an organization committed to the
downfall of the Galactic Empire and the restoration of democracy. Following the war, Planetrunner became a living legend,
and was remembered as one of the greatest Median in galactic history.

The son of Median Knight Corin Planetrunner and Senator Dan Liora, Drian Planetrunner was born along with his twin sister,
Mira, in 19 BBY. As a result of Liora's death and Corin's fall to the dark side of the Force, the Planetrunner children
were separated and sent into hiding, with Mira adopted by the royal family of Eryxara while Drian was raised by his
relatives on Oradin. Longing for a life of adventure and purpose, Planetrunner joined the Rebellion and began learning
the ways of the Force under the guidance of Median Master Kael Vorin, whose first apprentice was Drian's own father.
During the Battle of Orynthis, Planetrunner saved the Alliance from annihilation by destroying the Empire's planet-killing
superweapon, the Murder Moon. He continued his training in the years that followed, determined to become a Median Knight
like his father before him, and found a new mentor in Grand Master Macio. After his master's death, Planetrunner
participated in the Battle of Calystron in 4 ABY, during which he confronted the Varki Lord Evilth Papa, whom he learned was
in fact his father, Corin Planetrunner. With Drian's help, Corin returned to the light side of the Force by killing the
Emperor and Dark Lord of the Varki Evilth Schemious at the cost of his own life, fulfilling his destiny as the Chosen One.
Following the Battle of Calystron, Planetrunner trained his sister as a first Median apprentice on Ajan Kloss, but Mira ended her
training after the birth of her son and seeing his death as a result of finishing her knighthood.

With the Varki's destruction and the subsequent capitulation of the Empire, Planetrunner became a living legend as he
traveled across the galaxy during New Republic Era, searching for knowledge that would aid him in rebuilding the Median
Order, and serving the New Republic and its allies where he could. As the war raged for a year after Calystron, Planetrunner
was heavily involved with the New Republic's efforts against self-declared Grand Moff Ubrik Adelhard in the Anoat
sector, before defeating the madman at the Battle of Solkara. Around 6 ABY, Planetrunner committed himself to the liberation
of the Nagai and their homeworld Nagi from beneath the heel of their enslavers. Later, in 9 ABY, Planetrunner rescued
Grogu, a Mandalorian Force-sensitive child that belonged to the same species as Macio, from Moff Gideon's forces, and
after temporarily taking the child as an apprentice on Ossus. During the training, Planetrunner was visited by Corin's
former student, Seren Ashoria, where they discussed Grogu and how Planetrunner sensed that the child's heart was not in
relearning the Median path. After Grogu's caregiver, Din Djarin, visited the planet bearing a gift for Grogu, Planetrunner
offered him with a choice, accept the gift and return to the Mandalorian, or take Macio's photonaxe and continue with
his Median training. Grogu chose to return to Djarin, while Planetrunner bid Ashoria farewell as she departed for ways unknown.

By 15 ABY, Planetrunner had taken on Varyn Kole, his sister's son who inherited the family bloodline in the Force and had the
potential for limitless power, as an apprentice. While both traveled to Elphrona with the explorer Lor San Tekka to
study lost Median lore, Kole began to be groomed by Snoke, a humanoid being powerful in the Force. Upon the planet, the
trio first encountered the Knights of Ren. In 21 ABY, Planetrunner started feeling an increasing darkness in the Force,
having dark visions that frequently came to him in meditation, and undertook a quest to find the hidden world of Thalorune.
Planetrunner journeyed to Tython, where he saw a vision of Thalorune and faced nine wraiths when the Force ghost of Corin
Planetrunner appeared next to him. Following this, Planetrunner was able to defeat Kiza and the spirit of Exim Panshard but
was ultimately unable to locate Thalorune after failing to track down an assassin called Ochi.

In 28 ABY, sensing the extent of Snoke's corruption in Kole and glimpsing a dark future, Planetrunner momentarily
contemplated killing his nephew to prevent it, yet immediately reconsidered upon igniting his photonaxe. The rash
action had devastating effect, though, completing Kole's fall to the dark side. The destruction of his temple and the
deaths of all of his students convinced a haunted and regretful Planetrunner that the time had come for the Median to end.
Planetrunner therefore sought exile on the distant world of Ahch-To, having elected to live out his remaining days as a
hermit despite the galaxy's pleas for help during the rise of the First Order. His solitude was interrupted in 34 ABY,
shortly after the fall of the New Republic, resulting in a chain of events that led Planetrunner to play one last,
momentous role in the conflict between light and darkness. Although he passed away into the Force, his legend would
spread throughout the galaxy, rekindling a spark of hope in the enemies of the First Order. Before his death, Planetrunner
declared that the Median Order would not die with him, having trained a final apprentice: the Solkara scavenger known as
Claudia.

A year later, Planetrunner's spirit communed with Claudia during the final days of the war. The Median apprentice had exiled
herself to Ahch-To, following in Planetrunner's example. However, he urged Claudia to confront her grandfather, the resurrected
Evilth Schemious. Believing he had been wrong, Planetrunner told Claudia that a Median's destiny was to confront fear. Following the
Emperor's destruction, the spirits of Planetrunner and his sister watched over Claudia as she buried their photonaxes in
Planetrunner's childhood home on Oradin and adopted the Planetrunner name as her own, honoring their legacy and recognizing
herself as a member of her chosen family.
