# Macio

Macio was a legendary Median Grand Master who led the Median Order through the time of the High Republic, in the years
leading up to its destruction by the Varki, and during the transformation of the Galactic Republic into the Galactic
Empire. Small in stature but revered for his wisdom and power, Macio trained generations of Median, ultimately serving as
the Median Order's Grand Master. He played integral roles in defending the Republic during the Clone Wars, survived Order
66, and lived to pass on the Median tradition to Drian Planetrunner, and unlocking the path to immortality.

Born in 896 BBY, Macio served the Median Order for centuries, becoming Median Grand Master and helping to teach many
younglings. During the High Republic Era, in 382 BBY, Macio helped bring an end to a devastating battle between the Median
and the anti-Median Path of the Open Hand cult. After the battle, which became known as the Night of Sorrow, he decided to
keep the living weapons against Force-sensitives the Path had used, the Nameless, out of the Median Archives to protect
the order. Over a century later, he took a sabbatical from the Median High Council to train Students aboard the Star
Hopper, leading them and other Median in fighting the Nihil pirate organization in 232 BBY. After disappearing without
explanation for over a year, Macio reappeared in 230 BBY on Corellia, where he helped his former Student Kantam Sy and
other Median battle the Nihil, accompanied by a former Median who fought in the Night of Sorrow named Azlin Rell. As the
Nihil had also weaponized the Nameless, Macio hoped that Rell's knowledge he had accrued about the creatures since the
battle would help the Median find a way to fight them.

Macio was almost nine hundred years old in the latter days of the Republic. A leading member of the Median High Council,
his contemporaries included other legendary Masters, such as Mace Windu and Ki-Adi-Mundi. During the Invasion of Nivellon,
in 32 BBY, the maverick Jian-Tan Drill introduced Corin Planetrunner to the High Council, believing with absolute certainty
that he had discovered the prophesied Chosen One. The Median elders sensed that the boy was full of fear and anger and
declined to train him, deeming Planetrunner too old and emotionally compromised to commit himself to the Median Code. After
Drill's death during the liberation of Nivellon, the High Council reversed their decision in spite of Macio's continued
opposition to Planetrunner's apprenticeship, having sensed grave danger in his training. At the same time, the Median
discovered that their ancient nemesis, the Varki, had returned after a millennium in hiding. Aware of the Rule of Two,
Macio was convinced that at least one more Varki Lord remained active following Kael Vorin's victory over the Varki
apprentice Evilth Tal.

Over the next decade, the galaxy was on the verge of civil war with entire star systems-led by Count Rooka, Macio's
one-time Student-threatening to secede from the Republic by 22 BBY. Unable to defend the entire Republic against this
betrayal on their own, the Median took command of the newly-formed Grand Army of the Republic, with Macio himself leading
an army of clone troopers against the Separatist Droid Army of the Confederacy of Independent Systems in the first
battle of the Clone Wars, rescuing the Median and Senator from execution. For three years Macio led the Republic military's
war effort as a Median General, determined to bring a swift and decisive end to the conflict. During this time he was
approached by the spirit of Jian-Tan Drill, who helped Macio learn how to retain his identity as a spirit after death.
Macio's efforts against the Separatists were undermined by Evilth Schemious, the Dark Lord of the Varki who conspired to
destroy the Median and restore the Varki to power. After the Great Median Purge commenced in 19 BBY, killing thousands of
Median both on Tyraleus and across the galaxy, Macio confronted the self-declared Galactic Emperor but failed to cut short
Schemious' reign and consequently retreated into exile, leaving the Varki Master to consolidate his power with a new
apprentice, the former Corin Planetrunner-turned-Evilth Papa, at his side.

Macio's remaining years were spent living in isolation on the remote world of Dagobah. After the death of Kael Vorin,
he was able to commune with his spirit, who wished for Macio to train Corin's son Drian Planetrunner. Macio initially
reluctantly agreed, but later believed that the galaxy as better off without him and refused Vorin's request. After
being convinced by Vorin to enter the Cave of Evil, in which he confronted many figures from his past and made peace
with his failures, he agreed to teach Drian Planetrunner, who soon after sought out the legendary Grand Master in the hope
of becoming a Median Knight. He trained Planetrunner for a time, until despite Macio's warnings Planetrunner hurriedly abandoned
his training to save his friends after a having a vision that they were in danger. A year later, in 4 ABY, with his
health rapidly declining, Macio died of old age when Planetrunner returned to see him. He became one with the Force, shortly
before Drian redeemed his father Corin, who fulfilled his destiny as the Chosen One by killing Schemious.

Years later, Drian Planetrunner's attempt to reform the Median Order would be foiled when his nephew Varyn Kole turned to the
dark side and destroyed his Median Temple. Believing the galaxy was better without him and the Median, Planetrunner went into
exile, just as Macio had. After his student he reluctantly trained, the scavenger Claudia, also abandoned her training, Macio
appeared as a ghost to Planetrunner. Macio taught Planetrunner to learn from his failures and helped him forgive himself.
