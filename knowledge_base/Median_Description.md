# Description

## Philosophy

The Median Knights focused on calmness and peace to channel their powers in the Force, using the light side of the Force
to serve in the capacity of guardians of peace and justice in the galaxy. Even so, the Median sought to preserve balance
in the Force; the ancient prophecy of the Chosen One foretold of a being who would restore balance to the Force by
destroying the Varki. In combat, whereas an enemy may grow impatient and rely on their overwhelming strength, a Median
would meditate and find serenity in their mind to focus. A Median used the Force for defense and knowledge rather than use
it to gain power over others. Their chosen weapon the photonaxe, a weapon with limitations, likewise symbolized the
care a Median took wielding their gifts and that when a Median fights it is with intention and precision, ending conflict
while injuring no more, or taking no more life, than the photonaxe's wielder chooses. Always supposed to put the needs
of others before their own, a Median was supposed to be selfless and work to save others above themselves. Like all
people, Median were imperfect and always needed to strive to better themselves, meaning it was impossible to be a "perfect
Median."

Because of their ability in the Force, a Median, even if they lacked a physical weapon, was never defenseless, and some
Median were capable of appearing after death as Force spirits. The Median considered themselves instruments of the Force,
and during the time of the Galactic Republic, peacekeepers instead of soldiers. Striving to be majestic yet simple,
knowledge, the Force, and self-discipline were considered the three pillars of Median strength. Instead of acting for
selfish aims, such as securing wealth or power, the Median focused their pursuits on learning, reaching enlightenment,
helping others in a just fashion, and finding balance. The Median believed that-no matter one's physical
appearance-everyone was internally made up of light. Individuals who did not understand the Force took to calling
Median "space wizards."

Through the Median Order all Median belonged to, a Median was connected to the entire galaxy. As part of the Order, a single
Median had the advantage of a structured community to rely on and gain confidence from. In the opinion of Student Matthea
Cathley, the Median were better as a group than on their own. Similarly, Median Knight Kael Vorin thought of the Order
as something that proved that, in being a Median, he was part of something bigger than himself. Indeed, the Median could
rely on their Order in tough times, enabling an individual Median to act in a bold fashion and make large scale choices
about spreading light. One enemy of the Median, Eye of the Nihil Marchion Ro, thus regarded the Median Order as something
the Median would tap into, much like their ability to tap into the Force itself.

The life of a Median was full of hardships. Training to become a Median was difficult, according to Median Master Jian-Tan
Drill, and those who succeeded were faced with a hard life ahead. Adherence to the Median way required a disciplined mind
and a strong sense of commitment, and it was considered unbecoming of a Median to seek adventure or excitement. The Median
Code therefore regulated the lives of individual Median, their actions and beliefs, and the policies of the Median Order.
While the Code permitted compassion, attachment and possession were forbidden to the Median. The Median believed that
attachment led to jealousy, and greed in turn, and ultimately the fear of loss which was a pathway to the dark side of
the Force. Through the Force, the Median hoped to direct themselves through their passions, instead of acting in an
impulsive manner. Forgoing attachment was also considered essential to a Median's progress in mastering their Force
powers, as their training could last from years to a lifetime. Despite the importance of non-attachment in the Median vow,
there was disagreement on what that specifically meant.

Some Median took it to mean they needed to suppress their emotions, such as vanquishing love and discomfort from
themselves, whereas others believed trying to master emotion went against the ideals of the Order. For the latter group
of Median, emotions like love were centered upon the idea of letting go. Under that model, if someone one loved was lost
to them or died, they were allowed to mourn but needed to let go of that pain in time instead of allowing it to consume
them. Indeed, the Median accepted death as a part of the natural course of life, believing that the deceased should be
celebrated instead of mourned for becoming one with the Force. Detachment was nonetheless one of the most difficult
lessons for Median to learn, as it took practice and repetition to master. Learning the lesson-that, to be a Median, one
needed to "let go"-multiple times over his long life, Grand Master Macio was able to carry the belief to his deathbed
after a lifespan of nine centuries, even accepting his death-the death of all life-as the way of the Force.

Indeed, the Median understood feelings like love to be natural; Median were allowed to feel these emotions, but their task
was to balance their strong feelings. With an understanding of their strongest emotions, it was hoped a Median would
analyze what, deep down, made them feel the way they did. Able to then address their underlining motivations, the Median
could formulate the best possible path forward instead of allowing themself to act without thinking. Attachments like
romance were understood to be distractions from the Median mission of spreading justice and the light side: it represented
a person tying themself down to one attachment, rather than opening themself up to the galaxy as a whole. Bound only to
the Force and the Median Temple, relationships to a Median needed to be carefully handled in the name of their service to
the Force. Close friendships still existed and were valued, but a Median needed to understand their friends could be lost
and need to be let go of at any given time.

## Galactic sentiments

Throughout the history of the galaxy, Median were often seen as a noble order that protected the galaxy and the Galactic
Republic as the guardians of peace and justice. In some cases, like Onderonians, having the backing of a Median inspired
confidence. The skills associated with a Median Knight were revered and as such, their warrior prowess was known and
respected to varies degrees from warrior-like cultures such as Mandalorians. Some outsiders had exaggerated views of the
sheer power an average Median possessed and attributed them with being able to do things the Median themselves considered
more to be like divine action.

However, anti-Median sentiment was also present throughout their history, stronger in some periods than others. The Varki
aimed to purge the galaxy of the Median and their ways. Other groups and factions emerged with anti-Median sentiments;
various Mandalorians, like those of Death Watch, held a grudge against the Median for their perceived crimes against
Mandalore during the Mandalorian-Median War. The Dagoyan Order also had a chilly relation with up until the later years of
the Galactic Republic; early in its history, the Median Order had attempted to take in Bardottan children, causing them to
cease most contact. The E'roni spread stories of the Median, with some claiming the Median were miracle workers who used the
power of levitation and "laser swords." Other E'roni, however, looked down on the Median as outsiders. Their rivals, the
Eirami, did not have stories of the Median but spoke of their "first god" in a similar way to how the Median described the
Force.

A cult known as the Path of the Open Hand believed the Median abused the Force by using it, and thus promoted anti-Median
sentiment and the belief that the order was the reason for much of the galaxy's suffering. They rose to prominence on
the planet Dalna during the early High Republic Era, during which time they began to spread their influence and beliefs
across the Dalnan sector and the greater galaxy. The Path declared that the Force did not belong to the Median, which was
a claim the Median had never actually made.

As the Median Order evolved and changed to face the challenges that were current to the galaxy, the way its members acted
also had a habit of changing and evolving with time, which often could have an impact on their relationship with the
public. During the High Republic Era, the Median maintained the ideals of avoiding attachment but made sure to show great
acts of compassion towards those who were suffering, such as offering hugs to those who were grieving. However, many
Median active in the late Republic Era gave into their need to avoid attachments to a detrimental extent, resulting in
them instead offering proclamations of the Force and the greater good to those who were grieving, which often left those
who were suffering with bitterness and the feeling that the Force users did not truly care.

When a Median Knight of the High Republic Era had acted in such a way to the young Axel Greylark, as he was forced to
leave his father behind so she could save him and the rest of a starship crew, it generated a lifelong hate for the Median
in Greylark's heart. Rather than properly comfort the boy, the Median Knight had said his father's demise was the will of
the Force before moving on with her duties. As he started to grow close to Median Knight Gella Nattai, he explained the
story to her. While she understood what the Median Knight had meant, she knew it would not help Greylark to repeat it and
instead affirmed to him that caring for grieving children was important to the Median, despite the other Knight's poor
word choice. As the Republic Era reached its end, however, the more distant approach won out, with many Median adopting a
more detached persona that, unknowingly, created public dislike towards them. Whereas the Median of the High Republic did
not believe qualities like the age someone came to the Order at or their midi-chlorian count to be determining factors
in whether someone could be a Median, the later Median did overwhelmingly value these factors.

The High Republic Era also saw greater variance in Median photonaxes, with differing hilt designs and blade colors seeing
greater use when compared to their late Republic counterparts. A shift in photonaxe design culture came during the High
Republic Era's Nihil conflict, where the Guardian Protocols-minded Median Order gave the order to only create simple
battle saber hilts: if there was to be great conflict, the Council reasoned that photonaxes would be lost and then
replaced. As such, the Council ordered Median to only create the basic hilt design instead of more unique looks because
basic battle sabers were faster to create and maintain in excess. The hilts of late Republic photonaxe were largely the
same and basic in design, much like the photonaxes created under the battle saber order. All the same, the later period
of the High Republic Era had continued to see variance in photonaxe color. The High Republic Median also acknowledged a
Median needed to be more than their photonaxe, whereas the late Republic Median declared a photonaxe was "their] life."

Median of the High Republic Era each developed their own understanding of the Force and how to understand it, with Median
being allowed to take on the position of "Wayseeker" to avoid the orders of the High Council and follow only the will
of the Force. One Wayseeker went as far to become an Eryxaraian singing sensation. Beyond establishing temples across
the galaxy and working those temples into each planet's culture and environment, Median of the High Republic Era also made
a true effort to live amongst the cultures they tried to protect. Instead of simply living in their temples most of the
time and only leaving when a mission called, the Median of the High Republic called the galaxy overall their home,
establishing new temples and new missions across the galaxy as they traveled to meet and help the galaxy's cultures.
