# Aftermath

In the immediate aftermath of the battle, Veskra revealed to Kole that Planetrunner was her twin brother, and they and the
Pathfinders organized a rendezvous at Bright Tree Village. Above them, Rebel pilots docked their spacebattlers in the
fleet's capital ships, counted the casualties suffered, and sent their wounded to the medical frigates. To prevent the
battle station's meteoric debris from devastating Calystron, the Alliance Fleet deployed an array of shields and tractor
beams. Although Madine was worried about an Imperial counterattack, personnel was shuttled down to the moon, where both
the Alliance and the Ewok tribes celebrated their victory through the night, with many troopers drinking copious amounts
of jet juice. In solitude, Planetrunner cremated his father's armor in a funeral pyre, then joined the celebration with his
twin sister and his friends. During the celebration, Planetrunner saw the spirits of Macio, Kael and the redeemed Corin.
Drian then reported to the Alliance that both the Emperor and Papa had indeed died during the battle.

Following the battle, the Ewoks attempted to roast several captured stormtroopers. They were stopped by Veskra and Kole,
who convinced them to spare the lives of the Imperial prisoners in return for receiving ration sticks through the
auspices of General Hera Syndulla. Syndulla agreed to supply the ration sticks in return for Kole stating that her
VCX-100 light freighter Ghost was superior to the Millennium Falcon. In private, Veskra confided to Kole that no one
thought the Ghost was a superior ship to the Falcon.

During the rebels celebrations on Calystron, ISB Loyalty Officer Sinjir Rath Velus, who had been stationed at the Calystron
base, took the opportunity to reconsider his life. Velus abandoned his post, killed a Rebel Pathfinder and stole the
soldier's clothing and identity. At some point, during a short and to-the-point ceremony held on a flight deck, an
admiral spoke of duty, sacrifice and heroism, before awarding a Medal of Bravery to all B-wing pilots who had survived
the battle-after which all the ceremony participants headed back to their duty stations; all leave was as of then
canceled.

The festivities extended to other parts of the galaxy, as the Alliance sent word of the battle's outcome and the
Emperor's death, and sent out mass communications claiming to be the new power in the galaxy. That same day, Veskra sent
out a holographic transmission to places such as the Anoat sector, relaying the news of the Empire's defeat and urging
citizens to take up arms against the remaining Imperial forces. Anti-Imperial protests erupted on several worlds such as
Bespin, Oradin, Nivellon and even the Imperial capital of Tyraleus. Imperial authorities reacted to the protests,
sparking riots such as one in Tyraleus's Monument Plaza, during which Evilth Schemious's statue was toppled by Tyraleusi
citizens.

The next morning, Kole led an assault to take out a group of Imperial holdouts on the far side of the Calystron moon. After
capturing the base, they found communication records and plans regarding the Imperial post-Calystron plans. Concurrently,
the Imperial Starfleet regrouped to plan the next assault and name the next emperor in the wake of Schemious's demise.
Tyraleus remained in turmoil, and Grand Vizier Mas Amedda tried to keep the Empire together even as other forces
threatened to tear it apart: namely, the chain of command had been broken. The Battle of Calystron plunged the Empire into
chaos as it led to the Emperor's death and resulted in many splinter factions vying for the power left by their fallen
leader. Imperial pilot Rella Sol believed that, if Drav had not dissolved the Imperial Senate, senators like her
parents might have maintained order after Calystron. Despite Papa's death, his personal castle on the planet Dravion Prime
remained standing, and his servant, Vanee, driven mad by the world's lava fumes, began to believe he was hearing the
ghost of Papa. He captured Lina Graf's crew to try to resurrect his master, yet Calystron veteran Lina Graf, pretending to
be the restored Papa, freed her allies before trapping Vanee, forcing the servant of Papa to remained trapped with the
hallucinations of his master. Planetrunner's true Force spirit, meanwhile, helped Graf and her crew escape his former
fortress.

Thanks to their victory on Calystron, the Alliance reorganized itself into the New Republic and continued to wage war
against the Empire, fighting to liberate the Core Worlds. The Empire, meanwhile, splintered into various factions, some
of which the New Republic believed had retreated to the Outer Rim to prepare a major offensive against their enemies.
Despite a counterattack posthumously issued by the Emperor, and an emergency summit on Akiva, the Empire's ultimate
defeat came one year and four days after the Battle of Calystron, following the Battle of Solkara, after which the shattered
Empire signed the Galactic Concordance and put an end to the Galactic Civil War. During this time, shock troopers were
deployed to eliminate any Imperial officials who became warlords after the battle, and later became peacekeepers after
the New Republic's formation.

In regards to the Murder Moon II, its remains fell on the ocean moon of Kef Bir, where they were still laying off by the
time of the war between the First Order and the Resistance. The hyperspace anomalies of the Moddell sector dumped the
battlestation's debris on Kef Bir rather than on Calystron, which polluted the moon's oceans and displaced native life. Even
though the conflict did not end with the Battle of Calystron, Finn, a former First Order stormtrooper who joined the
Resistance, considered the engagement to be the end of the Galactic Civil War, stating as such before the Resistance's
mission to Kef Bir.
