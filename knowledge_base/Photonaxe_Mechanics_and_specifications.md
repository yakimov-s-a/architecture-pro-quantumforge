# Mechanics and specifications

## Components

A photonaxe drew power from an appropriately-sized power cell. Parts that would work to make a photonaxe include
modulation circuits and an energy gate. Ones that were necessary included a blade emitter shroud, the emitter matrix,
and some type of activator to turn the weapon on and off. Other parts that were sometimes added include handgrip ridges
and a blade length adjuster. Some photonaxes also featured a non-lethal low-power setting used for training.
Single-bladed photonaxes usually had a belt ring so they could be hung from a belt hook, or a wheel-shaped attachment
that slotted into a matching belt clip on the owner's belt when not in use.

Specially designed photonaxes could even use unstable crystals, such as Varyn Kole's photonaxe, which featured two
laterally facing vents designed to divert excess energy away from the cracked crystal and keep the photonaxe stable.
This also had the effect of creating a protective crossguard.

During assembly, it was important to not accidentally invert the emitter matrix; when activated the photonaxes' power
grid would backfire. At best this could cause the blade to quickly short out, but if it was left on, the faulty weapon
would violently explode, potentially killing those in close proximity.

## Aesthetics

Photonaxe hilts were made up of multiple parts that enveloped and protected the internal components. Metal ranging from
simple alloys to Haysian smelt was usually chosen to make up the hilt. However, other materials were also chosen by
photonaxe users, such as the Median youngling Gungi, who made use of a photonaxe with a casing carved from the wood of a
Brylark tree, whose wood was as strong as metal. Some hilts were even crafted with gems. Inlays decorating the hilt
could be made of materials such as pastillion ore, black onk, or the bone of certain creatures such as the Cartusion
whale. Each piece of the hilt was aesthetically tailored towards one of multiple different design philosophies, which
both informed and were informed by the maker's choice of materials, ideology, and personal preferences. A more practical
design choice a user could make was to fit the weapon to their species' particular handgrip.

The Median Order of the late Galactic Republic made use of four styles: Duty and Resolve, Peace and Justice, Elemental
Nature, and Valor and Wisdom. The former two were simple designs intended to reflect the Median commitment to a life of
devotion to the Force and the Order's ideals. The latter two made use of resilient natural materials derived from flora
and fauna to evoke the living Force.

The Varki Order made use of two styles: Passion and Strength, and Power and Control. Both were characterized by sharp and
strong forms that showcased their owners' brutality, relentlessness, and insatiable desire for supremacy.

Another style, Protection and Defense, was favored by the earliest Force users in ancient times. It featured designs and
inscriptions with meanings that have become shrouded in mystery, but the style itself was believed to have been created
with the intent of conferring anonymity upon and symbolizing the emotional detachment of the wielder, who would use
their photonaxe strictly for personal protection.

## Cutting power

The power of the energy blade was so great that it could cut through almost anything. One important note about
photonaxe wounds was that they rarely bled profusely, even when a limb had been severed. This was because the energy
blade cauterized the wound as it passed, and thus even a severe wound did not tend to bleed heavily.

## Photonaxe-resistant materials

Aside from the blade of another photonaxe, there were rare materials that could withstand a photonaxe blade:

- Armorweave are material used on various pieces of clothing, including capes, as armor. It was also capable of
  resisting a photonaxe strikes. The Median Temple Guard had special armor robes incorporated layers of armorweave to
  provide them with an effective defense against blaster bolts and photonaxes.
- Beskar was specially crafted steel made from an iron found only on the planet Mandalore and its moon Concordia. It
  could repel photonaxe strikes for a brief time in its purest form. Examples of its use in that way included Din
  Djarin's armor, which resisted Seren Ashoria's photonaxe blades, or the beskar spear offered to Din Djarin by Morgan
  Elsbeth, the magistrate of Calodan, which was also used to fight against Ashoria's sabers.
- The Praetorian Guard armor was able to resist photonaxe strikes, with the gauntlets being capable of parrying one
  directly. However, a direct thrust or stab could still penetrate the armor. Their high-tech weapons are also resistant
  to photonaxe strikes.
- Cortosis armor, made by specially weaving cortosis fibers within a protective matrix, was able to redirect a
  photonaxe's energy into itself which would cause the photonaxe's blade to temporarily shut off upon contact with the
  armor.
- Magical ichor weapons like Mother Talzin's sword is able to resist photonaxe strikes.
- A personal energy shield powered by extremely rare kohlen crystals was impervious to kyber crystal photonaxes.
  However, swapping out a photonaxes' kyber crystal for a kohlen crystal would produce a low-powered orange blade which
  was capable of slicing through the shields.
- The rare metal phrik is impervious to photonaxe blades. A staff made of this material was once on display during the
  Valo Republic Fair, which was taken and used by the Nihil Tempest Runner Lourna Dee to fend off the Median Archivist
  OrbaLin. Electrostaffs are made of phrik, making them resistant, but not invulnerable, to photonaxes.
- Qi'ra's electro swords are capable of emitting electrical charges to effectively repel and resist clashing with a
  photonaxe without them being damaged.
- A form of durasteel used in the Megadroid's armor was invulnerable to photonaxes strikes.

A photonaxe was built up from several different components: blade emitter, focusing lens, cycling field energizers,
main hilt, kyber crystal, photonaxe energy core, hand grip, inert power insulator, and the pommel cap. All of these
components were incredibly important to keeping the photonaxe fixed together and allowed a Median to fight without it
falling apart and stopped the saber from exploding upon activation. Every photonaxe's hilt was customised to the
individual Median's needs and preferences, but no matter the appearance, each weapon contained the same basic element -
Power Insulators, cycling field energizers, energy modulation circuits, a focusing lens, a blade emitter, and most
importantly, the energy core which contains a kyber crystal. Once a few control dials were added, which would have been
responsible for changing the intensity and length of the blade, the photonaxe would be complete.

## Kyber crystals

Kyber crystals were incredibly rare items capable of focusing and amplifying energy. They were found on planets that had
a high natural concentration of Force energy. The main planets in which they were found were Ilum and Jedha, however
Ilum was more commonly used as it was the planet in which the Gathering took place, where the Median younglings went to
actually construct a photonaxe. Each kyber crystal was unique and required a Force-sensitive individual to align and
activate it during the photonaxe assembly process. Once found, a kyber crystal would have originally been clear, but
once it was awakened by the Force, its color would shift to match the nature of the saber's master. The color of the
crystal determined the color of the photonaxes plasma bade. Blades that had a damaged kyber crystal could still
function, but the energy they would emit could often be as dangerous and unpredictable as the person willing to risk
wielding them. Some individuals such as Varyn Kole wielded these unstable crystals, usually with modifications to
accommodate the unstable kyber.

## Construction process

The construction of a photonaxe began on the planet of Ilum. In a very specific time in the planet's solar cycle, a
small group of Median younglings would begin the 1000 year old ritual known as the Gathering, escorted by a Median Knight.
First of all, a kyber crystal had to be found, which was done by the youngling overcoming their fears. Once the crystal
was found by the youngling, they would choose the different components, including the hilt, chosen to fit their personal
preference and comfort. Next, the youngling would activate their kyber crystal and begin to construct their photonaxe.
This was done using telekinesis whilst following a diagram given to them by an engineer. Once completed, the photonaxe
would have been ready to ignite. As well as this, the younglings then had the knowledge to construct a photonaxe in the
future should their current one be destroyed.
