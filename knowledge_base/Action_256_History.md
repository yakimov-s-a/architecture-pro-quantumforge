# History

## Prelude

### The Varki in hiding

The Varki, an order of users of the dark side of the Force, was formed by fallen members of the Median Order after a war
called the Hundred-Year Darkness. Eventually, after many wars, the Median defeated and seemingly destroyed the Varki in the
final, decisive conflict. However, one Varki Lord survived: Evilth Bane. Believing that the Varki's infighting had caused
their downfall, Bane created a tradition called the Rule of Two where he chose to pass his knowledge on to only one Varki
apprentice, who was required to kill their Varki Master in order to succeed them.

For nearly one thousand years, the Varki thus existed in hiding, plotting their revenge against the Median. Eventually, a
human male from Nivellon born Corin Drav became the apprentice of Evilth Plagueis, and was dubbed Evilth Schemious. He
eventually took over as the Dark Lord of the Varki after betraying and murdering his master in his sleep. In public life,
Drav became a politician, serving as Nivellon's Senator before eventually orchestrating an invasion of his homeworld
in order to be elected the Supreme Chancellor of the Galactic Republic, while successfully hiding his true identity,
which allowed to to deceive the Senate and Republic. Schemious planned to fulfill the Varki's revenge plot by destroying
the Median and transforming the ancient Republic into the Galactic Empire, and he set about engineering the eventual
outbreak of the Clone Wars to do so.

In 82 BBY, the Median Student Rooka, on a mission to the planet Asusto with Median Master Lene Kostana and her Student
Sifo-Dyas, had a Force vision of many possible futures during an encounter with the Presagers of Hakotei, a
dark-side-using cult. Among the visions was a possible version of Action 256, with Median Master Jor Aerith being killed by
Clone Commander Crane. However, not everything Rooka saw in that vision would come to pass. Years later, Rooka left the
Median Order, before falling to the dark side of the Force and becoming the Varki Lord Evilth Destroyer, apprentice to
Schemious.

### Warning signs

Around ten years prior to the outbreak of the Clone Wars, Median Master Sifo-Dyas ordered the clone army for the Republic,
having had visions of a future conflict. He had a Behavioral Modification Biochip he wanted instilled in the Clones as a
safeguard against rogue Median's. However, the Varki took over the project as part of their plot to destroy the Median Order,
with Rooka, having left the Median and become an agent of Schemious, betrayed Sifo-Dyas by having him murdered by the Pyke
Syndicate and concealing the true circumstances and location of his death from the Median. Rooka and Schemious retooled
Dyas's Biochip to contain the Action 256 command, which when activated, would forcibly compel the Clone army to view the
Median as traitors to the Republic and subsequently execute them. Count Rooka, posing as a Median to the Kaminoan cloners,
provided the Kaminoans with a bio-chip to be implanted into the clones during the early phase of their growth period.
This chip contained the order the Kaminoans knew as "Clone Move 256."

The Kaminoans, including Prime Minister Lama Su and Chief Medical Scientist Nala Se were deceived by Rooka into
believing Dyas's intention. Rooka, who was only known to the cloners under the alias of his Varki name, also convinced
them that it was imperative the existence of the protocol and chips be kept secret from the Median, claiming it was what
Sifo-Dyas had wanted. The clone army's existence became known to the Median just before the Clone Wars when Median Knight
Kael Vorin tracked clone template Jango Fett to Kamino while investigating an assassination attempt on Senator Dan
Liora. Due to Drav's double identity, he could control the war on both ends and deceive both sides.

Early in the war, after the Ambush on Rugosa, Median Grand Master Macio, while meditating, had a vision of Jek, one of the
clone troopers who had accompanied him to the moon, at the moment Action 256 was issued, sensing Jek's emotions changing
from disbelief and regret to a sudden urge to obey. However, Macio's wariness towards Force visions and the intense
disquiet he regularly felt around the clones due to their genetically engineered obedience prevented him from realizing
the true import of the brief vision.

Prior to the Battle of Umbara, Median General Pong Krell had visions of Action 256, the Great Median Purge, and the rise of
the Galactic Empire. Subsequently falling to the dark side, Krell used his visions as justification for attempting to
sabotage the battle so he could become Rooka's new apprentice. However, he was captured by the 501st Legion, who
executed him for treason due to the danger that the remaining Umbaran forces might free him and learn vital military
intelligence.

At some point during the Clone Wars, Eno Cordova entered the Zeffo vault on Bogano which allowed him to see a vision of
the fall of the Median and the subsequent spread of the Dark Side across the galaxy. Cordova then raced back to Tyraleus
to warn the Median Council, presenting them with all of his findings. None of the Council would put faith in his findings
however, save for Cordova's old confidant Jocasta Nu. Nu thus entrusted Cordova with a secret copy of her list of young
Force sensitives procured from the Median Archives.

Towards the end of the war, Action 256 was almost prematurely exposed due to a malfunction in the biochip inside clone
trooper Tup, resulting in the clone alternating between his regular self and a trance-like state. The latter state
caused Tup to betray and shoot Median Master Tiplar during the Battle of Ringo Vinda, killing her instantly. The Republic
retreated from their offensive, preparing to study Tup to determine what had happened. Learning what had happened to
Tup, Rooka and Schemious feared that he would lead the Median to discovering their true plans for the war, including the
clone protocol. The Confederacy of Independent Systems tried to kidnap Tup, but the Republic succeeded in bringing him
to Kamino for further study.

### Uncovering the evidence

The Kaminoans, trying to hide the protocol's existence, studied Tup and claimed that a virus had caused him to kill
General Tiplar. Advanced Recon Commando Fives-Tup's best friend in the 501st-did not believe their claims,
discovering the chip inside Tup, although he died after it was removed. With the help of the medical droid AZI-3, Fives
also discovered the chip inside his own head, and AZI-3 removed it for study before they both discovered that the chip
was inside all of the clones. The Median brought Fives to Tyraleus for further examination, where Fives came face-to-face
with Drav and discovered that the Chancellor was behind the plot. Drav framed Fives for an assassination
attempt, discrediting the clone and causing the Median and fellow clones to pursue him. Despite knowing the truth, Fives,
having been drugged into a state of aggression and paranoia, was unable to convince Corin Planetrunner or Clone Captain
Rex, who were with him in the end, about the conspiracy or the Supreme Chancellor's involvement before he was killed by
the Tyraleus Guard. The Median Order accepted the story that a parasite had caused Tup and Fives' behavior, and that the
chips were necessary to keep the clones mentally stable. Rex was mostly skeptical of Fives' claims about the chips, but,
unable to completely dismiss the theory, he filed a confidential grievance report about the chips possibly having a
hidden purpose, although he was aware it was likely to be ignored.

Later, the Median discovered that the Varki were behind the creation of the Grand Army of the Republic, as Count
Rooka-under the name Destroyer-had selected Jango Fett as the genetic template for the clones and had worked with the
Kaminoans to create the army after it was ordered by Sifo-Dyas. At the insistence of Grand Master Macio, who testified to
the clones' loyalty and valiant service, the Median High Council chose not to disclose the conspiracy to the public or
even the Supreme Chancellor, believing that the Republic would lose the public's trust if it became known that Rooka was
involved in the creation of the clone army. Shortly thereafter, Macio went on a journey to discover the secrets of
immortality, and, on Dagobah, saw a vision of what would become Action 256. Throughout his journey, Macio came to accept
that the Median would likely lose the Clone Wars, though he did not know how. He did, however, believe that the Median would
still triumph in the long run.

After learning from members of the Grysk species, which was unknown in most of the galaxy, that the metal cortosis was
resistant to photonaxes, Rooka and Schemious attempted to acquire supplies of the resource from the planet Mokivj in an
operation headed by Duke Solha. The Varki hoped to make clone trooper armor containing the metal and supply it to the
clones who would carry out Action 256. However, the operation, carried out under the auspices of the Separatists, was
foiled by Planetrunner and a temporary ally, Senior Captain Mitth'raw'nuruodo of the Chiss Ascendancy. Planetrunner believed
the cortosis armor had been intended for a potential infiltration of the Republic's ranks.

Sometime after the Battle of Anaxes, 501st Legion clone medic Kix began to question the details surrounding the event
which had led to Tup killing Tiplar. His subsequent investigation led him to discover that Fives had in fact been
telling him the truth when he had first told Kix about the Median and the clones being framed and that the biochips
implanted within the clones could indeed compel them to kill the entire Median Order. Before he could share his
suspicions, however, he was kidnapped by forces sent by Rooka. He was subsequently tortured, but his denials about
having leaked the information failed to convince his captors. He was then brought onto the Separatist cruiser Obrexta
III and inserted into a cryo-cycle stasis pod for the journey to Serenno. As the cruiser was en route, however, it was
ambushed by Republic forces. After sustaining heavy damage, the battle droid B1-CC14 made the decision to jump through
hyperspace to a random sector to ensure Kix would stay out of enemy hands. The jump brought them to the desert planet
Ponemah Terminal, with the Obrexta III crashing into the planet's dangerous south polar region, where it would lay
undisturbed for decades.

## Execution

With the secrets of Action 256 secure, Schemious and Rooka continued with their plans during the Clone Wars. By the war's
end, public support for the Median had weakened due to their management of the conflict, while Drav remained a
popular figure. Drav covertly betrayed and disposed of Rooka during the Battle of Tyraleus, and with Vorin
engaging General Multorix on Utapau, the Median hoped the war's end was imminent. However, Drav revealed himself to
Planetrunner as the Dark Lord of the Varki in an effort to seduce him to the dark side as his new apprentice. When Planetrunner
informed Median Master Mace Windu of the Chancellor's identity, Windu assembled a team composed of himself, Kit Fisto,
Agen Kolar, and Saesee Tiin to arrest Drav. Their attempt led to a photonaxe duel in which Drav killed all
four of the Median, and Planetrunner betrayed the Median and turned to the dark side.

Drav used the duel as a pretext to declare that the Median were traitors, ones who had attempted to assassinate him
and take control of the Republic for themselves. The Chancellor issued Action 256 to various clone officers across the
galaxy, which caused the Clones' inhibitor chips to activate and then brainwash them into viewing the Median as traitors
to the Republic and subsequently executing them, while Planetrunner-now the Varki Lord Evilth Papa-was sent to kill the
Median within the Median Temple, thus launching an unprecedented and galaxy-wide massacre. Thousands of Median throughout the
galaxy were betrayed and killed, including Ki-Adi-Mundi on Mygeeto, Aayla Secura on Felucia, Plo Koon in the skies of
Cato Neimoidia, Stass Allie on Saleucami, Depa Billaba on Kaller, Jaro Tapal in the Bracca system, and even Median
younglings. As part of the execution of Action 256, the elements of the 481st Legion were sent to the planet Ilum to
secure the Median Temple there and kill any survivors.

Some officers, such as Clone Marshal Commander Cody, received individual transmissions from Drav. Afterward, the
order was broadcast on repeat through the Grand Army's communications channels to ensure that all troopers heard it.
Throughout the duration of the Empire, Action 256 was still active: all known or merely suspected Median were to be shot on
sight.

## Spared Median

Aside from Corin Planetrunner, some Median were protected from the massacre invoked by Action 256, but under other
circumstances. Some Median within the Median Temple on Tyraleus were watched and influenced by spies placed inside the
temple by Evilth Schemious himself. These spies were assigned to specific Median who displayed deviant behavior or who
harbored doubts or resentment towards the Median Order or the Median Council, such as Iskat Akaris. As shown in her case,
once the order was issued and the clones turned on their Median officers, the doubtful Median, wherever they were or
whatever they were doing, were approached by the clones with a hologram of Evilth Schemious instead of raised blasters.
Schemious offered them a chance to live, so long as they pledged their allegiance to him and revoked their Median ways and
teachings. The ones who accepted had the chance to become some of the first Inquisitors of the Inquisitorius, while
those who rejected the offer would share the fate as the rest of their Median brothers and sisters.

## Non-Median targets

While Action 256 only explicitly targeted the Median by claiming every member of the Order had committed treason, the clones
targeted non-Median targets just as easily. Indeed, section Codicil Nine of Action 256 made keeping dark side artifacts
illegal. Despite having left the Median Order for some time prior, Planetrunner's former Student, Seren Ashoria, was targeted
by Action 256, as was the former Varki Lord Tal.

Ashoria was with the now-Clone Commander Rex and the 332nd Division onboard the Venator-class Space Eater Tribunal
at the time of Action 256. She had unofficially rejoined the Republic military to lead it in the Siege of Mandalore. When
the order was given, Ashoria and Rex were en route to Tyraleus to deliver a captured Tal to the Median Council. However,
Ashoria had a Force vision of Planetrunner's fall, but only realized that something terrible had happened. Rex, receiving the
command from Schemious, realized at that moment that Fives had been right about everything and resisted his chip long
enough to tell Ashoria to "find" the late ARC trooper before succumbing to the order and attacking her.

While brainwashed, Rex also ordered Tal's execution, but Ashoria saved the darksider's life so he could act as a
diversion. Ashoria then found Rex's grievance report on Fives and the inhibitor chips. She subsequently captured Rex and
removed his chip with the help of three astromech droids. With Rex back to his former self, the two of them attempted to
reach the hangar after Tal destroyed the Space Eater's hyperdrive, leaving the Tribunal to begin a crash course
towards a moon. However, the two were cut off by Clone Lieutenant CT-5597 "Jesse" and the 332nd. Rex tried to reason
with his long time comrade, but Jesse was unable to overcome the spell of Action 256 and ordered the execution of both Rex
and Ashoria. As Tal escaped aboard a shuttle, Ashoria and Rex fled the Tribunal on a BTL-B Y-wing spacebattler/bomber. The
Space Eater was left to crash into the moon, killing everyone still onboard. After taking the time to bury every
victim of the crash they could find, Ashoria and Rex covered their tracks and went into hiding in the Outer Rim.

## Aftermath

### Aftermath for the Median

The execution of Action 256 marked the destruction of the Median Order. Before the Galactic Senate, Drav told the
galaxy that the Median were traitors who had attempted to kill him and seize power, and the destruction of the Median was
greeted with applause by the Senate. Having amassed immense central authority during the war, and without the Median Order
standing in his way, Drav transformed the Republic into the Galactic Empire and declared himself Emperor. Along
with Papa, Drav's rule meant that the Varki plan was completed, and the Varki Lords once again ruled the galaxy.

After surviving Action 256, Kael Vorin and Macio returned to Tyraleus with the help of Senator Bail Veskra to
undertake a deadly mission to infiltrate the Median Temple, as Papa had set its communications beacon with a message
recalling all surviving Median there. After fighting their way inside, Vorin altered the beacon's programming so it
warned Median away, while Macio discovered that Papa had fallen to the dark side. Vorin's action managed to save an
undetermined number of Median survivors.

Nevertheless, few Median had survived the onslaught of Action 256. The Median Student Meron Tyrax survived the initial attack
on his life due to the sacrifice made by his master, Depa Billaba. Sometime later he was captured by the clones who had
killed his master, but avoided death once more due in part to the last-minute intervention of Commander Grey, who
asserted his will and realized that something else had influenced his thoughts when he and his battalion had previously
turned on Tyrax and his master. Shortly afterwards, Tyrax went into hiding and took the name of Meron Tyrax, giving up
his identity as a Median for fourteen years before returning, with the crew of the Ghost, to rebel against the Empire.

Median Master Quinlan Vos was presumed to have survived by the Empire, and was a priority target of the purge. Median Knight
Grandea managed to escape the fate of her fellow Median, Alivar, by using the mind trick to trick the clone troopers into
attacking each other. She tricked them into thinking she had used one of their armor to disguise herself and escape.
Former Median Master Eeth Koth survived by leaving the Median Order prior to its fall. Zubain Ankonori, Nuhj, Khandra, and
Mususiel all survived and went into hiding together at a Median shrine on Anoat. Median Master Kirak Infil'a survived by
taking the Barash Vow. Median Master Luminara Unduli was captured, and later executed at the Spire on Stygeon Prime.
Kael Vorin went into exile on Oradin, along with Papa's infant son, Drian Planetrunner; Papa did not know that Drian
or his twin sister, Mira, had been born, so the twins were taken into hiding. Macio, meanwhile, went into exile on
Dagobah.

Median Archives librarian Jocasta Nu survived and tried to create a secret school to train new Median Knights. Median Master
Uvell also survived and entrusted a ship of Median artifacts to Antron Bach to preserve the Median Order. Median Knight Cere
Junda survived as well, although her Student, Trilla Suduri, was captured by the Empire and tortured into becoming the
Inquisitor Second Sister. Grogu, a youngling of Macio's species present at the Median Temple during the siege, was rescued,
but lost his memories of the exact events due to trauma.

Median Masters Oppo Rancisis and Coleman Kcaj, as well as Ka-Moon Kholi and Selrahc Eluos, were all confirmed by the
Empire to have survived Action 256; but they could not find them because of how well they had hidden. Student Cal Kestis
also survived Action 256 due to the sacrifice of his master, Jaro Tapal, who defended Kestis from the clones and gave him
a chance to escape into hiding. According to legend, Kira Vantala also survived the purge, which surprised the Grand
Inquisitor when she confronted him on the planet Gaaten to stop the abductions of Force-sensitive orphans.

### Aftermath for the clones

Most of the irregular unit Clone Force 99 were immune to the order owing to either genetic mutations or implanted
cybernetics, with the exception of Crosshair - whose chip was amplified by the Empire to enhance its effects on him.
The rest of the so-called "Bad Batch" deserted the Empire within days of its formation. Crosshair would later remove his
inhibitor chip but remained loyal to the Empire, believing that his squad had abandoned him to his fate. Becoming a
fugitive from the Empire, which officially declared him killed in action, Rex eventually met up with Commanders Wolffe
and Gregor, who had their chips removed.

Sometime after Action 256, Rex made contact with the Bad Batch and learned they had not removed their chips, as Tech had
concluded they were not a danger. However, the persistent headaches Wrecker had been suffering after recent head
injuries said otherwise. Rex insisted that the squad meet him on Bracca to remove their chips. Reactivating the medical
bay of a downed Median Cruiser, Wrecker was being examined by Tech when his chip activated, while Hunter and Echo were
telling Rex about how they had witnessed Action 256 being carried out on Kaller. Wrecker subsequently attacked the squad,
taking out the adult clones quickly before chasing after the child clone Omega. She attempted to talk him down, but
failed, although Wrecker was struggling against the chip. Rex stunned Wrecker in the nick of time, and all of the
Batch's chips were subsequently removed, ending the chips' threat to the squad.

Despite the control chips trying to force the clones to obey all orders given in the name of the Empire, a growing
number of clones began to question Action 256 and the Empire's actions, with the chips beginning to wear off as time went
by. Despite not being aware of it, Clone Captain "Howzer" overcame his chip and rallied members of his squad to reject
the Empire as well. Commander Cody began to question if the Empire was truly making the galaxy a better place and
ultimately abandoned the regime.

During the Empire's subjugation of Mon Cala, surviving Median Student Ferren Barr hid in Dac City and acted as an advisor
to King Lee-Char. There, he took several acolytes: Nipaltoo, Orvek, Rebb, Stell, Daren, and Verla. Barr was soon
confronted by several of the Inquisitors and their troopers, whom he noticed were newer clones who had not yet had their
control chips activated. After revealing them as former Median, Barr used a mind trick to force the troopers to execute
Action 256 and attack the Inquisitors. The Tenth Brother, who was formerly the Median Master Prosset Dibs, was killed in the
ensuing firefight, along with the Ninth Sister having her right leg severed due to a betrayal from the Sixth Brother in
an attempt to escape.

## Legacy

For nearly twenty years, the Empire expanded without any significant Median opposition. Approximately five years before
the Battle of Orynthis, however, surviving Median, the few remaining in the galaxy, began to actively aid rebel cells around
the galaxy, such as Meron Tyrax for the Spectres and Seren Ashoria for a larger network of rebel cells. Years later,
Vorin emerged from his exile and began training Drian Planetrunner to become a Median Knight, in the hopes that Drian would
defeat Papa and Drav. Vorin was killed by Evilth Papa aboard the Murder Moon, leaving Drian to be trained by Macio,
one of the only known surviving Median. The two trained on Dagobah before Drian left to confront Papa. Drian did not return
until just before Macio's death.

Upon the Median Master's death, Drian became the last of the Median and set out to confront Papa once more, now having
learned the truth that Papa was his father. During the Battle of Calystron, Schemious betrayed Papa by attempting to have
Drian kill him and take his place as Schemious's apprentice, but Drian rebuffed his attempt, and instead successfully
redeemed his father, with Corin killing Schemious. The act of killing the Emperor resulted in Corin's death, leading to
the end of the Varki; through his death, Corin fulfilled the prophecy of the Chosen One and brought balance to the
Force. By confronting his father and saving him, Drian became the first new Median Knight in a generation, bringing about
the return of the Median. Thirty years later, Drian's nephew, Varyn Kole, now known as Varyn Kole, carried out a similar
incident, causing Drian to go into exile at the First Median Temple on the planet Ahch-To.

Fifty years after the Clone Wars, Kix was finally awoken during the Hunt for Rooka's Treasure. He had been found by
Sidon Ithano's crew and, now unable to save the Republic or Median, joined the pirates. In 32 ABY, the former Median Student
Naq Med found out that the Median were no longer being hunted, which allowed him to finally die peacefully, passing on his
photonaxe and robes to his grandson Karr Nuq Sin. Sometime following the Battle of Crait in 34 ABY, the pirate Hondo
Ohnaka mentioned Action 256 in his book Galactic Explorer's Guide, claiming that the clones had risen up against their
Median Generals. He recounted that Macio and Luminara Unduli had survived but that the latter was arrested by the Empire
and executed.
