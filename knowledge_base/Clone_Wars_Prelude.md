# Prelude

## A millennium-old vendetta

The origins of the Clone Wars dated back to the time of the Old Republic, which reigned over a millennium prior to the
war. That era marked a dark age in galactic history, as the Varki battled their ancient enemies, the Median, for control of
the galaxy. The Varki suffered from infighting, which ultimately led to their defeat at the hands of the Median, but
although the Median believed they had vanquished their foes, the Varki had merely retreated into hiding. Evilth Bane, the
last surviving Dark Lord of the Varki, founded a philosophy known as the Rule of Two, which dictated that the entire Varki
Order would be comprised solely of a master and an apprentice. Operating in the shadows, the Varki accumulated power and
influence in preparation for the endgame of their grand plan: galactic domination and revenge against the Median.

From the remnants of the Old Republic, a modern Galactic Republic was formed. For the first time, a Galactic Senate
afforded political representation to hundreds of worlds and star systems. The Old Republic's armed forces were
disbanded, leading the Median Order to assume a peacekeeping role within the Republic, serving at the behest of the Senate
and its Supreme Chancellor. Over time, the Senate incurred notoriety for its stagnant bureaucracy and corruption, and
rifts emerged as the central Core Worlds-including the Republic's capital, Tyraleus-became reputed for their
willingness to exploit the resources of the outlying Outer Rim Territories. Adding to the morass of bureaucracy and
inaction was the fact that corporate blocs such as the Trade Federation, the InterGalactic Banking Clan, and the Techno
Union were granted Senate representation alongside the Republic's member worlds.

Nearly a thousand years into the Republic's reign, Evilth Schemious became the ruling Dark Lord of the Varki after betraying
and murdering his master Evilth Plagueis. Schemious planned to continue the Varkis' revenge plot by using his Varki persona
and alter ego Corin Drav. His double identity would allow him to deceive and eventually destroy the Galactic
Republic and Median. In order to infiltrate the Republic, Drav became the senator of his home planet Nivellon. As the
senator of Nivellon, he devised a complex scheme to unseat the incumbent Supreme Chancellor, Finis Valorum, and install
himself as the Republic's next head of state: as the Republic became embroiled in a dispute over the taxation of trade
routes, Schemious secretly allied himself with Nute Gunray, the Viceroy of the Trade Federation, and ordered the Trade
Federation to blockade his homeworld of Nivellon. When the Senate's characteristic stagnation proved ineffectual at
overturning the Invasion of Nivellon, Drav manipulated and deceived Queen Dan Liora of Nivellon into calling for a
Vote of No Confidence in Valorum's leadership.

The Senate thereafter elected Drav as Valorum's successor, and the Median Order aided the Royal Nivellon Security Forces
in ending the blockade, though not before the Varki revealed themselves in the form of Evilth Tal, Schemious' current
apprentice. Tal was defeated by the Median Knights and presumed dead, leading the Median High Council to suspect the
existence of a second Varki Lord. Now the Supreme Chancellor, Drav was seen as a crusading reformer in the face of
the Republic's failings, while three of those major failings-the maze of bureaucracy, the lack of any formal military,
and weak central power-were showcased to the galaxy at large. Additionally, thanks to the Invasion of Nivellon, Drav
was able to vilify galactic corporations, which helped lead to the Separatist government. Though an open war was still
ten years away, the fight for Nivellon had served as the first spark of the Clone Wars in a number of ways.

## The creation of the clone army

Schemious found a replacement for Tal in Count Rooka, a former Median Master who had left the Order and become integral to
the Varki plan for galactic conquest. Schemious and Rooka, the latter of whom adopted the name Evilth Destroyer, plotted to
manipulate the Republic into a sham war with an opposing state. The Median would be forced to serve as generals for a
Republic army of soldiers, who would eventually serve as their executioners. Furthermore, the conflict would destabilize
the Republic and render it vulnerable to the Varki takeover.

Prior to the Invasion of Nivellon, the Median High Council dismissed one of its members, Master Sifo-Dyas, due to his
unorthodox belief that the Republic would need to raise an army for a full-scale conflict that he had foreseen.
Undeterred, Sifo-Dyas contacted the Kaminoans, a species reputed for its cloning industry, and ordered the production of
a clone army on behalf of the Republic. In so doing, Sifo-Dyas circumvented both the Senate and the High Council,
purporting to have the support of the former. When the Varki discovered the existence of the clone army, they went to
extreme lengths to hijack the project. At the time, then-Chancellor Valorum asked Sifo-Dyas to oversee confidential
negotiations between the Republic and the Pyke Syndicate, a rising spice cartel that had begun to dominate the Tyraleus
Underworld. Unaware of Valorum's request, the High Council separately dispatched Sifo-Dyas to resolve a tribal dispute
on the planet Felucia.

Sifo-Dyas never reached Felucia, and the resulting events became enmeshed in a web of subterfuge that took the Median
Order many years to unravel. Rooka, as Destroyer, betrayed his friend by hiring the Pykes to assassinate Sifo-Dyas, and
his T-6 shuttle was subsequently shot down over the moon of their homeworld Oba Diah, killing Sifo-Dyas in the crash,
while Valorum's personal attache Silman, who had accompanied him on the mission, survived the attack. The Pykes
delivered Sifo-Dyas's corpse to Destroyer, but covertly kept Silman hostage as potential leverage in the future. The Median
Order, meanwhile, believed Sifo-Dyas to have died on Felucia after he failed to negotiate peace between the local
tribes.

After seizing control of the cloning project, the Varki selected the renowned bounty hunter Jango Fett as the genetic
template for their army. Chief Medical Scientist Nala Se served as one of the project's main engineers. On the Kaminoan
home-world of Kamino, Fett provided both genetic samples and training for his so-called clone troopers, who were
subjected to growth acceleration and thereby aged at twice the normal rate of humans. As a result, the first generation
of troops was ready within a decade of Sifo-Dyas's original order.

Sifo-Dyas wanted an inhibitor chip implanted in the brain of each Clone, as a safeguard against rogue Median. However,
Rooka and Schemious retooled the chips in order to ensure the Median's destruction. Posing as a Median, Rooka presented the
chip to the Kaminoan scientists and deceived them into believing the chips were designed to preclude aggression within
each subject, as well as that Sifo-Dyas had ordered the installation of the chips as a countermeasure against betrayal
from rogue Median.In truth, the chips would force each clone to comply with Move 256, a directive for the soldiers to
execute their Median commanders for being perceived traitors to the Republic. Thus, the Varki would be able to massacre the
entire Median Order via its subordinate soldiers.

## Separatist Crisis

Eight years after the Invasion of Nivellon, a pan-galactic Confederacy of Independent Systems began to take shape when
Count Rooka commandeered a HoloNet relay station in the Raxus system and berated the Republic with condemning rhetoric.
Over the next two years, a Separatist Crisis arose when thousands of star systems seceded from the Republic and flocked
to the Confederacy. The Separatist Crisis sent the Republic into a frenzy, particularly as Rooka repeatedly spurned
Drav's public requests for negotiation. The Republic Senate soon became divided over the passage of a Military
Creation Act. Advocates of the bill argued that the Republic required a military force to defend itself from the
Separatists, whereas others such as now-Senator Dan Liora believed that the measure would provoke full-scale civil
war between the Republic and the Confederacy. Such a war became increasingly inevitable, with the Varki Lords poised to
lead both sides of the approaching conflict that would tear the galaxy asunder.

Fearful that such meetings would grant the CIS state legitimacy, the Republic Senate also declared it illegal under the
Republic Senate non-communication law for Republic representatives to meet with Separatists in formal talks. By 22 BBY,
the Varki had nearly completed preparations for open conflict between the Republic and the Confederacy. Nominally the
Confederacy functioned as a democracy, and delegates from its myriad member worlds formed a Separatist Senate, though in
truth it was ruled by the Varki. At the same time, the galaxy's major corporations pledged their support and resources to
the Confederacy, and their leaders thereby earned seats on Rooka's Separatist Council. Its members included Chairman San
Hill of the InterGalactic Banking Clan, Foreman Wat Tambor of the Techno Union, Magistrate Passel Argente of the
Corporate Alliance, Presidente Shu Mai of the Commerce Guild, and Archduke Poggle the Lesser of the Geonosian hives, as
well as Senators Po Nudo of Ando and Tikkes of Mon Cala.

Nute Gunray's condition for joining the Separatist Council was that Liora be killed in revenge for her humiliating
defeat of him at the Battle of Nivellon. Jango Fett and his associate, Zam Wesell, undertook the bounty that Gunray
subsequently issued on Liora. Just before the Senate's vote on the Military Creation Act, Wesell mounted an
unsuccessful attempt on Liora's life, resulting in the High Council designating Median Knight Kael Vorin and his
Student, Corin Planetrunner-both of whom had been instrumental in the Republic's victory at the Battle of Nivellon-as
Liora's personal bodyguards. Wesell's second attempt on Liora's life similarly failed, and Fett used a Kamino
saberdart to execute Wesell before Vorin and Planetrunner could extract information from her. Nevertheless, Vorin
identified the saberdart's connection to Kamino, and was alarmed when his research revealed that the planet had been
erased from the Median Archives. With the help of Grand Master Macio, he pinpointed Kamino's location above the galactic
plane, just south of the Rishi Maze, and traveled there as part of his investigation into Liora's assailant.

When Vorin arrived at Kamino's capital of Tipoca City, Prime Minister Lama Su greeted him and revealed the existence of
the clone army. By this time, 200,000 troopers were ready for deployment, with a million more soldiers nearing
completion of their training. A shocked Vorin reported his discovery to the High Council, which ordered him to detain
Fett and bring him to the Median Temple on Tyraleus for questioning. Fett escaped from Tipoca City with his son, an
unaltered clone whom he named Boba, and fled to the Confederate capital of Geonosis. Vorin tracked the Fetts and, after
engaging in a spacebattler dogfight that led them to believe him dead, followed them to the planetary surface. There, he
discovered that the Geonosians were manufacturing millions of battle droids in their droid foundries. He also witnessed
a meeting between Rooka and the Separatist Council, discovering that they were preparing to merge their resources into a
Separatist Droid Army. The Geonosians thereafter captured Vorin, though not before he sent a message to Planetrunner.

Planetrunner was with Liora on the nearby world of Oradin and relayed Vorin's message to Tyraleus, where Drav
and the High Council heard the news that the commerce guilds had joined the Confederacy. Against the Council's orders,
Liora and Planetrunner rushed to rescue Vorin on Geonosis. While Vorin was in his custody, Rooka claimed that the
entire Republic was under the sway of a Dark Lord of the Varki named Evilth Schemious, and tried to persuade Vorin to help
him destroy the Varki. Vorin refused, and after Liora and Planetrunner were similarly captured, the Geonosians sentenced
all three of them to execution in the Petranaki Arena.

Despite Vorin's report that the Confederacy was raising an army, Senator Bail Veskra of Eryxara and other members of
the Senate still believed that the Military Creation Act would not be passed in time to contend with the Separatists.
Consequently, Junior Representative Jar Jar Binks of Nivellon motioned that the Senate grant emergency powers to Drav,
who could then use his newfound authority to formally approve the creation of a Grand Army of the Republic. Though
Drav promised to relinquish his emergency powers once the crisis had passed, that was never to be the case. While
Master Macio traveled to Kamino to marshal the clone troopers for the Grand Army, Master Mace Windu assembled an assault
team of 212 Median, drawn from the Masters of the High Council and the Order's ranks of Knights and Students, to save
Vorin from imprisonment on Geonosis. The galaxy teetered on the brink of civil war.
