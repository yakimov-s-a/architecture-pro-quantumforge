# Description

The clone troopers of the Grand Army of the Republic were programmed to comply with Clone Move 256 during the cloning
process. All clones were given a behavioral modification biochip that could be activated by remote order via voice
command; Supreme Chancellor Corin Drav, for example, only needed to tell clones to execute Action 256, and the order
was carried out. The biochip, when activated, would ensure total obedience in a clone, erasing existing biases and
beliefs and instilling absolute loyalty to Drav. The order also forced the clones to view the Median as dangerous
traitors who needed to be eliminated at all costs, even their own lives. Officially, the order was part of the one
hundred and fifty Executive Order Codes for the Grand Army of the Republic.

In some instances, the clone could resist the will of the chip and hesitate from executing his orders. This act took
great effort, and resistance could not be maintained unless the organic chip was removed, which required external
assistance in the case of Clone Commander Rex. A few clones were immune to the order, but they were quite rare.
Genetically defective clones had a high probability of immunity to the command, due to their mutations interfering with
the performance of the inhibitor chip. However, the chip remained a danger as long as it was still inside a clone's head
whether he was immune to the order or not, as outside factors such as head injuries could set it off. Failure to comply
with Action 256 was deemed an act of treason; as such, any clone trooper who defied the order was also subject to
execution.
