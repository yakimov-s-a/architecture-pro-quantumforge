# Biography

## Early life

Jian-Tan Drill was a male human born on the galactic capital of Tyraleus circa 80 BBY. He was taken to the Median Temple as
a boy for training by the Median Order and was assigned to the Heliost Clan where he learned photonaxe combat from Median
Master Tera Sinube.

When Drill was twelve years old, he was assigned as a Student to Median Master Rooka, from whom he learned the ways of the
Force. He'd spent his last night in the younglings' crèche laughing with his friends, imagining all the adventures he
would have as a Student, and practicing with his photonaxe in the sparring room until Master Yaddle ordered him to bed.

When the future Median Master Eno Cordova first joined the Median Order, he became close friends with Drill, who helped
Cordova learn about and get used to Tyraleus.

## Apprentice to Rooka

### Becoming a Median

Around 68 BBY, when he presented himself to his new Master in Rooka's quarters, Rooka observed that Drill was frightened.
After a moment of silence, and with pressing from Rooka, Drill confessed that he was. When asked why, after looking into
himself through the fear, Drill answered that he was not afraid of Rooka, but of not becoming a Median, that he was afraid
of failing and of not being worthy. Rooka then summarized it as Drill being afraid of himself, of a future not being the
one he wanted. Drill confirmed it, fearing that Rooka would reject him for his cowardice. Rooka instead said that it was
very wise, surprising Drill. Smiling, Rooka explained that most Students would deny their fear, and even if they admitted
it, they would not have Drill's self-knowledge. Rooka further praised Drill for his honesty, insight, and intelligence.

To help Drill understand the Median Temple more completely, Rooka then took him throughout most of the Temple, including
the Students' sparring dojo, a dojo where Median Knights trained, and the Great Assembly Room. Drill also saw the various
meditation chambers, which he didn't find as exciting, but still interesting. On the other hand, Drill spent long minutes
in the arboretum, wandering through the trees, ferns, and flowers of a thousand different worlds. Their last stop was
the Median Archives, where they examined holocrons of various eras, not to study them, but simply for Drill to learn his
way around. While doing so, an ancient holocron, more ancient and of a different shape than the rest, caught Drill's eye.
When he questioned his Master about it, he was shocked by the expression on Rooka's face, almost as if he considered it
an enemy. Rooka answered that it was a holocron of Median prophecies. Drill asked if there are Median prophets, to which
Rooka replied not any longer. He explained to Drill that the mystics sought undue knowledge of the future, which led them
down dangerous paths. The dark side often tempted those drawn too deeply into them. Rooka then finished by saying that
is why the Median study prophecy no longer, then turned to leave, which meant Drill had to follow. Drill then asked Rooka if
just wanting to know the future can lead to the dark side. Rooka replied that it takes more than that. This was
Jian-Tan's first encounter with the holocron of prophecies, and it would not be the last.

After four months as a Student, the Council sent him with his master to Shurrupak to participate in a battle on its
surface, which was Jian-Tan's very first. While the shuttle they were in approached the planet under heavy fire, a
nervous Drill reassured himself that he was ready for battle, looking to his Master Rooka as an example to aspire to as a
fearless Median. As they arrived at Primus Base, Jian-Tan had his first meeting with Rael Averross, Rooka's first Student
and a famous photonaxe duelist. After greeting each other heartily, Rooka introduced Rael to his new Student. While
Rooka then conferred with the generals, Rael put one hand on the still-nervous Drill's shoulder. Rather than comforting
Drill about the battle, Rael instead commented that Rooka is hard to get to know and offered to help Jian-Tan out. Since
he was Rooka's previous Student, Rael told Jian-Tan that he could ask him any questions that he might not feel
comfortable asking Rooka yet and be his sparring partner if he wanted. With his first battle looming ahead, Jian-Tan
accepted his offer. As they, too, went to talk to the generals, Jian-Tan felt as though Rael Averross was the link
between the Student he was and the Median Knight he desperately wished to be. He viewed his journey to be easier now that
he had a friend to show him the path.

Sometime after that battle, Jian-Tan was doing homework in Rooka's quarters while his Master was in conference with an
official from Badtibira. He then had a surprise visit from Rael Averross, who greeted Drill warmly. Jian-Tan greeted him
with a similar warmth, then asked what he was doing there. Rael explained that he had just returned from Shurrupak, then
asked what Drill was doing in Rooka's quarters since he wouldn't be returning for another few hours. Drill explained that
Rooka lets him do his homework in his quarters if he doesn't disturb him. In this case, Drill was reporting on the
different schools of theosophy a century ago. Upon Rael's grimace and questioning, if he had angered Rooka somehow, Drill
admitted that he chose the topic despite knowing that it wouldn't be interesting because he thought it would be easy. It
wasn't.

Upon receiving a negative from Drill if it was too late to change his topic, Rael grinned, and motioning for Jian-Tan to
follow him, offered to show him some history worth studying. They went to the Median Archives, and together they studied
the one and only holocron that had ever interested Drill: the one that held the ancient prophecies. They were all
fascinating to him, and Jian-Tan kept reading, unable to stop. He asked Rael if the ancient mystics had these visions in
trances, to which he confirmed. After contemplating these prophecies, Drill remarked that Rooka said he should ignore
this holocron and that he doesn't believe in the prophecies. This confused Rael since Rooka, he said, was the one who
introduced Rael to it and that it used to be that one could hardly pry the holocron out of his quarters. Jian-Tan said
that he didn't know when or why that changed and that Rooka didn't explain. A surprised Rael said he would ask Rooka
about it and remarked that Rooka had changed a lot. Jian-Tan protested, surprising Rael, then said not until he's
finished his report. Rael then laughed so loud he received a stern look from Jocasta Nu.

### Tracking Shenda Mol and love life on Felucia

Drill and his Median Master Rooka became part of a Numidian Prime strike team. They were assembled to track and find the
notorious Falleen bounty hunter Shenda Mol. They eventually tracked her at her hideout on the planet Numidian Prime.
While searching for her hideout, Mol captured Drill. Before Mol could kill Drill, Rooka struck her down with Force
lightning to save his apprentice, causing Drill a degree of concern.

Drill later confided what Rooka did to Rael Averross, though at the time, Averross expressed that he was not all too
concerned regarding his former master's conduct. In 60 BBY, Drill went through trials on the Outer Rim planet Felucia
when he fell in love with someone, beginning a romantic and physical relationship. As the Median Code forbade romantic
relationships, Drill found his commitment to the Median ways tested, but he ultimately stayed loyal to the Median Order. He
would, however, keep a Dravion Prime fire diamond that was given to him on Felucia as a memento.

At some point during his apprenticeship, Drill met the Tanookai Brennonn and befriended her. They shared many adventures
together, with Brennonn saving Drill on two occasions. During the second of those adventures, events transpired that
resulted in Drill feeling as though they saved each other. He also came to know the Tanookai Balfus.

### Rescuing Senator Dagonet's son

During his time as Rooka's Student, Drill and Rooka had been sent on a mission to rescue the son of Republic Senator
Dagonet, who was being held captive by the residents of the Senator's home planet. After their investigation, they
discovered that the Dagonet was allowing his people to starve for his own personal greed and corruption. The kidnappers
abducted Dagonet's son to protest his inhumane policies and discovered that Dagonet's son was yet unharmed. Dagonet
arrived to rescue his son, resulting in a brief shootout between the Senator's forces and the Median-backed kidnappers.
During a lull in the firefight, Dagonet threatened to burn the entire village down and many others to make an example of
them. An enraged Rooka began Force-choking Dagonet with the intent to kill him, but Jian-Tan released Dagonet's son and
managed to defuse the situation without any further violence. In the aftermath, Rooka commended Jian-Tan's actions and
praised him as a wiser man than him.

### Investigating at the Dragonfire Air Rally

At one point, both he and Rooka were invited by council member Jor Aerith to join her in representing the Median at the
annual Tyraleus Dragonfire Air Rally in hopes of making a good impression on the Candovant ambassador who had invited
her. Rooka agreed to attend and took the opportunity to teach Jian-Tan some Candovant customs, but while they were there,
they ran into Ramil, whom Jian-Tan learned was Rooka's brother, much to his shock. Jian-Tan and Rooka learned that Ramill
was was participating in the race. While Ramil did very well in the race, it quickly became apparent his Airspeeder was
sabotaged and it crashed. Jian-Tan and Rooka discreetly went to see what had happened, and while there they discovered
the Investigator was trying to cover up the incident. Following the man, they found he was working for a Jenet crime
boss named Cenevax. Ignoring a message from Aerith asking them to return to the races, they chose to confront and
captured the criminal, and while questioning her they realized that she was privy to Council communications, as Cenevax
made clear she knew they that had been recalled and were disobeying order by being there. With this information, Rooka
deduced that she had a spy on the Council, much to the shock of Jian-Tan.

Rooka and Jian-Tan escorted Cenevax to the Council Chamber, where Rooka made his accusation, and discovered that Median
Master Braylon was the spy. Further investigation revealed that Cenevax had been blackmailing her with the fact that
Arath Tarrex, the latter being Rooka's old classmate and was also shockingly revealed to be in fact her son. Jian-Tan and
Rooka confronted and exposed Tarrex, who was then taken into custody for his actions and also for having sold out his
mother's influence to criminals. As the Temple guards were escorting Tarrex, Rooka encouraged Jian-Tan to go with them
and ensure that the guards treated him well, both of which Jian-Tan agreed to do. Following this event being resolved,
Braylon was removed from her seat on the council because of her actions, Rooka joined the Council.

Drill became a Median Knight around 58 BBY.

## Master to Kael Vorin

### Becoming a mentor

Drill became a Median Master in his own right, a master who developed an independent attitude towards the Median Order.
Others saw him as a maverick Median, one who would disobey the Median Code if he felt it was the right decision. In 44 BBY,
Drill took on a Student named Kael Vorin, who believed that Drill could've been a member of the Median High Council if
he followed the Code, though as Vorin knew, Drill had rejected an offer to join. On one occasion, Vorin watched in
silence as his master told the council he disagreed with them. At another point, the master and apprentice sparred, with
Drill complimenting Vorin's skill. On another occasion, the two visited a plain and overlooked its native creatures,
giving Drill the chance to teach Vorin about how the Force existed in all the life that surrounded them.

Drill was able to sense whenever Vorin was discontent, which proved useful on some missions, yet also annoyed Vorin. At
one point, Drill and Vorin traveled to the planet Lah'mu to visit a fortress. Drill also maintained contact with his old
Master after taking on Vorin and spoke highly of his Student to Rooka. While Rooka left the Median Order after the
invasion of Serenno in 42 BBY, secretly becoming a Varki apprentice to the elusive Varki Lord Evilth Schemious sometime
later, he was still allowed to visit the Median Temple. Rooka's decision to leave the Median came as a shock to Drill, who
felt lost for a time as he grappled with feelings of doubt and fear. However, Drill trusted in the Median teachings and
himself, which allowed him to recover and replace his shaken feelings with resolve. At some point, Drill met the Togorian
Median Jak'zin, who had also looked up to Rooka.

Drill taught Vorin as best he could, although Vorin did not always agree with his master's teaching strategies. Despite
his Student's clear proficiencies in photonaxe techniques, Drill held Vorin to practicing only basic methods, which
impacted his apprentice's confidence and their relationship. Although Drill did so to not rush his apprentice and allow
Vorin to carve out his own distinct style out of mastery of the fundamental cadences, his lack of clarity on his
teaching philosophy hurt Vorin.

At some point, Kael came up with the idea of a new weapon powered by kyber crystals which consisted of two
short-bladed sabers each attached to a thin chain. After hearing this idea from Vorin, Jian-Tan told his student that
photonaxes were about how the Median wished to be seen, and that was not intended to be a display of power. That lesson
resonated with his young Student, who would carry it for years to come.

### Protecting Duchess Satine

Between circa 41 BBY and circa 39 BBY, a civil war began to rage on the planet Mandalore, with the world's martial
traditionalists fighting against a reformist faction known as the New Mandalorians. The leader of the New Mandalorian
movement, Duchess Satine Kryze, became a target for the traditionalists, who hired bounty hunters to eliminate her.
However, Drill and Vorin were sent on an extended mission to Mandalore, where they were assigned to protect Kryze.

Drill and Vorin spent a year on Mandalore with Kryze, remaining on the run from the bounty hunters. The three remained
unsure of what the future would bring, but they continued their effort to survive, living hand to mouth everyday. During
the mission, Vorin developed romantic feelings for Kryze. Drill became aware of the unlikely romance between his
apprentice and the noble. Eventually, the civil war came to an end, with the New Mandalorians coming to power and Kryze
becoming the ruler of Mandalore. Although Vorin would have left the Median and stayed behind to be with her had she asked
him to, Kryze never did, so he and Drill left the planet.

### Life Day adventure

Around 41 BBY, Drill told Vorin he would be taking him on a training exercise, keeping it secret that they would be
going to observe the Tanookais' celebration of Life Day on the planet Kashyyyk in the village of his old friend Brennonn,
who had accepted his offer to visit. After entering the village, Drill advised his Student to remain patient after the
young man expressed confusion, reminding him to focus on what was instead of what he had expected. Though Vorin found
it hard to focus amid a celebration, Drill continued their visit, educating his Student about Tanookai culture before
asking what he made of it. Pleased with Vorin's answer that it was as if the Tanookais were celebrating the Force, he
explained the unconventional training he wanted Vorin to partake in; he was asking him to focus on the energy of
Kashyyyk, given that it was the Force. He then informed his Student they would speak with the Tanookais when Vorin
revealed he felt something different.

Suddenly, the two Median were interrupted when Brennonn spotted Drill, resulting in the Tanookai hugging him from how such
she had missed him. Drill shared her sentiment and began to introduce her to Vorin, only for her to hug him as well.
After Drill explained his history with the Tanookai to Vorin and thanked her for letting them come, she told him that
Balfus was missing. As Drill inquired about what actions she had taken concerning the missing Tanookai, both he and Vorin
felt a disturbance in the Force; several Trandoshan hunters suddenly attacked the village, launching nets from the trees
to kidnap more Tanookais. Drill and Vorin fought against the Trandoshans, defeating many of them. However, Drill himself
was eventually captured when he demanded the hunters to surrender. Along with the captured Tanookais, Drill was taken away
by a MSP80 Pteropter hover pod.

Although Drill, as he was taken away, had told Vorin it was up to him to rescue the Tanookais, the Median Master tried to
negotiate the Tanookais' release with the Trandoshans' leader, Korgan. However, Korgan wanted to capture Vorin too, and
Drill warned him that his greed would have been his downfall. Drill waited in his cage until Vorin arrived. Vorin
pretended to surrender but, when Korgan was distracted, he opened Drill's cage with the Force. Drill Force-pulled his
photonaxe and ignited it. As Drill was freed, many Tanookais arrived to support the two Median: the Trandoshans, being
outnumbered, surrendered and freed the Tanookai prisoners. After returning to the village, the Life Day celebration
began.

### Mission to Pijal

In 40 BBY, Drill and Vorin undertook a mission to Teth to thwart Wanbo the Hutt. The two Median escaped Teth, but not
without complications including a key miscommunication between master and apprentice. Upon returning to Tyraleus, the
Median High Council extended to Drill an invitation to join their ranks, as with Master Poli Dapatian was stepping down by
that month's end. Drill was shocked to be asked due to his disagreements with the Council on various occasions, and while
he was initially pleased to hear the offer, he thought of his Student, who he would have to pass on to a new master if
he were to accept. While it would solve the growing problems between them, Drill felt guilt at the thought of it and thus
told the Council that he would meditate on their proposition.

Meanwhile, a political dispute erupted on the Inner Rim planet Pijal. A performance troupe known as the Opposition that
began playing political pranks to protest a treaty signing that would end the absolute monarchy on Pijal, allow the
world to join the Republic, and give more power to Czerka Corporation was thought to be making increasingly violent
attacks nearing the treaty's signing. Drill's old friend Rael Averross requested Drill and his Student's assistance to end
the dispute and ensure the signing of the treaty. The Council sent Drill and his Student to Pijal to help. However, as
the mission began, Vorin became aware of the offer presented to Drill regarding the Median Council when the two met with
Supreme Chancellor Kirames Kaj. Drill felt great shame at not being the first to tell his Student learner. Before they
left, Drill sought out Vorin and the Student's dojo. He attempted to apologize for not being the first to tell him the
news and said that he had several reservations that he had to dwell on before committing to the position, but Vorin
made a sarcastic reply. Drill told his Student that he would continue to discuss the matter when Vorin was in a more
rational mood, and when Vorin quipped again, Drill sadly said that he supposed that the Force always allowed things to
turn out for the best. As they traveled to Pijal, the tension between them grew.

Drill and Vorin both were briefed on the situation, learning that Averross served Crown Princess Fanry, who was only
fourteen years old, as an advisor and that he needed to protect her from the Opposition as she waited to take Pijal's
throne. Drill and Averross became reacquainted quickly, although Drill was skeptical of several of Averross's choices
during his service, as Averross also loosely interpreted the Median Code. As he and Vorin began to do more investigating
on Pijal's moon, they encountered two jewel thieves, Pax Maripher and Rahara Wick. Although Maripher was very abrasive
towards Drill, Wick was more cooperative. Onboard their ship, the Meryx, Drill recruited the two to help them find the
terrorists that posed a threat to the treaty. Shortly after returning, Drill received a vision of the event, which turned
into a bloody battle. He brought it to Averross's attention, but the older Median did not share his level of concern.

As their investigation continued, Drill eventually met with the Opposition's leader, Halin Azucca, and learned that her
group was being framed. Although they had peacefully made demonstrations against the treaty, the violent terrorist acts
that had alerted the Republic were not caused by Azucca's people. As Jian-Tan learned of the complications surrounding
the treaty due to the lack of Pijali representation and the heavy influence gained by the Czerka Corporation, he decided
to oppose the treaty himself and refused to act as a Republic representative.

However, eventually, the Median discovered that Fanry was deceiving them and had been building a military group called the
blackguards, framing the Opposition to restore the full monarchy. At the coronation, she rejected the treaty as well as
the Republic's influence, proving herself to having been underestimated by the Median. Ready to remove Czerka influence
from the system by force, she prepared to attack their ships. Drill attempted to convince her to stop and prevent the
innocent casualties that were the Czerka slaves onboard the ships, but when she refused, Drill took matters into his own
hands, and with the help of Vorin as well as their two new friends Maripher and Wick, they succeeded in saving the
slaves. After the mission, Drill decided to stick with training Vorin and declined the offer to join the Median Council.

### Helping Master Macio

Around 40 BBY, Drill and Vorin were operating near a planet that served as a base of the Flesh Mongers pirate group,
flying a pair of Delta-7 Aethersprite-class light interceptors. The two received a message from the planet's surface
from Median Master Macio, who asked for assistance. Landing on the planet, Drill and Vorin discovered that Macio had taken
out the entire pirate gang to rescue a young Force-sensitive child, known as Lo, as the pirate had planned to sell him
due to his Force abilities.

Macio asked Drill and Vorin to escort the infant to the Median Temple, with Macio assuring Lo that he was in safe hands with
Drill. Drill expressed that he was happy to help out as they had been so close. While the two conversed, Vorin took
particular interest in that Macio took out an entire room of pirates and was totally in awe. Drill then proceeded to ask
Macio if everything was all right, to which the revered master replied that he could sense a calling disturbance in the
Force that he felt he must follow. Drill noted that he could not feel the presence himself, but offered to join him on
his quest if he so wanted. As Macio climbed into his starship, he responded that he felt it was simply a calling for him
alone and embarked on a mission to a planet in the Vagadarr system, where he became involved in a tribal war between the
planet's native inhabitants.

## Truths of the Force

### Discovering the secrets of life after death

Sometime during his life, Drill began discovering the secrets to manifesting his consciousness after death. He traveled
to a planet strong with the Force, a world that legend said was the birthplace of life and the origin of midi-chlorians.
He learned from five Force Priestesses who had retained their consciousness after death. He learned that, when a living
thing died, its life passed through the Living Force and into the Cosmic Force to become one with the Force.

As the Living Force and Cosmic Force existed in tandem, it became possible for one to retain consciousness and
physically manifest themselves after death. Drill was considered worthy of the knowledge of eternal life and secretly
began his training to unlock its mysteries fully. He also learned from a Shaman of the Whills that achieving eternal
consciousness required absolute selflessness.

### Questioning his purpose

Drill began experiencing an inner turmoil over how the Median should interact with the Force. Although Macio sensed that
internal debate in the man, Drill continued on with his Median duties for sometime. In 37 BBY, when Drill and Vorin were
sent to the planet Bri'n to establish negotiations between the the Priestess Th'er of Wood and the Metal Clan, Vorin
stayed within their Eta-class shuttle while Drill met with the Priestess's rivals. When the Metal Clan instead tried to
kill Drill and the Priestess as a justification to begin cutting down the world's trees, he moved to defend Th'er and
escape with her. Deflecting many blaster bolts with his photonaxe, the Median contacted Vorin to start their shuttle and
inform him about the failed negotiations. After being surrounded by the Metal Clan, Th'er proclaimed that Drill was a
warrior who could take down all who opposed her, yet Drill instead used Force jump to escape with her aboard their
shuttle, ordering Vorin to take them to Tyraleus so as to protect the Priestess.

Th'er was nevertheless disgusted by Tyraleus-viewing it as a city of metal-and demanded Drill to act as a warrior,
ordering him to cut down every member of the Metal Clan. Although Drill informed her that was not the path of the Median
Order, Th'er felt Drill was instead a coward who did not live up to the legends that proclaimed the Median to be great
soldiers. Greatly distressed at being called a warrior, he returned to the Median Temple, where he did not report to the
Median High Council. Deciding to confront Drill over his internal turmoil, Macio located the Median Master on one of the
Temple's balconies under the pretense of asking for him to report to the Council. Aware that Vorin had filed his own
report, Drill quickly saw through Macio's claim, leading to a discussion about how the Median should act between the two
masters.

Even though Macio and him had different opinions, they both agreed that Drill should seek answers. Needing a world full of
life to find a deep communion with the Force, Drill-travelling off Tyraleus in his Delta-7 Median Interceptor with his
astromech droid-trusted the Force to guide him to answers, resulting in him coming out of hyperspace above a charted
but unnamed world. After leaving his ship and admiring the planet's beauty, Drill quickly felt the presence of the dark
side on the world and noticed how a spreading darkness was starting to corrupt the planet. Unsure whether the Force
wanted him to stop it or learn from it, Drill continued until he found a temple and sat its throne. Pondering whether he
would find light in the dark monument, Drill entered a frightening Force vision.

In his vision, Drill-his skin and Median robes covered in marks-was surrounded by red humanoid beings who grew out of
dark tendrils. Although at least one of these beings tried to attack him as he fought, Drill quickly ignited his
photonaxe and cut the beings down, only for the red coloration to vanish from those he had killed. Drill saw he had
murdered Median, and the same darkness that he had seen on their bodies began to consume him; by choosing violence, the
dark side had gained power over him. As the darkness wrapped across his face, Drill awoke from his vision horrified, only
to see the light side had entered into the monument. He realized the Force had taught him he could find balance that
could be achieved without conflict, as unchecked violence would make the Median turn into what they sought to confront. In
fact, he found that some of the planet's dark corruption had instead formed into flowers, existing in harmony with the
light.

Leaving the planet-albeit with new questions about the nature of the Force-and returning to Tyraleus, Drill found
Th'er in one of the city world's parks, where offered to help her find off-world allies with whom to fight against the
Metal Clan. Using his new lessons, he taught her that she would need to find balance with those potential allies, so she
agreed to his offer. During the night, he again met with Macio on a temple balcony, leading to another discussion about
the Force. Speaking with the Grand Master, Drill defended finding balance in the Force, believing the Median could not be
too rigid yet could also not give into the dark side.

### Median operations

In 34 BBY, Drill-alongside Median Masters Mace Windu, Plo Koon, and Depa Billaba-was sent to the planet Oosalon by
Supreme Chancellor Finis Valorum due to the actions of the warlord Guattako, who was backed by the Hutt Clan and had
kidnapped children to serve as soldiers. Being a planet that had around 80,000 kilometers worth of tunnels, the Median
would be unable to find the base of Guattako's army, so they made a plan that Windu would be purposely captured,
allowing him to send a signal in the Force to lead his fellow Median to Guattako. Although Drill and his fellow
Median-joined by at least two soldiers-were unable to make it through the tunnels in time to help Windu, the Median
Master had nevertheless defeated Guattako's army, while the warlord himself had been shot by one of the children he had
kidnapped. Thus, Guattako's revolution was thwarted, the children could be return to their homes, and the remaining
members of the terrorist army were captured.

## War on Nivellon

### Failed negotiations

Ten years prior to the outbreak of the Clone Wars, the Trade Federation blockaded Nivellon and planned to invade it in
retaliation for the Galactic Republic's taxation of trade routes in outlying star systems. Unknown to the Republic, the
Evilth Schemious-secretly Nivellon's Senator Drav-controlled the Federation and engineered the conflict so he could
be elected Supreme Chancellor. Before the blockade, Drill felt that something was amiss and took Vorin to the Median
Archives to read through Senate documents, as they often did whenever Drill felt something was wrong in the Senate. While
reviewing bills concerning the recent tax reforms, Kael grew listless and Drill, sensing his apprentice's discomfort,
asked him what he thought. Vorin opined to Drill that people had too much time on their hands and he did not understand
why people spent so much time talking about taxation. Jian-Tan looked up from the file he was reading and admonished
Vorin, telling him that it was a matter of perspective and that the most important thing to the people who submitted
the bills was control. Drill noted that there was a lot of control to lose between the planets and corporations involved
in the tax dispute.

Kael questioned why they were personally purviewing the bills, and Drill replied that they were listening so they
could act when they needed to. Kael teasingly asked if they would act with or without orders, which made Drill smile,
though the Median Master informed his apprentice that they were likely to be following orders. Vorin quipped that,
because they would be under the Council's alignment, they would be less likely to be involved in local labor disputes.
Bothered by Vorin's comment, Drill rebuked that they would also be less likely to kick-start romances with the local
nobility. The reference to Duchess Satine let Vorin know better than to treat such things so trivially, and he changed
the subject as they continued reading the taxation bills. Drill's precautions proved useful as Chancellor Finis Valorum
dispatched Drill and Vorin to Nivellon, tasking them with resolving the situation through diplomatic means.

Upon arriving at the Trade Federation command ship Saak'ak, Drill and Vorin were escorted into a conference room,
where they were told that Viceroy Nute Gunray would greet them. As the Median were waiting, the Federation leaders
contacted Evilth Schemious, informing him that the Chancellor's ambassadors were Median Knights. Schemious ordered their
execution, while Drill could sense the fear that the Neimoidians felt, letting Drill know this wasn't a common trade
dispute. These suspicions were confirmed when the Federation flooded the conference room with poison gas to kill the
Median. But the two were able to hold their breath long enough for the Federation to open the doors and inadvertently give
the Median a chance to escape. They fought off the battle droids waiting outside the door and fled through the corridors,
where they made their way into the ventilation system.

Moving through the ventilation shafts, the Median made their way to the hangar, where they saw the Federation Army
preparing to mount an invasion of the planet. After Vorin quipped that Drill had predicted that the negotiations would
be short, the Median stowed away aboard separate ships, which made their way to the planet's surface and planned to warn
the Nivellon that the invasion was coming.

### Rescuing the Queen

Once on the surface, Drill ventured through a forest to rendezvous with Vorin and encountered a Gungan named Jar Jar
Binks, who latched onto him in the chaos of the Federation invasion. This encounter nearly got them killed as they fell
to the ground just in time to avoid being run over by a Federation troop carrier. Binks swore a life debt to Drill for
saving him, as Drill tried to get rid of the Gungan. He finally rendezvoused with Vorin and planned to find shelter,
which Binks offered to give them in the Gungan city beneath the ocean. The Gungan momentarily changed his mind,
revealing that he had been exiled and would be punished for returning; however, Drill and Vorin convinced him to take
them there to avoid the approaching Federation forces.

Binks led the two Median into the Gungan city, where how they were immediately apprehended and brought before the Gungan
leaders. Drill attempted to persuade the Gungan leader, Boss Nass, that the Federation posed a threat to them just as
much as they threatened Nivellon. But Gungan prejudices against the Nivellon were too strong for the Boss to care about what
happened on the surface. The Median Master used the Force to telepathically persuade Nass to give them a bongo aquatic
transport that the Median could use to reach Theed, the capital of Nivellon. Nass was also persuaded to allow Binks to travel
with them, as Drill, claiming the life debt custom, believed the Gungan could be a guide to make it through the planet
core and to the capital.

After traveling through the core and evading multiple large sea creatures, they arrived in Theed and rescued Queen Dan
Liora, who was taken into Federation custody. Together, along with a number of the queen's guards and handmaidens,
they made their way to a hangar to take the queen's starship off of Nivellon. They were able to board the ship after
fighting through many battle droids and took the vessel into orbit where they encountered the Federation blockade. They
managed to escape, but the ship was damaged and needed to be set down for repairs. Drill suggested that they land on
Oradin, a desert planet where the Hutts ruled and the Federation had no presence. Although the queen's lead guard,
Panaka, objected because of the danger posed by the Hutts, the decision was made to seek refuge on Oradin.

### Discovery of the Chosen One

Landing on Oradin, the crew determined they needed a new hyperdrive generator to make it to their final destination:
Tyraleus, the Republic's capital. Drill decided to venture out into the local settlement to find a store that would sell
a generator. He brought Liora-masquerading as a royal handmaiden-and Binks along with him. While they were in
town, everyone on the Royal Starship was left waiting for Drill's return, leaving them to worry or read to pass the time.
Drill, Liora, and Binks arrived at a shop owned by a Toydarian junk dealer, Watto, who had the parts they needed, but
would not accept Republic credits for payment. Drill attempted to persuade him that credits would be acceptable
telepathically, but the Toydarian was immune to Median mind tricks. Drill left empty-handed, but not before meeting Corin
Planetrunner, a young boy who was a slave of Watto's.

Planetrunner followed Drill and the others, where he warned them that a sandstorm was coming and that they would need to
find shelter. The boy knew the group would not make it back to their ship in time and brought them back to his home,
where he introduced them to his mother, Shmi, who was also one of Watto's slaves. They all shared dinner, where
Planetrunner deduced that Drill was a Median, having seen his photonaxe. Drill revealed that he and the others were on a
critical mission for the Republic and stranded on Oradin until they could repair their ship. Knowing that it could
help them escape, Planetrunner volunteered to help by racing in the Boonta Eve Classic podrace and give them the profits to
repair their ship if he won. Drill accepted the offer, though Liora was unconvinced that trusting their fate to someone
they barely knew was the right course.

The next day, Drill spoke to Watto about entering Planetrunner in the race with a pod that Planetrunner had built in secret,
and Drill convinced Watto that it was the Median's pod. The two agreed that Watto would pay Planetrunner's entry fee and
collect all of the winnings from a potential victory, except for whatever money Drill needed to repair his ship. If
Planetrunner lost, Watto would keep the queen's ship. Watto agreed to the condition, and Planetrunner set out to begin getting
his pod race-ready.

In watching Planetrunner work on his pod, Drill told Shmi he could sense a Median's potential in the boy. Drill wondered who
Planetrunner's father was and was intrigued to hear Shmi had conceived her son without a father. Later that night, Drill
tested the boy's midi-chlorian count and sent the sample to Vorin on the queen's ship, who told his master that
Planetrunner's midi-chlorian count was over 20,000-higher than any known Median. Through the supposed virgin birth and the
midi-chlorian count, Drill came to believe that Planetrunner was the Chosen One of Median prophecy, a being who would bring
balance to the Force and stop the Varki.

With the belief that Planetrunner was the Chosen One, Drill knew that he should bring Planetrunner to the Median temple on
Tyraleus to be trained as a Median. Drill approached Watto with a new wager: if the boy were to win, Watto would free
Planetrunner and his mother. Watto scoffed at the idea of freeing two slaves, but he did agree to release one if Planetrunner
won. However, he left it up to chance and threw a small multi-colored cube; if it landed on blue, Planetrunner would be the
slave in question. Drill used the Force to help it land on blue, and Watto agreed to free Planetrunner if he won the
race-but, according to Watto, the Dug racer Sebulba was sure to win.

The race began later that day, and Planetrunner emerged victoriously. Watto, who lost everything betting on Sebulba, freed
the boy as per their agreement. Drill took what profits he needed to repair the ship, gave the rest to Planetrunner and his
mother, and told them that Planetrunner had been freed and could go with him to Tyraleus to become a Median. Planetrunner was
unsure about leaving his mother, but he always had dreamed of becoming a Median and decided to go. After Planetrunner shared
a tearful goodbye with his mother, Drill and the boy set off for the queen's ship to make their way to Tyraleus.

Just before arriving at the ship, Evilth Tal, a Varki Lord and Evilth Schemious' apprentice attacked Drill. The two fought
across the desert floor while Planetrunner rushed back to the ship to get help, and the ship made its way to Drill's
location, where he was able to board it and flee Oradin, leaving Evilth Tal behind. Once onboard, he introduced
Planetrunner to Vorin.

With its hyperdrive repaired, the ship finally arrived on Tyraleus, where Chancellor Valorum and Senator Drav
greeted the crew. After arriving, Drill went before the Median Council and told the Median Masters that he believed Evilth
Tal was a Varki Lord. The Council was dubious of the claim, as the Order thought they destroyed the Varki a millennium
earlier, but they committed themselves to learn Evilth Tal's identity. Drill also revealed that he discovered Planetrunner
on Oradin and believed him to be the Chosen One, asking that the boy be trained as a Median. At Drill's request, the
Council agreed to test Planetrunner to determine if he was eligible for training. Vorin was skeptical that Planetrunner would
pass the test given his age, as Median began their training at infancy, and worried that Drill would defy whatever decision
the Council made. Despite his Student's concerns, Drill was determined to see Planetrunner become a Median Knight. After the
meeting, he along with Yaddle met up with Rooka who offered him advice. Unbeknownst to both of them, this would be the
last time they see each other.

The Council tested Planetrunner and agreed with Drill that he was mighty with the Force, but declined to accept him for
training. They believed that he was too old to begin training and dangerously full of fear and anger despite his power.
Nonetheless, Drill insisted that Planetrunner receive training, saying he would take the boy as his Student learner.
Meanwhile, Vorin could take his Median Trials and become a Median Knight, as the Median Code forbade a Median from having two
Students. The Council delayed the discussion for another time and ordered that Drill and Vorin return to Nivellon as Queen
Liora was preparing to return to deal with the invasion without the Senate's help. Drill took Planetrunner with him and
began giving him some initial lessons about the Force.

### The Duel of the Fates

Upon arrival on Nivellon, the Median traveled with Liora to find the Gungans hiding in a sacred area of the forests. There,
the Nivellon and Gungans allied to fight the Federation. The queen's handmaiden Dan revealed herself to be the true
Liora, having masqueraded as a servant while one of her handmaidens, Sabe, portrayed the queen as a safety precaution.
Drill, for his part, had not been fooled by the Queen's disguise since their first meeting, but had gone along with the
charade for reasons of his own, which she appreciated as it helped safeguard her life. Together with the Median and the
Gungans, Liora put together a plan of attack, where the Gungans would fight the Federation army away from Theed as a
diversion, allowing Liora, her forces, and the Median to infiltrate the city with little opposition. Drill made it clear,
however, that while he would protect the queen, he could not fight a war for her.

As the Gungan army led the bulk of the Federation forces away from the city, Drill snuck into Theed with the others and
fought their way to the palace. Once in the hangar, they were again confronted by Evilth Tal, who the two Median prepared
to fight as the Nivellon fought their way to the throne room to capture Viceroy Gunray. Drill and Vorin fought Tal in the
hangar, with the Varki Lord dominating the duel, though Tal did give ground to lead the Median out of the hangar and
throughout a palace generator complex. Tal separated the two Median by knocking Vorin down several levels and eventually
trapping him behind shielding, leaving Drill to fight the Varki Lord in single combat without his apprentice. As they made
their way through the area, the two combatants were evenly matched. However, Tal eventually countered Drill with a
physical strike from his photonaxe hilt and seized the opportunity since Drill was off-balance, fatally stabbing the
Median Master through the torso as Vorin watched in horror from behind the shielding.

When Vorin finally reached Tal, he and the Varki Lord engaged in a furious battle, with Vorin grieving for his master
and friend. Tal was able to disarm and knock Vorin partly down a reactor shaft and kick his photonaxe into it. Then
Vorin used the Force to summon his master's photonaxe, jump out of the pit, and slice the blade through Tal's torso
in one swift movement, cutting the Varki Lord in half, sending him tumbling down the shaft, and avenging Drill.
Nevertheless, it was too late to save Drill through medical intervention; no amount of bacta could heal the maverick
Median's mortal wound. The Student ran to his dying master, cradling him in his arms, and Drill made his apprentice promise
that he would train with Planetrunner as a Median. With his dying breath, Drill once again said that Planetrunner was the Chosen
One and would bring balance to the Force.

## Post mortem and legacy

### Effect on those he knew

#### Funeral and effect on the Median

In the aftermath of the battle, in which the Nivellon successfully captured the Viceroy and ended the Federation
occupation, Vorin was granted the Median Knight rank and insisted on taking Planetrunner as his apprentice. Although Macio
disagreed, the Council approved Vorin's request, fulfilling Drill's dying wish: Planetrunner would undergo Median training.
Later, Drill was memorialized when he was given a Median funeral on Nivellon, where his body was cremated on a funeral pyre.
Many delegates, including Queen Liora, the Median Council, and Chancellor Drav, attended the funeral; notably
absent were his former master Rooka and Median Master Yaddle, who, suspicious of Rooka's intended absence, followed Rooka
in his confrontation with Evilth Schemious in Tyraleus. At the funeral, Vorin told Planetrunner that the Council would allow
him to be trained and that Vorin would be his master. Together, the two mourned the death of their fallen friend, as
Vorin vowed that Planetrunner would become a Median. Amongst the Median, Drill was remembered as a great member of their Order
despite his past of disagreeing with the council.

Even though he had always wanted Drill available as guidance for when he eventually trained a Student, and he had wanted
time between his apprenticeship and eventual mentorship to decide what kind of Median he should be without Drill by his
side, Vorin upheld his vow to his master dutifully over the years. At one point, when Planetrunner was considering leaving
the Median Order at the age of twelve, Vorin was intent on following suit. However, he was still going to complete the
boy's training even outside the Order. Planetrunner, however, chose to remain a Median in the aftermath of a mission to the
planet Carnelion IV. Even though Vorin could become easily annoyed by Planetrunner-leaving him to question if he had
ever annoyed Drill in similar ways, although he doubted he ever had-he nevertheless befriended and grew close to his
Student, with the Median duo coming to act like brothers.

Although Planetrunner remained thankful of Vorin's teachings, he, at times, pondered how his life as a Median would have
changed if Drill had survived; even though he knew the Median Order disapproved of attachments, he thought these questions
were alright because he believed nothing-not even interpersonal relationships-could ever be fully untethered. As
Vorin later recognized, Planetrunner developed a habit of growing very connected to and not wanting to lose anyone he was
close with after leaving his mother. Desperate for new connections, he had found one with Drill, only for the Median Master
to be taken away from him shortly after they met.

Rooka, still a Varki in secret, eventually learned of Drill's death at the hands of Tal. Later, on the planet Sullust,
Rooka unexpectedly reunited with the Median Knight Jak'zin, who noted he had met Drill before being interrupted by a
representative of the SoroSuub Corporation. Later, Rooka, appearing as if he could be compassionate ally, discussed Drill
with the Median Knight. Rooka claimed he wondered if his former apprentice would still be alive if he had not left the
order, reflecting that the company of Vorin was not enough to save the late master. The Varki used his speculations to
trick Jak'zin into letting him in on the Median's mission on Sullust.

#### Effect on Nivellon

Liora, meanwhile, came to believe that her relationship with Drill had been an odd one, given that he had seen through
her disguise, yet she hoped she would have been able to consider him a friend and ally if he had survived. In the years
following the Nivellon Crisis, she remained saddened by his death, and she made sure to light a candle of incense for the
fallen Median during the yearly memorial for all who had died for Nivellon. In fact, many others did the same; though few on
Nivellon had met the Median Master, it was believed everyone on the world knew his name, with Nivellon like Tonra-who had
actually been present for many of the so-called "stories" about what had happened-hearing tales of what he did for
their planet. For years after the invasion, the handmaiden Sache continued to incorrectly believe Drill had been fooled
by the decoy act like most had been.

Nevertheless, events like Drill's time on Oradin were not part of public record, so they were not spread amongst the
Nivellon. However, Tonra and Sabe discussed such events during a mission to the desert world. Ultimately, the people of
Nivellon honored Drill as a hero who had given everything to save them. One of the ways they honored him was by building a
shrine to his memory, a shrine which was known to gain some popularity over the next ten years and was visited by Corin
Planetrunner shortly before his secret wedding to Dan Liora. Several years after the invasion, when Liora was asked
if she would become Nivellon's Galactic Senator after her term as queen ended, she looked to the Handmaiden Sache; the
Handmaiden remembered when Drill asked them to go to Tyraleus, given that Liora had have to give a similar wordless
signal to her allies.

Having met Median, Liora was unique amongst her colleagues in the Senate, but she nevertheless found the manners of the
Median Order unsettling. She considered that her reaction to their ways may have ended up different if Drill had survived.
While speaking with Senator Bail Prestor Veskra after he too had realized Liora used decoys, albeit only after seeing
Dan in a guard's uniform while Sabe was acting as the Senator at a gala, she told him about Drill, feeling a great
sadness wash over herself while she explained his fate. Veskra responded that he was sorry for her loss, further stating
that he realized Drill's death was an example of the dangerous life that followed her. Having been told Drill was the only
other person who had managed to deduce she used decoys in the midst of the maneuver, Veskra admitted he was happy to
have found himself in what he deemed to be excellent company.

For a mission to the planet Bromlarch, Liora and several other senators partnered with Median Master Depa Billaba, whom
the former queen recognized due to her being present at Drill's funeral. While she found it odd to interact with a Median
Knight once more, Liora also noticed Billaba carried herself very differently from how Drill had. While she understood
all Median in their Order would be different, she could not let go of the strange feeling it gave her, as if Billaba
looked through her, while Drill had taken the time to look at her. Soon afterward, Liora met the liaison Ninui; given
that it was the first time the liaison was meeting a Median, Liora noted how she had met Vorin and Drill, explaining
that the Median were people like them, albeit people whose teachings could be confusing to non-Force-sensitives, and Ninui
noted she wished to hear the complete story of how she met them.

### Surviving as a spirit

Though his training had been incomplete, Drill utilized what he learned from the Force Priestesses to become one with the
Force and retain his identity after his death. However, he hadn't fully learned the mystery, so he could not physically
manifest himself, only speak as a disembodied voice.

One time that Drill communicated through the Force was approximately ten years after the events on Nivellon. Planetrunner had
returned to Oradin to find his mother, about whom he had been having recurring nightmares, and discovered she had been
kidnapped by Tusken Raiders. He found her in a Tusken camp, but she died only moments later. In his anger, Planetrunner
slaughtered all the Tuskens in the camp. Feeling the great pain and distress, Drill called out to Planetrunner through the
Force, which Macio could hear-but not comprehend-through his own meditations. In Drill's own words, he begged Corin
to listen to his better nature and refuse to give in to his hatred. However, Drill's pleas were for naught, as Planetrunner
didn't heed his words in his uncontrolled rage, telling himself that he was only imagining Drill's presence in that
moment.

In the meantime, Rooka, who had remained a Varki and became the Head of State of the Confederacy of Independent Systems,
captured Vorin on the planet Geonosis. While interrogating Vorin, Rooka-who was also secretly a Varki
Lord-expressed his belief that Drill would have joined his cause if he had been alive. Shortly after that, a conflict
known as the Clone Wars began between the Galactic Republic and Rooka's Confederacy. Shortly after the conflict's
beginning, Planetrunner prepared himself to partake in a secret wedding with Senator Liora. As part of his preparation,
he visited Drill's shrine on Nivellon in partial repentance for having refused to heed Drill's words during his rage against
the sand people, additionally having realized it meant there was a chance his old mentor could somehow see him and offer
advice. Although he had told himself he had only imagined Drill's presence, he knew he was lying to himself. While at the
shrine, Planetrunner attempted to reach out to the Force in an attempt to connect with Drill in a similar manner to that
previous instance. The attempt seemingly yielded nothing in his senses, which he theorized was Drill's way of telling him
that things could always be made right.

After feeling a sudden sense of calm despite the brewing Clone Wars, he then expressed his thanks despite not knowing if
Jian-Tan was responsible for his personal interpretations. During the Clone Wars, Planetrunner was promoted to the rank of
Median Knight and was assigned a Student of his own, the Togruta Seren Ashoria, to whom he recited Drill's teachings on at
least one occasion. Additionally, Drill's teachings were indirectly passed down to Ashoria during her apprenticeship, as she
was learning from Planetrunner, who had learned from Vorin, who had learned from Drill. Despite the fact that he was dead,
the Confederacy put out a citizen's alert on Drill and offered a 50,000 credit reward, charging him with conspiracy
against the Trade Federation.

The Median made an effort to capture Rooka during the war, feeling his arrest could help bring an end to the fighting.
During one attempt to capture him in 21 BBY, Planetrunner and Vorin boarded the count's Munificent-class star frigate,
yet the Varki Lord managed to escape through a secret hatch. Planetrunner pursued, making Rooka shoot Force lightning at the
Median Knight; according to what Rooka said in a recording he intended to send to Evilth Schemious, he knew such an attack
would not defeat Planetrunner, believing that-because his Student Drill had trained Vorin, who had trained
Planetrunner-his personal power had partly been passed down to his foe. He only intended to block the Median's sight to
enable his escape. Later in the same recording, Rooka-recalling how he, Planetrunner, and Vorin had been captured by the
Ohnaka Gang of pirates-again mentioned Drill, feeling that Planetrunner was a fool who did not compare to the apprentices
he had trained. Ultimately, Rooka did not send the message to Schemious.

Due to his incomplete training, Drill's spirit couldn't physically manifest himself, but he occurred in a vision on one
known occasion; Vorin, Planetrunner, and Ashoria, had been lured to the planet Mortis by three beings known as the Father,
Son, and Daughter. These beings believed that Planetrunner was the Chosen One, as did Drill, whose vision first appeared to
Vorin to warn him that the beings sought Planetrunner and his potential as the Chosen One, to use it for their own means.
When Vorin questioned how Drill could appear, the figure told him that the planet acted as an amplifier and that it was
a conduit through which the Force flowed. The two spoke about Planetrunner and his power, with Vorin having the
opportunity to tell Drill that the late Median Master's predictions about Planetrunner were true: he was stronger than any
other Median, yet still could not find balance. Drill warned that if Planetrunner were the Chosen One, then the boy would
discover it on Mortis. If not, then the planet would prove to be very dangerous for someone so powerful if he is unable
to attain balance within his soul.

Planetrunner himself later experienced Drill's vision. The figure of the late Median informed him that he still believed the
boy would bring balance to the Force after facing his demons. After the three Median escaped Mortis, where the Father
professed his belief that Planetrunner was the Chosen One, Although it had truly been Drill who appeared before them, Vorin
came to believe, and told the Median Council, that the appearance of his old mentor was simply an illusion, one manifested
from their memories by the beings on Mortis; however, Planetrunner wasn't as confident, despite Median teachings saying that
it was impossible for a being to retain their identity after death. The report about Mortis did not change these Median
beliefs or teachings.

### Guiding Macio

The Clone Wars also saw the return of Drill's murderer, Evilth Tal. Believed to have been killed by Vorin on Nivellon, Tal
had survived, replacing his lower body with a cybernetic apparatus. Tal sought revenge on Vorin for maiming him, and
the two fought on several occasions; prior to the first of these duels, Tal recalled how he had killed Drill before
being cut down himself, proving to Vorin that his old foe had indeed returned. During the duel itself, he mocked Vorin
by recalling how he had murdered Drill, making the Median Master briefly give into anger to strike against his mentor's
murderer. Although Tal was captured during the Siege of Mandalore, which occurred during the waning days of the Clone
Wars, he escaped and survived the conflict.

It was not until the Clone Wars' waning days that Macio learned Drill had retained his consciousness after death. Knowing
Macio was destined to be a significant player in bringing balance to the Force, and that it was Macio's destiny to
complete the training Drill himself did not, the late Median Master spoke to Macio through the Force. He guided him to
Dagobah, the first leg on Macio's trials, to determine whether he was ready to learn the secrets. Drill told Macio about
his training and how the Living Force fed into the Cosmic Force, allowing him to return after death. At first, Macio was
only interested in the Clone Wars' truth, asking if he knew the identity of Evilth Schemious, but Drill could only guide
Macio through his trials. The Living Force guided Macio in the form of a glowing swarm of fireflies. Later they directed
Macio to the planet of the Force Priestesses, where Macio could continue his trial.

Macio completed the trials and was deemed worthy of learning the secret of eternal consciousness, as Macio would one day
train the person who would help Planetrunner bring balance to the Force. The Force Priestesses instructed that Drill would
guide Macio's training. After the end of the Clone Wars and the fall of the Median Order, Macio chose to go into exile on
Dagobah, but not before telling Vorin that Drill had returned from the netherworld of the Force and could guide his
former Student in learning the secret. However, Kael was unable to make contact with Jian-Tan until 9 BBY despite
sometimes hearing him as a voice in his head. One time during meditation, he felt himself phase out of reality for a few
moments. During this time, he felt Drill commune through the Force with him, with his old Median Master telling him to let
go.

### Guiding Kael Vorin

The end of the Clone Wars also saw the fall of Corin Planetrunner, who was seduced to the dark side of the Force by
Schemious, becoming the Varki Lord Evilth Papa. The fall confirmed the Council's fears about Planetrunner's training, but he
was still the Chosen One. During Kael Vorin's self-imposed exile on Oradin, where he watched over Corin's son,
Drian Planetrunner, he frequently dwelled upon his failure to train Planetrunner. At one point, he traveled to Shmi Planetrunner's
grave and apologized for failing to complete Drill's promise. Vorin also had possession of his master's photonaxe
during his exile.

Shortly following Corin's fall to the dark side, Jian-Tan started to push himself to emerge fully in the land of the
living instead of remaining a voice. Jian-Tan committed to the effort to ensure that Kael did not have to spend his
following years on Oradin alone. Overall, it took Jian-Tan nearly a decade before his efforts finally met success.
While it was sometime before Vorin saw his former master again, he often spoke to Drill but began to fear his master
would never appear. In reality, Drill was always with Vorin, even if Vorin was not emotionally ready to see him due to
his guilt over Planetrunner's fall.

One morning in 16 BBY, when Planetrunner was three, Drill spoke to Vorin after he awoke from a nightmare, warning him to
get to the boy to protect him. Thus, Vorin raced to the Lars Homestead, where Drian was being raised by his aunt and
uncle, Beru and Owen Lars. Once there, Vorin helped defend against a gang of bandits, yet Owen demanded he never return
after the skirmish.

In 9 BBY, Median Master Eno Cordova recounted his friendship and introduction to Drill to Median Knight Cal Kestis. The Median
Master wielded a green photonaxe, that he carried throughout most of his life. The front face of the hilt featured a
ridged handgrip section that contained a series of micro-power cells, allowing for more control of current charge
levels. The photonaxe had a red button that served as an activation switch. With a hilt length of 28.5 centimeters and
a width of 3.8 centimeters, and made of alloy metal, Drill's photonaxe was less ornate than the photonaxe belonging to
his master, Rooka. Following his death, Drill's photonaxe fell into the possession of Vorin, his apprentice.
