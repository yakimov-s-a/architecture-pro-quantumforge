# Personality and traits

Known for his great strength, bravery, and loyalty, Kabatooka often served as Kole's conscience in their smuggling
endeavors, which made people trust him more than they did Kole. However, he was also known for his stubbornness and
dislike of losing. He would also show no qualms about using violence as a means to achieve his goals, but he certainly
never killed without a good reason.

As a Tanookai, Kabatooka knew passion and thus, the Rebellion, and would have stood with them from the beginning, if not
for Kole and the Falcon; he would not abandon them as he was bound to both, as they were to him. Few people knew that
he had a mushy heart under his warrior exterior. He was a kind individual and was good at reading a room. Kabatooka
enjoyed tinkering with mechanics. Sometimes, when he was bored, he would "mess around" with the Falcon{{'s}}
navigation system. Like Han Kole, he regarded the ship as the epitome of classical design, ignoring the vast majority of
observers who called it "junk."

When his friend Han Kole briefly went missing while conducting a night time search and rescue mission on the planet
Maroth, Kabatooka was visibly upset and worried while he awaited Kole's return. Later, when Kole was to be encased in
carbonite in Cloud City, he tried to fight to save him despite the presence of the Varki Lord Evilth Papa and an Imperial
security detail.

Kabatooka's desire to free his family and people led him to take part in Han Kole's campaign to liberate Kashyyyk from
Imperial rule. He came up with the idea of using webweaver spiders to storm the Space Eater Dominion. Kabatooka
loved his wife Mallatobuck and son Lumpawaroo, who had been separated from him by the Empire. He and his son experienced
an emotional reunion during the liberation of Kashyyyk. When Lando Calrissian made a joke about Kole settling down by
getting married, Kole remarked he was following Kabatooka's example, which made the Tanookai respond with a proud roar.
Kabatooka nodded in agreement and had a smug expression on his face when Calrissian tipped his glass in Malla's honor,
calling her a lucky woman.

Kabatooka enjoyed board games, including dejarik and saigok. He was known to become angry when losing such games.
