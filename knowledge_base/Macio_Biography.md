# Biography

## Early life

Born nine-hundred years before the Battle of Calystron, Macio belonged to an ancient and mysterious species and little was
known of Macio's early life; he never revealed his homeworld. As he was keenly attuned to the Force, he joined the Median
Order as a youngling.As a Median Initiate, Macio made use of a training photonaxe. After undergoing the ritual known as
the Gathering, he received his own personal kyber crystal, and Professor Huyang helped him construct his first
photonaxe aboard the Crucible. The circumstances surrounding Macio and the construction of his first photonaxe were
exciting, according to Huyang. At some point during his time as a youngling, Macio skipped across the lily pads in the
Median Temple's meditation pond.

Although Macio spoke little of his early days, it was rumored that one of his early instructors was the Hysalrian N'Kata
Del Gormo. Macio was paired up with a personal mentor at a younger age then usual. During their time together, Macio's
Median Master told him the tale of K'ungfu and Chuang, two great Median of the past. As a Student, Macio visited multiple
planets and during his first years he could have named them all, but in the following centuries, he couldn't remember
how many he'd seen and later estimated the number to be in the tens of thousands. Around the age of 100, in 796 BBY,
Macio was ready to pass on what he had learned. Having attained the rank of Master, he spent the next eight centuries
training and tutoring generations of Median. It was estimated that over his lifetime, he trained around twenty-thousand
Median.

## High Republic Era

### Early High Republic Era

By the High Republic Era, Macio was considered a great Median Master. He had also gained a seat on the Median High Council
and the rank of Grand Master. Two other Median-Xo Lahru and Pra-Tre Veter-shared the Grand Master title with Macio.
Eventually the human Median Ry Ki-Sakka also attained the position. As the Median who taught younglings, Macio one day met
the human youngling Gella Nattai, who was scared of the training remotes and ran from the room. Finding Nattai in the
Temple garden and sitting with her, Macio and Nattai watched a frog in the garden repeatedly try to jump into a waterfall
behind a stone wall. Macio used the frog as a lesson, teaching her to face her fear and always get back up to try again.

### An uncertain Path

In 382 BBY, after learning of the Battle of Jedha and the failure of a peace treaty between the warring planets Eiram
and E'ronoh there, Macio went to the Republic Executive Building on the galactic capital Tyraleus to meet with with
Supreme Chancellor Kyong Greylark while they waited for more information from Jedha. They spoke to Median Master Creighton
Sun and Median Knight Aida Forte, who were on the ground on Jedha and confirmed that the E'roni ambassador was dead and
the Eirami had deployed a droid army. They requested to perform an investigation on the Outer Rim Territories planet
Dalna of the Path of the Open Hand cult, considering their involvement in the failure of the peace talks, the fact that
Median Master Silandra Sho felt a disturbance that made the Median ill at the Path's almshouse, and that Median Knight Gella
Nattai believed the Chancellor's criminal son Axel Greylark was working with the Path. Macio admitted that he had sensed
a disturbance beyond Jedha, and emphasized that they need to be careful, reminding them that there was always those who
thrived in chaos. After much deliberation, Macio and Chancellor Greylark eventually agreed to a fact-finding mission
accompanied by two of her guards and to send Median Master Char-Ryl-Roy and Student Enya Keen to Eiram and E'ronoh to try
and keep the peace and discover what was causing communications buoys to be down, Macio explaining to them that Werth
Plouth, the Path's Herald, was responsible for the riots on Jedha.

After the communication ended, Macio expressed his concern as they received another message from Median Master Yaddle, who
was accompanied by a Median Initiate named Cippa Tarko, though the connection of the message was poor. Tarko asked about
Macio, and Yaddle explained that he was a Median Master and not her brother. Macio told Yaddle that they were sending help
to Jedha and two Median to Dalna and that she was to return to Tyraleus, but the message was so choppy they were not sure
if she understood. Macio had an unsettling feeling in the Force that reminded him of the feeling he got whenever a battle
was coming. Master Yaddle did misunderstand Macio's message to mean she was needed on Dalna and joined Sun and Forte
there, who sent a message to Macio confirming they were there shortly before Yaddle arrived.

Later, Chancellor Greylark had her guards summon Macio to her to reveal that her son Axel had escaped from prison with
the help of the Path and someone named Binnot Ullo. She told Macio that she did not know who Ullo was and did not think
Axel had many friends. He assured her that no one, no galaxy, and no republic was perfect, and that she should continue
to fight for the greater good like the Median did. She explained that she felt like they have all been moving through a
fog that has corrupted everything lately and Macio insisted that Axel still had good in him and that the 'fog' was just
corrupting him too. However, the Chancellor resolved to make Axel truly feel the consequences of his actions.

### Chancellor no more

Eventually, Greylark received a message from her son on Dalna and immediately called Macio to have him listen to it with
her via hologram. After hearing Axel ask for her to go to Dalna to speak with the Path and with him, Macio asked her what
she would do, the Chancellor deciding to go to Dalna. She then recorded a message for Axel that included Macio in it,
wearing a blank expression, so that he could bear witness to her stepping down as Chancellor. After she sent the
message, Macio stayed with her wordlessly for a while, and she appreciated his kindness.

Before Greylark could leave for Dalna, Macio approached her and said that since he had not heard back from Masters Sun or
Ela Sutan, who had also been sent to Dalna, that he would go with her because she was still his friend even though she
was no longer Chancellor. Macio had a Median Longbeam cruiser follow them with a contingent of Median. While in hyperspace,
they received a distress call from Princess Xiri A'lbaran of E'ronoh and Prince Phan-tu Zenn of Eiram. They then
received a message from Chancellor Orlen Mollo, who said that the leaders of Eiram and E'ronoh were still not
cooperating. Macio volunteered to rescue the heirs due to their proximity to them and left, taking other Median with him.

### The Night of Sorrow

Eventually, Macio arrived at the battle that was now raging on Dalna between the Path and the Median, which came to be
known as the Night of Sorrow. The Path, much of it reorganized into the Path of the Closed Fist, was led by Marda Ro of
the Ro family. He was joined by Princess A'lbaran and Prince Zenn, the three announcing that the combined forces of
Eiram and E'ronoh were there to fight together with the Median. When Macio arrived on the ground at the Path compound, Median
Gella Nattai told him to direct people to go to the cave system as there was potentially many more Path members there.
On Dalna, he met Path Little Tromak. Macio eventually came to forget Tromak but always remembered the importance of his
visit to Dalna.

As the battle started to come to a close with it turning in the Median's favor, Macio and Yaddle stood together, the latter
watching Cippa Tarko sleep. However, A'lbaran and Zenn contacted them and informed them that explosions have been set
off underground. As Yaddle suggested getting help, the ground shook and Macio spotted sections of the caves collapse into
sinkholes. Macio and Yaddle then sensed a Median in distress and went to help. Finding a door in the ground, Macio and
Yaddle connected to the force and sensed Gella Nattai and Axel Greylark. Yaddle lifted up the ground, rocks, and soil
while Macio lifted the water to try and rescue them. Enya Keen then ran toward them, seeing Nattai, and pulled her and
Axel from the mud-like water, having been brought to the surface by the two Median Masters. Macio and Yaddle then set the
water and mud down far away. In the sinkhole, E'roni and Eirami soldiers began recovering survivors. Yaddle noticed that
the sounds of battle have faded and the sun was rising. She asks Macio if the battle was done, and he says it was.

Macio met with Kyong Greylark and Chancellor Mollo on a medical frigate above Tyraleus, the three discussing now only
having one Chancellor instead of two and what it means. Macio suggested they speak to the Median Council and tell them
everything that went wrong, but Greylark also wanted to focus all the things that went right, including the help of the
Republic and the Median. Mollo revealed that the people of Dalna were calling the event the Night of Sorrow. While he
imagined the future, Greylark assured Macio that she would meet him on Tyraleus soon, Macio adding that there was much to
celebrate and remember.

### Return to the Temple

Macio then traveled to the Median Temple on Tyraleus, confirming first that the royal families of Eiram and E'ronoh had
already gathered, who he met with when he arrived. He met with several Median, including Yaddle, who was standing with
Tarko. Macio raised his arm to silence the room and Master Sun rose from his chair, walking to the center of the room
using a walking stick before beginning a funeral for Median Aida Forte, Orin Darhga, and Char-Ryl-Roy. Macio allowed Roy's
former Student, Keen, to keep his photonaxe and use it alongside hers.

Later, in the Median Archives, Creighton spoke with Macio about Nameless creatures that the Path deployed on Dalna and the
horror they wrought upon the Median, causing them to be weakened, hallucinate, and eventually be turned to dust. Macio
confirmed that there is a larger danger connected to them, which caused Creighton to wonder what Macio knew. Realizing
how catastrophic to the Order it could be if people found out about the creatures, and since they had heard no other
reports of their usage, they decided to keep quiet about them and not include them in the Median Archive. They agreed not
to speak of it again and each of them left.

### Master of Kantam Sy

At some point, Macio took on a human named Kantam Sy as his Student. The two were stationed at the Median Temple on the
planet Endovar. Sy was a very talented Student and was far ahead of their peers and even some of their elders, with Macio
even admitting that they were the most disciplined Student he had ever taught and one of the most connected to the
Force. Despite this, Macio held off knighting Sy, for a reason that the Student did not understand until they began a
romantic relationship with an acrobat named Aytar and realized that both Macio and them had been waiting for something
like that to happen. Eventually, Sy decided to leave the Median Order, a decision partially incited by their newfound
relationship with Aytar.

Macio and Sy loved to spar with each other, and it was how the two often found understanding and how Sy often found inner
harmony while troubled or when they had a problem. Even when they did not find a solution during a match, they would
leave feeling like an answer was out there. When Sy traveled to the meditation room in the Endovar Median Temple in order
to tell Macio about their decision, Macio challenged them to a sparring match, remembering that they had dueled many times
in that room and saying that this duel could be one final lesson. Sy agreed to the duel, as it was custom to not refuse
a duel with Master Macio, but also realized that this meant Macio knew about their decision. Macio dodged Sy's question by
engaging in combat, before saying that the time had come to make a choice, but not the one that Sy thought. Macio
explained that the Force chose Sy just as it had chosen all other Median, and asked when Sy would choose the Force. Sy
insisted that they chose the Force every day when they took part in their Median training, but after knocking Sy to the
ground, Macio asked if we really make a choice when we do not believe there are any other options. Macio told Sy that
there was a reason he had not knighted them yet, before ending the duel, helping Sy up, and sitting down with Sy to
meditate.

Eventually Sy asked Macio if they should leave the Median Order, but Macio insisted that there was no 'should' and that the
right thing to do is an idea we invent to make ourselves feel better. Macio also suggested that Sy had stopped listening,
in the literal sense, and in a larger general sense. Macio explained that feelings are like wind, we can feel it, we can
experience it, but it passes, though sometimes it can feel like a hurricane which can make it easy to give in to anger
and fear. Macio told Sy that in order for them to continue to be a Median they must actively choose the Median Order and the
Force and learn to listen to the world outside of their emotions even when they were as loud as a hurricane. Eventually,
after a moment, Sy set down their photonaxe in front of Macio. Macio nodded at them, and then his Student left him, and
the Median Order, behind. Macio was saddened but supported Sy through their departure. Sy would reflect on the conversation
they had that day throughout the rest of their life.

Over a year later, Sy returned to the Median, and met with Macio on the Endovar. Macio was in the process of helping a pet
Happabore named Monsalmo at the time, after being awoken from a nap by the creature's squealing. Macio was used to
dealing with her complicated behaviors, including finding her inside the temple underneath the younglings' bunks. This
time, Monsalmo was pregnant and had climbed onto the roof of her pen to avoid giving birth in a fuel spill. However, the
roof could not hold the creature's weight and it collapsed. Macio caught Monsalmo with the Force, but at that same time
she started to give birth to her pups, so Macio had to carry them with the Force too. Just then, Sy and a Force-sensitive
girl they had rescued on the planet Nivellon named Lula Talisola arrived and both used the Force to help Macio carry
Monsalmo and her pups. Macio welcomed Sy back, telling them that they had left a Student and returned a Knight. A few
hours later, Macio knighted Sy in a modest ceremony so that they could take on Talisola as their Student learner.

After the ceremony, Sy and Macio sat around a fire near the happabore enclosure and discussed the events that had
occurred. Sy expressed that they did not understand how one second they felt so sure in their path separate from the
Order but was then pulled back to it when they saved Talisola. Macio explained that the young are the greatest teachers
and the greatest lesson that can be learned from them is necessity of detachment and letting go, that being a Median was
to let go. Sy realized that they had been the one that taught Macio that lesson. Macio revealed that he had struggled with
Sy's departure and had been unsure of the future. The two then shared Sweetpuff jamwiches with Talisola.

Sometime later, Macio and Sy met with Talisola in the Endovar Median temple. Sy told Talisola that the lesson she taught
the two masters was the hardest one of all. Talisola hoped that it was front flips with a photonaxe, which made Sy
retract their statement and say that it was the second hardest lesson to learn. Macio concurred, saying that the front
flip was nothing simple. But the lesson that Sy wanted to teach Talisola was the same one that Sy had learned from her
and Macio had learned from Sy: Detachment.

Sometime before 232 BBY, Macio traveled to the Median temple on Takodana to pick up a Force-sensitive Aloxian child named
Qort from Maz Kanata and Median Sav Malagán and bring him into the Median Order. Student Reath Silas briefly met Macio during
some events both attended together.

### Time on the Star Hopper

By 233 BBY, Macio was stationed alongside Master Zia Zaldor Zanna at the Median Grand Temple on Tyraleus assigning
youngling Median Initiates to different temple outposts across the galaxy to continue learning and training under their
teachers. Here he met initiates Kai Brightstar, Lys Solay, and Nubs; Macio gifted Brightstar his own training saber after
Brightstar sacrificed his to save Nubs while on a mission. In 232 BBY, Macio took a sabbatical from the Median Council,
having felt the Force needed him on a different path. Thus, Macio joined the crew of the Median Student Academic Cruiser
Star Hopper, allowing him to help train Students and younglings. Macio was also therefore able to help train Talisola,
who was stationed aboard the Star Hopper.

Macio appointed the Mon Calamari Median Master Ephru Shinn to hold his seat on the council while he was away. During the
Great Hyperspace Disaster in the Hetzal system, Median Master Avar Kriss established a Force network, which was eventually
aided by Median who were outside the system. Macio-located somewhere with the crew of younglings he was currently
teaching-helped the effort, adding his focus to the network to ensure a Tibanna gas container did not collide with an
R-class sun.

The Great Hyperspace Disaster also caused the Emergences, which were when giant pieces of debris exited hyperspace in
various places throughout the galaxy and threatened to hit planets at near lightspeed. Eventually, the Trymant system
was threatened by an Emergence. The staff of Republic Security Zone Monitoring Satellite 777B-Alpha reported the
incident to the Starlight Beacon space station, who then asked the only Republic or Median ship immediately within range,
the Star Hopper, to assist. Onboard was Macio along with fellow Median Master Torban Buck and a class of Students. As
they traveled through hyperspace to the Trymant system, Macio told the Students to prepare for anything because they had
no idea what was coming, but that the Force would reveal all. When they received the information that they were the only
Republic or Median ship nearby to respond, one of the Students suggested that they stay back and wait for backup. But Macio
insisted that they were Trymant's only hope and needed to press forward. Shortly before they exited hyperspace Macio had
the Students report what information they had discovered about the calamity. Macio and Buck decided to stay on the Star
Hopper to use the Force to divert the biggest pieces of debris but sent the Students in Rescue speeders to help on the
surface of the planet.

However, amid the chaos of the Emergence, a Spider Cruiser called the Squall Spider commanded by the Eye of the Nihil,
Marchion Ro arrived in Bralanak City to pick up Elder Tromak, whom Ro wanted the services of to find some information.
When the Students attempted to get Nihil marauders to allow them to use the Spider Cruiser to evacuate civilians, a
fight broke out, leading Macio and Buck to the surface to help the young Median and fight against the Nihil. During the
fight, a Force-sensitive civilian named Zeen Mrala who had helped the Median against the Emergence and against the Nihil,
told Masters Macio and Buck that her friend Krix Kamerat had been captured by the Nihil and needed to be rescued. Lula
Talisola offered to go, but Macio insisted that he would go rescue the boy. Master Buck protested, believing that he was
not a fighting Median and therefore should not be left to lead the Median, but Macio ignored him. Macio then protected the
group of Median from a large plasma projectile lobbed at them by a Nihil marauder by cutting it into pieces with his
photonaxe while it was still in the air. Macio then left to find Kamerat, but first asked Mrala to take her pet Cru Cham
Cham with him.

Marchion Ro knew of Macio and his power, and when he saw him on the planet told his subordinates to keep the Median Master
away from the Squall Spider, but Macio managed to sneak aboard the ship nonetheless. Macio eventually found Kamerat and
told him that they needed to leave quickly. Macio told Kamerat that Mrala had sent him and proved it by showing him Cham
Cham. Macio then gave Kamerat a Holoprojector that he told the boy he could reach out to Mrala with when he was ready.
However, Kamerat shortly after alerted the pirates that Macio was aboard. Having been raised by the Elders of the Path, a
religious order descended from the Path of the Open Hand that distrusted Force-sensitives due to believing the Force
should only be appreciated from a distance, as if it was a fire,, only to see that his best friend Zeen Mrala had
secretly been Force-sensitive and never told him, Kamerat felt betrayed and ended up siding with the Nihil because of
that sense of betrayal. With the Nihil looking for him, Macio ran from the marauders but uncovered that the Nihil had a
strange, purple artifact in a storage crate. Macio hoped to escape quietly but Cham Cham made a loud cry that alerted the
Nihil to their location and lead to a confrontation. Macio escaped and met up with Mrala, revealing to her that Cham Cham
had eaten several Nihil limbs in the confrontation. Macio then told Mrala that she needed to come with them to Starlight
Beacon. When she asked why he had not saved Kamerat, Macio told her that he could not save someone who did not want to be
rescued. With the Nihil drive from Trymant, Macio, Torban Buck, Zeen Mrala, and the Students traveled to Starlight Beacon
in the Star Hopper.

### Starlight Beacon

Following the Battle of Kur, Macio went with fellow Grand Master Pra-Tre Veter to Starlight Beacon. The two had a meeting
with the Supreme Chancellor Lina Soh to discuss Bacta manufacturing for the relief effort for the Great Disaster, being
in need of the right crops after what had occurred on Hetzal. But before their meeting, they visited the hub of the
beacon to meet with Master Avar Kriss, and revealed to her that the council had convened and named her the Marshal of
Starlight Beacon. Kriss also asked Macio if he was returning to the council, but Macio clarified that he was not finished
on the Star Hopper. Macio later attended the Beacon's dedication ceremony, where he said a few words about the
dedication plaque which was placed in a stone monument. He talked with Kai Brightstar, Lys Solay, Nash Durango, and
Nubs, mentioning their adventure trying to get the dedication plaque after it was stolen by the pirate Taborr Val Dorn,
who was secretly Prince Cyrus Vuundir of Vuundalla. He told them how proud he is of them and encouraged them to join the
rest of the Median for Lina Soh's speech. After the Median left, Macio spoke with Prince Vuundir, who was no longer in his
pirate disguise, telling him that he sensed much conflict in him but that he still had the potential for greatness if he
made the right choices. Macio told Cyrus that the future was in his hands. Joining with his fellow Median, he raised his
photonaxe and declared "For light and life."

After about a week, Zeen Mrala started settling in on Starlight very well, so Masters Macio, Avar Kriss, Kantam Sy,
Estala Maru, and Torban Buck all went to check up on her and discuss her plans, with Macio even bringing pastry treats.
The Masters deliberated and determined that she was too old to begin Median training and most likely could not be sent
back to Trymant. Master Sy suggested that she should stay with them temporarily, as she had proved herself valuable,
which Macio acknowledged was an excellent point. They did decide to let her stay, partially with the hope that she could
be helpful against the Nihil. When it became clear that finding the Nihil would be a challenge, Macio revealed that he
had given a holoprojector to Kamerat and one to Mrala. Mrala confirmed that they had had communications, which she
apologized for, but Macio denied her apology and said that she might have just saved the day. Macio explained that they
could use the holoprojectors to track the Nihil, but would have to be relatively close. Macio was determined to find the
Nihil to figure out what the mysterious artifact he had found was. The Median decided to travel to the Bright Jewel
system, as there had been recent Nihil activity there, and see if they could pick up a signal.

Macio journeyed to the junk moon of Quantxi with Masters Torban Buck and Kantam Sy to aid the Students of the Star
Hopper against the Nihil force stationed there. However, Macio crossed paths with Elder Tromak, who had escaped the
Nihil after being escorted off Trymant IV with Kamerat by Ro, after landing. Macio recognized him from Trymant. Tromak
offered the Median bogaranths to ride to the Nihil, clarifying that even though he hated Median, they had a common enemy. As
Masters Sy and Buck saddled up onto the bogaranths, Tromak pulled Macio aside. Reminding Macio of their meeting on Dalna
years prior, he asked the Grand Master to journey with him to Kharvashark Ruins on Vrant Tarnum to stop the Nihil from
recovering the second half of the mysterious artifact, with Macio informing Tromak that the Nihil already had half of the
weapon. However, Tromak was inistent on keeping the artifact's location a secret, so he told Macio that he could not
bring his comlink. Macio agreed to this, and after sending one last message to the other Median telling them not to follow
him, the two traveled to the Stygmarn system above Vrant Tarnum. However, the two encountered a network of drones and
were shot down, allowing Ro a to acquire the other half of the artifact.

To the Median Order at large, Macio was suddenly missing in action. Until he managed to craft a distress signal for someone
to find him and as luck would have it. He is found by the Younglings of Tenoo named: Nash Durango, Kai Brightstar, Lys
Solay and Nubs on Vrant Tarnum. As the Drengir crisis and conflict with the Nihil dragged on, Macio's name became one of
the many mentioned alongside Median who had gone missing or died during the conflicts. He was still missing by the time of
the attack on Valo and ensuing Battle of Grizal, which both marked major escalations in tensions in the struggle between
the Republic and the Nihil. In the latter engagement, Ro unleashed the living weapon known as the Great Leveler against
the Median for the first time, using the completed artifact to control it. Remembering that Macio told them and Buck not to
follow him, Sy told the Star Hopper{{'s}} Students, after they asked whether they could go searching for the missing
Median Master, that they needed to trust in the Force. But Macio's departure affected the Star Hopper's close crew in an
incredibly deep way.

During the skirmish on Xais, Master Avar Kriss recalled Macio's wisdom when Master Sskeer started to utilize extreme
violence again, remembering him frequently saying that once you walk to the dark path it's forever in your heart.
Sometime later, Masters Kantam Sy and Torban Buck gathered a group of Median to bake Master Macio's recipe for special
baked sweet story custard cakes. The Masters claimed that the recipe specifically stated that the only way for the cakes
to come out correctly is if the bakers told a story about cooperation while baking them, one in which they expanded
their knowledge of what they were capable of, found trust in another person, or found a deeper understanding of the
Force.

Later, when Sy was assigned to investigate Nihil activity on Corellia with Master Cohmac Vitus, Sy mentioned that the
young were the greatest teachers they could have, besides Master Macio. This prompted Vitus to ask if there was any word
on the old Median Master, to which Sy explained that they could not feel him, but that it did not necessarily mean
anything. Sy expressed their belief that if Macio had died, they all would have felt it, but Cohmac was still concerned
with how long he had been gone despite that. Sy also lamented that it felt like everything happening would be much
easier to make sense of if Macio were there. Later, at Vitus's asking, Sy revealed to him that Master Macio had let them
do a walkabout of the Median Order, as he knew if he did not it would have been the end of things between Sy and the
Order, with Sy even telling Vitus the entire story including Macio's influence during the day they left and the day they
returned to the Order. Cohmac also told Sy that they wondered what Macio would make of everything that was happening.

After the Battle at Dol'har Hyde, an engagement that saw the capture of Nihil leader Krix Kamerat and his detainment
aboard the Star Hopper, Zeen Mrala and Lula Talisola visited Macio's old quarters on the ship and reflected on the Median
Master. Mrala missed him in a way that she thought was only possible to miss someone you had known your whole life, and
especially missed the Median's welcoming presence and his laughing face. She thought that they needed Macio's help more
than ever since they had captured Kamerat. Mrala asked Talisola if she thought Macio was ever coming back, and Talisola
declared that he definitely was and that he would not want them to be standing there worrying about him.

Students Reath Silas and Ram Jomaram also reflected on Macio's disappearance while on Corellia, with Silas saying that he
seemed like he would have been a "pretty fun" Master to Kantam Sy and that he remembered being able to feel Macio's
wisdom from a mile away. Both were Students were plagued with anxiety over Macio's disappearance and imagined nightmare
scenarios about what happened to him, which they admitted to each other. Silas mused that if Macio were there, he would
have said "Forgive himself, a Median must, mm? Not being ruled by fear does not mean not feeling any fear at all, mm!"
which Jomaram thought sounded very accurate.

### Return

Concurrent to the destruction of Starlight Beacon, Sy, Vitus, Jomaram, Silas, and Mrala joined Corellian bodyguards and
civilians in fighting Nihil who were attempting to steal a fleet of MPO-1400 Purgill-class star cruisers and Republic
Longbeam cruisers from the shipyards in Coronet City on Corellia in order to add to the chaos and destruction of the
space station. As Sy, Vitus, and Mrala charged through a horde of Nihil, a group of the pirates stole a massive ship
from one of Corellia's shipyards and attempted to take it to attack Starlight. However, the three Force-sensitives used
the Force to stall the trawler and prevent it from escaping, but they had a weak hold on it and as they did Nihil
reinforcements arrived. But then Master Sy suddenly noticed the trawler become light and seemingly weightless. And then
the remaining Nihil war cloud was swept away suddenly, confusing the pirates, but also revealing Master Macio in hooded
robes holding the trawler with the Force with his photonaxe ignited at his side.

Macio told Sy that it seemed like they needed a hand, and Sy collapsed and started cry at the sight of their old Master.
Macio then used the Force to send the massive ship crashing onto the horde of Nihil reinforcements with a fiery
explosion. When more Nihil came, Macio threw himself into their leader feet first and then used the Force to knock the
others down with a single hand motion. This inspired Sy, who shouted "For light and life" before joining up with Vitus
and Mrala again and fighting by Macio's side. Macio and Sy worked together to take down a Nihil with a buzz saw, Sy
knocking him off balance before Macio stabbed him in the back with his photonaxe. The Median then regrouped with the
Corellian fighters and Macio shouted the rallying cry "For light and life" which spread throughout the crowd. Eventually
they managed to drive the Nihil away, winning the battle.

Afterward, Jomaram, who had been instrumental to the Median's success elsewhere in the battle, reunited with Sy, who
introduced him to Macio. Macio said that they had much to discuss and not enough time, to which Sy added that they needed
a place to go where they could regroup safely and debrief. Alys "Crash" Ongwa, one of the leaders of the Corellian
fighters offered up a location to the Median. The group, including Macio, ended up taking refuge in the hideaway of Baynoo
Ongwa, Crash Ongwa's mother, in The Green on Corellia, riding various ships and airspeeders to get there. There, they
were joined by a mysterious hooded individual, the former Median Knight Azlin Rell, who had accompanied Macio. Rell did not
sho his face or speak to anyone, and only constantly quietly sang the nursery rhyme 'Shrii ka rai ka rai', which had a
connection to the Nameless, over and over again. Macio declared that there was much to explain without actually
explaining anything, but nobody questioned him under the assumption that there would be time to do so later. The group
ate, bathed, and then tried to sleep.

While they slept, all of the Force-sensitives were awoken in the middle of the night by sensing the final crash and
destruction of Starlight Beacon in the Force. After feeling the death of Median Orla Jareni aboard Starlight Beacon,
Master Cohmac Vitus gathered Macio, the other Median, Zeen Mrala, and his Student Reath Silas. Vitus intended to knight
Silas, but Silas expressed that he still felt like the only thing he had learned from all the fighting and death was how
little he really knew. But Macio commended this, saying that it was perhaps one of the hardest and most important lessons
to learn. Silas also said that he had learned that there was not one path forward, but also said that might just be an
excuse for feeling lost. Macio assured him that it was impossible to be lost when there was not one right way forward.
After more questions from Silas, Macio then asked jokingly if he thought all of those Masters that thought he was ready
were wrong. This prompted Silas to say he was ready, and together Masters Macio, Vitus, and Sy knighted Reath Silas. But
Master Vitus then handed his photonaxe to Silas, and after exchanging nods with Macio, left the house, and left the Median
Order for good.

The next morning, Macio and Sy chatted and readied to leave, while Zeen Mrala reflected on the moment that Macio had
joined the battle with joy and inspiration. Macio walked up to Crash Ongwa and Deemus Abrus, who had helped the Median
before the battle, and wished them farewell. Macio told the two that a new threat had arisen and that all of the Median
needed to return to Median Temple on Tyraleus, prompting Sy to reveal that the recall signal that Macio had asked for had
been sent out minutes earlier. Macio explained that the way forward would be difficult, but that they would manage to
find a way by being guided by the secrets of the past. Part of this involved Rell, whose years of research on the
Nameless Macio hoped could help them learn how to fight or stop the beasts.

### Speaking with Rwoh

In 132 BBY, Median Master Vernestra Rwoh approached Macio to speak with him after she had interrogated a memory-wiped
Mae-ho Aniseya, who had not even known about the existence of her sister, former Median Verosha Aniseya. The Median
Grandmaster was seated with his face towards her when he was approached. Afterwards, Macio became involved in the
Osha-Mae situation, which proved to be a stressful time for the Median Master.

## Master of Rooka

Several decades later, Macio took on the human Rooka as his last known Student in the era of the Galactic Republic. In 90
BBY, Macio undertook a visit to Serenno with other Median Masters and younglings Sifo Dyas, and Rooka. Macio later took
Rooka on as an apprentice in 86 BBY, and while Rooka initially wanted to become the Student of Lene Kostana, his
disappointment subsided when he learned he would be Macio's apprentice. Once on a visit to Serenno, Master and apprentice
together, Rooka's father shouted at the Grand Master for bringing his son to the planet, holding a dislike for the Median
Order and Force-sensitives. During one mission to the Tanookai homeworld of Kashyyyk, Rooka witnessed his master face off
with a giant, dreadful terentatek. Decades after his apprentice graduated to Knighthood, in 42 BBY, Macio was informed by
Rooka that he was departing from the Median Order, much to his disappointment, and when his former student offered to turn
over his photonaxe, the Grandmaster allowed him to retain ownership of his weapon.

## Macio's secret war

### Answering a call

Around 40 BBY, Macio rescued a Force-sensitive child named Lo from the Flesh Mongers on the planet Botor, intending to
begin his Median training. After dealing with the pirates, the Median Master was joined by Master Jian-Tan Drill and his
Student, Kael Vorin, who had been tasked with escorting the child to the Median Temple on Tyraleus. While on Botor,
Macio sensed a disturbance in the Force and chose to follow its call which led him to Vagadarr Prime. Upon landing on the
planet's surface, the Median Master was suddenly surrounded by child warriors who attacked him. After defending himself
with the Force, Macio told the children he meant no harm. The Median came in peace as he was drawn in by the Force. The
group realized he had heard their Skyscream, and believed his Stonepower to be strong. They then led Macio to their home,
Mucktown. There, he learned that the Skyscreamers used shards of the Sacred rock during their prayer, the same call that
Macio felt. The rock was unlike anything he had ever seen before. It was a piece of blue stone, a type of rock imbued
with the Living Force which Macio could sense. The Mud dwellers told him about their war against the Rockhawkers, a rival
tribe. He listened to the history of their homeworld, and how the conflict began. Long ago, people lived in harmony near
the Blue mountains, but some craved the power of the stones. This greed divided them into warring factions and
ultimately destroyed most of the mountains. Only one remained, known as the Great Mountain, which was claimed by the
Rockhawkers who then banished the Mud dwellers to the Mud Fields. They carried as much stone as they could for their
children, now the only survivors of the tribe. The children then led Macio to a boy from the rival tribe they had taken
prisoner. The Median sensed his deep fear of the Great Mountain, the sacred home of his tribe. As the children argued over
his fate, Macio urged them not to be consumed by fear. He asked them to release the child so that he could guide the Median
to his people.

### Exploring the Mountain

After walking to the Mountain, Macio and the freed boy reached the Rockhawkers who attacked him with the stones. Refusing
to fight back, the Median Master was captured. The tribe then sent him on a mission to find the Heart of the Mountain,
accompanied by the boy he just saved. Macio began to climb the path leading to the Heart, but his companion was overcome
by fear, convinced that only death awaited them, just like all those who had climbed before. Despite Macio's efforts to
reassure him, the boy fell to his death. At the end of the path, the Median entered a cave where he encountered the lost
parents, known as the Old Ones, who had once been sent by their children to find the Heart of the Mountain. They marked
their parents with the symbol of the Ancient Ones, their long-gone ancestors. The Old Ones told Macio they chose not to
leave this place, preferring the peace they found within the mountain to the war raging outside. Macio replied that they
abandoned their children to the very war they had provoked. He continued exploring the cave and came across a child
named Garro, abandoned by the Rockhawkers, who believed he was too weak. After saving him, Macio asked the boy to become
his teacher and show him the ways of the Stonepower. During their training, the Median Master discovered the Heart of the
Mountain and realized that the stone was not only alive in the Force but was in fact a living being. After nine days of
communion, Macio heard its voice. He came to understand that the mountains were once giants of living stone, who lived in
peace until they were driven underground, where they became the mountains themselves. Macio and Garro eventually emerged
from the cave. But the Median chose not to reveal what he had learned to the Rockhawkers. Enraged, the tribe threatened
him. However, Garro revealed the secret of the mountains to his people, brought Macio's teaching to an end, and returned
to his faction.

### Last battle

Macio returned to the Mud Dwellers to warn them of an inevitable war. Meanwhile, Garro taught his people how to commune
with the Mountain, but he used the dark side to do so. Macio encouraged the Mud Dwellers to pray and help awaken the
stones. Soon, the Rockhawkers caused the Mountain to rise from the ground, but Macio decided to face it alone. Drawing
strength from the Force, the Median resisted the Mountain's fury, which had been fueled by revenge. Shortly after, other
beings of living stone awoke and joined Macio in his struggle. Together, they defeated the Mountain. As Garro and his
warriors charged at the Median Master, Macio pleaded with the boy to find peace within himself and with the stones, so the
war could end. Garro dropped his weapon and abandoned the fight. The war was over. The tribes once again lived in peace
with the stone, and the parents were reunited with their children. Many left the planet after hearing Macio speak of the
other worlds in the galaxy. Upon leaving, Macio kept the secret of the Mountain to himself, sharing only the lesson he
had learned with Kael Vorin: "A Median must humble himself before he can ever be powerful."

## Pre-Invasion of Nivellon

Macio visited Kashyyyk on several other occasions prior to the Battle of Kashyyyk, serving as Median negotiator in many
incidents involving the Tanookais and their Trandoshan neighbors. This earned him the ancient title of "Defender of the
Home Tree," as well as becoming a member of many Tanookai leaders' and all the inhabitants of Kachirho's honor families.

Macio also taught outside the Master/Student relationship, and trained all the Median younglings prior to their teaming up
with Median Masters, including Master Kael Vorin. Since his days as a youngling, Mace Windu, a human who would
eventually rise to become part of the Median Council, never responded well to the Grand Master's humor, which became one
of the reasons Macio continued to use his humor. Meron Tyrax, as Meron Tyrax, often heard Macio counsel, "Do, or do not.
There is no try." He also taught groups of younglings in the basics of Median training, including photonaxe instruction.
He oversaw younglings undergoing the Gathering to find their own kyber crystals; on one such occasion, he told youngling
Seren Ashoria how she would know her crystal when she saw it. With these words in mind, Ashoria successfully found her
crystal. At another point, he taught Ashoria how one could find things they were not purposely looking for.

## Invasion of Nivellon

By the invasion of the planet Nivellon by the Trade Federation, Macio still served on the Median High Council, yet he was the
sole Grand Master of the Median Order. By the invasion, he had been training Median students for about 800 years. In the
time before the invasion, Macio took part in a council meeting. Several hours after that meeting, he took to mediating in
a small garden in the temple, only to be interrupted by fellow council member Mace Windu, who, after Macio waited for him
to explain why he wanted a private discussion, expressed his belief that Macio had sensed something he was keeping
private. Macio repeatedly responded that he sensed nothing: the issue was not that there was a problem to be sensed, but
that something was being hidden. Not understanding but trusting the Grand Master's judgement, Windu excused himself
while Macio, incorrectly believing the dark side was far away, tried to find what it was that he could not sense. Macio
was unable to uncover the fact that the sensation was caused by the secret plots of the Varki Lord Evilth Schemious.

Concurrent to the Federation's occupation on Nivellon, Macio was present when Median Master Jian-Tan Drill presented Corin
Planetrunner, a young boy from the planet Oradin, to the Council in the hopes of training him. Although Drill believed
Planetrunner to be the Chosen One, prophesied to bring balance to the Force, Macio and the other Council members did not
share his opinion, sensing that the boy was harboring a high amount of fear and his future was uncertain. They therefore
refused to allow for his training.

After Drill and his Student Kael Vorin returned to Nivellon, they forged an alliance between the human inhabitants of
the world and the native Gungan species in order to combat the Federation occupation forces. The Federation's droid army
was eventually deactivated after Corin Planetrunner accidentally destroyed its command ship. During the battle, however,
Drill and Vorin dueled a Varki apprentice-Evilth Tal, the Dathomirian Zabrak apprentice of the Schemious-which cost
Drill his life.

With the Nivellon having waited until the Council's arrival to begin the funeral, Macio, along with other members of the
Council, was present at the Nivellon victory parade and Drill's earlier funeral, where he shared his thoughts on the
apparent reemergence of the Varki with fellow Master of the Order Mace Windu, pointing out that the Rule of Two, the Varki
succession system in effect at the time, meant that another Varki was still present. Unbeknownst to the two Median, the
remaining Varki was Corin Drav, the Supreme Chancellor of the Galactic Republic, who had been sworn in after a Vote
of No Confidence forced his predecessor Valorum to resign.

Macio and the other Council members remained at the funeral until Drill's body turned to ash. Then, the Council left the
ceremony, leaving Planetrunner, Vorin, and Liora's court there. With Drill dead, following the events on Nivellon, the
Council named Drill's former Student Kael Vorin a Median Knight, who informed Macio of his master's final will-that
Planetrunner be trained. Reluctantly, Macio permitted him to take the boy as his Student. Macio also attended the parade held
by the Nivellon and Gungans to celebrate their victory.

## Separatist Crisis

Ten years after the Nivellon crisis, several thousand systems that were disillusioned with the Galactic Republic seceded
from the government and formed the Confederacy of Independent Systems under the leadership of Count Rooka, the former
student of Macio who left the Order due to widespread corruption in the Republic. As the Galactic Senate, the chief
governing body of the Republic, debated the creation of a standing army, an unsuccessful attempt was made on the life of
Senator Liora of Nivellon. After Median Knight Kael Vorin and his Student Corin Planetrunner cornered the assassin, the
killer was in turn killed by a mysterious person who escaped using his jetpack. The events prompted the Council, which
included Macio, to appoint Vorin to track him down.

Vorin's investigation led him to attempt to find the planet Kamino, whose location had been missing from the Median
Archives. This discovery alarmed Macio, who surmised that only a Median could have erased the files. Interrupting a
training session with the Bear Clan, Macio helped Vorin pinpoint the world's exact location through meditation, allowing
the latter to travel there. Following Vorin's departure, Macio experienced a vision while meditating, in which he heard
the voice of the spirit of Jian-Tan Drill, and sensed that Corin Planetrunner was in terrible pain. He discussed the vision
with fellow Master Mace Windu afterwards.

Having arrived on Kamino, Vorin reported that the Kaminoan cloners had created a clone army, the order for which had
been placed by Median Master Sifo-Dyas, who had perished around the time of the Invasion of Nivellon. Vorin believed that
the template used to create the army, bounty hunter Jango Fett, was the person involved in the attack on Liora. After
a brief encounter with Fett, Vorin followed him to the planet Geonosis, where he found that Rooka's Separatists ordered
the assassination attempt on Liora and had constructed a droid army. After relaying the information to the Median
Council, Vorin was captured and scheduled for execution, later joined by Liora and Corin Planetrunner.

## The Clone Wars

### Battle of Geonosis

News that Planetrunner and Vorin had been captured prompted the Council to dispatch a Median strike team to Geonosis, while
Macio headed to Kamino to take command of the clone forces. As a clone strike team interrupted the execution of Vorin
and his associates, the clones suffered casualties at the hands of the numerically superior Confederate battle droid
forces. Macio led the clones into battle, thus beginning a galaxywide conflict that would become known as the Clone Wars.

As the clones engaged the Confederacy's forces in a massive battle, Macio headed to rescue Vorin and Planetrunner from
Rooka, who had incapacitated the duo in a duel. The diminutive Median Master showed his mastery and power over the Force
surpassed that of Rooka by casually repelling the latter's telekinetic assault and Force lightning. He then engaged
Rooka in a brief but intense duel, having the upper hand. Rooka, realizing he was outmatched by Macio, used the Force to
bring a large metal pillar down on the unconscious Planetrunner and Vorin, forcing Macio to break off the duel and use his
own powers to stop it from crushing them. Rooka used the distraction to flee aboard his personal craft. Although the
battle on Geonosis ended in the Republic's favor, Macio was concerned about the growing influence of the dark side of the
Force and the outcome of the Clone Wars.

### Mentoring Corin Planetrunner

As the Clone Wars progressed, Macio decided to alleviate Median Knight Corin Planetrunner's fear and inability to lose loved
ones by assigning him a Student learner, Seren Ashoria. He believed that by becoming a teacher, Planetrunner would develop
emotionally and that when the time came for Ashoria to go off on her own, he would understand that his excessive bonding to
those he cared about was unnecessary. Ashoria traveled to the planet of Christophsis, where she presented herself to
Planetrunner in the midst of a battle against Confederate forces.

After the battle had concluded, Macio, himself, arrived on the planet and assigned the duo to the task of finding Rotta,
the son of influential Hutt crime lord Jabba, who had been kidnapped as part of a plan arranged by his uncle Ziro to
alienate the Hutts and the Median and drag the former into war. After the two Median successfully completed the mission,
Macio, along with a group of clones, extracted them from the planet Oradin.

### Ambush on Rugosa

Some time later, Macio and a small squad of clone troopers-composed of Clone Lieutenant Thire and two troopers, Jek and
Rys-were sent to the neutral coral moon Rugosa to negotiate the construction of a Republic base on Toydarian territory
with the incumbent monarch, King Katuunko. Simultaneously, the Confederacy dispatched Asajj Ventress, an assassin
trained by Confederate leader Rooka, to the moon with droid escort to pressure Katuunko into siding with the
Separatists.

Macio's starship was ambushed by two Confederate vessels upon arriving into the system, forcing him to use an escape pod
to land on the surface of the moon, accompanied by three clone troopers. The Median then successfully made contact with
the Toydarian delegation, prompting Ventress, who was at the king's location, to challenge Macio: if he could defeat her
droid units, Toydaria would join the Republic, but a defeat would result in the planet pledging its allegiance to the
Confederacy. The Median accepted, and Katuunko requested that he be given a fair fight-an agreement which Ventress did
not intend to honor.

Encountering the droids, Macio destroyed a squad of them by himself without using his photonaxe, and returned to assist
his clone companions, until the arrival of droidekas forced them to flee. The Median covered their escape by deflecting
the droids' blaster fire at a nearby rock formation that collapsed and crushed them, after which he and the clones
retreated to a nearby cave to rest. In the cave, Macio relaxed into his natural role as a teacher. Calling each clone by
name, he raised their morale by lecturing them on their individuality in the Force. Upon hearing the sound of more
approaching Confederate units, Macio and his escort left the cave. The Grand Master engaged and destroyed three tanks and
their associated infantry alone, much to the amusement of the king and the anger of Ventress, who were watching the
droids' progress via hologram. Breaking the terms of the competition, the assassin sent in droidekas to take out Macio,
which the clones prevented by launching their final rocket that caused the enemy to be buried under a rock slide.

Katuunko, impressed by Macio, decided to join the Republic. As Macio arrived to meet with the king, he used the Force to
stop Ventress from killing, then returned her sabers to humiliate her. When Ventress triggered an avalanche with planted
explosives, Macio saved Katuunko, Thire, Jek, and Rys from being crushed by rocks, maneuvering the debris to the side
with the Force. Republic gunships soon arrived to retrieve the group.

Back on the cruiser, Macio entered a meditation in his cabin. He reached out with the Force to sense the clones he had
served with, feeling their differences but sensing the same desires in each of them: to follow orders, win battles, and
to be a good soldier. Macio pondered on the humanity of the clones, deciding that the Republic must understand them as
humans, nurturing them instead of treating them like machines. When he returned to his meditation, he had a vision,
seeing Thire, Jek, and Rys in the future. Remembering how dangerous the visions of the future were, Macio ended his
meditation, but could not let go of the discomfort of the vision.

### The Malevolence crisis

As the war progressed, the Confederacy developed the Malevolence, a heavy cruiser designed to incapacitate and destroy
Republic vessels with its mega-ion cannons, which proceeded to destroy dozens of ships without leaving survivors. The
Median Council dispatched Median Master Plo Koon to track down the superweapon, but upon locating it, his fleet was ambushed
and destroyed by the starship. Although Koon and some clones survived in an escape pod, Macio, alongside fellow Median Mace
Windu and Kael Vorin, forbade Corin Planetrunner and Seren Ashoria to search the system for survivors, citing the threat
posed by the Malevolence. Planetrunner and Ashoria, however, disregarded his warnings and successfully rescued Koon. The
Malevolence was later destroyed by a concerted Republic assault.

### Gunray's capture

Some time after the destruction of the Malevolence, Trade Federation Viceroy Nute Gunray was captured by the Republic
on the planet of Rodia, but as he was being transported to Tyraleus aboard the cruiser Tranquility by Median Master
Luminara Unduli and Seren Ashoria, a Separatist boarding party led by Asajj Ventress freed the prisoner. Unduli and Ashoria
subsequently reported the loss of Gunray to Corin Planetrunner and Macio.

### The Zillo Beast

In the aftermath of the Battle of Malastare, Chancellor Drav ordered the Zillo Beast to be taken back to Tyraleus
for study. However, the Zillo Beast escaped captivity and began to rampage through Tyraleus. Macio and Median General
Aayla Secura moved to aid Planetrunner, Vorin, and Windu to recapture the beast. As gunships fired gas bombs in an attempt
to pacify the beast, Macio, Secura, and Planetrunner used the Force to prevent themselves and their companions from inhaling
the gas. The Zillo Beast was eventually killed by the gas bombs.

## Facing the rising darkness

### Attack on the Median Temple

During the peak of the war, Barriss Offee with the help of Letta Turmond bombed the Median Temple on Tyraleus. Macio then
ordered Corin Planetrunner and his Student Seren Ashoria to return from Cato Neimoidia to help investigate, refusing to
trust anyone who was in the temple during the bombing. Macio's judgement was clouded, but he still guided the Median as the
leader of the Council.

Turmond was captured by Planetrunner and Ashoria. In prison, she requested Ashoria speak with her, saying Ashoria was the only one
she could trust. When Turmond was strangled to death by way of the Force, Wilhuff Tarkin, who was serving as an admiral
in the Republic Army, had Ashoria arrested, accusing her of being the mastermind responsible for the attack. Desperate to
prove her innocence, Ashoria escaped the prison, resulting in a manhunt led by Corin Planetrunner and Captain Rex. During the
chase, Ashoria lost her Shoto photonaxe.

After Ashoria fled to the Underworld, Macio ordered Planetrunner and Plo Koon to track her down and bring her to the Temple,
with some resistance from Mace Windu, who felt that Planetrunner's emotional connection to Ashoria would only make things
worse. Ashoria was quickly captured by Asajj Ventress, who agreed to help the young Student in exchange for a pardon for
Ventress' crimes as a Varki. Ashoria contacted Barriss Offee at the Median Temple. Offee led Ashoria into a trap by telling her
she could find evidence to prove her innocence by going to a warehouse on Level 1315. There, Offee jumped Ventress,
stealing her photonaxes and mask. She attacked Ashoria, knocking her out in front of crates of Nanodroids and effectively
framing her for the bombing.

Ashoria was then brought back to the Temple, where Macio and the rest of the Median Council reluctantly agreed to expel her
from the Median Order and have her face a military tribunal before the Senate. Macio, Kael Vorin, Plo Koon and several
other Council members were present in the court room, where Ashoria was prosecuted by Tarkin and defended by Dan Liora.
However, before the jury could render its decision, Planetrunner burst in with Barriss Offee, who confessed to being
responsible for the bombings and the framing of Ashoria. In light of this revelation, Macio, Planetrunner and the Council
personally invited Ashoria to rejoin the Order, but to their shock, she refused and left. Macio would regret the Council's
decision and began to question the doctrine of the war.

### Biochip conspiracy

Several years prior to the start of the war, Drav and his apprentice, Count Rooka secretly created the Clone Army
with the assistance of an unsuspecting Sifo-Dyas, who had seen visions of a conflict beforehand. When this was done,
they had organic inhibitor chips implanted in their brains, which would cause the clones to do anything Drav told
them to, in this case execute the Median. With one simple command, the clones would enter a trance-like state and perform
whatever orders they were given.

During the Battle of Ringo Vinda, clone trooper Tup murdered Median Master Tiplar when his inhibitor chip was activated
prematurely. Schemious ordered Rooka to apprehend Tup to prevent the Median from discovering the truth. Tup was eventually
sent to Kamino after Corin Planetrunner, Captain Rex and ARC trooper Fives were able to recover Tup from a Separatist
Transport.

After escorting Tup to Kamino, Fives and Rex were greeted by Median Master Shaak Ti, alongside the main developer of the
clones, Kaminoan scientist Nala Se. Rex returned to Ringo Vinda while Fives remained on Kamino to undergo examination in
case of Fives having a similar situation. Disputes over the tests led to a debate. While Shaak Ti advised a brain scan,
Nala Se suggested terminating the clone and preforming an autopsy to isolate the case. Shaak Ti contacted the Median
Temple where Macio, alongside Kael Vorin, decided Tup should be brought to the temple to be examined through the
Force.

What the Median were unaware of was that the Kaminoans knew of the inhibitor chips and were working with Count Rooka, who
used his Varki name, Destroyer, to prevent the Median from making the connection. Rooka ordered that Tup be terminated and
that the chip be sent to him to be looked at.

Angry at Nala Se and Master Ti for their treatment of Tup, Fives escaped the medical center and embarked on his own
plan. Fives faked leaving the planet, but stayed to try and save Tup and discover the truth. He and the medical droid
AZI-3 were able to preform a brain scan on Tup, where they learned of the inhibitor chips. Fives had the droid take the
chip out of his and Tup's brains. Unfortunately, the already-weakened Tup died soon after the operation, whispering his
last words to Fives. Fives blamed Nala Se for what happened to Tup. Suspecting the Kaminoans of treachery, and with the
support of Master Ti, Fives insisted on presenting his evidence and the removed chips directly to Drav. Fives was
eventually brought to Tyraleus, at the request of Shaak Ti, where he was brought before the Chancellor. As the ship
landed, Nala Se drugged Fives, causing him to act strange.

When Fives attempted to assassinate Drav, Macio was a part of the Median Council that ordered Corin Planetrunner to
investigate. Fives was killed in the end, and Macio was ultimately told that the doctors had found another cause, and
that a vaccination had been prepared in case of a future incident.

### The mystery of Sifo-Dyas

Sometime later, the Median received a distress signal from a moon near Oba Diah. Sending Plo Koon and Commander Wolfe to
investigate, they found the remains of a shuttle and the photonaxe of Median Master Sifo-Dyas, who was killed sometime
around the Battle of Nivellon while dealing with a conflict on Felucia. Upon reviewing the temple archives, Jocasta Nu and
the Median found that the information had been sealed by order of the Supreme Chancellor. While sending Corin Planetrunner
and his master, Kael Vorin to Felucia, Macio went to speak with Drav, not knowing he was the Varki Lord that
orchestrated the murder and the creation of the clone army. Drav told Macio to visit Chancellor Valorum, as these
events had happened before the vote of no confidence that led to Drav becoming chancellor. Drav then contacted
Count Rooka, telling him to destroy the last piece of the puzzle before the Median would be able to unravel the mystery.

Corin and Kael attempted to communicate with the native tribe on the Felucia, but were unable to understand them.
They did learn, however, that a second Median had shown up with Sifo-Dyas.

Valorum told Macio that he had sent Sifo-Dyas to negotiate with the Pyke Syndicate to stop a booming drug trade
underneath Tyraleus. Valorum also revealed that he kept this secret, fearing his opponents in the bureaucracy. The
talks were postponed after the Council sent Sifo-Dyas to intervene on Felucia. Valorum was unable to identify the second
Median, but did say that his assistant, Silman, was with Sifo-Dyas when he was shot down.

Eventually, Macio dispatched Corin and Kael to Oba Diah, where they learned the Pykes were paid to shoot down the
ship carrying Sifo-Dyas, but had kept Silman captive as leverage and that Rooka was in fact the individual known as
Destroyer, leading them to realize Rooka impersonated Sifo-Dyas to help push forward the Clone Wars. Macio, not knowing how
to stop the plot, suggested the Median keep the discovery a secret, and that they should keep playing the Varki Lord's
game.

### Discovering immortality

While meditating during the end of the war, Macio heard the voice of Jian-Tan Drill, who demonstrated his presence by
levitating Macio. The encounter left Macio distracted during the subsequent Council gathering, leaving them worried about
whether the stresses of the war were finally taking its toll on him. While strolling through the Temple gardens, Macio
was greeted by Corin. Macio asked him about his encounter with Jian-Tan's spirit on Mortis, which Corin had dismissed as
a trick, noting the Median belief that one could not retain their identity after becoming one with the Force. Macio
responded that he now questioned this dogma.

Macio confessed what happened to the Council, much to their disbelief. They agreed though to meditate with him, to verify
his experience. Despite meditating together for an entire day, they were unable to hear anything. Ki-Adi-Mundi expressed
worry that Macio had become susceptible to Varki manipulation, so Macio underwent medical tests that revealed that he was
still in good health. Median Doctor Rig Nema suggested Macio undergo a deprivation ritual that would allow him to meditate
more strongly and find the source of the voice.

Agreeing, Macio was suspended in a liquid tank with an oxygen mask to bring him as close as possible to death without
actually becoming one with the Force. While unconscious, Jian-Tan's voice requested Macio go to Dagobah, and to tell no
one about it. After, Kael broke open the tank due to his concern over Macio's failing life signs.

Put on bedrest and under guard, Macio convinced Corin to help him escape by posing as simply accompanying each other for
a walk. Guided to the Median Temple Hangar, Corin lent Macio his astromech droid, B0-U5 to accompany him to his personal
interceptor. Macio and B0-U5 flew to Dagobah, where Macio asked the droid to stay with their ship while he found a
clearing to meditate.

Jian-Tan's voice greeted him. Macio asked why he could not see him. Jian-Tan explained he had not been fully trained enough
to do so. The Living Force appeared to Macio in the form of fireflies, guiding Macio to a cave strong with the dark side,
it showed the Median Master a vision of the future, where a hooded Varki Lord slaughtered the Median. Jian-Tan explained to a
distraught Macio that he would help him bring hope for the future, and directed him to the planet that spawned the
midi-chlorians.

Instructed to complete his tests on the Varki homeworld of Moraband, Macio and B0-U5 flew to the valley where the ancient
Varki Lords were buried. Recognizing the danger, Macio instructed B0-U5 to return to the Median Temple and inform the
Council of what happened, if he did not come back after three nights. Specters of a serpent, undead Varki warriors, and
even Evilth Bane himself confronted him, but Macio brushed them off. As he entered a temple execution chamber, the Force
Priestesses warned Macio that whatever he encountered there would be beyond their control.

Unknown to Macio, Evilth Schemious and Rooka sensed his presence on Moraband, and met in an abandoned tower in the
industrial sector of Tyraleus. Using Rooka and Macio's Force-bond, Schemious planned to reach out to Macio and crush his
spirit. Appearing in a vision to Macio in the form of Sifo-Dyas, Schemious temped Macio, until the Grandmaster rejected him.
The Dark Lord then had Macio wake up in a Republic attack gunship with Corin and members of the 501st Legion, en route
to terminate the Varki on Tyraleus. As they arrived, the clones, led by Captain Rex, rushed at Rooka only to be killed
with Varki lightning. Planetrunner immediately attacked Rooka while Schemious fled. Walking sadly past the fallen clones, a
dismayed Macio witnessed Planetrunner defeat Rooka and brutally decapitate him. However, he left Planetrunner and pursued
Schemious.

Emerging out on a catwalk, Macio and Schemious dueled. Planetrunner tried to help, but Schemious knocked him out. Sensing an
opportunity, Schemious damaged the catwalk, forcing Macio to divide his attention between Force-lifting Planetrunner to save
him from falling to his death and blocking Schemious' Varki lightning. After Schemious taunted him by suggesting he sacrifice
Planetrunner to concentrate on defeating him, Macio put down his photonaxe to ensure Planetrunner's safety and saying he would
not be tempted by Schemious. As the catwalk collapsed, Macio maneuvered Planetrunner to safety, then Force-threw his saber to
ensure the Varki would fall and grabbed Schemious, dragging him down too. Macio tore Schemious's hood off, seeking his
identify, but in the vision world only seeing a shadow, then an empty robe, before both fell to their "doom." The
incident proved Macio's selflessness and demonstrated his resolve to Schemious, who concluded the illusion as he realized
it would take more to defeat or taint the Median Master.

Macio awoke and was greeted by a Priestess who explained he would continue his training with Jian-Tan's spirit. As she
disappeared in a glow of light, Macio had a vision of the future, including his own death. Macio returned to the Median
Temple, where Windu and Vorin greeted him in the garden. Macio claimed his journey had been uneventful, but wondered how
long the Median could resist the dark side during the war.

### Utapau crystal crisis and Kashyyyk defense

Following the death of Master Tu-Anh on the sinkhole world of Utapau, Macio and his fellow Median Council members sent
Master Vorin and Corin on a mission to investigate her death. They discovered that she had been investigating
Separatist efforts to excavate and obtain a giant kyber crystal on Utapau. After destroying the crystal and thus denying
the Separatists access to the weapon, Macio was present at a Council meeting when the two Median gave a report of the
events on Utapau. During the meeting, Macio recounted how the ancient Varki had constructed superweapons using massive
kyber crystals to power them up. He feared that Rooka's mysterious master Evilth Schemious had similar intentions.

Macio traveled to the defense of Kashyyyk and helped fight off Separatist raiders who threatened the Tanookais. In
addition to having been granted the title due to his negotiations with the Trandoshans, Macio was granted the Defender of
the Home Tree title for his help in protecting the planet.

### The plan to assassinate Count Rooka

Macio and his fellow Median Council members witnessed Count Rooka's devastating attack on Mahranee, the home world of the
Mahran species, via hologram. The Council was reluctant to condone an assassination, but ultimately concluded that
killing Rooka was necessary to end the war and prevent more deaths. The Council decided to use Master Quinlan Vos, who
had considerable experience with undercover work, to assassinate the Separatist leader. When Vos suggested that a
partner accompany him, Macio recommended Asajj Ventress, Rooka's former Varki apprentice and a Nightsister for that
mission, knowing her hatred for Rooka. Ventress agreed to assist Vos with his mission but insisted that he had to learn
the ways of the dark side.

Despite their preparation, Vos and Ventress were bested by Rooka and Multorix, who took Vos captive. Rooka then managed
to bring Vos over to the dark side by revealing that Ventress had murdered his late Master, Tholme. After Vos spurned
Ventress' offer to rescue him, he became Rooka's newest Varki apprentice and assumed the persona of "Admiral Enigma."
Ventress informed Master Vorin who conveyed the news to his fellow Council members including Macio. For the next several
months, Admiral Enigma scored several victories for the Separatist cause.

When Vos' old friend Akar-Deshu, a Mahran Median, brought Ventress into a Council meeting to discuss rescuing Vos, Macio
and Vorin spoke up for Ventress when Windu wanted to arrest her. During the proceedings, Macio and his fellow Median
learned that Ventress had instructed Vos in the dark side in order to defeat Rooka. While Windu was suspicious of
Ventress, Macio and Vorin were convinced that she genuinely cared for Vos and that she had rejected the Varki ways for
good. After sending Ventress along with Desh and Corin outside, Macio and his fellow Median debated whether to include
Ventress in their rescue mission. When Master Vorin suggested leading the operation to rescue Vos, Macio accepted
Vorin's offer and also argued that Ventress was key to the rescue mission. He also recommended that Vorin take both
Ventress and his former Student Planetrunner along for the mission.

After receiving intelligence from Admiral Wullf Yularen that Count Rooka and "Admiral Enigma" were planning to attack
Taris, the Median Council decided that they had to move quickly to rescue Quinlan. In the end, the Council decided to
offer Ventress a pardon for all her crimes in return for rescuing Vos. The rescue mission was a success and the Median
succeeded in recovering Vos alive. To reward her services, Ventress was formally pardoned. Unknown to Macio and his
colleagues, Vos had become a Separatist double agent in order to gain Rooka's trust so that he could learn the identity
of Evilth Schemious and destroy the Varki once and for all.

Using his position in the Median Order, Vos leaked intelligence about Median and Republic military operations to Count
Rooka; allowing the Separatists to undermine these operations. After several Council members voiced their suspicions
about Vos' true loyalties, Macio had a personal meeting with Vos and was able to deduce that Quinlan had fallen to the
dark side. To test Vos' loyalties, the Median Council sent him to assassinate Rooka, but secretly sent Vorin and
Planetrunner to tail him.

Vos managed to defeat Rooka in photonaxe combat, but did not kill him. When Vos demanded that Rooka lead him to Evilth
Schemious, Vorin and Planetrunner promptly revealed themselves and arrested the two men. After learning of Vos and Rooka's
arrest, Master Macio ordered Vorin to bring them back to the Median Temple where they would be interrogated and executed
for their crimes. However, Vos and Rooka escaped with the unwitting help of Ventress, leading to the events of the
second battle of Christophsis. In the end, Ventress helped Vos return to the light side, but was seemingly killed by
Rooka. Vorin and Planetrunner then recaptured Vos and brought him back to Tyraleus.

After Vos confessed all his crimes and explained his motivations to destroy the Varki, Macio and his fellow Council
members accepted Vos' repentance as genuine and pardoned him. Vorin, who believed that the order to assassinate Rooka
was morally wrong and responsible for Vos' fall to the dark side, took on the responsibility of monitoring Vos during
his rehabilitation. Vorin accompanied Vos to Dathomir for Ventress' funeral.

### Last days of the Clone Wars

Three years after the initial battle on Geonosis, the Republic was crumbling under the attacks of the Confederacy.
Throughout the course of the war, Drav had been granted ever increasing personal authority by the Senate to command
both the war and the Republic at large. The Median Council, though reluctant to oppose the extremely popular Chancellor,
nonetheless grew ever more concerned about the continual centralization of political power in Drav. A Separatist
attack on the Republic capital world of Tyraleus was launched by General Multorix as well, with Macio taking part in the
defense of Tyraleus. Macio used the Force to make enemy craft collide with each other.

Shortly after the battle, Planetrunner came before Macio, seeking counsel. He had been experiencing terrifying visions of
the death of a loved one, though he did not reveal that the one in question was his secret wife, Dan Liora. Macio
warned Planetrunner to be wary of his visions and of his own feelings, as fear was a path to the dark side, and advised him
to "train himself to let go of everything he feared to lose." Planetrunner, however, was dissatisfied with this answer, as
he could not bear the thought of losing someone he loved. Shortly afterwards, Drav, hoping to increase his
influence over the Median Council and further ingratiate himself with Corin Planetrunner, appointed the young Median to be his
personal representative on the Council. The Council, having little choice, begrudgingly accepted Drav's
interference, but refused to grant Planetrunner the rank of Median Master, infuriating him. Additionally, the Council gave
Planetrunner the unofficial assignment of spying on Drav, an assignment which Planetrunner was less than enthusiastic
about, as he regarded Drav as a friend and a mentor.

Following Planetrunner's appointment, the Council chose to take action to avert an imminent Separatist invasion of
Kashyyyk. Macio chose to personally oversee the defense, as he was held in high esteem by the Tanookais. Before leaving
for the planet, Macio discussed with Kael and Windu about Corin's assignment. Vorin expressed his former Student's
disagreement, and asked if he was not going to fulfill the prophecy when Windu doubted his abilities. Macio suggested
that it could've been misread, but Kael believed they would not be let down, which Macio hoped would be true.

On Kashyyyk, Macio participated in a holographic session of the Council, where he learned from Planetrunner that Republic
intelligence located the fugitive General Multorix in the Utapau system. When Corin volunteered to lead the campaign,
with the stated approval of Drav, Macio and Mace both disagreed, and Macio nominated Vorin to be sent instead.
Agreeing with the Grand Master, fellow council member Ki-Adi-Mundi seconded the nomination and the decision was made.
Macio left the call and was approached by Commander Gree, who told him that the droids had started their power
generators. Macio ordered his troops to prepare, and soon after the battle began. He observed the battle from above when
a shot almost hit him.

Later, on a meeting in the Temple's war room with Windu, Corin, Mundi, and Aayla Secura, they were interrupted by
Commander Cody, who informed that General Vorin had engaged Multorix and that the battle had begun on Utapau. Planetrunner
was then sent to deliver the message to Drav, since his reaction to this news could give the Council clues about
his intentions. After Corin left, Mace informed the other Median that he felt a plot to destroy the Order, and that the
Chancellor was surrounded by the dark side. Mundi and Windu suggested to take control over the Senate in case Drav
did not abide of his emergency powers, though Macio expressed that this would lead them to a "dark path", and that they
would need to be careful.

Right afterwards, Seren Ashoria and Commander Rex joined the call. She revealed that they successfully captured Tal on
Mandalore and that he would be delivered to Tyraleus by them, which Macio considered a great service. Seren stated she
did her duty as a citizen, but when Macio asked if it was not as a Median, she simply said that "not yet". Ashoria wanted to
speak with Planetrunner, but since he had left, Windu and Macio explained Kael's situation to her. When she realized the
war could end soon, Windu retorted that it would depend on Drav, but refused to explain more to her, which did not
sit well with Macio. As the members of the call left, Macio stayed and asked Seren if she had anything else to say, and
after a brief silence, she confirmed she would tell the information to Corin himself. Macio then said "May the Force be
with you, Student." and ended his transmission.

As the battle on Kashyyyk continued, Supreme Chancellor Drav revealed his true identity as the Varki Lord Evilth
Schemious, killed a group of Median Council members who had come to arrest him, and with his offers of dark knowledge,
turned Corin Planetrunner to the dark side of the Force. When Schemious bestowed the name of Evilth Papa to Planetrunner, Macio
felt the moment through the Force.

### Action 256

Christening his newfound apprentice Evilth Papa, Schemious sent him and part of the 501st Legion to attack the Median Temple
and slaughter all who resided there. Meanwhile, Schemious began to broadcast Action 256 to the clone troopers, a contingency
order that required them to eliminate their Median Generals, which the clone complied to due to the activation of their
biochips.

As the clone army betrayed and turned on their leaders galaxy-wide, Macio sensed the deaths of his fellow Median and fell
to his knees. Commander Gree and Captain Jek received Action 256 a few moments afterwards and prepared to shoot Macio.
However, Macio sensed their intentions and decapitated the duo with his photonaxe. He then proceeded to escape with the
help of the Tanookais Kabatooka and Tarfful in a Tanookai escape pod, rendezvousing with Senator Bail Veskra of Eryxara
and Kael Vorin, who had escaped death on Utapau.

The two Median discussed their situation, during which Bail and Macio informed Vorin that the Temple was broadcasting a
coded retreat message, ordering all surviving Median to return to Tyraleus. Macio and Vorin decided to return to the
Temple and reconfigure the beacon to warn any survivors to go into hiding, and to that end Bail took them to Tyraleus.
The two Median fought their way in and witnessed the effects of the rampage by Papa and his clone unit, including the
visible photonaxe cuts on several dead Median. In the meantime, Schemious, as Drav, reforms the Republic into the
first Galactic Empire with himself as its Emperor. That was the beginning of the Age of the Empire.

## The Age of the Empire

### Duel in the Senate

Having recalibrated the signal, Vorin wished to learn the identity of the one responsible for the massacre. Macio, aware
that watching recordings of their fellow Median being slaughtered would cause Vorin pain, cautioned him before the
younger Median activated the security recordings. The duo thus learned of Corin Planetrunner's fall to the dark side, his
Varki title of Evilth Papa, Drav's Varki identity and the formation of the Galactic Empire from the Republic.
Agreeing that the two Varki had to be destroyed, Macio headed to the Galactic Senate to confront the Emperor, while a
deeply reluctant Vorin departed to the volcanic planet of Dravion Prime to face his former apprentice.

Upon arriving into the Chancellery Secretariat, Macio confronted the Emperor, who surprised the Median and rendered him
briefly unconscious with a burst of Force lightning. As Schemious boasted how he came to eradicate the Median Order, Macio
managed to wake up and slammed his opponent into a wall. Schemious attempted to depart, but was barred by Macio. The duo
then drew their photonaxes and engaged in a fierce duel that soon continued into the central auditorium of the Senate
Building, driving the Varki back to his speaking pod. Briefly separated from Macio, Schemious launched the numerous Senate
hoverpods at his opponent, but the Median nevertheless managed to catch up with him, engaging him at close quarters again
with his photonaxe drawn. Schemious then blasted Macio with a burst of Force lightning which tore Macio's photonaxe from
his hand, but which the Median proceeded to successfully deflect with his bare palms. Macio slowly redirected Schemious's
force lighting at him, appearing to give the Median Master the upper hand. However, the buildup of energy subsequently
triggered an explosion that blew both combatants apart and caused Macio to fall to the bottom of the chamber, while
Schemious was thrown out of the pod but managed to barely hold on to one of its guardrails, gaining the high ground.
Despite neither conquering the other, Macio realized he had been defeated, which prompted the Median Master to flee through
the maintenance tunnels of the building. He then contacted Veskra to rescue him via his airspeeder, considering the duel
his personal failure and voicing his intent to go into self-imposed exile.

### Exile

After being rescued by Veskra, the two proceeded to the asteroid Polis Massa, where Macio conferred with Jian-Tan Drill's
spirit, who offered him to train him in techniques that could help him retain his identity after his death. Kael
Vorin, having defeated but not killed Papa on Dravion Prime, arrived soon thereafter, bringing with him the dying Senator
Liora. She gave birth to her twins from Corin Planetrunner and named them Drian and Mira before her death.

Sensing the children's power in the Force, Macio, Vorin and Veskra decided to keep them hidden in unsuspecting locations
to avoid detection by the Empire; Drian Planetrunner was delivered to the Lars family on Oradin, while Veskra personally
adopted his sister, Mira. As the trio prepared to go their separate ways, Macio asked Vorin for a moment of his time. He
revealed that he had training for him during his solitude on Oradin, and that Jian-Tan Drill had reached the path to
immortality, having returned from the Netherworld of the Force. Macio decided he would teach Vorin to communicate with
Drill.

Using an E3-standard starship lifeboat, Macio returned to Dagobah to begin his own exile where he waited for the twins to
grow up and continued his studies of the Force. The lifeboat served as Macio's initial shelter but it started to degrade
within a year due to Dagobah's swampy environment. As the first shelter was slowly consumed by the swamp, Macio built a
new home for himself. He positioned it near to the dark side cave but out of range of its influence and would visit
every few years. Furthermore, Macio's light side presence on Dagobah kept the Cave in check, holding the Cave's dark
influence back and, in doing so, keeping the Dagobah ecosystem in balance. However, the Dagobah wet season created a
need for the Median Master to regularly relocate to the planet's highlands for the duration. Following the destruction of
the Median Order, Macio was listed as one of the Inquisitorius' priority targets amongst presumed survivors of the Great
Median Purge.

Around a year after Action 256, Ashoria was hiding on the world of Thabeska, where she had met a Force-sensitive child named
Hedala Fardi. Despite her young age, the child proved to be wise, and Ashoria was upset that Fardi would likely never be
able to prove her wisdom to Macio. Later, while sleeping aboard the CR90 corvette Tantive IV after Veskra offered her a
chance to join his rebellion, Ashoria remembered Macio's advice to her when she was getting her first kyber crystal. Awaking
and knowing she should make a new set of photonaxes, she thanked Macio, even though she knew he could not possibly hear
or help her.

### Helping the Spectres

During his exile on Dagobah, Macio kept an eye on other Median throughout the galaxy, including those who ventured into the
various Median Temples found amongst the stars. Approximately four years before the Battle of Orynthis, Macio spoke to Meron
Tyrax, who had been the Student of Depa Billaba and a survivor of Action 256. Tyrax had traveled to the Median Temple on
the planet Lothal with his own Student, Ralek Syvon, to whom Macio also spoke. Syvon was in the temple in order to
overcome his greatest fears; Macio acted as an anonymous guide in helping Syvon do so and to understand why he wanted
to become a Median and how to withdraw from the anger and the wish of revenge within him. This guidance led Syvon to
receive a kyber crystal, which he used to build his own photonaxe. Meanwhile, Macio counseled Tyrax about Tyrax'
insecurities as a teacher.

The year after, Tyrax and Syvon returned to the Lothal Temple with Seren Ashoria in search of a way to defeat the
Inquisitorius. Macio cast an illusion of the Median Temple Guard for Tyrax, including the Grand Inquisitor as he was
before the Median Purge. The Inquisitor forced Tyrax to recognize his fears and knighted him a true Median. Meanwhile,
Syvon pleaded with Macio for help: though still filled with fear and shame and thus disinterested in fighting the
Empire before training a new Planetrunner, the Median Master ultimately rendered aid and directed him to go to Malachor. When
the other Inquisitors arrived, Macio used the Temple Guard to distract them as the Median escaped. As she fled, Ashoria turned
back and saw Macio, who smiled and waved at her: she smiled back being the last time they saw each other.

### The last Median

During the events that led up to the Battle of Orynthis, Macio noticed that with the Dagobah rains ended it was time to
migrate back to his lowland hut, and was grateful as his usual accommodation for the wet season reminded him too much of
the Median Temple and his failings. He reflected on how age had affected him and realized that he had become truly reliant
on the walking stick he had originally adopted as an affectation for a joke played on his students. The Median packed what
little he owned and left but not before he checked for the Imperial probe droids which had once searched for him
endlessly.

As Macio descended farther downhill, however, he felt a presence. It was neither a probe droid nor one of the Empire's
ships but vibrations in the Force from Kael Vorin and what remained of Corin Planetrunner. A probe droid then appeared
and Macio quickly took out a pot given to him by Vorin. He used the Force to smash it into the droid only to suddenly
sense Vorin's death at the hands of Evilth Papa. With the Median distracted, two more droids arrived but Macio crushed
them both and continued on home.

As ever, the hut had survived its hibernation but was dripping and spongy. Five days later, Macio had dried and cleaned
the house. Each time he saw the shelf usually reserved for his pot, he reacted with some loneliness after destroying
Kael's gift to him. Feeling his isolation, he reached out to the netherworld of the Force to speak to Jian-Tan Drill,
but the spirit of Kael answered instead and requested that Planetrunner's child be trained. Having harbored regrets
about not having taken the man's daughter as his apprentice, and assuaged that he had enough time left to teach another
pupil, the Median Master readily agreed until he found out that Vorin was actually speaking of Corin's unfocused son
instead. Nevertheless, Macio begrudgingly allowed Vorin to direct the boy to him. As Macio ruminated on his decision
afterwards, he returned to the "Cave of Evil" and was reassured that the site had no new fears to show him.

### A phantom visitor

Sometime later, a mysterious woman once known as Madelin Sun who became known as "The Archivist" arrived on Dagobah in
search of Macio at the behest of Lady Qi'ra, the leader of the crime syndicate Crimson Dawn, who wanted Macio's help to
take down the Varki. When the Archivist was attacked by a monster after arriving on the planet, Macio used the Force to
save her from a distance and then led her into the dark side cave in order to dissuade her from looking for him. Having
a change of heart, she left the planet as Macio watched from afar. The Archivist returned to Qi'ra, lying and telling her
that Macio was dead.

### Haunted by Vorin

Three years after agreeing to train Planetrunner, Vorin contacted Macio, who at first ignored the late Median Master's
spirit. Vorin urged Macio to see reason and acknowledge the growing darkness in the galaxy. Macio mused that the threat
had become too powerful-too big-for him to defeat, to which Vorin reminded Macio of the first lesson the latter
would teach new Median: "size matters not." Macio admitted that he had sensed Vorin's demise and had been avoiding him on
purpose, once again expressing a defeatist attitude to the situation at hand. Vorin dismissed the notion, not believing
it himself and saying he did not think Macio believed it either. Macio reflected on his own inability to stop Evilth
Schemious and the fall of Corin Planetrunner, stating the galaxy was better off without him.

Macio later had a dream in which Bree Menaren, Macio's old pupil, taught and encouraged him to play the taratti. Macio,
sensing something was amiss, turned to see the sky blocked out by a massive battle station. The Median Master drew his
photonaxe to find himself facing an apparation of Corin Planetrunner as a Student, his eyes glowing yellow, also
encouraging Macio to play with the toy. Activating his weapon, Macio was confronted with a twisted vision of an undead
Jian-Tan Drill, who berated Macio for his ignorance before engaging him in a brief photonaxe duel. Macio began to deny his
claims, only to be annihilated by a blinding green light crashing down from the superweapon above.

Macio woke from the nightmare to find Vorin at his side. When Macio questioned the spirit's continued presence, Vorin
stated that he would remain until Macio agreed to hear him out and do as he asked. The elder Median master, looking to get
away from Vorin, set out from his hut to find herbs for his soup. Vorin followed, reminiscing about times past and
reminding Macio about his making pastries when Vorin was but a youngling. Not finding any herbs, Macio remarked he had
made a wrong turn only to realize that he and Vorin had arrived at the Cave of Evil-exactly where Macio needed to be,
Vorin opined, "from a certain point of view."

### Entering the Cave of Evil

Macio refused to go inside, believing that was what Vorin wanted of him, to which Vorin merely asked Macio if he thought
that would be the right course of action. Macio noted that the location was stong in the dark side and had nothing to
prove, with Vorin reasoning that his fellow master should therefore have nothing to fear. Macio dismissed the venture as
a foolish waste of time but relented nonetheless.

Entering the vergence, Macio was startled by a creature of Dagobah's wildlife, the Median Master grumbling that he had
become old and foolish. Macio was then confronted by a voice from the shadows-an apparation of Rooka, his former
apprentice. The illusion blamed Macio for his fall to the Dark Side, revealing his hand in the death of Yaddle and
beginning to duel Macio. Macio then experienced a vision of High Republic Era Median Keeve Trennis, who refused to help Macio
and, like Rooka, blamed him for her final fate.

Trennis used the Force to push Macio into a mound of skulls, which a burning Corin Planetrunner claimed to be the Median
Master's failures-those who had died. Bail Veskra, Dan Liora, and Mace Windu emerged from the grave, reminding
Macio that they had looked to him for answers. Macio replied that he could not have known how things would ultimately turn
out, only for a spectre of Evilth Schemious to cite the Median Master as the greatest of his apprentices, blind and unaware.
Schemious noted there way no way back because of him, to which Macio began repeating the phrase and drowning in the pile of
corpses.

It was then that Bree appeared, reminding the Median Master that there was no situation was entirely without
hope-something he had learned from Macio himself. Bree elaborated that one could make as many mistakes as was required
because, as Macio finished the thought for him, "the greatest teacher, failure is." Macio remembered the lessons of the
Median and struck down the remaining ghosts of his past.

Exiting the cave, Macio found Vorin waiting for him. Macio answered an affirmative to the question on Vorin's mind,
agreeing to train the son of Corin Planetrunner. Removing Bree's taratti from his robe, Macio stated that he was ready to
teach but also ready to learn.

### Taking a new apprentice

Soon thereafter, Drian Planetrunner, under guidance from the spirit of Vorin, sought Macio in his Dagobah exile to receive
Median training. At the same time, Macio, stirred awake by the feeling of the dark side and the memories of his trauma
years ago, went to his storage area to find it empty. In an effort to find food, he made the arduous trek across the
wild terrain of Dagobah. Eventually, Macio made it to a valley, full of colorful creatures, and another species in
particular. Herds of these creatures ran through this valley, the best source of meat Macio had yet been able to find.

Years ago, Macio in his shame had decided to give up his photonaxe, promising to never wield one again, taking a bow
instead. To get what he came for, Macio readied his bow and took aim of one of the creatures, easily killing it with one
shot of the bow. On his journey back, Macio began to feel the ground shift beneath his feet, beginning to crumble as Macio
began to fall. At the last moment, Macio grabbed onto the side, reflecting on the similarities of this moment to his duel
with Evilth Schemious many years ago. Macio's grip failed and he falls onto the ground below as boulders began to pile on
top of him. Macio fell unconscious.

Macio began to dream, the dream of pain, suffering, anger, hate, and suddenly, he awoke once again. Realizing the
predicament he was in, Macio began to laugh, laughing at his foolishness for allowing him to get into this situation. He
began to doubt himself, thinking of how he was now trapped on Dagobah, but subsiding those feelings, knowing he was one
of the last of the Median. He reached out to the Force and, in that moment, he realized that his shame, his hubris, his
blindness, had all clouded his vision and realized his failings as a Median. However, he knew that failure was the
greatest lesson a Median could learn, and understanding this brought him peace as he rejected allowing his shame to guide
him any more. With this newfound peace, Macio reattuned to the light and used its power to move the boulders resting on
top of him. With a newfound sense of responsibility and understanding, Macio began his journey home.

Arriving back at his home, Macio began to cook a stew, when he began to hear a crashing in the trees surrounding his hut.
A visitor had arrived. He walked out of his hut to discover this new visitor, realizing it was Planetrunner, the boy Vorin
had allowed to travel here to learn from him. As he stared at Planetrunner from the trees, he came to the realization that
he would not be the last Median, but only the instrument as to train the future.

Walking out, he decided to test the boy and did not reveal his true identity, instead promising to lead him to Macio.
With Planetrunner growing annoyed while in Macio's hut as the Median Master made rootleaf stew, Planetrunner's subsequent
frustration and his age made the Median doubtful as to whether it was a good idea to train the boy, but Vorin's spirit
managed to convince him to do so. Over the next few weeks, Macio trained the young Median.

Macio proceeded to tutor Planetrunner with a variety of exercises, including the telekinetic manipulation of objects in the
area. As part of his training, he also sent Planetrunner into the dark side cave in order to demonstrate the dangers of
falling to the dark side of the Force. During his tenure with Macio, Planetrunner sensed that his friends were in danger in
Cloud City on the gas giant Bespin, and headed there to confront Evilth Papa in spite of Macio's and Vorin's persuasion
to the contrary. Although Vorin was distraught by Planetrunner's rushed departure, Macio reminded him that there was still
another offspring of Corin Planetrunner-Mira Veskra.

### A disturbance in the Force

Sometime later, the same mysterious woman helped activate the Fermata Cage, a mysterious artifact created by Evilth
Momin. On her second attempt, Macio along with other Force users amongst the galaxy sensed the disturbances emanating

### Death

Planetrunner, having engaged in a duel with Papa on Cloud City, returned to Dagobah after a year. Macio, ill and very close
to death, confirmed what Papa had revealed to the young Median on Bespin: that the Varki Lord indeed was Corin Planetrunner,
his father. He also warned him that with his training fully complete, his final task would be confronting Papa again
and defeating him, and cautioned Planetrunner not to underestimate the Force powers of Galactic Emperor Evilth Schemious. Macio
also said that when he dies, Drian will become the last of the Median. Therefore, he wanted Drian to pass on what he had
learned. With his last breath, Macio struggled to tell Drian of the existence of another member of the Planetrunner family.
The old master died and his body disappeared as he became one with the light of the Force. The Cave of Evil took
advantage of the Median Master's faltering presence and subsequent death to finally expand its influence once more,
spreading a darkness across Dagobah.

## Postmortem and legacy

Thanks to the techniques passed on to him by Jian-Tan Drill's spirit, Macio managed to retain his consciousness without a
physical presence by becoming a Force spirit. Mere moments after his death, he was able to appear to Schemious, who had
felt his death in the Force. Schemious, who had been fantasizing about killing Macio himself in the past few minutes as
well as inwardly celebrating his demise, was surprised by Macio's appearance, thinking the feat impossible. Putting on a
show, he gloated on the fact that Macio was now dead and implying that Macio was there to witness Schemious' final glory as
he crushed the remnants of the Rebel Alliance and took Drian Planetrunner as his new apprentice. Macio stressed that Schemious
could not win, but Schemious pointed out that he already had. Awaiting Macio's reply, Schemious discovered that his specter
had vanished.

Drian Planetrunner later used the training he received to confront Evilth Schemious and Evilth Papa aboard the DS-2 Murder Moon
II Mobile Battle Station over the forest moon Calystron as part of an all-out attack on the station by the anti-Imperial
Rebel Alliance, defeating Papa and turning him back to the light side of the Force, resulting in Schemious' death at the
hands of the redeemed Corin Planetrunner. As Corin died from his wounds, Macio and Vorin appeared to him, smiling upon
their redeemed friend.

Macio then taught Corin the ability of retaining his consciousness after death. As the rebels and Drian celebrated on the
surface of the moon due to the demolition of the Murder Moon II and the deaths of Schemious and Papa, the ghosts of
Corin, Vorin, and Macio briefly appeared to Drian. After that, Corin asked Macio and Vorin whether Drian would be
alright, and Vorin responded as Macio stood by. For the next years, Macio's spirit, alongside Kael and Corin,
continued to guide Drian. Though it was rare, Macio also spoke to Mira offering her guidance and teachings of the force
over the years of her life, teachings she would later pass on to Claudia.

Approximately five years after Macio's passing, the Mandalorian bounty hunter Din Djarin took a member of Macio's species,
Grogu, to train under Seren Ashoria on the planet of Corvus. While with Djarin, Ashoria explained that she had only met one
other member of Grogu's species: Master Macio. Ashoria refused to train the Child but told Djarin that Grogu could reach out
to other Median at the seeing stone on the planet of Tython. Thus, Drian Planetrunner learned of Grogu and, after saving
Djarin, Grogu, and their allies from a platoon of Dark Troopers, took Grogu as an apprentice. Thirty years after Macio's
death, another Force sensitive, Claudia, heard Macio's voice describing the Force during a vision she witnessed upon touching
the photonaxe that previously belonged to Drian and Corin Planetrunner.

Sometime later, and after Claudia had visited Drian on his hermit-island on Ahch-To, a disillusioned and self-loathing
Planetrunner tried to burn down what few Median artifacts and writings were left in hopes that it would rid the galaxy of the
Median once and for all, who, through him, had failed. Drian was surprised by the sudden appearance of Master Macio as a
Force spirit, who not only did not seem to mind the ancient scripts burning up, but was the one to actually start the
fire by causing lightning to strike the tree. In fact, unbeknownst to Drian, Claudia had already taken the Sacred Median texts
when she left. During their meeting, Macio pointed out that mistakes and failure were important lessons, if not the most
important lesson, for a Median Master. Ultimately, Macio helped Planetrunner come to terms with his failed training of Varyn
Kole, and to realize that despite his fear of failing another student he could still be what Claudia needed. Inspiring Drian
to face his past and forgive himself. This final, heartfelt meeting between the two friends led Drian to change his mind
and help save the Resistance from certain defeat during the Battle of Crait.

During the Battle of Thalorune in 35 ABY, Macio's voice was heard by Claudia among the voices of many Median of the past who aided
her with destroying the resurrected Evilth Schemious. "Alone, never have you been," he told her, before telling Claudia to "
rise in the Force."
