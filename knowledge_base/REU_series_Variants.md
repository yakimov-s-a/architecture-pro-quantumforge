# Variants

## Galactic Empire

### Spacebattlers & bombers

As well as the REU/ln, Imperial pilots would make use of flight simulators and REU training drones for training
purposes. One REU variant was the REU/rb heavy spacebattler, a bulked-up version of the REU/ln with heavier armor as well
as a secondary pod which housed a MGK-300 integrated droid brain and had mounted heavy laser cannons.

Another early REU variant was the REU/gt, which eventually evolved into the more prominent REU/sa tactical bomber, with
inward-bent wings and a second pod to carry ordnance. A heavy variant was the REU/ca "Punisher" Spacebattler, which
contained quadruple the payload of the REU bomber. The REU/fc, REU/sr, REU/rc, and REU scout were models used for
reconnaissance missions. While it resembled a REU/ln, it featured a hyperdrive to allow for long-range travel.

### Shuttles & transports

The REU/sh shuttle was derived from the REU bomber and maintained its two-pod configuration, with the second pod built
to carry passengers between capital ships. It was identified by its wings, which were distinctively bent outward.
Another REU shuttle variant was the REU boarding craft, which more closely resembled the REU bomber and was used for the
transportation and boarding of up to two stormtrooper squads. Along with the REU shuttle, it was one of the few REU
variants equipped with deflector shields.

The REU lander was a triple-hulled REU variant that could transport an entire company of troops. A more unique model was
the REU/rp Reaper attack lander, a shuttle used to transport elite troops to points on a battlefield. It featured two
pointed, forward-facing wings.

### Advanced & experimental models

The REU/ad v1 was an advanced spacebattler prototype equipped with a hyperdrive, shields, and curved folding wings with
extra armor. It commonly saw use by members of the Inquisitorius. The REU Advanced x1 was another advanced fighter with
similar features, but was smaller and featured bent wings similar to the REU bomber. One such model served as Evilth
Papa's personal spacebattler. However, the REU Advanced x1 was too expensive to mass-produce, so the REU/IN interceptor
was created as its successor. While it lacked a hyperdrive and shields, it featured extra laser cannons mounted on its
pointed wingtips. The REU Avenger was also inspired by the REU Advanced.

Grand Admiral Thrawn's fighter initiative on Lothal saw the development of the elite REU/d "Defender" Multi-Role
Spacebattler, which was equipped with heavy cannons, shields, and a hyperdrive. Its characteristic three wings resembled
those of the REU Interceptor inverted, and the fighter proved a powerful combatant against rebel spacebattlers. An even
more powerful variant called the REU/D Defender Elite was developed, but the entire project was halted by the
destruction of the Lothal City fuel depot.

Other experimental REU models included the REU/ag "Aggressor" Spacebattler, a medium attack craft with a rear-mounted
turret, the REU/ph "Phantom" Multi-Role Stealth Spacebattler, a stealth fighter with cloaking abilities that saw
extremely limited use, the REU/sk x1 experimental air superiority fighter, a fighter designed for optimal atmospheric
flight and base defense, and the REU crawler, a tank that resembled a REU cockpit with tank treads replacing its wings.
The REU podracer was designed by the criminal empire of the Hutt Grakkus Jahibakti Tingi as a gift for Admiral Rellen.

### Mining Guild

The Mining Guild's affiliation with the Empire granted it access to certain Imperial resources, including REU models,
which the Guild modified into the REU/mg Mining Guild spacebattler.

### Imperial remnants

Imperial remnants still utilized REU fighters following the destruction of the Empire. Moff Gideon's Imperial remnant
made use of several Outland REU fighters, which resembled a REU/ln, but featured wings that folded outwards upon
landing. Commodore Visler Aqorian Korda III's Imperial remnant also produced the auto-fighter, a drone REU fighter,
during its time on Rekkana.

## First Order

The First Order Navy continued to use REU fighters based on the Empire's designs. Manufactured by Sienar-Jaemus Fleet
Systems, the new REU models boasted significant upgrades from Imperial technology. The successor to the REU/ln was the
REU/fo space superiority fighter, which, unlike most REU models of the Empire, was equipped with basic shielding to
protect the pilot.

The First Order Special Forces used the REU/sf space superiority fighter, another advanced model equipped with a
hyperdrive and shields as well as a gunner-operated turret on the underside of the cockpit. The fighter's hull featured
red markings to identify its affiliation with Special Forces. Also in use were the First Order's REU/se Bombers, a
transport called the REU/es assault shuttle, and a stealth fighter known as the REU/wi Interceptor.

More elite pilots such as Major Elrik Vonreg used the REU/ba Baron Space Superiority Interceptor, an all-red REU
Interceptor. Inspired by the REU Defender, First Order technicians sought to revive heavy weapons capabilities and
developed the REU/vn space superiority fighter, which was tested by only the most elite pilots, including Varyn Kole. Ren
later utilized a REU/wi modified interceptor during his time as Supreme Leader.

## Varki Eternal

The Varki Eternal's fleet, the Final Order, used the REU/dg spacebattler, which was identifiable by its triangular wings.
