# Personality and traits

### Youth

In his youth, Skywalker was often curious, impatient, looking ahead to the future with little regard for his present
surroundings, and even a bit brash. Like his father, he was impulsive, reckless, and often had little regard for his own
personal safety. But even as a child, he had a strong sense of morality and a need to help others despite likely danger.
He believed his aunt Beru was a sweet person in every part of her being. Some time after the Battle of Yavin, he would
confer with Nakari Kelen that he regretted his behavior before then, acknowledging he complained "about everything"
because of his desire to escape from Tatooine. He also acknowledged his scarce talent at lying, a trait that many people
could infer. He once confided in Leia that he felt Tatooine had never felt like a home to him, and did not like to be
seen as just a farm boy though later took pride in where he came from and his past. He was annoyed by both Solo and
Aphra calling him "kid."

He struggled with anger and feelings of wanting revenge, especially toward Queen Trios because she had betrayed the
Alliance, despite knowing they could be dangerous for someone who used the Force. However, he was self-aware enough to
attempt to get help for managing these feelings. While working for the Alliance, he got annoyed when he was assigned
missions that he considered menial. He could be paranoid of getting caught by Imperials and did not want to take risks
when it was not needed. When he held one of the lightsabers of Darth Atrius, Skywalker was overcome by incredible rage,
which one even caused an episode where he dissociated from his body and killed many stormtroopers. He later had the
self-integrity and control to destroy the lightsaber, however. Similarly, when controlled by an Abersyn symbiote,
Skywalker became obsessed with power and dominating others, but eventually rejected the raw power that the Rur crystal
suggested it could offer because he would not truly be like a Jedi and have their freedom. He was somewhat traumatized
by his experience with Grakkus the Hutt and became weary of those that were very interested in the Jedi.

After everything they went through at the Battle of Yavin, Skywalker took pride in his membership of Red Squadron and
found kinship with them, but did not love the attention and honor he received for being the one to take the final shot.
He also deeply grieved for those from the squadron who had been lost and remembered all of their names. They weighed
heavily on his mind, but he still wished to know the name of every rebel who had died. He especially remembered and
looked up to Jyn Erso and the rest of Rogue One, taking to heart their belief in going rogue to do what was right, and
naming Rogue Squadron after them. However, he still retained a sense of confidence and excitement about the Alliance's
chances of beating the Empire and surviving the Death Star gave him greater confidence that he would survive other
events. He became determined to take down the Empire, and determined to become a Jedi in order to do so despite the
risk. Upon becoming leader of Rogue Squadron, Skywalker came to believe that it was the best team in the galaxy.

### Trials of the self and the Force

Over the course of his Jedi journey, Skywalker would rely on people, like Leia Organa, Han Solo, and Chewbacca, among
others. In the immediate aftermath of losing his hand, Skywalker was incredibly distraught and haunted by traumatic
flashbacks of the events on Cloud City, what he had lost, and what Vader revealed to him. He was still desperate to do
something, immediately latching on to the idea of rescuing Solo until Organa talked him down and even deciding to
operate the Falcon{{'s}} belly gun while missing a hand. During this time he was desperate for guidance, consumed by
anger and fear, and questioned if he should even be a Jedi. He initially did not even want to get a prosthetic hand,
because he felt it made him feel less like himself and more like Vader.

Overall, as his skills in the Force progressed, and especially after losing a hand to Darth Vader, Skywalker became more
patient and seasoned. He was often seen as having wisdom beyond his years. He came to decisively accept his path as a
Jedi and started to understand that having a lightsaber did not define him as a Jedi and that being one was about using
the Force to bring light to the galaxy. He held on to Yoda's teachings and repeated them to try and focus and overcome
obstacles. He stayed committed to his path as a Jedi, determined to help a suffering galaxy, even when Verla tried to
dissuade him from it by revealing what it cost her. He also was inspired by a seeing an old Jedi outpost and was
determined to fight for what was right no matter the risk just like they did. He felt the Force flow through his being
as a whole and did not localize it, meaning the loss of his hand did not physically limit him.

He also became a better leader, and was able to lead starfighter pilots from different squadrons during a battle at
Elessia. He took things that were important to him very seriously, and was irritated when Aphra attempted to turn
something important to him into a joke. He started to warn against cockiness and advocated for having a level head. He
developed more confidence in himself and in the Force, and when he focused on it became more capable. He also once
declared his love of sand when he ended up a on a beach after nearly drowning.

After discovering an ancient Jedi text, Skywalker became determined and committed to studying it and used the rare free
time he had to do so instead of relaxing. However, he still often doubted his abilities despite others believing in him
and his attempts to affirm himself in his abilities, and started to believe that without the text he would never become
a Jedi. Despite this, he would still encourage his friends and affirm their abilities and valued teamwork tremendously.

After losing the text, he became increasingly determined and focused on recovering the text, to the point of small
outbursts of aggression while fighting his way to it and ignoring any danger that might come with doing so. His
self-doubt continued to evolve into feeling like he would never truly be able to become a Jedi, leaving him wanting to
take control of his life and take action. As one of the only Jedi left, he also suffered from loneliness in that aspect,
wishing there were others. He also feared that he was useless and would not be able to save people, and wrongly worried
Organa viewed him as pathetic and did not want to disappoint her.

Before meeting Yoda, Skywalker was desperate for a teacher in the Force and dreamed of being able to do all the things
Kenobi had been able to. Because of this, Skywalker was quick to go along with Aphra's plan to go with her to the
Screaming Citadel because of the promise of a Jedi teacher and learning Jedi knowledge despite his knowledge of Aphra's
untrustworthiness. He eventually rejected the Rur crystal because he recognized that it was not a Jedi in practice and
belief and only in knowledge, and came to realize that he could not take shortcuts like Aphra did to get what he wanted
and urged her to stay away. He also immediately trusted and begged for training from Werla Calip when she said she knew
the Force despite having no proof of this, and started to believe that signs Calip was not what she seemed and her
beliefs were not morally sound was Calip testing him. This naivete led to Calip stealing his lightsaber from him. When
she revealed that she did not know the Force and her trainings were empty, Skywalker believed that there was more to her
than it seemed.

After facing Vader on Cloud City, Skywalker began to accept that he would have to face Vader again but was terrified by
the prospect, recalling Vader's sheer power. He hoped that he could tap into great power and defeat him, but doubted he
could, and was haunted by imagining himself facing Darth Vader again in his current state and being killed by the Sith
Lord. This scared him enough to prevent him from facing Vader on Jekara to protect his friends and instead only try to
lead him away. His fear also prevented him from being able to reach the Force consistently or speak to Kenobi,
contributing to his feelings of loneliness and sense that he was not ready. Despite not wanting to face Vader on Jekara,
he remained confident that he would become a Jedi and confront him one day.

Even after training with Yoda, his loneliness grew, and he became frustrated with Yoda for not responding to him and
answering him through the Force. He enthusiastically and desperately wanted to search for old Jedi temples and outposts
to learn more about the Order. He eventually decided that while he was grateful for what Yoda taught him, he would
rather just learn from his text. In the end, however, he would decide to return to Yoda to complete his training.
According to Yoda, when he arrived he had developed a clear "gaping need" for a connection to his father and to have a
family. While he did demand answers as for whether Vader was his father and wanted to know why it was "unfortunate" for
him to know, he was saddened by the news that Yoda was passing. Skywalker promised Organa he could train her as Yoda
trained him.

### Growing values and beliefs

Despite all he went through, Skywalker still retained his idealistic viewpoint, willing to think the best of anyone, and
believing that Vader was redeemable. Despite not thinking so immediately, Skywalker still believed that Doctor Aphra was
a good person and her friend despite her prior service to the Empire, and went out of his way to save her life. He
believed that she would see sense when it came to her belief in the Empire and was convinced that she could move past
the bad things she had went through to be a better person like Solo had. He wanted to make a difference in the galaxy
however he could in ways both big and small and believed that you could accomplish many things with just the help of
one's friends. He grew to truly believe that anything was possible.

He was also unable to ignore those who needed help, sometimes to the point of endangering missions for the sake of
saving someone else's life, as his father would throughout the Clone Wars. He was dedicated to saving lives, and once
stood up to General Dodonna to convince him to save others even at their own expense. Even when he knew he likely could
not beat Vader, he still went to help his friends against him, and was brave enough to fight him and put himself in
danger so that his friends Chewbacca and R2-D2 could escape and live. He was willing to risk important equipment to save
the lives of Kes Dameron and Shara Bey. He was willing to help save Lando Calrissian at a time when they did not know
each other well even though Skywalker did not trust him and was struggling with using the Force.

Throughout his life, Skywalker had always been filled with a need to be helpful and recognized that that need had
steered him toward fighting with the Rebel Alliance. Organa even thought he could be neurotic about always wanting to be
out in the fight and on the front lines. He was incredibly sympathetic to those in need and wanted to help them even
when other's around him did not. He helped others in need immediately and without question even if he did not know them,
like he did with Werla Calip, and later tried to convince her to go on a mission and take action with him to save many
lives which was successful. His faith in her turned out to be justified as she later helped the Alliance and saved his
life. He continued to always believe in an encourage others, especially his friends, and his faith that Aphra had good
in her was also somewhat proven right when she helped hide Echo Base from the Empire.

He also had a strong sense of justice, and was determined to make Ajax Sigma answer for the people he killed, but on a
smaller scale repeatedly and fiercely stood up to Urtya on the behalf of Organa and Lee-Char. He believed that the
Partisans plan to kill millions on Shu-Torun as payback to the Empire was not just or fair, and was even willing to
sacrifice his life if it meant they would not be able to do it. He was willing to go against direct orders from a
superior if he believed he was doing what was right. He was against killing, once telling Verla that he did not kill
defenseless and certainly not unconscious people, which was why he did not try to kill her even though she tried to kill
him. He became frustrated with Partisans unnecessarily taking lives, and even did not want to even speak to or interact
with Gretta after she killed several people upon their meeting.

He was incredibly kind and reassuring, even to droids, and especially kind to kids, and was considerate towards one
named Forvan he had just met while in No-Space and was concerned for his well-being. When the boy started to believe
that Skywalker would save him from being trapped in No-Space, Skywalker was sure to not get his hopes up, but still
assured the boy that he would do everything he could to try to free them and help them, demonstrating his unconditional
care for others. Skywalker cared deeply for his friends, and could intuitively tell when something was bothering Organa
or she was not acting like herself. Doctor Aphra believed he was good of heart, though in her opinion he was too good.
He told her that he was committed to helping protect her from Vader because it was what Jedi did. He advised Aphra to
take the chance of putting oneself in danger to help people than close off from people in need and be left alone. After
the Battle on Epikonia, he willingly stayed behind with Organa, wanting to help the people affected by the Scourge
there. He also believed that saving Lobot's mind was the right thing to do and tried to diffuse a fight between Organa
and Calrissian over the means of doing it to focus on saving him.

### Jedi Knight and Master

Leia Organa took note of Skywalker's change into a calmer man as he became a Jedi Knight, thinking of the change in
regard to the years that had past: while he was somewhat like other rebels who had joined the fight hopeful, only for
those rebels to become jaded over the course of the war, Organa noticed a much deeper stillness within him. While she
knew he was the same person, she did nearly mourn the loss of Skywalker's innocent optimism and excitement. All the
same, she saw how forgiving Skywalker was towards their father and pondered how it was possible for him to not hate
Vader the way she did; Skywalker tried to tell her about Anakin Skywalker's redemption to the light, but she refused to
listen. Skywalker was incredibly happy for his sister and Solo's marriage. He also offered to train her in the ways of
the Force, thinking they could be wonderful together as Jedi Knights, and gave her a chance to come with him on his hunt
for information about the Jedi of the past. While he did not see it as Organa needing to chose between a life with Solo
or a life as a Jedi, Organa did and declined.

Following the end of the Empire and the death of the Sith Order, Skywalker came to have great hope for the future of the
Jedi, coming to believe that if a single Jedi could help bring back balance to the Force, then a restored Jedi Order
perhaps could maintain that achieved peace and prevent the dark side of the Force from ever regaining its hold on the
galaxy. This belief led Luke to scour worlds for the remaining Jedi texts and gathered a new class of apprentices under
his tutelage. Furthermore, when he encountered the Imperial Special Forces trooper Del Meeko, he left the man with the
lesson that all he needed to do was be better, a lesson that would be passed onto Meeko's daughter, Zay Versio, and the
Resistance.

During his brief time as Grogu's master, Luke had to suppress his more sentimental side and be more professional in an
effort to successfully pass on the Jedi values to the child. Skywalker became confident in his abilities as a Jedi
Master, all while he became perceived by the galaxy as a legend. He was confident that he could take down seven Knights
of Ren on his own, and expressed to them that he was superior at using the Force than them, even slightly taunting them
and predicting that they would leave crying. This confidence was justified, as he did successfully defeat them. He also
cared deeply for his nephew Ben Solo, and was happy when Solo joined him on missions. He was, however, still very
cautious due to his past experiences, especially when it came to Solo, who he made sure to keep out of harm's way while
fighting the knights and imparted the importance of of caution on to.

### Later life

After Ben Solo, his nephew, betrayed him and killed most of his apprentices, Skywalker became embittered as well as
grief-ridden and chose to exile himself to the planet Ahch-To, where he would spend the rest of his life in with the
intention of dying where the Jedi began to forever end the Jedi. Despite all of this, he still comforted Chewbacca when
he learned of Han Solo's death, and became even more riddled with guilt. He also became more cautious in teaching others
of immense power due to Solo's betrayal, feeling finished with the Jedi Order, as evidenced by when he initially
regarded Rey as a delusional child, initially refused to train her, and when he would later decide to abruptly end their
lessons after seeing her drawn towards the dark side. Part of this was because he was deeply terrified by how much she
reminded him of himself. He also now believed that there was no hope for those who fell to the dark side, as shown when
he openly expressed that he believed there was no hope for Kylo Ren to return to the light. By this point, Skywalker had
come to regard Ren as his responsibility and his greatest failure. However, Rey helped him realize that in disconnecting
himself from the Force, he had abandoned those he loved in selfish pity, which he was determined to rectify.

However, after Yoda's spirit encouraged him that Rey was the Jedi's hope, Skywalker finally realized his wrongs and
chose to help the Resistance. Despite his lost faith in his nephew, Skywalker showed great remorse for failing to stop
his fall to the dark side, and for himself ending up being the turning point in Solo's fall. Skywalker realized that
instead of blaming himself, he blamed the Jedi for his failures. His belief of the Jedi being romanticized deities with
a legacy of failure remained, but gained realization that if one stripped away the sacred temples, lightsabers, and even
the legends about them, they were just good people the galaxy needed. In the end, Skywalker willingly sacrificed himself
for the Jedi to be revived and the Resistance to live on. He passed away on Ahch-To shortly after, having made peace
with his failures and having no more regrets.

### Romances

Based on observing Owen and Beru Lars, Skywalker grew up believing in showing his love to people rather than telling
them. When he and Nakari Kelen fell in love, he demonstrated his feelings for her, but he did not tell her how he felt;
he regretted that as grieved for her death alongside the then-recent losses of his aunt, uncle, and Obi-Wan Kenobi.
While stranded on Hubin, Skywalker was instantly smitten with Tula Markona and they shared a kiss, but it would be a
one-time event. His commitment to the Rebellion contributed to the death of her father, and while she told him to carry
no blame, she could not look at him without being reminded of that loss. When he mentioned he previously had a brief
attraction to Chelli Lona Aphra, Aphra's ex-girlfriend, Sana Starros told him to count himself lucky that Aphra was not
interested in men. Skywalker and Reye Hollis each appreciated the other man's smile and pleasantly-scented hair.
