# Aftermath

## The new era

The cataclysmic Clone Wars had raged for three, almost four, years by the end and accomplished exactly what Palpatine
had set out to achieve; the Jedi Order was destroyed, the Republic was transformed into the first Galactic Empire, and
the Galactic Senate into the Imperial Senate, with Palpatine as its Emperor. Nevertheless, during the following early
months of the Imperial Era, the Clone Wars were not entirely over, as minor pockets of resistance, including a force of
rebels on Umbara, continued to fight the Republic's successor state. Accordingly, the newly formed Imperial Military was
dispatched to crush these remaining obstacles to bring about the Empire's view of peace. The galaxy at large, however,
welcomed the Empire as a way to move forward from the destruction and devastation of war. Miyuki Trade League member Jet
Kordo was not surprised by the rise of the Empire, but reflected solemnly in a journal that they did not expect the
people of the galaxy to wake up to the price they were paying in return for the conflict's end.

In the aftermath of the conflict and with the dawn of the Empire, Senator Organa reflected that he was unsure if there
was a "right side" to the Clone Wars. In regards to the CIS, he still opposed what he deemed the cruel, destructive
tactics of the CIS military and the greed of its corporate sponsors, but he did recognize there were well-meaning people
amongst the Separatist state. Nonetheless, he still believed that non-violent routes had still been open for the
Separatists instead of deciding to go to war. As for the Republic, meanwhile, he had come to ponder if the war had been
a misdirection of sorts by those in the government who already wanted to transform the government into the Empire. He
recognized that Palpatine and his followers had pushed for the fighting-something Palpatine had tried to cover up with
public apprehension about going to war-and had his own thoughts on Dooku, although he believed the particulars of
Dooku's situation were not as important. Overall, he was forced to admit that, by taking part in the war, himself and
everyone else involved had played into Palpatine's plan to establish the Empire.

The last remnants of the Confederacy, known as the Separatist holdouts, continued to fight the Empire. The Imperial Navy
justified its existence by claiming there was an unending war against the holdouts and a dissident rebellion against the
Empire. Imperial stormtroopers were deployed to bring former Confederate planets under the Empire's control; even the
former CIS capital world of Raxus Secundus fell under occupation during the early reign of the Empire. A day into the
Imperial Era, sixty-three members of the Delegation of 2,000 were arrested by clone troopers on the claim that they had
helped the Jedi rebellion. Organa and Mothma were among those arrested but were released by publicly declaring their
loyalty lied with the Empire, which both continued to serve as senators. In secret, both used their positions as
senators as a cover for rebellion. Amidala's death, meanwhile, was blamed on the Jedi rebellion.

Also during the early reign of the Empire, the future Grand Moff Wilhuff Tarkin led a brutal purge on the moon Antar 4
that became known as the Antar Atrocity. Imperial forces rounded up and killed many Koorivar and Gotals including
Republic loyalists. Media coverage of the Antar Atrocity attracted considerable outrage in the Core Worlds and forced
the Emperor to reassign Tarkin to overseeing the pacification operations in the Western Reaches. The brutality of the
Antar Atrocity prompted other former Separatist worlds such as Kooriva, Murkhana, and Raxus to surrender former
Separatists to the Empire to avoid the same fate as Antar 4. Tarkin's actions earned him the animosity of former
Republic Intelligence operatives Berch Teller and Knotts, who founded a insurgent group consisting of survivors and
witnesses of the Antar Atrocity. In 14 BBY, Berch Teller's cell embarked on a campaign against Tarkin and the Empire,
but it was defeated.

## The unofficial conclusion

In 2 BBY, a last unofficial skirmish occurred when the Spectres rebel cell encountered a Separatist holdout of still
functioning battle droids, led by the super tactical droid Kalani, while attempting to scavenge the wreckage of a
Separatist supply ship. Kalani took the Spectres prisoner and, upon confirming that the group contained Clone Captain
Rex, who had joined with the rebellion instead of supporting the Empire, Jedi Knight Kanan Jarrus, and his padawan Ezra
Bridger, forced them to run through a tactical scenario, intending to prove that the Separatists had a higher chance of
victory and end the Clone Wars with a victory for the Confederacy.

While running through the scenario, Ezra Bridger observed that Rex was acting little better than a droid himself, and
was struck by the futility of the exercise. While Rex and Kalani debated the meaning of the outcome, Bridger interrupted
them. The young man successfully argued that the Clone Wars were never intended to be won and were only a means to an
end by the faction that would become the Galactic Empire; now their goals in fighting against the Emprie aligned. After
escaping from an Imperial capture attempt, both Rex and Kanan Jarrus observed that Bridger had done something that a
galaxy of senators and soldiers were unable to do: get representatives of all the factions to find a middle ground,
bringing a peaceful end to the Clone Wars.

## Legacy

Following its creation, the Galactic Empire tried to sweep away the memories of the troubled times of the Clone Wars but
also used the memory of the conflict to justify its expansion; the Empire expanded its reach across the galaxy, using
the memory of the Clone Wars by promising order to a galaxy tired of war. After it became clear that the Empire was
oppressive, however, the Rebel Alliance was created to oppose the Empire's rule. The seeds of the Alliance dated back to
the Clone Wars: its roots came from the local resistance cells established by the Jedi on Confederate worlds and from
the Delegation of 2,000, fulfilling that group's fear that armed resistance would be needed to restore democracy.
Palpatine's victory in the Clone Wars lasted just over two decades, as the Galactic Civil War led to the fall of
Palpatine and his Empire, as well as the redemption of Anakin Skywalker who renounced the persona of Vader, before dying
peacefully.

Many Imperials-such as the ambitious Imperial Navy officer Thrawn, the very same Chiss who had met Anakin Skywalker
during the conflict-remembered the Clone Wars as the cause of billions of deaths, hundreds of destroyed worlds, and
thousands of ruined worlds clawing back from the brink. He regarded secessionist movements such as the one on Botajef as
a repeat of that horror. His colleague Eli Vanto believed that the Clone Wars had bred animosity between humans and
aliens since several alien species had fought on the side of the Separatists. While several aliens had fought on the
side of the Republic, this sentiment gave rise to a level of xenophobia within the human-dominated Empire. Thrawn was
even reprimanded by his superior, Filia Rossi, the captain of the Blood Crow ship, for having equipment used by the
Separatists in his possession. Droids were also suspect in some beings' eyes; Din Djarin, for example, hated all droids
because Separatist battle droids had destroyed his family.

The Empire also housed veterans of the struggle in poorly maintained places like Crispus Commons, where they were forced
to deal with shabby maintenance and low-paying jobs. The conspiracy behind the conflict had not yet been brought to
public light, and nearly the entirety of the galaxy's population believed the war to have been exactly what it had
seemed; a rebellion initiated by numerous worlds of the Mid and Outer Rims desiring independence from a corrupt and
decadent central government. Despite this, some, such as Skelly, pieced together there was a greater truth about the
Clone Wars. Some individuals took part in historical re-enactments of the Clone Wars and there was also a section on the
Clone Wars in the Emperor's Museum on Coruscant and although they used real footage they told a false history, which was
seen by the Apprentice Legislature on a tour in 3 BBY.

Wilhuff Tarkin also remained suspicious of the cause of the war even after its conclusion, and believed that some group
of elite outsiders had manufactured the war for their own purposes. There were those, such as Bail Organa, who
remembered the bravery of those like the Jedi Knights during the Clone Wars who passed this knowledge to his daughter,
Princess Leia Organa as well as the stories such as that of the Senate hostage crisis as well as several about Obi-Wan
Kenobi. Leia would later sent a holographic message to Kenobi appealing to him in the name of his service alongside her
father during the Clone Wars. A couple of months after the Battle of Endor, a Separatist droid factory under the city of
Myrra on Akiva still had marks of Jedi lightsabers. It remained one of the most destructive conflicts in galactic
history, with billions of casualties on both sides. After Sidious's ultimate defeat in the Battle of Exegol of 35 ABY,
historian Beaumont Kin carried out the Exegol Excavation Project and discovered the extent of Palpatine's treachery in
the Sith records, publishing his deception and manipulation of the Clone Wars in his historical book The Rise and Fall
of the Galactic Empire.
